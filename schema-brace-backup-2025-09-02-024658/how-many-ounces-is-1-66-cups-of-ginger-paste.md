<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can throw off textures and flavours. Ginger paste, for instance, is dense, and using too much can overpower a dish. Conversely, too little might leave your meal bland. By converting 1.66 cups of ginger paste to ounces, you ensure the right balance. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes call for ginger paste, especially in Asian-inspired dishes like stir-fries or glazes. If you're unsure about measurements, weighing ingredients is often more reliable than volume. A kitchen scale can be a game-changer, especially for sticky ingredients like ginger paste. <h3>How to measure ginger paste correctly</h3>  
<p>Ginger paste can be tricky to measure due to its thick consistency. To get an accurate 1.66 cups, use a liquid measuring cup and level it off with a spatula. Alternatively, weigh it directly for precision—13.28 ounces is your target. If you're scaling a recipe, this method ensures consistency every time.</p>

<h3>Tips for storing ginger paste</h3>  
<p>Once opened, ginger paste should be refrigerated and used within a few weeks. If you've measured out 1.66 cups (13.28 ounces) but don’t need it all, freeze portions in ice cube trays for later use. This way, you’ll always have the right amount on hand for your air fryer creations.</p>
