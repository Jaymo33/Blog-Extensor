<h2>How to Measure 660 ml of Beef Stock</h2>
<p>Measuring 660 ml of beef stock accurately is simple with the right tools. A liquid measuring cup is the best option, as it provides clear markings for millilitres and cups. If you don't have one, a standard measuring cup will do, but you'll need to convert 660 ml of beef stock to cups first. Remember, 1 cup is roughly 236.59 ml, so 660 ml divided by 236.59 gives you 2.79 cups.</p>

<h3>Using a Kitchen Scale</h3>
<p>If you prefer precision, a kitchen scale can help you measure 660 ml of beef stock by weight. Beef stock typically weighs about 1 gram per millilitre, so 660 ml would be 660 grams. This method is especially useful for baking or making sauces in your air fryer, where exact measurements are critical. <h2>Tips for Storing Leftover Beef Stock</h2>
<p>If you don't use all 660 ml of beef stock at once, proper storage is key. Pour the leftover stock into an airtight container and refrigerate it for up to 3 days. For longer storage, freeze the stock in ice cube trays or small portions. This way, you can easily thaw and use the exact amount needed for future air fryer recipes.</p>
