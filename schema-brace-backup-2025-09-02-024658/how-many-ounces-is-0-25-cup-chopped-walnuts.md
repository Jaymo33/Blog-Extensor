<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Using 0.25 cup of chopped walnuts without knowing the weight might throw off your recipe’s texture and flavour. For example, if you’re making a walnut-crusted salmon, too many walnuts could make the coating overly dense. Too few, and you’ll miss out on that delicious crunch. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> helps you avoid these pitfalls.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, like our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>walnut brownies</a>, call for chopped walnuts in ounces rather than cups. This is because weight measurements are more precise, especially when dealing with ingredients that can vary in density. A loosely packed 0.25 cup of chopped walnuts might weigh slightly less than a tightly packed one, so weighing ensures accuracy.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.25 cup of chopped walnuts correctly</h3>  
<p>To get the most accurate measurement, use a kitchen scale for 1 ounce (28 grams) of chopped walnuts. If you don’t have one, lightly spoon the walnuts into a measuring cup without packing them down. Avoid tapping the cup, as this can compress the walnuts and lead to an incorrect measurement. For more tips, check out our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>.</p>
