<p>1.25 cups of tomato ketchup equals approximately 10 ounces. This conversion is handy when following air fryer recipes that require precise measurements. Whether you're whipping up a batch of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer chicken wings</a> or a tangy glaze for ribs, knowing the exact weight ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and precise measurements help achieve the perfect texture and flavour. Too much or too little tomato ketchup can throw off the balance of your recipe. For example, a marinade with 1.25 cups of tomato ketchup (10 ounces) will coat your ingredients evenly, ensuring a delicious outcome. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>

<p>Many air fryer recipes, like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken dishes</a>, call for tomato ketchup as a base for sauces or glazes. Measuring 1.25 cups (10 ounces) accurately ensures your dish isn't too sweet or too tangy. It's also useful for meal prepping, where consistency is key. If you're unsure, a kitchen scale can help verify your measurements.</p>

<h2>How to measure 1.25 cups of tomato ketchup</h2>
<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like tomato ketchup can be tricky. To get 1.25 cups (10 ounces), use a liquid measuring cup and level it off with a spatula. Alternatively, weigh it on a kitchen scale for precision. This method is especially useful for recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/beef>air fryer meatloaf</a>, where the sauce needs to be just right.</p>

<h3>Using a kitchen scale</h3>
<p>A kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure 1.25 cups of tomato ketchup. Simply place your bowl on the scale, tare it to zero, and add ketchup until it reads 10 ounces. This eliminates guesswork and ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> to keep your scale in top condition.</p>

<h2>Common uses for 1.25 cups of tomato ketchup</h2>
<p>1.25 cups (10 ounces) of tomato ketchup is a versatile amount for many air fryer recipes. It's enough to make a glaze for a family-sized portion of wings or a base for BBQ sauce. You might also use it in <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>potato dishes</a>, where the tangy flavour complements crispy fries or wedges.</p>

<p>Another popular use is in homemade ketchup-based dips or marinades. Whether you're cooking for a crowd or just meal prepping, 1.25 cups (10 ounces) is a practical measurement to remember. For more inspiration, browse our collection of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> that feature tomato ketchup as a key ingredient.</p>

<h3>Storing leftover tomato ketchup</h3>
<p>If you have leftover tomato ketchup after measuring out 1.25 cups (10 ounces), store it in an airtight container in the fridge. This keeps it fresh for future air fryer recipes. Always check the expiry date and give it a stir before using it again to ensure consistency.</p>
