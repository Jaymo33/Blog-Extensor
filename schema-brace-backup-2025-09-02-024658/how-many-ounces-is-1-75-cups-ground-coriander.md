<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of ground coriander can elevate your dish from good to great. Too little, and the flavour might be underwhelming; too much, and it could overpower other ingredients. The air fryer’s intense heat means spices like coriander release their flavours quickly, so precision is key. <h3>How to Measure Ground Coriander Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a kitchen scale to weigh your ground coriander. If you don’t have one, spoon the coriander into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to an incorrect measurement. This method ensures you get the right amount for your air fryer recipes every time.</p>

<p>Ground coriander adds a warm, citrusy note to dishes like air-fried chicken, roasted vegetables, and even homemade falafel. <h3>Storing Ground Coriander for Freshness</h3>  
<p>To keep your ground coriander fresh, store it in an airtight container away from heat and light. Proper storage ensures the spice retains its flavour, so your air fryer dishes always taste their best. If you notice a dull aroma, it might be time to refresh your stash.</p>
