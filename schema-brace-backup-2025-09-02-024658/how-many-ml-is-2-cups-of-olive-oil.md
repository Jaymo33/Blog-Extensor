<h2>Why Accurate Olive Oil Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of olive oil in your air fryer ensures even cooking and prevents dryness or sogginess. Too much oil can make food greasy, while too little may leave it undercooked. <h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, while the US uses 237 ml per cup. For 2.0 cups of olive oil, this means 568 ml (UK) or 473 ml (US). Most modern recipes, including those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, follow the US standard unless specified.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Olive Oil Without a Cup</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have measuring cups, use a kitchen scale for precision. Olive oil weighs approximately 0.92 grams per ml, so 473 ml (2.0 cups) equals about 435 grams. This method ensures accuracy, especially for baking recipes where ratios matter.</p>  

<p>Alternatively, use a standard tablespoon (15 ml) to measure 2.0 cups of olive oil—that’s roughly 31.5 tablespoons. <h2>Storing Olive Oil for Air Fryer Use</h2>  
<p>Store olive oil in a cool, dark place to maintain freshness. If you’ve measured 2.0 cups (473 ml) but only need half, transfer the rest to an airtight container. Proper storage prevents oxidation, ensuring your oil stays perfect for future air fryer dishes.</p>
