<p>425 grams of plain flour equals approximately 3.4 cups in UK measurements or 3.5 cups in US measurements. This conversion is essential for air fryer baking, where precision ensures perfect textures and consistent results. Whether you're whipping up cakes, cookies, or bread in your air fryer, knowing how to measure flour correctly can make all the difference. <h2>Why Accurate Flour Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small measurement errors can lead to dense cakes or dry cookies. When converting 425 grams of plain flour to cups, factors like scooping methods and flour density come into play. For air fryer recipes, where cooking times are shorter, precision ensures even browning and perfect rise. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies these calculations, so you never have to guess.</p>  

<p>Using a kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure flour, but if you only have cups, fluff the flour first before lightly spooning it into the cup. Avoid packing it down, as this can add up to 20% more flour than needed. For 425 grams of plain flour, this method ensures you get the right consistency for air fryer-friendly batters and doughs.</p>  

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, 1 cup of plain flour weighs about 125 grams, making 425 grams roughly 3.4 cups. In the US, a cup is slightly larger at 120 grams, so 425 grams equals about 3.5 cups. This small difference matters in recipes like air fryer cakes or bread, where excess flour can make results dry. Check your recipe’s origin to use the correct cup measurement.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Flour Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method: gently spoon flour into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. For 425 grams of plain flour, this means filling and leveling 3.5 US cups or 3.4 UK cups. Storing flour in an airtight container also prevents moisture absorption, which can throw off measurements.</p>
