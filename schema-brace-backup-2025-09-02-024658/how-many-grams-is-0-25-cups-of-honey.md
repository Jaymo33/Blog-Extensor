<h2>Why Accurate Honey Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of honey can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too much can lead to overly sweet or sticky dishes, while too little might leave your food lacking flavour. Honey’s density varies slightly depending on factors like temperature and type, but 0.25 cups typically converts to 85 grams. This measurement is especially useful for baking, where precision is key to achieving the right texture and taste.</p>

Whether you’re making cakes, cookies, or glazes, 0.25 cups of honey in grams ensures consistency. No more guessing—just delicious, perfectly balanced results.</p>  

<h3>Types of Honey and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Not all honey weighs the same due to differences in moisture content and viscosity. For example, thicker varieties like manuka honey might weigh slightly more than lighter, runny honey. However, for most standard honey types, 0.25 cups will still hover around 85 grams. If you’re unsure, using a kitchen scale is the best way to guarantee accuracy.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Honey Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem! You can still measure 0.25 cups of honey using simple household tools. A standard measuring cup works, but remember to lightly grease it first—honey’s stickiness can make it hard to pour out completely. Alternatively, use tablespoons: 0.25 cups equals 4 tablespoons, which is roughly 85 grams.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Honey</h2>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is not accounting for honey’s sticky nature, which can lead to inaccurate measurements. Always level off your measuring cup or spoon to avoid excess. Another pitfall is assuming all liquid sweeteners weigh the same—maple syrup, for example, has a different density. Stick to 85 grams for 0.25 cups of honey to keep things consistent.</p>
