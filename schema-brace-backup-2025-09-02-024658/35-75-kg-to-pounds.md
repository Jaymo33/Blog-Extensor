<p>The conversion from kilograms to pounds is straightforward once you know the multiplier. This calculation gives you the precise figure of 78.815425 pounds, which is typically rounded to 78.82 pounds for most practical purposes. This level of accuracy is particularly important in air frying, where even small weight discrepancies can affect cooking time and texture. <h2>Tips for Handling Large Quantities in Your Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Working with a quantity like 35.75 kg (78.82 lbs) obviously isn't for a single air fryer basket—this highlights the importance of batch cooking. The key to successfully cooking large amounts of food is to do it in manageable batches without overcrowding the basket. Overcrowding prevents the hot air from circulating freely, resulting in steamed rather than crispy food. For large families or meal prepping, cook your food in sequential batches, keeping the cooked portions warm in a low oven while you finish the rest. This method ensures every bite is as perfectly cooked as the first, making your effort well worth it.</p>

<p>After cooking such a large quantity, cleaning your air fryer is essential. Food debris and grease can build up quickly, especially after a big cooking session. To keep your appliance in top condition, make sure to use a non-abrasive sponge and a suitable <a href="https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner">air fryer cleaner</a> to wipe down the basket and interior after it has cooled down. Regular cleaning not only maintains the performance and longevity of your air fryer but also ensures that your food always tastes its best, with no lingering flavours from previous meals. A clean appliance is a happy appliance!</p>
