<h3>How to Measure 177 ml of Maple Syrup Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug with ml markings, use tablespoons. One tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so 177 ml is roughly 12 tablespoons. For thicker syrups, lightly grease your measuring spoon first to help it slide off easily. This trick works wonders when prepping <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>air fryer desserts</a>.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Air Fryer Times for Maple Syrup-Based Recipes</h3>  
<p>Recipes using 0.75 cups of maple syrup converted to ml (177 ml) may need slight timing adjustments. Sugar content accelerates browning, so reduce heat by 5°C or check food 2-3 minutes early. This prevents burning in dishes like air fryer roasted carrots or sticky tofu.</p>  

<h2>Cleaning Maple Syrup Residue from Your Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>After cooking with 0.75 cups maple syrup measurement (177 ml), sticky residue is inevitable. For baked-on spots, a baking soda paste works wonders without damaging non-stick coatings.</p>
