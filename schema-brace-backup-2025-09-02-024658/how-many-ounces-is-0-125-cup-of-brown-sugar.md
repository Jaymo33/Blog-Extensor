<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve even cooking and crispiness. Using the correct amount of brown sugar—like 0.125 cup to ounces—ensures your dishes caramelise properly without burning. Too much sugar can lead to sticky, overcooked results, while too little may leave your food bland. <p>Brown sugar’s moisture content makes it denser than granulated sugar, so volume-to-weight conversions like 0.125 cup to ounces are crucial. Packing the sugar tightly or loosely can alter the weight, affecting your recipe. Always spoon and level brown sugar for accuracy, especially in air fryer baking where heat distribution is rapid.</p>

<p>To measure 0.125 cup (2 tablespoons) of brown sugar, use a dry measuring spoon and pack it lightly. For exact ounces, a kitchen scale is ideal—0.125 cup should weigh just under 1 ounce.
