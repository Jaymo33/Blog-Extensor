<h2>Understanding the Conversion: 310 ml to Cups</h2>  
<p>Converting 310 ml of yogurt to cups is straightforward once you know the ratio. One standard US cup holds about 236.59 ml, while a UK cup is slightly larger at 284.13 ml. For most recipes, especially those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a>, the US measurement is used. This means 310 ml of yogurt equals roughly 1.31 cups. If you're using a UK cup, it’s closer to 1.09 cups, but the difference is minimal for most air fryer dishes.</p>  

<p>Why does this matter? Air fryer recipes often rely on precise liquid measurements to achieve the right texture. Whether you're making a yogurt-based marinade for chicken or a cake batter, knowing how many cups 310 ml of yogurt is ensures consistency. <h3>Why Yogurt Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>Yogurt adds moisture and tenderness to air fryer recipes, from marinades to baked goods. Using the correct amount—like 310 ml (or 1.31 cups)—ensures your dish isn’t too dry or soggy. For example, a yogurt-based coating for air-fried chicken stays crispier when measured accurately. <h3>Tips for Measuring Yogurt Accurately</h3>  
<p>To measure 310 ml of yogurt (1.31 cups), use a liquid measuring cup for the most precision. Stir the yogurt first to eliminate air pockets, then level it off with a knife. For thicker yogurts, like Greek yogurt, slight adjustments may be needed. Always refer to your recipe’s requirements, especially for air fryer baking, where consistency is key.</p>
