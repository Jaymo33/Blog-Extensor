<p>3 tablespoons (tbsp) of baking powder equals approximately 42 grams (g). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, where precise measurements can make or break your baked goods. Whether you're whipping up cakes, muffins, or even savoury dishes, knowing how to convert 3 tbsp of baking powder to grams ensures consistency. <h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer baking</h2>  
<p>Baking powder is a leavening agent, and even small discrepancies can affect the texture and rise of your baked goods. Too much can leave a bitter taste, while too little may result in dense, flat cakes. For air fryer recipes, where heat distribution differs from conventional ovens, precision is even more critical. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies these measurements, so you never have to second-guess.</p>  

<p>If you're adapting a traditional recipe for the air fryer, converting 3 tbsp of baking powder to grams ensures you maintain the right balance. This is especially useful for recipes like scones or quick breads, where the leavening agent plays a starring role. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes</a> section for foolproof ideas tailored to your air fryer.</p>  

<h3>How to measure baking powder correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate conversion of 3 tbsp baking powder to grams, use a digital kitchen scale. Spoon the baking powder into the tablespoon without packing it down, then level it off with a knife. This avoids over-measuring, which is easy to do if you scoop directly from the container. If you don’t have a scale, remember that 1 tbsp of baking powder weighs roughly 14 grams, so 3 tbsp equals 42 grams.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 3 tbsp of baking powder</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes call for 3 tbsp of baking powder, particularly larger batches of cakes or batters. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> often use this quantity to ensure a light, fluffy texture. Savoury dishes like air-fried onion rings or tempura batter may also require it for crispiness.</p>  

<p>If you're experimenting with gluten-free or vegan baking, precise measurements become even more crucial. Substitutes like almond flour or chickpea flour behave differently, so knowing exactly how many grams are in 3 tbsp of baking powder helps you adjust accordingly. <h3>Converting other baking powder quantities</h3>  
<p>Need to scale up or down? Here’s a quick reference: 1 tbsp = 14g, 2 tbsp = 28g, and 4 tbsp = 56g. This makes it easy to adapt recipes without recalculating. Whether you're halving a recipe or doubling it, these conversions ensure your air fryer bakes turn out perfectly every time.</p>
