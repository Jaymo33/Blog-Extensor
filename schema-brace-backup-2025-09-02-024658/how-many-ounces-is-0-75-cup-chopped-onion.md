<h2>Why accurate onion measurements matter in air frying</h2>
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly using circulating hot air, so ingredient proportions directly affect texture and doneness. Too much onion can lead to steaming rather than crisping, while too little may leave dishes lacking flavour. <h3>Tips for measuring chopped onion</h3>
<p>For most accurate results when measuring 0.75 cup (3 ounces) of chopped onion, use these techniques: Chop onions uniformly to prevent air gaps in measuring cups, gently fill without packing down, and level off the top with a straight edge. These methods ensure consistency across all your air fryer preparations.</p>

<h3>Adjusting onion quantities in recipes</h3>
<p>If a recipe calls for 0.75 cup chopped onion (3 ounces) but you prefer stronger or milder onion flavour, you can adjust slightly. Remember that increasing onion may require extending cook time slightly in your air fryer, as extra moisture needs evaporating before browning occurs.</p>
