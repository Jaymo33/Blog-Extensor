<p>0.25 cup of soy sauce equals approximately 2 fluid ounces (or 2 oz) in the UK. This measurement is essential for air fryer recipes where precision ensures the best flavour and texture. Whether you're marinating chicken or whipping up a stir-fry, knowing this conversion helps you nail the recipe every time.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of soy sauce can make or break your dish. Too little, and your meal might lack depth; too much, and it could become overly salty. The air fryer’s intense heat means liquids like soy sauce reduce quickly, so measuring correctly is key. For more air fryer tips, check out <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>our conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, like teriyaki chicken or glazed salmon, rely on soy sauce for that umami kick. If you’re prepping a batch of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, knowing 0.25 cup soy sauce to ounces ensures consistency. Plus, it’s handy for meal prepping sauces or marinades in advance.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.25 Cup of Soy Sauce</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup, use a kitchen scale for accuracy. Pour your soy sauce until the scale reads 2 oz (about 59 ml). Alternatively, a standard UK tablespoon holds 0.5 oz, so four tablespoons will give you the right amount. For more precision, explore <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>.</p>

It’s also ideal for marinating <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/shrimp>shrimp</a> before air frying—just enough to coat without overpowering.</p>
