<h2>Why accurate tahini measurements matter</h2>
<p>Using the correct amount of tahini can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too little, and your dish might lack flavour; too much, and it could become overly rich or sticky. Tahini’s thick consistency means volume measurements like tablespoons can vary slightly, making weight more reliable. For example, 0.25 tbsp of tahini to grams ensures you’re adding just the right amount to your marinades or dressings. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those for Middle Eastern-inspired dishes, rely on tahini for its creamy texture and nutty taste. Whether you’re making hummus, baba ganoush, or tahini cookies, precise measurements ensure balanced flavours. A slight miscalculation in converting 0.25 tbsp of tahini into grams could throw off the entire dish. That’s why we recommend using a digital scale for the most accurate results.</p>

<h2>How to measure tahini for air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Measuring tahini can be tricky due to its thick, sticky consistency. To accurately measure 0.25 tbsp of tahini in grams, use a spoon to scoop it lightly without packing it down. Alternatively, weigh it directly on a kitchen scale for precision. This method is especially useful when preparing air fryer recipes like tahini-glazed chicken or roasted vegetables with tahini sauce.</p>

<h3>Tools for accurate tahini measurements</h3>
<p>A digital kitchen scale is the best tool for converting 0.25 tbsp of tahini to grams. If you don’t have one, use a measuring spoon and level it off with a knife for consistency.
