<p>Air fryers rely on precise cooking times and temperatures, and ingredient measurements are just as crucial. Using 2 cups of whole milk when a recipe calls for 16 ounces can affect texture and taste. Whole milk, with its higher fat content, adds richness to dishes like custards or batters. If you're experimenting with new recipes, always measure liquids carefully. Visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially baked goods, depend on the right balance of wet and dry ingredients. Too much or too little milk can lead to dry or soggy results. Measuring 2 cups of whole milk correctly ensures your dish turns out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 2 cups of whole milk accurately</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for the most precise results. Pour the milk to the 2-cup mark, checking at eye level to avoid overfilling. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale can help—16 ounces is your target. For more tips on air fryer cooking, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning and maintenance guide</a>.</p>
