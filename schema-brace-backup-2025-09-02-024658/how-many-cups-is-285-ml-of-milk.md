<p>285 ml of milk equals approximately 1.2 cups in the UK or 1.2 cups in the US (though cup sizes differ slightly). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, ensuring precise measurements for perfect results every time. Whether you're whipping up a cake or a creamy sauce, knowing how to convert 285 ml of milk to cups makes cooking effortless.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Milk Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Precision is key when using an air fryer, as even small measurement errors can affect texture and cooking times. For example, adding too much milk to a batter can make it too runny, while too little can dry it out. Recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> often rely on exact measurements for consistent results. Converting 285 ml of milk to cups ensures your dishes turn out just right.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake category</a>, call for milk as a key ingredient. Whether you're making pancakes, muffins, or custards, knowing that 285 ml of milk is roughly 1.2 cups helps streamline your prep. This conversion is especially useful if you're scaling recipes up or down.</p>  

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for 285 ml of Milk</h3>  
<p>In the UK, a standard cup holds 250 ml, meaning 285 ml is just over 1.1 cups. In the US, a cup is 240 ml, so 285 ml equals about 1.2 cups. While the difference seems minor, it can matter in baking. Always check whether your recipe uses UK or US measurements to avoid mishaps.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 285 ml of Milk Without a Measuring Cup</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup, you can use a kitchen scale. Since milk’s density is close to water, 285 ml weighs roughly 285 grams. Alternatively, use tablespoons—1 UK tablespoon is 15 ml, so 285 ml equals 19 tablespoons. For more tips, check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Another handy trick is repurposing containers. A standard UK mug holds about 300 ml, so filling it nearly to the top gives you roughly 285 ml of milk. For air fryer recipes like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert section</a>, this method works in a pinch.</p>  

<h3>Common Recipes Using 285 ml of Milk</h3>  
<p>Many air fryer favourites, such as Yorkshire puddings or creamy pasta bakes, use around 285 ml of milk. This measurement is also common in custards and batters. For inspiration, browse <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> for dishes that make the most of this quantity.</p>  

<h2>Converting 285 ml of Milk for Different Dairy Alternatives</h2>  
<p>If you’re using plant-based milk, the volume conversion remains the same—285 ml still equals 1.2 cups. However, thickness may vary, so adjust cooking times slightly. For example, almond milk may thin out sauces faster than cow’s milk. Always test and tweak as needed.</p>
