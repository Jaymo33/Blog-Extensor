<h3>How to Measure 0.66 Cup of Hot Sauce Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem! You can still measure 0.66 cup of hot sauce using common kitchen tools. A liquid measuring cup with clear markings is your best friend here. Fill it to the 2/3 cup line, then subtract a teaspoon to approximate 0.66 cup. Alternatively, use 10 tablespoons + 2 teaspoons of hot sauce, as this equals roughly 5.28 ounces. For sticky sauces, lightly grease the measuring cup first to ensure easy pouring.</p>  

<h2>Converting 0.66 Cup of Hot Sauce to Ounces for UK Cooks</h2>  
<p>In the UK, fluid ounces and weight ounces can cause confusion, but for hot sauce, 0.66 cup equals 5.28 fluid ounces (or ~150 grams by weight). This conversion is handy when using American recipes or scaling portions. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipe category</a> often includes hot sauce measurements, so knowing this conversion is a game-changer. Remember, UK cups are slightly larger (284ml vs. 240ml), but for hot sauce, the difference is negligible in most recipes.</p>

<h3>Tips for Using Hot Sauce in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>Hot sauce can enhance flavour but also affect cooking times due to its sugar content. For 0.66 cup (5.28 oz) of hot sauce in marinades, reduce other liquids slightly to avoid sogginess. Brushing sauces on during the last few minutes of cooking prevents burning. Always preheat your air fryer for even heat distribution, especially with sticky sauces.
