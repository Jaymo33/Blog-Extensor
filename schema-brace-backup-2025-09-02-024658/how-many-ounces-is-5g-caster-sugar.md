<h2>Why Convert 5g of Caster Sugar to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but some kitchen scales or international recipes may list ingredients in ounces. Converting 5g of caster sugar to ounces (0.18 oz) helps maintain accuracy, especially in air fryer baking where small quantities matter. A slight miscalculation can affect texture and taste, so it’s worth double-checking. <h3>How to Measure 5g of Caster Sugar Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, 5g of caster sugar is about 1 teaspoon. However, for precise air fryer recipes, we recommend using digital scales. A small investment in accuracy can make a big difference in your baking results. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 5g of Caster Sugar in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Small amounts of caster sugar, like 5g (0.18 oz), are often used in glazes, meringues, or dusting desserts. In air fryers, even a tiny quantity can enhance flavour without overpowering. For example, a light sprinkle over <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a> before air frying adds a delicate sweetness.</p>  

<p>You might also find 5g of caster sugar in recipes for air fryer sponges or syrups. Since air fryers cook faster than conventional ovens, precise measurements prevent burning or uneven cooking. Always level off your measurements for the best results.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Storing Caster Sugar</h3>  
<p>Keep caster sugar in an airtight container to prevent clumping. Humidity can affect its texture, so store it in a cool, dry place. Proper storage ensures your 5g (0.18 oz) measurements remain accurate for future recipes.</p>
