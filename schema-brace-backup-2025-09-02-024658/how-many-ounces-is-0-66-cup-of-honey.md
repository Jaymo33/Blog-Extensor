<h2>Why converting 0.66 cup of honey to ounces matters</h2>  
<p>Honey is denser than many liquids, meaning a cup doesn’t always weigh the same as water or milk. This makes converting 0.66 cup of honey to ounces crucial for accuracy in recipes. Air fryer cooking, especially with sticky ingredients like honey, benefits from precise measurements to avoid over-sweetening or uneven cooking. If you’re preparing a honey-glazed salmon or a batch of air-fried cookies, getting the weight right ensures perfect texture and flavour. For more recipe ideas, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes collection</a>.</p>  

<p>Using a kitchen scale for honey is far more reliable than relying on cup measurements alone. A slight variation in how you fill the cup can lead to significant differences in weight. This is why professional bakers and air fryer enthusiasts prefer weighing ingredients like honey in ounces. <h3>How to measure 0.66 cup of honey accurately</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.66 cup of honey, use a liquid measuring cup with clear markings and a spout for easy pouring. Lightly coat the cup with oil or non-stick spray to help the honey slide out smoothly. Alternatively, weigh it directly on a kitchen scale—8 ounces is your target for 0.66 cup. This method is especially useful for sticky ingredients like honey, which can cling to measuring cups and lead to inaccuracies. <h3>Tips for substituting honey in air fryer recipes</h3>  
<p>If you’re out of honey, you can substitute 0.66 cup (8 ounces) with maple syrup or agave nectar in a 1:1 ratio. For savoury dishes, a mix of brown sugar and water can mimic honey’s glaze, though the flavour profile will differ. Always test substitutions in small batches to ensure they work with your air fryer’s cooking style.</p>
