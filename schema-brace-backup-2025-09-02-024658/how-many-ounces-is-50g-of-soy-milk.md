<h3>How to Measure 50g of Soy Milk Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use volume measurements as a close alternative. Since soy milk’s density is similar to water, 50g equals roughly 50ml. This translates to about 3.5 tablespoons or just under ¼ cup. For air fryer recipes, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a>, small inaccuracies won’t ruin your dish, but precision always helps.</p>

<h3>Does Soy Milk Weight Vary by Brand?</h3>  
<p>Most commercial soy milks have a similar density, so 50g will consistently equal ~1.76 oz. However, fortified or sweetened varieties might slightly differ. Always check the label if precision is critical, especially for air fryer baking. When in doubt, stick to the standard conversion for reliable results.</p>
