<h2>Why accurate garlic measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Garlic is a powerhouse of flavour, and using the right amount can elevate your dish. Too little, and your meal might lack depth; too much, and it could overpower other ingredients. In an air fryer, where cooking times are shorter, precise measurements ensure even flavour distribution. <h3>How to measure chopped garlic accurately</h3>
<p>Measuring chopped garlic can be tricky due to its irregular shape. For the most accurate results, use a kitchen scale to weigh out 15 grams. If you don’t have a scale, lightly pack the garlic into the tablespoon to ensure consistency. This method works well for most air fryer recipes, from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>garlic chicken</a> to roasted veggies.</p>

<h2>Common uses for 1.5 tbsp of chopped garlic in air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>1.5 tbsp of chopped garlic (15 grams) is a versatile amount for many dishes. It’s perfect for marinades, sauces, and rubs, adding a robust flavour without being overwhelming. In the air fryer, this quantity works well for recipes like garlic butter shrimp or crispy garlic potatoes. <h3>Tips for storing chopped garlic</h3>
<p>If you’ve chopped more garlic than needed, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. You can also freeze it in ice cube trays for longer storage. This way, you’ll always have the right amount of chopped garlic ready for your next air fryer adventure.</p>
