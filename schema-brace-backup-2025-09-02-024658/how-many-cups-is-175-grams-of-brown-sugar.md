<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but precision in ingredients ensures consistency. Too much or too little brown sugar can affect texture and sweetness in cakes, cookies, or glazes. For example, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipe</a> with 175 grams of brown sugar requires the right cup measurement to avoid dryness or excessive moisture. Always measure brown sugar by lightly spooning it into a cup and levelling it off for accuracy.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 175 Grams of Brown Sugar Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, use the spoon-and-level method for the most accurate 175g brown sugar to cups conversion. Fill your measuring cup loosely with brown sugar, then level it with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can add up to 20% more sugar than intended. For recipes needing precision, like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cookies>cookies</a>, this method prevents dough from spreading too much or becoming overly dense.</p>

For foolproof results, stick to weight (grams) where possible.</p>
