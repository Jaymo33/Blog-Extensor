<h2>Why accurate soy sauce measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of soy sauce can make or break a dish. Too little, and your meal might lack depth; too much, and it could become overly salty. In air fryer cooking, where flavours intensify due to rapid air circulation, precision is key. For example, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>teriyaki chicken recipe</a> relies on the perfect balance of soy sauce to achieve that glossy, umami-rich glaze. Measuring 2 cups of soy sauce in ounces ensures consistency, especially when scaling recipes up or down.</p>  

<p>Many UK cooks wonder whether fluid ounces and weight ounces differ for liquids like soy sauce. The good news is that for soy sauce, 1 fluid ounce roughly equals 1 weight ounce, simplifying the process. If you’re prepping a large batch of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>air-fried vegetables</a>, this conversion saves time and avoids kitchen mishaps. Keep a measuring jug or digital scale handy for foolproof results.</p>  

<h3>Converting 2 cups of soy sauce to ounces</h3>  
<p>In the UK, 1 cup equals 8 fluid ounces, so 2 cups of soy sauce translate to 16 fluid ounces. This standard applies to most liquid ingredients, including soy sauce, making it easy to remember. If your recipe calls for 2 cups of soy sauce but you only have a scale, now you know it’s 16 oz.
