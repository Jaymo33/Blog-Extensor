<h2>Why Convert 150g of Soft Cheese to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, while others rely on ounces, especially older or international cookbooks. Converting 150g of soft cheese to ounces ensures you’re using the right amount for your dish. Soft cheese, like cream cheese or ricotta, is often used in air fryer recipes for desserts, dips, and even savoury bakes. Accurate measurements prevent your dish from being too dense or too runny.</p>  

<p>If you’re working with an air fryer, precise measurements are even more critical due to its compact cooking space. Too much or too little cheese can affect cooking times and textures. For more air fryer measurement tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 150g of Soft Cheese in Ounces</h3>  
<p>To convert 150g of soft cheese to ounces, multiply the grams by 0.035274. This gives you roughly 5.29 ounces. If you don’t have a scale, use measuring spoons or cups—though weighing is always more accurate. Soft cheese can vary in density, so spooning it into a measuring cup may not give you the exact weight.</p>

<p>Soft cheese is versatile in air fryer cooking. From cheesecakes to stuffed mushrooms, 150g (or 5.29 ounces) is a common quantity. You can also use it in dips, stuffed chicken breasts, or even as a base for air-fried desserts.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing and Handling Soft Cheese</h3>  
<p>Soft cheese should be stored in the fridge and used within a few days of opening. If you’re measuring 150g for a recipe, let it soften slightly for easier mixing. Avoid overheating it in the air fryer, as it can separate or become grainy.
