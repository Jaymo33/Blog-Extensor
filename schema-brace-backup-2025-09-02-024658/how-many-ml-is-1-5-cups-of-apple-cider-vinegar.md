<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of apple cider vinegar ensures your dishes have the right balance of acidity and flavour. Too little, and your marinade might lack punch; too much, and it could overpower other ingredients. Air fryer recipes, like those found on our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes page</a>, often rely on precise liquid measurements for even cooking. A 355 ml measurement for 1.5 cups of apple cider vinegar guarantees consistency, whether you’re cooking for one or meal prepping for the week.</p>  

<p>Many home cooks underestimate how small measurement errors can affect texture and taste. For example, a dressing with too much vinegar might become overly sharp, while a marinade with too little could fail to tenderise meat properly. By converting 1.5 cups of apple cider vinegar to 355 ml, you eliminate uncertainty and achieve restaurant-quality results at home.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 1.5 Cups of Apple Cider Vinegar in ml</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale or liquid measuring jug can help. Since 1 cup equals 237 ml, multiplying by 1.5 gives you 355.5 ml, which rounds to 355 ml for simplicity. For air fryer recipes, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken category</a>, this precision ensures your dish isn’t too dry or overly acidic. Always measure liquids at eye level to avoid discrepancies.</p>  

<h3>Using ml vs. Cups in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>Millilitres are often more accurate than cups, especially for liquids like apple cider vinegar. A slight overpour in a cup measurement can throw off your recipe, whereas ml measurements are exact. If you’re adapting a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>our conversion chart</a>, stick to ml for reliability. This is particularly useful for baking, where precision is key to achieving the perfect rise or texture.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 1.5 Cups (355 ml) of Apple Cider Vinegar</h2>  
<p>This quantity is ideal for making large batches of dressings, pickling vegetables, or marinating proteins like chicken or tofu. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetables section</a> for recipes that use apple cider vinegar for a flavour boost. You might also use 355 ml of apple cider vinegar for homemade cleaning solutions—just be sure to keep cooking and cleaning vinegar separate!</p>  

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Smaller or Larger Quantities</h3>  
<p>Need half or double the amount? Simply halve or double the 355 ml measurement. For example, 0.75 cups would be 177.5 ml, while 3 cups would be 710 ml.
