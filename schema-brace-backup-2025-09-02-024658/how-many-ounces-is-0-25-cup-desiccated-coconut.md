<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect ingredient quantities can throw off cooking times. Using 0.25 cup of desiccated coconut (0.7 oz) ensures consistency, especially in baked goods like coconut macaroons or crumb coatings. Too much or too little can affect texture and browning. <p>Desiccated coconut is lightweight, so volume-to-weight conversions are crucial. A packed 0.25 cup will weigh slightly more than a loosely filled one. For best results, spoon the coconut into your measuring cup and level it off. This method ensures you’re using the correct 0.7 ounces every time. <h3>How to measure desiccated coconut without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use the spoon-and-level method for 0.25 cup desiccated coconut. Gently spoon the coconut into a dry measuring cup without compacting it, then level the top with a straight edge. This technique approximates the 0.7-ounce weight needed for recipes. <h3>Storing desiccated coconut for freshness</h3>  
<p>To keep your 0.25 cup measurements accurate, store desiccated coconut in an airtight container. Moisture can clump it, affecting volume and weight. If your coconut feels damp, spread it on a tray and air fry at 120°C for 2 minutes to dry it out. This ensures your 0.7-ounce portions remain consistent for future recipes.</p>
