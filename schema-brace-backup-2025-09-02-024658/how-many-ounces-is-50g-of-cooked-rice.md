<h3>How to Measure 50g of Cooked Rice</h3>  
<p>To measure 50g of cooked rice, use a digital kitchen scale for accuracy. If you don't have one, a rough estimate is about 3-4 tablespoons, but this can vary depending on rice type. Fluffy long-grain rice will weigh less per tablespoon than sticky short-grain rice. For consistent results in your air fryer, weighing is always best. <p>50g of cooked rice (1.76 oz) is perfect for single-serving air fryer dishes like fried rice or stuffed peppers. This portion size works well when combined with proteins or vegetables for a balanced meal. It's also ideal for testing new recipes without wasting ingredients. Remember, air fryers work best with smaller, evenly spaced portions. If you're cooking for more people, simply scale up the measurements accordingly.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Cooking Rice in an Air Fryer</h3>  
<p>While most people cook rice separately, you can use your air fryer to reheat or crisp up cooked rice. Spread 50g (1.76 oz) of cooked rice evenly in the basket and heat at 180°C for 3-5 minutes. This works great for giving leftover rice new life in fried rice dishes.
