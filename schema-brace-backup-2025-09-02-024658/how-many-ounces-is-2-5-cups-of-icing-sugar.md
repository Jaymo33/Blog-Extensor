<h2>How to Measure Icing Sugar Correctly</h2>  
<p>When measuring 2.5 cups of icing sugar, it's important to use the correct technique to avoid packing too much or too little into your measuring cup. Fluff the icing sugar with a fork before lightly spooning it into the cup, then level it off with a straight edge. This method ensures you're not accidentally adding extra weight, which could throw off your recipe.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Icing Sugar</h2>  
<p>One of the most frequent errors bakers make is scooping icing sugar directly from the bag with a measuring cup, which compresses the sugar and leads to an inaccurate measurement. Another mistake is failing to sift the sugar before measuring, which can result in lumps and uneven distribution in your batter or frosting.</p>  

<p>For air fryer baking, these mistakes can be particularly problematic because the appliance's intense heat can amplify texture issues. <p>When using icing sugar in air fryer recipes, it's important to consider how the sugar will behave under the appliance's high heat.
