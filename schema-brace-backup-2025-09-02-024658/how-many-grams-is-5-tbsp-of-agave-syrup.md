<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on precise cooking times and temperatures to deliver perfect results. Using the correct amount of ingredients, like agave syrup, ensures your dishes turn out just right. Too much or too little can affect texture, browning, and overall flavour. That’s why converting tablespoons to grams is so useful for air fryer recipes.</p>

<h2>How to measure agave syrup accurately</h2>
<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like agave syrup can be tricky. For the most precise results, use a digital kitchen scale. Simply place your measuring spoon on the scale, tare it to zero, then add the syrup until you reach 100 grams. This method eliminates guesswork and ensures consistency.</p>
<p>If you don’t have a scale, levelling off each tablespoon with a knife helps. Remember, 5 tbsp of agave syrup to grams is roughly 100g, but slight variations can occur depending on how densely you pack the syrup. <h3>Common uses for agave syrup in air fryer recipes</h3>
<p>Agave syrup is a versatile sweetener in air fryer cooking. It works beautifully in glazes for meats like chicken or salmon, adding a subtle sweetness and glossy finish. It’s also great in desserts, such as air-fried cookies or cakes, where precise measurements are key.</p>

<h2>Converting other sweeteners to grams</h2>
<p>If you’re out of agave syrup, you might wonder how other sweeteners compare. Honey, for example, is slightly denser—5 tbsp equals about 105 grams. Maple syrup is closer to agave, at roughly 100 grams for 5 tablespoons. Always adjust recipes accordingly to maintain balance.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with agave syrup</h3>
<p>Agave syrup can be sticky, so lightly coating your measuring spoon with oil helps it slide off easily. It also caramelises quickly in the air fryer, so keep an eye on cooking times to prevent burning. For best results, use it in moderation and pair it with complementary flavours like citrus or spices.</p>
