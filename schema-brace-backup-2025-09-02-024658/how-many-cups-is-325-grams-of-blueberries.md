<h2>Why Accurate Blueberry Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of blueberries can make or break your air fryer recipe. Too few, and your dish lacks flavour; too many, and it may become soggy. For 325 grams of blueberries, 2.5 cups is the sweet spot. This measurement works for both fresh and frozen blueberries, though frozen berries may settle differently. <h3>Fresh vs. Frozen Blueberries</h3>  
<p>Fresh blueberries and frozen blueberries measure slightly differently due to density. While 325 grams equals roughly 2.5 cups of fresh berries, frozen ones may compact more. If using frozen, fluff them lightly before measuring for accuracy. This ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>Converting Grams to Cups for Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking requires precision, and converting 325 grams of blueberries to cups is no exception. A kitchen scale is the most accurate tool, but if you don’t have one, 2.5 cups is a reliable estimate. For more baking conversions, visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a> for expert tips.</p>  

<p>When measuring blueberries, avoid packing them down to prevent excess moisture in your dish. Gently fill your measuring cup to the brim for the best results. Whether you're making pancakes or a blueberry crisp, this method ensures consistency. Need a quick clean-up tip? Check our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>keeping your air fryer spotless</a>.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Blueberries</h3>  
<p>One mistake is using liquid measuring cups instead of dry ones, which can lead to inaccuracies. Another is not accounting for berry size—larger blueberries take up more space. For 325 grams, always use a dry measuring cup and level it off for precision.</p>  

<h2>Using Blueberries in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Blueberries add a burst of flavour to air fryer recipes, from breakfasts to desserts. Whether you're making a compote or folding them into batter, 325 grams (2.5 cups) is a great starting point. For more ideas, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast recipes</a> featuring this superfood.</p>  

<p>Air fryers excel at even cooking, making them perfect for blueberry-based dishes. Just remember to preheat your air fryer for best results. With 325 grams of blueberries, you can whip up everything from fruity granola to a quick dessert in minutes.</p>  

<h3>Storing Leftover Blueberries</h3>  
<p>If you have leftover blueberries, store them in an airtight container in the fridge. They’ll stay fresh for up to a week, ready for your next air fryer creation. For longer storage, freeze them in a single layer before transferring to a bag.</p>
