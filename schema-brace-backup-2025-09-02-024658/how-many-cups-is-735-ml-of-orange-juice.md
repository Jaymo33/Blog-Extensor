<h2>Understanding the Conversion from ml to Cups</h2>
<p>Converting millilitres to cups can be tricky, especially when dealing with liquids like orange juice. One standard metric cup holds 250 ml, so dividing 735 ml by 250 gives you roughly 2.94 cups. However, in practical terms, most home cooks round this up to 3.1 cups for simplicity. This slight adjustment ensures accuracy without overcomplicating your measurements.</p>
<p>If you're using an air fryer for recipes like orange-glazed chicken or citrus-infused desserts, knowing this conversion helps maintain consistency. <h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of orange juice can make or break a recipe. Too little, and your dish might lack flavour; too much, and it could become soggy or overly acidic. This is especially important in air frying, where precise ingredient ratios affect texture and cooking times. For example, a marinade with 735 ml of orange juice (3.1 cups) will evenly coat your protein without drowning it.</p>
<p>If you're exploring <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, you'll notice many rely on balanced liquid measurements. Whether you're making a tangy glaze or a moist cake, getting the 735 ml to cups conversion right ensures professional results every time.</p>

<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements</h3>
<p>It's worth noting that US and UK cup measurements differ slightly. A US cup is 240 ml, while a UK metric cup is 250 ml. For 735 ml of orange juice, this means roughly 3.06 US cups or 2.94 UK cups. In practice, the difference is minimal, but if precision is key, always check which standard your recipe follows.</p>
