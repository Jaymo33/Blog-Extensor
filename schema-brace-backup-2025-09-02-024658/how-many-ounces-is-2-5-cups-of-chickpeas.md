<h2>Why Accurate Chickpea Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of chickpeas ensures even cooking and optimal texture in your air fryer. Too many chickpeas can overcrowd the basket, leading to uneven crisping, while too few might dry out. For 2.5 cups of chickpeas (15 ounces), you’ll achieve the perfect balance for roasting or frying. This measurement is especially useful for batch cooking or meal prep, where consistency is key. <h3>How to Measure Chickpeas Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate conversion of 2.5 cups of chickpeas to ounces, use a kitchen scale. Scoop the chickpeas into a measuring cup without packing them down, then level the top. If you don’t have a scale, remember that 1 cup of chickpeas is roughly 6 ounces, so 2.5 cups equal 15 ounces. This method works for both canned and cooked-from-dry chickpeas.</p>  

<h2>Using 2.5 Cups of Chickpeas in Your Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Roasting 15 ounces (2.5 cups) of chickpeas in the air fryer creates a delicious, protein-packed snack. Toss them with olive oil, salt, and your favourite spices before air frying at 200°C for 12–15 minutes. Shake the basket halfway for even crisping. This quantity fits perfectly in most standard air fryer baskets without overcrowding. For more recipe ideas, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>.</p>  

<p>Chickpeas also work wonderfully in air-fried falafel or as a crunchy topping for salads. Since 2.5 cups equal 15 ounces, you can easily adjust seasonings or oil quantities for larger batches. If you’re meal prepping, cooked chickpeas store well in the fridge for up to 5 days. Just reheat them in the air fryer for a few minutes to restore their crispiness.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Chickpeas</h3>  
<p>Avoid packing chickpeas tightly into the measuring cup, as this can skew the weight. For 2.5 cups to equal 15 ounces, they should be loosely filled. Also, drain canned chickpeas thoroughly before measuring to prevent excess moisture. If using dried chickpeas, cook them until tender before measuring for recipes. Proper storage is key too—keep cooked chickpeas in an airtight container to maintain freshness.</p>  

<h2>Converting Chickpeas for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>Whether you’re doubling a recipe or scaling it down, knowing that 2.5 cups of chickpeas equal 15 ounces simplifies the process. For smaller batches, halve the amount (1.25 cups = 7.5 ounces), or double it for larger gatherings (5 cups = 30 ounces). This conversion is handy for everything from soups to air-fried snacks.
