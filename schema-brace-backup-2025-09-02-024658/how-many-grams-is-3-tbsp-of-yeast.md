<p>3 tablespoons of yeast equals approximately 28 grams. This conversion is essential for air fryer baking, where precise measurements can make or break your recipe. Whether you're making dough for bread, pizza, or even sweet treats, knowing the exact weight ensures consistent results. <h2>Why accurate yeast measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Yeast is a crucial ingredient in baking, and even small deviations can affect texture and rise. Too little yeast, and your dough may not rise properly; too much, and it could overproof. For air fryer recipes, where cooking times are shorter, precision is even more critical. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> helps simplify these measurements, so you never have to guess.</p>  

<p>Many bakers prefer weighing ingredients like yeast instead of relying on volume measurements. This is because scooping methods can vary, leading to inconsistencies. A digital kitchen scale ensures you get exactly 28 grams for 3 tbsp of yeast, eliminating guesswork. If you're new to air fryer baking, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes</a> section for foolproof ideas.</p>  

<h3>How to measure yeast without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method for accuracy. Lightly spoon the yeast into the tablespoon without packing it down, then level it off with a straight edge. This method helps approximate the 28-gram weight for 3 tbsp of yeast. For more tips on air fryer baking, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/bread>bread recipes</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common yeast types and their weights</h2>  
<p>Active dry yeast and instant yeast weigh slightly differently, but for most recipes, the 28-gram rule applies. Active dry yeast may require activation in warm water, while instant yeast can be mixed directly into dry ingredients. Both work well in air fryer recipes, but always check your specific yeast packaging for exact weights.</p>  

<p>Fresh yeast, often sold in blocks, has a different conversion. If substituting fresh yeast for dry, use about twice the amount by weight. For example, 3 tbsp of fresh yeast would be roughly 56 grams. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a> guide also helps keep your appliance in top shape for baking.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting yeast for high-altitude baking</h3>  
<p>At higher altitudes, you may need slightly less yeast due to faster rising times. Reduce the amount by about 10-15% to prevent overproofing. For 3 tbsp of yeast, this means using around 25 grams instead of 28. Always monitor your dough closely when using an air fryer, as cooking times can vary.</p>
