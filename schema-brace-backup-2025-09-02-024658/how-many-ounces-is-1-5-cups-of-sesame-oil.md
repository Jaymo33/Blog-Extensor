<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and too much or too little oil can affect texture and crispiness. Sesame oil, with its rich flavour, is a favourite for Asian-inspired dishes like stir-fries or glazes. Using 1.5 cups (12 ounces) ensures your recipe isn’t overly greasy or dry. For more conversion tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes call for sesame oil, especially for marinades or dressings. If you’re preparing a batch of crispy tofu or chicken wings, 12 ounces of sesame oil will coat ingredients evenly. Too little oil, and your food may stick; too much, and it could become soggy. Precision is key, and our guide takes the guesswork out of measuring.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Sesame Oil Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a liquid measuring cup for 1.5 cups of sesame oil. If you’re converting to ounces, a kitchen scale is ideal. Pour the oil slowly to avoid spills, and level it off for consistency. This method ensures you’re using exactly 12 ounces, whether you’re cooking for one or meal prepping for the week.</p>
