<h2>Why Convert Fan Oven to Conventional Oven Temperatures?</h2>  
<p>Fan ovens cook faster and more evenly than conventional ovens because they circulate hot air. This means recipes designed for conventional ovens may need adjustments when using a fan setting. For example, 285°F in a fan oven translates to 325°F in a conventional oven to achieve similar results. This is especially useful when adapting recipes for air fryers, which also rely on rapid air circulation.</p>  
<p>Understanding these conversions helps prevent undercooking or overcooking your dishes. Whether you're baking a cake or roasting vegetables, accurate temperature settings are key. <h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>  
<p>When converting 285°F fan oven to conventional oven, cooking times may also need tweaking. Fan ovens typically reduce cooking time by 10-15%, so keep an eye on your dish to avoid overdoing it. For air fryers, which work similarly to fan ovens, you might even shorten the time further. Always check for doneness a few minutes early.</p>
