<p>158°C in a fan oven equals approximately 175°C in a conventional oven. This conversion ensures your recipes cook evenly when switching between oven types. Understanding these differences is crucial for perfect results, especially when using an air fryer which often mimics fan oven behaviour.</p>

<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air constantly, cooking food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens. This means you typically need to reduce temperatures by 20°C when converting from conventional to fan settings. The reverse applies when converting 158 fan oven to conventional oven - you'll need to increase the temperature.</p>
<p>This principle applies to all cooking methods, whether you're baking cakes or roasting meats. <h3>Adjusting Cooking Times</h3>
<p>When converting 158 fan oven to conventional oven settings, remember cooking times may need adjusting too. Conventional ovens generally require 10-15% longer cooking times than fan ovens at equivalent temperatures. Always check food 5-10 minutes before the recipe suggests.</p>
