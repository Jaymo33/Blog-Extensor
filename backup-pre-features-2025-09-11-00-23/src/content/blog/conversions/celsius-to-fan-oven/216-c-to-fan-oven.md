<p>216°C in a conventional oven converts to approximately 190°C in a fan oven. This 20-25°C reduction is standard when switching between conventional and fan-assisted cooking. If you're using an air fryer, which works similarly to a fan oven, you'll typically use the same 190°C setting for most recipes.</p>

<h2>Understanding Fan Oven Conversions</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food using a built-in fan, cooking food more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. This means you'll need to adjust temperatures downward when converting recipes. The general rule is to reduce the temperature by 20°C when using a fan oven, so 216°C becomes 190°C. This conversion works perfectly for everything from roasting vegetables to baking cakes.</p>

<h3>Why Temperature Accuracy Matters</h3>
<p>Getting your 216°C to fan oven conversion right ensures your food cooks properly without burning or drying out. At 190°C in a fan oven or air fryer, proteins will brown nicely while staying juicy inside, and baked goods will rise evenly. This temperature is particularly good for roasting vegetables like potatoes or making homemade crisps.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times</h3>
<p>While the 216°C to 190°C conversion gives you the right temperature, remember that fan ovens and air fryers cook faster. Reduce cooking times by about 20% initially, then check for doneness. For example, if a recipe says 30 minutes at 216°C conventional, start checking at 24 minutes when using 190°C in a fan oven or air fryer.</p>

<h2>Tips for Perfect Results Every Time</h2>
<p>To get the best results when converting 216°C to fan oven settings, always preheat your appliance. This ensures even cooking from the start. Use an oven thermometer to verify your fan oven or air fryer's accuracy, as some models may run slightly hot or cold. Regular <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning</a> also helps maintain consistent temperatures.</p>
<p>For foods that need particularly even cooking, like whole chickens or large roasts, consider rotating the food halfway through cooking when using fan settings. In an air fryer, shaking the basket or turning food ensures all sides get equal exposure to the circulating hot air.</p>
