<h3>The science behind flour measurements</h3>
<p>Wholemeal flour weighs about 4.5 ounces per cup, compared to 4.25 ounces for all-purpose flour. This difference occurs because wholemeal flour contains the entire wheat kernel, making it denser. When converting 1.66 cups wholemeal flour to ounces, this density factor is crucial for recipe success.</p>

<p>Because wholemeal flour contains the wheat germ, it can go rancid faster than white flour. Store your flour in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. For best results in air fryer recipes using 1.66 cups (7.5 ounces) measurements, use fresh wholemeal flour within 3 months of opening.</p>
