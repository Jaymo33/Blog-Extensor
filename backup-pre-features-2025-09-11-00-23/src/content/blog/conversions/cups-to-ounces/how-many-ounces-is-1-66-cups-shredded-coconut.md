Whether you’re baking coconut macaroons or adding texture to a dish, knowing the weight ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>How to Measure Shredded Coconut Correctly</h2>  
<p>For the most accurate conversion, lightly pack the shredded coconut into your measuring cup. Don’t press it down too firmly, as this can compact the flakes and skew the weight. If you’re using a kitchen scale, tare it with the measuring cup first, then add the coconut until you reach 5.3 ounces (or 150 grams).</p>

<h2>Converting Other Coconut Measurements</h2>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of shredded coconut, here’s a quick reference: 1 cup equals about 3.2 ounces (90 grams), while 2 cups weigh roughly 6.4 ounces (180 grams). These conversions are handy for adjusting portion sizes or substituting ingredients. For example, if you’re making a half-batch of coconut cookies, you’ll need 2.65 ounces (75 grams) for 0.83 cups.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Shredded Coconut</h3>  
<p>To keep shredded coconut fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. If you’ve opened a large bag, consider transferring it to a jar to prevent staleness. Proper storage ensures your coconut stays fluffy and ready for your next air fryer creation.</p>
