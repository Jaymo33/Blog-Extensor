<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so using the correct amount of ingredients is crucial. Too much desiccated coconut can lead to uneven cooking or a dry texture, while too little might not deliver the desired flavour. This is especially important for recipes like coconut shrimp or crispy coconut chicken, where the coating needs to be just right. <p>Converting cups to ounces can be tricky because the weight of desiccated coconut varies slightly depending on how it’s packed. Lightly packed coconut will weigh less than firmly packed, so it’s always best to use a kitchen scale for accuracy. If you don’t have one, our conversion of 1.25 cups to 3.5 ounces is a reliable starting point for most recipes.</p>

<h2>How to measure desiccated coconut for air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>For the most accurate results, use a digital kitchen scale to weigh your desiccated coconut. If you’re measuring by volume, spoon the coconut into the measuring cup and level it off with a knife—don’t pack it down. This method ensures consistency and helps avoid over-measuring, which can affect the texture of your air fryer dishes.</p>

<h3>Common uses for desiccated coconut in air fryer cooking</h3>
<p>Desiccated coconut is often used as a coating for proteins like chicken or shrimp, providing a crispy, golden finish. It’s also a key ingredient in many baked goods, such as coconut macaroons or air-fried cakes. Because of its light texture, it crisps up beautifully in the air fryer, making it a favourite among home cooks.</p>

<h2>Tips for storing and handling desiccated coconut</h2>
<p>To keep your desiccated coconut fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. It can also be frozen for longer shelf life.
