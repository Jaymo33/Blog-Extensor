<h3>How to Measure Gelatin Powder Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 1.5 cups of gelatin powder accurately, use a dry measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Scooping directly from the bag can compress the powder, leading to an incorrect weight. For best results, weigh 12 ounces (340 grams) on a kitchen scale—this eliminates guesswork.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Gelatin</h3>  
<p>Avoid packing gelatin powder into the measuring cup, as this can add extra weight. Instead, lightly spoon it in and level it off. Also, ensure your measuring cups are dry—moisture can cause clumping and throw off the 1.5 cups to ounces conversion.</p>  

<h2>Gelatin Powder Alternatives in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>If you’re out of gelatin powder, agar-agar is a plant-based substitute. Use 1 teaspoon of agar-agar per 1.5 cups of liquid (instead of gelatin powder’s 12 ounces). Note that agar-agar sets firmer, so adjust quantities carefully for air fryer recipes.</p>
