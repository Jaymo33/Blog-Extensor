<p>1.5 cups of chopped walnuts equals approximately 6 ounces (170 grams). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, where precise measurements ensure even cooking and perfect texture. Whether you're making walnut-crusted chicken or a batch of air-fried cookies, knowing the exact weight saves time and guarantees consistency.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on hot air circulation to cook food evenly, and incorrect measurements can throw off cooking times. For example, using too many walnuts in a coating might prevent proper crisping. On the other hand, too few could leave your dish lacking flavour. That’s why converting cups to ounces is so helpful—especially for ingredients like chopped walnuts, which can vary in density.</p>

If you’re working with a recipe that lists walnuts in cups but your kitchen scale uses ounces, this conversion ensures you stay on track. Plus, many air fryer models have specific capacity limits, so knowing weights helps avoid overloading.</p>  

<h3>How to measure chopped walnuts correctly</h3>  
<p>Chopped walnuts can settle or pack differently depending on how they’re stored. For the most accurate measurement, lightly spoon the walnuts into a measuring cup without pressing down. Then, level off the top with a straight edge. If you’re using a scale, 1.5 cups should weigh roughly 6 ounces (170 grams).</p>

<h3>Converting other nut measurements</h3>  
<p>While this guide focuses on walnuts, other nuts like almonds or pecans have different weights per cup due to their density. Always check a reliable <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> if substituting. For example, 1.5 cups of whole almonds weigh closer to 7 ounces, so adjustments may be needed.</p>  

<h2>Tips for storing chopped walnuts</h2>  
<p>To keep walnuts fresh, store them in an airtight container in a cool, dark place or the fridge. They can go rancid quickly due to their high oil content. If you’ve pre-measured 1.5 cups (6 ounces), label the container to avoid confusion later. For longer storage, freeze them in portions—they’ll stay fresh for months.</p>
