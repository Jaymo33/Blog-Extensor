<h2>Why converting 150g breadcrumbs to ounces matters</h2>  
<p>Air fryers thrive on consistency, and breadcrumb measurements can make or break a dish. Too little, and your coating won’t crisp up; too much, and it might burn. Converting 150g of breadcrumbs to ounces (5.29 oz) helps you follow recipes accurately, whether you’re making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air-fryer chicken</a> or crispy tofu. Most UK recipes list weights in grams, but if your kitchen scales default to ounces, this conversion is a lifesaver.</p>  

<p>Breadcrumbs vary in density, so weight is more reliable than volume. Panko, for example, is lighter than standard breadcrumbs, but 150g of either still equals 5.29 oz. <h3>How to measure 150g breadcrumbs without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, 150g of breadcrumbs is roughly 1½ lightly packed UK cups. Fluff the breadcrumbs with a fork before measuring to avoid compacting them. For panko, use 1¾ cups to hit 150g (5.29 oz). This method works well for coatings on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/fish>air-fryer fish</a> or veggies, but scales are best for precision.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 150g breadcrumbs in air fryers</h2>  
<p>From schnitzel to mozzarella sticks, 150g of breadcrumbs (5.29 oz) covers about 4–6 servings. <h3>Pro tips for breading with 150g breadcrumbs</h3>  
<p>For extra crunch, mix 150g breadcrumbs (5.29 oz) with 1 tbsp grated parmesan or paprika. Dip food in beaten egg first to help the coating stick.
