<h3>Common Pasta Types and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Not all pasta weighs the same per cup, so here's a quick guide. Spaghetti, when broken into 2-inch pieces, measures about 200 grams for 2.0 cups. Smaller shapes like orzo or ditalini may weigh slightly less, around 180-190 grams for the same volume. Larger tubes like rigatoni or pappardelle can reach 210-220 grams per 2.0 cups. Always check your specific pasta type if precision is key for your air fryer dish.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 2.0 Cups of Pasta Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, you can still estimate 2.0 cups of pasta in grams. Fill a measuring cup loosely with dry pasta, levelling it off without packing it down. Remember that 2.0 cups of small pasta shapes will appear denser than larger ones. For air fryer cooking, slight variations won't ruin your meal, but for baking or meal prep, weighing is best. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep recipes</a> for perfectly portioned ideas.</p>  

<p>Another handy trick is to use common household items for estimation. Two handfuls of long pasta like spaghetti roughly equal one cup, so four handfuls would approximate 2.0 cups (about 200 grams). For tube pasta, a standard coffee mug holds roughly one cup when filled to the brim. These methods aren't as precise as scales but work in a pinch for casual air fryer meals.</p>  

<h3>Converting Cooked Pasta Back to Dry Measurements</h3>
