<p>75 grams of brown sugar is roughly equivalent to 0.38 cups (or just over 1/3 cup) when lightly packed. This measurement is crucial for baking, especially when using an air fryer, where precise ingredient ratios can make or break your recipe. Whether you're whipping up cookies, cakes, or glazes, knowing how to convert 75 grams of brown sugar to cups ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Baking</h2>
<p>Baking in an air fryer requires precision because the compact cooking environment amplifies the effects of ingredient imbalances. Too much sugar can cause over-browning or even burning, while too little can leave your baked goods bland. Converting 75 grams of brown sugar to cups correctly helps maintain the perfect texture and sweetness. For more air fryer baking tips, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a>.</p>

<p>Brown sugar, whether light or dark, has a unique moisture content that affects its density. This means 75 grams of brown sugar won’t fill the same volume as granulated sugar. Lightly packing the sugar into your measuring cup ensures accuracy. If you’re unsure, a kitchen scale is your best friend for nailing the 75-gram mark.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 75 Grams of Brown Sugar Without a Scale</h2>
<p>Don’t have a scale? No problem. To measure 75 grams of brown sugar in cups, use the spoon-and-level method. Gently spoon the sugar into a measuring cup until it’s slightly heaped, then level it off with a straight edge. This method typically yields around 0.38 cups for 75 grams. For sticky brown sugar, lightly grease the cup first to help it slide out easily.</p>

<p>If your recipe calls for tightly packed brown sugar, the volume will be slightly less—closer to 0.33 cups for 75 grams. Always check your recipe’s instructions, as packing density can vary.
