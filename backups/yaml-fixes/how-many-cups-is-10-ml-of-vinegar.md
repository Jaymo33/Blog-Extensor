<h2>Converting 10 ml of Vinegar to Cups</h2>  
<p>Since cup sizes differ between the UK and US, it's important to use the right conversion. In the US, 1 cup equals 240 ml, so 10 ml is roughly 0.042 cups. In the UK, where 1 cup is 284 ml, 10 ml converts to about 0.035 cups. For small amounts like this, a measuring spoon is more practical than a cup, but knowing the equivalent helps when scaling recipes.</p>  

<h3>Tools for Measuring Small Quantities</h3>  
<p>For 10 ml of vinegar, a metric measuring spoon is ideal. If you only have imperial spoons, 10 ml is just under 2 teaspoons (1 tsp = 5 ml). A syringe or small jug with millilitre markings also works well. When preparing air fryer marinades, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, precise vinegar measurements ensure balanced flavours without excess acidity.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Recipes with 10 ml Vinegar</h3>
