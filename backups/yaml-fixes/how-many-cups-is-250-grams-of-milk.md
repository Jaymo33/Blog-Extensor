<p>250 grams of milk is roughly equivalent to 1 cup in US measurements or just under 1 cup (0.98 cups) in UK measurements. Getting this conversion right is essential for air fryer recipes, where precise liquid ratios can affect texture and cooking times. Whether you're whipping up a cake, custard, or a creamy sauce, knowing how many cups 250 grams of milk translates to ensures consistency in your dishes.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Milk Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and liquids like milk can influence how ingredients cook. Too much milk might make batters runny, while too little can dry out baked goods. For example, if you're making air fryer pancakes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>, the right milk measurement ensures fluffy results. Similarly, creamy dishes like mac and cheese or custards need precise milk quantities for the perfect consistency.</p>  

<p>Different recipes may call for millilitres, grams, or cups, so understanding conversions avoids mishaps. A <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>handy conversion chart</a> can simplify this, but for milk, 250 grams typically fills a standard US cup. In the UK, where cups are slightly larger, it’s just shy of a full cup. Always check your recipe’s origin to confirm which measurement system it uses.</p>  

<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements for Milk</h3>  
<p>In the US, 1 cup equals 240 millilitres, so 250 grams of milk (which is roughly 250 ml for milk) fits neatly into 1 cup. However, UK cups hold 250 millilitres, meaning 250 grams of milk is 0.98 cups. This small difference might not matter for soups or sauces, but for baking—like air fryer cakes or muffins—it’s worth measuring carefully. Use a kitchen scale for grams or a liquid measuring cup for millilitres to avoid guesswork.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 250 Grams of Milk Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>No scale? No problem. If your recipe calls for 250 grams of milk, you can use a liquid measuring cup. Pour milk to the 1-cup line (US) or just below the 1-cup mark (UK). For thicker recipes, like air fryer bread pudding from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>our dessert category</a>, eyeballing isn’t ideal—opt for precise tools. Alternatively, tablespoons can help: 250 grams equals about 16.5 tablespoons, though this method is less practical for larger quantities.</p>
