<h2>Why 424 F to Gas Mark 7 Matters</h2>
<p>Gas Mark 7 (424°F) is a versatile temperature for cooking. It’s hot enough to crisp up chicken wings in an air fryer yet gentle enough to bake a Victoria sponge without burning. Many traditional British recipes, like Sunday roasts or shepherd’s pie, rely on this setting. If you’re adapting oven recipes for an air fryer, knowing this conversion is crucial for timing and texture.</p>

<h3>How to Convert 424 F to Gas Mark for Air Fryers</h3>
<p>Most air fryers display temperatures in Fahrenheit or Celsius, but some models allow you to switch between units. If yours doesn’t support Gas Mark, simply set it to 424°F. For recipes specifying Gas Mark 7, this is the exact equivalent. Preheating your air fryer for 3–5 minutes ensures even cooking at this temperature.</p>
