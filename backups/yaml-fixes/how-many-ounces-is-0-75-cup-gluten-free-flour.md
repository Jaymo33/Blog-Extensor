<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer baking</h2>  
<p>Gluten-free flour behaves differently than regular flour, making accurate conversions crucial. Too much or too little can affect texture, rise, and even cooking time in your air fryer. Unlike traditional ovens, air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, so ingredient ratios must be spot-on. This is especially true for delicate bakes like muffins or bread. <p>Measuring by weight (ounces or grams) is far more reliable than volume (cups) for gluten-free flours. Brands vary in density, so 0.75 cup of one brand might weigh differently than another. <h3>How to measure 0.75 cup of gluten-free flour correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 0.75 cup measurement, spoon the flour into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Scooping directly packs the flour, leading to excess weight. If using a scale, tare it with your bowl, then add flour until you hit 3.2 ounces (90 grams). This method works for all gluten-free flour blends, whether store-bought or homemade.</p>
