<h2>Understanding Air Fryer Baking Paper Safety</h2>
<p>Baking paper, also called parchment paper, can withstand typical air fryer temperatures up to 220°C. However, you must avoid letting it touch heating elements or blocking the air fryer's fan. Always trim baking paper to fit your basket with at least 2cm clearance on all sides. This prevents the paper from flying up during cooking and maintains proper air circulation. Some models like the <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>Ninja Air Fryer</a> have specific guidelines about baking paper use in their manuals.</p>
<p>For optimal safety, look for baking paper labelled as heat-resistant up to 230°C. Standard supermarket baking paper usually meets this requirement. Avoid wax paper at all costs - it melts at air fryer temperatures. If you're unsure about your baking paper's heat tolerance, test a small piece at 180°C for a few minutes first. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner</a>, we recommend baking paper for messy foods but always supervise your air fryer when using it.</p>

<h3>When to Use Baking Paper in Your Air Fryer</h3>
<p>Baking paper works best for foods that might stick or drip, like marinated meats or cheesy dishes. It's perfect for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cookies>air fryer cookies</a> or reheating pizza without a soggy base. The paper creates a non-stick surface while absorbing excess grease. Just remember to weigh it down with food - never use empty baking paper in an air fryer as it could blow around and block airflow.</p>

<h2>Potential Risks of Using Baking Paper</h2>
<p>While baking paper is generally safe, improper use can cause problems. The main risks include blocking airflow (leading to uneven cooking), paper touching heating elements (potential fire hazard), or using low-quality paper that might scorch. Always check your air fryer's manual first - some manufacturers explicitly advise against baking paper. If your model has a very powerful fan, consider perforated baking paper designed specifically for air fryers.</p>
<p>Another consideration is the baking paper's size relative to your food. For small items like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/frozen-foods>frozen snacks</a>, you might not need paper at all. The air fryer's non-stick basket often works fine. Save baking paper for particularly messy or sticky foods where cleanup would otherwise be difficult. <h3>Alternatives to Baking Paper</h3>
<p>If you're concerned about using baking paper, consider silicone mats made for air fryers. These reusable options provide similar non-stick benefits without the fire risk. Some air fryer accessories include specially designed perforated baking paper holders that ensure proper airflow. For quick reheating jobs, a light spritz of oil on the basket often prevents sticking without needing any liner at all.</p>
