<h2>Why Convert 349°F to Fan Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air for even cooking, which means they often require lower temperatures than conventional ovens. When converting 349°F to a fan oven setting, you're ensuring your food cooks evenly without drying out. This is especially important for air fryer recipes, where precise temperatures make all the difference. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> originally designed for conventional ovens need this adjustment for best results.</p>

<h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>While 349°F equals 175°C in a fan oven, cooking times may vary slightly. Fan ovens and air fryers cook faster due to their efficient air circulation. As a general rule, reduce cooking times by 10-15% when using a fan oven or air fryer at 175°C. For example, if a recipe states 30 minutes at 349°F, start checking at 25 minutes.</p>

<p>This adjustment is particularly useful for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, where overcooking can lead to dryness. Always use a meat thermometer for proteins to ensure they reach safe internal temperatures. The same principle applies to baked goods - a toothpick test a few minutes early can prevent overbaking.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations for Air Fryers</h3>
<p>Air fryers operate similarly to fan ovens but with more intense air circulation. When converting 349°F for air fryer use, you might find slightly lower temperatures (160-170°C) work better for delicate items. Always preheat your air fryer for 3-5 minutes when using fan oven conversions to ensure even cooking from the start.</p>
