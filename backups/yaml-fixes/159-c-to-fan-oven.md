<h2>Why Convert 159 C to Fan Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air evenly, which means they cook food faster and more efficiently. This is why you often need to lower the temperature when converting from a conventional oven. For air fryers, which also use a fan mechanism, this conversion is equally important. If you're used to cooking at 159 C in a regular oven, you'll need to adjust to 140 C in a fan oven or air fryer. This ensures your food doesn't overcook or burn. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more details.</p>

<h3>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h3>
<p>When converting 159 C to a fan oven setting, you'll also need to adjust the cooking time. Generally, fan ovens cook food about 20% faster than conventional ovens. This means if a recipe calls for 30 minutes at 159 C in a conventional oven, you'd cook it for around 24 minutes at 140 C in a fan oven. However, air fryers can be even faster, so always check your food a few minutes early. Use a food thermometer to ensure everything is cooked through. <h3>Tips for Perfect Results</h3>
<p>Always preheat your air fryer or fan oven to the desired temperature before adding food. This ensures even cooking from the start. For baking, use a light-coloured tray to prevent over-browning. Finally, let your food rest for a few minutes after cooking to allow the heat to distribute evenly.</p>
