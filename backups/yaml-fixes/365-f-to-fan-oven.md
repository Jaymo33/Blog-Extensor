<h2>Why Convert 365°F to Fan Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air for faster, more even cooking, so temperatures often need adjusting compared to conventional ovens or air fryers. Converting 365°F (185°C in a conventional oven) to 180°C in a fan oven accounts for this efficiency. Many recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a> use this temperature for dishes like roasted vegetables or fish fillets. It’s also a safe starting point for reheating frozen foods without overcooking.</p>
<p>Air fryers, which work similarly to fan ovens, often list temperatures in Fahrenheit, especially in US-based recipes. Knowing that 365°F equals 180°C in a fan oven lets you replicate results accurately. <h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>At 365°F (180°C fan), most foods cook 20% faster than in a conventional oven. For air fryers, reduce the time further by 5–10 minutes due to their compact size. For instance, chicken wings might take 25 minutes in a fan oven but just 18 in an air fryer. Always check food early and use a meat thermometer for precision.</p>
<p>Recipes like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken category</a> often specify both time and temperature for fan ovens and air fryers. If a recipe only lists 365°F, assume it’s for a conventional oven and reduce the time by 15–20% for fan settings. Stirring or flipping food halfway through ensures even browning.</p>

<h3>Common Dishes at 365°F Fan</h3>

<h2>Air Fryer vs. Fan Oven at 365°F</h2>
<p>Air fryers achieve crispier results at 365°F (180°C fan) due to their rapid air circulation, while fan ovens offer more space for batch cooking. For example, fries crisp evenly in an air fryer but may need tossing in a fan oven. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> helps maintain both appliances for consistent performance.</p>
<p>Energy efficiency differs too: air fryers use less power for small portions, whereas fan ovens excel for larger meals. If converting a 365°F air fryer recipe to a fan oven, increase the time slightly and monitor moisture levels—cover dishes with foil if drying out.</p>

<h3>Troubleshooting Temperature Issues</h3>
<p>If food browns too quickly at 365°F fan, lower the temperature by 10°C and extend the time. Conversely, undercooked food may need a higher shelf position or preheating. Calibrate your oven annually with a standalone thermometer for accuracy.</p>
