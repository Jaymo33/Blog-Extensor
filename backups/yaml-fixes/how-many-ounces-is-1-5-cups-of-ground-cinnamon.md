<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient proportions must be spot-on. Too much cinnamon can overpower a dish, while too little might leave it bland. Measuring by weight (ounces) is often more reliable than volume (cups) for dry ingredients like ground cinnamon. This is especially true for recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer cinnamon rolls</a>, where balance is key. Consistency ensures your meals turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<p>Many home cooks underestimate the importance of precise measurements, but it’s a game-changer for air fryer success. Ground cinnamon, in particular, can vary in density depending on how it’s packed. A tightly packed cup will weigh more than a loosely filled one, which is why weighing in ounces is ideal. <h3>How to Measure Ground Cinnamon for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>To measure 1.5 cups of ground cinnamon accurately, use a kitchen scale set to ounces. Scoop the cinnamon into a measuring cup without packing it down, then level it off. Transfer it to the scale to confirm it’s around 6 ounces. If you don’t have a scale, fluff the cinnamon with a spoon before measuring to avoid compaction. This method ensures your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> have the perfect spice balance.</p>
