<h2>Why knowing the grams in 1 tbsp of vegetable oil matters</h2>  
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial for air fryer cooking, where too much oil can lead to soggy results, and too little may leave food dry. <h2>How to measure 1 tbsp of vegetable oil accurately</h2>  
<p>Using a kitchen scale is the most precise way to measure 14 grams of vegetable oil. Simply place your container on the scale, tare it to zero, and pour the oil until it reaches 14g. If you don’t have a scale, a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) will give you roughly the same amount.</p>  
<p>For air fryer recipes, lightly coating food with oil is key. A spray bottle can help distribute oil evenly without overdoing it. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> for dishes that use just the right amount of oil for perfect air frying.</p>  

<h3>Common uses for 1 tbsp of vegetable oil in air fryers</h3>  
<p>One tablespoon of vegetable oil is often enough to coat a batch of homemade chips or roasted vegetables. It helps achieve that desirable crispiness while keeping the dish lighter than deep-fried alternatives. For meats like chicken or salmon, a light brush of oil enhances browning.</p>  

<h3>Does the type of vegetable oil affect the weight?</h3>  
<p>Most vegetable oils, like sunflower or rapeseed, weigh around 14g per tablespoon. However, denser oils like coconut oil may weigh slightly more.
