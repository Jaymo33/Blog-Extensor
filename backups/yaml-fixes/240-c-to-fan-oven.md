<h2>Understanding Fan Oven Temperature Conversion</h2>
<p>The general rule for converting conventional oven temperatures to fan oven settings is to reduce the temperature by 20 degrees Celsius. This means 240 C becomes 220 C in a fan-assisted oven. The science behind this lies in how convection ovens circulate hot air, creating more efficient heat transfer to your food. Air fryers operate on this same principle, which is why many recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe section</a> specify slightly lower temperatures than traditional oven methods.</p>

<p>When baking at 240 C in a conventional oven, the fan equivalent of 220 C will yield similar results in less time. This temperature works particularly well for items like pastries, pies, and roasted meats where you want crisp exteriors and moist interiors. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> provides quick references for all common temperature adjustments between oven types.</p>

<h3>Why 240 C Converts Differently in Fan Ovens</h3>
<p>The 20-degree reduction accounts for the fan's ability to distribute heat more evenly throughout the cooking chamber. Without this adjustment, food would cook too quickly on the outside while remaining undercooked inside. This principle applies equally to fan ovens and air fryers, both of which use forced air circulation to speed up cooking times while maintaining food quality.</p>

<h2>Applying 240 C Fan Oven Settings to Air Fryers</h2>
<p>Modern air fryers essentially function as compact convection ovens, making the 240 C to 220 C conversion directly applicable. When preparing crispy foods like chicken wings or roast potatoes at what would be 240 C in a conventional oven, setting your air fryer to 220 C will achieve similar browning and texture. Many of our popular <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> use this temperature adjustment for perfect results every time.</p>

<p>The smaller cooking chamber of an air fryer means heat reaches food even more efficiently than in a full-size fan oven. This is why monitoring cooking times becomes crucial - items may finish 10-20% faster than the original recipe suggests. Always check for doneness a few minutes early when converting conventional oven recipes to air fryer preparation at the 240 C fan oven equivalent.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations for Baking at 240 C</h3>
<p>When baking cakes or breads that normally require 240 C, the fan oven equivalent of 220 C may need slight recipe modifications. The increased air circulation can cause batters to set faster on top while remaining wet underneath. Reducing the temperature a further 10 degrees (to 210 C) and extending the cooking time often solves this issue. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> include specific adjustments for fan-assisted baking.</p>
