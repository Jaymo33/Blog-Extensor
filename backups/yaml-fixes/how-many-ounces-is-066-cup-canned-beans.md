<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on hot air circulation to cook food evenly, so ingredient quantities can make a big difference. Too many beans packed tightly might not crisp up, while too few could dry out. For recipes like air-fried bean burgers or crispy spiced beans, 0.66 cup (5.28 oz) is a common portion size. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement swaps.</p>  
<p>If you're adapting a stovetop recipe for your air fryer, converting cups to ounces ensures consistency. Canned beans vary in density, so weighing them removes guesswork. For example, 0.66 cup of black beans weighs slightly more than chickpeas due to their smaller size. Always drain and rinse beans before measuring for the most accurate results.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.66 Cup of Canned Beans</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for the most precise 0.66 cup measurement. Fill it to just below the 2/3 cup line, or measure 160 ml if using metric. For weight, a kitchen scale set to ounces will show 5.28 oz. This method works for all types of canned beans, from kidney beans to butterbeans, making it a versatile skill for your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>.</p>
