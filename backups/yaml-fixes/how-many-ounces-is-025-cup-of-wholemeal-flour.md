<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Wholemeal flour behaves differently than plain flour due to its higher density and coarser texture. Measuring by weight (ounces) rather than volume (cups) eliminates inconsistencies caused by how tightly packed the flour is. This is especially crucial in air fryer recipes, where even small deviations can affect cooking times and textures. If you're new to air frying, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> for foolproof ideas.</p>  

<p>Professional bakers always weigh ingredients for precision, and home cooks should too when using an air fryer. A slight overmeasurement can make baked goods dense, while too little flour may cause them to spread or collapse. <h3>How to measure 0.25 cup of wholemeal flour correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.25 cup of wholemeal flour accurately without scales, fluff the flour first with a fork to aerate it. Gently spoon it into your measuring cup without packing or tapping, then level off the top with a straight edge. This method helps approximate the 1.06-ounce weight, though weighing is always more precise. For more baking tips, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cake recipes</a>.</p>

<h2>Tips for baking with wholemeal flour in air fryers</h2>  
<p>Wholemeal flour absorbs more liquid than white flour, so you may need to adjust recipe wet ingredients slightly.
