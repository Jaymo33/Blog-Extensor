<p>0.5 cup of minced garlic equals approximately 2.5 ounces (70 grams). This measurement is essential for air fryer recipes, where precision ensures even cooking and perfect flavour. Whether you're making garlic bread, roasted vegetables, or marinades, knowing this conversion helps avoid overpowering or underwhelming dishes.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Garlic Measurements Matter in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>Air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, intensifying flavours—especially potent ingredients like garlic. Too much minced garlic can dominate a dish, while too little may leave it bland. For consistent results, stick to the 0.5 cup to 2.5-ounce ratio. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient swaps.</p>  
<p>Garlic’s density varies slightly based on mince size, but 0.5 cup typically weighs 2.5 ounces. If you’re using pre-minced jarred garlic, ensure it’s drained well to avoid excess moisture skewing measurements. Freshly minced garlic packs more punch, so adjust recipes accordingly.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 0.5 Cup Minced Garlic Correctly</h2>  
<p>For accuracy, lightly pack minced garlic into a dry measuring cup without crushing it. Level the top with a knife for a precise 0.5 cup. If you lack measuring cups, use a kitchen scale: 2.5 ounces equals roughly 70 grams. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>garlic-infused recipes</a> rely on these measurements for foolproof results.</p>  
<p>Pre-minced garlic saves time but may contain preservatives. For the best flavour, mince fresh cloves and measure immediately. Store leftovers in olive oil for future use—just note that oil adds weight, so adjust measurements if substituting.</p>

<p>This quantity works perfectly for garlic butter (mix with 4 oz melted butter), marinades, or <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>garlic roasted potatoes</a>. <h2>Garlic Measurement FAQs</h2>  
<p>Why does minced garlic weight vary? Freshness and mince fineness affect density. Cloves minced finely yield more per cup than coarse chops. Always measure after mincing, not before, for consistency.</p>  
<p>For garlic-heavy dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>garlic chicken</a>, err on the side of less garlic initially—you can always add more after tasting. Air fryers amplify flavours, so subtlety often works best.</p>
