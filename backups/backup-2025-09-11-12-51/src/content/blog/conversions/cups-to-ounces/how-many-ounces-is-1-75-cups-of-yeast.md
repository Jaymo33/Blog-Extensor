<h2>Why Accurate Yeast Measurement Matters</h2>  
<p>Yeast is a living organism, and using too much or too little can drastically affect your baking results. Too much yeast can cause dough to rise too quickly, leading to a collapsed or overly dense texture. Too little yeast might mean your dough doesn’t rise at all. For air fryer recipes, where cooking times are shorter, precision is even more critical. That’s why converting 1.75 cups of yeast to ounces (14 oz) ensures you get the perfect rise every time.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Yeast for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>Measuring yeast by weight (ounces) is more reliable than using cups, as volume can vary based on how packed the yeast is. For 1.75 cups of yeast, a kitchen scale will give you the most accurate 14-ounce measurement. If you don’t have a scale, lightly spoon the yeast into a measuring cup and level it off—never pack it down. This method helps avoid over-measuring, which could throw off your air fryer baking.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Yeast</h3>  
<p>To keep your yeast fresh and effective, store it in an airtight container in the fridge or freezer. Proper storage ensures your 1.75 cups (14 ounces) of yeast stays active for months. If you’re unsure whether your yeast is still good, dissolve a teaspoon in warm water with a pinch of sugar—if it bubbles within 10 minutes, it’s ready to use. This simple test can save your air fryer baking projects from disaster.</p>
