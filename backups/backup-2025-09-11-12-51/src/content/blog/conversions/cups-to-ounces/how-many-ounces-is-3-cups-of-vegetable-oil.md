<h2>Why accurate oil measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of vegetable oil in your air fryer is crucial for achieving crispy, evenly cooked food. Too much oil can lead to greasy results, while too little may leave your dish dry. Air fryers rely on hot air circulation, so a light coating of oil is often all you need. For example, many recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> recommend just 1-2 tablespoons of oil for perfect fries or chicken wings.</p>

<h3>How to measure vegetable oil accurately</h3>  
<p>When converting 3 cups of vegetable oil to ounces, it’s important to note whether you’re measuring by volume (fluid ounces) or weight (ounces). Fluid ounces are used for liquids, while weight ounces account for density. Vegetable oil weighs about 7.2 ounces per cup, so 3 cups equal roughly 21.6 ounces by weight. For most cooking purposes, fluid ounces (24 fl oz per 3 cups) are the standard reference.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 3 cups of vegetable oil</h2>  
<p>While air fryers use minimal oil, traditional frying methods may require larger quantities. If you’re adapting a deep-fry recipe for your air fryer, you’ll need to drastically reduce the oil—usually to just a light spray or a tablespoon.</p>
