<h3>How to Measure 0.33 Cup of Self-Raising Flour Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can still measure 0.33 cup of self-raising flour accurately. Use a standard UK measuring cup and fill it one-third full, levelling off the top with a knife. For smaller quantities, a tablespoon can help—0.33 cup equals roughly 5 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon of flour. Remember, fluffing the flour before measuring prevents overpacking, which can skew your results.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Self-Raising Flour for Optimal Results</h3>  
<p>To keep your self-raising flour fresh and effective, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Humidity can cause the flour to clump and lose its raising power over time. If you bake infrequently, consider labelling the container with the date of purchase to ensure you use it within its best-by period. Proper storage guarantees your 0.33 cup measurements remain accurate for all your air fryer creations.</p>
