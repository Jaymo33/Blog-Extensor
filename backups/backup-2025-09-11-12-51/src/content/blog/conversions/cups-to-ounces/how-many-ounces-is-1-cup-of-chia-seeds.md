<p>1 cup of chia seeds weighs approximately 4.5 ounces (128 grams). This measurement is essential for air fryer recipes, ensuring accuracy for baking, coatings, or meal prep. Whether you're making chia pudding or adding a crunchy topping, knowing the weight helps achieve consistent results.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Using the correct weight of chia seeds ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly. Too many chia seeds can make coatings clumpy, while too few might leave dishes lacking texture. For example, if you're making air-fried chicken with a chia seed crust, 4.5 ounces per cup ensures even crispiness. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>Chia seeds absorb liquid, so precise measurements prevent overly dense or dry results. This is especially important for air fryer baking, where moisture balance affects texture. Whether you're making energy bars or coating fish, accurate ounces-to-cup conversions guarantee success.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Chia Seeds Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 1 cup chia seeds to ounces conversion, use a kitchen scale. Scooping directly from the bag can compress the seeds, leading to uneven measurements. If you don’t have a scale, fluff the chia seeds with a spoon before lightly filling your measuring cup. Avoid tapping or pressing down, as this increases density.</p>  

<h2>Using Chia Seeds in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Chia seeds add nutrition and crunch to air-fried dishes. Sprinkle them over roasted vegetables or mix into coatings for chicken or tofu. Just remember: 1 cup equals 4.5 ounces for consistent results.</p>

<h3>Converting UK and US Measurements</h3>  
<p>UK and US cup sizes are nearly identical, so 1 cup of chia seeds equals 4.5 ounces in both systems. However, if using a metric scale, 128 grams is the equivalent. Always double-check your air fryer’s manual for specific measurement guidelines, especially for baking.</p>  

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Chia Seeds</h2>  
<p>Overpacking the measuring cup is the biggest error, leading to more chia seeds—and thus more ounces—than intended. This can throw off recipes, especially in air fryer baking where ratios matter. Another mistake is assuming volume equals weight; 1 cup of chia seeds isn’t the same as 1 cup of flour in ounces.</p>  

<p>Storing chia seeds improperly can also affect measurements. Keep them in an airtight container to prevent moisture absorption, which alters density.
