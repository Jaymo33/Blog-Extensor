<h2>How to Measure 1.25 Cups of Wholemeal Flour Correctly</h2>  
<p>The most accurate way to measure wholemeal flour is by using a kitchen scale. Simply set your scale to ounces or grams, tare the weight of your bowl, and spoon the flour in until you reach 5.3 ounces (150 grams). If you don’t have a scale, fluff the flour in its bag first, then lightly spoon it into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to over-measuring.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Flour</h3>  
<p>Many bakers scoop flour directly from the bag with their measuring cup, which compacts it and adds extra weight. This mistake can throw off your recipe, especially in air fryer baking, where even heat distribution relies on balanced ingredients. For foolproof results, always fluff, spoon, and level—or better yet, invest in a digital scale.</p>
