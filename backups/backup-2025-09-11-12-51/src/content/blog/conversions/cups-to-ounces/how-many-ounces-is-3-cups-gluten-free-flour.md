<h2>Why accurate measurements matter</h2>
<p>Gluten-free flour behaves differently than regular flour, so measuring by weight (ounces) is more reliable than volume (cups). A slight variation can affect texture, rise, and even cooking time in your air fryer. <h2>How to measure gluten-free flour correctly</h2>
<p>To convert 3 cups of gluten-free flour to oz accurately, use a kitchen scale. Scooping flour directly from the bag can lead to compacted cups, resulting in more flour than needed. Instead, spoon the flour into the measuring cup and level it off with a knife. This method ensures you're closer to the standard 4 ounces per cup for most gluten-free flours.</p>

<h3>Common brands and their weights</h3>
