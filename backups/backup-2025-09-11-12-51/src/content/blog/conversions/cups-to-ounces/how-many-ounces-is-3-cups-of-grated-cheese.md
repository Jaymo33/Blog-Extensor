<p>Different cheeses have slightly varying densities, but as a general rule, 1 cup of grated cheese weighs about 4 ounces. This means 3 cups of grated cheese will typically weigh around 12 ounces. For best results, lightly pack the cheese when measuring to avoid air gaps.</p>  

<h3>Types of Cheese and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Not all cheeses weigh the same when grated. Hard cheeses like cheddar or parmesan will be denser than softer varieties like mozzarella. If you're using pre-shredded cheese, it may also have anti-caking agents that slightly alter the weight. For air fryer cooking, cheddar is a popular choice due to its melting properties.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Grated Cheese</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a kitchen scale to weigh your grated cheese. If you don't have one, lightly spoon the cheese into a measuring cup and level it off. Avoid packing it down too tightly, as this can lead to using more cheese than intended. Pre-shredded cheese is convenient, but freshly grated often melts better in the air fryer.</p>
