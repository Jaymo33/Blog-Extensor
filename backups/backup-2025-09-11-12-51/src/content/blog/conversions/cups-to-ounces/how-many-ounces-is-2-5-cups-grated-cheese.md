<h2>Why Accurate Cheese Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of grated cheese ensures your air fryer dishes turn out perfectly balanced. Too little cheese can leave your meal dry, while too much may cause uneven cooking or excess oil. For recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer quesadillas</a>, precision is key. Grated cheese melts differently depending on density, so measuring by weight (ounces) is often more reliable than volume (cups).</p>  

<p>Different cheeses also vary in weight—cheddar, mozzarella, and parmesan all have slightly different densities. That’s why knowing that 2.5 cups of grated cheese equals roughly 10 ounces helps standardise your cooking. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Grated Cheese Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, lightly pack the grated cheese into a measuring cup without pressing too hard. Overpacking can add extra weight, skewing your measurements. If you’re making a dish like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken parmesan</a>, weighing the cheese ensures consistency.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Grated Cheese</h3>  
<p>To keep grated cheese fresh, store it in an airtight container in the fridge. Pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents, which can affect melting—consider grating your own for better results. For longer storage, freeze grated cheese in portions, like 2.5 cups (10 ounces), for easy use later.</p>
