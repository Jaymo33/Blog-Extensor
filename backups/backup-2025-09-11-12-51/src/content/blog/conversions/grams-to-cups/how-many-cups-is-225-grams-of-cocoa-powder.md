<p>225 grams of cocoa powder is roughly equivalent to 2.25 cups in standard US measurements or 2.5 cups in UK measurements. This conversion is essential for baking, especially when using an air fryer for desserts like brownies or cakes. Getting the measurement right ensures your recipes turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>Understanding Cocoa Powder Measurements</h2>  
<p>Cocoa powder can be tricky to measure because its density varies depending on how it's packed. Scooping directly from the bag can result in a denser measurement, while sifted cocoa powder is lighter. For accuracy, it's best to weigh your cocoa powder using a kitchen scale. If you don't have one, spoon the cocoa powder into the measuring cup and level it off with a knife.</p>  

<p>When converting 225 grams of cocoa powder to cups, remember that US and UK cup sizes differ slightly. A US cup holds 240ml, while a UK cup is 250ml. This small difference can affect your baking, so always check which measurement system your recipe uses. For air fryer recipes, precision is key to achieving the perfect texture.</p>  

<h3>Why Accuracy Matters in Air Fryer Baking</h3>  
<p>Air fryers cook food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens, so ingredient measurements need to be spot-on. Too much cocoa powder can make your baked goods dry or bitter, while too little can result in a bland flavour. <h2>Converting Grams to Cups for Different Cocoa Powders</h2>  
<p>Not all cocoa powders are created equal. Dutch-processed cocoa powder is darker and slightly denser than natural cocoa powder, which can affect the conversion. For 225 grams of Dutch-processed cocoa, you might need slightly fewer cups compared to natural cocoa. Always refer to the packaging for specific density information.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Cocoa Powder Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, use the spoon-and-level method for the most accurate cup measurement. Gently spoon the cocoa powder into the cup without packing it down, then level it off with a straight edge. Avoid tapping the cup, as this compacts the powder and leads to over-measuring. For best results, consider investing in a digital scale—it's a game-changer for baking.</p>  

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Cocoa Powder</h2>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is scooping cocoa powder directly with the measuring cup, which can result in up to 25% more powder than needed. Another error is using liquid measuring cups instead of dry ones, as they aren't designed for accurate dry ingredient measurements. Always use proper dry measuring cups for cocoa powder.</p>
