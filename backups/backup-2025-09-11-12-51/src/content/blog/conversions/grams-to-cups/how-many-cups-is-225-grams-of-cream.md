Always measure carefully to avoid kitchen mishaps.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>While 225 grams of cream converts to roughly 0.95 cups in both UK and US measurements, slight differences exist due to cup sizes. A UK cup is 284ml, whereas a US cup is 240ml. In practice, this means 225 grams of cream fills a US cup almost entirely but leaves a small gap in a UK cup. For air fryer recipes, either works, but consistency is key—stick to one measurement system.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 225 Grams of Cream Without Scales</h2>  
<p>No scales? No problem. To measure 225 grams of cream (about 0.95 cups), use a liquid measuring cup and fill it just below the 1-cup mark. For thicker creams like double or clotted, gently spoon it into the cup to avoid air pockets. Level off the top with a knife for accuracy. This method works well for air fryer recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a> or creamy pasta bakes.</p>  

<p>If you’re using a metric jug, 225ml of cream is close in volume to 225 grams, as cream’s density is nearly 1g/ml. However, for whipped cream or aerated varieties, weigh it for precision.
