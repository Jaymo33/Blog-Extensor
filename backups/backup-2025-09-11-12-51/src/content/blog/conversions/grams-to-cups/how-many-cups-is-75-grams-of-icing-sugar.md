<h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h2>
<p>It's important to note that cup measurements differ between the UK and the US. In the UK, a standard cup holds 250ml, while in the US, it's 240ml. This slight difference can affect your baking if you're following a recipe from another country. For 75 grams of icing sugar, the UK measurement is approximately 0.6 cups, whereas the US measurement is closer to 0.75 cups.</p>
<p>If you're unsure which measurement to use, always check the origin of the recipe. Many modern recipes specify whether they use UK or US measurements, but older ones might not. When in doubt, stick to the UK standard if you're baking in the UK, or use a kitchen scale for absolute precision. <h3>How to Measure Icing Sugar Without Scales</h3>
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 75 grams of icing sugar using cups. Spoon the sugar into the cup lightly without packing it down, then level it off with a knife. Remember, icing sugar tends to settle, so fluff it up with a fork before measuring for the most accurate results. This method works well for both UK and US cup measurements.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Icing Sugar</h3>
<p>To keep your icing sugar fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Exposure to moisture can cause it to clump, making it harder to measure accurately. If your sugar does clump, simply sift it before use to restore its fine texture. Proper storage ensures your 75 grams of icing sugar is always ready for your next baking adventure.</p>
