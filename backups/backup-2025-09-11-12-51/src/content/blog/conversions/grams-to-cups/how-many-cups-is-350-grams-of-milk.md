<p>350 grams of milk is roughly equivalent to 1.48 cups in the UK or 1.47 cups in the US. This conversion is essential for anyone using recipes that list ingredients by weight rather than volume, especially when preparing dishes in an air fryer. Whether you're whipping up a cake, a creamy sauce, or a breakfast bake, knowing how many cups is 350 grams of milk ensures accuracy and perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Convert Grams to Cups for Milk?</h2>  
<p>Many recipes, especially those from professional chefs or baking experts, use weight measurements like grams for precision. However, home cooks often rely on volume measurements like cups for convenience. Converting 350 grams of milk to cups helps bridge this gap, ensuring your dishes turn out just right. This is particularly useful for air fryer recipes, where precise measurements can affect cooking times and textures.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>It's important to note that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup of milk is approximately 250ml, while in the US, it's about 240ml. This means 350 grams of milk equals 1.48 cups in the UK and 1.47 cups in the US. The difference is minimal but can matter in recipes requiring high precision.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 350 Grams of Milk Accurately</h2>  
<p>The easiest way to measure 350 grams of milk is by using a kitchen scale. Simply place your measuring jug on the scale, tare it to zero, and pour the milk until it reaches 350 grams. If you don't have a scale, you can use the cup conversion mentioned earlier. Just remember to use the correct cup size for your region.</p>  
<p>For air fryer cooking, accuracy is key. Too much milk in a batter, for instance, can make it too runny, affecting how it cooks in the air fryer. <p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 350 grams of milk using cups. Fill a UK measuring cup to just under 1.5 cups or a US cup to the same level.
