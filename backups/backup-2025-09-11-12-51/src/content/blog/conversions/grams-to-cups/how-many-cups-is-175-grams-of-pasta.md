<h2>Understanding Pasta Measurements</h2>  
<p>Pasta can be tricky to measure because different shapes take up varying amounts of space in a cup. For example, 175 grams of penne will fill a cup differently than spaghetti. Generally, 100 grams of dry pasta equals about 1 cup, so 175 grams works out to roughly 1.75 cups. This is a handy rule of thumb for quick conversions, but if you want exact measurements, a kitchen scale is your best friend.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a scale, fluff the pasta lightly before scooping it into a measuring cup. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to using too much. For air fryer recipes, like those found on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipes page</a>, accurate measurements help prevent overcrowding and ensure even cooking.</p>  

<h3>Does the Type of Pasta Affect the Cup Measurement?</h3>  
<p>Absolutely! Smaller pasta shapes like macaroni or orzo will yield more cups per gram compared to larger shapes like rigatoni or fettuccine. For instance, 175 grams of small shells might measure closer to 2 cups, while the same weight of lasagna sheets could be just over 1.5 cups. Always check the packaging for specific conversion guidelines if precision is key.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>While the difference is minimal, UK cups are slightly larger (284ml) compared to US cups (240ml). For 175 grams of pasta, this means roughly 1.7 UK cups versus 1.75 US cups. Unless you’re baking, the variance won’t drastically affect your dish, but it’s good to know for precision cooking.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Measuring Pasta Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you’re in a pinch, use the “finger method” for long pasta like spaghetti: a bundle roughly the diameter of a £1 coin equals about 100 grams. For 175 grams, aim for 1.5 times that amount. Alternatively, a standard mug holds about 1.5 cups of dry pasta, so filling it just over halfway will get you close to 175 grams.</p>
