<h2>Why Accurate Mustard Measurements Matter</h2>

<p>Mustard’s weight can vary slightly depending on its type (e.g., whole grain, Dijon, or yellow). However, 30g is a reliable average for most recipes. <h3>Types of Mustard and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Different mustards have slightly different densities, which can affect their weight per tablespoon. Yellow mustard, for instance, is typically smoother and slightly lighter, while whole grain mustard is chunkier and denser. For most standard mustards, 2 tbsp will still hover around 30g, but it’s worth checking if precision is crucial. If you're preparing a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipe</a>, a consistent mustard measurement ensures even flavour distribution.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Mustard Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 2 tbsp of mustard accurately using standard spoons. A level tablespoon is key—avoid heaping or scant measurements to stay close to the 30g mark. For sticky mustards, lightly grease the spoon to help it slide off easily. This method works well for air fryer sauces and glazes, ensuring your dishes aren’t overly tangy or mild.</p>  

<p>Another tip is to use a liquid measuring cup for runnier mustards. Fill it to the 30ml mark, which is roughly equivalent to 2 tbsp by volume.
