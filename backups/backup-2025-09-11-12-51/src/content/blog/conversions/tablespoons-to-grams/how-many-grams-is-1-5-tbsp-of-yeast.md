<h3>How to Convert 1.5 Tbsp Yeast to Grams Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t own a kitchen scale, you can still estimate 1.5 tbsp of yeast in grams. One level tablespoon of active dry yeast typically weighs around 9 grams, so 1.5 tbsp would be roughly 14 grams. However, this can vary slightly depending on how tightly packed the yeast is. For best results, always fluff the yeast before measuring.</p>

<h3>Does Yeast Type Affect the Weight?</h3>  
<p>Active dry yeast and instant yeast have slightly different weights per tablespoon due to granule size. However, the difference is minimal—about 0.5 grams per tablespoon. For most home bakers, this won’t drastically impact results. If you’re using fresh yeast, note that 1.5 tbsp of fresh yeast equals about 21 grams, as it’s denser.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing Yeast for Air Fryer Baking</h2>  
<p>Yeast loses potency over time, especially if exposed to air or moisture. To keep it fresh, store opened yeast in an airtight container in the fridge or freezer. For long-term storage, freeze it in small portions. When ready to use, let it come to room temperature before measuring. Proper storage ensures your 1.5 tbsp of yeast (14 grams) remains effective for months.</p>  

<p>If you’re unsure whether your yeast is still active, proof it by dissolving 1.5 tbsp (14 grams) in warm water with a pinch of sugar. If it bubbles within 10 minutes, it’s good to go.
