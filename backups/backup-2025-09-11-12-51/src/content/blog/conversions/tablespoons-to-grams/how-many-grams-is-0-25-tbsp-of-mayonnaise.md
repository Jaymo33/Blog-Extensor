<p>Accurate measurements are essential in cooking, particularly for air fryer recipes where even small deviations can impact the final dish. Mayonnaise, being a dense condiment, requires precise weighing to avoid overly greasy or dry results. For example, if you're making a marinade or coating for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer chicken</a>, too much mayonnaise could lead to uneven browning.</p>  
<p>Many home cooks rely on volume measurements like tablespoons, but weight (grams) provides far greater accuracy. This is especially true for sticky ingredients like mayonnaise that don't level neatly in measuring spoons. By converting 0.25 tbsp to grams, you eliminate guesswork and ensure your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>air fryer creations</a> turn out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>How to measure 0.25 tbsp mayonnaise accurately</h2>  
<p>Measuring small amounts of mayonnaise can be tricky due to its thick consistency. For the most accurate 0.25 tbsp (3.75g) measurement, we recommend using a digital kitchen scale. Simply tare your container, then add mayonnaise until you reach 3.75 grams. This method is far more precise than trying to eyeball a quarter tablespoon.</p>  
<p>If you don't have scales, you can approximate 0.25 tbsp by filling a standard tablespoon about one-quarter full. However, this method is less reliable as mayonnaise tends to cling to the spoon. For best results in your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>air fryer potato recipes</a>, we always recommend weighing ingredients when possible.</p>

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring mayonnaise</h3>  
<p>The biggest mistake people make is packing mayonnaise into the measuring spoon, which can nearly double the actual amount. For 0.25 tbsp (3.75g), the mayonnaise should sit loosely in the spoon without being pressed down. Another error is using cold mayonnaise straight from the fridge - letting it soften slightly at room temperature makes for more accurate measuring.</p>
