<h3>How to measure 5 tbsp of oats without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a standard UK tablespoon to measure your oats. Fill it lightly without packing the oats down, then level off the top. Five level tablespoons should give you roughly 40g. For best results, consider investing in digital scales—they’re a game-changer for air fryer cooking.</p>

<h3>Does oat type affect the weight?</h3>  
<p>Rolled oats, jumbo oats, and quick oats have slightly different densities, but the difference in weight per tablespoon is minimal. For most recipes, 5 tbsp of any oat type will still be around 40g. If you’re using ground oats (oat flour), the weight may vary slightly—always check the packaging for specifics.</p>  

<h2>Tips for storing and using oats in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Keep your oats in an airtight container to maintain freshness. Stale oats can affect the taste and texture of your dishes. When measuring, fluff them up with a fork before scooping to ensure an even amount per tablespoon.
