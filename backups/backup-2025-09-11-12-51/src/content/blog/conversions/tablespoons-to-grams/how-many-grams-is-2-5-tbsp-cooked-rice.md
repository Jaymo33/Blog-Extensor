<h2>Why convert 2.5 tbsp of cooked rice to grams?</h2>  
<p>Converting tablespoons to grams ensures consistency in your air fryer recipes. Unlike volume measurements (like tbsp), weight (grams) accounts for variations in how rice is packed or fluffed. For example, 2.5 tbsp of tightly packed cooked rice might weigh slightly more than loosely scooped rice. If you’re meal prepping or following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, grams provide reliable results every time.</p>

<h3>How to measure 2.5 tbsp of cooked rice in grams</h3>  
<p>Use a digital kitchen scale for the most precise measurement. Spoon cooked rice into a tablespoon without packing it down, then level it off with a knife. Repeat until you’ve measured 2.5 tbsp, then transfer it to the scale. If you’re working with sticky rice, lightly grease the spoon to prevent clumping. <h3>Variations in rice types and their weights</h3>  
<p>Different rice varieties can slightly alter the weight per tablespoon.
