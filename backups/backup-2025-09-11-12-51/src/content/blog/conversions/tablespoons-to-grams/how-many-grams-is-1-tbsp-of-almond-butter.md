<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient ratios can make or break a dish. Too much almond butter might lead to soggy results, while too little could leave your recipe dry. Measuring by weight (grams) is more reliable than volume (tablespoons) because almond butter’s density varies with brands and textures. For example, crunchy almond butter may settle differently than smooth. If you love nut-based recipes, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<p>Using a kitchen scale eliminates guesswork. Simply zero out your scale, scoop 1 tbsp of almond butter, and note the grams. This method is especially useful for calorie tracking or replicating recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>. Pro tip: Lightly oil your measuring spoon to help sticky almond butter slide off easily.</p>  

<h3>How Brand and Texture Affect Weight</h3>  
<p>Not all almond butters weigh the same per tablespoon. Natural varieties with oil separation may be denser (closer to 18g), while stabilised brands could hover around 15g. Stirring well before measuring ensures consistency. If you’re adapting a recipe from cups to grams, remember that 1 cup of almond butter equals roughly 240 grams.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Almond Butter</h3>  
<p>Packing almond butter into a tablespoon can add up to 20% extra weight, skewing your recipe. Instead, use the spoon-and-sweep method: lightly fill the spoon, then level it off with a knife. Also, avoid measuring cold almond butter straight from the fridge—it’s harder to portion accurately. Let it soften slightly for smoother scooping.</p>
