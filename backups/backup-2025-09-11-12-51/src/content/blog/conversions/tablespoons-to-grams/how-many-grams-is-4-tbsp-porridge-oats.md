<h2>How to Measure 4 Tbsp of Porridge Oats Correctly</h2>  
<p>To measure 4 tablespoons of porridge oats accurately, use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and level off the excess with a knife. Scooping directly from the bag can lead to packed or uneven measurements, affecting the final weight. For best results, lightly spoon the oats into the tablespoon before levelling.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Porridge Oats</h3>  
<p>One frequent error is using a heaped tablespoon instead of a levelled one, which can add up to 5 extra grams per tablespoon. Another is assuming all oats have the same density—rolled oats and quick oats may vary slightly in weight. Always double-check your measurements for the best results.</p>

<h3>Why Use Grams Instead of Tablespoons?</h3>  
<p>Weight measurements (grams) are more reliable than volume measurements (tablespoons) because they account for variations in ingredient density. A tablespoon of finely ground oats will weigh more than a tablespoon of whole rolled oats. For consistent results, especially in baking, a kitchen scale is a worthwhile investment.</p>
