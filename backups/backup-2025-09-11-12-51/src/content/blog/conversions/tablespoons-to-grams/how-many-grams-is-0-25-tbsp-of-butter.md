<p>0.25 tbsp of butter equals approximately 3.55 grams in the UK. This conversion is essential for precision in air fryer recipes, where accurate measurements can make or break a dish. <h2>How to measure 0.25 tbsp of butter</h2>  
<p>Measuring small amounts of butter can be tricky, but there are a few easy methods. If you’re using a block of butter, you can slice it thinly to approximate 0.25 tbsp (3.55g). Alternatively, softened butter can be pressed into a measuring spoon and levelled off with a knife. For the most accurate results, though, a digital kitchen scale is unbeatable.</p>  

<p>If you’re working with pre-packaged butter, check the wrapper for markings. Many UK butter brands include tablespoon and gram indicators, making it easier to measure out 0.25 tbsp without guesswork. <h2>Tips for using butter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Butter adds richness and flavour to air fryer dishes, but it’s important to use it wisely. For greasing baskets or trays, a light coating is best—0.25 tbsp (3.55g) is often plenty. Melted butter can also be brushed onto foods like garlic bread or vegetables for extra crispiness. Just avoid overloading, as excess butter can cause smoking or uneven cooking.</p>  

<p>If you’re watching your fat intake, consider reducing the butter in recipes or substituting with a spritz of oil. Many air fryer dishes, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable category</a>, are delicious with just a hint of butter. Remember, a little goes a long way in the air fryer’s concentrated heat.</p>  

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring butter</h3>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is using cold butter straight from the fridge, which is harder to measure accurately. For small amounts like 0.25 tbsp (3.55g), let the butter soften slightly first. Another pitfall is packing butter into a measuring spoon, which can lead to using more than intended. Always level off the spoon for the correct amount.</p>
