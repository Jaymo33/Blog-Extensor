<h2>How to Measure 185 ml of Maple Syrup Without a Scale</h2>
<p>Not everyone has a kitchen scale, but most have measuring cups. To measure 185 ml of maple syrup in cups, fill a liquid measuring cup to just under 4/5 of a cup (0.78 cups). For sticky liquids like syrup, lightly grease the cup first to help it slide out easily. This trick works wonders for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a> or marinades.</p>
<p>If you’re using a standard 250 ml measuring cup, pour the syrup until it’s about three-quarters full. Remember, 185 ml of maple syrup in cups isn’t a whole number, so eyeballing it isn’t ideal for precision. For best results, use a metric measuring jug with clear ml markings.</p>

<h3>US vs UK Cup Measurements</h3>
<p>In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, while in the US, it’s 240 ml. This means 185 ml of maple syrup is roughly 0.65 UK cups or 0.78 US cups. Always check where your recipe originates to avoid mishaps. Most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> use US measurements unless specified.</p>
