<h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for 510 ml of Cream</h2>  
<p>In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, making 510 ml roughly 2.15 cups. In the US, where 1 cup is 240 ml, 510 ml converts to about 2.16 cups. This slight difference might not matter for some recipes, but for delicate desserts like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>air fryer cheesecake</a>, precision is key.</p>  
<p>If you're adapting a US recipe to UK measurements, always double-check the cup size. Many modern UK recipes use the US cup standard (240 ml), so it's worth confirming before you start. <h3>How to Measure 510 ml of Cream Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a measuring jug with millilitre markings, use standard cups. Fill a UK cup (284 ml) twice, then add roughly 1 tablespoon to reach 510 ml. For US cups (240 ml), fill two cups and add about 2 tablespoons. Stir the cream gently to ensure uniformity before adding it to your recipe.</p>
