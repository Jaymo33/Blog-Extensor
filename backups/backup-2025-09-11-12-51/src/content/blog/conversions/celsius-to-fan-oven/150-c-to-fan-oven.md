<p>150 C in a fan oven is equivalent to approximately 130 C in a conventional oven with the fan setting. This 20-degree reduction is standard for fan-assisted cooking, as the circulating air distributes heat more efficiently. <h2>Understanding Fan Oven Conversions</h2>
<p>The key principle when converting 150 C to fan oven is recognising that forced air circulation changes how heat transfers to food. Traditional ovens rely on radiant heat, while fan ovens and air fryers use convection currents. This means a 150 C fan oven setting will cook food faster and more evenly than a standard oven at the same temperature. Many modern recipes at <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> specifically provide fan oven temperatures for this reason.</p>
<p>When adapting older recipes that don't specify fan settings, the general rule is to reduce the temperature by 20 C. So if a recipe states 170 C conventional, you'd set your fan oven or air fryer to 150 C instead. This conversion works particularly well for baked goods like the cakes and cookies found in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes collection</a>, ensuring they cook through without over-browning.</p>
