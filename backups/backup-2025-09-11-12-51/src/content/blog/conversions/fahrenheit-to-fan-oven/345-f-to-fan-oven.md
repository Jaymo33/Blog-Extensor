<h3>How Does 345°F Compare to Other Oven Types?</h3>
<p>While 345°F equals 170°C in a fan oven, it’s 175°C in a conventional oven without fan assistance. Gas mark ovens would set this at around mark 4. These differences highlight why conversions matter for recipe success. Air fryers typically use Fahrenheit, so knowing these equivalents helps when switching appliances. <h2>Adjusting Recipes Between Air Fryer and Fan Oven</h2>
<p>When moving from air fryer to fan oven, the 345°F to 170°C conversion is just the starting point. Air fryers cook faster due to their compact size and intense air circulation, so reduce times by 20-30%. Conversely, fan oven recipes converted for air fryer use need increased temperatures by about 25°F (15°C) less than standard conversions suggest. Experimentation helps perfect these adjustments for your specific appliances.</p>

<p>Texture differences also occur – air fryers excel at crisping, while fan ovens provide more even browning. For dishes where both matter, consider starting in the air fryer and finishing in the fan oven. Humidity plays a role too; fan ovens may dry foods faster, so covering with foil can help retain moisture. The key is treating conversions as guidelines rather than strict rules, adjusting based on your observations.</p>
