<p>284°F converts to approximately 140°C in a fan oven. This is a common temperature for slow roasting or baking delicate dishes, and it’s useful to know when adapting recipes for your air fryer. Fan ovens circulate hot air for even cooking, much like an air fryer, but their temperature scales differ slightly. Whether you’re using a conventional oven or an air fryer, getting the conversion right ensures your food cooks perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 284°F to Fan Oven?</h2>
<p>Many recipes, especially those from the US, list temperatures in Fahrenheit. If you’re using a UK fan oven or an air fryer, you’ll need to convert 284°F to Celsius for accurate results. Fan ovens typically require a 20°C reduction compared to conventional ovens, so 284°F (140°C) is ideal for gentle cooking. This temperature works well for dishes like custards, slow-cooked meats, or even air-fried desserts from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>our dessert collection</a>.</p>

<p>Air fryers, which also use convection heating, can often use the same fan oven conversions. However, because air fryers are more compact, you might need to adjust cooking times slightly. Always check your food a few minutes early to avoid overcooking. For more tips, see our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>

<h3>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h3>
<p>When converting 284°F to a fan oven, remember that fan-assisted cooking is faster. Reduce the cooking time by 10-15% compared to a conventional oven. For air fryers, this reduction might be even greater—up to 20% in some cases. For example, a recipe calling for 30 minutes at 284°F in a conventional oven might only need 24 minutes in an air fryer.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for 284°F in Fan Ovens</h2>
<p>284°F (140°C fan) is perfect for dishes that benefit from low-and-slow cooking. Think tender pulled pork, melt-in-the-mouth cakes, or even dehydrating fruits. In an air fryer, this temperature works well for reheating leftovers without drying them out. Try it with <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our air fryer recipes</a> for best results.</p>

<p>Another great use for 284°F in a fan oven is proofing dough. The gentle heat helps yeast activate without killing it, giving you perfectly risen bread. Air fryers can also handle this task—just place a bowl of water in the basket to maintain humidity.</p>

<h3>Air Fryer vs. Fan Oven at 284°F</h3>
<p>While both appliances use convection heating, air fryers are more intense due to their smaller size. At 284°F (140°C fan), an air fryer will cook food faster than a fan oven. Always monitor your food closely, especially when trying new recipes. <h2>Tips for Perfect Results</h2>
<p>When cooking at 284°F in a fan oven or air fryer, always preheat the appliance first. This ensures even cooking from the start. Use a thermometer to check internal food temperatures, especially for meats. For air fryers, shaking the basket halfway through cooking helps everything cook evenly.</p>
