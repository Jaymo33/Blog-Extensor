<h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>When using 338°F (170°C fan), reduce cooking times by 10-15% compared to conventional ovens. Air fryers cook faster due to concentrated heat circulation. For dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, start checking doneness a few minutes early.</p>

<h3>Common Dishes at 338°F Fan</h3>
<p>This temperature works well for roasting nuts, dehydrating fruits, or reheating leftovers. It’s also ideal for delicate pastries that might burn at higher heats. Always use an oven thermometer if precision matters.</p>

<h2>Air Fryer vs. Fan Oven Performance</h2>
<p>While both appliances use fans, air fryers are more compact, intensifying heat distribution. At 170°C fan, an air fryer cooks faster than a full-sized oven. Adjust recipes accordingly—especially for items like frozen chips or <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>potato dishes</a>.</p>

<h3>Troubleshooting Temperature Issues</h3>
<p>If food isn’t crisping at 170°C fan, extend cooking time slightly or increase by 5°C. Conversely, reduce heat if edges brown too quickly. Air fryers vary by model, so note what works best for yours.</p>
