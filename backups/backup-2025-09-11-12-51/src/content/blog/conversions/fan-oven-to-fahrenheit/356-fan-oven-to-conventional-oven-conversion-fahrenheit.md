<h3>How to Calculate the Conversion</h3>  
<p>The general rule is to increase the conventional oven temperature by 20°F when converting from a fan oven. So, 356°F fan oven becomes 375°F in a conventional oven. <h2>Fan Oven vs. Conventional Oven: Key Differences</h2>  
<p>Fan ovens use a fan to circulate hot air, reducing cooking times by up to 20%. Conventional ovens rely on radiant heat, which can create hot spots. This is why converting 356 fan oven to conventional oven requires a higher temperature. The same principle applies when adapting air fryer recipes, as both use rapid air circulation.</p>  

<p>For <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a> and delicate bakes, fan ovens can dry out edges faster. The converted 375°F in a conventional oven gives more control. Always adjust cooking times accordingly, checking for doneness a few minutes early.</p>
