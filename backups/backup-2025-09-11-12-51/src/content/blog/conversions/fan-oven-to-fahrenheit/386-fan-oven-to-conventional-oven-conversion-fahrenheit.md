<h2>Why Convert 386 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air, cooking food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens. This means recipes often require lower temperatures or shorter cooking times. When a recipe calls for 386°F in a fan oven, using the same temperature in a conventional oven could lead to overcooking. By reducing the temperature to 356°F, you achieve similar results without compromising texture or flavour. This is particularly important for delicate dishes like cakes or pastries.</p>

<h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>Temperature isn't the only factor to consider when converting 386 fan oven to conventional oven. Cooking times may also need adjustment. As a general rule, reduce the cooking time by 10-15% when using a conventional oven. For example, if a recipe suggests 30 minutes in a fan oven, try 25-27 minutes in a conventional oven. Always check for doneness a few minutes early to avoid overbaking.</p>
<p>When using an air fryer, cooking times can be even shorter due to the compact cooking space and intense heat circulation. Start by reducing the time by 20% and monitor closely.
