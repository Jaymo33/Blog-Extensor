<h2>Why Convert Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>  
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air, reducing cooking time and temperature compared to conventional ovens. Recipes designed for conventional ovens may overcook in a fan oven if not adjusted. Converting 374 fan oven to Fahrenheit for a standard oven ensures your dishes turn out perfectly. This is particularly useful for baking cakes or roasting meats, where precise temperatures matter. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe hub</a> for adapted fan-oven recipes.</p>  

<p>Air fryers, like fan ovens, rely on rapid air circulation, so understanding these conversions helps when switching between appliances. For example, if a recipe calls for 374°F in a fan oven, setting your air fryer to 350°F should yield similar results. Always monitor cooking times, as air fryers can be even faster than fan ovens.</p>  

<h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>  
<p>When converting 374 fan oven to conventional oven, reduce the temperature by about 20-25°F. This means 374°F fan becomes 350°F conventional. Cooking times may also need adjusting—fan ovens typically cook 10-15% faster. For air fryers, reduce time by another 5-10% due to their compact size and intense heat.</p>
