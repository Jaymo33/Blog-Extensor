<h3>Tips for working with icing sugar</h3>  
<p>Icing sugar tends to clump, so it's best to sift it before use, especially in air fryer recipes where even texture matters. Store it in an airtight container to prevent moisture absorption. If your sugar has hardened, you can pulse it briefly in a food processor to restore its powdery texture.</p>  

<p>When making frostings or glazes, the ratio of liquid to sugar is crucial. Too much liquid and your frosting will be runny; too little and it will be stiff. For perfect consistency every time, measure your 500g of icing sugar accurately, whether you're using ounces or grams.</p>
