<h3>How to measure 10g of cream cheese without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a digital scale, you can estimate 10g of cream cheese using tablespoons. One level tablespoon is roughly 15g, so two-thirds of a tablespoon will give you close to 10g. For ounces, this works out to just over a third of an ounce. While not as precise as weighing, it’s a handy trick for quick recipes.</p>

<h2>Tips for storing cream cheese for air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Once opened, cream cheese should be stored in an airtight container in the fridge. If you’ve measured out 10g for a recipe, ensure the rest stays fresh by sealing it properly. For longer storage, cream cheese can be frozen, though the texture may change slightly. Thaw it in the fridge before using it in your air fryer.</p>  

<p>If you’re using cream cheese frequently, consider portioning it into 10g amounts and freezing them individually. This makes it easy to grab the exact quantity you need for recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>air-fryer breakfast pastries</a>. Always label your portions with the weight to avoid confusion later.</p>
