<h2>How to Measure 75g of Butter Without Scales</h2>  
<p>Don’t have kitchen scales? No problem. You can still measure 75g of butter using tablespoons or visual cues. One tablespoon of butter is roughly 14g, so 75g would be about 5.3 tablespoons. Alternatively, a standard UK block of butter is often marked in 50g increments, making it easy to eyeball 75g.</p>  

<h3>Using Butter Wrappers for Measurement</h3>  
<p>Most butter wrappers in the UK have measurements printed on them. If your butter comes in a 250g block, simply divide it into thirds to get roughly 83g, then shave off a small amount to reach 75g. This method isn’t exact but works in a pinch for recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cookies>cookies</a> or sauces.</p>
