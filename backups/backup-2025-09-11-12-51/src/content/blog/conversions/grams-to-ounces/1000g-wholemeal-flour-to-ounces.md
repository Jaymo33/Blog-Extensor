<h2>How to Measure 1000g of Wholemeal Flour Accurately</h2>
<p>To measure 1000g of wholemeal flour (35.27 oz), use a digital kitchen scale for precision. Scoop the flour into your measuring bowl until the scale hits 1000g. Avoid packing the flour down, as this can lead to over-measuring. If you don’t have a scale, 1000g roughly translates to about 8 UK cups, but weights can vary based on how you scoop.</p>
<p>For air fryer recipes, accuracy is key. Too much flour can make baked goods heavy, while too little can leave them crumbly. If you’re making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes</a> or bread in your air fryer, stick to the 1000g-to-35.27-ounce conversion for consistency. Remember, wholemeal flour absorbs more liquid, so adjustments might be needed.</p>

<h2>Tips for Baking with Wholemeal Flour in an Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Wholemeal flour behaves differently than white flour, so adjustments are often needed. When converting 1000g to ounces (35.27 oz), consider adding a bit more liquid to your recipe. Wholemeal flour absorbs moisture faster, which can dry out your bakes. For air fryer recipes, reduce the temperature slightly and extend the cooking time to avoid burning.</p>
<p>Another tip is to sift the flour before measuring. This aerates it, ensuring you don’t end up with too much in your mix. <h3>Storing Wholemeal Flour for Freshness</h3>
<p>Once you’ve measured out your 1000g (35.27 oz) of wholemeal flour, store the rest in an airtight container. Wholemeal flour has a shorter shelf life than white flour due to its higher oil content. Keep it in a cool, dark place to prevent rancidity. If you bake often, consider buying in bulk and freezing portions to maintain freshness.</p>
