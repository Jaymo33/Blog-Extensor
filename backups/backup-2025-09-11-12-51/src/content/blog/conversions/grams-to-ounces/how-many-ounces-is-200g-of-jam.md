<h2>How to Measure 200g of Jam Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>Not everyone has a kitchen scale, but there are easy ways to measure 200g of jam (7.05 oz) without one. A standard tablespoon holds about 20g of jam, so 10 tablespoons will give you roughly 200g. Alternatively, a metric cup filled to the 200ml mark will also approximate 200g, as jam’s density is close to 1g per ml.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Jam</h2>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is assuming all jams weigh the same. Thicker jams, like marmalade, may weigh slightly more per tablespoon than runny jams. Always check the consistency and adjust measurements if needed. For sticky ingredients like jam, lightly greasing your measuring spoon or cup can make it easier to get an accurate amount.</p>
