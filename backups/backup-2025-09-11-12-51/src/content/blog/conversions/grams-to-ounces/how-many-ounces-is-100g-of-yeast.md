Getting the measurement right ensures your dough rises perfectly and your air fryer creations turn out light and fluffy.</p>  

<h2>Why converting 100g of yeast to ounces matters</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but if your kitchen scales only measure ounces, knowing how to convert 100g of yeast to ounces is crucial. Air fryer baking relies on precise measurements, and yeast is no exception. Too little yeast, and your dough won’t rise; too much, and it might overproof. For perfect results, stick to the recommended 3.53 ounces for 100g.</p>

<h3>How to measure 100g of yeast without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have digital scales, you can still measure 100g of yeast using volume. One tablespoon of active dry yeast weighs about 9g, so 100g would be roughly 11 tablespoons. However, for accuracy, we recommend investing in a good set of kitchen scales, especially if you use your air fryer for baking often.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 100g of yeast in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>100g of yeast is a common quantity for larger batches of dough. If you’re making air fryer pizza dough, bread rolls, or even cinnamon buns, this measurement will come in handy. For smaller portions, you might only need 7g (one sachet) of yeast, but bulk baking often requires 100g or more.</p>

<h3>Does yeast type affect the conversion?</h3>  
<p>Active dry yeast, instant yeast, and fresh yeast all have slightly different weights per volume. However, when converting 100g to ounces, the type doesn’t change the weight—3.53 ounces is accurate for all. Just remember that substitution ratios may vary, so check your recipe notes if swapping yeast types.</p>
