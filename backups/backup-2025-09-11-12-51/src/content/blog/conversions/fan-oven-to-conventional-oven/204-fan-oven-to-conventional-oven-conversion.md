<p>204°C in a fan oven equals approximately 190°C in a conventional oven. This 10-20°C reduction is standard when converting between fan and conventional ovens. Understanding this conversion is crucial for perfect baking results, especially when using recipes designed for different oven types.</p>

<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air constantly, cooking food more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. The forced air movement means you can typically reduce the temperature by 10-20°C compared to conventional oven settings. This principle applies whether you're baking cakes at <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> or roasting meats.</p>
<p>The 204 fan oven to conventional oven conversion follows this standard adjustment. <h2>Practical Conversion Examples</h2>
<p>When converting 204 fan oven to celsius for conventional ovens, remember the general rule of thumb. For most recipes, reducing the temperature by 10-20°C works perfectly. So 204°C fan becomes 190°C conventional, or sometimes 180°C for more delicate bakes.</p>
<p>This conversion applies across various cooking methods. Whether you're roasting vegetables from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a> section or baking cookies, the same principle holds true. <h3>Adjusting Cooking Times</h3>
<p>When using 204 fan oven to conventional oven conversion, cooking times may need slight adjustment too. Conventional ovens generally take slightly longer to cook food at the lower temperature. Keep an eye on your dish and use a timer to prevent over or undercooking.</p>

<p>If you're wondering about 204 fan oven to air fryer equivalent temperatures, the principle is similar. Air fryers cook even more efficiently than fan ovens, so you'll typically reduce the temperature by another 10-20°C. This means 204°C fan might become 170-180°C in an air fryer.</p>
