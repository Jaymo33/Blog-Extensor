<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>Fortunately, for liquids like evaporated milk, UK and US cup measurements are the same—1 cup equals 240 ml. This means 1.75 cups of evaporated milk is consistently 414 ml in both regions. However, dry ingredients can vary, so always double-check if a recipe specifies which cup measurement it uses.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Evaporated Milk Correctly</h2>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a liquid measuring jug with clear millilitre markings. Pour the evaporated milk slowly to avoid air bubbles, and check the measurement at eye level. If you don’t have a jug, a kitchen scale can help—1.75 cups of evaporated milk weighs roughly 420 grams, which is close enough for most recipes.</p>

<h3>Substitutes for Evaporated Milk</h3>  
<p>If you're out of evaporated milk, you can make a substitute by simmering regular milk until it reduces by half. For 1.75 cups (414 ml) of evaporated milk, start with about 3.5 cups (828 ml) of whole milk. This works well in most air fryer recipes, though the richness may vary slightly.</p>
