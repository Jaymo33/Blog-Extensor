<h2>Why Convert 388 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. This means recipes designed for conventional ovens often need temperature adjustments when using a fan oven. For example, 388°F in a fan oven translates to 350°F in a conventional oven, a difference of 38°F. This adjustment prevents overcooking and ensures your baked goods or meats come out just right. <h3>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h3>
<p>When converting 388 fan oven to conventional oven, remember that cooking times may also need adjustment. Fan ovens cook faster, so if a recipe calls for 30 minutes at 388°F fan, you might need to add 5-10 minutes at 350°F conventional. Always check your food a few minutes early to avoid overcooking. This tip is particularly useful for roasting vegetables or meats, where timing can make or break the dish.
