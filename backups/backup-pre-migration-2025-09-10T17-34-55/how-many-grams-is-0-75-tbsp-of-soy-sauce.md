<h2>Why accurate soy sauce measurements matter</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of soy sauce can make or break your dish. Too little, and your meal might lack depth; too much, and it becomes overpoweringly salty. In an air fryer, where flavours concentrate, precision is key. A measurement like 0.75 tbsp (11.25 grams) ensures balanced seasoning for smaller portions or delicate recipes. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> for dishes that benefit from this exact measurement.</p>

This is particularly useful for marinades or glazes where soy sauce plays a starring role.</p>

<h3>How to measure 0.75 tbsp of soy sauce</h3>
<p>Measuring odd fractions like 0.75 tbsp can be tricky without the right tools. Use a tablespoon measure and fill it three-quarters full for accuracy. Alternatively, measure 11.25 grams directly on a digital kitchen scale for perfect precision. For sticky sauces like soy, lightly grease your measuring spoon first to ensure all the liquid slides out cleanly into your mixing bowl or marinade.</p>
