<h2>Why Convert 10g of Lime Juice to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those from the UK, use metric measurements like grams. However, some kitchen scales or recipes might require ounces. Converting 10g of lime juice to ounces (0.35 oz) ensures you’re using the right amount for marinades, dressings, or glazes. Precision is key in air frying, as too much or too little liquid can affect texture and cooking times. For more conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Lime juice adds brightness to dishes like air-fried shrimp or citrus-glazed salmon. A slight miscalculation could overpower the dish or leave it lacking flavour. By converting 10g lime juice to ounces accurately, you’ll maintain the balance of flavours in recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>. This is especially important for delicate recipes like desserts or light seafood dishes.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 10g of Lime Juice</h3>  
<p>Measuring lime juice by weight (10g) is more accurate than using volume (like tablespoons), as juice density can vary. Use a digital kitchen scale for the best results. If you don’t have one, 10g of lime juice is roughly 2 teaspoons, but weighing is preferable. For sticky residues, clean your scale with <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a> to maintain accuracy.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 10g of Lime Juice in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>Lime juice is a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. From marinades for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> to dressings for shrimp tacos, 10g (0.35 oz) can elevate your dish. It’s also great for balancing rich flavours in air-fried desserts like citrus-infused cakes. The acidity helps tenderise meats and adds a fresh note to vegetables.</p>  

<p>If you’re preparing a large batch, multiply the conversion accordingly. For example, 30g of lime juice would be 1.05 ounces. Always adjust seasoning after adding lime juice, as its acidity can intensify other flavours. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> for creative ways to use lime juice in your air fryer.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Storing Lime Juice</h3>  
<p>Fresh lime juice is best, but if you’re measuring 10g from a bottle, ensure it’s well-shaken for consistency. Store leftover juice in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze lime juice in ice cube trays—each cube is roughly 15g (0.5 oz), so adjust your measurements accordingly.</p>
