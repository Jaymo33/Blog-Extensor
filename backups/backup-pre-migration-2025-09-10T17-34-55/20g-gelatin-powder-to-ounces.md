<h2>Why accurate gelatin measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Gelatin powder is a finicky ingredient—too much, and your dessert turns rubbery; too little, and it won’t set. For air fryer-friendly desserts like panna cotta or jelly-based treats, 20g of gelatin powder (0.71 oz) is a common measurement. <h3>How to measure 20g of gelatin powder without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use a tablespoon to measure 20g of gelatin powder. One level tablespoon holds about 7g, so you’ll need just under 3 tablespoons for 20g (0.71 oz). For powdered gelatin, always fluff it up before scooping to avoid compacting. This method isn’t as precise as weighing, but it works in a pinch for recipes like air fryer marshmallows or mousses.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 20g of gelatin powder</h2>  
<p>From air fryer-friendly jellies to no-bake cheesecakes, 20g of gelatin powder (0.71 oz) is a versatile amount. It’s enough to set 500ml of liquid firmly, making it ideal for individual desserts or small batches.
