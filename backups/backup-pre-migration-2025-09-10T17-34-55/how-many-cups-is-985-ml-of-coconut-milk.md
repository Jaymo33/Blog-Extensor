<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of coconut milk ensures your dish has the right consistency and flavour. Too little, and your recipe might turn out dry; too much, and it could become overly soggy. This is particularly important for air fryer cooking, where even moisture distribution affects crispiness. For more conversion tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Coconut milk is a staple in many cuisines, from Thai curries to vegan desserts. Whether you're making a fragrant <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken curry</a> or a dairy-free cake, knowing how to convert 985 ml to cups simplifies the process. The slight variance between UK and US cup sizes (240 ml vs. 236.588 ml) is negligible for most home cooks, but precision matters in baking.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Coconut Milk Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a liquid measuring cup rather than dry measuring cups. Fill it to the 985 ml mark or measure out 4.16 cups, ensuring the liquid sits level with the measurement line. Stirring the coconut milk before measuring helps avoid separation, giving you a consistent texture in your dish.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 985 ml of Coconut Milk</h2>  
<p>This quantity is perfect for batch cooking or larger air fryer recipes. You might use it in a family-sized portion of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>coconut rice pudding</a> or a creamy soup. Since coconut milk freezes well, any leftovers can be stored for future meals. Just remember to shake or stir it well after thawing.</p>
