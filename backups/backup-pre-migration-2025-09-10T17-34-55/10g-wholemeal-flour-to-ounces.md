<h2>Why Convert 10g of Wholemeal Flour to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes, especially those for air fryers, use metric measurements like grams. However, some kitchen scales or older cookbooks might display ounces. Converting 10g of wholemeal flour to ounces (0.35 oz) helps avoid guesswork. This is particularly useful for baking, where even small discrepancies can affect texture. For example, air fryer cookies or cakes rely on precise flour ratios. If you’re exploring our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, you’ll notice how measurements impact outcomes.</p>  

<p>Wholemeal flour, being denser than white flour, requires careful measuring. A slight overpour could lead to dry or crumbly results. Using a digital scale is the most accurate method, but if yours only shows ounces, this conversion is a lifesaver. For more tips on air fryer baking, check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> designed for perfect results every time.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 10g of Wholemeal Flour Accurately</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, 10g of wholemeal flour is roughly two levelled teaspoons. However, for air fryer recipes, we always recommend weighing ingredients. Scooping flour directly from the bag can compact it, leading to uneven measurements. Instead, spoon the flour into your measuring tool and level it off. This ensures you’re using the correct 0.35 ounces (10g) for your dish.</p>  

<h3>Using a Digital Scale for Precision</h3>  
<p>Digital scales are a game-changer for air fryer cooking. Simply set your scale to grams, tare the weight of your bowl, and add flour until it hits 10g. If your scale defaults to ounces, aim for 0.35 oz. This method eliminates errors and is especially handy for recipes like air fryer bread or pastry. For a full breakdown of conversions, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> covers all common ingredients.</p>  

<h2>Common Recipes Using 10g of Wholemeal Flour</h2>  
<p>Small quantities of wholemeal flour, like 10g (0.35 oz), often appear in coatings or thickeners. For instance, a light dusting on air fryer chicken or fish adds crispness without overpowering. It’s also used in small-batch baking, such as single-serving muffins or scones. Wholemeal flour’s nutty flavour enhances savoury dishes, making it a staple in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a>.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Flours</h3>  
<p>If substituting wholemeal flour for plain or self-raising, note that 10g (0.35 oz) behaves differently due to its density. Wholemeal absorbs more liquid, so you might need slight adjustments in wet ingredients. For air fryer recipes, this can affect cooking times slightly. Always test a small batch first, especially with delicate bakes like air fryer cookies.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing Wholemeal Flour</h2>  
<p>To keep your wholemeal flour fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. Since it contains more oil than refined flour, it can turn rancid faster. Buying smaller quantities ensures you’re always using fresh flour for your 10g (0.35 oz) measurements.
