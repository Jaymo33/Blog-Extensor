<h2>Understanding Fan Oven vs Conventional Oven</h2>
<p>Fan ovens, also known as convection ovens, use a fan to distribute heat evenly throughout the cavity. This means they can cook food about 20°C lower than conventional ovens while achieving similar results. The 87 fan oven to conventional oven conversion is particularly important for delicate bakes where precise temperature control matters. Many modern recipes, including those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a>, now specify both fan and conventional oven settings to avoid confusion.</p>
<p>When converting 87 fan oven to celsius for a conventional oven, you'll typically add 10-20 degrees. This adjustment compensates for the less efficient heat distribution in conventional ovens. For most baking needs, whether you're making cookies from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/Cookies>cookie recipes</a> or roasting vegetables, this conversion will ensure your dishes cook properly without burning or undercooking.</p>

<h2>How to Convert 87 Fan Oven to Conventional</h2>
<p>The standard conversion for 87 fan oven to conventional oven is simple: add 13 degrees to get 100°C. This conversion works for most cooking scenarios, from baking cakes to roasting meats. However, some recipes might require slight adjustments based on the dish's sensitivity to temperature changes. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> includes similar principles that can help with various oven type conversions.</p>
<p>For those wondering what is 87 fan oven in celsius when using a conventional oven, remember that fan ovens cook more efficiently. The circulating air means heat reaches all surfaces of the food more evenly. This is why the 87 c fan oven to normal oven conversion requires a higher temperature - to compensate for the less efficient heat distribution in conventional ovens.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations for Baking</h3>
<p>When converting 87 fan oven to conventional oven for baking, you might need to monitor your goods more closely. Cakes and pastries can be particularly sensitive to temperature changes. If you're adapting a recipe from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/Cakes>cake collection</a>, consider checking for doneness a few minutes earlier than the recipe suggests when using a conventional oven.</p>

<h2>87 Fan Oven to Air Fryer Equivalent</h2>
<p>Interestingly, the 87 fan oven to air fryer equivalent follows similar conversion principles. Air fryers are essentially small, powerful convection ovens, so they cook similarly to fan ovens but faster. When converting 87 fan oven to air fryer, you might actually reduce the temperature slightly (about 10°C) and shorten the cooking time by 20-30%.</p>
