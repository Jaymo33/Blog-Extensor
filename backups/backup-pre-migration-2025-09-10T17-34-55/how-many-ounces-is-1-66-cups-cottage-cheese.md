<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. For example, using too much cottage cheese in a recipe might make it overly dense, while too little could leave it dry. That’s why converting 1.66 cups of cottage cheese to ounces (13.28 oz) is so important. It helps you follow recipes precisely, whether you’re making a cheesy bake or a protein-packed snack.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Cottage Cheese Correctly</h3>  
<p>Cottage cheese can be tricky to measure because it’s chunky and often contains liquid. For the most accurate conversion of 1.66 cups to ounces (13.28 oz), use a kitchen scale. If you don’t have one, spoon the cottage cheese into a measuring cup and level it off without packing it down. This method ensures you’re not over or under-measuring.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing and Using Cottage Cheese</h3>  
<p>Once you’ve measured out your 1.66 cups (13.28 oz) of cottage cheese, store any leftovers properly to keep them fresh. Transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to five days. Cottage cheese can also be frozen, though the texture may change slightly. Thaw it in the fridge before using it in your next air fryer creation.</p>
