<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>While 1.0 US cup of beef stock is 240 ml, UK cups measure slightly different at 284 ml. Always check your recipe’s origin to avoid discrepancies. Most modern UK recipes use metric measurements, so 240 ml is the standard for 1.0 cups of beef stock in air fryer cooking.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Beef Stock for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>For best results, use a liquid measuring jug to measure 1.0 cups (240 ml) of beef stock. Pour it slowly to the marked line at eye level for accuracy. If your air fryer recipe calls for a smaller amount, like 0.5 cups, simply halve the measurement to 120 ml. Need a substitute? Try our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/beef>beef stock alternatives</a> for flavourful twists.</p>  

<p>Avoid using dry measuring cups for liquids, as they can lead to uneven amounts. For sticky reductions or glazes, lightly grease the measuring cup to ensure all 240 ml of beef stock transfers smoothly into your air fryer basket or pot. This trick works wonders for dishes like air-fried beef bourguignon.</p>
