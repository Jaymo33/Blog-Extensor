<h3>Avoiding Common Rice Cooking Mistakes</h3>
<p>One of the biggest mistakes people make is not rinsing their rice before cooking, which can leave excess starch on the grains and make the final dish gummy. Another error is guessing the liquid amount; always use a measured amount of liquid based on the weight of your rice. For 10.5 ounces of rice, you would typically use about 15.75 fluid ounces of liquid. Also, avoid lifting the air fryer basket too frequently during cooking, as this releases the crucial steam and heat needed for the rice to cook properly. Letting the rice rest for a few minutes after the cooking cycle ends allows the grains to firm up and absorb any remaining liquid evenly.</p>

<h3>Storing and Reheating Cooked Rice</h3>
<p>Once you have cooked your perfect batch of rice, proper storage is essential for food safety. Allow the rice to cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container and refrigerating it; it will keep for 3-4 days. Your air fryer is also a brilliant tool for reheating leftover rice quickly and effectively, helping to restore its texture without making it soggy. Spread the cold rice in an even layer in an air fryer basket, spritz it lightly with water to add moisture back, and heat at 350°F (175°C) for 2-4 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through. This method is far superior to microwaving, which often leaves you with hot, hard clumps.</p>
