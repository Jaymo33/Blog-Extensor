<h3>Converting cups to ounces for buttermilk</h3>
<p>The standard conversion shows 1 cup of buttermilk equals 8.45 ounces, but this can vary slightly based on buttermilk thickness. Full-fat versions may weigh marginally more than low-fat options. For absolute precision when preparing <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, we recommend using digital scales.</p>

<h2>Measuring buttermilk without scales</h2>
<p>If you don't have kitchen scales, you can still measure 1 cup of buttermilk accurately. Use proper liquid measuring cups with spouts, not dry ingredient cups. Fill to exactly the 1 cup line without estimating - buttermilk's viscosity means it clings to measuring cups differently than water.</p>
<p>For smaller amounts, remember that 1 tablespoon of buttermilk equals about 0.5 ounces. This helps when adjusting <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> or making substitutions. Always stir buttermilk well before measuring as it can separate during storage.</p>

<h3>Buttermilk substitutes in air fryer cooking</h3>
<p>If you're out of buttermilk, you can make a substitute by adding 1 tablespoon lemon juice or vinegar to 1 cup regular milk (8 ounces). Let it sit for 5 minutes before using. This works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> though the flavor won't be identical.</p>

<h2>Storing buttermilk for air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Properly stored buttermilk lasts about two weeks refrigerated. Freeze leftover buttermilk in 1 cup (8.45 ounce) portions for future air fryer use. Thaw overnight in the fridge and shake well before measuring.
