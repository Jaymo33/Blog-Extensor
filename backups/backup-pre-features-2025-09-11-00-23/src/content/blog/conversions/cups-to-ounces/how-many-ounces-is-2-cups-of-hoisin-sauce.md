<h3>How hoisin sauce density affects measurements</h3>  
<p>Hoisin sauce is denser than water, which means 2 cups weigh more than the standard 16 ounces for liquids like broth or milk. Its thick, syrupy consistency means it packs more into each cup, making weight measurements more reliable. This is particularly important for air fryer cooking, where precise ingredient ratios impact cooking times and results.</p>  

<h2>Converting 2 cups of hoisin sauce to ounces</h2>  
<p>To convert 2 cups of hoisin sauce to ounces, use the standard weight of 8 ounces per cup, totalling 16 ounces. However, slight variations can occur depending on the brand or how tightly packed the sauce is. For absolute accuracy, especially in recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer ribs</a>, a kitchen scale is your best friend.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a scale, levelling off your measuring cups ensures consistency. Scoop the hoisin sauce into the cup and use a straight edge to remove excess. This method minimises discrepancies, whether you're making a glaze for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>air fryer vegetables</a> or a dipping sauce for spring rolls.</p>

<h2>Tips for working with hoisin sauce in the air fryer</h2>  
<p>Hoisin sauce can burn quickly due to its sugar content, so applying it in thin layers works best. For dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/beef>air fryer beef</a>, brush on the sauce during the last few minutes of cooking. This prevents charring while allowing the flavours to meld beautifully.</p>
