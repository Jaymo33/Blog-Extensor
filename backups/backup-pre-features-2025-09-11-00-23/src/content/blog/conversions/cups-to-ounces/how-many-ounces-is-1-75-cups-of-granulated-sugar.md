<h2>Why accurate sugar measurements matter in air fryer baking</h2>
<p>Air fryers cook food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens, making precise measurements crucial. Too much sugar can cause over-browning or even burning, while too little might leave your baked goods lacking in sweetness. For perfect results, always measure ingredients like granulated sugar by weight rather than volume. This is especially important for recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, where precision is key.</p>
<p>Granulated sugar’s density can vary slightly depending on how it’s packed, which is why weighing it in ounces gives you the most accurate measurement. If you’re converting 1.75 cups of granulated sugar to ounces for an air fryer recipe, using a kitchen scale is the best approach. This ensures your desserts come out perfectly every time, whether you’re making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a> or cookies.</p>

<h2>How to measure 1.75 cups of granulated sugar correctly</h2>
<p>To measure 1.75 cups of granulated sugar accurately, use the spoon-and-level method. Spoon the sugar into your measuring cup without packing it down, then level it off with a straight edge. This prevents over-measuring, which is common when scooping directly from the bag. For air fryer recipes, even small measurement errors can affect cooking times and results.</p>
<p>If you don’t have a 1.75-cup measure, you can use a combination of cups: 1 cup plus 3/4 cup. Alternatively, weigh out 12.25 ounces on a kitchen scale for perfect precision. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion charts</a> recommend using weight measurements for dry ingredients like sugar to ensure consistency across recipes.</p>
