<h2>How to Measure 110 ml of Corn Syrup</h2>
<p>Measuring 110 ml of corn syrup can be tricky if you don’t have a metric measuring cup. A standard US cup holds 240 ml, so 110 ml is just under half a cup. For UK bakers, a metric cup holds 250 ml, making 110 ml roughly 0.44 cups. Either way, it’s close enough to half a cup for most recipes.</p>
<p>If you’re using an air fryer, precision matters even more. Air fryers cook food quickly, and imbalances in ingredients can affect the outcome. For example, a glaze with too much corn syrup might caramelise too quickly, while too little could leave your dish lacking sweetness. <h3>Tools for Accurate Measurement</h3>
<p>A kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure 110 ml of corn syrup, as it accounts for density variations. However, if you’re using measuring cups, opt for a liquid measuring cup with clear markings. Pour the corn syrup slowly to avoid air bubbles, which can skew the measurement. For sticky ingredients like corn syrup, lightly greasing the cup can help it slide out easily.</p>
