<h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h2>  
<p>In the UK, a metric cup holds 250 ml, while a US cup is slightly smaller at 240 ml. This means 835 ml of condensed milk equals 3.3 UK cups or 3.5 US cups. The difference might seem minor, but it can impact recipes requiring exact ratios, like custards or caramel sauces.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Condensed Milk Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy. Pour the condensed milk slowly to avoid air bubbles, and level it off with a spatula. For sticky ingredients like condensed milk, lightly greasing the cup can help it slide out easily.</p>

<h3>Storing Leftover Condensed Milk</h3>  
<p>Transfer unused condensed milk to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze it in ice cube trays and thaw as needed. Avoid leaving it at room temperature, as it can spoil quickly.</p>
