<h3>How to Measure 535 ml of Olive Oil Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug with millilitre markings, use standard cup measurements. Since 1 cup is roughly 237 ml, 535 ml translates to just over 2 and a quarter cups. <h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>While the UK and US use slightly different cup sizes, the difference is minimal for most recipes. In the UK, 1 cup is approximately 284 ml, whereas in the US, it’s 237 ml. For 535 ml of olive oil, this means roughly 1.88 UK cups or 2.26 US cups. Unless precision is critical, either measurement will work in your air fryer.</p>

<p>Another mistake is using the wrong type of measuring cup. Liquid measuring cups have a spout for easy pouring, while dry measuring cups are designed for solids. <p>If you’ve measured out 535 ml of olive oil but only used part of it, store the rest properly to maintain freshness. Transfer it to an airtight container and keep it in a cool, dark place. Avoid exposing it to heat or sunlight, as this can degrade the oil’s quality over time.</p>
