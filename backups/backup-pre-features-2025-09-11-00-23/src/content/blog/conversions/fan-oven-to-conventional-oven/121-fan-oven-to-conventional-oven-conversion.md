<p>Converting 121°C in a fan oven to a conventional oven temperature is straightforward: it equals approximately 140°C in a standard oven. This adjustment accounts for the more efficient heat distribution of fan-assisted ovens. Whether you're baking cakes or roasting vegetables, understanding this conversion ensures your dishes turn out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. This means recipes designed for fan ovens often need temperature adjustments when using traditional ovens. The general rule is to increase the temperature by 20°C when converting from fan to conventional. So 121°C fan becomes 140°C conventional.</p>
<p>This difference is particularly important when using air fryers, which work similarly to fan ovens. <h3>Air Fryer Equivalent Temperatures</h3>
<p>Since air fryers use rapid air technology similar to fan ovens, their temperature settings are usually comparable. A 121°C fan oven recipe would typically use the same temperature in an air fryer. However, cooking times are often shorter in air fryers due to their compact size and efficient heating.</p>

<h2>Common Conversion Mistakes to Avoid</h2>
<p>One frequent error is forgetting that fan ovens cook faster as well as at lower temperatures. When converting 121 fan oven to conventional oven settings, you might need to reduce cooking time slightly even after adjusting the temperature. This is especially true for items like cookies or pastries.</p>
<p>Another pitfall is assuming all ovens are calibrated perfectly. It's worth investing in an oven thermometer to verify your temperatures. This is good practice whether you're using conventional ovens, fan ovens, or air fryers from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> recommendations.</p>

When converting 121°C fan recipes for poultry or beef in a conventional oven, stick close to the 140°C equivalent but monitor internal temperatures carefully.</p>
