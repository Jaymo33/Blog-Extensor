<h3>How to Measure 100g of Cream Cheese Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can estimate 100g of cream cheese using volume. One tablespoon of cream cheese weighs roughly 15g, so 100g is about 6.5 tablespoons. However, for best results, especially in air fryer recipes, we recommend using a digital scale for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Does Cream Cheese Type Affect the Weight?</h3>  
<p>Full-fat, low-fat, and whipped cream cheese all have slightly different densities, but the weight-to-volume ratio remains fairly consistent. 100g of any cream cheese type will still be close to 3.53 ounces, making this conversion reliable for most recipes.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Working with Cream Cheese in an Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Cream cheese can melt quickly in an air fryer, so it’s best used as a filling or mixed into batters. For example, stuffed mushrooms or jalapeño poppers benefit from a creamy centre. If you're making a cheesecake, ensure your air fryer is preheated for even cooking.</p>  

<p>Always use room-temperature cream cheese for smoother mixing. Cold cream cheese can lead to lumps, which might affect the texture of your dish. <h3>Storing Leftover Cream Cheese</h3>  
<p>After measuring out 100g, reseal the remaining cream cheese tightly to prevent drying. Store it in the fridge and use within a week for best quality. For longer storage, freeze in 100g portions and thaw in the fridge overnight before use.</p>
