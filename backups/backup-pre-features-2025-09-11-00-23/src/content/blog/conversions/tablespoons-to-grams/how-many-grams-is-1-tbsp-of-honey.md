<h2>How to measure 1 tbsp of honey in grams</h2>
<p>The simplest way to measure honey is using a digital kitchen scale. Place your mixing bowl or spoon on the scale, tare it to zero, then add honey until it reads 21 grams. If you don’t have scales, use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and level it off with a knife. Remember that honey’s stickiness can make volume measurements tricky, so scraping the spoon clean ensures accuracy. For sticky ingredients like honey, we recommend using a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>non-stick air fryer liner</a> to simplify cleanup afterwards.</p>

<h3>Converting other honey quantities</h3>
<p>If your recipe calls for multiple tablespoons, multiply accordingly: 2 tbsp = 42 grams, 3 tbsp = 63 grams, and so on. For smaller amounts, 1 teaspoon (tsp) of honey weighs about 7 grams. <h3>Storing and handling honey for best results</h3>
<p>Store honey in a cool, dry place to maintain its quality. If it crystallises, gently warm the jar in warm water to return it to liquid form. When measuring, warm honey flows more easily but doesn’t significantly affect the weight. Always use clean, dry utensils to prevent contamination. For easy cleanup after cooking with honey, wipe your air fryer basket while it’s still warm to remove any sticky residue before it hardens.</p>
