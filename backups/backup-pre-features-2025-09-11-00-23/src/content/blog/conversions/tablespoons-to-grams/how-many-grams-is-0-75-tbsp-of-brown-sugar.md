<p>0.75 tbsp of brown sugar equals approximately 9.4 grams. This conversion is essential for precise baking and cooking, especially when using an air fryer where measurements can make or break a recipe. Whether you're whipping up cookies or glazing meats, knowing the exact weight ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but they rely heavily on precise ingredient measurements. Too much or too little sugar can affect texture, browning, and even cooking times. For example, if you're making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer cookies</a>, 0.75 tbsp of brown sugar (9.4g) contributes to both sweetness and caramelisation. Even small deviations can lead to burnt edges or undercooked centres.</p>

Digital scales are your best friend here – they eliminate the guesswork of spooning and levelling tablespoons.</p>

<h2>How to measure 0.75 tbsp of brown sugar accurately</h2>
<p>Measuring 0.75 tbsp of brown sugar requires either a 3/4 tablespoon measure or simple maths with teaspoons. Since 1 tbsp equals 3 tsp, 0.75 tbsp is 2 1/4 teaspoons. However, for absolute precision, weighing 9.4 grams on a kitchen scale is foolproof. This method also accounts for how tightly packed the sugar is in your spoon.</p>

<h3>The science behind brown sugar density</h3>
<p>Brown sugar contains molasses, making it denser and moister than white sugar. This affects its weight per tablespoon – packed brown sugar weighs more than loosely spooned. Our 9.4g conversion assumes moderately packed sugar, the standard for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>. For sticky situations, lightly grease your measuring spoon first.</p>

<h3>Converting other brown sugar amounts</h3>
<p>If your recipe calls for different amounts, here's a quick reference: 1 tbsp brown sugar = 12.5g, so 0.5 tbsp = 6.25g and 1.5 tbsp = 18.75g. These conversions work for both light and dark brown sugar varieties, though dark brown sugar is slightly heavier due to extra molasses.</p>
