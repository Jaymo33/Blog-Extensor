<p>Rolled oats, also known as old-fashioned oats, have a specific density that affects their volume-to-weight ratio. Unlike liquid ingredients, oats can settle differently in a measuring cup, which is why weight measurements (grams) are often more accurate. When converting 150 grams of rolled oats to cups, it's important to note that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly - a detail that matters for air fryer recipes where precision counts.</p>

<h2>Converting 150g Rolled Oats to Different Cup Types</h2>
<p>When converting 150 grams of rolled oats to cups, you'll encounter different cup measurements around the world. The standard UK cup holds 250ml, while the US customary cup is about 240ml. This slight variation means 150 grams of rolled oats equals roughly 1.7 UK cups or 1.5 US cups. For metric cup users (250ml exactly), the conversion remains at approximately 1.7 cups.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring 150g Oats Without Scales</h3>
<p>If you don't have kitchen scales, you can still measure 150 grams of rolled oats using cups. Fill your cup loosely with oats, then level off the top with a straight edge. For UK measurements, you'll need about 1 and 3/4 cups, while US measurements require about 1 and 1/2 cups. This method works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast recipes</a> where slight variations won't dramatically affect the outcome.</p>
