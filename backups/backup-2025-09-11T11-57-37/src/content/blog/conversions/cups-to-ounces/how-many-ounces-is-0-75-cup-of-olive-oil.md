<h3>Converting cups to ounces for different oils</h3>  
<p>While 0.75 cup of olive oil equals 6 fluid ounces, other oils may vary slightly in weight due to density. For example, coconut oil is denser, so 0.75 cup would weigh closer to 6.5 ounces. Always check the specific type of oil you're using if precision is crucial. For air fryer cooking, sticking to fluid ounces (volume) is usually sufficient, but weight measurements can be helpful for baking.</p>  

<h2>How to measure 0.75 cup of olive oil without a scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup to measure 0.75 cup (6 fluid ounces) of olive oil. Fill the cup to the three-quarter mark, or use tablespoons—12 tablespoons equal 0.75 cup. For sticky oils like coconut oil, lightly grease the measuring cup first for easy release. <h3>Common mistakes when measuring olive oil</h3>  
<p>One common mistake is confusing fluid ounces (volume) with weight ounces. While 0.75 cup of olive oil is 6 fluid ounces, its weight is around 5.3 ounces due to density.
