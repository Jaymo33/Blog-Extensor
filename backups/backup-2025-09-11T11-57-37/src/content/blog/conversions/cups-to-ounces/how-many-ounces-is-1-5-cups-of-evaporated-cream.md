<h2>How to measure evaporated cream correctly</h2>
<p>When converting 1.5 cups of evaporated cream to ounces, using the right tools is key. A liquid measuring cup is best for accuracy, as it accounts for the cream's density. Scooping evaporated cream directly from the can with a dry measuring cup can lead to inconsistencies. For best results, level off the top with a straight edge.</p>
<p>If you don't have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale is a great alternative. Simply set it to ounces and pour until you reach 12 ounces (the equivalent of 1.5 cups). This method is especially useful for air fryer recipes where precision matters, like custards or creamy pasta bakes.</p>

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring evaporated cream</h3>
<p>One frequent error is confusing evaporated cream with condensed milk or regular cream. Evaporated cream is thicker and denser, so 1.5 cups will weigh more than other liquids. Another mistake is packing the cream into the cup, which can add extra ounces and throw off your recipe. Always measure lightly for accuracy.</p>
