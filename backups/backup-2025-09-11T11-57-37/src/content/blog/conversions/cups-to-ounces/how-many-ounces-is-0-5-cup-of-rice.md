<h3>Different Types of Rice and Their Weights</h3>
<p>It is important to note that the standard 3.5-ounce weight for 0.5 cup of rice typically refers to standard long-grain white rice. Other varieties can have different densities. Brown rice, for instance, is often a bit heavier and more dense due to its intact bran layer. Half a cup of uncooked brown rice can weigh closer to 3.75 ounces. Basmati and jasmine rice, known for their long, slender grains, might be slightly lighter, perhaps around 3.4 ounces for the same half-cup volume. Always check your specific recipe for guidance, as the type of rice specified will impact the final weight measurement.</p>

<h2>Step-by-Step Guide to Measuring 0.5 Cup of Rice</h2>
<p>The most accurate way to measure half a cup of rice for your air fryer recipes is to use a combination of volume and weight. First, take your dry measuring cups and gently spoon the rice into the half-cup measure. Avoid tapping the cup or packing the rice down, as this will compress the grains and give you more than intended. Instead, use the straight edge of a knife to level off the top, creating a perfectly measured half cup. This method ensures you are not accidentally adding extra rice that could throw off your recipe's balance. It is a simple technique that yields professional-level accuracy in your home kitchen.</p>
<p>For ultimate precision, place your measuring cup on a digital kitchen scale and tare it to zero. Then, add your levelled half cup of rice and note the weight. It should be very close to 3.5 ounces for white rice. If it is significantly different, you may need to adjust how you are filling your cup. This practice of double-checking is especially useful when you are trying a new recipe or working with a new type of rice.
