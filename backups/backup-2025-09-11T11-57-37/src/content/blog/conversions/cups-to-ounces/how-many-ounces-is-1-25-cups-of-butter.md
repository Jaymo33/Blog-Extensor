<h2>How to measure 1.25 cups of butter properly</h2>
<p>Measuring butter can be tricky since it comes in different forms - sticks, blocks, or tubs. For 1.25 cups (which is 10 ounces), you'll need 2.5 standard sticks of butter (as one stick typically equals ½ cup or 4 ounces). If using a block, you can weigh it on a kitchen scale for precision, which is particularly helpful for air fryer recipes where exact measurements matter.</p>
<p>When measuring without scales, use the markings on butter packaging. Most UK butter packages have measurements marked in grams and ounces, making it easy to portion out 10 ounces (283 grams). For tub butter, spoon it into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife, being careful not to pack it down as this can lead to using too much butter in your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> or other dishes.</p>

<h3>Converting other butter measurements</h3>
<p>While we've focused on 1.25 cups to ounces, you might need other conversions too. One cup of butter equals 8 ounces, so 1.25 cups is simply 1 cup plus ¼ cup (8 oz + 2 oz). This knowledge comes in handy when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer's capacity. Remember that these measurements apply to standard butter - reduced-fat varieties may have different weights.</p>

<h2>Butter measurement tips for air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>When using butter in air fryer recipes, consider that it cooks differently than in conventional ovens. The intense circulating heat means butter can brown or burn more quickly. For best results, measure your 1.25 cups (10 ounces) accurately and consider brushing rather than pouring melted butter onto foods to prevent pooling. This technique works wonders for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>air fryer potatoes</a> and other vegetables.</p>
<p>Another tip is to chill butter-measured portions before use in air fryer baking. Cold butter creates flakier pastries and better texture in items like scones or biscuits. After measuring your 10 ounces (1.25 cups), you might portion it into smaller amounts and refrigerate until needed. <h3>Common butter measurement mistakes to avoid</h3>
<p>One frequent error is confusing fluid ounces with weight ounces when measuring butter. While 1.25 cups of water would be 10 fluid ounces, butter is measured by weight, not volume. Another mistake is using melted butter when the recipe calls for solid - this can throw off measurements since melted butter packs differently in a measuring cup. Always check whether your air fryer recipe specifies melted or solid butter measurements.</p>
