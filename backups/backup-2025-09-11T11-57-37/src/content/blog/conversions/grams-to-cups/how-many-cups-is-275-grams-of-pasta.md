<h2>Why Pasta Shape Affects Cup Measurements</h2>  
<p>Different pasta shapes have varying densities, which means 275 grams won’t always fill the same cup volume. Compact shapes like elbow macaroni or shells pack tightly, resulting in more pasta per cup. Conversely, ridged or twisted pasta like rotini or cavatappi creates air gaps, reducing the cup measurement. For air fryer recipes, this matters because overcrowding can lead to unevenly cooked pasta. If you're prepping a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken pasta bake</a>, weighing your pasta ensures consistency.</p>

<h2>Converting 275 Grams of Pasta for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Air fryers excel at crispy pasta dishes, but portion control is key. Since 275 grams of pasta expands when cooked, it’s best to divide it into batches if your air fryer is small. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Pasta</h3>  
<p>Overpacking cups is the top error—scooping directly from the bag compresses pasta, leading to excess. Instead, spoon pasta into the cup lightly. Also, avoid using liquid measuring cups; their tapered shape distorts dry measurements. For air fryer success, err on the side of less pasta to prevent clumping. Lastly, remember that cooked pasta doubles in volume, so 275 grams dry yields about 550 grams cooked.</p>
