<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for 100 Grams of Water</h3>
<p>In the UK, 100 grams of water is roughly 0.42 cups, while in the US, it’s about 0.4 cups. This difference stems from varying cup sizes—a UK cup holds 284 ml, whereas a US cup is 240 ml. If you’re following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a>, always check which measurement system it uses to avoid mistakes.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 100 Grams of Water Without a Scale</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 100 grams of water accurately. Use a liquid measuring cup and fill it to the 0.4-cup (US) or 0.42-cup (UK) mark. For smaller quantities, a tablespoon holds about 15 grams of water, so roughly 6.5 tablespoons will get you close to 100 grams. This method works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion needs</a>.</p>
<p>Another handy trick is to use common household items. A standard shot glass holds about 44 grams of water, so two shot glasses plus a teaspoon will approximate 100 grams. While not as precise as a scale, these methods are great for quick fixes. Just remember that for baking, precision is key, so investing in a scale is worthwhile.</p>
