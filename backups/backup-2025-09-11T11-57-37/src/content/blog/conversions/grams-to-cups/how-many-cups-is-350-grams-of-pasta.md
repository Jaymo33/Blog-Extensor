<h3>How to Measure 350 Grams of Pasta Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use the cup method as a rough guide. A standard UK measuring cup holds about 100 grams of dry pasta, so 3.5 cups should get you close to 350 grams. For smaller shapes like macaroni, you might need slightly more. Always fluff the pasta lightly to avoid compacting it in the cup.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Pasta</h3>  
<p>One mistake is packing pasta tightly into cups, which leads to inaccurate measurements. Instead, lightly spoon the pasta into the cup and level it off. Another error is assuming all pasta weighs the same—long strands like linguine will weigh less per cup than small shapes like fusilli.</p>
