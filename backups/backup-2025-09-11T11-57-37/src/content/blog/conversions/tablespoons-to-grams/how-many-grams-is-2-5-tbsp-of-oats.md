<h3>Types of oats and their weights</h3>
<p>Not all oats weigh the same - rolled oats, quick oats, and steel-cut oats all have slightly different densities. Our 2.5 tbsp to 20g conversion applies to standard rolled oats, the most common type used in air fryer recipes. If using quick oats, you might find 2.5 tablespoons weighs about 18g, while steel-cut oats could be closer to 22g for the same volume.</p>

<h3>Tips for measuring oats without scales</h3>
<p>If you don't have scales, the spoon-and-level method works best for measuring 2.5 tbsp of oats. Use a proper tablespoon measure, scoop the oats, then level off the top with a straight edge. Avoid packing the oats down or tapping the spoon, as this compresses them and leads to inaccurate measurements that could affect your air fryer results.</p>
