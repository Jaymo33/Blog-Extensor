<h2>Why 475 F Works in Fan Ovens</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air for even cooking, similar to air fryers. The 475 F to fan oven conversion (220 C) creates the perfect environment for caramelisation without drying out food. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> specifically call for this temperature when cooking frozen foods or achieving restaurant-style crispness. The forced air circulation ensures heat penetrates every surface evenly, reducing cooking times by about 20% compared to conventional ovens.</p>

<p>When using this 475 F fan oven setting, always preheat your appliance for 3-5 minutes. This mimics the instant heat of an air fryer, giving you consistent results. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> shows this temperature works particularly well for breaded items and roasted potatoes, creating that golden crunch everyone loves.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times at 475 F</h3>
<p>Since fan ovens cook faster at 475 F (220 C), reduce typical air fryer times by 10-15%. For example, chicken wings that take 25 minutes in an air fryer will be done in 20-22 minutes. Always check food 5 minutes before the recommended time, especially when trying new <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> at this high temperature.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for 475 F in Fan Ovens</h2>
<p>This high heat setting excels for several air fryer-style preparations. Roasted vegetables like Brussels sprouts develop beautiful caramelisation at 475 F in a fan oven. Pizza reheats with a crisp base in just 4-5 minutes, while homemade chips achieve that perfect crunch. Even <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a> like churros benefit from this temperature for golden exteriors.</p>

<p>For frozen foods, the 475 F fan oven conversion works wonders on items like spring rolls or breaded fish. The rapid air circulation prevents sogginess while ensuring thorough cooking. Just remember to flip items halfway through, as you would in an air fryer. Our testing shows this maintains even crispness on all surfaces.</p>

<h3>Safety Tips for High-Temperature Cooking</h3>
<p>Always use oven-safe cookware at 475 F (220 C) in fan ovens. Avoid overcrowding the tray - leave space for air circulation just as you would in an air fryer basket. After cooking, let the oven cool before <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning</a> to prevent thermal shock to surfaces. Use oven mitts when handling trays, as they retain heat longer than air fryer baskets.</p>

This makes fan ovens the closest match to air fryer performance for most home cooks.</p>
