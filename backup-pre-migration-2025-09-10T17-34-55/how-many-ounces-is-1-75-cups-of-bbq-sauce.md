<h2>Understanding the Conversion</h2>  
<p>Converting 1.75 cups of BBQ sauce to ounces is straightforward once you know the basics. In the UK and US, 1 cup of liquid typically equals 8 fluid ounces, but BBQ sauce's thickness can slightly alter this. For accuracy, 1.75 cups of BBQ sauce weighs roughly 14 ounces, accounting for its density. This measurement is essential for recipes like air-fried chicken or ribs, where precise sauce quantities affect flavour and texture. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>If you're working with a UK recipe, rest assured that fluid ounces are consistent across regions for liquid measurements. However, weighing ingredients like BBQ sauce ensures consistency, especially when preparing larger batches. Many air fryer recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipes page</a> use ounces for precision, so this conversion will come in handy frequently.</p>  

<h3>Why Accuracy Matters in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but incorrect sauce measurements can lead to soggy or unevenly coated dishes. Using 14 ounces of BBQ sauce for 1.75 cups ensures your marinades and glazes adhere perfectly. Too much sauce can drip and cause smoke, while too little may leave your dish dry. For best results, measure your BBQ sauce by weight when possible, especially for popular <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Using BBQ Sauce in Your Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>BBQ sauce adds incredible flavour to air-fried dishes, but applying it correctly is key. For wings or ribs, lightly coat the meat with 1.75 cups (14 ounces) of sauce before cooking, then add more in the last few minutes for a sticky glaze. Avoid overloading the basket, as excess sauce can cause smoking. Preheating your air fryer ensures even cooking and helps the sauce caramelise beautifully.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring BBQ Sauce</h3>  
<p>One frequent error is using volume measurements (cups) instead of weight (ounces) for thick sauces like BBQ. A liquid measuring cup can give inconsistent results due to air pockets. For 1.75 cups of BBQ sauce, weighing out 14 ounces eliminates guesswork. Another mistake is not adjusting for recipe size—doubling a recipe requires doubling the sauce accurately to maintain flavour balance.</p>
