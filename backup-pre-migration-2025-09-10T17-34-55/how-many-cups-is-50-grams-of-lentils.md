<p>50 grams of lentils equals approximately 0.25 cups (or 1/4 cup) when measured uncooked. <h2>Why Accurate Lentil Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right measurement for lentils is crucial, especially in air fryer cooking where space and timing are key. Too many lentils can overcrowd the basket, leading to uneven cooking, while too few might dry out. For consistent results, stick to the 50 grams to 0.25 cups ratio. <h3>Types of Lentils and Their Measurements</h3>  
<p>Not all lentils weigh the same due to size and density variations. For example, red lentils are smaller and may pack slightly differently than green or brown lentils. However, 50 grams of any lentil type will roughly equal 0.25 cups when uncooked. If you're using pre-cooked lentils, note that their volume increases, so adjust accordingly.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 50 Grams of Lentils Without Scales</h2>  
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem! Use a standard measuring cup to approximate 50 grams of lentils. Fill the cup to just under the 1/4 mark for an accurate measure. For best results, fluff the lentils lightly with a spoon before measuring to avoid compaction. This method works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable-based air fryer dishes</a>.</p>  

<p>Another handy trick is using tablespoon conversions. Since 50 grams of lentils is about 4 tablespoons, you can use this as a quick reference. Keep in mind that this method is less precise but works in a pinch. For more measurement hacks, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion guides</a>.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Lentils</h3>  
<p>Avoid scooping lentils directly from the bag with the measuring cup, as this can lead to uneven amounts. Instead, spoon them gently into the cup for consistency. Also, remember that 50 grams of lentils in cups differs between the UK and USA due to slight cup size variations—always double-check your recipe’s origin.</p>
