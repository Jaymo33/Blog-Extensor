<h3>How to measure gelatin powder correctly</h3>
<p>Measuring gelatin powder accurately is simple if you follow a few basic steps. First, fluff the powder in its container to prevent clumping. Then, lightly spoon it into a measuring cup without packing it down. Level off the excess with a straight edge for an exact measurement. If you’re converting to ounces, use a kitchen scale for the most precise results. This method ensures you’re using the right amount of gelatin powder every time, whether you’re making a small batch or a large one.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with gelatin powder</h3>
<p>Gelatin powder can be tricky to work with if you’re not familiar with its properties. Always bloom it in cold water before adding it to hot liquids to ensure it dissolves properly. Avoid boiling gelatin, as this can weaken its setting properties. Stir gently to prevent air bubbles, which can affect the texture of your final dish.
