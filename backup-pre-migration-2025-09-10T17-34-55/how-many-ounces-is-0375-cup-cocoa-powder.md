<h2>Why accurate cocoa powder measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Cocoa powder is a key ingredient in many air fryer recipes, from cakes to cookies. Too much can make your bake bitter, while too little may leave it bland. <h3>How to measure 0.375 cup cocoa powder without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, spoon cocoa powder into your measuring cup and level it off. Avoid packing it down, as this can add extra weight. For 0.375 cup (1.5 oz), use three 1/8-cup measures or half of a 3/4-cup measure. This method works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common cocoa powder conversions for air fryers</h2>  
<p>Beyond 0.375 cup, here are other useful cocoa powder conversions: 1/4 cup = 1 oz, 1/2 cup = 2 oz, and 1 cup = 4 oz. These help scale recipes up or down for your air fryer. For more baking inspiration, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a>.</p>  

<p>Remember, UK and US cup sizes differ slightly, so always check your measuring tools. If a recipe calls for 0.375 cup cocoa powder (1.5 oz), stick to weight for accuracy. Air fryers excel with precise measurements, ensuring even cooking and perfect results every time.</p>  

<h3>Storing cocoa powder for air fryer use</h3>  
<p>Keep cocoa powder in an airtight container to maintain freshness. Humidity can clump it, making 0.375 cup measurements inconsistent. For long-term storage, freeze it in portions—just thaw before use. This ensures your 0.375 cup (1.5 oz) remains accurate for future bakes.</p>
