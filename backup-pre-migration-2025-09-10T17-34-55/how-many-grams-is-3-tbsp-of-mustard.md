<h2>Why accurate mustard measurements matter</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of mustard can make or break your air fryer dish. Too little, and your marinade might lack flavour; too much, and it could overpower the other ingredients. Mustard’s thickness and density mean volume measurements like tablespoons aren’t always precise. That’s why converting 3 tbsp of mustard into grams (45g) gives you consistency. <p>Mustard isn’t just a condiment—it’s a key ingredient in many air fryer recipes. From crispy chicken wings to glazed salmon, it adds tang and depth. If you’re working with a recipe that calls for grams, knowing that 3 tbsp equals 45g saves time and hassle. <h2>How to measure mustard for air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>For the most accurate results, use a digital kitchen scale to weigh your mustard. Scoop the mustard into a tablespoon, level it off, and then transfer it to the scale. Repeat until you’ve reached 45 grams (or 3 tbsp). This method eliminates guesswork and ensures your dish turns out as intended. If you don’t have a scale, spooning mustard into a measuring spoon works, but remember it’s less precise.</p>

<h3>Types of mustard and their weights</h3>
<p>Not all mustards weigh the same. Dijon, English, and whole-grain mustards have slightly different densities due to their textures. However, for most recipes, the 3 tbsp to 45g conversion holds true. If you’re using a particularly thick or runny mustard, adjust slightly. For example, American yellow mustard might be closer to 42g for 3 tbsp. When in doubt, weigh it!</p>
