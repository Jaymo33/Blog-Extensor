<h2>Why Convert 15g of Powdered Milk to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes, especially those for air fryers, use grams as the standard measurement. However, some kitchen scales or older recipes might list ingredients in ounces. Converting 15g of powdered milk to ounces (0.53 oz) ensures you’re using the right amount. This is particularly important for baking, where even small deviations can affect texture and taste. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurements.</p>  

<p>Powdered milk is a versatile ingredient, often used in air fryer recipes like breaded coatings or creamy desserts. Whether you’re making a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake</a> or a savoury dish, precise measurements matter. Too much or too little can alter the moisture content, leading to dry or overly dense results. That’s why knowing how to convert 15g of powdered milk to ounces is so useful.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 15g of Powdered Milk Accurately</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate measurement, use a digital kitchen scale set to grams. Scoop the powdered milk into a small bowl until the scale reads 15g. If you don’t have a scale, 15g is roughly two level tablespoons. However, weighing is always more precise, especially for air fryer recipes where consistency is key. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> for more tips on perfect measurements.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 15g of Powdered Milk in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Powdered milk is a staple in many air fryer recipes, from crispy coatings to fluffy baked goods. For example, 15g of powdered milk can be mixed with breadcrumbs for a golden, crunchy texture on chicken or fish. It’s also great for making instant custards or adding richness to <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a>. The key is measuring correctly to avoid clumping or uneven distribution.</p>  

<p>Another popular use is in homemade seasoning blends. A small amount, like 15g of powdered milk, can add a creamy depth to spice rubs for meats or vegetables. If you’re experimenting with new recipes, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> has plenty of inspiration. Just remember to convert measurements like 15g to ounces (0.53 oz) if your scale uses imperial units.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Storing Powdered Milk</h3>  
<p>To keep your powdered milk fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Exposure to moisture can cause clumping, making it harder to measure out 15g accurately. If you’re using powdered milk frequently in air fryer recipes, consider buying in smaller quantities to maintain freshness.
