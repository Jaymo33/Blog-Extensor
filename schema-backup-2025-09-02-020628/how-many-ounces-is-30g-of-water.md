<h2>How to measure 30g of water accurately</h2>
<p>Measuring 30g of water doesn’t require scales if you know the volume equivalents. One tablespoon of water weighs about 15g, so two tablespoons will give you 30g. Alternatively, 30ml of water also equals 30g, as water’s density is 1g per ml. For air fryer recipes, using a measuring jug with both metric and imperial markings can simplify the process. This ensures you get the exact 1.06 ounces needed without fuss.</p>
