<h2>Why Accurate Cream Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of cream can make or break a recipe. Too little, and your dish might lack richness; too much, and it could become overly heavy or even split. For air fryer recipes, precise measurements ensure even cooking and perfect texture. Whether you're making a creamy pasta sauce or a luscious dessert, knowing that 1.5 cups of cream is 355 ml helps eliminate guesswork.</p>

This is especially true for baking, where precision is key to achieving the right consistency and flavour.</p>

<h2>How to Measure Cream Correctly</h2>
<p>Measuring cream accurately starts with using the right tools. A liquid measuring cup with clear ml and cup markings is ideal for converting 1.5 cups of cream to 355 ml. Pour the cream slowly to avoid air bubbles, and check the measurement at eye level for accuracy. If you're using a scale, remember that 1 ml of cream weighs roughly 1 gram, making conversions straightforward.</p>
<p>For air fryer cooking, where ingredients need to be evenly distributed, precise measurements prevent overloading or underfilling. Whether you're making a creamy dip or a rich sauce, knowing the exact ml equivalent of 1.5 cups of cream ensures your dish turns out perfectly every time. Check out the <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>

<h3>US vs UK Cup Measurements</h3>
<p>It's worth noting that US and UK cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup is 284 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. However, for cream, the difference is minimal, and 1.5 cups will still roughly equate to 355 ml in both systems. Always double-check your recipe's origin to ensure accuracy.</p>

<h3>Substitutes for Cream in Recipes</h3>
<p>If you're out of cream, you can substitute it with evaporated milk, coconut cream, or even Greek yogurt mixed with a bit of milk. However, keep in mind that these alternatives may alter the texture and flavour of your dish. For best results, stick to the original measurement of 1.5 cups (355 ml) of cream when possible.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Leftover Cream</h2>
<p>If you have leftover cream after measuring out 1.5 cups (355 ml), store it in an airtight container in the fridge. It typically lasts 3-5 days, but always check for off smells or curdling before use. For longer storage, freeze it in ice cube trays and thaw as needed. This way, you'll always have the right amount on hand for your next air fryer creation.</p>
