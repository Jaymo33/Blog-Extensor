<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve the best results. Too much or too little tomato paste can affect the texture and flavour of your dish. For example, if you’re making a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>spicy tomato chicken</a>, the right amount of tomato paste ensures a balanced taste. Accurate measurements also help you avoid waste, as you won’t end up with leftover ingredients.</p>
<p>Using the correct amount of tomato paste is especially important in air fryer recipes because the cooking process is faster and more intense. Unlike traditional ovens, air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, which can amplify flavours. A slight miscalculation in measurements could lead to an overpowering taste or a dish that’s too dry. That’s why knowing how to convert 1.66 cups of tomato paste to ounces is so valuable.</p>

<h2>How to measure tomato paste for air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Measuring tomato paste can be tricky due to its thick consistency. Here’s a simple method: use a dry measuring cup for solids like tomato paste. Scoop the paste into the cup and level it off with a straight edge for accuracy. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale is your best friend. Simply weigh out 13.28 ounces, and you’re good to go.</p>
<p>For those who prefer visual cues, one standard can of tomato paste is roughly 6 ounces, so 1.66 cups would be a little over two cans. This is handy when you’re in a pinch and need to eyeball measurements. Remember, consistency is key in air fryer cooking, so always aim for precision. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>

<h3>Tips for storing leftover tomato paste</h3>
<p>If you have leftover tomato paste after measuring out 1.66 cups, proper storage is key. Transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze it in ice cube trays—each cube is roughly one tablespoon, making future measurements a breeze. This way, you’ll always have the right amount on hand for your next air fryer creation.</p>
