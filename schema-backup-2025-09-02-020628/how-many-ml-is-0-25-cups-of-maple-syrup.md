<h2>Why Accurate Maple Syrup Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of maple syrup can make or break your air fryer dishes. Too little, and your glaze might lack sweetness; too much, and it could burn or overpower other flavours. <p>Maple syrup’s viscosity means it doesn’t pour like water, so measuring by volume (cups or ml) is more reliable than weight. If you’re working with sticky ingredients, a good trick is to lightly grease your measuring cup or spoon for easier pouring. This small step can save you time and mess in the kitchen.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.25 Cups of Maple Syrup Without a Measuring Cup</h3>  
<p>Don’t have a measuring cup? No problem! You can use a standard tablespoon to measure out 0.25 cups of maple syrup. Since 1 tablespoon holds about 15 ml, you’ll need roughly 4 tablespoons to reach 59 ml. For even smaller increments, a teaspoon (5 ml) can help fine-tune your measurement. This method works well for air fryer recipes where precision is key, like glazes or marinades.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Maple Syrup</h3>  
<p>One mistake people make is assuming all liquid sweeteners measure the same. For example, 0.25 cups of honey isn’t identical in weight or volume to maple syrup.
