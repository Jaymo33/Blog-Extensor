<h2>Understanding Cup Measurements for Single Cream</h2>  
<p>Single cream is a staple in many recipes, from desserts to savoury dishes. The difference between UK and US cup sizes can be confusing, but it’s crucial for accuracy. In the UK, a standard cup holds 250 ml, while in the US, it’s 236.59 ml. This means 660 ml of single cream translates to roughly 2.8 UK cups or 2.79 US cups. If you’re using an air fryer for recipes like custards or creamy pasta bakes, precise measurements matter.</p>  

<p>For those who love baking, single cream is often used in cakes, scones, and even air fryer-friendly desserts. A slight miscalculation can affect texture and taste. <p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but they rely on precise ingredient ratios. Too much or too little single cream can throw off the consistency of your dish. If you’re experimenting with air fryer recipes, always measure your 660 ml of single cream carefully to avoid disappointments.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Single Cream Without a Jug</h3>

While this method isn’t as precise, it works in a pinch. For best results, though, invest in a good-quality measuring jug—especially if you’re a frequent air fryer user.</p>
