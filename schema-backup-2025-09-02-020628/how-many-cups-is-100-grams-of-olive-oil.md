<h2>Why Accurate Olive Oil Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of olive oil in your air fryer affects texture, crispiness, and even cooking times. Too little oil, and your food may dry out; too much, and it can become greasy. For example, when making crispy roasted potatoes, 100 grams of olive oil (0.44 cups) coats evenly without excess. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> helps streamline these calculations for all your favourite dishes.</p>  
<p>Olive oil is a staple in Mediterranean and healthy cooking, often used in air fryers for its high smoke point and rich flavour. Whether you're preparing a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable medley</a> or marinating chicken, precise measurements ensure balanced flavours. A slight variation in oil quantity can alter the outcome, especially in baking recipes where ratios are critical.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 100 Grams of Olive Oil Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup to approximate 0.44 cups (about 7 tablespoons) for 100 grams of olive oil. Note that olive oil’s density means it won’t fill a cup the same way water does—always measure at eye level for accuracy. For more tips, check our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> that rely on perfect oil ratios.</p>  

<h2>Converting 100 Grams of Olive Oil for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>Air fryer recipes often list oil in grams or millilitres, but cups are more familiar to many home cooks. For instance, 100 grams of olive oil works perfectly in a batch of air-fried <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken wings</a>, ensuring crisp skin without dripping excess grease. Remember, 0.44 cups is the standard conversion, but slight adjustments may be needed based on oil viscosity or brand.</p>  
<p>When adapting traditional oven recipes for the air fryer, reducing oil by 10–20% is common due to the appliance’s efficiency. So if a recipe calls for 100 grams (0.44 cups), you might use 80 grams instead. <h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for Olive Oil</h3>  
<p>While 100 grams of olive oil equals 0.44 US cups, UK cups are slightly larger (0.42 UK cups). For most air fryer recipes, the difference is negligible, but for precision baking, stick to one measurement system.
