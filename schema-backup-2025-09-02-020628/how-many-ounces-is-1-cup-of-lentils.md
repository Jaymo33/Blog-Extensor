Whether you're cooking a hearty lentil stew or crispy air-fried lentil snacks, knowing the exact weight helps with portion control and recipe accuracy. <h2>Why Accurate Lentil Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct weight for 1 cup of lentils ensures your recipes turn out as intended. Too many lentils can overcrowd your air fryer basket, leading to uneven cooking, while too few might leave your dish lacking in texture. For air fryer recipes like crispy lentil falafels or spiced lentil bites, 7.05 ounces (1 cup) is the ideal starting point. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips. Whether you're following a UK or US recipe, this conversion keeps things simple.</p>  

<p>Lentils expand when cooked, so measuring them dry is crucial. If you’re preparing a batch for meal prep, knowing that 1 cup of lentils equals 7.05 ounces helps with scaling recipes. For example, doubling a recipe means you’ll need 14.1 ounces (2 cups) of dry lentils. This precision is especially useful for air fryer cooking, where space and timing are key. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>lentil-based recipes</a> to put this knowledge to delicious use.</p>  

<h3>Converting 1 Cup of Lentils to Ounces</h3>  
<p>The standard conversion for 1 cup of lentils to ounces is 7.05 oz, but slight variations can occur based on lentil type. Green and brown lentils are denser, while red lentils may weigh slightly less due to their split nature. For UK cooks, this conversion remains consistent, making it easy to follow recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> or other sources. If you’re using a kitchen scale, simply measure out 7.05 ounces for perfect results every time.</p>  

<h2>How to Use Lentils in Your Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Lentils are incredibly versatile in the air fryer. From crispy snacks to protein-packed additions to salads, their mild flavor and quick cooking time make them a staple. Start by rinsing 1 cup (7.05 oz) of lentils, then pat them dry before tossing with oil and seasonings. Air fry at 180°C for 10–12 minutes, shaking the basket halfway for even crispiness. For inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable-based recipes</a>, where lentils often star.</p>  

<p>Pre-soaking lentils isn’t necessary for air frying, but it can reduce cooking time slightly. If you’re making lentil burgers or patties, combine 1 cup (7.05 oz) of cooked lentils with breadcrumbs and spices before air frying. This method ensures a crispy exterior and tender interior. Don’t forget to clean your air fryer afterward—our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> has all the tips you need.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Lentils</h3>  
<p>One frequent error is packing lentils into the measuring cup, which can lead to an inaccurate weight. Instead, lightly spoon lentils into the cup and level it off for a true 7.05-ounce measurement. Another mistake is confusing dry and cooked weights—1 cup of cooked lentils weighs roughly 12 ounces, so always check the recipe’s requirements. For air fryer cooking, sticking to the dry measurement (7.05 oz per cup) ensures consistency.</p>
