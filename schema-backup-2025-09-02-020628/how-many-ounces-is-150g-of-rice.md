<h3>How to measure 150g of rice without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have kitchen scales, you can still measure 150g of rice using a measuring cup. One UK metric cup holds about 180g of uncooked rice, so 150g is just under a cup. Remember, this method is less precise than weighing, but it works in a pinch. For best results, invest in a digital scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking.</p>

<p>150g of rice (5.29 oz) is a versatile quantity for many air fryer dishes. It’s perfect for making two servings of fried rice, stuffing vegetables, or creating crispy rice cakes. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable-based recipes</a>, we often use this amount for stuffed peppers or mushrooms. The air fryer gives rice a lovely texture, especially when combined with other ingredients.</p>

<h3>Does rice type affect the 150g to ounces conversion?</h3>  
<p>While 150g always equals 5.29 ounces, different rice types have varying volumes. For example, 150g of basmati rice fills more space than short-grain rice. This doesn’t change the weight conversion but may affect how you measure it. When adapting recipes, stick to weight measurements for accuracy, especially in air fryer cooking where space is limited.</p>
