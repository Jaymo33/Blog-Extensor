<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve the perfect texture and flavour. Too much or too little oil can affect crispiness, browning, and even cooking times. For example, 5g of vegetable oil (0.18 ounces) is often used to lightly coat vegetables or proteins before air frying. This small amount ensures even cooking without excess grease. If you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, sticking to the recommended measurements guarantees the best outcome.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those for crispy potatoes or chicken wings, call for minimal oil. Converting 5g of vegetable oil to ounces (0.18 oz) helps you avoid over-pouring, which can lead to soggy results. A kitchen scale is the most accurate tool for this, but if you don't have one, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> can be a lifesaver. Remember, precision is key when working with compact appliances like air fryers.</p>  

<h2>How to measure 5g of vegetable oil without a scale</h2>  
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 5g of vegetable oil (0.18 ounces) using common household items. One teaspoon of vegetable oil weighs roughly 4.5g, so a slightly heaped teaspoon will get you close to 5g. For UK cooks, this is particularly handy when following recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>our vegetable section</a>, where oil measurements are often small.</p>

<h2>Common uses for 5g of vegetable oil in air fryers</h2>  
<p>Five grams of vegetable oil (0.18 ounces) is perfect for lightly coating foods before air frying. For example, tossing sweet potato fries or Brussels sprouts in this amount ensures they crisp up without becoming greasy. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we recommend this quantity for most vegetable-based dishes to achieve that golden crunch.</p>  

<p>Another great use is for marinating proteins like chicken or fish. A small amount of oil helps spices adhere while keeping the dish moist. If you're preparing a batch of air-fried cod or shrimp from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/mixed-seafood>seafood category</a>, 5g of oil is often all you need. <p>Some air fryers require slightly more or less oil depending on their design.
