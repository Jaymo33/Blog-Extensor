<p>50g of condensed milk equals approximately 1.76 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements can make or break your dish. Whether you're baking a cake or making a sweet glaze, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why converting 50g condensed milk to ounces matters</h2>
<p>When using your air fryer, accurate measurements are crucial for consistent cooking. Many UK recipes use grams, while some appliances or measuring tools display ounces. Converting 50g of condensed milk to ounces (1.76 oz) helps you follow recipes precisely. This is especially important for desserts where texture and sweetness depend on exact quantities.</p>

<h2>How to measure 50g condensed milk accurately</h2>
<p>For perfect results, use digital kitchen scales set to grams when measuring condensed milk. If your scales only show ounces, look for 1.76 oz to get 50g. This small quantity is common in recipes like air fryer cheesecakes or sticky toffee puddings where condensed milk adds richness.</p>
<p>When measuring sticky ingredients like condensed milk, lightly grease your measuring spoon or cup first. This helps the milk slide out cleanly, giving you the exact 50g (1.76 oz) needed. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> for inspiration on using condensed milk in your cooking.</p>

<h3>Common uses for 50g condensed milk in air fryers</h3>
<p>This small amount of condensed milk works perfectly in many air fryer desserts. Try it in banoffee pies, fudge, or as a glaze for air-fried doughnuts. The 50g (1.76 oz) measurement creates just enough sweetness without overpowering other flavours.</p>

<h2>Converting other condensed milk quantities</h2>
<p>Once you've mastered converting 50g condensed milk to ounces (1.76 oz), you can easily scale recipes up or down. For example, 100g equals 3.53 oz, while 25g is 0.88 oz. These conversions help when adjusting portion sizes in your air fryer.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with condensed milk</h3>
<p>Always store condensed milk in a cool, dry place and check the expiry date before use. For easy measuring of 50g (1.76 oz), warm the can slightly in warm water to make pouring simpler.
