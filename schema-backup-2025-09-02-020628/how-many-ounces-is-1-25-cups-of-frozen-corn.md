<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but portion sizes can affect the outcome. Too much or too little of an ingredient, like frozen corn, might lead to uneven cooking or texture issues. For example, overcrowding the basket with excess corn can result in steaming rather than crisping. On the other hand, too little might burn before achieving the desired doneness. That's why converting 1.25 cups of frozen corn to ounces (10 oz) helps maintain consistency.</p>

<h3>How to measure frozen corn correctly</h3>  
<p>When measuring frozen corn, it's best to use a dry measuring cup for accuracy. Lightly fill the cup without packing the kernels down, then level it off with a straight edge. If your recipe calls for ounces, a kitchen scale is the most precise tool. For 1.25 cups of frozen corn, you'll need about 10 ounces, but slight variations can occur depending on the corn's density.</p>  

<h2>Best ways to use frozen corn in an air fryer</h2>  
<p>Frozen corn is a fantastic ingredient for air fryer cooking because it retains moisture while crisping up nicely. You can toss it with a bit of oil and seasoning for a simple side dish or mix it into other recipes. For example, try adding it to a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable medley</a> or using it in a Mexican-inspired dish. The air fryer locks in flavour while giving a slight char, making frozen corn taste freshly grilled.</p>

<h3>Storing and reheating frozen corn in the air fryer</h3>  
<p>If you have leftover cooked corn, the air fryer is an excellent way to reheat it without losing texture. Spread the corn in a single layer and heat at 180°C for 3-5 minutes.
