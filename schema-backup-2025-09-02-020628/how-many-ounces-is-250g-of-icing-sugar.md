<h2>Why Convert 250g of Icing Sugar to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, while others rely on ounces, especially older or international recipes. Converting 250g of icing sugar to ounces (8.82 oz) helps avoid measurement mishaps. This is particularly useful for air fryer baking, where precision affects texture and taste. If you’re exploring <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, accurate measurements are key to perfect results.</p>  

<p>Icing sugar, also known as powdered sugar, is finer than granulated sugar. This means its weight-to-volume ratio differs, making conversions like 250g to ounces crucial. Whether you’re dusting desserts or making glaze, getting the amount right matters. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 250g of Icing Sugar Accurately</h3>  
<p>For the best results, use a digital kitchen scale to measure 250g of icing sugar. If you don’t have one, spoon the sugar into a measuring cup and level it off. Remember, 250g of icing sugar is roughly 8.82 ounces, but slight variations can occur due to packing density. For air fryer baking, consistency is key, so always aim for precision.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 250g of Icing Sugar</h2>  
<p>250g of icing sugar is a common quantity in many recipes. From buttercream frosting to air fryer cookies, this amount is versatile. If you’re making a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake in your air fryer</a>, 250g of icing sugar could be the base for your glaze or topping. It’s also perfect for dusting desserts like doughnuts or brownies.</p>  

<p>Another popular use is in royal icing for decorating biscuits or gingerbread houses. Since air fryers can bake small batches efficiently, 250g of icing sugar is often just the right amount. For more inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer dessert recipes</a> to put your perfectly measured icing sugar to good use.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Storing Icing Sugar</h3>  
<p>Keep your icing sugar fresh by storing it in an airtight container. Humidity can cause clumping, which affects measurements like 250g to ounces. If your sugar hardens, sieve it before use to restore its fine texture. Proper storage ensures your air fryer bakes turn out light and fluffy every time.</p>
