<h3>How to measure ginger paste without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 20g of ginger paste using tablespoons. Roughly, 20g equals about 1.5 tablespoons, but this can vary slightly depending on the paste's consistency. For best results, invest in a digital scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking. <h3>Storing ginger paste for freshness</h3>  
<p>To keep ginger paste fresh, store it in an airtight container in the fridge. It typically lasts up to 3 weeks, but you can also freeze it in ice cube trays for longer storage. This way, you’ll always have the right amount—whether you need 20g or just a teaspoon—for your air fryer creations.</p>
