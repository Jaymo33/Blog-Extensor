<p>2 cups of plain flour equals approximately 8.8 ounces (250 grams) in the UK. This conversion is essential for precise baking, especially when using an air fryer where measurements matter for perfect texture and consistency. Whether you’re whipping up air fryer cakes or crispy coatings, knowing the exact weight ensures your recipes turn out just right. <h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct weight for flour is crucial because volume measurements can vary. A loosely packed cup of plain flour weighs less than a tightly packed one, leading to inconsistent results. In air fryer cooking, precise measurements help avoid overly dense or dry outcomes. For example, air fryer cookies or cakes rely on the right flour-to-liquid ratio. If you’re exploring new <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, always measure by weight for reliability.</p>  

<p>Many UK bakers prefer weighing ingredients for accuracy, especially in air fryer baking where even small deviations can affect texture. <h2>How to measure 2 cups of plain flour correctly</h2>  
<p>To ensure you’re getting the right 8.8 ounces (250g) from 2 cups of plain flour, spoon the flour into the measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid scooping directly from the bag, as this compacts the flour and leads to excess. This method is particularly important for air fryer batters and doughs, where precision ensures even cooking.</p>  

<h3>Using a kitchen scale for better accuracy</h3>  
<p>For the most reliable results, skip the cups and weigh your flour directly. Place your mixing bowl on a digital scale, tare it to zero, then add flour until you reach 8.8 ounces (250g). This eliminates guesswork and guarantees consistency, whether you’re making air fryer bread or coating chicken for crispy tenders.</p>
