This calculation gives you 13.778875 pounds, which you can round to 13.78 pounds for practical use. Remember that this conversion applies to the weight of the prawns themselves, regardless of whether they're raw, cooked, or frozen. <h2>Tips for Cooking Prawns in the Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Cooking prawns in the air fryer requires a few simple techniques to ensure perfection every time. Pat the prawns dry before seasoning to help them crisp up rather than steam. Lightly oiling them promotes browning and prevents sticking, especially if you're using a marinade. Avoid overcrowding the basket to allow hot air to circulate freely around each prawn, which is crucial when working with larger quantities like 6.25 kg prawns to pounds. Shake the basket halfway through cooking for even results, and always check for doneness a minute early to avoid overcooking.</p>
<p>Seasoning plays a key role in enhancing the natural flavour of prawns. Simple combinations like salt, pepper, and lemon work wonderfully, but you can also experiment with spices like paprika, cumin, or Old Bay seasoning. For a richer taste, toss the prawns in melted butter or olive oil with minced garlic before air frying. After cooking, let them rest for a minute or two to allow the juices to redistribute. <h2>Buying and Storing Prawns</h2>
<p>When purchasing prawns, whether you're buying 6.25 kg or a smaller amount, quality matters. Look for prawns that smell fresh and oceanic, not fishy or ammonia-like. They should have firm flesh and a translucent appearance, with no black spots or discolouration. If buying frozen, ensure the packaging is intact with no signs of freezer burn. Once home, store prawns in the coldest part of your fridge and use them within one to two days. For longer storage, keep them frozen until ready to use, then thaw overnight in the fridge or under cold running water.</p>
<p>Proper storage maintains the texture and flavour of prawns, especially important when working with larger quantities like 6.25 kg prawns to pounds. If you've bought in bulk, consider dividing them into portion-sized bags before freezing for easier use later. Label each bag with the weight and date to keep track of your inventory. When thawing, avoid using warm water or the microwave, as this can lead to uneven cooking and toughness.
