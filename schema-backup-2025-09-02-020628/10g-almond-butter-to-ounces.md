<h2>Why Convert 10g of Almond Butter to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but some kitchen scales or international recipes might list ounces instead. Converting 10g of almond butter to ounces (0.35 oz) helps you follow recipes accurately. This is especially useful for air fryer cooking, where ingredient ratios can affect texture and cooking times. If you're prepping a batch of almond butter energy balls, precise measurements ensure consistency. Explore more <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> that use almond butter for inspiration.</p>  

<p>Almond butter is a versatile ingredient, whether you're making sauces, desserts, or snacks. A 10g portion (0.35 oz) is roughly a tablespoon, but weighing it guarantees accuracy. For air fryer users, this precision prevents over- or under-measuring, which can impact browning and crispiness. Need to scale up? Just multiply the conversion—20g would be 0.7 oz, and so on. <h3>How to Measure 10g of Almond Butter Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, 10g of almond butter is about a level tablespoon. However, this can vary depending on the brand’s thickness. For air fryer recipes, we recommend weighing for consistency—especially with sticky ingredients like almond butter. A slight excess could make cookies spread too much or sauces too thick. <p>From breakfast to desserts, 10g (0.35 oz) of almond butter adds richness without overpowering. Try it in air-fried banana oats or as a glaze for chicken skewers. <h3>Storage Tips for Almond Butter</h3>  
<p>To keep almond butter fresh, store it in a cool, dark place after opening. If you’ve measured out 10g (0.35 oz), seal the jar tightly to prevent oil separation. For air fryer users, pre-portioned almond butter saves time during meal prep. Stir well before each use to maintain consistency for accurate measuring.</p>
