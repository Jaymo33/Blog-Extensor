<h2>Understanding Flour Measurements</h2>  
<p>Measuring flour by weight (grams) is more accurate than using cups, as volume can vary based on how the flour is packed. For 475 grams of plain flour, the UK cup measurement is slightly larger than the US standard. This difference is crucial when following recipes from different regions. If you're using an air fryer for baking, precise measurements ensure even cooking and perfect texture. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies these calculations for you.</p>  

<p>Many bakers prefer weighing ingredients to avoid inconsistencies. Scooping flour directly from the bag can compress it, leading to more flour than intended. For 475 grams of plain flour, spooning it lightly into a measuring cup and levelling it off gives the most accurate result. This method is especially helpful for air fryer recipes, where even small deviations can affect the outcome. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, one cup of plain flour weighs about 125 grams, meaning 475 grams equals roughly 3.8 cups. In the US, a cup is around 132 grams, so 475 grams converts to approximately 3.6 cups. This distinction is vital for international recipes or when using imported ingredients. If you're adapting a traditional recipe for your air fryer, always double-check the measurements for consistency.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h3>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes when measuring flour is tapping or shaking the cup, which compacts the flour and leads to excess. For 475 grams of plain flour, always use the spoon-and-level method for consistency. Another error is using liquid measuring cups for dry ingredients, as they aren't designed for accurate flour measurements.
