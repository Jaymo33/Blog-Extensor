<h2>Understanding Lime Juice Measurements</h2>
<p>Lime juice is a common ingredient in many air fryer recipes, from marinades to desserts. Measuring it correctly ensures your dishes have the right balance of acidity and flavour. The difference between UK and US cup measurements can be confusing, but we’ve got you covered. For 185 ml of lime juice, the US standard cup (240 ml) gives you roughly 0.78 cups, while the UK metric cup (250 ml) results in about 0.65 cups. This small difference matters in recipes where precision is key, like baking or sauces.</p>

<p>Baking with lime juice, such as in key lime pies or citrus cakes, requires exact measurements. 185 ml of lime juice translates to just under 3/4 of a US cup or roughly 2/3 of a UK cup. <h3>Tips for Measuring Lime Juice</h3>
<p>To measure 185 ml of lime juice accurately, use a liquid measuring cup with clear markings. Freshly squeezed lime juice is ideal for the best flavour, but bottled juice works too. If your recipe calls for 185 ml of lime juice and you’re using a US cup, fill it to just below the 3/4 mark. For UK cups, aim for slightly less than 2/3. Always level off the measurement for consistency.</p>
