<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect ingredient quantities can lead to uneven cooking or soggy results. For example, using too much mayonnaise in a coating can make food greasy, while too little might leave it dry. Converting 10g of mayonnaise to ounces (0.35 oz) helps maintain balance, especially in recipes like crispy chicken or loaded potato skins. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>  

<p>Mayonnaise is a staple in many air fryer recipes, from dips to marinades. Its weight can vary slightly depending on brand and density, but 10g remains a reliable benchmark. If you're preparing a batch of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken</a>, this measurement ensures your coating is spot-on. Precision is key, and our guide takes the guesswork out of your cooking.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 10g of mayonnaise without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a measuring spoon to approximate 10g of mayonnaise. A level tablespoon holds about 15g, so two-thirds of a tablespoon will give you roughly 10g (or 0.35 oz). For thicker mayo, compact it slightly for accuracy. This method works well for recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer sauces</a>, where small adjustments matter.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 10g of mayonnaise in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Ten grams of mayonnaise might seem small, but it’s perfect for adding richness without overpowering a dish. Mix it with breadcrumbs for a crispy coating on fish or tofu, or blend it into dips for a creamy texture. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a> often use this amount to enhance flavour without excess calories. It’s also ideal for binding ingredients in stuffed mushrooms or mini frittatas.</p>  

<p>For those who love meal prep, 10g of mayonnaise per portion keeps things light but tasty. Try it in <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep wraps</a> or as a base for herb spreads. Since air fryers cook quickly, precise measurements prevent waste and ensure even results. Remember, 10g converts to 0.35 oz—a little goes a long way!</p>  

<h3>Converting other mayonnaise amounts for air fryer cooking</h3>  
<p>Need to scale up? Double 10g to 20g (0.7 oz) for larger batches, or halve it for subtle touches.
