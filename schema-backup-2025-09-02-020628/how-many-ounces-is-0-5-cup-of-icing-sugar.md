<h2>How many ounces is 0.5 cup of icing sugar?</h2>
<p>0.5 cup of icing sugar equals approximately 2 ounces. This conversion is essential for precise baking, especially when using an air fryer where measurements matter. Whether you’re whipping up a cake or dusting desserts, knowing the exact weight ensures perfect results. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we focus on accuracy to help you bake with confidence. Keep reading for more tips on measuring icing sugar for your air fryer recipes.</p>

<p>Converting cups to ounces can be tricky, but icing sugar is a common ingredient that needs precise measurement. A 0.5 cup of icing sugar typically weighs around 2 ounces, but this can vary slightly based on how packed the sugar is. For air fryer baking, where consistency is key, using a kitchen scale is the best way to ensure accuracy. If you don’t have one, fluffing the sugar before measuring can help. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurements.</p>

<h2>Tips for measuring icing sugar</h2>
<p>When measuring 0.5 cup of icing sugar, it’s best to use the spoon-and-level method. This means spooning the sugar into the measuring cup and leveling it off with a knife. Avoid packing the sugar down, as this can lead to more than 2 ounces per 0.5 cup. For air fryer recipes, consistency is crucial, so take your time with measurements. If you’re unsure, weigh the sugar for the most accurate result.</p>

<p>Another tip is to sift the icing sugar before measuring. This breaks up any lumps and ensures a lighter, more even measurement. Whether you’re making a glaze for air fryer cookies or frosting for a cake, sifted sugar blends more smoothly. For more baking tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> designed specifically for air fryers.</p>
