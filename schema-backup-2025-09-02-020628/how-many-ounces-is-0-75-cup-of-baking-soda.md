<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Using the correct amount of baking soda is crucial for air fryer recipes. Too much can leave a bitter taste, while too little might not give the desired rise. For example, if you're making air fryer cookies, 0.75 cup of baking soda (6 ounces) is often the perfect amount. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes</a> section for more inspiration.</p>
<p>Baking soda also plays a role in cleaning your air fryer. A mix of 0.75 cup baking soda and water can help remove stubborn grease. This simple solution is part of our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a> guide, ensuring your appliance stays in top condition.</p>

<h2>How to measure 0.75 cup of baking soda accurately</h2>
<p>Measuring 0.75 cup of baking soda correctly ensures your recipes turn out perfectly. Use a dry measuring cup and level it off with a straight edge for precision. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale can help you weigh out 6 ounces instead. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> has more handy tips like this.</p>
<p>For those who prefer metric measurements, 6 ounces is roughly 170 grams. This conversion is handy if you're following a UK-based recipe. Whether you're making air fryer cakes or cleaning your appliance, accuracy is key.</p>

<h3>Common uses for 0.75 cup of baking soda in air fryers</h3>
<p>Beyond baking, 0.75 cup of baking soda has multiple uses in air fryer cooking. It’s great for creating a crispy coating on chicken or fish. Simply mix it with flour and spices for a perfect crunch. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> for ideas on how to use this trick.</p>

<h3>Tips for storing baking soda for air fryer use</h3>
<p>Keep your baking soda fresh by storing it in an airtight container. This ensures it remains effective for both cooking and cleaning. If you’re unsure about its potency, test it with a bit of vinegar—it should fizz vigorously. Fresh baking soda ensures your 0.75 cup measurements are always accurate.</p>
