<h2>Why Accurate Flour Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Gluten-free flour behaves differently than regular flour, making precise measurements crucial. Too much flour can lead to dry, dense baked goods, while too little may cause them to fall apart. Converting 1.66 cups of gluten-free flour to ounces (7.5 oz) ensures consistency, especially in air fryer recipes where even heat distribution is key. For more tips on perfecting your bakes, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Gluten-Free Flour Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 1.66 cups of gluten-free flour accurately, fluff the flour with a spoon before lightly spooning it into a measuring cup. Level it off with a knife—never pack it down. For best results, weigh it on a digital scale to confirm 7.5 oz. This method prevents over-measuring, a common issue with gluten-free blends.</p>  

<h2>Converting Cups to Ounces for Different Flours</h2>  
<p>Not all gluten-free flours weigh the same per cup. Almond flour, for example, is lighter, while coconut flour is denser. If your recipe calls for 1.66 cups of a specific gluten-free flour, always check its weight per cup. <p>Gluten-free flours often require extra moisture. When converting 1.66 cups (7.5 oz) for air fryer recipes, consider adding a tablespoon of liquid if the batter seems dry. <h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Gluten-Free Flour</h2>  
<p>Scooping flour directly from the bag compacts it, leading to excess flour—a major culprit in dense bakes. Always fluff, spoon, and level for 1.66 cups (7.5 oz) accuracy. Another mistake is substituting gluten-free flour 1:1 in non-gluten-free recipes without adjusting liquids or binders.
