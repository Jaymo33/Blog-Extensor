<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of oat milk ensures your air fryer recipes turn out as intended. Too much liquid can make batters runny, while too little may dry out your dish. For example, if a recipe calls for 585 ml of oat milk (roughly 2.47 cups), precision guarantees the right texture and consistency. Many of our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> rely on exact measurements for optimal results.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 585 ml of Oat Milk Without a Measuring Jug</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can still measure 585 ml of oat milk using standard cups. One UK metric cup holds 250 ml, so 2 cups equal 500 ml. Adding another half cup (125 ml) brings you to 625 ml, which is slightly over. To get closer to 585 ml, use 2 cups (500 ml) plus 3 tablespoons (85 ml), totaling 585 ml. This method works well for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast recipes</a> like overnight oats or pancakes.</p>

<h3>Does Oat Milk Brand Affect the Conversion?</h3>  
<p>Most commercial oat milks have a similar density, so 585 ml will generally equal 2.47 cups regardless of brand. However, homemade oat milk may vary slightly in thickness. If your oat milk is thicker, you might need to adjust by a tablespoon or two for accuracy. Always stir well before measuring to ensure consistency, especially for recipes like custards or batters where precision is crucial.</p>
