<h2>Why knowing 3 tbsp rice to grams matters</h2>  
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial for air fryer recipes, especially when cooking rice. Too much rice can lead to uneven cooking, while too little might leave you hungry. Converting 3 tbsp of rice into grams ensures consistency, whether you’re making fried rice or a rice-based casserole. Many air fryer recipes, like those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipes page</a>, rely on precise measurements for perfect results.</p>  

<p>Rice expands when cooked, so starting with the right amount is key. For example, 45 grams of uncooked rice yields about 135 grams when cooked. This ratio helps when planning portions for meals. If you’re unsure, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> can help with other common measurements.</p>  

<h3>Types of rice and their weights</h3>  
<p>Different rice varieties have slightly different weights per tablespoon. Long-grain white rice weighs around 15 grams per 3 tablespoons, while brown rice might be closer to 48 grams. Basmati and jasmine rice fall somewhere in between. Always check your specific rice type for the most accurate conversion.</p>  

<h2>How to measure 3 tbsp of rice accurately</h2>  
<p>Using proper measuring spoons ensures you get the correct 3 tbsp rice in grams. Level off the rice with a straight edge for precision. For even better accuracy, a kitchen scale is ideal—simply weigh out 45 grams. This method is especially useful for air fryer cooking, where exact amounts matter.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a scale, remember that 3 tablespoons equal roughly 45 grams for most white rice varieties. For more tips on perfect rice portions, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep recipes</a>. Keeping your measurements consistent helps replicate successful dishes every time.</p>  

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring rice</h3>  
<p>One mistake is packing rice into the tablespoon, which adds extra weight. Another is using heaped spoons instead of level ones. Both can throw off your 3 tbsp rice how many grams calculation. Always measure lightly for the most accurate results.</p>
