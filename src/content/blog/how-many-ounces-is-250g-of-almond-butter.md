<h3>How to measure almond butter without scales</h3>  
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem. You can still measure 250g of almond butter (8.82 oz) using standard spoons or cups. One tablespoon of almond butter weighs roughly 16g, so 250g would be about 15.5 tablespoons. Alternatively, 250g fills just over half a standard US cup (1 cup ≈ 454g). Keep in mind that these are approximate—stirring the almond butter first ensures a more accurate measurement.</p>

<h3>Storing almond butter after measuring</h3>  
<p>Once you’ve measured out your 250g (8.82 oz) of almond butter, proper storage keeps it fresh. Always seal the jar tightly and store it upside down to prevent oil separation. If you’ve scooped out more than needed, refrigerate the excess in an airtight container for up to two weeks. For longer storage, freeze measured portions in ice cube trays—each cube holds about 30g (1 oz), making future recipes a breeze.</p>  

<h2>Common mistakes when converting 250g almond butter to ounces</h2>  
<p>One frequent error is confusing fluid ounces (volume) with avoirdupois ounces (weight). Since almond butter’s density varies, weight measurements (like 250g to 8.82 oz) are far more reliable than volume. Another pitfall is using compacted almond butter straight from the fridge—always let it soften slightly for easier, more accurate measuring.</p>
