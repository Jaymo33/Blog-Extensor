<h2>How to Measure 610 ml of Honey Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>Not everyone has a kitchen scale, but most have measuring cups. To measure 610 ml of honey, use the 2.57-cup conversion. Fill a liquid measuring cup to the 2-cup mark, then add another half cup plus a tablespoon. Honey’s viscosity means it clings to utensils, so lightly grease your measuring cup for easier pouring. This trick works for sticky ingredients like syrup or molasses too.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting for Honey’s Density</h3>  
<p>Honey is denser than water, so 610 ml doesn’t equal 610 grams. One cup of honey weighs about 340 grams, whereas water weighs 236 grams per cup. This density affects recipes where weight matters, like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>air fryer desserts</a>. If a recipe calls for weight, use a scale. Otherwise, the 2.57-cup conversion works perfectly for volume-based measurements.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Leftover Honey</h3>  
<p>If you don’t use all 610 ml of honey at once, store it properly to maintain freshness. Keep it in an airtight container at room temperature, away from direct sunlight. Honey crystallises over time, but a warm water bath restores its smooth texture. Avoid refrigerating honey, as it accelerates crystallisation. With proper storage, your honey will stay ready for the next air fryer adventure.</p>
