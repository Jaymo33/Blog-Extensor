<h2>Why accurate oat measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of oats is crucial for texture and taste. Too little, and your dish might turn out dry; too much, and it could become dense. <h3>Types of oats and their weights</h3>  
<p>Rolled oats, jumbo oats, and quick oats all have slightly different densities. However, 2 tbsp of any type will generally weigh around 16 grams. If you're using steel-cut oats, the weight may vary slightly due to their larger size. Always measure oats by weight for baking or air fryer recipes to avoid inconsistencies.</p>  

<h2>How to measure 2 tbsp of oats accurately</h2>  
<p>For the most precise measurement, use a digital kitchen scale. Scoop the oats into a tablespoon and level it off with a knife for consistency. If you don't have scales, fluff the oats first to prevent compacting. This method ensures you get the correct 16 grams for 2 tbsp of oats every time.</p>
