<h3>How to Measure Almond Milk Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a liquid measuring cup when converting 2.0 cups of almond milk to ml. Place the cup on a flat surface and pour the almond milk to the 2-cup mark—this ensures you’re not over or under-measuring. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale set to millilitres can also do the trick. Remember, 2.0 cups of almond milk is 473 ml, so adjust accordingly if your recipe requires a different quantity.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Almond Milk</h2>  
<p>Once opened, almond milk should be refrigerated and used within 7–10 days. If your recipe calls for 2.0 cups (473 ml) but you only need half, store the remainder in an airtight container. Shake well before using, as separation is natural. For longer storage, freeze almond milk in ice cube trays—perfect for future air fryer recipes. Just thaw and measure out 473 ml when needed.</p>
