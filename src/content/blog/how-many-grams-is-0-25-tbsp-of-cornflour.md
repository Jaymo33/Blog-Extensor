<h2>How to measure 0.25 tbsp of cornflour accurately</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a scale handy, you can still measure 0.25 tbsp of cornflour using standard spoons. A UK tablespoon holds 15ml, so a quarter tablespoon is roughly 3.75ml. However, for the most accurate results, especially in baking or coating mixes, a digital scale is ideal. Lightly spoon the cornflour into your measuring spoon and level it off for consistency. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to over-measuring.</p>

<h2>Common uses for 0.25 tbsp of cornflour in air fryer cooking</h2>

<h2>Tips for working with small cornflour measurements</h2>
<p>When measuring tiny amounts like 0.25 tbsp of cornflour, it’s easy to accidentally use too much or too little. Here are some professional tips: use a dedicated set of measuring spoons that includes quarter-tablespoon measures, or invest in a precision digital scale that measures to 0.1 gram accuracy. Always store your cornflour in an airtight container to prevent moisture absorption, which can affect its weight and performance in recipes.</p>

<h3>Alternative thickeners if you’re out of cornflour</h3>
<p>If you find yourself without cornflour, arrowroot or potato starch can work as substitutes in similar quantities. For every 0.25 tbsp (2 grams) of cornflour required, you can typically use the same amount of these substitutes. Experiment with small batches first to ensure you achieve the desired results.</p>
