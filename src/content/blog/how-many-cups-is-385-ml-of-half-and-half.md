<p>385 ml of half and half equals approximately 1.63 cups in US measurements or 1.52 cups in UK measurements. This conversion is essential for baking, cooking, or preparing creamy dishes in your air fryer. Whether you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> or adapting a family favourite, knowing the exact measurement ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Understanding Half And Half Measurements</h2>  
<p>Half and half is a dairy product made by combining equal parts whole milk and light cream. It's commonly used in recipes to add richness without being as heavy as full cream. When converting 385 ml of half and half to cups, it's important to note that US and UK cup measurements differ slightly. In the US, 1 cup is 240 ml, while in the UK, 1 cup is 250 ml. This means 385 ml converts to 1.63 US cups or 1.52 UK cups.</p>  

<p>For air fryer recipes, precise measurements matter, especially when making sauces, custards, or baked goods. If you're working with a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a>, check whether it uses UK or US measurements. A slight variation in liquid quantities can affect texture and cooking times, particularly in air fryers where evaporation happens faster.</p>  

<h3>Why Accurate Conversions Matter</h3>  
<p>When preparing dishes like creamy pasta sauces or custards in your air fryer, the right amount of half and half ensures the perfect consistency. Too much liquid can make sauces runny, while too little might result in a dry or overly thick dish. Using 385 ml of half and half (1.63 US cups) precisely helps maintain balance in recipes.</p>  

<h2>Converting 385 ml of Half And Half for Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking requires exact measurements for successful results. If your recipe calls for 385 ml of half and half and you only have measuring cups, knowing the conversion is crucial. For US bakers, 1.63 cups is roughly 1 and 2/3 cups. For UK bakers, 1.52 cups is about 1 and 1/2 cups plus a teaspoon. A digital scale can help verify these measurements if precision is key.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer dessert recipes, like those found in the <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a>, use half and half for richness. Whether making custards, puddings, or cakes, measuring 385 ml correctly ensures your treats turn out perfectly every time. Always level off your measuring cups for accuracy.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Cup Sizes</h3>  
<p>If you're using a US recipe with UK measurements (or vice versa), remember that 385 ml of half and half will fill slightly different cup amounts. For best results, stick to one measurement system throughout the recipe. If you need to switch, refer to a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> to adjust all ingredients proportionally.</p>  

<h2>Practical Tips for Measuring Half And Half</h2>  
<p>When measuring 385 ml of half and half, use a clear liquid measuring cup for accuracy. Pour slowly to the 385 ml mark at eye level to avoid over-pouring. If you don't have a measuring cup, use a kitchen scale: 385 ml of half and half weighs roughly 396 grams due to its density. This method is especially useful for air fryer recipes where precision matters.</p>
