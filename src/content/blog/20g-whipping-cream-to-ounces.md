<h2>Understanding whipping cream measurements</h2>
<p>Whipping cream is a versatile ingredient used in both sweet and savoury air fryer recipes. When a recipe calls for 20g of whipping cream, it's referring to the weight rather than volume. This distinction matters because cream's density means volume measurements can vary. For consistent results in your air fryer cooking, weight measurements are always more accurate.</p>

<h3>Measuring without scales</h3>
<p>If you don't have kitchen scales, you can estimate 20g of whipping cream using volume measurements. Approximately 20g equals 4 teaspoons or just under 1 tablespoon. However, this method is less accurate than weighing, especially for recipes where precision matters. For best results, we always recommend using scales.</p>

<h3>Storing leftover whipping cream</h3>
<p>After measuring out your 20g (0.71 oz) of whipping cream, you might have leftovers. Store unused cream in its original container in the fridge and use within 2-3 days.
