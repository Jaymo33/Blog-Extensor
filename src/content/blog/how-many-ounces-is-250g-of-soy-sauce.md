<h2>Why converting 250g of soy sauce to ounces matters</h2>
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial in cooking, particularly when using an air fryer. The air fryer’s rapid circulation of hot air means ingredients cook faster and more evenly, so precise quantities matter. For example, 250g of soy sauce (8.82 ounces) might be needed for a marinade or sauce. Too little, and your dish lacks flavour; too much, and it becomes overly salty. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> can help you avoid these pitfalls.</p>

<h2>How to measure 250g of soy sauce without scales</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 250g of soy sauce (8.82 ounces) using volume measurements. One tablespoon of soy sauce weighs about 15g, so 250g would be roughly 16-17 tablespoons. However, this method is less precise than weighing, so we recommend using scales for accuracy. For more precise measurements, consider investing in a digital scale, which is a must-have for air fryer enthusiasts.</p>

<h3>Common uses for 250g of soy sauce in air fryer cooking</h3>
<p>250g of soy sauce is a versatile quantity for many air fryer dishes. It’s perfect for marinating chicken, tofu, or shrimp, giving them a rich umami flavour. You can also use it in stir-fries, glazes, or dipping sauces. <h2>Tips for storing and using soy sauce in air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Soy sauce should be stored in a cool, dark place to maintain its flavour. Once opened, it can last for up to three years if stored properly. When using 250g of soy sauce in air fryer recipes, consider reducing the salt elsewhere in the dish, as soy sauce is naturally salty.
