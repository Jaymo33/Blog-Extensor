<h2>Why Convert Cups to Grams?</h2>  
<p>Measuring ingredients by weight (grams) is far more accurate than using volume (cups). Yogurt’s density can vary based on thickness and brand, making cup measurements unreliable for recipes requiring precision. For air fryer cooking, where even small deviations can affect texture, grams are the gold standard. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies these calculations for you.</p>  

<p>If you’re adapting a recipe from cups to grams, this conversion eliminates guesswork. Whether you’re making a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert</a> or a savoury dish, 1.75 cups of yogurt equals roughly 420 grams. This consistency is key for air fryer success, where even moisture levels impact cooking times.</p>  

<h3>Types of Yogurt and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Not all yogurts weigh the same per cup. Greek yogurt, for example, is denser than regular yogurt, so 1.75 cups may weigh closer to 450 grams. Always check your yogurt’s consistency before measuring. For <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes</a> requiring exact proportions, weighing ensures reliability.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Yogurt for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Using a kitchen scale is the easiest way to measure 1.75 cups of yogurt in grams. Place your bowl on the scale, tare it to zero, then add yogurt until it hits 420 grams. This method avoids messy cups and guarantees accuracy. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> to keep your air fryer in top shape.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a scale, spoon yogurt into a measuring cup without packing it down. Level it off with a knife for the closest approximation. Remember, though, that this method isn’t as precise as weighing—especially for air fryer recipes where moisture balance matters.</p>
