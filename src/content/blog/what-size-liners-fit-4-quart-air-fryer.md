<h2>Types of Liners for a 4 Quart Air Fryer</h2>
<p>When choosing liners for your 4 quart air fryer, you’ll find three main types: silicone, parchment paper, and reusable fabric liners. Silicone liners are durable and heat-resistant, making them perfect for frequent use. Parchment paper liners are disposable and ideal for messy foods like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken wings</a>. Reusable fabric liners, often made from fiberglass, are eco-friendly but require thorough cleaning. Each type has pros and cons, so consider your cooking habits before picking one. For more tips on maintaining your air fryer, check out our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaning</a>.</p>

<p>Silicone liners are particularly popular because they’re non-stick and easy to wash. They usually come in round or square shapes to match your 4 quart air fryer basket. Parchment liners, on the other hand, are pre-cut and often perforated for better airflow. If you’re cooking greasy foods, these disposable liners save time on scrubbing. Reusable liners are a great choice if you’re looking to reduce waste, though they may need occasional seasoning to maintain non-stick properties. Whatever you choose, ensure it’s labelled as compatible with a 4 quart air fryer.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Your Air Fryer Basket</h3>
<p>Before buying liners, it’s wise to measure your 4 quart air fryer basket to avoid misfits. Use a tape measure to check the diameter for round baskets or the length and width for square ones. Most 4 quart models have baskets around 7.5 inches in diameter, but brands like Ninja or Philips might differ slightly. If your liner is too large, it can block airflow, leading to uneven cooking. Too small, and it won’t catch drips or crumbs effectively.
