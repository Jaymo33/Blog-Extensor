This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise liquid measurements can make or break a dish. Whether you're baking, steaming, or preparing sauces, knowing how to convert millilitres to cups ensures consistency. <h2>Understanding the Conversion: 185 ml to Cups</h2>  
<p>Converting 185 ml of water to cups depends on whether you're using the US or UK measurement system. In the US, 1 cup is 240 ml, so 185 ml is roughly 0.78 cups. In the UK, where 1 cup is 250 ml, 185 ml translates to about 0.74 cups. This small difference matters in baking, where precision is key. For air fryer recipes, especially those involving batters or liquids, getting this right ensures even cooking.</p>  

<p>If you're working with an <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>, you'll often see both measurements listed. Many recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe hub</a> specify ml and cups to cater to all cooks. Always double-check which system your recipe follows to avoid mishaps.</p>  

<h3>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>Air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, so incorrect liquid measurements can lead to uneven cooking or soggy results. For example, adding too much water to a batter might prevent crisping, while too little could dry out your dish. Measuring 185 ml of water correctly ensures your recipes turn out as intended. This is especially crucial for delicate dishes like cakes or steamed puddings.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 185 ml of Water in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>185 ml of water is a common measurement in many air fryer recipes. It's often used for steaming vegetables, thinning sauces, or preparing batters. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cake recipes</a> might call for this amount to achieve the perfect consistency. Similarly, 185 ml of water can be used to rehydrate ingredients or create steam for moist cooking.</p>  

<p>If you're preparing a marinade or glaze, 185 ml of water might be part of the liquid base. Always measure carefully, as even small deviations can alter the flavour and texture. For more tips, check our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning your air fryer</a> after working with liquids.</p>  

<h3>Tools for Measuring 185 ml of Water</h3>  
<p>A liquid measuring cup with millilitre markings is the most accurate tool for measuring 185 ml of water. If you only have a cup measure, use the conversions mentioned earlier. For smaller quantities, a kitchen scale can also help, as 185 ml of water weighs 185 grams. Avoid using dry measuring cups, as they won't give you the precision needed for liquids.</p>  

<h2>Converting 185 ml of Water for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>When adapting recipes for your air fryer, you might need to adjust liquid measurements.
