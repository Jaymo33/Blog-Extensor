<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer baking</h2>  
<p>Baking powder is a leavening agent, and even small measurement errors can affect how your dish rises. Too much can leave a bitter taste, while too little may result in dense, flat bakes. The air fryer’s rapid circulation means ingredients must be perfectly balanced. That’s why converting 1.33 cups of baking powder to ounces (10.64 oz) is crucial for recipes like air fryer cakes or quick breads. <h3>How to measure baking powder correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate conversion from cups to ounces, use a kitchen scale. Scooping baking powder directly from the bag can lead to compacted measurements, affecting the weight. Instead, spoon the powder lightly into the measuring cup and level it off. This method ensures that 1.33 cups of baking powder truly equals 10.64 ounces, giving your air fryer recipes the best chance of success.</p>

<h2>Tips for storing baking powder for air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>To keep your baking powder fresh and effective, store it in an airtight container away from moisture. Humidity can cause clumping, affecting both weight and performance. If you’ve measured out 1.33 cups (10.64 oz) and have leftovers, ensure the container is sealed tightly. Proper storage means your next air fryer bake will rise perfectly.</p>  

<p>Regularly check your baking powder’s freshness by mixing a teaspoon with hot water. If it bubbles vigorously, it’s still active.
