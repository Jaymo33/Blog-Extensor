<h2>Cooking your converted salmon fillet in the air fryer</h2>
<p>Once you've completed your 8 kg salmon fillet to pounds conversion and portioned the fish appropriately, air frying becomes a straightforward process. For best results, pat your salmon portions dry with paper towels before seasoning – this helps achieve a crispy exterior while keeping the interior moist and flaky. Preheat your air fryer to 200°C (390°F) for about 3 minutes before adding the salmon, which ensures immediate crisping action when the fish hits the basket. Cook times will vary based on the thickness of your portions, but generally, 1-inch thick pieces from your 8 kg salmon fillet will need 8-12 minutes in the air fryer. Always check for doneness by ensuring the salmon flakes easily with a fork and has reached an internal temperature of 63°C (145°F).</p>

<p>When air frying portions from your 8 kg salmon fillet converted to pounds, avoid overcrowding the basket to ensure proper air circulation and even cooking. Leave at least 1-2 centimeters between pieces to allow the hot air to circulate freely around each portion. If your air fryer has multiple racks, you can cook several portions simultaneously, but may need to rotate them halfway through cooking for even results. The beauty of air frying salmon is that it requires little to no added oil, making it a healthier cooking method that still delivers delicious, restaurant-quality results. <h3>Storing and reheating your cooked salmon</h3>
<p>After cooking portions from your 8 kg salmon fillet converted to pounds, proper storage ensures you can enjoy your meal prep throughout the week. Allow cooked salmon to cool completely before transferring to airtight containers – this prevents condensation that can make the fish soggy. Refrigerated cooked salmon will keep for 3-4 days, while properly wrapped portions can be frozen for up to 3 months. When reheating, your air fryer is again your best friend – it can restore that freshly cooked texture better than a microwave. Reheat at 175°C (350°F) for 3-5 minutes until just warmed through, being careful not to overcook and dry out the salmon. This makes your 8 kg salmon fillet to pounds conversion not just about immediate cooking, but about efficient meal planning for days to come.</p>
