<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>When using an air fryer, precise measurements ensure even cooking and perfect texture. Molasses, being dense, can throw off a recipe if not measured correctly. Too much can make dishes overly sweet or sticky, while too little might leave them lacking flavour. This is especially important for air fryer baking, where balance is crucial. For more tips on air fryer baking, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>.</p>

<p>Converting cups to ounces is straightforward once you know the density of molasses. A standard cup of molasses weighs about 12 ounces, so 3 cups would be 36 ounces. This measurement is consistent whether you're using UK or US cups, as the difference is minimal. Always use a kitchen scale for the most accurate results, especially when working with thick liquids like molasses.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Molasses for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>
<p>Measuring molasses can be tricky due to its sticky consistency. To get an accurate 3 cups, lightly grease your measuring cup with oil to help the molasses slide out easily. Alternatively, weigh it on a kitchen scale for precision. This method is particularly useful for air fryer recipes, where exact proportions matter.
