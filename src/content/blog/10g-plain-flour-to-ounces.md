<p>10 grams of plain flour equals approximately 0.35 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precision matters, ensuring your dishes turn out perfectly every time. Whether you're whipping up a batch of air-fried cookies or coating chicken for a crispy finish, knowing how to convert grams to ounces is a game-changer.</p>

<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of plain flour can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too much flour can lead to dry, dense results, while too little might leave your dishes undercooked or lacking texture. For example, a light dusting of flour helps achieve that golden crispiness on air-fried chicken or fish. If you're exploring recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, you'll notice many rely on precise measurements for consistent outcomes.</p>  

<p>Converting 10g of plain flour to ounces (0.35 oz) ensures you stay on track, especially when scaling recipes up or down. This is particularly useful for baking in the air fryer, where even small deviations can affect rise and texture. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient swaps.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 10g of plain flour without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a digital scale, you can use a measuring spoon to approximate 10g of plain flour. One tablespoon of plain flour typically weighs around 8-10g, so a slightly heaped tablespoon will do the trick. For air fryer recipes, it’s best to fluff the flour first with a fork to avoid compacting it, which could lead to over-measuring.</p>

<h3>Converting other flour quantities for air fryer use</h3>  
<p>Once you’ve mastered the 10g-to-ounces conversion, you can apply the same logic to larger or smaller amounts. For example, 20g of plain flour equals roughly 0.7 ounces, while 5g is about 0.18 ounces. This flexibility is handy when adapting recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> to suit your portion sizes or dietary needs.</p>  

<h2>Tips for working with plain flour in air fryers</h2>  
<p>Plain flour behaves differently in air fryers compared to conventional ovens due to the intense circulating heat. To prevent clumping or uneven cooking, always sift flour before use and shake the basket halfway through cooking. This ensures even browning and avoids floury hotspots in dishes like air-fried fritters or breaded fish.</p>
