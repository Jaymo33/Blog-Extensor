<h3>How to Measure 15g of BBQ Sauce Accurately</h3>
<p>Use a digital kitchen scale for the most precise measurement of 15g of BBQ sauce. If you don’t have one, two level teaspoons will get you close to 0.53 ounces. For sticky sauces, lightly grease the spoon to ensure it slides off easily. This method works well for air fryer marinades and glazes.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for 15g of BBQ Sauce in Air Fryers</h2>
<p>15g of BBQ sauce (0.53 oz) is perfect for coating a single chicken breast or a small batch of wings in the air fryer. It’s also great for glazing salmon or tofu, adding flavour without overpowering. <p>You can also mix 15g of BBQ sauce with other ingredients like honey or mustard for a custom marinade. This small quantity ensures even distribution without wasting sauce. <h3>Tips for Working with BBQ Sauce in Air Fryers</h3>
<p>BBQ sauce can caramelise quickly in the air fryer, so apply it in the last few minutes of cooking. For 15g (0.53 oz), a thin layer ensures even cooking without burning. If you’re using it as a marinade, pat excess sauce off to avoid smoking. These small adjustments make a big difference in your results.</p>
