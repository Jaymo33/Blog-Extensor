Whether you're whipping up a garlic-infused dish or adjusting a recipe, knowing this conversion ensures your flavours are spot-on. <h2>Why accurate garlic measurements matter</h2>
<p>Garlic is a powerhouse ingredient that can transform a dish, but too much or too little can throw off the balance. In air fryer cooking, where heat distribution is rapid, precise amounts ensure even flavour infusion. For example, 15g of chopped garlic (0.53 ounces) is roughly two tablespoons, but weighing it removes any doubt. If you're preparing a garlic-heavy dish like roasted vegetables or chicken, this accuracy is key. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes</a> section for inspiration on how to use garlic perfectly.</p>

<p>Many UK cooks prefer weighing ingredients like garlic for consistency, especially when scaling recipes up or down. A kitchen scale is your best friend here, as volume measurements can vary based on chop size. Whether you're using fresh or pre-chopped garlic, 15g will always be 0.53 ounces. This precision is particularly handy for air fryer recipes, where small batches are common. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>

<h2>How to measure 15g of chopped garlic</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can estimate 15g of chopped garlic as roughly two level tablespoons. However, this can vary depending on how finely the garlic is chopped. For minced garlic, the volume might be slightly less due to tighter packing. To ensure accuracy, we recommend investing in a digital scale—they’re affordable and invaluable for air fryer cooking. You’ll find that 15g of chopped garlic (0.53 ounces) is a common measurement in many recipes, from marinades to sauces.</p>

<h3>Converting other garlic amounts</h3>
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of garlic, the same principle applies. For instance, 30g of chopped garlic equals about 1.06 ounces, while 5g is roughly 0.18 ounces. These conversions are especially useful when adapting recipes for your air fryer. Whether you’re cooking for one or meal prepping, precise measurements save time and reduce waste. For more garlic-centric dishes, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> section.</p>

<h2>Common uses for 15g of chopped garlic</h2>
<p>15g of chopped garlic (0.53 ounces) is a versatile amount that works well in many air fryer dishes. It’s enough to flavour a batch of roasted potatoes, a garlic butter sauce for shrimp, or a marinade for chicken thighs. The key is distributing it evenly so every bite is packed with flavour. If you’re new to air fryer cooking, start with this amount and adjust to taste. You’ll soon see why garlic is a staple in so many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes</a>.</p>

<p>Garlic’s potency means a little goes a long way, especially in the concentrated heat of an air fryer. For dishes like garlic bread or mushrooms, 15g provides a robust flavour without overwhelming other ingredients. Remember, fresh garlic will have a sharper taste than pre-chopped, so adjust accordingly. If you’re unsure, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> can help you scale recipes up or down with confidence.</p>

<h3>Storing chopped garlic</h3>
<p>If you’ve chopped more garlic than needed, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze 15g portions in ice cube trays—they’ll be ready to pop into your next air fryer creation. This is a great way to save time and ensure you always have the right amount on hand. Just remember that frozen garlic may have a slightly milder flavour, so you might want to use a touch more.</p>
