<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect ingredient quantities can lead to uneven cooking or texture issues. For example, using too much wheatgerm in a coating might make it overly dense, while too little could leave your dish lacking crunch. Measuring 0.375 cup of wheatgerm correctly (1.5 oz) ensures balanced results every time. <p>Beyond texture, accurate measurements affect nutritional calculations. Wheatgerm is packed with fibre and vitamins, so knowing the exact amount (0.375 cup = 1.5 oz) helps track your intake. <h3>How to Measure 0.375 Cup of Wheatgerm Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method for dry ingredients like wheatgerm. Lightly spoon the wheatgerm into a measuring cup until it’s slightly heaped, then level it off with a straight edge. This avoids compacting the ingredient, which could skew the measurement. Remember, 0.375 cup is equivalent to 6 tablespoons, so you could also use a tablespoon measure for smaller batches. For recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>air fryer breakfast muffins</a>, this method ensures consistency without fuss.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 0.375 Cup of Wheatgerm in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Wheatgerm adds a nutty flavour and nutritional boost to many dishes. In your air fryer, 0.375 cup (1.5 oz) works perfectly for breading fish or vegetables, mixing into granola, or enhancing baked goods.
