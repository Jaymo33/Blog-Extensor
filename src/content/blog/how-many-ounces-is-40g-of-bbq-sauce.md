<h2>Why Convert 40g of BBQ Sauce to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but some kitchen scales or international recipes might list ounces instead. <h3>How to Measure 40g of BBQ Sauce Accurately</h3>  
<p>To measure 40g of BBQ sauce (1.41 oz), use a digital kitchen scale for precision. If you don’t have one, two tablespoons roughly equal 40g, though thickness affects volume. For sticky sauces, lightly grease the spoon for easier pouring. This method works well for air fryer marinades or glazes.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 40g of BBQ Sauce in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>40g of BBQ sauce is ideal for coating 4-6 chicken wings or a small rack of ribs in the air fryer. It’s enough to add flavour without making the dish soggy. For larger batches, scale up proportionally—80g would be roughly 2.82 ounces. <h3>Tips for Storing Leftover BBQ Sauce</h3>  
<p>If you have leftover BBQ sauce after measuring 40g, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a month. To prevent contamination, use a clean spoon each time. For longer storage, freeze small portions in ice cube trays—each cube is roughly 15g (0.53 oz), making future 40g measurements easy.</p>
