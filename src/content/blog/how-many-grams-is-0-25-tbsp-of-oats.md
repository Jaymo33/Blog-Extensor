<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and small discrepancies in ingredient weights can affect texture and doneness. Using grams instead of tablespoons for dry ingredients like oats ensures accuracy, especially in recipes with tight ratios. For example, adding too many oats to a batter can make it dense, while too few might leave it lacking structure. <h2>How to measure 0.25 tbsp of oats without a scale</h2>
<p>If you don't have a scale, you can still measure 0.25 tbsp of oats using measuring spoons. Fill a 1 tbsp measure halfway to get 0.5 tbsp, then divide that portion in half visually. While this method isn't as precise as weighing, it works in a pinch for small quantities. Remember that oat sizes and densities vary slightly between brands, which is why weight measurements are superior for baking.</p>

<h3>Types of oats and their weights</h3>
<p>Rolled oats, quick oats, and steel-cut oats have slightly different weights per tablespoon due to their textures. Rolled oats (the most common type for air fryer recipes) weigh about 6.25 grams per tablespoon, making 0.25 tbsp roughly 1.56 grams. Quick oats are finer, so they pack more densely, while steel-cut oats are heavier and more irregular. Always check your recipe for which oat type to use.</p>
