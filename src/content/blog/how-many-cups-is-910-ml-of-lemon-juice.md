<p>910 ml of lemon juice is roughly equivalent to 3.85 cups (US) or 3.2 cups (UK). Whether you're whipping up a tangy dessert or a citrusy marinade for your air fryer, knowing this conversion ensures your recipe turns out perfectly. Precise measurements matter, especially when working with acidic ingredients like lemon juice, which can affect the balance of flavours and textures in your dish.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Lemon Juice Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Lemon juice plays a crucial role in both baking and cooking, adding brightness and acidity to dishes. Too much or too little can throw off the taste and consistency of your recipe. For air fryer cooking, where marinades and glazes are common, getting the right amount of lemon juice ensures your food isn’t overly tart or bland. For air fryer recipes, especially those involving seafood or chicken, the right amount of lemon juice enhances flavour without overpowering. <h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>It's important to note that US and UK cup measurements differ slightly. A US cup holds 240 ml, while a UK cup is 284 ml. This means 910 ml converts to about 3.85 US cups or 3.2 UK cups. If your recipe originates from a different region, double-check which standard it uses to avoid errors. Many recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> specify measurements for clarity.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Lemon Juice</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a liquid measuring cup rather than dry measuring cups. Fill it to the 910 ml mark or use a kitchen scale for precision. Freshly squeezed lemon juice offers the best flavour, but bottled works in a pinch—just check for additives. If your air fryer recipe calls for zest too, remember that one lemon yields about 1 tablespoon of juice and 1 teaspoon of zest.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 910 ml of Lemon Juice</h2>  
<p>This quantity of lemon juice is ideal for batch cooking or large recipes. Think lemon curd, citrus dressings, or marinades for meal prep. In the air fryer, it’s perfect for glazing multiple portions of chicken or fish. You could also use it in baking—like a lemon drizzle cake—where precise acidity ensures the right rise and flavour.</p>
