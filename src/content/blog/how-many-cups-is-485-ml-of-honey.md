<h2>Why Accurate Honey Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Honey’s thick, sticky consistency makes it tricky to measure, especially when converting between millilitres and cups. Too much honey can overpower a dish, while too little might leave it lacking flavour. In air fryer cooking, precise measurements help achieve the perfect balance of sweetness and texture. Whether you’re making honey-glazed salmon or a batch of air-fried cookies, accuracy is key. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>Different brands of honey may vary slightly in density, but 485 ml generally translates to 2 cups in the US and 1.7 cups in the UK. This difference arises because US cups are slightly larger (240 ml) compared to UK cups (284 ml). For air fryer recipes, sticking to one measurement system avoids confusion. If you’re adapting a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, always double-check the units.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 485 ml of Honey Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug or scale, you can still measure 485 ml of honey using common kitchen tools. A standard US cup holds 240 ml, so two cups will give you 480 ml—close enough for most recipes. For UK cooks, 1.7 cups (using a 284 ml cup) will approximate 485 ml. To avoid sticky messes, lightly grease your measuring cup before pouring honey. <h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Honey Measurements</h3>  
<p>Some recipes call for weight (grams) rather than volume (ml or cups). Honey weighs about 340 grams per cup (US), so 485 ml would be roughly 680 grams. If your air fryer recipe uses grams, a kitchen scale is your best friend.
