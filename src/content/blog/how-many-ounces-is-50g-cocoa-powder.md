<h2>Why Convert 50g of Cocoa Powder to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but some baking tools or international recipes might list ingredients in ounces. Converting 50g of cocoa powder to ounces (1.76 oz) helps avoid guesswork. This is especially useful when using an air fryer, where precise measurements can affect texture and cooking time. <h3>How to Measure 50g of Cocoa Powder Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>No scale? No problem! One tablespoon of cocoa powder weighs about 7g, so 50g is roughly 7 tablespoons. However, for air fryer recipes, we recommend investing in a digital scale for consistency. Scooping directly from the bag can lead to uneven measurements, affecting your bake.</p>  

<h2>Using Cocoa Powder in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Cocoa powder is a staple in air fryer desserts, from molten cakes to crispy cookies. Measuring 50g (1.76 oz) correctly ensures balanced flavour. For a decadent treat, try our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer chocolate cake</a>, which uses this exact measurement.</p>  

<p>Remember, cocoa powder can clump, so sift it before mixing. This is especially important in air fryer baking, where even heat distribution is key. For cleaning tips after baking, explore our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>keeping your air fryer spotless</a>.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Cocoa Powder</h3>  
<p>Packing cocoa powder into a measuring cup can lead to using more than 50g (1.76 oz), resulting in a bitter taste. Always spoon it lightly into the cup or use a scale. In air fryer recipes, too much cocoa powder can also dry out your bake.</p>
