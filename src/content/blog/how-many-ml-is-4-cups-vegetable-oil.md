<h3>How to Measure 4.0 Cups of Vegetable Oil Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 946 ml measurement, use a clear liquid measuring cup with ml and cup markings. Fill to the 4-cup line, then double-check at eye level to avoid over-pouring. If your cup measures 240 ml per cup (US standard), 4.0 cups will total 960 ml – close enough for most recipes. UK cups (250 ml each) would equal 1000 ml, so adjust slightly if following a British recipe. For sticky oils, lightly coat the cup with non-stick spray to ensure every drop counts.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Vegetable Oil</h3>  
<p>Pouring oil directly from the bottle often leads to over-measuring, as the thick liquid clings to containers. Instead, use a spouted measuring cup for clean 946 ml (4.0 cups) portions. Another pitfall is confusing weight with volume – oil’s density means 946 ml won’t equal 946 grams. For sticky recipes like marinades, warm the oil slightly for easier pouring. Always level off cups with a straight edge for precision, especially when scaling recipes up or down.</p>
