For salmon, which can vary in thickness and density, an accurate weight ensures even cooking and optimal flavour. <h2>Practical tips for cooking a 3 kg salmon fillet in your air fryer</h2>
<p>Cooking a large salmon fillet in the air fryer requires some preparation to ensure even results. First, pat the fillet dry with kitchen paper to help the skin crisp up. Season generously with salt, pepper, and your favourite herbs—dill and lemon work wonderfully with salmon. Given that 3 kg salmon fillet to pounds is about 6.61, you'll need to adjust cooking time accordingly. Most air fryers cook salmon at around 180°C for 10-15 minutes per pound, so a fillet this size may take 20-25 minutes.</p>
<p>It's also important to consider the size of your air fryer basket. A 3 kg salmon fillet is quite large, so you may need to cut it into smaller pieces that fit comfortably without overlapping. This ensures hot air can circulate freely around each piece, cooking them evenly. Check out our <a href="https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart">air fryer conversion chart</a> for more tips on adapting recipes for different weights. Remember, a meat thermometer is your best friend—insert it into the thickest part of the fillet, and when it reads 63°C, your salmon is perfectly cooked.</p>
