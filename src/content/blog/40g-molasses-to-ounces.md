<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve crispy, evenly cooked dishes. Too much or too little molasses can alter the texture and flavour of your recipe. <p>Molasses is a dense liquid, so measuring by weight (grams) is more accurate than volume (cups or tablespoons). A digital kitchen scale is your best friend here. If your recipe calls for 40g of molasses, you’ll need roughly 1.41 ounces. This small but critical detail can elevate your air fryer dishes from good to great.</p>  

<h2>How to measure 40g of molasses without a scale</h2>  
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem. You can still measure 40g of molasses using common household tools. One tablespoon of molasses weighs about 20g, so two tablespoons will give you roughly 40g (or 1.41 ounces). For smaller quantities, a teaspoon holds around 7g, so you’d need about 5.5 teaspoons to reach 40g.</p>  

<p>Keep in mind that molasses is sticky, so lightly greasing your measuring spoon with oil can help it slide off easily. This trick is especially handy when prepping ingredients for air fryer recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a> or marinades. Accuracy matters, but a little flexibility won’t hurt—just aim for close approximations if you’re without a scale.</p>
