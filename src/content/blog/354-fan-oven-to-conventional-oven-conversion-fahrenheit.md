<h2>Why Convert 354 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>  
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air, reducing cooking time and ensuring even heat distribution. A conventional oven, however, relies on natural convection, meaning it often requires a higher temperature to achieve the same results. For example, 354°F in a fan oven might feel closer to 374°F in a standard oven. This difference is crucial for recipes that demand precision, like cakes or roasts.</p>  
<p>If you're adapting a recipe from a fan oven to a conventional one, always adjust the temperature upwards by about 20°F. This ensures your dish cooks properly without drying out or undercooking. For air fryer users, the conversion might differ slightly—our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> has tailored guides to help.</p>  

<h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>  
<p>Beyond temperature, cooking times may also need tweaking. Fan ovens cook faster, so if you're switching to a conventional oven, expect to add 5–10 minutes to the original time. Keep an eye on your dish, especially when baking delicate items like cookies or pastries.</p>  
<p>For air fryers, the cooking time might be even shorter due to their compact size and rapid air circulation. <p>One of the biggest mistakes is assuming fan and conventional ovens are interchangeable without adjustments. Skipping the temperature conversion can lead to overcooked or undercooked food. Always double-check the recipe’s oven type before starting.</p>
