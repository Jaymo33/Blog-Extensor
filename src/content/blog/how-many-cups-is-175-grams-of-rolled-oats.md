<h2>Understanding Rolled Oats Measurements</h2>
<p>Rolled oats, also known as old-fashioned oats, have a unique density that affects their volume-to-weight ratio. Unlike flour or sugar, oats don’t pack uniformly, so 175 grams of rolled oats in cups can vary slightly based on how you scoop them. For air fryer recipes, we recommend lightly spooning oats into your measuring cup and levelling off the top for accuracy. This method prevents compacting, which could lead to using more oats than intended.</p>
<p>If you’re converting 175g rolled oats to cups for baking, note that UK and US cup sizes are nearly identical (1 UK cup = 250ml, 1 US cup = 240ml). The difference is negligible for most air fryer recipes, including those found on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>. However, for absolute precision in recipes like oat cookies or crumble toppings, weighing your ingredients is always best.</p>
