<h2>Why measuring olive oil in grams matters</h2>  
<p>Using grams instead of tablespoons removes the guesswork from air fryer recipes. Different oils have varying densities, and measuring by weight ensures you’re using the right amount. <h3>How to measure 2.5 tbsp of olive oil without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can still measure 2.5 tablespoons of olive oil accurately. Use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and fill it halfway for the 0.5 measurement. Combine two full tablespoons and one half tablespoon to get 2.5 tbsp. While this method works, weighing in grams is more precise for air fryer cooking.</p>

For larger amounts, 100ml of olive oil equals approximately 92 grams. These conversions are handy when scaling recipes up or down.
