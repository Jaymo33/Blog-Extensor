<h2>How to Measure 460 ml of Cream</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can still measure 460 ml of cream accurately. A standard metric cup holds 250 ml, so 460 ml is roughly 1 cup plus 3/4 of another cup. For those using US cups, which are slightly smaller at 240 ml, 460 ml equals about 1.94 cups. This slight difference might not seem like much, but it can affect the outcome of your dish. Always double-check your measurements, especially when trying a new <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipe</a> in your air fryer.</p>

<h3>Using a Kitchen Scale</h3>
<p>For even greater accuracy, consider using a kitchen scale. Cream weighs approximately 1 gram per millilitre, so 460 ml of cream should weigh around 460 grams. This method eliminates any guesswork and ensures your measurements are spot-on. Whether you're making a rich <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert</a> or a creamy soup, precision is key. A kitchen scale is a small investment that pays off in perfectly cooked meals every time.</p>
