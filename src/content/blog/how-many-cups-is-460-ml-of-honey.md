<p>460 ml of honey is approximately 1.94 cups in the US measurement system and 1.62 cups in the UK measurement system. Whether you're baking or cooking in your air fryer, getting the right measurement is crucial for perfect results. Honey's thick consistency can make measuring tricky, but this guide will help you nail it every time.</p>

<h2>Understanding Honey Measurements</h2>
<p>Honey is denser than water, which means its volume-to-weight ratio differs. While 460 ml of water would be simpler to convert, honey requires a bit more precision. This is especially important for air fryer recipes, where exact measurements can affect cooking times and textures. For more conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>
<p>Using the right cup measurement is key. The US cup is 240 ml, while the UK cup is 284 ml. This difference means 460 ml of honey converts to roughly 1.94 US cups or 1.62 UK cups. Always check your recipe's origin to ensure accuracy, especially when using your air fryer for dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>honey cakes</a>.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>In baking, even small measurement errors can lead to dry or overly dense results. Honey's sweetness and moisture content mean it plays a big role in texture. If you're adapting a recipe for your air fryer, precision ensures even cooking and perfect browning. Too much honey can make things overly sticky, while too little might leave your dish lacking flavour.</p>
<p>For air fryer users, measuring honey correctly is also about avoiding mess. A sticky air fryer basket is no fun to clean, so using the right amount helps keep things tidy. If you do end up with a sticky situation, our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning your air fryer</a> has you covered.</p>

<h3>Tools for Measuring Honey</h3>
<p>A liquid measuring cup with clear markings is ideal for honey. For 460 ml, you’ll need a 2-cup (US) or 500 ml (UK) measure. Lightly greasing the cup can help the honey slide out more easily. If you're working with smaller amounts, a kitchen scale set to grams can provide even more precision for your air fryer recipes.</p>

<h3>Converting Honey for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>
<p>Many air fryer recipes, like glazes or marinades, call for honey. Knowing that 460 ml equals roughly 1.94 US cups helps you scale recipes up or down. For example, if a recipe for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>honey-glazed chicken</a> calls for 230 ml of honey, you’d use about 0.97 US cups. This precision ensures your dish turns out perfectly every time.</p>

In the air fryer, 460 ml could glaze several racks of ribs or coat a large batch of roasted vegetables.</p>
