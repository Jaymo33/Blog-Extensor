<h2>Why accurate almond milk measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of almond milk ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time. Too little can dry out baked goods, while too much may make them soggy. Keep a kitchen scale handy for the most accurate conversions.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer enthusiasts overlook liquid measurements, focusing solely on cook times or temperatures. However, liquids like almond milk play a crucial role in texture and browning. For savoury dishes, such as creamy sauces, 0.5 cup of almond milk (4 ounces) balances richness without overpowering other flavours. Always measure liquids at eye level for the best accuracy.</p>  

<h3>Converting 0.5 cup almond milk to ounces</h3>  
<p>The conversion from cups to ounces is straightforward: 1 cup of almond milk equals 8 ounces, so 0.5 cup is 4 ounces. This applies to both US and UK measurements, though slight variations may exist between brands. If you’re using a kitchen scale, set it to ounces and pour until it hits 4. For those without scales, a liquid measuring cup with clear markings works well. Remember, almond milk is slightly lighter than dairy milk, but the volume-to-weight ratio remains consistent for recipes.</p>

<h3>Tips for measuring almond milk accurately</h3>  
<p>To ensure your 0.5 cup of almond milk equals 4 ounces, use a transparent liquid measuring cup on a flat surface. Pour slowly and check at eye level—the meniscus (the curve at the liquid’s surface) should align with the 0.5 cup mark. For sticky or thicker almond milk varieties, lightly grease the measuring cup first. If scaling up recipes, remember that 1 cup equals 8 ounces, 1.5 cups equal 12 ounces, and so on. Precision is key for repeatable results in air fryer cooking.</p>
