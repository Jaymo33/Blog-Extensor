This method restores the muffin’s crisp exterior while keeping the interior soft and fluffy. Whether you’re working with a fresh muffin or one from the fridge, your air fryer can revive it beautifully. <h2>Why Use an Air Fryer to Reheat English Muffins?</h2>
<p>Air fryers circulate hot air around food, creating an even reheating process that toasters simply can’t match. Unlike microwaves that leave bread soggy, air fryers restore the English muffin’s signature nooks and crannies to crispy perfection. The controlled temperature prevents burning while ensuring thorough warming. Many home cooks find this method superior for reheating leftover English muffins from breakfast or brunch. <p>Another advantage is how quickly the air fryer works compared to conventional ovens. You’ll have warm muffins ready in minutes, making this ideal for busy mornings. The process also requires minimal supervision – just pop them in and set the timer. For those meal prepping English muffins, the air fryer offers the best way to reheat them throughout the week. <h3>Preparing Your English Muffin for Reheating</h3>
<p>Start by splitting your English muffin if it isn’t already halved – this ensures even heating throughout. For refrigerated muffins, let them sit at room temperature for 5 minutes to take the chill off. If you want extra crispness, lightly brush the cut sides with butter or olive oil before air frying. Stale muffins benefit from a quick spritz of water to restore moisture before reheating.</p>

<h2>Step-by-Step Guide to Reheating</h2>
<p>Preheat your air fryer to 160°C (320°F) for about 3 minutes for best results. Place the English muffin halves in the basket, cut side up, ensuring they aren’t overlapping. Cook for 2 minutes, then check for doneness – add 30-second increments if needed. The perfect reheated English muffin should be golden with crisp edges and a warm, soft interior. For variety, try our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast recipes</a> featuring these delicious muffins.</p>

<p>Different air fryer models may require slight adjustments to the timing. Compact air fryers might need the temperature reduced by 10°C, while larger models could cook faster. Always keep an eye on your muffins during the final minute to prevent over-browning. The beauty of using an air fryer to reheat English muffins lies in how easily you can control the outcome. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>our cleaning guide</a> to maintain your appliance after cooking.</p>

<h3>Troubleshooting Common Issues</h3>
<p>If your English muffin comes out too dry, reduce the temperature by 10°C next time and check it earlier. For uneven heating, try flipping the muffins halfway through the cooking time. Soggy results usually mean the air fryer wasn’t hot enough – ensure proper preheating. Storing muffins properly before reheating makes a big difference in the final texture too.</p>
