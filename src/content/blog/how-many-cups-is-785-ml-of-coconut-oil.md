<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of coconut oil in your air fryer recipes ensures even cooking and perfect results. Too much oil can make food greasy, while too little may lead to dryness. <h3>How to Measure 785 ml of Coconut Oil Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup to measure 785 ml of coconut oil. Since 1 cup equals 237 ml, you’ll need roughly 3 cups plus 3 tablespoons for accuracy. Melted coconut oil is easier to measure, but ensure it’s cooled slightly before adding it to your air fryer recipes to avoid altering textures.</p>

<p>Whether you’re making savoury or sweet dishes, 785 ml of coconut oil (3.3 cups) works well in most air fryer recipes. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Coconut Oil</h3>  
<p>One mistake is measuring solid coconut oil in a dry cup, which can lead to uneven amounts. Always melt it first for accuracy. Another pitfall is forgetting that coconut oil has a lower smoke point than some oils, so adjust air fryer temperatures accordingly to avoid burning.</p>
