<h2>How to Measure 335 ml of Cream Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use standard cups to measure 335 ml of cream. A UK metric cup holds 250 ml, so 335 ml is roughly 1 and 1/3 cups plus a tablespoon. For US cups (240 ml), it’s about 1.4 cups. Always level off the cream with a knife for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Converting 335 ml of Cream for Different Recipes</h3>  
<p>Whether you’re making a custard, soup, or frosting, 335 ml of cream translates to 1.4 cups. For thicker creams like double cream, the volume remains the same, but the weight may differ slightly. Always check your recipe’s requirements, especially for air fryer dishes where balance is key.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Cream</h2>  
<p>One frequent error is scooping cream directly from the container, which can lead to uneven measurements. Instead, pour it into a measuring cup at eye level. Another mistake is confusing millilitres with grams—cream’s density means 335 ml isn’t the same as 335g.</p>
