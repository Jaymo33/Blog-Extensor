<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Too many rolled oats might dry out your dish, while too few could leave it soggy. This is especially important for recipes like oat-crusted chicken or homemade granola bars. If you’re new to air frying, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> offers plenty of inspiration.</p>  

<p>Rolled oats are a versatile ingredient, often used in baking, coating, or as a binder. Their light texture means even small weight differences can affect your dish. For example, 20g of rolled oats to ounces (0.71 oz) is a common measurement for single servings or small batches. Always use a digital scale for the best accuracy.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 20g of rolled oats without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can use tablespoons to approximate 20g of rolled oats. Roughly 3 level tablespoons equal 20g, but this can vary slightly depending on how tightly packed the oats are. For air fryer recipes, it’s always better to weigh ingredients when possible.
