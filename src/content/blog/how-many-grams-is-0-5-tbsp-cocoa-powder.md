<h3>How to measure 0.5 tbsp cocoa powder without scales</h3>
<p>If you don't have digital scales, you can still measure 0.5 tbsp of cocoa powder accurately. Use a proper tablespoon measure and fill it halfway, levelling off the top with a straight edge. Remember that 0.5 tablespoon of cocoa powder equals about 2.5 grams, which is roughly half of a standard UK teaspoon when packed lightly.</p>

<h2>Common mistakes when measuring cocoa powder</h2>
<p>One frequent error is scooping cocoa powder directly from the bag with the measuring spoon, which compacts it and leads to using too much. For 0.5 tablespoon of cocoa powder to grams conversion, always spoon the powder lightly into the measure. Another mistake is confusing US and UK tablespoon measurements - all conversions here use UK standard measures.</p>

<p>Humidity can also affect how much 0.5 tbsp of cocoa powder weighs in grams. If your cocoa powder has clumped, sift it before measuring to ensure accuracy. This step is particularly crucial for air fryer recipes where moisture content significantly impacts cooking times and results.</p>

<h3>Storing cocoa powder for accurate measuring</h3>
<p>To maintain consistent measurements like 0.5 tbsp cocoa powder to grams, store your cocoa powder in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. This prevents moisture absorption which can change the weight and density. Proper storage also preserves the flavour, essential for delicious air fryer chocolate treats.</p>
