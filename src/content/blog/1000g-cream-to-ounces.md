<h2>How to Measure 1000g of Cream Accurately</h2>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the easiest way to measure 1000g of cream. Simply place your container on the scale, tare it to zero, and pour the cream until it hits 1000g. If you don’t have a scale, 1000g of cream roughly equals 35.27 ounces, or about 4.4 cups. However, scales are more precise, especially for air fryer cooking.</p>  
<p>For those who prefer volume measurements, remember that cream’s density can vary slightly. Whipped cream, for instance, will take up more space than liquid cream. Always opt for weight measurements when precision is key, like in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>. A small discrepancy can alter the outcome, so stick to grams or ounces for best results.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Leftover Cream</h3>  
<p>If you don’t use all 1000g of cream at once, store it in an airtight container in the fridge. It typically lasts 3–5 days, but always check for spoilage. For longer storage, freeze it in portions. Thawed cream works well in cooked dishes, though it may not whip as smoothly.
