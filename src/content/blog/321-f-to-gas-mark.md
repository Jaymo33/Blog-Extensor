<h2>Gas Mark vs. Fahrenheit: Key Differences</h2>

Air fryers, especially UK models, often display both, but knowing equivalents helps when adapting older recipes. For instance, a 1970s cookbook’s "Gas Mark 3" instruction translates perfectly to modern air fryers at 321°F.</p>
<p>Unlike ovens, air fryers don’t have "Gas Mark" buttons, but their digital controls make inputting 321°F straightforward.
