<h2>How to measure 0.125 cup of sunflower seeds</h2>
<p>Measuring small quantities like 0.125 cup of sunflower seeds can be tricky without the right tools. A standard measuring cup with clear markings is ideal. Alternatively, a kitchen scale can provide the most accurate measurement, showing 0.5 ounces for 0.125 cup of sunflower seeds.</p>
<p>If you don't have a measuring cup, you can use tablespoons as a substitute. 0.125 cup equals 2 tablespoons, which is roughly 0.5 ounces of sunflower seeds. This method works well for small batches of air fryer snacks or toppings.</p>

<h3>Converting 0.125 cup of sunflower seeds to other units</h3>
<p>Besides ounces, you might need to convert 0.125 cup of sunflower seeds to grams or milliliters. 0.125 cup equals about 14 grams or 30 milliliters. These conversions are handy when following international recipes or using digital scales that display metric measurements.</p>

<h2>Using sunflower seeds in air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Sunflower seeds add crunch and nutrition to many air fryer dishes. From sprinkling 0.125 cup over salads to incorporating them into energy bars, their versatility is unmatched. <p>When roasting sunflower seeds in your air fryer, spreading them evenly is crucial. A 0.125 cup portion (0.5 ounces) is perfect for a single layer, ensuring even cooking. Shake the basket occasionally for uniform browning and to prevent burning.</p>

<h3>Storing sunflower seeds for air fryer use</h3>
<p>Proper storage keeps sunflower seeds fresh for your air fryer creations. After measuring out 0.125 cup portions, store the rest in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. This maintains their quality for future use in recipes or as standalone snacks.</p>

<h2>Common mistakes when measuring sunflower seeds</h2>
<p>One error is packing sunflower seeds into the measuring cup, which can nearly double the weight. For 0.125 cup, gently pour the seeds into the cup without pressing them down. This ensures you get the correct 0.5 ounce measurement.</p>
<p>Another mistake is using liquid measuring cups for dry ingredients like sunflower seeds. These cups are designed differently and can lead to inaccurate measurements. Always use dry measuring cups for 0.125 cup portions of sunflower seeds or other dry ingredients.</p>
