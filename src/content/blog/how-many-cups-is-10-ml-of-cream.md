<h2>How to Measure 10 ml of Cream Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>Not everyone has a kitchen scale, but that doesn’t mean you can’t measure 10 ml of cream accurately. A standard metric measuring spoon set usually includes a 10 ml (or 2 tsp) measure, making it easy to portion out small amounts. If you don’t have one, a tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so you can estimate slightly less than a full spoon.</p>  

<p>For those who prefer using cups, remember that 10 ml of cream is roughly 0.042 cups. While this might seem negligible, in delicate recipes like custards or mousses, even small deviations can affect the outcome. <h3>Converting 10 ml of Cream in Different Cup Sizes</h3>  
<p>Depending on where you live, cup measurements can vary. In the UK, a standard metric cup is 250 ml, while in the US, it’s 240 ml. This means 10 ml of cream converts to 0.04 UK cups or 0.042 US cups. While the difference is minor, it’s worth noting if you’re following an international recipe.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Leftover Cream</h3>  
<p>If you’ve opened a carton of cream for a recipe that only requires 10 ml, you’ll want to store the rest properly. Transfer it to an airtight container and keep it refrigerated for up to five days.
