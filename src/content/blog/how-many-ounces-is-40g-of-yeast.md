<h2>How to measure 40g of yeast in ounces</h2>
<p>Measuring yeast by weight (grams or ounces) is more accurate than using volume (teaspoons or tablespoons). A digital kitchen scale is the best tool for this job. Simply place your container on the scale, tare it to zero, and add yeast until you reach 40g (or 1.41 oz). This method eliminates guesswork and ensures precision, which is especially helpful for air fryer baking where small adjustments matter.</p>

<h3>Using measuring spoons as an alternative</h3>
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can approximate 40g of yeast with measuring spoons. One tablespoon of active dry yeast weighs about 9g, so 40g would be roughly 4.5 tablespoons. However, this method is less precise, so for best results, invest in a scale. <p>Not all yeast is the same, and the type you use can affect measurements. Active dry yeast, instant yeast, and fresh yeast have different weights per volume. For example, 40g of fresh yeast isn’t the same as 40g of dry yeast in terms of rising power. Always check your recipe for the specific type required.
