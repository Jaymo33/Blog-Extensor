<h2>Why accurate maple syrup measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of maple syrup ensures your air fryer dishes aren’t overly sweet or too dry. A slight variation can alter the texture and flavour of baked goods or glazes. For example, air fryer desserts like cinnamon rolls or sticky toffee pudding rely on precise syrup quantities. Too much syrup can make them soggy, while too little may leave them bland. <h3>How to measure maple syrup without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy. Pour the syrup slowly to avoid air bubbles, which can skew measurements. For sticky ingredients like maple syrup, lightly greasing the cup helps it slide out easily. Alternatively, use the displacement method by measuring water first, then adding syrup until the liquid rises to the desired level. These tricks ensure precise amounts for air fryer cooking.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for maple syrup in air fryers</h2>  
<p>Maple syrup isn’t just for pancakes—it’s a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. Use it to glaze roasted vegetables like carrots or sweet potatoes for a caramelised finish. It also works wonders in marinades for chicken or salmon, adding a subtle sweetness. For desserts, drizzle it over air-fried apples or mix it into granola for a crunchy topping. You can even brush it over bacon for a sweet-and-savory twist. Since maple syrup burns easily, adjust your air fryer temperature to avoid scorching. <h3>Converting maple syrup for UK vs. US recipes</h3>  
<p>UK and US cup measurements differ slightly, which can affect conversions. A UK cup is 284ml, while a US cup is 240ml, meaning weights vary too. For maple syrup, 1 US cup equals 11.5 ounces, whereas 1 UK cup is roughly 13 ounces. Always check the recipe’s origin to ensure accuracy.
