<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so using the right amount of tomato sauce prevents soggy or unevenly cooked meals. <h3>How to Measure Tomato Sauce Correctly</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy when converting 3.25 cups of tomato sauce to millilitres. Fill the cup to the 3.25 mark without pressing down, as compacting can skew the measurement. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale set to millilitres works too. For sticky sauces like tomato, lightly grease the cup for easy pouring.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Tomato Sauce Thicknesses</h3>  
<p>Thicker sauces may measure slightly differently in millilitres due to density. If your tomato sauce is very chunky, consider blending it briefly for a smoother consistency before measuring. For thin sauces, reduce the liquid elsewhere in the recipe to balance the texture. Always taste and adjust seasonings after converting measurements.</p>
