<h3>How to Measure 0.25 Cups of Yogurt Without a Measuring Cup</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup handy, use a tablespoon for smaller quantities. Since 1 tablespoon is roughly 15 ml, 4 tablespoons will give you close to 59 ml (0.25 cups). For thicker yogurts like Greek yogurt, levelling off the spoon ensures accuracy.</p>  

<h2>Converting 0.25 Cups of Yogurt to ml for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>Whether you're following a British or American recipe, 0.25 cups of yogurt converts to 59 ml universally. This measurement works for everything from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer pancakes</a> to marinades. Need a larger batch? Simply multiply—0.5 cups would be 118 ml, and so on.</p>  

<p>For baking, precise yogurt measurements prevent dry or dense results. If a recipe calls for 0.25 cups of yogurt (59 ml), stick to it unless adjustments are noted. <h3>Does Yogurt Type Affect the ml Measurement?</h3>  
<p>While 0.25 cups of any yogurt equals 59 ml by volume, thicker varieties like Greek yogurt may weigh more. For absolute precision in baking, consider weighing it—59 ml of regular yogurt is about 60 grams, while Greek yogurt could be closer to 70 grams.</p>  

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Yogurt for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>One frequent error is scooping yogurt straight from the tub without levelling, leading to excess. Always use a flat edge to scrape off the top for an exact 0.25 cups (59 ml). Another pitfall? Confusing fluid ounces with millilitres—remember, 59 ml is the golden number here.</p>
