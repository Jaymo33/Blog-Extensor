<h2>How to measure 0.25 cup of baking soda</h2>
<p>Measuring 0.25 cup of baking soda is simple with the right tools. Use a dry measuring cup for the most accurate results, and level it off with a straight edge. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale can help you measure out 2 ounces directly. This method is especially useful for air fryer recipes where precision is key.</p>
<p>For those who prefer visual aids, 0.25 cup of baking soda fills about half of a standard 1/2 cup measure. You can also use tablespoons—0.25 cup equals 4 tablespoons. <h2>Converting other baking soda measurements</h2>
<p>If you need to convert other amounts, here’s a quick reference: 1 cup of baking soda equals 8 ounces, so 0.25 cup is 2 ounces. This ratio is consistent, making it easy to scale recipes up or down. For example, if a recipe calls for 0.5 cup of baking soda, that’s 4 ounces. Keeping these conversions in mind simplifies your cooking process.</p>

<h3>Tips for storing baking soda</h3>
<p>To keep your baking soda fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Exposure to moisture can cause clumping, which makes measuring 0.25 cup (2 ounces) more difficult. Proper storage ensures your baking soda stays effective for all your air fryer recipes and cleaning needs.</p>
