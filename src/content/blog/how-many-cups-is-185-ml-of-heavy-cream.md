<p>185 ml of heavy cream is approximately 0.78 cups (just under ¾ cup). This conversion is essential for precise baking and cooking, especially when using an air fryer where measurements can impact texture and consistency. Whether you're whipping up a creamy dessert or a rich sauce, knowing the exact cup equivalent of 185 ml of heavy cream ensures your dish turns out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>When working with heavy cream, even small measurement discrepancies can affect the outcome of your dish. Too much cream can make sauces overly rich or desserts too dense, while too little might leave them dry or lacking flavour. For air fryer recipes, precise measurements are even more critical, as the cooking process is faster and more intense. A slight variation could mean the difference between a perfectly crispy dish and one that’s undercooked or soggy.</p>

Measuring 185 ml of heavy cream correctly ensures your dish maintains the intended texture and flavour.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 185 ml of Heavy Cream Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 185 ml of heavy cream accurately using standard measuring cups. Since 185 ml converts to roughly 0.78 cups, you can fill a measuring cup just under the ¾ cup mark. For even more precision, use a liquid measuring cup with millilitre markings. This method is especially useful when preparing air fryer recipes where consistency matters.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 185 ml of Heavy Cream</h2>  
<p>Heavy cream is a versatile ingredient in both baking and cooking. In air fryer recipes, it’s often used to create rich, creamy textures in dishes like mashed potatoes, soups, or desserts. If you're experimenting with your own recipes, keeping conversions like 185 ml to cups handy ensures you can adapt measurements on the fly without compromising quality.</p>  

<h3>Converting 185 ml of Heavy Cream for Different Recipes</h3>  
<p>While 185 ml of heavy cream is roughly 0.78 cups, some recipes may call for slight adjustments. For instance, if a recipe asks for 200 ml, you’ll need just over ¾ cup. Referencing a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> can help you make these small tweaks effortlessly. <h2>Tips for Storing and Using Heavy Cream</h2>  
<p>Once opened, heavy cream should be used within a few days for the best results. If your recipe calls for 185 ml of heavy cream but you’ve only used part of the container, ensure the rest is stored properly in the fridge. For air fryer cooking, room-temperature cream blends more smoothly into batters and sauces, so take it out a few minutes before use.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Cream Types</h3>  
<p>Not all creams are the same—double cream, whipping cream, and single cream have different fat contents. If a recipe specifies 185 ml of heavy cream, substituting it with a lighter cream may alter the texture. For air fryer recipes, heavy cream’s higher fat content often provides the best results, so stick to the recommended type whenever possible.</p>
