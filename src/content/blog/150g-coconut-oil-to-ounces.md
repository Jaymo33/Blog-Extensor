Whether you’re making crispy roasted vegetables or a decadent dessert, getting the right amount of coconut oil ensures perfect results. <h2>Why converting 150g of coconut oil to ounces matters</h2>
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial in cooking, especially when using an air fryer. Coconut oil is a popular choice for air frying because of its high smoke point and rich flavour. Converting 150g of coconut oil to ounces (5.29 oz) helps you follow recipes precisely, whether you’re baking or frying. Many UK recipes use grams, but if your kitchen scales only show ounces, this conversion is a lifesaver. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>

<p>Using the right amount of coconut oil ensures your food cooks evenly and doesn’t dry out. Too little oil, and your chips might stick; too much, and they could become greasy. For perfect air fryer results, measure your coconut oil carefully. If you’re experimenting with new recipes, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>, precise measurements are even more important. A little attention to detail goes a long way in the kitchen.</p>

<h3>How to measure 150g of coconut oil without scales</h3>
<p>If you don’t have kitchen scales, you can still measure 150g of coconut oil using volume. One tablespoon of coconut oil weighs around 14g, so 150g is roughly 10.5 tablespoons. Remember, coconut oil’s density can vary slightly depending on temperature, so this method isn’t as precise as weighing. For best results, especially in air fryer cooking, we recommend investing in digital scales. They’re affordable and make conversions like 150g coconut oil to ounces effortless.</p>

<h2>Using 150g of coconut oil in air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Coconut oil is versatile in the air fryer, perfect for everything from roasting potatoes to baking cakes. When a recipe calls for 150g of coconut oil (5.29 oz), you’re likely making something deliciously rich. Try it in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> for a tropical twist on classic bakes. The oil’s subtle sweetness enhances flavours while keeping treats moist. Just remember to melt it gently before measuring for accuracy.</p>

<p>For savoury dishes, 150g of coconut oil can coat vegetables or marinade proteins beautifully. Its high smoke point makes it ideal for air frying at higher temperatures. If you’re cooking frozen foods, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/frozen-foods>frozen foods collection</a>, a light coating of coconut oil helps achieve that perfect crispness. Always measure carefully – whether you’re working in grams or ounces – for consistent results every time.</p>

<h3>Storing and handling coconut oil for air frying</h3>
<p>Proper storage keeps your coconut oil fresh and ready for air fryer use. At room temperature, it’s solid but melts easily when heated. When measuring 150g (5.29 oz), scoop it packed if solid or pour carefully if liquid. After use, clean your air fryer with our recommended <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a> to remove any oil residue. Well-maintained equipment and precise measurements are the secrets to air fryer success.</p>
