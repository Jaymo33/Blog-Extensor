<h2>Why Accurate Rice Measurements Matter in an Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect rice quantities can lead to uneven cooking or soggy results. Using 1.5 cups of rice (300g US / 270g UK) ensures the grains cook uniformly without clumping. For best results, pair your rice with proteins like chicken or shrimp—check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> for inspiration. Precise measurements also help when scaling recipes up or down for meal prep. Too much rice can overcrowd the basket, while too little may dry out quickly.</p>  

<p>Different rice types have slight weight variations, but 1.5 cups generally fall within the 270–300g range. Brown rice, for example, is denser than white rice, so adjust slightly if needed. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> simplifies these adjustments for hassle-free cooking. Remember, rinsing rice before cooking removes excess starch, improving texture in the air fryer.</p>  

<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements for Rice</h3>  
<p>In the US, 1 cup of rice equals 200g, making 1.5 cups 300g. UK metric cups, however, hold 180g, so 1.5 cups equal 270g. This difference matters when following international recipes or using imported rice brands. Always check the origin of your recipe to avoid measurement mishaps. For UK home cooks, weighing rice in grams eliminates guesswork.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 1.5 Cups of Rice Without Scales</h2>  
<p>If you don’t own kitchen scales, use the spoon-and-level method for accuracy. Gently spoon rice into a measuring cup until heaped, then level off the top with a knife. Avoid packing the rice down, as this adds extra weight. For 1.5 cups, simply repeat the process once for a full cup and once for a half-cup. This technique works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable-packed rice dishes</a> in the air fryer.</p>  

<p>Another trick is to repurpose household items: a standard UK mug holds roughly 250g of rice, while a US coffee cup measures about 240g. These approximations help in a pinch but aren’t as reliable as proper measuring tools. Invest in a set of nested cups or a digital scale for consistent results. Clean measuring tools are crucial—learn how to maintain them with our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner guide</a>.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times for 1.5 Cups of Rice</h3>  
<p>In an air fryer, 1.5 cups of rice (300g US / 270g UK) typically cooks in 15–20 minutes at 180°C. Add a splash of water or broth to prevent drying, and stir halfway for even cooking. For firmer rice, reduce the liquid slightly; for stickier rice, add a teaspoon more.
