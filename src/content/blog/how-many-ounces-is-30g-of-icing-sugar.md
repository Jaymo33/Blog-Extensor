<h2>How to Convert 30g of Icing Sugar to Ounces</h2>  
<p>The conversion from grams to ounces is straightforward. One gram is approximately 0.035 ounces, so 30g of icing sugar works out to roughly 1.06 ounces. For most recipes, rounding to 1 ounce is acceptable, but for precision baking, sticking to 1.06 ounces ensures accuracy. If you're using a digital scale, simply switch the unit setting from grams to ounces for an instant readout.</p>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can use measuring spoons or cups, though this method is less precise. One tablespoon of icing sugar is about 8g, so 30g would be just under 4 tablespoons. However, for best results, especially in air fryer baking, we recommend weighing your ingredients. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> for more ideas on using icing sugar in your air fryer creations.</p>  

<h3>Common Uses for 30g of Icing Sugar</h3>  
<p>Icing sugar is incredibly versatile. A 30g portion is perfect for dusting desserts like air fryer doughnuts or making a small batch of glaze for cookies. It’s also ideal for lightening cake batters or sweetening whipped cream. Because air fryers cook quickly, using the right amount of icing sugar ensures your treats caramelise evenly without burning.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Measuring Icing Sugar</h3>  
<p>Always sift icing sugar before measuring to avoid lumps. If your recipe calls for 30g of icing sugar to ounces, fluff it up with a fork first for an accurate measure. Store icing sugar in an airtight container to prevent clumping, and keep it in a cool, dry place.
