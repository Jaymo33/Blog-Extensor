<p>2 cups of minced onion equals approximately 8 ounces (or 226 grams). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, where precise measurements ensure even cooking and perfect results. Whether you're prepping a savoury onion topping or adding depth to a dish, knowing the weight helps streamline your cooking process.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on hot air circulation, so ingredient quantities directly impact cooking times and textures. Too much minced onion can overcrowd the basket, leading to uneven crisping. Too little might dry out your dish. For consistent results, measuring by weight (ounces) is often more reliable than volume (cups). <h3>How to Measure Minced Onion Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 2 cups to ounces conversion, lightly pack the minced onion into a measuring cup without compressing it. Overpacking can add extra weight, skewing your results. If your recipe calls for UK ounces (1 UK fluid ounce = 28.4 ml), the weight remains roughly the same—around 8 ounces per 2 cups.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Onion Measurements</h3>  
<p>If your recipe uses different units, remember: 1 cup of minced onion equals about 4 ounces. Larger quantities, like 4 cups, would be roughly 16 ounces. For whole onions, one medium onion yields about 1 cup minced (4 ounces). Adjustments may be needed based on onion variety and moisture content.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Cooking with Minced Onion in an Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>To prevent burning, toss minced onion with a bit of oil before air frying. Spread it evenly in the basket for consistent crisping. For caramelised results, cook at 160°C for 10–12 minutes, stirring halfway.
