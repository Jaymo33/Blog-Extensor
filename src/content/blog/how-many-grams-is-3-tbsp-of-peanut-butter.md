<h2>Why accurate peanut butter measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of peanut butter ensures your air fryer dishes turn out just right. Too much can make recipes overly dense, while too little might leave them dry. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>peanut butter cookies</a> rely on this 3 tbsp to grams conversion for the ideal texture. Consistency is key, especially when adapting traditional recipes for air frying. Always measure carefully to avoid disappointment.</p>

<h2>How to measure peanut butter without scales</h2>  
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem. You can still measure 3 tbsp of peanut butter accurately using simple household tools. A standard UK tablespoon holds about 16 grams of peanut butter, so three level tablespoons will give you roughly 48 grams. Avoid heaping spoonfuls unless the recipe specifies otherwise. For sticky ingredients like peanut butter, lightly greasing the spoon helps it slide off cleanly.</p>  

<h3>Using measuring spoons vs. weight</h3>  
<p>While measuring spoons are convenient, weighing ingredients is often more precise. This is especially true for air fryer recipes, where even small deviations can affect cooking times. If you’re serious about perfect results, investing in a digital scale is worthwhile. Many budget-friendly options are available, and they’re indispensable for baking. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> where precision is paramount.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 3 tbsp of peanut butter in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>This quantity is perfect for small-batch cooking. Try it in peanut butter glazes for chicken or as a base for satay sauces. It’s also ideal for energy bites or <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast bars</a> made in your air fryer. The rich, nutty flavour enhances both sweet and savoury dishes. Remember, 3 tbsp (48g) strikes the right balance without overpowering other ingredients.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting recipes for different peanut butter types</h3>  
<p>Natural peanut butter tends to be runnier, while commercial brands are thicker due to added oils. If substituting, you may need to tweak other liquids in your recipe. For instance, when making air fryer granola, reduce added oil if using standard peanut butter.
