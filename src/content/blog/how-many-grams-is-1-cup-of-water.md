<p>1.0 cups of water weighs exactly 240 grams. This measurement is standard for both UK and US cup measurements, making it easy to convert for recipes. Whether you’re using an air fryer or a conventional oven, knowing this conversion ensures precision in cooking and baking.</p>

For example, a perfect <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer cake</a> relies on precise liquid ratios.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer enthusiasts prefer weighing ingredients instead of using volume measurements. Grams provide a more reliable standard, especially when scaling recipes up or down. If you’re experimenting with <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a> or sauces, this conversion ensures your results are spot-on every time.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 1.0 Cups of Water Correctly</h2>  
<p>Using a liquid measuring cup is the easiest way to measure 1.0 cups of water. Fill it to the 240ml mark, which directly translates to 240 grams. For even greater accuracy, a digital kitchen scale eliminates any guesswork. This method is especially helpful for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>converting other ingredients</a> in your air fryer recipes.</p>  

<h3>Does Temperature Affect the Weight of Water?</h3>  
<p>While water’s density changes slightly with temperature, the difference is negligible for cooking purposes. Whether cold or boiling, 1.0 cups of water will still weigh approximately 240 grams. This consistency makes it a reliable measurement for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable steaming</a> or broth-based dishes in the air fryer.</p>
