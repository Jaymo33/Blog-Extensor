<h2>Why accurate honey measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so using the right amount of honey ensures your food cooks perfectly. Too much honey can cause burning or uneven caramelisation, while too little might leave your dish lacking flavour. For example, a honey-glazed salmon or sticky chicken wing recipe depends on precise measurements. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> includes plenty of dishes where honey plays a starring role. Always measure honey by weight for the best results in your air fryer.</p>

<h3>How to measure honey without the mess</h3>  
<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like honey can be tricky, but a few simple tricks make it easier. Lightly greasing your measuring cup with oil helps the honey slide out cleanly. Alternatively, warm the honey slightly to make it less viscous. If you’re converting 1.25 cups of honey to ounces regularly, consider investing in a digital scale for accuracy. Many air fryer enthusiasts swear by weighing ingredients for consistent results.
