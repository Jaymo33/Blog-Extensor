<h2>Understanding Icing Sugar Measurements</h2>
<p>When converting 150 grams of icing sugar to cups, the method of filling your cup matters greatly. Scooping directly from the bag compacts the sugar, while spooning it lightly gives a more accurate measurement. For air fryer recipes where consistency is key, we recommend using digital scales for 150 grams of icing sugar rather than relying solely on cup measurements. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> includes all the common baking measurements you'll need.</p>
<p>Different countries have varying cup sizes - the UK uses 250ml cups while the US uses 240ml. This means 150 grams of icing sugar in UK cups might differ slightly from US measurements. When following recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake category</a>, always check which cup measurement system the recipe uses to ensure your air fryer bakes turn out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Why Accuracy Matters in Air Fryer Baking</h2>
<p>Air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, meaning ingredient ratios need to be precise. Using exactly 150 grams of icing sugar (rather than estimating cups) ensures your frostings and batters have the right consistency. Too much sugar can make batters spread too thin in the air fryer basket, while too little affects texture and sweetness.</p>
<p>Many air fryer dessert recipes at <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a> specify weights rather than volumes for this reason. When converting 150g icing sugar to cups for air fryer cookies or cakes, consider that humidity and how packed the sugar is can affect the volume measurement. That's why professional bakers prefer weighing ingredients like 150 grams of icing sugar for consistent results.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Icing Sugar Perfectly</h3>
<p>To measure 150 grams of icing sugar accurately without scales, fluff the sugar in its bag first, then gently spoon it into your measuring cup. Level off the top with a straight edge - don't tap or pack it down. Remember that 150 grams of icing sugar equals about 1.2 cups when measured this way. For air fryer baking where precision counts, investing in a £10 digital scale from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/accessories>accessories guide</a> is worthwhile.</p>

<p>Once you've mastered converting 150 grams of icing sugar to cups, you can apply the same principles to other quantities. The general rule is that 125 grams of icing sugar equals 1 cup, so 150 grams is slightly more. When adapting conventional oven recipes for your air fryer at <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>, these conversions become particularly important due to the air fryer's intense heat circulation.</p>

Keeping our conversion charts handy helps ensure your air fryer bakes are consistently successful.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Icing Sugar</h3>
<p>The biggest error when converting 150 grams of icing sugar to cups is packing it down too firmly. This can make your measurement up to 25% off, leading to overly sweet or dense results in air fryer baking. Another pitfall is using liquid measuring cups instead of dry measuring cups - the spout on liquid cups makes leveling dry ingredients like 150 grams of icing sugar difficult.</p>
