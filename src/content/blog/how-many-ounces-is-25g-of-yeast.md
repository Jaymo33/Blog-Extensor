<p>25g of yeast equals approximately 0.88 ounces. This conversion is essential for bakers who need precise measurements, especially when adapting recipes for air fryer baking. Whether you're making bread, pizza dough, or sweet treats, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h3>How to measure 25g of yeast without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have digital scales, you can still measure 25g of yeast using volume. One teaspoon of active dry yeast weighs roughly 3g, so 25g would be about 8 teaspoons. However, for accuracy, we always recommend using a kitchen scale. This is especially true for air fryer baking, where even small measurement discrepancies can affect the outcome.</p>

<h3>Storing yeast for best results</h3>  
<p>To maintain your yeast’s potency, store it in an airtight container in the fridge or freezer. This is particularly important if you buy yeast in larger quantities. Proper storage ensures your 25g measurements remain consistent in their leavening power. Always check the expiry date too – old yeast won’t give you the rise you need for air fryer baking success.</p>  

<h2>Common yeast measurement conversions</h2>  
<p>Beyond 25g of yeast to ounces, here are some other useful conversions for UK bakers: 7g of yeast equals 0.25 oz (one standard packet), while 50g equals 1.76 oz. These conversions come in handy when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/bread>bread recipe category</a> includes several yeast-based recipes with measurements in both grams and ounces.</p>  

<p>When working with fresh yeast instead of dry, remember that the weights differ. 25g of fresh yeast equals about 12g of dry yeast (approximately 0.42 oz). This conversion is important as fresh and dry yeasts aren’t interchangeable weight-for-weight. Always check which type your recipe specifies before measuring.</p>  

<h3>Troubleshooting yeast measurements in air fryer baking</h3>  
<p>If your air fryer bakes aren’t rising properly, double-check your yeast measurements first. An accurate 25g to ounces conversion (0.88 oz) is just the start. The water temperature when activating yeast should be warm but not hot – about 38°C is ideal. Too hot, and you’ll kill the yeast; too cool, and it won’t activate properly.</p>
