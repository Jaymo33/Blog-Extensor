<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of flour is crucial, especially in air fryer cooking. Too much flour can make baked goods dense, while too little may cause them to fall apart. For example, if you're making air fryer cookies, 250g of plain flour (or 8.82 ounces) ensures the perfect texture. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> rely on precise measurements for foolproof results.</p>  

<p>Air fryers cook faster than conventional ovens, so ingredient ratios matter even more. A slight deviation in flour weight can affect cooking times and textures. That's why converting 250g of plain flour to ounces (8.82 oz) helps maintain accuracy. For more baking tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 250g of plain flour without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, you can use measuring cups to approximate 250g of plain flour. One UK cup of plain flour weighs around 120g, so 250g is roughly 2 cups plus 1 tablespoon. However, for air fryer recipes, we recommend using scales for precision. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> for more measurement hacks.</p>

<h3>Converting other flour quantities</h3>  
<p>Once you’ve mastered converting 250g of plain flour to ounces (8.82 oz), you can apply the same logic to other amounts. For example, 125g is roughly 4.41 ounces, and 500g is about 17.64 ounces. This knowledge is handy when scaling air fryer recipes up or down.</p>
