<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small deviations in measurements can affect texture and taste. Using 5 tbsp of plain flour without converting to grams might lead to overly dense or dry results. Air fryer recipes, in particular, rely on precise ratios to ensure even cooking. For example, a slight excess of flour in air-fried cookies can make them crumbly instead of chewy. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies these calculations, so you never have to guess.</p>  

<p>Many UK bakers prefer weighing ingredients for accuracy, as volume measurements like tablespoons can vary. A packed tablespoon of plain flour will weigh more than a lightly scooped one. By converting 5 tbsp of plain flour to grams, you eliminate this inconsistency. This is especially useful for air fryer recipes, where even distribution is key to crispy, golden results. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> for foolproof ideas.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 5 tbsp of plain flour correctly</h3>  
<p>To get the most accurate 40 grams from 5 tbsp of plain flour, use the spoon-and-level method. Lightly spoon the flour into the tablespoon without packing it down, then level off the excess with a knife. Avoid scooping directly from the bag, as this compacts the flour and leads to over-measuring. For air fryer baking, precision ensures your cakes rise evenly and your coatings stay crisp.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 5 tbsp of plain flour</h2>  
<p>This measurement is handy for small-batch recipes, like air-fried chicken tenders or single-serving cakes. Coating proteins with 5 tbsp of plain flour (40 grams) creates a light, crispy crust when air-fried. It’s also perfect for thickening sauces or making roux in smaller quantities. <h3>Converting other flour quantities</h3>  
<p>Once you’ve mastered the 5 tbsp to grams conversion, you can apply the same principles to other amounts. For instance, 1 tbsp of plain flour equals roughly 8 grams, while 10 tbsp would be 80 grams. This knowledge is invaluable when adapting recipes for your air fryer, ensuring consistent results every time.</p>
