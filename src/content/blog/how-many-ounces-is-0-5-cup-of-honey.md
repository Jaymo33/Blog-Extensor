<h2>Why Accurate Honey Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of honey in your air fryer recipes ensures balanced flavours and textures. Too much honey can make dishes overly sweet or sticky, while too little might leave them lacking depth. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>honey-glazed salmon recipe</a> relies on precise honey measurements to achieve the perfect caramelisation. Measuring 0.5 cup of honey in ounces (6 oz) guarantees consistency, whether you’re cooking for one or meal prepping for the week.</p>  

<p>Honey’s viscosity means volume measurements like cups can vary slightly, but weight measurements like ounces are far more reliable. This is especially important for air fryer cooking, where even heat distribution depends on ingredient consistency. If you’re unsure about conversions, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies the process, so you never have to guess.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.5 Cup of Honey Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 0.5 cup of honey accurately. Use a liquid measuring cup and fill it to the halfway mark, ensuring the honey settles evenly. For sticky ingredients like honey, lightly greasing the measuring cup with oil helps it slide out cleanly. This method is handy for quick recipes like our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>honey-mustard chicken</a>, where precision saves time.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 0.5 Cup of Honey in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Half a cup of honey (6 oz) is a versatile quantity for many air fryer dishes. It’s perfect for glazing proteins like chicken or salmon, adding sweetness to roasted vegetables, or even creating sticky desserts like honey-drizzled apples. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> often call for this amount to balance sweetness without overpowering other flavours. Honey’s natural sugars also help achieve a beautiful golden finish in the air fryer.</p>  

<p>For those who love meal prep, 0.5 cup of honey can be portioned into smaller batches for marinades or dressings. Just remember to store honey properly to maintain its quality. <h3>Tips for Working with Honey in the Air Fryer</h3>  
<p>Honey can caramelise quickly in the air fryer, so keep an eye on cooking times to avoid burning. For best results, mix honey with a bit of oil or water to thin it slightly before brushing it onto food. This ensures even coverage and prevents clumping.
