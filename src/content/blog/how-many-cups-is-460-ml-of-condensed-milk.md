<p>460 ml of condensed milk is approximately 1.94 cups (just under 2 cups). This conversion is essential for baking and cooking, especially when using an air fryer for recipes that call for precise measurements. Whether you're making fudge, cakes, or no-churn ice cream, knowing how to convert 460 ml of condensed milk to cups ensures your desserts turn out perfectly every time.</p>

<p>Condensed milk is a staple in many desserts, and even a slight variation in quantity can affect texture and sweetness. <h2>How to Measure 460 ml of Condensed Milk</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use standard cups to measure 460 ml of condensed milk. Since 1 cup equals 240 ml, 460 ml is just shy of 2 cups. For greater accuracy, use a kitchen scale—460 ml weighs about 520 grams. This method is especially useful for air fryer recipes where precision matters.</p>  

<h3>Using Condensed Milk in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>Condensed milk works wonderfully in air fryer desserts like banoffee pie or sticky toffee pudding. Its thick consistency helps bind ingredients without adding excess moisture. <h2>Common Conversions for Condensed Milk</h2>  
<p>Besides 460 ml, other common condensed milk measurements include 200 ml (0.85 cups) and 400 ml (1.69 cups). Knowing these conversions helps when scaling recipes up or down. If you're preparing a larger batch, like for a party, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion guide</a> ensures you get the ratios right.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Cup Sizes</h3>  
<p>In the UK, metric measurements are standard, while US recipes often use cups. If a recipe calls for 460 ml of condensed milk, but you only have cup measures, remember that 1.94 cups is the equivalent. This small adjustment ensures your air fryer bakes turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Working with Condensed Milk</h2>  
<p>Condensed milk can be sticky, so lightly greasing your measuring cup or spoon makes it easier to pour. For air fryer recipes, warming the condensed milk slightly helps it blend smoothly with other ingredients. If you're making a no-bake dessert, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> for creative ideas.</p>
