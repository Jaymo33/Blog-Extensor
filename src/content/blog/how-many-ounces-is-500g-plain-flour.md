Knowing how to convert 500g of plain flour to ounces ensures you never mess up a recipe due to measurement confusion. This is particularly useful for air fryer baking, where precision affects texture and rise.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 500g of Plain Flour Accurately</h3>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the best way to measure 500g of plain flour. Scooping flour directly from the bag can compress it, leading to inaccurate measurements. Instead, spoon the flour into your measuring cup or onto the scale lightly. If you don’t have a scale, 500g is roughly 4 cups, but weighing is always more precise for air fryer recipes.</p>

<p>500g of plain flour is a versatile quantity for many air fryer dishes. From crispy coatings for chicken to fluffy dough for air fryer cakes, this amount covers multiple servings. <h3>Does Plain Flour Weight Change in Different Brands?</h3>  
<p>While 500g of plain flour should always weigh 500g, density can vary slightly between brands. However, this difference is minimal and rarely affects recipes. For absolute precision in air fryer baking, stick to one brand if possible, especially for delicate recipes like soufflés or sponges.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing 500g of Plain Flour</h3>  
<p>Keep your flour fresh by storing it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. This prevents clumping and extends shelf life, ensuring your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time. If you buy in bulk, consider freezing half to maintain freshness—just bring it to room temperature before use.</p>
