Mustard is a common ingredient in many air fryer recipes, from glazes to dips, so getting the measurement right is key. <h2>Why Convert Grams to Ounces for Mustard?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, while others rely on ounces, making conversions essential for accuracy. Mustard, whether Dijon, English, or wholegrain, can vary in density, so measuring correctly ensures consistency. Air fryer cooking often requires smaller quantities, so even a slight mismeasurement can affect flavour. For more conversion tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Converting 5g of mustard to ounces is particularly useful when following international recipes or using digital scales. Most UK scales toggle between grams and ounces, but knowing the conversion helps when using measuring spoons or cups. Mustard’s thickness means volume measurements can be tricky, so weight is often more reliable. Whether you're making a glaze for salmon or a rub for chicken, precision matters.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 5g of Mustard Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, 5g of mustard is roughly half a tablespoon. However, this can vary depending on the mustard’s consistency—smooth mustard will weigh slightly less than grainy varieties. For air fryer recipes, using a measuring spoon ensures you don’t overpower delicate flavours. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>mustard-based recipes</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 5g of Mustard in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Mustard adds depth to marinades, glazes, and coatings in air fryer dishes. A small amount, like 5g, can enhance flavour without overwhelming. Try it in a honey-mustard glaze for chicken or a quick dip for air-fried potatoes. Just 5g can elevate a simple dish, making it a staple in many kitchens. <h3>Does Mustard Type Affect the Weight?</h3>  
<p>Yes, thicker mustards like wholegrain may weigh slightly more per gram than smoother varieties. However, 5g of any mustard will still be close to 0.18 ounces. For recipes requiring exact measurements, stick to weight rather than volume. This is especially important in air fryer cooking, where balance is key.</p>
