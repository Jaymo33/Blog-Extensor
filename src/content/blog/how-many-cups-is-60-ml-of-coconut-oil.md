<p>60 ml of coconut oil equals approximately 0.25 cups (or 1/4 cup). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, ensuring precise measurements for perfect results every time. Whether you’re baking, frying, or roasting, knowing how to convert 60 ml of coconut oil to cups can make all the difference in your cooking.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of coconut oil in your air fryer ensures even cooking and prevents dryness or greasiness. Too little oil, and your food might stick or dry out; too much, and it can become overly oily. For example, when making crispy air-fried chicken or roasted vegetables, 60 ml of coconut oil (or 0.25 cups) is often the perfect amount to coat ingredients evenly.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 60 ml of Coconut Oil Without a Measuring Cup</h2>  
<p>Don’t have a measuring cup? No problem! You can use a tablespoon to measure 60 ml of coconut oil. Since 1 tablespoon holds about 15 ml, you’ll need 4 tablespoons to reach 60 ml (which, as we’ve established, is 0.25 cups). This method is especially useful for smaller quantities like those needed for air fryer recipes.</p>

<h3>Converting 60 ml of Coconut Oil for Different Recipes</h3>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions About Coconut Oil Measurements</h2>  
<p>Many home cooks wonder whether 60 ml of coconut oil is the same as 60 grams. While the volume (ml) measures space, the weight (grams) measures mass.
