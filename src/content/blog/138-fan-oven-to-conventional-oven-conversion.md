<h2>Why Convert 138 Fan Oven to Celsius?</h2>
<p>Converting 138 fan oven to celsius is crucial for accurate baking and roasting. Fan ovens circulate hot air, reducing cooking times by up to 20%. This means a 138°C fan oven setting might cook food faster than a conventional oven at the same temperature. For air fryer users, this is particularly relevant, as air fryers also use rapid air technology. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> includes adjusted timings for all cooking methods.</p>
<p>When converting 138 fan oven to conventional oven temperatures, always consider the recipe type. Delicate items like soufflés or custards might need finer adjustments. For hearty dishes like roasted meats or potatoes, the 20°C increase usually suffices. If you're unsure, start checking for doneness a few minutes earlier than the recipe suggests. This proactive approach prevents overcooking and ensures perfect results.</p>

<h3>How Does 138 Fan Oven Compare to Air Fryer Temperatures?</h3>
<p>Air fryers and fan ovens operate similarly, both using fans to circulate hot air. This means 138°C in a fan oven often translates directly to an air fryer setting. However, air fryers are smaller, so cooking times might be shorter. For example, crispy <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>potato recipes</a> might cook 5-10 minutes faster in an air fryer. Second, consider the dish's moisture content - drier foods might need slight temperature reductions to prevent over-browning. This simple rule makes recipe adaptation straightforward, whether you're using an oven or air fryer.</p>
