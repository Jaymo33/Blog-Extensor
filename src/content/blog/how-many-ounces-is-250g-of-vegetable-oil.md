<p>Using the right amount of vegetable oil in your air fryer can affect texture, crispiness, and even cooking times. Many UK recipes use grams, but if your kitchen scale measures in ounces, this conversion will save the day. <h2>How to measure 250g of vegetable oil without a scale</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 250g of vegetable oil using volume. One tablespoon of vegetable oil weighs about 14g, so 250g is roughly 18 tablespoons or just over 1 cup. However, for the most accurate results, especially in baking, a scale is best. Converting 250g of vegetable oil to ounces (8.82 oz) gives you a precise measurement that’s easy to work with. <h2>Converting other amounts of vegetable oil</h2>
<p>If you need to convert other amounts of vegetable oil from grams to ounces, the formula is simple: multiply the grams by 0.035274. For example, 100g of vegetable oil is 3.53 ounces, and 500g is 17.64 ounces. This conversion works for all types of cooking oils, including olive oil and sunflower oil. Remember, 250g of vegetable oil is 8.82 ounces, a measurement you’ll likely use often in air frying.</p>
