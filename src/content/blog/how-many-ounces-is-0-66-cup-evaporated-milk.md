<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven cooking. Too much evaporated milk might make your dish soggy, while too little could dry it out. This is especially crucial for recipes like custards or creamy sauces. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe hub</a> for dishes that require precise measurements. Always measure ingredients like evaporated milk carefully for the best results.</p>

<h3>How to measure 0.66 cup of evaporated milk</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for the most accurate results. Fill it to the 2/3 cup mark, then check the weight on a kitchen scale for confirmation. If you don’t have a scale, remember that 0.66 cup is roughly 5.28 ounces. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> that often use evaporated milk.</p>

<h2>Common uses for evaporated milk in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Evaporated milk adds richness to dishes like mac and cheese, custards, and even some bread recipes. Its concentrated texture makes it ideal for air fryer cooking, where moisture control is key. Try it in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> for a creamy twist. Always measure carefully—0.66 cup (5.28 ounces) is a common quantity for smaller batches.</p>
