<h2>How to measure 10g of chopped onion</h2>
<p>Measuring 10g of chopped onion is simple with the right tools. A digital kitchen scale will give you the most accurate measurement, but if you don't have one, there are alternative methods. Approximately, 10g of chopped onion equals about 2 tablespoons when lightly packed.</p>
<p>For those who prefer visual measurements, 10g of chopped onion is roughly equivalent to a small shallot or about one-eighth of a medium onion. These estimates can be particularly helpful when preparing quick <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> where precision matters but time is limited.</p>

<h3>The importance of onion size in measurements</h3>
<p>Onion sizes can vary significantly, which is why weight measurements are more reliable than volume for consistent results. A 10g portion from a large onion will look quite different from the same weight of a small onion. This is particularly important in air fryer cooking where even distribution affects cooking time and crispiness.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with chopped onion in air fryers</h3>
<p>When using chopped onion in your air fryer, there are a few tricks to ensure the best results. First, pat the onion dry after chopping to remove excess moisture. This helps achieve better browning and crispiness. Second, distribute the onion evenly in the basket to promote even cooking.</p>
