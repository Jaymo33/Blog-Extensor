<h2>Understanding the conversion</h2>
<p>Converting 0.125 cup of tomato paste to ounces is straightforward once you know the ratio. One standard US cup of tomato paste weighs about 8 ounces, so 0.125 cup is simply one-eighth of that amount. This measurement is particularly useful when following air fryer recipes that call for small quantities of tomato paste. Many of our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> use tomato paste as a flavour base, so getting the measurement right is key.</p>

<p>For UK cooks, it's important to note that fluid ounces and weight ounces can differ slightly. However, for tomato paste, which has a thick consistency, the weight measurement in ounces is what matters most in cooking. This small amount of tomato paste can make a big difference in dishes like our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken recipes</a>, where it adds depth to marinades and sauces.</p>

<h3>Why accurate measurements matter</h3>
<p>When using your air fryer, precise measurements ensure consistent results every time. Too much tomato paste can make a dish overly tangy, while too little might leave it lacking flavour. <h3>Measuring tips for tomato paste</h3>
<p>Measuring 0.125 cup of tomato paste can be tricky due to its thick consistency. The easiest method is to use measuring spoons - 0.125 cup equals 2 tablespoons. Alternatively, you can weigh it on a kitchen scale for perfect accuracy. This precision is especially valuable when adapting conventional oven recipes for your air fryer.</p>

<h2>Converting other tomato paste measurements</h2>
<p>While we've focused on 0.125 cup of tomato paste, you might need to convert other amounts too. Here's a quick reference: 0.25 cup equals 2 ounces, 0.5 cup equals 4 ounces, and 1 full cup equals 8 ounces. These conversions are essential when scaling air fryer recipes up or down to suit your needs.</p>

<p>Tomato paste often comes in small cans or tubes, so knowing these measurements helps prevent waste.
