<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve the perfect texture and flavour. Too much golden syrup can make dishes overly sweet or sticky, while too little might leave them dry. Measuring 0.75 cup of golden syrup as 8.5 ounces ensures consistency across recipes. This is especially important for baked goods like cakes or cookies, where balance is key. <h3>How to measure golden syrup without a scale</h3>
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 0.75 cup of golden syrup accurately. Lightly grease your measuring cup with oil to help the syrup slide out easily. Fill the cup to the 0.75 mark, then level it off with a straight edge. Remember, 0.75 cup equals 8.5 ounces, so adjust your recipe accordingly. <h3>Storing golden syrup for air fryer cooking</h3>
<p>Proper storage keeps golden syrup at the right consistency for accurate measuring. Always seal the container tightly to prevent crystallization. If your syrup hardens, gently warm it before measuring out 0.75 cup (8.5 ounces). This ensures you get the correct amount for your air fryer recipes. Store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight for best results.</p>
