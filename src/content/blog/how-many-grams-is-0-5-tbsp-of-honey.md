<h2>Why measuring honey accurately matters</h2>  
<p>Honey’s thickness and stickiness can make it tricky to measure by volume alone. Converting 0.5 tbsp of honey to grams (10.5g) ensures you get the right sweetness and texture in dishes like air-fried salmon or honey-glazed carrots. Too much honey can overpower a dish, while too little might leave it lacking flavour. Using grams instead of tablespoons eliminates guesswork, ensuring your air fryer meals turn out perfectly every time. <h3>How to measure 0.5 tbsp of honey without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have digital scales, lightly grease a measuring spoon with oil to help the honey slide off easily. Fill the spoon just halfway for 0.5 tbsp (10.5g), levelling it with a knife. This method works well for recipes like air-fried honey cake or glazed tofu found in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 0.5 tbsp honey in air fryers</h2>  
<p>Half a tablespoon of honey (10.5g) is perfect for adding subtle sweetness to air-fried dishes. Try it in marinades for chicken thighs, as a glaze for roasted root vegetables, or drizzled over air-fried apples. The precise 0.5 tbsp honey to grams conversion ensures balanced flavours without making dishes overly sweet.</p>  

<p>For breakfast ideas, mix 10.5g honey into yogurt or oatmeal before air frying for a caramelised topping. <h3>Honey alternatives at 0.5 tbsp measurements</h3>  
<p>If substituting honey, note that 0.5 tbsp maple syrup weighs about 9g, while golden syrup is closer to 11g. These small differences matter in air fryer recipes where moisture content affects crispiness. Always adjust cooking times slightly when swapping sweeteners.</p>
