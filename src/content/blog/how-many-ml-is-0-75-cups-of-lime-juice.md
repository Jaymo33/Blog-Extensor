<h2>Why Accurate Lime Juice Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of lime juice can make or break a recipe, especially in air fryer cooking. Too little, and your dish might lack flavour; too much, and it could become overly acidic. Converting 0.75 cups of lime juice to ml ensures consistency, whether you’re following a British or American recipe. Many air fryer recipes, like those for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken marinades</a>, rely on precise liquid measurements. This accuracy is why we recommend using millilitres for smaller quantities like lime juice.</p>  

<p>Millilitres are a metric unit, making them easier to scale up or down compared to cups. If you’re adjusting a recipe, knowing that 0.75 cups lime juice equals 177 ml simplifies the process. <h3>How to Measure Lime Juice Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.75 cups of lime juice in ml, use a liquid measuring jug with millilitre markings. Pour the juice up to the 177 ml line for accuracy. If you don’t have a jug, a standard metric measuring cup will work too. Freshly squeezed lime juice is best for flavour, but bottled juice can also be used if measured correctly. For more air fryer measurement guides, explore <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>.</p>
