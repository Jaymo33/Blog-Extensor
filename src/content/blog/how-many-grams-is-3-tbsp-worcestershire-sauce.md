<h3>How to measure worcestershire sauce correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate conversion of 3 tbsp worcestershire sauce to grams, use a digital kitchen scale. Simply tare your scale with the measuring spoon on it, then pour the sauce until you hit 45 grams. This method eliminates guesswork and ensures your air fryer recipes, like those found on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, are consistently delicious.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 3 tbsp worcestershire sauce in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Worcestershire sauce adds depth to many air fryer dishes. Three tablespoons (45g) is often used in marinades for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken</a> or as a flavour booster for roasted vegetables. Its tangy, umami-rich profile works wonderfully in air-fried meats, giving them a caramelised exterior without excess oil.</p>  

<p>You might also use 3 tbsp (45g) of worcestershire sauce in homemade burger patties or meatloaf mixtures for the air fryer. The sauce’s acidity helps tenderise proteins while adding complexity. <h3>Converting other worcestershire sauce quantities</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for more or less than 3 tbsp, here’s a quick guide: 1 tbsp equals about 15g, while 2 tbsp equal 30g. For larger amounts, 4 tbsp would be 60g. These conversions are handy when adjusting air fryer recipes or creating your own marinades from scratch.</p>  

<h2>Storing worcestershire sauce for optimal freshness</h2>  
<p>Once opened, worcestershire sauce lasts about 1-2 years when stored properly. Keep it in a cool, dark place (not necessarily refrigerated) with the lid tightly sealed. This ensures your 3 tbsp (45g) measurements remain consistent in flavour over time, whether you’re using it weekly for air fryer meals or occasionally for special dishes.</p>
