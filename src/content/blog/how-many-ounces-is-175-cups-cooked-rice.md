<p>1.75 cups of cooked rice equals approximately 14 ounces (or 14.17 oz to be precise). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, ensuring accurate portioning and consistent results. Whether you’re meal prepping or following a specific recipe, knowing this measurement helps avoid guesswork.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Rice Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of cooked rice is crucial for air fryer cooking. Too much rice can lead to uneven heating, while too little might dry out. For dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer stuffed peppers</a> or <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken fried rice</a>, precise measurements ensure perfect texture and flavour. Using 1.75 cups (14 oz) of cooked rice guarantees balanced portions every time.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes call for cooked rice as a base ingredient. Whether you’re making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable rice bowls</a> or reheating leftovers, knowing how to convert 1.75 cups to ounces simplifies the process. This measurement is especially handy for meal prep, where consistency is key.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Cooked Rice for Your Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Measuring cooked rice by weight (ounces) is more accurate than using cups, as rice density can vary. To convert 1.75 cups of cooked rice to ounces, use a kitchen scale for precision. Fluff the rice lightly with a fork before measuring to avoid compacting it, which could skew the results.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a scale, the general rule is that 1 cup of cooked rice weighs roughly 8 ounces. So, 1.75 cups would be around 14 ounces. For air fryer recipes, this method works well for dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>rice cakes</a> or crispy rice balls.</p>

<h2>Tips for Cooking Rice in Your Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>While most air fryers aren’t designed to cook raw rice, they excel at reheating or crisping cooked rice. Spread 1.75 cups (14 oz) of cooked rice evenly in the basket for best results. Lightly mist with oil if you want a crispy texture, and heat at 180°C for 5–7 minutes.</p>
