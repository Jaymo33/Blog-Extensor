<h2>How 312 F Compares to Other Oven Types</h2>
<p>312 F in a conventional oven isn’t the same as in a fan oven or air fryer. Conventional ovens lack the circulating air, so they often require higher temperatures or longer cook times. For instance, 312 F in a conventional oven might equate to 170 C, whereas the fan oven equivalent is 155 C. This difference is crucial for air fryer users, as these appliances mimic fan ovens’ efficiency.</p>
<p>Gas mark conversions are another consideration. While 312 F isn’t a common gas mark setting, it falls between Gas Mark 2 and 3.
