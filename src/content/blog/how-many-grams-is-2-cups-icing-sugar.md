<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking in an air fryer requires precision because the cooking environment is more compact and intense than a conventional oven. Using 2.0 cups of icing sugar without converting to grams could lead to overly sweet or dense results. Air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, so balanced ingredients are key. <p>Icing sugar, also known as powdered sugar, is finer than granulated sugar, which affects its volume-to-weight ratio. A slight mismeasurement can alter the texture of your baked goods. For example, too much icing sugar in a cake batter might make it crumbly, while too little could leave it bland. Always use a kitchen scale for the most accurate results.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Icing Sugar Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 2.0 cups of icing sugar accurately, fluff the sugar first with a spoon to avoid compacting it. Then, lightly spoon it into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. This method ensures you don’t accidentally use more than 250 grams. If you’re working with a sticky recipe, like frosting, consider sifting the sugar afterward for a smoother texture.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Icing Sugar</h2>  
<p>Keep your icing sugar fresh by storing it in an airtight container away from moisture. Humidity can cause clumping, which makes measuring 2.0 cups accurately more difficult. If your sugar has hardened, pulse it in a blender or sieve it before use. Proper storage ensures your air fryer desserts turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<p>For those who bake frequently, buying icing sugar in bulk and portioning it into smaller containers can save time. Label each container with the gram equivalent (e.g., “2.0 cups = 250g”) for quick reference. This small step can streamline your baking process and reduce errors.</p>
