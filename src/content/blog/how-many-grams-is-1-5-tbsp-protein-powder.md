<p>1.5 tablespoons of protein powder typically equals around 12 grams, though this can vary slightly by brand and density. For air fryer recipes, precise measurements ensure your protein-packed dishes turn out perfectly every time. Whether you're making protein muffins or adding powder to coatings, knowing the exact conversion helps avoid dry or dense results.</p>

<p>Using the correct amount of protein powder ensures your air fryer recipes maintain the right texture and flavour. <h3>How to Measure Protein Powder Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don't have digital scales, spoon your protein powder into the tablespoon without packing it down, then level it off. For 1.5 tablespoons, measure one full tablespoon plus half of another. This method works well for most UK protein powder brands when preparing air fryer meals.</p>

<h3>Common Protein Powder Measurement Mistakes</h3>  
<p>The biggest error is scooping directly from the bag, which compacts the powder and leads to inaccurate measurements. Always use a proper measuring spoon for your 1.5 tablespoons, especially when preparing air fryer recipes where precision matters. Another mistake is assuming all protein powders weigh the same - whey, casein and plant-based varieties can differ.</p>  

<h2>Converting Other Protein Powder Measurements</h2>  
<p>If your recipe calls for different amounts, here's a quick guide: 1 tablespoon equals about 8 grams, while 2 tablespoons equal roughly 16 grams.
