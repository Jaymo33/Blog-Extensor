<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of coconut milk can make or break a dish. Too little, and your recipe might lack creaminess; too much, and it could become overly rich or soggy. Air fryers, in particular, rely on balanced ingredients for even cooking. For example, if you're making a coconut-based curry in your air fryer, 535 ml of coconut milk (or 2.26 cups) ensures the perfect consistency.</p>

Plus, knowing how to measure 535 ml of coconut milk in cups saves time and reduces kitchen stress.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 535 ml of Coconut Milk</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use standard cups to measure 535 ml of coconut milk. Since 1 cup equals roughly 237 ml, 535 ml works out to just over 2 cups. For precision, use a kitchen scale or a liquid measuring cup with millilitre markings. This method is especially useful for air fryer baking, where exact ratios matter.</p>

<h2>Common Conversions for Coconut Milk</h2>  
<p>Beyond 535 ml, it’s helpful to know other coconut milk conversions. For instance, 250 ml is about 1.06 cups, while 1 litre equals roughly 4.23 cups. <p>When substituting coconut milk in recipes, remember that its thickness varies by brand. Light coconut milk is more watery, while full-fat versions are creamier. Adjust your measurements accordingly, especially when using an air fryer, as texture impacts cooking times.</p>
