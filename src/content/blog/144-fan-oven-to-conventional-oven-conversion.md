<p>144 fan oven converts to approximately 160°C in a conventional oven. This 16-degree difference accounts for the more efficient heat distribution in fan-assisted ovens. Whether you're adapting recipes or troubleshooting baked goods, understanding this conversion is essential for air fryer users who often need to translate oven temperatures.</p>

<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air constantly, cooking food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens. The forced air movement means you can typically reduce temperatures by 20°C compared to conventional ovens. However, at 144 fan oven (160°C conventional), the difference is slightly less pronounced for optimal cooking results.</p>

<h3>Baking at 144 Fan vs Conventional</h3>
<p>Baking at 144 fan oven (160°C conventional) is ideal for delicate sponges and custards. The slightly lower fan temperature prevents rapid browning while ensuring even heat distribution. When using an air fryer at these temperatures, consider reducing quantities as the smaller space cooks faster.</p>

<h2>Adjusting Cooking Times</h2>
<p>While 144 fan oven converts to 160°C conventional, cooking times may vary by 10-15%. Fan ovens and air fryers cook faster due to the circulating air, so always check food 5-10 minutes before the recipe's suggested time. <p>Meats roasted at 144 fan (160 conventional) will need careful monitoring. Use a meat thermometer to ensure proper doneness rather than relying solely on time estimates. The same applies when using your air fryer - the compact space can accelerate cooking.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations for Air Fryers</h3>
