<h3>The Science Behind Whipping Cream Measurements</h3>
<p>Whipping cream has a specific density that affects its volume-to-weight conversion. While water converts at 8 ounces per cup, whipping cream is slightly different due to its fat content. This is why 0.66 cup of whipping cream equals 5.28 ounces rather than the 5.28 ounces you'd get with water.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 0.66 Cup of Whipping Cream</h2>
<p>For those without measuring cups, you can use a kitchen scale to measure out 5.28 ounces of whipping cream directly. This method is often more accurate than using cups, especially for air fryer recipes where precision matters. If you're working with a recipe that calls for 0.66 cups, simply weigh out 5.28 ounces instead.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Whipping Cream</h2>
<p>One frequent error is packing whipping cream into measuring cups, which leads to inaccurate measurements.
