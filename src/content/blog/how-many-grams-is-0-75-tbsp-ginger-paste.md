<h2>Why accurate ginger paste measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of ginger paste can make or break your air fryer dish. Too little, and the flavour falls flat; too much, and it overpowers other ingredients. For 0.75 tbsp ginger paste to grams, 11.25g is the sweet spot, ensuring your marinades and sauces are perfectly balanced every time.</p>  

<h3>How to measure ginger paste without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have kitchen scales, you can still measure 0.75 tbsp ginger paste accurately. Use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and fill it three-quarters full. For thicker pastes, level it off with a knife. While this method works in a pinch, investing in digital scales guarantees precision, especially for recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for ginger paste in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Ginger paste is a staple in many air fryer recipes, from Asian-inspired dishes to hearty roasts. <h3>Does ginger paste brand affect weight?</h3>  
<p>Different brands of ginger paste vary in consistency—some are thicker, others more watery. While 0.75 tbsp ginger paste generally weighs 11.25g, denser pastes might be slightly heavier. For critical recipes, always weigh your ginger paste rather than relying solely on volume measurements. This ensures your air fryer dishes turn out perfectly every time.</p>
