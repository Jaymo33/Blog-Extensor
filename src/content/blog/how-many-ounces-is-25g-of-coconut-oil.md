<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of coconut oil in your air fryer can affect texture, crispiness, and even cooking times. Too little oil, and your food might turn out dry; too much, and it could become greasy. For recipes like air-fried sweet potatoes or coconut shrimp, 25g of coconut oil (0.88 oz) is often the perfect amount. <h3>How to measure 25g of coconut oil without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 25g of coconut oil using tablespoons. One tablespoon of coconut oil weighs roughly 14g, so 25g is just under two tablespoons. Melted coconut oil can also be measured by volume—25g is about 1.75 tablespoons. <h3>Converting other coconut oil amounts</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for more or less coconut oil, here’s a quick reference: 50g is about 1.76 oz, and 100g is roughly 3.53 oz. For larger batches, like air-frying <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/frozen-foods>frozen foods</a>, scaling up the oil proportionally ensures even cooking.
