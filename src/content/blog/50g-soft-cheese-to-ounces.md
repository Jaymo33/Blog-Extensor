<h2>Why converting 50g of soft cheese to ounces matters</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes, especially those for air fryers, use grams, while some older cookbooks or international recipes list ounces. Soft cheese, like cream cheese or ricotta, is often used in air fryer recipes for dips, stuffed mushrooms, or even cheesecakes. A slight variation in weight can affect texture and taste. <h3>How to measure 50g of soft cheese without scales</h3>  
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem. One tablespoon of soft cheese weighs roughly 15g, so 3 tablespoons plus a teaspoon will give you close to 50g. For a visual guide, 50g of soft cheese is about the size of a small egg. While this method isn’t as precise as weighing, it works in a pinch for recipes where exact measurements aren’t critical. For air fryer dishes like stuffed peppers or cheesy toast, a slight variation won’t ruin the dish.</p>

<h3>Storing leftover soft cheese after measuring</h3>  
<p>Once you’ve measured out 50g of soft cheese, store the rest in an airtight container in the fridge. It typically lasts up to a week if sealed properly. To prevent drying, place a piece of cling film directly on the surface before closing the lid. If you notice any mould, discard the cheese immediately. For longer storage, freeze it in 50g portions—thaw in the fridge before using in your next air fryer creation.</p>
