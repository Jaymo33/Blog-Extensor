<p>140°C in a fan oven converts to approximately 160°C in a conventional oven. This simple adjustment ensures your dishes cook evenly without burning or undercooking. Fan ovens circulate hot air for faster, more efficient cooking, while conventional ovens rely on radiant heat. Understanding this conversion is key for adapting recipes, especially when using an air fryer, which operates similarly to a fan oven.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 140 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens cook food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens due to their built-in fan. This means recipes designed for conventional ovens may need temperature adjustments. Converting 140 fan oven to conventional oven settings ensures your dishes turn out perfectly. For example, a cake baked at 140°C in a fan oven would need 160°C in a conventional oven. This prevents overcooking and maintains the desired texture and moisture.</p>

<h2>How to Calculate 140 Fan Oven to Celsius</h2>
<p>Converting 140 fan oven to Celsius for a conventional oven is straightforward. Simply add 20°C to the fan oven temperature. So, 140°C fan becomes 160°C conventional. This rule works for most recipes, from roasting vegetables to baking cookies. Always preheat your oven to the converted temperature for consistent results.</p>
<p>If you’re using an air fryer, you might not need to adjust the temperature at all. Air fryers and fan ovens operate similarly, circulating hot air around the food.
