<p>1.33 cups of mustard equals approximately 10.64 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements can make all the difference. Whether you're whipping up a tangy glaze or a marinade, knowing the exact weight ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of mustard can elevate your air fryer dishes, from crispy chicken wings to glazed salmon. Too little, and the flavour falls flat; too much, and it overpowers the dish. That’s why converting 1.33 cups of mustard to ounces (10.64 oz) is so important. For more air fryer tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>
<p>Mustard’s consistency can vary, so weighing it ensures accuracy regardless of brand or type. Whether you’re using Dijon, English, or wholegrain, 1.33 cups will always translate to roughly 10.64 ounces. This precision is especially handy for batch cooking or meal prep, where consistency is key.</p>

<h2>How to Measure Mustard for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>
<p>Measuring mustard by weight (ounces) is more reliable than volume (cups), as it accounts for density differences. To convert 1.33 cups of mustard to ounces, use a kitchen scale for the most accurate result. If you don’t have one, remember that 1 cup of mustard is about 8 ounces, making 1.33 cups roughly 10.64 ounces.</p>
