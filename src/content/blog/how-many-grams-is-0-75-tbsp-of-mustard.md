Using grams instead of tablespoons eliminates guesswork, especially with sticky ingredients like mustard. <h2>How to measure 0.75 tbsp of mustard without scales</h2>
<p>If you don’t have kitchen scales, you can still measure 0.75 tbsp of mustard accurately. Fill a tablespoon three-quarters full, levelling it off with a knife for precision. Alternatively, use a teaspoon: 0.75 tbsp equals 2.25 tsp, so measure two full teaspoons plus a quarter teaspoon. This method works well for smaller quantities in air fryer recipes, like marinades for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken</a> or coatings for vegetables.</p>

<p>Need to convert different amounts of mustard? Here’s a quick reference: 1 tbsp of mustard weighs roughly 15 grams, while 1 tsp is about 5 grams. For larger quantities, 50 grams equates to roughly 3.33 tbsp. These conversions are handy when adjusting recipes or experimenting with flavours in your air fryer. <h3>Storing mustard for optimal freshness</h3>
<p>To keep mustard fresh for air fryer recipes, store opened jars in the fridge and use within 2-3 months. Exposure to air can dull its flavour over time. If your mustard separates, simply stir it back together before measuring. For longer storage, consider portioning mustard into ice cube trays and freezing—thaw as needed for precise 0.75 tbsp (11g) portions.</p>
