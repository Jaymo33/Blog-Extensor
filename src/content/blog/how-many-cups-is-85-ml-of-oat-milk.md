<p>85 ml of oat milk is approximately 0.36 cups (just over 1/3 cup). This conversion is essential for baking, cooking, or preparing recipes in your air fryer. Whether you're whipping up a cake or a creamy sauce, knowing how to measure 85 ml of oat milk in cups ensures precision.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of oat milk can make or break a recipe. Too little, and your dish might turn out dry; too much, and it could become soggy. For air fryer recipes, precise measurements are especially important because the cooking process is faster and more intense. A slight imbalance in liquid ingredients like oat milk can affect texture and cooking time.</p>  

<p>If you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, getting the conversion right ensures your dish turns out perfectly. Whether you're making pancakes, muffins, or a vegan quiche, knowing that 85 ml of oat milk equals roughly 0.36 cups helps maintain consistency.</p>  

<h3>Measuring 85 ml of Oat Milk Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug with millilitre markings, you can still measure 85 ml of oat milk accurately. A standard metric measuring cup will have millilitre indicators, but if yours only has cups, remember that 1 cup is about 237 ml. To measure 85 ml, fill the cup just over one-third of the way.</p>  

<h2>Converting 85 ml of Oat Milk for Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking requires precision, and substituting oat milk for dairy milk means getting the measurements spot-on. Since 85 ml of oat milk is roughly 0.36 cups, you can adjust your recipe accordingly. If a recipe calls for 1 cup of milk and you're using oat milk, you might need to tweak other ingredients slightly for the best texture.</p>  

<p>For air fryer baking, such as making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes</a> or cookies, the right oat milk measurement ensures even cooking. Too much liquid can lead to uneven browning, while too little might dry out your baked goods.</p>  

<h3>Using 85 ml of Oat Milk in Savoury Dishes</h3>  
<p>Oat milk works well in creamy sauces, soups, and even marinades. If a recipe calls for 85 ml of oat milk, knowing it's just over 1/3 cup helps streamline preparation. For air fryer dishes like vegan mac and cheese or creamy mushroom pasta, accurate measurements ensure the sauce thickens properly.</p>  

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Oat Milk</h2>  
<p>One frequent error is confusing fluid ounces with millilitres, leading to incorrect measurements. Another is not levelling off the measuring cup, which can add extra millilitres unintentionally. Always use a clear measuring jug for liquids like oat milk to avoid over-pouring.</p>

<h3>Storing Leftover Oat Milk</h3>  
<p>If you only need 85 ml of oat milk for a recipe, you might wonder what to do with the rest. Store opened oat milk in the fridge for up to five days. Shake well before using, as separation is normal. You can also freeze oat milk in ice cube trays for future use in smoothies or sauces.</p>
