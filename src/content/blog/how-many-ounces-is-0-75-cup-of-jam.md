<h2>Why accurate jam measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of jam is crucial, especially in air fryer cooking. Too much jam can make dishes overly sweet or sticky, while too little might leave them lacking flavour. Measuring 0.75 cup of jam in ounces helps standardise recipes, whether you’re making a glaze or filling. <p>Jam’s sticky texture can make measuring tricky, so using weight (ounces) is often more reliable than volume (cups). A kitchen scale ensures precision, but if you don’t have one, knowing that 0.75 cup of jam is roughly 6 ounces simplifies things. This conversion works for most jams, though thicker varieties like marmalade might weigh slightly more.</p>  

<h3>Converting 0.75 cup of jam to ounces</h3>  
<p>The general rule is that 1 cup of jam weighs about 8 ounces, so 0.75 cup equals 6 ounces. This applies to most standard jams, including strawberry, raspberry, and apricot. For thicker spreads, like fig jam, you might find 0.75 cup closer to 6.5 ounces. Always check your recipe for specific requirements, especially when using an air fryer, as consistency affects cooking times.</p>
