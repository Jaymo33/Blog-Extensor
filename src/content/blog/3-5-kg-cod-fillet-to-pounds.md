<h2>Practical tips for cooking a large cod fillet in the air fryer</h2>
<p>Cooking a 3.5 kg cod fillet in the air fryer requires some planning due to its size. First, you'll likely need to cut the fillet into smaller portions that fit your air fryer basket without overlapping. Overcrowding prevents proper air circulation and results in uneven cooking. Aim for pieces that are similar in thickness to ensure they cook at the same rate. Pat the cod dry with paper towels before seasoning to help achieve a crispy exterior. Lightly brushing with oil can enhance browning and prevent sticking, but it's not always necessary for non-stick baskets.</p>

<p>Preheating your air fryer is recommended for best results, especially with a dense protein like cod. Set the temperature to around 200°C (400°F) for most cod recipes. The cooking time will depend on the thickness of your pieces, but typically ranges from 10-15 minutes for average portions. Always check for doneness with a fork – the fish should flake easily and be opaque throughout.
