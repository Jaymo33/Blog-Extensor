<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small deviations in flour weight can affect texture and rise. Using 250g of flour instead of the correct ounce equivalent might lead to dense or dry results. Air fryers, with their rapid heat circulation, demand precision to avoid under or overcooking. For perfect bakes, always measure flour by weight, not volume, as scooping can compress it. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient conversions.</p>

<h3>How to measure 250g of flour without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, use the spoon-and-level method: lightly spoon flour into a measuring cup and level it off. However, this isn’t as precise as weighing, so for 250g of flour, you’d need about 2 cups (though this can vary). For air fryer recipes, we recommend investing in scales for reliability.
