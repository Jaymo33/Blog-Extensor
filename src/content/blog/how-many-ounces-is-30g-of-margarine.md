<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of margarine can affect texture, moisture, and browning in your air fryer dishes. Too little, and your food might dry out; too much, and it could become greasy. Converting 30g of margarine to ounces (1.06 oz) ensures you follow recipes correctly. For more conversion help, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Margarine is often used in air fryer recipes for its lower water content compared to butter. This makes it ideal for achieving crispy textures. Whether you’re making roasted vegetables or baked goods, 30g of margarine (1.06 oz) is a common measurement in many UK recipes.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 30g of Margarine Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can use tablespoons to measure 30g of margarine. One tablespoon of margarine weighs roughly 14g, so 30g is just over two tablespoons.
