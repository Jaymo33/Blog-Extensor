<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for 200 Grams of Water</h3>  
<p>While 200 grams of water converts to roughly 0.85 cups in both UK and US measurements, slight variations exist. A UK cup holds 250ml, whereas a US cup is 240ml. For most air fryer recipes, this difference is negligible, but it’s worth noting for precision baking. <h2>How to Measure 200 Grams of Water Without Scales</h2>  
<p>No kitchen scales? No problem. To measure 200 grams of water in cups, use a liquid measuring jug marked in millilitres. Since 1 gram of water equals 1ml, 200 grams is 200ml—just under a full cup. Alternatively, 16 tablespoons equal 1 cup, so 200 grams of water is roughly 13.5 tablespoons. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning and maintenance guides</a> to keep your tools accurate.</p>  

<p>Another handy trick is repurposing standard household items. A standard UK teacup holds about 190ml, so 200 grams of water fills it just slightly above the rim. For US cooks, a standard coffee mug averages 240ml, meaning 200 grams is about 5/6 of the mug. These approximations work in a pinch but invest in scales for consistent air fryer results.</p>

<h2>Converting Other Liquids: Is 200 Grams Always 0.85 Cups?</h2>
