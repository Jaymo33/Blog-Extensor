<h2>How to Measure 30g of Oats Without Scales</h2>
<p>Don’t have kitchen scales? No problem. You can still measure 30g of oats (1.06 ounces) using simple household items. A standard UK tablespoon holds about 5g of oats, so six level tablespoons will give you roughly 30g. For a more precise method, use a measuring cup: 30g of oats is roughly ⅓ cup. Remember, though, that these are approximations—scales are always best for accuracy. If you’re making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> regularly, investing in digital scales is worth it.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Oats</h2>
<p>One mistake people make is packing oats into measuring cups, which leads to inaccuracies. For 30g (1.06 ounces), always spoon oats lightly into the cup and level off the top. Another error is confusing rolled oats with quick oats—they have different densities, so stick to the type specified in the recipe.
