<h2>How to measure 100g of powdered milk accurately</h2>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the easiest way to measure 100g of powdered milk. Simply place your container on the scale, tare it to zero, and add the powdered milk until you reach 100g. If your scale displays ounces, you’ll see it settle around 3.53 oz. For those without a scale, a measuring cup can work, but it’s less precise—100g of powdered milk is roughly ¾ cup.</p>

<h3>Does the type of powdered milk affect the conversion?</h3>  
<p>No—whether you’re using full-fat or skimmed powdered milk, 100g will always equal approximately 3.53 ounces. The density of powdered milk is consistent, so the conversion remains the same. However, always check your recipe for specific instructions, as some may call for reconstituted powdered milk (mixed with water) rather than the dry form.</p>  

<h2>Tips for storing powdered milk</h2>  
<p>To keep your powdered milk fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Exposure to moisture can cause clumping, which may affect measurements. If you’re using powdered milk frequently for air fryer recipes, consider buying in bulk and portioning it into smaller containers. This ensures you always have the right amount on hand without waste.</p>

<h3>Can you substitute fresh milk for powdered milk?</h3>  
<p>Yes, but the conversion isn’t 1:1. To replace 100g of powdered milk (3.53 oz), you’d need about 800ml of fresh milk, as powdered milk is concentrated. However, this substitution may alter the texture of your dish, especially in baked goods. For best results, stick to powdered milk when the recipe calls for it.</p>
