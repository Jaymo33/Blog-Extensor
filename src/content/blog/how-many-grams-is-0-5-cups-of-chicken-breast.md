<h3>How to Measure 0.5 Cups of Chicken Breast Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, lightly packed, finely diced chicken breast fills a 0.5-cup measure to about 70 grams. Avoid pressing it down, as this compresses the meat and skews the measurement. For shredded chicken, fluff it lightly before measuring for consistency. This method works well for recipes like tacos, wraps, or casseroles where exact proportions aren’t critical. For dishes like air-fried chicken bites or skewers, though, weighing ensures uniform cooking.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Chicken Breast</h3>  
<p>Overpacking the measuring cup is a frequent error, leading to heavier portions and uneven cooking. Another pitfall? Using liquid measuring cups instead of dry ones, which can compress the chicken. For diced breast, aim for pea-sized pieces to ensure consistency. If you’re tracking macros or calories, weighing raw chicken (not cooked) gives the most accurate nutritional data. And remember: 0.5 cups of cooked chicken breast weighs less than raw due to moisture loss—about 50 grams versus 70 grams raw.</p>
