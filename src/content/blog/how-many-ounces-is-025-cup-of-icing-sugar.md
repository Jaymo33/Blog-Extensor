<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking in an air fryer requires precision, especially when working with ingredients like icing sugar. Too much or too little can affect texture, sweetness, and even cooking time. For example, if you’re making air fryer cookies and add too much icing sugar, they might spread too thin or burn. On the other hand, too little could leave them bland. <h3>How to Measure 0.25 Cup of Icing Sugar Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.25 cup of icing sugar accurately, use a dry measuring cup and level it off with a straight edge. Scooping directly from the bag can compress the sugar, leading to an incorrect measurement. For best results, fluff the icing sugar with a fork before measuring to ensure it’s light and airy. This method guarantees you’ll get the right amount—1 ounce—for your air fryer recipes.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Icing Sugar for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Keep your icing sugar fresh by storing it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Humidity can cause clumping, which makes measuring 0.25 cup (1 oz) tricky. If your sugar does clump, sift it before use to ensure smooth, even distribution in your recipes. Proper storage also prevents contamination, keeping your ingredients safe for all your air fryer creations.</p>
