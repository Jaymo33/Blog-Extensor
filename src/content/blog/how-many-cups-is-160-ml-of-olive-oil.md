<h2>How to Measure 160 ml of Olive Oil Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>Not everyone has a kitchen scale, but you can still measure 160 ml of olive oil accurately using cups. A standard US cup holds 240 ml, so 160 ml is roughly two-thirds of a cup. For UK measurements, a metric cup is 250 ml, making 160 ml about 0.64 cups.</p>  
<p>If you don’t have measuring cups, use a tablespoon. One tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so 160 ml equals roughly 10.5 tablespoons. This method works well for drizzling oil over <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>air-fried vegetables</a> or marinating proteins.</p>

<h2>Converting 160 ml of Olive Oil for Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking in an air fryer? 160 ml of olive oil replaces about 0.68 cups of butter or other fats. This substitution works well in cakes, muffins, and even bread. <h3>Tips for Storing and Using Olive Oil</h3>  
<p>Store olive oil in a cool, dark place to preserve its quality. If you’ve measured 160 ml but don’t need it all, transfer the excess to an airtight container. Use it within a few weeks for the best flavour in your air-fryer dishes.</p>
