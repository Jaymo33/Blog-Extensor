<h3>How to measure 2.5 tbsp of margarine without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a standard tablespoon to measure margarine. Fill it slightly heaped for accuracy, as margarine can be softer than butter. For 2.5 tbsp, measure two level tablespoons and add half another. This method works well for most air fryer recipes, though a scale is always best for exactness.</p>

<h3>Margarine vs. butter in air fryer cooking</h3>  
<p>Margarine and butter have similar weights, but margarine often has a higher water content. This means 2.5 tbsp of margarine (35g) may behave slightly differently in an air fryer compared to butter. Adjust cooking times if substituting one for the other, and always monitor your dish to avoid over- or undercooking.</p>  

<h2>Tips for storing and handling margarine</h2>  
<p>Keep margarine refrigerated to maintain its consistency, making it easier to measure accurately. If softened, stir it well before measuring 2.5 tbsp to ensure uniformity. For sticky residues, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner guide</a> helps keep your appliance spotless after cooking with fats like margarine.</p>  

<p>Margarine can also be frozen for longer storage, though it’s best measured fresh for accuracy. If using frozen margarine, thaw it completely and remeasure before adding to your air fryer recipes. This prevents inconsistencies that could affect your dish’s outcome.</p>  

<h3>Scaling recipes up or down</h3>  
<p>If a recipe requires double or half the amount of margarine, simply adjust the grams accordingly. For instance, 5 tbsp would be 70g, while 1.25 tbsp would be roughly 17.5g. This flexibility makes it easy to adapt recipes to your needs while maintaining precision.</p>
