<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect ingredient quantities can throw off textures and flavours. For example, using too much marmalade in a glaze might make it overly sticky, while too little could leave your dish bland. That’s why converting 0.125 cup of marmalade to ounces (1 oz) is so useful—it keeps your recipes consistent. If you’re new to air frying, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>  

<p>Marmalade’s thickness can vary by brand, but 0.125 cup generally weighs around 1 ounce. For best results, use a kitchen scale if precision is crucial. This is especially helpful when adapting traditional oven recipes for your air fryer, as cooking times and quantities often need slight adjustments.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.125 Cup of Marmalade Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use a standard measuring cup. Fill it to the 1/8 cup mark (which is 0.125 cup) for roughly 1 ounce of marmalade. Stirring the marmalade first ensures an even consistency, making your measurement more accurate.
