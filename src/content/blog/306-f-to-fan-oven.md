<h2>Why Convert 306°F to Fan Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens and air fryers work similarly, using circulating air for even cooking. Converting 306°F to fan oven settings ensures accuracy, especially when following recipes designed for conventional ovens. This temperature is often used for baking, roasting, and reheating. For air fryer users, it’s a sweet spot for crispy fries or golden chicken wings. <h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>When using 306°F (150°C fan) in an air fryer, reduce cooking times by 15-20%. The rapid air circulation cooks food faster than conventional ovens. For example, if a recipe suggests 30 minutes at 306°F in a fan oven, try 24-26 minutes in the air fryer. Always check food early to avoid overcooking.</p>

<p>Preheating your air fryer at this temperature ensures consistent results. Some models have presets, but manual settings give more control. <p>Air fryers are more energy-efficient than fan ovens at this temperature. They heat up faster and use less space, ideal for small batches. However, fan ovens accommodate larger dishes better. For air fryer users, 306°F (150°C fan) delivers crispiness without excessive browning.</p>
