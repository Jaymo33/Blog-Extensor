For perfect roast potatoes or crispy chicken wings, this temperature delivers excellent results in both appliances.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times at 244 C</h3>
<p>While 244 C converts to Gas Mark 9 in traditional ovens, air fryer cooking times differ significantly. As a general rule, reduce cooking times by 15-25% when using an air fryer at this temperature. For example, what might take 30 minutes in a conventional oven could be done in 22-25 minutes in an air fryer. Always check food a few minutes early to prevent overcooking.</p>

<h2>Safety Tips for High-Temperature Air Frying</h2>
<p>Working with 244 C (Gas Mark 9) in air fryers requires some safety precautions. Always use heat-resistant utensils to avoid damaging the non-stick coating. The exterior of air fryers can become very hot at this temperature, so keep them away from flammable materials. Allow the appliance to cool completely before cleaning to prevent warping or other damage.</p>

<p>When cooking fatty foods at 244 C, there's potential for smoke production. Position your air fryer under an extractor fan if possible, and ensure good kitchen ventilation. Some foods might brown too quickly at this high temperature, so consider covering with foil partway through cooking if needed. Regular maintenance, including cleaning the heating element, ensures consistent performance at high temperatures.</p>
