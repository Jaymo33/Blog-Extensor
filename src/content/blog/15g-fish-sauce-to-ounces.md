<h2>Why Convert 15g of Fish Sauce to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those from the UK or US, use ounces as a standard measurement. If you're working with a recipe that lists fish sauce in grams, converting 15g to ounces (0.53 oz) ensures you add the right amount. Too much fish sauce can overpower a dish, while too little might leave it lacking flavour. When using an air fryer, precise measurements help balance flavours without overwhelming other ingredients. For example, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipe</a> might call for 15g of fish sauce, and knowing it's 0.53 ounces ensures perfect seasoning.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for 15g of Fish Sauce in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Fish sauce is incredibly versatile in air fryer recipes. A 15g (0.53 oz) portion can elevate marinades for proteins like chicken, shrimp, or tofu. It also works wonders in dipping sauces or as a seasoning for vegetables. For example, tossing <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>roasted vegetables</a> with a splash of fish sauce adds a savoury kick.</p>  

<p>Another great use for 15g of fish sauce is in glazes or dressings. Mix it with honey, lime, and garlic for a sticky glaze on air-fried salmon or drizzle it over a fresh salad. The key is balancing the strong flavour, which is why measuring 15g (0.53 oz) accurately is so important. <h3>Tips for Storing Fish Sauce After Measuring</h3>  
<p>Once you've measured out 15g of fish sauce, store the bottle in a cool, dark place to maintain its quality. Fish sauce has a long shelf life but can lose its potency if exposed to heat or light. For best results, use it within a year of opening.
