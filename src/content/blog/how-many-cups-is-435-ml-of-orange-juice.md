<h2>Understanding the Conversion</h2>  
<p>Converting millilitres to cups can be tricky, especially when dealing with liquids like orange juice. The standard conversion is 1 cup = 236.588 ml, meaning 435 ml is roughly 1.84 cups. This measurement is crucial for recipes where liquid ratios impact texture and flavour. For example, if you're making an orange-infused cake in your air fryer, even a slight miscalculation could affect the outcome.</p>  

<p>Different countries use varying cup sizes, but the metric cup (250 ml) and US cup (236.588 ml) are the most common. If you're following a UK-based recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, you might see ml measurements, while US recipes often use cups. Always double-check which standard your recipe follows to avoid confusion.</p>  

<h3>Why Accuracy Matters in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve crispy, evenly cooked dishes. Too much liquid, like orange juice, can lead to soggy results, while too little might dry out your food. For example, a marinade for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken</a> using 435 ml of orange juice should be measured correctly to balance acidity and moisture.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h2>  
<p>One frequent error is confusing fluid ounces with millilitres, leading to incorrect measurements. Another is using dry measuring cups for liquids, which can result in inaccuracies. Always use a liquid measuring cup for orange juice to ensure the correct volume. For air fryer recipes, even small measurement errors can impact cooking times and textures.</p>  

<p>Another pitfall is assuming all orange juice brands have the same density. Freshly squeezed juice may differ slightly from store-bought varieties, but for most recipes, the difference is negligible. Stick to the 435 ml to 1.84 cups conversion unless the recipe specifies otherwise.</p>  

<h3>Storing Leftover Orange Juice</h3>  
<p>If your recipe doesn't use the full 435 ml, store the remaining juice in an airtight container in the fridge. Use it within 3-4 days for optimal freshness.
