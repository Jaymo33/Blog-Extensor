<p>166°C in a fan oven converts to approximately 185°C in a conventional oven. This 20°C difference accounts for the circulating hot air in fan-assisted ovens that cooks food more efficiently. Understanding this conversion is crucial when following recipes or adapting cooking times for different appliances.</p>

<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens use a built-in fan to circulate hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. This means you can typically reduce the temperature by about 20°C when converting from conventional to fan oven settings. The 166 fan oven to conventional oven conversion follows this standard rule, making it easy to adjust recipes between oven types.</p>
<p>When using your air fryer, which also relies on rapid air circulation, you might find similar temperature adjustments necessary. Many air fryer recipes at <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> include conversion charts to help you adapt conventional oven recipes. The principle remains the same whether you're baking cookies or roasting vegetables.</p>

<h2>Practical Applications for 166°C Fan</h2>
<p>The 166 fan oven to celsius conversion is particularly useful for delicate baked goods that require precise temperatures. Cakes, biscuits, and pastries all benefit from accurate temperature control. At 166°C fan (185°C conventional), you're in the perfect range for Victoria sponges, shortbread, and many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a>.</p>
<p>This temperature also works well for roasting vegetables or cooking white fish fillets. The slightly lower fan-assisted temperature prevents over-browning while ensuring thorough cooking. If you're using an air fryer instead of an oven, you might need to reduce the time rather than the temperature for similar results.</p>
