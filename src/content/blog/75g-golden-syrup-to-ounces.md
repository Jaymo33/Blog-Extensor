<h2>Why Convert 75g of Golden Syrup to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many recipes, especially those from the UK, list golden syrup in grams, while others use ounces. Converting 75g of golden syrup to ounces ensures you get the right consistency and flavour in your dishes. Golden syrup is thicker than honey, so accurate measurements prevent overly sweet or dense results. If you're using an air fryer, precise measurements help avoid sticky messes or uneven cooking. For more conversion tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Golden syrup is a versatile ingredient, often used in flapjacks, treacle tarts, and even glazes for air-fried meats. Whether you're making a classic dessert or a modern air fryer recipe, knowing that 75g of golden syrup is roughly 2.65 ounces keeps your cooking on track. If you're working with larger quantities, remember that 150g would be about 5.3 ounces, and so on.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 75g of Golden Syrup Accurately</h2>  
<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like golden syrup can be tricky, but a few simple tricks make it easier. First, lightly grease your measuring spoon or cup with oil to help the syrup slide off cleanly. For 75g of golden syrup, you can use a digital kitchen scale for the most accurate result. If you don’t have a scale, note that 75g is roughly 3 tablespoons, but weighing is always best for precision.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for 75g of Golden Syrup</h2>  
<p>Golden syrup is a key ingredient in many British desserts, like sticky toffee pudding or parkin. It’s also fantastic in marinades for air-fried meats, adding a sweet, glossy finish. <h3>Storing Golden Syrup for Freshness</h3>  
<p>Golden syrup has a long shelf life but can crystallise if not stored properly. Keep it in a cool, dry place, and ensure the lid is tightly sealed. If your syrup hardens, gently warm the jar in hot water to restore its smooth texture. This way, your 75g (2.65-ounce) measurements will always be spot-on.</p>
