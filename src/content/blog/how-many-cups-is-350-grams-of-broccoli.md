<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of broccoli is crucial for even cooking in your air fryer. Too much can lead to steaming instead of crisping, while too little might dry out. For perfect results, aim for 350 grams of broccoli, which translates to about 3.5 to 4 cups when chopped. This measurement works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, from side dishes to main courses.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 350 Grams of Broccoli in Cups</h2>
<p>To measure 350 grams of broccoli in cups, start by chopping the florets into even-sized pieces. This ensures uniformity in cooking and makes measuring easier. A standard cup of chopped broccoli weighs about 90 to 100 grams, so 350 grams will fill roughly 3.5 to 4 cups. If you’re using frozen broccoli, note that it may pack differently, so adjust accordingly.</p>

<h3>Fresh vs. Frozen Broccoli Measurements</h3>
<p>Fresh broccoli and frozen broccoli can measure differently due to moisture content. Fresh broccoli tends to be lighter per cup, while frozen broccoli may weigh more because of ice crystals. For 350 grams of frozen broccoli, you might need slightly fewer cups than fresh. Always fluff the broccoli lightly before measuring to avoid compacting it.</p>

<h2>Tips for Cooking 350 Grams of Broccoli in Your Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Cooking 350 grams of broccoli in the air fryer is a breeze once you’ve got the measurements down. Preheat your air fryer to 180°C for even cooking. Toss the broccoli with a bit of oil and your favourite seasonings, then spread it in a single layer for optimal crispiness. This amount fits perfectly in most standard air fryer baskets.</p>
