<p>375 grams of milk is roughly equivalent to 1.5 cups in UK measurements or 1.6 cups in US measurements. This conversion is essential for anyone using precise measurements in baking, cooking, or air fryer recipes. Whether you're whipping up a cake or preparing a creamy sauce, knowing how to convert grams to cups ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<p>Getting the right amount of milk can make or break a recipe. Too much or too little can affect texture, moisture, and even cooking times. A slight imbalance in liquid ingredients like milk can lead to uneven cooking or soggy results.</p>
<p>If you're using an air fryer for baking, such as making muffins or cakes, the correct milk measurement ensures the batter has the perfect consistency. <h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h2>
<p>It's important to note that cup measurements differ between the UK and the US. In the UK, 1 cup of milk is approximately 250ml, while in the US, it's about 240ml. This slight difference means 375 grams of milk converts to 1.5 cups in the UK and 1.6 cups in the US. Always check which measurement system your recipe follows to avoid errors.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 375 Grams of Milk Without Scales</h3>
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 375 grams of milk using standard measuring cups. In the UK, 1.5 cups will give you roughly the right amount, while in the US, you'll need just over 1.5 cups. For the most accurate results, use a liquid measuring cup and fill it to the appropriate line.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Leftover Milk</h3>
<p>If your recipe calls for less than 375 grams of milk, you might wonder what to do with the leftovers. Milk can be stored in the fridge for up to five days, or you can freeze it for longer storage. Just be sure to give it a good stir after thawing, as it may separate slightly.</p>
