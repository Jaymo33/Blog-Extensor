<h3>How to measure agave syrup accurately</h3>  
<p>To measure 4 tbsp of agave syrup, use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml per tbsp). For grams, a kitchen scale is the most precise tool—simply tare your bowl and pour until it hits 85g. If you don’t have a scale, remember that 1 tbsp of agave syrup weighs roughly 21.25g, so 4 tbsp equals 85g. This method works for all air fryer recipes, from marinades to baked goods. For sticky ingredients like agave, lightly greasing your spoon helps it slide off easily.</p>

<h3>Agave syrup vs. other sweeteners in air fryer recipes</h3>  
<p>Agave syrup is thinner and sweeter than honey or maple syrup, so 4 tbsp (85g) may require recipe adjustments. For instance, if substituting sugar, use about ⅔ the amount of agave to avoid excess sweetness. Its low viscosity also means it spreads more evenly in batters or coatings. Unlike granulated sugar, agave adds moisture, which can affect crispiness in air-fried foods. Experiment with small batches to find your perfect balance.</p>  

<h2>Tips for storing and using agave syrup</h2>  
<p>Store agave syrup in a cool, dark place to prevent crystallisation. If it thickens, gently warm the bottle in hot water before measuring your 4 tbsp (85g) portion. Pair it with spices like cinnamon or smoked paprika for quick, flavourful glazes.</p>
