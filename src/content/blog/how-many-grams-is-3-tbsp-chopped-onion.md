<h3>How to measure chopped onion correctly</h3>  
<p>For consistent results, lightly pack the onion into the tablespoon without crushing it. Level off the top with a knife for an exact 3 tbsp portion. If using a kitchen scale, tare the bowl first, then add the onion until it hits 30 grams. This method works for both fresh and pre-chopped onions. <h3>Scaling up or down</h3>  
<p>Need more or less? Here’s a quick reference: 1 tbsp ≈ 10g, 2 tbsp ≈ 20g, and 4 tbsp ≈ 40g. These ratios help when adapting recipes for smaller or larger batches. For frozen chopped onion, thaw and drain excess liquid before measuring. <h2>Pro tips for air frying with onions</h2>  
<p>Onions can burn quickly in air fryers due to their sugar content. Toss them in a bit of oil and cook at 160°C for 5-8 minutes, shaking halfway. For caramelised results, add a pinch of sugar. Pair them with other <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>potato-based recipes</a> for classic combos like roast potatoes with onions. Always measure after chopping—not before—as sizes vary widely.</p>
