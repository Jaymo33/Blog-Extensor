<h3>Types of Yogurt and Their Weights</h3>
<p>Not all yogurts weigh the same per cup. Greek yogurt, being thicker, can weigh around 240–250 grams per cup, while regular plain yogurt averages 245 grams. Flavoured or sweetened varieties might differ slightly due to added sugars. Always check your specific yogurt type for the most accurate conversion.</p>

<h2>Converting 2.0 Cups of Yogurt to Grams</h2>
<p>To convert 2.0 cups of yogurt to grams, multiply the weight of one cup (about 245 grams) by two, giving you roughly 490 grams. For recipes requiring absolute precision, like air fryer desserts or sauces, weighing ensures consistency. <h2>Tips for Measuring Yogurt Without a Scale</h2>
<p>No scale? No problem. Use the displacement method: fill a liquid measuring cup with water to the 2.0-cup mark, then add yogurt until the water reaches 4.0 cups. The yogurt will displace the water, giving you a close approximation. Alternatively, refer to your yogurt container’s nutritional info—many list grams per serving, which you can tally up.</p>
