Whether you're making garlic butter shrimp or roasted garlic potatoes, knowing the exact weight helps avoid over or under-seasoning. <h2>Why accurate garlic measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of chopped garlic ensures your air fryer dishes aren’t overpowering or bland. Garlic’s potency varies depending on how finely it’s chopped, so weighing it removes guesswork. If you’re making garlic bread or marinating chicken, 24 ounces of chopped garlic from 3 cups guarantees consistency. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>garlic-heavy recipes</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<p>Air fryers circulate hot air, so evenly distributed garlic prevents burning or uneven flavouring. Too much garlic can overpower delicate ingredients like fish or tofu, while too little leaves dishes lacking depth. For perfectly balanced meals, always measure chopped garlic by weight when possible.</p>  

<h3>How to measure chopped garlic correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 3 cups to ounces conversion, lightly pack chopped garlic into a measuring cup without crushing it. A kitchen scale is ideal, but if you don’t have one, fluff the garlic with a fork before spooning it into the cup. Remember, 3 cups of finely minced garlic will weigh slightly more than roughly chopped due to reduced air pockets.</p>
