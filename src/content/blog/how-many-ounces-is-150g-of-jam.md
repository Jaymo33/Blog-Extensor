<h2>Why Convert 150g of Jam to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, while others reference ounces, especially older cookbooks or international guides. Converting 150g of jam to ounces (5.29 oz) helps avoid guesswork. Air fryer recipes, like glazed chicken or jam-filled desserts, often need precise amounts for perfect texture and flavour. Over-measuring jam can make dishes overly sweet or soggy. Under-measuring might leave them dry. For more air fryer tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>.</p>  

<p>Jam density can vary slightly by brand or fruit type, but 150g reliably converts to 5.29 oz. This standardisation is key for consistent baking, whether you're using a fan oven or air fryer. If you’re scaling a recipe up or down, a digital scale is your best friend. Need more conversions? <h3>How to Measure 150g of Jam Without Scales</h3>  
<p>No scales? No problem. A standard UK tablespoon holds about 20g of jam, so 150g is roughly 7.5 tablespoons. For ounces, that’s just under 5.3 oz. Use a levelled spoon for accuracy, and avoid compacting the jam. This method works well for air fryer recipes like thumbprint cookies or sticky ribs. For more no-scale hacks, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 150g of Jam in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>From glazes to fillings, 150g of jam (5.29 oz) is versatile. Try it in air-fryer apricot-glazed chicken or raspberry-filled pastries. The sweetness balances savoury dishes, while precise measurements prevent sogginess. Jam also works as a binder in granolas or a topping for air-fryer cheesecakes. <p>Storing leftover jam? Transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate. 150g of jam typically fills a small jar, perfect for single-use recipes. Avoid reheating jam directly in the air fryer—it can caramelise too quickly. Instead, warm it slightly before brushing onto dishes. <h3>Converting Other Jam Quantities</h3>  
<p>Need 75g or 300g of jam? Here’s a quick cheat sheet: 75g = 2.65 oz, 300g = 10.58 oz. These conversions help scale recipes effortlessly. For larger batches, like jam-filled cakes or glazed hams, multiply accordingly. Remember, air fryers cook faster than ovens, so adjust cooking times when using sugary glazes.</p>
