<h2>Why accurate golden syrup measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Golden syrup is a staple in British baking, adding moisture and sweetness to air fryer treats like flapjacks or sticky puddings. Too much can make desserts cloying, while too little may leave them bland. Measuring 0.5 tbsp of golden syrup in grams (10.5g) ensures consistency, especially in smaller batches. For more air fryer dessert ideas, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>.</p>

<h3>How to measure 0.5 tbsp golden syrup without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, fill a tablespoon halfway (0.5 tbsp) with golden syrup, levelling it with a knife. Remember, 0.5 tbsp golden syrup to grams is roughly 10.5g, but slight variations occur based on syrup density. For sticky ingredients, lightly greasing the spoon helps with accurate pouring.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 0.5 tbsp golden syrup in air fryers</h2>  
<p>This small quantity works well in glazes for air-fried ham or brushed onto roasted carrots. It’s also perfect for single-serving desserts, like a drizzle over <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a>. The 10.5-gram measurement ensures balanced sweetness without overpowering other flavours.</p>  

<p>Golden syrup’s thick consistency can help bind granola clusters or crisp up pastry edges. For savoury dishes, combine 0.5 tbsp (10.5g) with mustard for a quick glaze. Find more creative uses in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a>.</p>  

<h3>Storing golden syrup for accurate measurements</h3>  
<p>Keep golden syrup in a cool, dry place to maintain its viscosity. If crystallised, warm the jar in hot water before measuring 0.5 tbsp (10.5g). Always reseal tightly to prevent hardening, which can skew measurements.</p>
