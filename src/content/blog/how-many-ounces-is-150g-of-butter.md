<h2>How to Measure 150g of Butter Accurately</h2>
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the easiest way to measure 150g of butter. Simply place your butter on the scale and adjust until it reads 150g. If you don’t have a scale, you can use the markings on butter packaging—most UK blocks are 250g, so 150g is just over half. For ounces, remember that 150g translates to 5.29 oz.</p>
<p>For air fryer cooking, precision ensures even cooking and perfect texture. Whether you're making buttery crumb toppings or glazing vegetables, accurate measurements matter. <h2>Tips for Working with Butter in Your Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Butter can smoke at high temperatures, so it’s best used in moderation or mixed with oils in air fryer recipes. When converting 150g of butter to ounces (5.29 oz), consider how it will interact with other ingredients. For example, melted butter works well for coatings, while cold butter is better for pastry.</p>
