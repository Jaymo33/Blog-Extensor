<p>3 tablespoons of icing sugar equals approximately 36 grams in the UK. This conversion is essential for precise baking, especially when using an air fryer for desserts like cakes or cookies. Whether you're whipping up a quick treat or following a detailed recipe, knowing the exact weight ensures perfect results every time.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer baking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but inaccurate measurements can lead to dry or dense results. Using the correct grams for 3 tbsp of icing sugar ensures your desserts have the right texture and sweetness. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> rely on precise measurements to achieve the best outcome.</p>  

<p>For example, if a recipe calls for 3 tbsp of icing sugar but you use too much, your dessert may become overly sweet or crumbly. On the other hand, too little can leave it bland. Measuring by weight (grams) is more reliable than volume (tablespoons) because it accounts for variations in how the sugar is packed.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 3 tbsp of icing sugar correctly</h3>  
<p>To get an accurate 36 grams from 3 tbsp of icing sugar, lightly spoon the sugar into the tablespoon and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to an incorrect measurement. If you're using a kitchen scale, simply weigh out 36 grams for the perfect amount.</p>
