<h2>Why accurate brown sugar measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct grams for 2.5 tbsp of brown sugar ensures consistency in your air fryer recipes. Too much sugar can cause excessive browning or caramelisation, while too little may leave baked goods bland. Brown sugar’s moisture content also affects texture, making weight-based measurements more reliable than volume. Explore <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a> for ideas on how to use this measurement in cakes, cookies, and more.</p>

<h3>How to measure 2.5 tbsp brown sugar without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, lightly spoon brown sugar into a tablespoon and level it off with a knife. Repeat until you’ve measured 2.5 tbsp, which should roughly equal 31.25 grams. Avoid packing the sugar unless the recipe specifies, as this can add extra weight. For more tips, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>

<h3>Does brown sugar type affect the weight?</h3>  
<p>Light and dark brown sugar have similar densities, so 2.5 tbsp will weigh roughly 31.25 grams for both. However, tightly packed sugar may weigh slightly more—always fluff it before measuring for accuracy. For recipes demanding precision, like macarons, scales are best.</p>
