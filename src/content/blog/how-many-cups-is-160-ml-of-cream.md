<h2>How to Measure 160 ml of Cream Without a Measuring Cup</h2>
<p>Don't have a measuring cup? No problem! You can still measure 160 ml of cream using everyday kitchen tools. A standard tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so 160 ml is roughly 10.5 tablespoons. Alternatively, use a kitchen scale—160 ml of cream weighs approximately 160 grams, as cream has a similar density to water.</p>
<p>For those who prefer visual cues, 160 ml of cream fills just over half a standard US cup (which is 240 ml). If you're in the UK, where a cup is 284 ml, 160 ml is slightly more than half a cup.
