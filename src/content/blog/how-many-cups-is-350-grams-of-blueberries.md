<h2>Why Accurate Blueberry Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of blueberries ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly. Too few berries, and your dish might lack flavour; too many, and it could become soggy. Blueberries are often used in air fryer desserts like crisps or pancakes, where balance is key. For more inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> featuring blueberries. Measuring 350 grams of blueberries in cups is simple once you know the standard conversion.</p>  

<p>Fresh blueberries are plump and juicy, but their size can affect cup measurements. Smaller berries pack more tightly, while larger ones leave more gaps. If precision matters, weighing them is best. For air fryer baking, consistency ensures even cooking and perfect texture. Whether you're in the UK or US, 350 grams of blueberries converts reliably to around 2.5 cups.</p>  

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>While the difference is minimal, UK and US cup sizes vary slightly. A UK cup holds 250ml, whereas a US cup is 240ml. For 350 grams of blueberries, this means roughly 2.5 cups in both systems. If you're adapting recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>, this small variance won’t drastically alter your results. However, for exact replication, sticking to grams is safest.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Blueberries</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 350 grams to cups conversion, lightly spoon blueberries into a measuring cup without packing them down. If you press them in, you’ll end up with more than 2.5 cups. If precision is crucial, use a kitchen scale. This method is especially helpful for air fryer recipes where balance affects texture and doneness.</p>
