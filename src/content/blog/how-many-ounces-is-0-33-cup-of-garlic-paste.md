<h2>Understanding Garlic Paste Measurements</h2>  
<p>Garlic paste is thicker than minced garlic, which means its weight can vary slightly depending on density. A standard conversion for garlic paste is 1 cup equals roughly 8 ounces, so 0.33 cup works out to just under 2.64 ounces. This measurement is particularly useful when following air fryer recipes that require precise seasoning. If you're unsure, a kitchen scale can help verify the amount. For more detailed conversions, visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Many home cooks prefer using weight measurements for accuracy, especially with sticky ingredients like garlic paste. Volume measurements (like cups) can be inconsistent due to air pockets or packing density. Weighing your ingredients ensures your air fryer dishes turn out perfectly every time. If you're working with a recipe that calls for ounces, this conversion will save you time and hassle. <h3>Why Weight Matters in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient quantities can affect cooking times and results. Too much garlic paste might overpower a dish, while too little could leave it bland. Measuring by weight eliminates guesswork and ensures balanced flavours. If you're new to air frying, precise measurements help you master the technique faster.
