<p>2.25 cups of water equals 540 grams. This conversion is essential for precision in cooking, especially when using an air fryer where exact measurements can impact texture and cooking times. Whether you're baking, steaming, or preparing sauces, knowing how to convert cups to grams ensures consistency in your recipes.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Water Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Water is a key ingredient in many recipes, and even small deviations can affect the outcome. In air fryer cooking, too much water can lead to soggy results, while too little may cause dryness. <h2>How to Convert Cups to Grams for Water</h2>  
<p>One cup of water weighs 240 grams, so 2.25 cups of water is simply 2.25 multiplied by 240, giving you 540 grams. This conversion is consistent whether you're in the UK or the USA, as water density doesn’t change. For other liquids like oil or milk, the conversion differs due to varying densities.</p>  

<h3>Using Grams in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>Air fryers excel at delivering crispy textures, but moisture balance is key. If a recipe calls for 2.25 cups of water (540 grams), sticking to grams ensures consistency. For example, when making air fryer steamed buns or dumplings, precise water measurements prevent dough from becoming too sticky or dry.</p>  

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Water</h2>  
<p>Many home cooks measure water by eye, leading to inconsistencies. Overfilling or underfilling cups can throw off a recipe. For best results, use a clear measuring cup on a flat surface and check at eye level. If you're preparing a dish like air fryer vegetable stir-fry, exact water measurements ensure even cooking.</p>  

<p>Another mistake is assuming all liquids weigh the same. Water’s density is 1g/ml, but oils and syrups differ. Always double-check conversions for non-water liquids. For more tips, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a> that rely on accurate liquid measurements.</p>  

<h3>Scaling Recipes Up or Down</h3>  
<p>If you need to adjust a recipe, converting cups to grams makes scaling effortless. For instance, halving 2.25 cups of water (540g) becomes 270 grams. This precision is especially useful for air fryer meal prep, where batch cooking requires consistency.
