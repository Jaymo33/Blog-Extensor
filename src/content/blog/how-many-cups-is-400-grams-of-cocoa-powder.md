<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, a standard cup holds 250ml, while in the US, it’s 240ml. This slight difference means 400 grams of cocoa powder converts to roughly 3.2 UK cups or 3.4 US cups. For best results, use a kitchen scale, but if you’re working with cups, fluff the cocoa powder first to avoid compacting it. This ensures your air fryer brownies or chocolate muffins turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Measuring Cocoa Powder</h2>  
<p>To measure 400 grams of cocoa powder accurately, avoid scooping directly from the bag—this compresses the powder and leads to excess. Instead, use a spoon to lightly fill the cup, then level it off with a knife. If your recipe calls for sifted cocoa powder, measure after sifting to prevent clumping. For air fryer recipes, like those found on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a>, this method guarantees a smooth batter.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h3>  
<p>One frequent error is confusing cocoa powder with drinking chocolate, which often contains sugar and milk powder. Always check your labels—pure cocoa powder is unsweetened and denser. Also, avoid tapping the cup after filling, as this settles the powder and skews the measurement. For air fryer baking, precision ensures even cooking, so take the extra moment to measure correctly.</p>
