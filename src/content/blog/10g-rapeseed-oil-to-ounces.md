<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of oil in your air fryer is crucial for achieving that perfect crispiness without overdoing it. Too much oil can lead to soggy results, while too little might leave your food dry. Rapeseed oil is a popular choice for air frying due to its high smoke point and neutral flavour, making it ideal for a wide range of dishes.</p>

<h2>How to measure 10g of rapeseed oil</h2>
<p>Measuring 10g of rapeseed oil can be done using a kitchen scale for accuracy. If you don’t have a scale, you can use a measuring spoon, keeping in mind that 10g is roughly two teaspoons. This small amount is often enough to lightly coat your ingredients, ensuring they cook evenly in the air fryer.</p>

<h3>Common uses for 10g of rapeseed oil in air fryers</h3>
<p>10g of rapeseed oil is often used to coat vegetables, chicken, or potatoes before air frying. This small amount is enough to create a light, crispy texture without excess grease. It’s also a great way to keep your air fryer basket clean, as too much oil can lead to messy splatters.</p>

<h2>Benefits of rapeseed oil in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Rapeseed oil is a versatile and healthy option for air frying. Its high smoke point means it won’t break down at high temperatures, making it safer and more effective for cooking. Additionally, its neutral flavour won’t overpower your dishes, allowing the natural tastes of your ingredients to shine through.</p>

<h3>Tips for using rapeseed oil in your air fryer</h3>
<p>To get the best results, always preheat your air fryer before adding oil-coated ingredients. This ensures even cooking and a crispier finish. Also, remember to shake or turn your food halfway through cooking to guarantee all sides are perfectly golden.</p>
