<p>2.5 cups of wholemeal flour equals approximately 10.6 ounces (oz). This conversion is essential for accurate baking, especially when using an air fryer for recipes that require precise measurements. Whether you're making bread, muffins, or other baked goods, knowing the weight of your flour ensures consistency and perfect results every time.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Flour Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small variations in flour measurements can affect the texture and rise of your baked goods. Wholemeal flour, in particular, has a different density compared to plain or self-raising flour, making weight-based measurements more reliable. Using an air fryer for baking? Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>Measuring by weight (ounces or grams) eliminates the guesswork of scooping flour, which can vary based on how tightly packed it is. For air fryer recipes, precision ensures even cooking and avoids dense or dry results. If you're new to air fryer baking, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>collection of recipes</a> designed for perfect results.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Wholemeal Flour Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate conversion, spoon the flour into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid tapping or packing the flour down, as this can lead to over-measuring. If you're using a kitchen scale, 2.5 cups of wholemeal flour should weigh around 10.6 ounces (300 grams).</p>  

<h2>Converting Cups to Ounces for Other Flours</h2>  
<p>Different types of flour have varying densities, so their weight per cup differs. For example, plain flour weighs about 4.4 ounces per cup, while wholemeal flour is slightly heavier at 4.25 ounces per cup. This means 2.5 cups of plain flour would be around 11 ounces, whereas wholemeal flour is 10.6 ounces.</p>  

<p>If you're adapting recipes for your air fryer, always check whether the measurements are given by volume (cups) or weight (ounces or grams). Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> often include both for convenience. For more tips on air fryer baking, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Flour</h3>  
<p>Scooping flour directly from the bag with a measuring cup can compact it, leading to up to 20% more flour than intended. This can make baked goods dry or dense. Always fluff the flour first, then spoon it into the cup. For air fryer recipes, accuracy is key—especially for delicate bakes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cookies>cookies</a>.</p>  

<h2>Using a Kitchen Scale for Precision</h2>  
<p>A digital kitchen scale is the best tool for measuring flour by weight. Simply place your bowl on the scale, tare it to zero, and add flour until you reach 10.6 ounces (for 2.5 cups of wholemeal flour). This method is foolproof and ensures your air fryer bakes turn out perfectly every time.</p>
