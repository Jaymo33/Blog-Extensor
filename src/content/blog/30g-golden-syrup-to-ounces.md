<h2>Why accurate conversions matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Golden syrup is a staple in many British desserts and glazes, and its thickness means even small measurement errors can affect texture. Air fryers cook food quickly, so precise ingredient ratios are crucial. Converting 30g of golden syrup to ounces (1.06 oz) helps avoid overly sweet or dry results. For more air fryer measurement tips, check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a>.</p>

<h3>How to measure golden syrup without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the displacement method: fill a measuring jug with water to a known volume, add golden syrup until the water rises by 30ml (equivalent to 30g), and you’ve got your 1.06 ounces. This trick works for most dense liquids in air fryer cooking. For more clever hacks, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 30g golden syrup in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Golden syrup adds moisture and caramelised flavour to air-fried cakes, flapjacks, and glazes. A 30g (1.06 oz) portion is ideal for drizzling over roasted vegetables or brushing onto meats. Its high sugar content also promotes browning, giving dishes a professional finish. <h3>Storing golden syrup for air fryer recipes</h3>  
<p>Keep golden syrup in an airtight container to prevent crystallisation. At room temperature, 30g (1.06 oz) will pour easily for precise measuring. If it hardens, warm the jar slightly in water before use. Proper storage ensures your 30g golden syrup to ounces conversions remain accurate batch after batch.</p>
