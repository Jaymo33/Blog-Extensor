<h2>Understanding Lentil Measurements</h2>  
<p>Lentils come in various types, from green and brown to red and black, but their weight-to-cup ratio remains fairly consistent. For 325 grams of lentils, you’ll need roughly 1.6 UK cups or 1.5 US cups. This slight difference arises because UK cups are slightly larger (284ml) compared to US cups (240ml). If you’re using an air fryer, measuring lentils correctly ensures even cooking and perfect texture.</p>

<h2>Converting 325 Grams of Lentils to Cups</h2>  
<p>To convert 325 grams of lentils to cups, use a kitchen scale for accuracy. If you don’t have one, remember that 1 UK cup of lentils weighs about 200 grams, while 1 US cup is roughly 220 grams. This means 325 grams of lentils is approximately 1.6 UK cups or 1.5 US cups. These measurements are essential for recipes like air-fried lentil patties or stuffed vegetables.</p>

<h3>Tips for Cooking Lentils in an Air Fryer</h3>  
<p>Pre-soaking lentils reduces cooking time and improves texture. For 325 grams of lentils, soak them for at least 30 minutes before air frying. Drain well to avoid excess moisture, which can lead to steaming instead of crisping. Season generously and toss with a bit of oil for even browning. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner guide</a> to keep your appliance in top shape after cooking lentils.</p>  

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Lentils</h2>  
<p>Packing lentils too tightly into a cup can lead to inaccurate measurements. Instead, spoon them lightly into the measuring cup and level off the top. For 325 grams of lentils, this ensures you get the right amount for your recipe. Overcrowding the air fryer basket is another common issue—cook in batches if needed for even results.</p>
