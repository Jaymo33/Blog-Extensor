<h2>How to Measure 50 Grams of Rolled Oats Without Scales</h2>
<p>Not everyone has kitchen scales, but you can still measure 50 grams of rolled oats using a standard cup. Lightly spoon the oats into a measuring cup without packing them down, then level off the top. Remember, 50 grams equals roughly 0.6 cups, so fill just over halfway for accuracy.</p>
<p>For best results, use the scoop-and-sweep method: gently scoop the oats into the cup and sweep away the excess with a straight edge. Avoid tapping the cup, as this compacts the oats and leads to incorrect measurements. If you're baking oat cookies or muffins, precision ensures the right rise and texture.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>
<p>While 50 grams of rolled oats equals 0.6 cups in both the UK and US, slight variations exist in cup sizes. A UK cup is 250ml, whereas a US cup is 240ml. For most recipes, this difference won’t drastically alter results, but it’s worth noting for exact conversions.</p>

<h3>Storing Rolled Oats for Freshness</h3>
<p>Keep rolled oats in an airtight container away from moisture to maintain freshness. Proper storage prevents clumping and preserves their nutty flavour, whether you’re using 50 grams or a larger batch. For long-term storage, freeze them in portioned bags for easy access.</p>

<h2>Tips for Cooking with Rolled Oats in an Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Rolled oats cook quickly in the air fryer, so watch them closely to avoid burning. Toss them occasionally for even crisping, especially when making granola. For binding mixtures like energy balls, combine oats with wet ingredients before air frying to prevent dryness.</p>
