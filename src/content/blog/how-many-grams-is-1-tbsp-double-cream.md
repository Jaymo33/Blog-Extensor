<h3>How to measure double cream without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a scale, you can still measure 1 tbsp of double cream accurately. A standard UK tablespoon holds 15ml, which equates to roughly 15 grams for double cream. However, slight variations can occur depending on the cream's thickness. For best results, use a proper measuring spoon and level it off with a knife. This method works well for most air fryer recipes, from creamy pasta dishes to indulgent puddings.</p>

<h3>Storing and handling double cream for best results</h3>  
<p>Double cream should always be stored in the refrigerator and used within its use-by date. When measuring 1 tbsp of double cream in grams, ensure it's at the right temperature - too cold and it might not pour accurately, too warm and it could spoil your measurements. For air fryer recipes, it's often best to let cream come to room temperature before use, unless the recipe specifies otherwise. This ensures even mixing and better texture in your finished dish.</p>
