<h2>Why Accurate Honey Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Honey is denser than many other liquids, meaning volume measurements like cups can vary slightly based on temperature and type. For air frying or baking, even small discrepancies can affect texture and sweetness. If a recipe calls for 250 grams of honey, using a scale eliminates guesswork. Alternatively, spooning honey into a measuring cup and levelling it off helps approximate the 0.75-cup mark. For more conversion tips, visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>Fortunately, 250 grams of honey converts to the same cup measurement in both the UK and US (0.75 cups). The difference lies in other ingredients—like flour or sugar—where UK cups are slightly larger. Always check the recipe’s origin to avoid confusion. For air fryer baking, sticking to weight measurements (grams) guarantees consistency.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Measuring Honey Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, lightly grease your measuring cup with oil to help the honey slide out easily. Fill it gradually, levelling off the top with a knife. Remember, 250 grams of honey equals roughly 12 tablespoons, which can be handy for smaller adjustments. For sticky situations, <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a> can tackle residue left behind.</p>  

<p>Honey’s viscosity means it settles differently in cups, so avoid packing it down. Instead, gently stir it to remove air bubbles before measuring. This is especially useful for air fryer desserts like honey-drizzled cakes or cookies.
