<h2>Why Convert 30g of Hot Sauce to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, while others rely on ounces, making conversions a daily kitchen task. Hot sauce, in particular, can overpower a dish if not measured correctly. <h3>How to Measure 30g of Hot Sauce Accurately</h3>  
<p>A digital kitchen scale is the easiest way to measure 30g of hot sauce. Simply place your container on the scale, tare it to zero, and pour until you hit 30g. If you don’t have a scale, remember that 30g equals roughly two tablespoons, but this can vary slightly based on the sauce’s thickness. For sticky sauces, lightly grease your spoon to ensure all 30g slides off effortlessly.</p>

<h3>Does Hot Sauce Weight Change with Temperature?</h3>  
<p>Hot sauce’s weight remains consistent regardless of temperature, but its viscosity can vary. Chilled sauce may pour more slowly, while room-temperature sauce flows easily. However, 30g will always equal 1.06 ounces, whether it’s cold or warm. This makes it a reliable measurement for both marinades and finishing drizzles.</p>
