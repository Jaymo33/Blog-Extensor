<h3>How to Measure 760 ml of Beef Stock Without a Measuring Cup</h3>
<p>Don’t have a measuring cup? No problem. You can still measure 760 ml of beef stock using common kitchen tools. A standard mug holds about 300 ml, so two and a half mugs will give you close to 760 ml. Alternatively, use a tablespoon—15 ml per tablespoon means you’d need roughly 51 tablespoons to reach 760 ml. While this method isn’t as precise, it works in a pinch. For best results, though, we recommend investing in a good set of measuring cups or a kitchen scale. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Beef Stock</h3>
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is assuming all cups are the same. As mentioned earlier, UK and US cups differ slightly, which can throw off your recipe. Also, avoid using dry measuring cups for liquids, as they won’t give you the correct volume. For 760 ml of beef stock, a liquid measuring cup with clear markings is your best bet.</p>
