<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small deviations in flour weight can affect texture and rise. Wholemeal flour, in particular, has a denser consistency than plain flour, so measuring by weight rather than volume is more reliable. <h3>How to measure wholemeal flour correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 1 cup of wholemeal flour accurately, fluff the flour in its bag or container first. Spoon it lightly into a measuring cup without packing it down, then level off the top with a straight edge. This prevents compacting, which can lead to using too much flour. For air fryer baking, where space and heat distribution matter, precise measurements are even more critical.</p>  

<h2>Converting cups to ounces for different flours</h2>  
<p>Not all flours weigh the same per cup. While wholemeal flour is around 4.5 ounces per cup, plain flour is closer to 4.25 ounces, and self-raising flour is similar. This variation is why weight-based measurements are preferred in professional baking. If you're adapting a recipe for your air fryer, always check whether it uses UK or US cup measurements, as they differ slightly.</p>

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring flour</h3>  
<p>One of the biggest errors is scooping flour directly with the measuring cup, which packs it down and adds extra weight. Another is tapping the cup to settle the flour, which can increase the amount by up to 20%. For air fryer recipes, where space is limited, these small differences can significantly impact cooking times and results.</p>
