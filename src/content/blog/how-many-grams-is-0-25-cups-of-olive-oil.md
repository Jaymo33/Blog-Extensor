<h2>Why Accurate Olive Oil Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of olive oil in your air fryer ensures even cooking and prevents excess grease. Too little oil can leave food dry, while too much may cause uneven crisping. For recipes like roasted vegetables or crispy chicken, 0.25 cups of olive oil (55 grams) is often the ideal amount. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurements.</p>  

<p>Olive oil is a staple in Mediterranean and air fryer cooking due to its high smoke point and health benefits. Whether you’re preparing a quick <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable dish</a> or a hearty meal, precise measurements guarantee better texture and flavour. Always measure oil by weight (grams) for baking, as volume (cups) can vary slightly.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.25 Cups of Olive Oil</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.25 cups of olive oil accurately, use a liquid measuring cup or a kitchen scale. If using cups, fill to the quarter-cup mark, ensuring the oil sits level. For grams, place your container on the scale, tare it, then pour until it reaches 55 grams. This method eliminates guesswork and improves recipe consistency.</p>
