<h2>How to measure 250g of sesame oil without scales</h2>
<p>If you don't have digital scales handy, you can still measure out 250g (8.82oz) of sesame oil using volume measurements. Approximately 250g of sesame oil equals 275ml or about 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> includes these handy equivalents for all your cooking needs.</p>
<p>For best results when measuring by volume, use clear liquid measuring cups at eye level. The meniscus (the curve at the oil's surface) should align with the measurement line. This technique helps ensure you're getting the right amount of sesame oil for your 250g requirement, whether you're making Asian-inspired dishes or salad dressings.</p>

<h3>The role of sesame oil in air fryer cooking</h3>
<p>Sesame oil adds incredible depth of flavour to air fried dishes while requiring minimal quantity. Just 250g (8.82oz) can go a long way in marinades, dressings or light coatings.
