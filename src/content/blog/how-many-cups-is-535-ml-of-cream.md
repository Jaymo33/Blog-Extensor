<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for Cream</h3>  
<p>It's important to note that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup equals 250 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. This means 535 ml of cream converts to 2.26 cups in the UK and 2.25 cups in the US. For most recipes, this small difference won't matter, but for precision baking, it's worth keeping in mind. If you're using a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>airfryerrecipe.co.uk's dessert category</a>, double-check which measurement system it follows.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 535 ml of Cream Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don't have a measuring jug, you can still measure 535 ml of cream using standard cups. In the UK, this would be just over 2 cups (500 ml) plus 2 tablespoons (35 ml). In the US, it's 2 cups (480 ml) plus 3 tablespoons (55 ml). This method is handy for quick adjustments, especially when preparing air fryer recipes that require a bit of flexibility. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning and maintenance guide</a> to keep your appliance in top shape.</p>  

<p>Another trick is to use a liquid measuring cup marked in millilitres. Simply pour the cream up to the 535 ml line, and you're good to go. This is the most accurate way to measure 535 ml of cream for recipes where precision is key, such as custards or whipped cream toppings for air fryer desserts.</p>
