<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and small discrepancies in ingredient weights can affect texture and flavour. For example, using too much apple cider vinegar in a marinade might overpower the dish, while too little could leave it bland. That’s why converting 0.25 tbsp of apple cider vinegar to grams is so useful—it takes the guesswork out of cooking.</p>

<h2>How to measure 0.25 tbsp of apple cider vinegar</h2>
<p>Measuring small quantities like 0.25 tbsp can be tricky without the right tools. A standard UK tablespoon holds 15ml, so a quarter of that is just 3.75ml. If you don’t have a measuring spoon set, you can use a kitchen scale to weigh out 3.75 grams directly. This method is especially handy for sticky or thick liquids like apple cider vinegar.</p>

<h3>Common uses for apple cider vinegar in air fryer recipes</h3>
<p>Apple cider vinegar is a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. It’s often used in marinades for meats like chicken or pork, adding a tangy kick. A small amount, like 0.25 tbsp, can also be mixed into dressings for salads or drizzled over roasted vegetables. Its acidity helps tenderise proteins and enhances flavours without overpowering them.</p>

<h2>Tips for storing and using apple cider vinegar</h2>
<p>To keep your apple cider vinegar fresh, store it in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. Always seal the bottle tightly to prevent evaporation.
