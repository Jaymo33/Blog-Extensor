<h2>How to Convert 2.25 kg Whole Chicken to Pounds Accurately</h2>
<p>Converting 2.25 kg whole chicken to pounds is straightforward once you know the basic formula. One kilogram is approximately equal to 2.20462 pounds, so to find the pound equivalent of 2.25 kg, you multiply 2.25 by 2.20462. This calculation gives you 4.960395 pounds, which is typically rounded to 5 pounds for simplicity in cooking. While the exact conversion is just under 5 pounds, rounding up is common practice in recipes to account for minor variations in chicken size and density. This approach ensures that your air fryer settings are aligned with standard recipe guidelines, reducing the risk of error. It's a small step that makes a big difference in achieving consistently delicious results.</p>

<h3>Practical Tips for Cooking a 2.25 kg Whole Chicken in Your Air Fryer</h3>
<p>To cook a 2.25 kg whole chicken to perfection in your air fryer, start by ensuring it is fully thawed if previously frozen. Pat it dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture, which helps achieve crispy skin. Season the chicken inside and out with your preferred herbs and spices, and truss it loosely to maintain an even shape. Preheat your air fryer to 180°C for about 3 minutes before placing the chicken breast-side down in the basket. Cook for 30 minutes, then flip it breast-side up and continue cooking for another 20-30 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 75°C. Let it rest for 10 minutes before carving to allow juices to redistribute. This method delivers a beautifully golden and juicy chicken that rivals traditional roasting.</p>

<p>For added flavour, consider marinating the chicken for a few hours before cooking or injecting it with a broth-based mixture. Basting with melted butter or oil during the last 10 minutes of cooking can enhance browning and flavour. If your air fryer has a rotisserie function, use it for even more consistent results with a 2.25 kg whole chicken. Always check the manufacturer's instructions for maximum weight capacity to avoid overloading. After cooking, clean your air fryer promptly to maintain its performance; leftover grease can affect future dishes.
