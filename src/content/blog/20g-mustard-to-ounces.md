<h3>Tools for measuring mustard</h3>  
<p>While digital scales are the most accurate, not everyone has one handy. A standard teaspoon holds about 5g of mustard, so 20g would be roughly 4 teaspoons. For ounces, that’s just under 0.71 oz—close enough for most home cooks. Keep a set of measuring spoons or a small jug marked in ounces nearby for quick checks.</p>

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring mustard</h3>  
<p>One error is using volume (like tablespoons) interchangeably with weight. Mustard’s thickness means 20g won’t always fill the same spoon size—especially with grainy varieties. Always opt for weight where possible. Another pitfall? Forgetting to level off your measuring spoon, which can add extra grams unintentionally.</p>
