<p>30g of yeast equals approximately 1.06 ounces. This conversion is essential for bakers who need precise measurements, especially when adapting recipes for air fryers. Whether you're making bread, pizza dough, or sweet treats, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures consistency in your results.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Yeast Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of yeast is crucial for successful baking. Too little yeast can result in dense, under-risen dough, while too much may cause an overly yeasty flavour or even dough collapse. For air fryer recipes, where cooking times are shorter, precision is even more important. A slight variation in yeast quantity can affect texture and rise, so getting your 30g to ounces conversion right is key.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 30g of Yeast Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don't have digital scales, you can estimate 30g of yeast using tablespoons. Generally, one level tablespoon of active dry yeast weighs about 9g, so 30g would be roughly 3 and 1/3 tablespoons. However, this method isn't as precise as weighing, so for best results in your air fryer baking, we recommend investing in a small kitchen scale.</p>  

<h2>Converting Between Grams and Ounces</h2>  
<p>The conversion factor between grams and ounces is straightforward: 1 ounce equals approximately 28.35 grams. Therefore, to convert 30g to ounces, you divide 30 by 28.35, giving you about 1.06 ounces. This conversion works for all types of yeast, whether you're using active dry, instant, or fresh yeast in your recipes.</p>

<h3>Types of Yeast and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Different yeast types have slightly different weights per volume. Active dry yeast is lighter than fresh yeast, so 30g of each would occupy different volumes. However, when measured by weight (grams or ounces), the quantity remains consistent. This is why weight measurements are preferred for baking accuracy, especially in air fryers where precision matters.</p>

<p>Whether you've measured out 30g or another quantity, proper yeast storage is essential. Keep opened yeast in an airtight container in the fridge, or freeze for longer storage.
