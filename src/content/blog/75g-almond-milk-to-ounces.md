<h2>Why converting 75g almond milk to ounces matters</h2>  
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial in air fryer cooking, especially for liquids like almond milk. Using 75g of almond milk when a recipe calls for ounces can lead to inconsistencies. For example, a batter might turn out too thick or too runny. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies these calculations, so you never have to second-guess.</p>  
<p>Almond milk is a popular dairy-free alternative, but its density can vary slightly between brands. That’s why weighing it (75g) or measuring it in ounces (2.65 oz) ensures reliability. If you’re prepping a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert</a> or a savoury dish, precision guarantees the best texture and flavour.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 75g of almond milk accurately</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 75g almond milk to ounces conversion, use a digital kitchen scale. Place your measuring cup on the scale, tare it to zero, then pour the almond milk until it hits 75g. If you don’t have a scale, 75g is roughly 5 tablespoons or ⅓ cup, but weighing is always more precise.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 75g almond milk in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Almond milk is a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. From <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast</a> pancakes to crispy coatings for chicken, 75g (2.65 oz) can be the perfect amount. For instance, a light batter for air-fried tofu might call for 75g of almond milk to achieve the ideal consistency.</p>  
<p>If you’re making a creamy dip or sauce, 75g of almond milk balances richness without overpowering other flavours. Precision ensures your dishes turn out just right every time.</p>  

<h3>Tips for storing and using almond milk</h3>  
<p>Once opened, almond milk should be refrigerated and used within 7–10 days. Shake well before measuring 75g, as separation is natural. For longer storage, freeze almond milk in 75g portions for future air fryer recipes. Just thaw and stir before use.</p>
