<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to overly salty or bland dishes. Fish sauce, in particular, is potent, so getting the 0.5 cup to ounces conversion right is crucial. Too much can overpower your meal, while too little might leave it lacking depth. If you're experimenting with new recipes, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> offers plenty of inspiration.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer enthusiasts overlook the importance of liquid measurements, assuming a rough estimate will do. However, fish sauce's strong umami flavour means even small discrepancies can alter the dish. For best results, use a kitchen scale to measure 0.5 cup of fish sauce in ounces accurately.</p>  

<h3>How to measure fish sauce without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use a liquid measuring cup for the 0.5 cup fish sauce to ounces conversion. Fill it to the 0.5 cup mark, ensuring the meniscus aligns with the measurement line. For sticky liquids like fish sauce, lightly grease the cup to help it pour smoothly. This method is handy for quick recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken wings</a>.</p>

<h3>Storing fish sauce for longevity</h3>  
<p>Fish sauce lasts longer when stored in a cool, dark place. Once opened, keep it refrigerated to maintain freshness. If you’ve measured out 0.5 cup of fish sauce in ounces (4 oz) and have leftovers, transfer them to an airtight container. This prevents contamination and preserves the sauce’s robust flavour for future air fryer creations.</p>
