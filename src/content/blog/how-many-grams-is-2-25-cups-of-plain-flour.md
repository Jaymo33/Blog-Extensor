<h2>Why Accurate Flour Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small deviations in flour quantity can affect texture and rise. Too much flour can make your baked goods dense, while too little may cause them to collapse. For air fryer recipes, where cooking times and temperatures differ from conventional ovens, precision is even more critical. A digital kitchen scale is the best tool for ensuring accuracy, but if you’re using cups, our conversion helps bridge the gap.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 2.25 Cups of Plain Flour Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 2.25 cups of plain flour accurately, fluff the flour with a spoon before lightly spooning it into the measuring cup. Level off the excess with a straight edge—avoid tapping or packing the flour down. This method helps prevent over-measuring, which is a common issue when using volume-based measurements. For best results, consider investing in a kitchen scale to weigh your flour directly.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Flour</h3>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes bakers make is scooping flour directly from the bag with a measuring cup. This compacts the flour, leading to an excess amount. Another error is failing to level off the cup, which can add unnecessary grams. For air fryer baking, where space and heat distribution are limited, these small errors can have a big impact. Always fluff, spoon, and level for the most accurate measurement.</p>
