<h2>Understanding Milk Measurements</h2>
<p>Milk is a common ingredient in many recipes, from cakes to creamy pasta dishes. When a recipe calls for 475 grams of milk, it's important to know how that translates to cups. The density of milk means that 475 grams doesn't perfectly align with whole cup measurements. This is why many professional bakers prefer weighing ingredients for precision. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> can help with other common conversions too.</p>

<p>In the UK, a standard cup measures 250ml, while in the US, it's 240ml. This slight difference affects how many cups 475 grams of milk will fill. For UK measurements, 475 grams equals about 1.93 cups, while in the US, it's a neat 2 cups. Knowing which measurement system your recipe uses is crucial for accuracy. This is particularly important when adapting recipes for your air fryer, where precise measurements can affect cooking times.</p>

<h3>Why Weight Measurements Are More Accurate</h3>
<p>Weight measurements like grams are more precise than volume measurements like cups. This is because ingredients can settle or be packed differently in a cup, affecting the actual amount. When you measure 475 grams of milk, you're getting exactly that - no more, no less. This precision is especially valuable in baking, where exact proportions matter. Many of our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> include both weight and volume measurements for this reason.</p>

<h2>Converting 475 Grams of Milk to Cups</h2>
<p>The conversion from 475 grams of milk to cups depends on the type of milk and its fat content. Whole milk, semi-skimmed, and skimmed milk have slightly different densities. However, for most practical purposes, we can use an average conversion. In the UK, where 1 cup equals 250ml, 475 grams of milk is approximately 1.93 cups. In the US, where 1 cup equals 240ml, it's exactly 2 cups.</p>

<p>This conversion is particularly useful when you're working with air fryer recipes that might originate from different countries. Many American recipes use cup measurements, while UK and European recipes often use grams. Being able to convert between these systems means you can try more <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> from around the world in your air fryer.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Milk Accurately</h3>
<p>When measuring 475 grams of milk, using a kitchen scale is the most accurate method. If you don't have scales, use a liquid measuring cup rather than dry measuring cups for better accuracy. Remember that milk expands when heated, so if you're using your air fryer for recipes like custards or puddings, the exact measurement matters.
