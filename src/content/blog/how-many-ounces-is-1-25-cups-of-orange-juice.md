<p>1.25 cups of orange juice equals approximately 10 fluid ounces (fl oz). This conversion is essential for accurate measurements, especially when preparing recipes for your air fryer. Whether you're making a glaze, marinade, or a refreshing drink, knowing the exact weight ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Precision is key when cooking with an air fryer, as even small variations can affect texture and cooking times. Converting 1.25 cups of orange juice to ounces (10 fl oz) helps maintain consistency. For example, a marinade with too much liquid might not adhere properly to your chicken or salmon, while too little could leave your dish dry. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Orange Juice for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>To measure 1.25 cups of orange juice accurately, use a liquid measuring cup with clear markings. Pour the juice to the 1.25-cup line, or weigh it to confirm it’s 10 fl oz. Freshly squeezed orange juice may vary slightly in density, so adjust if needed. For sticky glazes or sauces, consider using a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>non-stick air fryer liner</a> to prevent mess.</p>
