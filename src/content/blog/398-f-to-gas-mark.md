<h2>Understanding 398 F to Gas Mark Conversion</h2>
<p>The Gas Mark system is uniquely British, with each number representing a specific temperature range. Gas Mark 6 corresponds to 200-210°C, with 398°F sitting at 203°C - right in the middle of this range. When using your air fryer, this temperature works wonders for achieving crispy skins on chicken or perfect roast potatoes. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> originally designed for conventional ovens will specify 398°F, making this conversion particularly useful.</p>

<h2>Practical Applications in Air Fryers</h2>
<p>Modern air fryers with digital displays often show Fahrenheit, Celsius and sometimes Gas Mark equivalents. If yours doesn't, setting it to 200°C will give you the correct 398 F to Gas Mark 6 equivalent. This temperature works particularly well for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, creating that desirable golden-brown finish without drying out the meat.</p>
<p>When converting 398 degrees Fahrenheit to Gas Mark for air frying, remember that cooking times may be shorter than conventional oven recipes.
