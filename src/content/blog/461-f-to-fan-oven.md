<p>461°F converts to approximately 240°C in a fan oven - the standard setting for high-heat roasting and baking in the UK. This temperature is ideal for achieving crispy textures in air fryers while ensuring even cooking. Whether you're adapting an American recipe or troubleshooting your appliance settings, understanding this conversion is key to kitchen success.</p>

<h2>Why 461°F Matters for Air Fryer Cooking</h2>
<p>Many US-based air fryer recipes recommend 461°F as the optimal temperature for achieving restaurant-quality crispness. At 240°C in a fan oven, this heat level perfectly caramelises surfaces while locking in moisture. The fan-assisted circulation mimics an air fryer's rapid heat distribution, making it the closest match for UK kitchens.</p>
<p>When preparing <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> or roasted vegetables, 461°F (240°C fan) creates that desirable golden-brown finish. The high heat causes immediate surface drying, which is why many frozen foods also recommend this temperature range. Just remember to reduce cooking times by 20% compared to standard oven instructions, as fan ovens and air fryers work more efficiently.</p>

<h3>How Fan Ovens Differ From Conventional Heating</h3>
<p>Unlike traditional ovens that rely on radiant heat, fan ovens use forced air circulation - much like an air fryer's cooking mechanism. This means 461°F in a conventional oven doesn't translate directly to a fan setting. The moving air transfers heat more efficiently, allowing you to lower the temperature by about 20°C while achieving similar results. That's why 461°F becomes 240°C rather than the straight 238°C conversion.</p>

Always preheat your appliance for at least 10 minutes when using high temperatures to ensure consistent results.</p>

<h3>Temperature Accuracy and Calibration</h3>
<p>Oven thermostats can vary, so it's wise to verify your 461°F (240°C fan) setting with an independent thermometer. Place it in the centre of your air fryer basket or oven rack after preheating. If readings differ significantly, you may need to adjust cooking times or consult your appliance manual for recalibration instructions.</p>

However, for true air fryer results, nothing beats the original 240°C fan setting with proper air circulation.</p>
