<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Baking</h2>
<p>Air fryers cook food faster and more evenly than traditional ovens, making precise measurements crucial. Too much or too little brown sugar can alter the texture, browning, and moisture of your baked goods. For example, 1.5 cups of brown sugar in grams (300g) ensures your cookies spread just right without burning. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient conversions. Consistency is key, especially when adapting recipes for air fryers.</p>

<p>Brown sugar’s moisture content also affects measurements. Packed brown sugar will weigh more than loosely scooped sugar, so always level off your cups. For best results, use a kitchen scale to measure 300 grams directly. This eliminates guesswork and guarantees your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> for inspiration on using brown sugar in desserts and savoury dishes.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 1.5 Cups of Brown Sugar Correctly</h3>
<p>To measure 1.5 cups of brown sugar accurately, spoon the sugar into a measuring cup and press it down lightly. Avoid packing it too tightly, as this can lead to excess sugar and throw off your recipe. If you’re converting 1.5 cups of brown sugar to grams, a scale is your best friend. Simply tare your scale with the empty cup, then add sugar until it reads 300 grams.</p>
