<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can throw off the balance of your dish. It’s a common measurement in many recipes, especially those found on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>. <p>Many UK cooks prefer using grams, but some recipes, particularly older or American ones, list ingredients in ounces. Converting 250g of ginger paste to ounces bridges this gap. It’s also handy when scaling recipes up or down. If you're meal prepping or cooking for a crowd, knowing that 250g equals 8.82 ounces helps maintain consistency across batches.</p>  

<h3>How to measure ginger paste accurately</h3>  
<p>For the best results, use a digital kitchen scale to weigh your ginger paste. If you don’t have one, a standard measuring spoon can work in a pinch—though it’s less precise. One tablespoon of ginger paste weighs roughly 15g, so 250g would be about 16.5 tablespoons. However, for air fryer recipes, we recommend sticking to weight measurements for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Storing ginger paste for freshness</h3>  
<p>To keep your ginger paste fresh, store it in an airtight container in the fridge. It should last for up to two weeks. For longer storage, freeze it in ice cube trays—each cube holds about 15g (0.53 oz), making it easy to thaw just what you need. This method is perfect for air fryer cooks who want to minimise waste.</p>
