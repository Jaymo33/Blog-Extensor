<p>423°F converts to Gas Mark 7 in UK ovens. This is a common temperature for baking and roasting in both traditional ovens and air fryers. The conversion is essential when adapting recipes between different cooking appliances.</p>

<h2>Understanding Gas Mark and Fahrenheit</h2>
<p>Gas Mark is a temperature scale used primarily in the UK for ovens. It ranges from Gas Mark 1 (275°F) to Gas Mark 9 (475°F). When converting 423 Fahrenheit to Gas Mark, you land precisely on Gas Mark 7. This makes it easy to translate American recipes that use Fahrenheit to UK measurements.</p>
<p>Air fryers typically display temperatures in Celsius or Fahrenheit, but knowing the Gas Mark equivalent helps when following traditional British recipes. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we recommend keeping a conversion chart handy for quick reference between these measurement systems.</p>

<h2>Why 423°F Matters in Cooking</h2>
<p>423°F (Gas Mark 7) is a versatile cooking temperature suitable for many dishes. It's hot enough to create beautiful browning on meats and vegetables while being gentle enough for delicate baked goods. In air fryers, this temperature works particularly well for items that need crisp exteriors and moist interiors.</p>
<p>Many frozen foods cook perfectly at 423°F to Gas Mark 7. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/frozen-foods>frozen foods section</a> for specific timing recommendations. The high heat helps achieve that desirable crispy texture without drying out the food.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times for Air Fryers</h3>
<p>When using 423°F in an air fryer instead of a conventional oven, cooking times are typically shorter. The powerful fan circulation cooks food more efficiently. As a general rule, reduce cooking times by about 20% when converting from oven to air fryer at Gas Mark 7 temperatures.</p>

<h2>Common Foods Cooked at 423°F (Gas Mark 7)</h2>
<p>This temperature works wonderfully for roasted vegetables, giving them caramelised edges while keeping centres tender. Chicken pieces develop golden, crispy skin at 423°F to Gas Mark 7, while remaining juicy inside. Many baked goods like scones and biscuits also thrive at this heat.</p>
<p>For specific recipe ideas at this temperature, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>. You'll find everything from crispy potatoes to perfect pork chops all tested at 423°F Gas Mark 7 equivalents.</p>

<h3>Temperature Accuracy in Air Fryers</h3>
<p>While 423°F converts precisely to Gas Mark 7, air fryer temperatures can vary slightly between models. We recommend using an oven thermometer to verify your appliance's accuracy, especially when trying new recipes at this temperature setting.</p>

<h2>Converting Other Temperatures to Gas Mark</h2>
<p>While we've focused on 423 Fahrenheit to Gas Mark conversion, you might need other temperature references. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> includes all common oven temperatures and their Gas Mark equivalents. This resource is particularly helpful when adapting family recipes between measurement systems.</p>
