<h2>Understanding Heavy Cream Measurements</h2>
<p>Heavy cream, also known as double cream in the UK, is a staple in many recipes from sauces to desserts. When a recipe calls for 285 ml of heavy cream, you might wonder how that translates to cups. The answer depends on whether you're using UK or US measurements, as cup sizes differ slightly between the two systems. For most practical purposes, 1.2 cups (UK) or 1.19 cups (US) will work perfectly.</p>

Remember that while these conversions are close approximations, for baking where precision matters, using a kitchen scale might be best.</p>

<h3>UK vs US Cup Measurements</h3>
<p>It's worth noting that UK and US cups differ slightly - a UK cup is 284 ml while a US cup is 240 ml. This means 285 ml of heavy cream is 1.2 UK cups or 1.19 US cups. While the difference seems small, it can matter in precise baking recipes. Most modern UK recipes use metric measurements, but if you're using an older cookbook or American recipe, being aware of this distinction helps.</p>

<h2>Practical Tips for Measuring Heavy Cream</h2>
<p>When measuring 285 ml of heavy cream, using a liquid measuring cup with clear markings is ideal. Pour the cream to the 285 ml line, or if using cups, fill just slightly over the 1 cup mark for UK measurements. For sticky ingredients like cream, spraying your measuring cup with a bit of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a> first can help it pour out completely.</p>
