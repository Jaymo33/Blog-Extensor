<h2>Why accurate onion measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so using the right amount of chopped onion prevents overcrowding and uneven cooking. Too much onion can lead to steaming instead of crisping, while too little might burn. Recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer onion bhajis</a> or caramelised onions need precise measurements for the best texture. A kitchen scale is handy, but if you’re using cups, our 2.5 cups to ounces conversion ensures consistency.</p>  

<p>Chopped onion varies slightly in density based on how finely it’s diced, but 2.5 cups generally weigh around 10 oz. For firmer onions like red or shallots, the weight might be slightly higher. If you’re prepping ahead, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable air fryer recipes</a> offer more tips on storing chopped onions.</p>  

<h3>How to measure chopped onion correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 2.5 cups to ounces conversion, lightly pack the chopped onion into a measuring cup without pressing down. Overpacking can add extra weight and throw off your recipe.
