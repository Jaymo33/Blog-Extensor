<h2>Why Accurate Honey Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of honey is crucial for achieving the perfect balance of sweetness and texture in your dishes. Too much honey can make a recipe overly sticky or sweet, while too little might leave it bland. For air fryer recipes, precise measurements like 0.5 cups of honey in ml (118 ml) ensure even cooking and caramelisation. This is particularly important for glazes, sauces, and baked goods where honey’s viscosity plays a key role.</p>  

<p>If you’re working with international recipes, conversions become even more critical. Many European recipes list honey in grams or ml, while US and UK recipes often use cups. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies this process, so you never have to guess. Whether you’re making honey-glazed chicken or a sweet dessert, accuracy is key.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Honey Correctly</h3>  
<p>Honey’s thick consistency can make measuring tricky. To get an accurate 0.5 cups of honey in ml (118 ml), lightly grease your measuring cup with oil or spray. This helps the honey slide out easily, ensuring you don’t leave any behind. Alternatively, use a kitchen scale for grams or a liquid measuring jug for millilitres. For sticky ingredients like honey, these methods often yield the most precise results.</p>

Try it in a honey-mustard glaze for chicken or as a sweetener for air-fried desserts like cinnamon apples. The natural sugars in honey also help achieve a beautiful caramelised finish, perfect for crispy textures.</p>

<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of honey, here’s a quick reference: 1 cup of honey equals roughly 236 ml, while 1 tablespoon is about 15 ml. These conversions are handy for scaling recipes up or down. <h2>Tips for Working with Honey in the Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Honey can burn easily due to its high sugar content, so it’s best used in lower-temperature settings or added towards the end of cooking. When using 0.5 cups of honey (118 ml) in a glaze, brush it on during the last few minutes to avoid charring. For baked goods, mixing honey with other liquids helps distribute the sweetness evenly.</p>
