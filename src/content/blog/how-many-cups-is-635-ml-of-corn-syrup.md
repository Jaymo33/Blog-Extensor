<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of corn syrup can make or break a recipe. Too much can lead to overly sticky or sweet results, while too little may leave dishes dry. Air fryers cook quickly, so precision is key to avoid burning or undercooking. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer dessert recipes</a> rely on exact measurements for perfect texture. Always measure liquids like corn syrup in a clear measuring jug for accuracy.</p>  

<p>Corn syrup is thicker than water, so it’s best to use a liquid measuring cup rather than dry cups. <h3>How to Measure 635 ml of Corn Syrup Correctly</h3>  
<p>Pour corn syrup into a liquid measuring cup up to the 635 ml mark, then level it off. If you don’t have metric measurements, use the 2.68 cups conversion. For sticky ingredients like corn syrup, lightly grease the measuring cup first to help it slide out easily. This trick works wonders for air fryer glazes and sauces.</p>

<h3>UK vs US Cup Measurements for Corn Syrup</h3>  
<p>While 635 ml converts to 2.68 US cups, UK cups are slightly different at 2.52 cups. Always check which measurement system your recipe uses. Most modern UK recipes now adopt metric measurements or US cups for consistency. When in doubt, stick to millilitres for precision, especially in air fryer cooking where small differences can affect results.</p>
