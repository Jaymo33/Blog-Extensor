<h2>Why measuring cooked rice matters for air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial when using an air fryer, as cooking times and temperatures rely on portion sizes. Too much rice can lead to uneven cooking, while too little might dry out. By converting 4 tbsp of cooked rice to grams, you ensure perfect results every time. This is especially useful for meal prepping or batch cooking. <p>Rice expands when cooked, so volume measurements like tablespoons can be misleading. Weighing in grams provides consistency, whether you’re reheating leftovers or making crispy rice cakes. For best results, use a digital kitchen scale for precision. This method also works well for other grains like quinoa or couscous. <h3>How to measure 4 tbsp of cooked rice without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, lightly pack the rice into a tablespoon without pressing down. Four level tablespoons should give you roughly 60 grams. For sticky rice, fluff it first to avoid compacting. This method works for most types of cooked rice, from basmati to jasmine. Keep in mind that brown rice may weigh slightly more due to its density.</p>
