<h2>How to Measure 3.0 Cups of Milk in ml</h2>  
<p>To measure 3.0 cups of milk in millilitres, you'll need a liquid measuring jug with ml markings. Fill the jug to the 710 ml line, and you've got the exact amount needed. If your jug only has cup measurements, remember that 1 cup equals approximately 237 ml, so 3.0 cups would be 710 ml (237 x 3).</p>  
<p>For those who prefer weighing ingredients, milk has a density close to water, so 710 ml of milk weighs roughly 710 grams. This method is especially useful for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> where precision is key. Always use fresh, well-measured milk for the best results.</p>

<h3>Differences Between UK and US Measurements</h3>  
<p>It's worth noting that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. A UK cup is 284 ml, while a US cup is 237 ml. This means 3.0 UK cups of milk would be 852 ml, whereas 3.0 US cups are 710 ml. Most modern recipes, including those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, use US measurements unless specified otherwise.</p>
