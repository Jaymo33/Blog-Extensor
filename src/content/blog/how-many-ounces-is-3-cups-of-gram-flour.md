<h2>How to Measure Gram Flour Correctly</h2>  
<p>Measuring dry ingredients like gram flour can be tricky if you don’t use the right technique. For the most accurate results, spoon the flour into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to over-measuring.</p>

<h2>Converting Other Gram Flour Quantities</h2>  
<p>Need to adjust your recipe? Here’s a quick reference for other common gram flour conversions: 1 cup ≈ 4 ounces (113g), 2 cups ≈ 8 ounces (227g), and 4 cups ≈ 16 ounces (454g). Keeping these numbers handy simplifies scaling recipes up or down.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Gram Flour</h3>  
<p>To keep gram flour fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. It has a high fat content, which can turn rancid if exposed to heat or moisture. Proper storage ensures your flour stays ready for your next air fryer adventure.</p>
