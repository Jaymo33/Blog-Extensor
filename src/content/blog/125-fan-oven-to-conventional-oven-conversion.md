<p>125 fan oven converts to approximately 150°C in a conventional oven. This simple conversion ensures your recipes turn out perfectly, whether you're baking cakes or roasting vegetables. Understanding this difference is crucial for achieving consistent results in your kitchen.</p>

<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air evenly, allowing them to cook food faster and at lower temperatures. A 125 fan oven setting delivers similar results to a 150°C conventional oven because of this efficient air circulation. This difference is why conversion charts are essential for recipe success. Many modern recipes at <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> include both temperature settings for convenience. Always check whether your recipe specifies fan or conventional oven temperatures before starting.</p>

<p>The science behind fan ovens explains why they need lower temperatures. The moving air transfers heat more effectively to food surfaces, reducing cooking time by about 20%. This efficiency means you'll save energy while getting evenly cooked dishes. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> includes similar principles for different cooking appliances.</p>

<h2>How to Convert 125 Fan to Conventional</h2>
<p>Converting 125 fan oven to conventional oven temperature follows a standard rule: add 20-25°C to the fan setting. This means 125°C fan becomes 145-150°C in a traditional oven. The exact conversion may vary slightly depending on your oven model and altitude. For baking delicate items like the cakes in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a>, precision matters most.</p>

<h3>Practical Conversion Examples</h3>
<p>When converting 125 fan oven to celsius for conventional ovens, consider what you're cooking. Dense items like roasts might need the full 25°C increase, while delicate pastries may only need 20°C. This 125 c fan oven to normal oven adjustment ensures your food cooks through without burning. Test with an oven thermometer if you're unsure about your appliance's accuracy.</p>
