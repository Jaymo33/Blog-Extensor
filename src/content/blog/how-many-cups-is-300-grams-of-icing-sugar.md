<p>300 grams of icing sugar is approximately 2.4 cups in the UK and 2.5 cups in the US. This conversion is essential for baking, especially when using an air fryer for desserts like cakes or cookies. Getting the measurement right ensures your recipes turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and precise measurements are crucial for success. Using too much or too little icing sugar can affect the texture and sweetness of your desserts. Whether you're making frosting for cupcakes or dusting a cake, knowing how many cups 300 grams of icing sugar equals ensures consistency. <p>Different countries use different cup sizes, which can lead to confusion. In the UK, a standard cup holds 250ml, while in the US, it's 240ml. This slight difference means 300 grams of icing sugar converts to 2.4 UK cups or 2.5 US cups. Always double-check your measurements for the best results.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Icing Sugar Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate measurement, use a kitchen scale to weigh 300 grams of icing sugar. If you don't have one, spoon the sugar into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing the sugar down, as this can lead to over-measuring. For more conversion help, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>our conversion chart</a>.</p>
