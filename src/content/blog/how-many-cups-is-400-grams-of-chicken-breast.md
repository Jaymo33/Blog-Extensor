<p>400 grams of chicken breast is roughly equivalent to 2 cups when chopped or diced. This measurement can vary slightly depending on how finely the chicken is cut, but it’s a reliable starting point for most air fryer recipes. Whether you’re cooking a family meal or prepping ingredients for the week, knowing this conversion ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Factors That Affect the Cup Measurement</h2>
<p>The way you prepare the chicken breast impacts how it measures in cups. Diced or shredded chicken will pack more tightly into a cup than larger chunks. For 400 grams of chicken breast, finely chopped pieces might yield slightly more than 2 cups, while bigger chunks could be closer to 1.5 cups. Always consider the recipe’s requirements for the best results.</p>

<h3>Raw vs. Cooked Chicken Measurements</h3>
<p>Keep in mind that chicken shrinks during cooking, losing about 25% of its weight. If a recipe calls for 400 grams of cooked chicken breast, you’ll need to start with roughly 500 grams raw. This is especially important for meal prep or dishes like stir-fries where cooked volume matters. <h2>How to Measure 400 Grams of Chicken Breast Without Scales</h2>
<p>No kitchen scales? No problem. A standard cup holds about 200 grams of diced chicken breast, so two cups will give you approximately 400 grams. Visual cues can also help – 400 grams is roughly the size of two medium chicken breasts. For more precision, consider investing in a digital scale, but this method works in a pinch.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Chicken Cuts</h3>
<p>While this guide focuses on chicken breast, other cuts like thighs or wings have different densities. For example, 400 grams of boneless thighs might fill slightly more than 2 cups due to higher fat content. Always adjust measurements based on the cut and recipe.
