<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer baking</h2>
<p>Baking powder is a leavening agent, and even small deviations can affect how your air fryer recipes turn out. Too much or too little can lead to dense or overly fluffy results. Measuring 0.125 cup of baking powder correctly (about 1 ounce) ensures your cakes, muffins, or batters rise perfectly. Many air fryer recipes, like those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe hub</a>, rely on precise measurements for success.</p>

<p>Air fryers cook food faster than conventional ovens, so ingredient ratios are crucial. <h3>How to measure 0.125 cup of baking powder</h3>
<p>For the most accurate measurement, use a kitchen scale to weigh out 1 ounce of baking powder. If you don't have a scale, spoon the powder into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Never pack baking powder down, as this can lead to using more than the 0.125 cup (1 oz) your recipe requires. Keeping your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer clean</a> also ensures consistent results, as residue can affect cooking times.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with baking powder in air fryers</h3>
<p>Always check the expiration date on your baking powder, as old product loses its potency. Store it in a cool, dry place to maintain effectiveness.
