<p>660 ml of oat milk is approximately 2.79 cups. This conversion is essential for accurate measurements in baking, cooking, and air fryer recipes. Whether you're whipping up a cake or preparing a creamy sauce, knowing how to convert millilitres to cups ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Precision is key when working with oat milk, especially in air fryer recipes where texture and consistency are crucial. Too much or too little liquid can affect the outcome of your dish. For example, a batter that’s too thin won’t crisp up properly in the air fryer. That’s why knowing that 660 ml of oat milk equals roughly 2.79 cups is so helpful.</p>

Oat milk is a popular dairy-free alternative, and getting the measurements right ensures your dishes turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 660 ml of Oat Milk</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug with millilitre markings, you can still measure 660 ml of oat milk using standard cups. Since 1 cup is roughly 236.6 ml, dividing 660 ml by this gives you 2.79 cups. For practical purposes, you can round this to 2 and ¾ cups.</p>  

<h3>Using a Kitchen Scale</h3>  
<p>For even greater accuracy, weigh your oat milk. Oat milk has a similar density to water, so 660 ml weighs about 660 grams. This method eliminates guesswork and ensures your air fryer recipes turn out just right. If you’re baking, this precision can make all the difference.</p>
