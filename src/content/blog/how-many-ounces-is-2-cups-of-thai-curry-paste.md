<p>2 cups of Thai curry paste equals approximately 16 ounces (or 1 pound). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, ensuring accurate measurements for the best results. Whether you're making a fragrant curry or marinating meats, knowing the weight helps balance flavours perfectly.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Precision is key when using an air fryer, as ingredients cook quickly and evenly. Too much Thai curry paste can overpower a dish, while too little may leave it bland. For consistent results, measuring by weight (ounces) is often more reliable than volume (cups). This is especially true for dense ingredients like curry paste, which can vary in density.</p>  
<p>If you're exploring new recipes, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipe collection</a> for inspiration. <h3>How to Measure Thai Curry Paste for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate measurement, use a kitchen scale to weigh your Thai curry paste in ounces. If you don’t have one, spoon the paste into a measuring cup and level it off. Remember, 2 cups of Thai curry paste will weigh roughly 16 ounces, but slight variations can occur depending on brand and consistency.</p>  

<h2>Converting Cups to Ounces for Different Ingredients</h2>  
<p>Not all ingredients weigh the same per cup. While 2 cups of Thai curry paste equal about 16 ounces, other items like flour or sugar differ. For example, 2 cups of flour weigh around 9 ounces. This is why a conversion chart, like our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion guide</a>, is handy.</p>  
<p>Thai curry paste is denser than liquids like broth or oil, so its weight per cup is higher. Understanding these differences helps when adapting recipes for your air fryer. Whether you're cooking <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken dishes</a> or experimenting with tofu, precise measurements make all the difference.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Thai Curry Paste</h3>  
<p>One mistake is packing the paste too tightly into the cup, which can lead to using more than needed. Another is assuming all brands have the same consistency—some are thicker than others. Always check the label for weight equivalents if available, and adjust as needed for your air fryer recipes.</p>
