<p>426 fan oven converts to approximately 400°F in a conventional oven. This slight reduction accounts for the more efficient heat distribution in fan-assisted ovens. Whether you're baking, roasting, or air frying, understanding this conversion ensures your recipes turn out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Understanding Fan Oven vs Conventional Oven Temperatures</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food using a built-in fan, cooking food more evenly and quickly. Conventional ovens rely on natural heat circulation, which can create hot spots. The general rule is to reduce conventional oven temperatures by 20-25°F when converting from fan oven settings. This means 426 fan oven becomes 400-405°F in a traditional oven.</p>

Many British recipes specify fan oven temperatures, while American recipes typically use conventional oven settings. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> can help you navigate these differences with ease.</p>

<h3>Why the Temperature Difference Matters</h3>
<p>The 26°F reduction prevents overcooking when moving from fan to conventional ovens. Fan ovens' efficient heat distribution means they cook food faster at the same temperature setting. Without this adjustment, your cakes might burn or your roast could dry out. This principle applies whether you're using a full-size oven or an <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer for smaller portions</a>.</p>

<h2>Practical Conversion Examples</h2>
<p>Let's look at some common cooking scenarios where this conversion proves invaluable. When baking cookies at 426 fan oven, you'd set your conventional oven to 400°F. For roasting chicken, the same conversion applies - 426 fan becomes 400 conventional. This consistency makes recipe adaptation straightforward across different kitchen appliances.</p>
<p>The conversion also works in reverse. If a conventional oven recipe calls for 400°F, you'd set your fan oven to 426°F.
