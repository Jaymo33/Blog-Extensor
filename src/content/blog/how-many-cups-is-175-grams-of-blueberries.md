<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Frying</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of blueberries ensures even cooking and perfect texture in your air-fried dishes. Too many berries can make recipes soggy, while too few might leave them dry. <h3>How to Measure 175 Grams of Blueberries Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method for measuring blueberries in cups. Gently fill a measuring cup without packing them down, then level off the top. This prevents over-measuring and keeps your recipes balanced. For 175 grams, aim for just over 1 cup—around 1.25 cups should do the trick. If you’re unsure, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> often include weight and volume measurements for clarity.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 175 Grams of Blueberries</h2>  
<p>This quantity is perfect for small-batch baking or air frying. You could make a single-serving blueberry crisp, a handful of muffins, or a fruity compote for pancakes. Air-fried blueberries also make a great topping for yoghurt or oatmeal. For more inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast recipes</a> featuring this versatile fruit.</p>  

<p>If you’re meal prepping, 175 grams of blueberries can be portioned into individual servings for snacks. They’re also great for freezing—just spread them on a tray first to prevent clumping. Once frozen, transfer them to a container for later use in smoothies or baked goods. For tips on keeping your air fryer clean after cooking with berries, check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a>.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Blueberry Sizes</h3>  
<p>Larger blueberries take up more space, so 175 grams might fill slightly fewer cups. Smaller berries pack tighter, meaning you might need a bit less. If your recipe calls for a specific cup measurement, adjust accordingly. For example, if a recipe needs 1.5 cups and you’re using 175 grams, you might be slightly under—but it’s usually fine for most dishes.</p>
