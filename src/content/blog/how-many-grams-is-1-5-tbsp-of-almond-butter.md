Whether you're whipping up a batch of almond butter cookies or adding it to a savoury dish, knowing the exact weight helps avoid guesswork. <h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct weight for ingredients like almond butter ensures your air fryer dishes turn out perfectly. Too much or too little can affect texture, cooking time, and flavour. For example, adding extra almond butter to a cake batter might make it overly dense. On the other hand, skimping could leave your dish dry. That’s why converting 1.5 tbsp of almond butter to grams (24g) is so useful for consistency.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially baked goods, rely on precise measurements. If you're experimenting with healthy snacks like almond butter energy balls, getting the ratio right is key. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a> for inspiration on how to use almond butter in your air fryer creations.</p>  

<h3>How to measure almond butter accurately</h3>  
<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like almond butter can be tricky. For best results, lightly grease your spoon or use a kitchen scale for direct grams measurement. If you don’t have scales, levelling off the tablespoon with a knife ensures you’re close to the 24g mark. Remember, packed vs. loose almond butter can vary slightly, so adjust if needed.</p>

<p>Almond butter is a versatile ingredient in both sweet and savoury air fryer dishes. From drizzling over roasted vegetables to mixing into protein bars, its rich flavour enhances many meals. <h3>Converting other almond butter measurements</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount, here’s a quick reference: 1 tbsp almond butter = 16g, while 2 tbsp = 32g. These conversions help scale recipes up or down without hassle. Whether you’re making a single-serving dessert or a family-sized batch, knowing these weights ensures success.</p>  

<h2>Tips for storing almond butter</h2>  
<p>To keep almond butter fresh, store it in a cool, dark place or refrigerate after opening. Stir well before use, as natural separation is normal.
