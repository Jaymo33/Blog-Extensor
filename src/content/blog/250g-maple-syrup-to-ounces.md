<h2>Why Convert 250g of Maple Syrup to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes, especially those for air fryers, use metric measurements, while some kitchen scales or older cookbooks prefer ounces. Converting 250g of maple syrup to ounces (8.82 oz) helps avoid errors in sticky situations—literally! Air fryer recipes, like glazed salmon or maple-roasted veggies, often need precise syrup quantities for balanced flavours. A slight miscalculation could make a dish too sweet or underwhelming. For more air fryer-friendly recipes, explore <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection here</a>.</p>  

<p>Maple syrup’s density means 250g fills roughly 8.82 fluid ounces, but weight ounces (oz) are what most scales display. This distinction matters because fluid ounces measure volume, while weight ounces measure mass. For accuracy, always use a kitchen scale set to grams or ounces. If your air fryer recipe calls for 250g and your scale only does ounces, now you know it’s 8.82 oz. Need a cleaner for sticky spills? <h3>How to Measure 250g of Maple Syrup Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>No scale? No problem. Since 250g of maple syrup equals about 8.82 ounces, you can use measuring spoons or cups in a pinch. One tablespoon of maple syrup weighs roughly 20g, so 250g would be around 12.5 tablespoons. For cups, 250g is just under 1 cup (240ml). However, these methods aren’t as precise as weighing, so a scale is ideal for air fryer recipes where consistency is key.
