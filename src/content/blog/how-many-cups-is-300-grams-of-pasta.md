<h2>Understanding Pasta Measurements</h2>
<p>Pasta can be tricky to measure because different shapes occupy varying amounts of space. For example, 300 grams of spaghetti will measure differently than 300 grams of macaroni. Generally, 300 grams of dry pasta translates to about 3 cups, but it’s always best to check specific types. This is especially important when using an air fryer, as overcrowding can affect cooking results.</p>
<p>If you’re unsure, a kitchen scale is the most accurate tool for measuring pasta. However, if you don’t have one, using standard cup measurements will get you close. <h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryers</h2>
<p>Air fryers cook food by circulating hot air, so overcrowding the basket can lead to uneven cooking. Measuring 300 grams of pasta correctly ensures your dish cooks evenly and crisps up nicely. Whether you’re making a pasta bake or crispy noodles, the right amount is key.</p>
<p>For air fryer recipes, it’s also helpful to know how much cooked pasta 300 grams yields. Dry pasta roughly doubles in weight when cooked, so 300 grams of dry pasta becomes about 600 grams cooked.
