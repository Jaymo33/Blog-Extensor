<h2>Why Accurate Honey Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Honey is denser than many liquids, meaning 400 grams won’t fill the same space as water or milk. Too much honey can make dishes overly sweet or sticky, while too little might leave them lacking flavour. Whether you're making air-fryer cookies or a honey glaze for chicken, getting the conversion right ensures balanced taste and texture. <h3>How Temperature Affects Honey Measurements</h3>  
<p>Cold honey is thicker and harder to pour, which can lead to uneven measurements. Warming it slightly (not microwaving) makes it easier to level off in a measuring cup. If you're preparing a marinade for air-fryer salmon or tofu, this trick ensures consistency. Always let honey cool before adding it to batters or sauces to avoid altering the recipe’s chemistry.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Honey</h3>  
<p>Scooping honey straight from the jar often leads to packed cups, resulting in more than 400 grams. Instead, lightly coat the measuring cup with oil or spray to help honey slide out evenly. For recipes like air-fryer granola or roasted nuts, this ensures even distribution without clumping.</p>  

<h2>Handy Tools for Measuring Honey</h2>

For sticky ingredients, silicone spatulas help scrape every last gram into your mix—ideal for air-fryer desserts like baklava or flapjacks.</p>
