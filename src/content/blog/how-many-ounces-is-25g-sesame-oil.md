<h2>How to measure 25g of sesame oil without scales</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 25g of sesame oil (0.88 ounces) using tablespoons. One tablespoon of sesame oil weighs roughly 14g, so 25g is just under two tablespoons. This method isn’t as precise as using scales but works in a pinch. For best results, invest in a digital scale for accurate measurements.</p>
<p>Sesame oil is denser than water, so volume measurements like cups or millilitres aren’t ideal for converting to ounces. Stick to weight measurements for consistency, especially when air frying.
