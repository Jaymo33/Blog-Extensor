<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of buttermilk can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too much liquid can lead to soggy textures, while too little may dry out your dish. Converting 3.5 cups of buttermilk to ml (828 ml) ensures you follow recipes precisely. This is particularly important for baked goods like scones or muffins, where buttermilk’s acidity affects rising. <h2>How to Measure Buttermilk Correctly</h2>  
<p>When converting 3.5 cups of buttermilk to ml, always use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy. Pour the buttermilk to the 828 ml mark, checking at eye level to avoid over- or under-pouring. If you don’t have a measuring jug, weigh it—buttermilk weighs roughly 245 grams per cup, so 3.5 cups would be 857.5 grams. However, ml is the preferred measurement for liquids in air fryer cooking.</p>  

<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>Note that US and UK cups differ slightly—a US cup is 240 ml, while a UK cup is 250 ml. For 3.5 cups of buttermilk, this means 840 ml (US) or 875 ml (UK). Most modern recipes use the US standard, so 828 ml (3.5 US cups) is the safest bet. Always check the recipe’s origin to avoid confusion.</p>
