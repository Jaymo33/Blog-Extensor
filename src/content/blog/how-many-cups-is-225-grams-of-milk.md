<p>225 grams of milk is roughly equivalent to 0.95 cups in the UK or 0.89 cups in the US. This conversion is essential for anyone using an air fryer to bake or cook recipes that require precise measurements. Whether you're making a cake, custard, or a creamy sauce, knowing how to convert grams to cups ensures your dish turns out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Milk Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>Getting the right amount of milk in your recipes is crucial for texture and consistency. Too much milk can make your batter too runny, while too little can result in a dry or crumbly dish. For air fryer recipes, precision is even more important because the cooking environment is more intense than a conventional oven. If you're using an air fryer for baking, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient conversions.</p>
<p>Milk is a common ingredient in many recipes, from pancakes to creamy pasta sauces. Whether you're following a British or American recipe, the cup measurements can vary slightly. In the UK, a standard cup holds 250ml, while in the US, it's 240ml. This small difference can affect your results, so it's always best to double-check which system your recipe uses.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 225 Grams of Milk Without Scales</h2>
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 225 grams of milk using a measuring cup. Since milk has a similar density to water, 225 grams is roughly 225ml. In the UK, this is just under one cup (250ml), while in the US, it's slightly less than a cup (240ml). For best results, use a liquid measuring cup and fill it to the 225ml mark.</p>
<p>Another handy trick is to use tablespoons. There are approximately 15ml in a tablespoon, so 225 grams of milk equals about 15 tablespoons. This method is especially useful for smaller quantities or if you're adjusting a recipe on the fly. For more tips on measuring ingredients for your air fryer, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for Milk</h3>
<p>In the UK, 225 grams of milk is approximately 0.95 cups, while in the US, it's about 0.89 cups. This difference might seem small, but it can add up in recipes that require larger quantities. Always check whether your recipe uses UK or US measurements to avoid any mishaps. If you're unsure, sticking to grams is the safest bet for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Dairy Products</h3>
<p>While this guide focuses on milk, it's worth noting that other dairy products like cream or buttermilk have different densities. For example, 225 grams of heavy cream will take up slightly less volume than milk due to its higher fat content. Always check specific conversion charts for these ingredients to ensure accuracy in your recipes.</p>

<h2>Tips for Measuring Milk in an Air Fryer Recipe</h2>
<p>When using milk in air fryer recipes, it's best to measure it by weight for the most accurate results. If your recipe calls for 225 grams of milk, use a digital scale to weigh it out precisely.
