<h2>Cooking at 337 F (Gas Mark 3) in Your Air Fryer</h2>
<p>At 337°F (Gas Mark 3), your air fryer becomes ideal for slow-cooked dishes that need gentle heat. This temperature works wonderfully for roasting root vegetables, allowing them to caramelise without burning. It's also perfect for reheating leftovers like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/beef>beef dishes</a> without drying them out. The circulating air ensures even cooking at this moderate temperature, unlike conventional ovens that might have hot spots.</p>

<p>For best results at 337 F to Gas Mark conversion, always preheat your air fryer for 3-5 minutes. This ensures stable temperature before adding food. When baking at this temperature, use oven-safe containers that fit properly in your air fryer basket. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> to maintain your appliance after cooking greasy dishes at these temperatures.</p>

<h3>Temperature Conversion Tips</h3>
<p>Remember that 337°F to Gas Mark conversion applies to standard ovens and air fryers without fan assistance. If using a fan oven or convection air fryer, you might need to reduce the temperature by about 20°F for equivalent results. Always adjust cooking times accordingly, as air fryers cook faster than traditional methods.
