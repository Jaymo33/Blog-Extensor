<h3>How to Measure 485 ml of Beef Stock Without a Measuring Cup</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup, you can still measure 485 ml of beef stock accurately. A standard UK metric cup holds 250 ml, so 485 ml is just under two full cups (about 1 cup + 235 ml). Alternatively, use a kitchen scale—485 ml of beef stock weighs roughly 485 grams, as the density of stock is close to water. <h2>Converting 485 ml of Beef Stock for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>Whether you’re following a British or American recipe, conversions can vary slightly. In the UK, 1 cup is 250 ml, while in the US, it’s 240 ml. For 485 ml of beef stock, this means approximately 2.05 cups (UK) or 2.02 cups (US). This small difference usually won’t affect most air fryer dishes, but for baking, precision is key. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> covers all the bases.</p>  

<p>If you’re adapting a slow-cooker or oven recipe for the air fryer, you might need to adjust liquid quantities. Air fryers circulate hot air, so reducing liquids by 10-20% is often recommended. For 485 ml of beef stock, consider using around 400-435 ml (1.6-1.8 cups) to prevent sogginess.
