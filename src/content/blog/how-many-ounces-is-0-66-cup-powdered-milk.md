<p>0.66 cup of powdered milk equals approximately 2.64 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements ensure the best texture and consistency. Whether you're making creamy sauces, baked goods, or even air-fried desserts, knowing how many ounces are in 0.66 cup of powdered milk helps avoid kitchen mishaps.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of powdered milk can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too little, and your dish might lack creaminess; too much, and it could become overly dense. Powdered milk is a common ingredient in air fryer recipes, from crispy coatings to fluffy cakes. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> often call for precise measurements to achieve the perfect balance of flavours and textures.</p>  
<p>If you're converting 0.66 cup of powdered milk to ounces, it's helpful to know that 1 cup of powdered milk typically weighs around 4 ounces. This means 0.66 cup is roughly 2.64 ounces. Keeping a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> handy can save time and ensure consistency in your cooking.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Powdered Milk Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a kitchen scale to weigh your powdered milk. Scooping directly from the bag can lead to packed cups, which may throw off your measurements. Lightly spoon the powdered milk into your measuring cup and level it off for consistency. This method ensures you get the right amount—whether you need 0.66 cup or another measurement.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Powdered Milk</h3>  
<p>To keep your powdered milk fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Exposure to moisture can cause clumping, which makes measuring 0.66 cup or any other amount tricky. Proper storage ensures your powdered milk stays ready for all your air fryer adventures.</p>
