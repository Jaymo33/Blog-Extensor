<h2>Why Convert Cups to ml for Honey?</h2>  
<p>Converting 1.5 cups of honey to ml is essential for accuracy, especially in air fryer cooking. Cups can vary slightly depending on how the honey is scooped, but millilitres provide a consistent measurement. This is particularly useful for recipes where precision affects texture, like cakes or glazes. If you’re baking honey-glazed salmon or muffins, knowing the exact ml ensures perfect results. <h3>How to Measure 1.5 Cups of Honey in ml</h3>  
<p>To measure 1.5 cups of honey in ml, use a liquid measuring jug with clear ml markings. Lightly coat the cup with oil first—this helps the honey slide out cleanly. Pour slowly to avoid air bubbles, which can skew the measurement. For sticky ingredients like honey, a silicone spatula ensures you get every drop. <p>While 1.5 UK cups of honey equal 355 ml, US cups are slightly different (340 ml). Always check your recipe’s origin to avoid errors. Most modern recipes use metric measurements for consistency. If in doubt, stick to ml for foolproof results.</p>
