<p>500g of BBQ sauce is approximately 17.64 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, where precise measurements can make or break your dish. Whether you're marinating chicken, glazing ribs, or whipping up a quick sauce for veggies, knowing this conversion ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise cooking times and ingredient ratios to deliver crispy, evenly cooked meals. Using too much or too little BBQ sauce can affect texture and flavour. For example, 500g of BBQ sauce (17.64 ounces) is ideal for coating a family-sized portion of chicken wings or ribs in the air fryer. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, like those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe hub</a>, specify weights in grams or ounces. Converting 500g of BBQ sauce to ounces (17.64 oz) ensures you follow these recipes accurately. This is especially useful when preparing dishes like sticky BBQ chicken or glazed pork chops, where sauce consistency is key.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 500g of BBQ Sauce Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have kitchen scales, you can estimate 500g of BBQ sauce (17.64 ounces) using measuring cups. One cup of BBQ sauce weighs roughly 250g, so two cups will give you close to 500g. For more precision, use fluid ounces: 17.64 oz is just over 2 cups. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipe category</a> features plenty of dishes where this measurement comes in handy.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 500g of BBQ Sauce in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>500g of BBQ sauce (17.64 ounces) is perfect for batch cooking. You can marinate 1kg of chicken wings, glaze a rack of ribs, or even create a BBQ pulled pork filling for sliders. The air fryer locks in the sauce’s flavour while crisping the exterior, giving you restaurant-quality results at home.</p>  

<p>For vegetarian options, 500g of BBQ sauce works wonders with tofu or mushrooms. Try our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/tofu>tofu recipes</a> for a smoky, saucy twist. Remember to clean your air fryer after cooking sticky sauces—our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> makes it a breeze.</p>  

<h3>Storing Leftover BBQ Sauce</h3>  
<p>If you don’t use all 500g (17.64 ounces) of BBQ sauce, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to two weeks. You can also freeze it in portions for future air fryer meals. Just thaw and reheat gently before using.</p>
