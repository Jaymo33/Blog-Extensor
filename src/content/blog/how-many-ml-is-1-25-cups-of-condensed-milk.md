<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like condensed milk can be tricky. To get an accurate 295.74 ml (1.25 cups), use a liquid measuring jug with clear ml markings. Lightly grease the cup or spoon with oil to help the condensed milk slide out easily, ensuring you don’t leave any behind.</p>
<p>If you’re scaling a recipe up or down, remember that 1 cup of condensed milk equals 236.59 ml, so 1.25 cups is simply 1 cup plus a quarter cup. This method works for both UK and US measurements, though slight regional variations in cup sizes can occur.</p>
