This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, where precise measurements can make or break your dish. Whether you're whipping up fluffy pancakes or tender fried chicken, knowing how many cups is 185 ml of buttermilk ensures consistent results every time.</p>

Too little, and your air fryer cakes might turn out dense; too much, and batters can become soggy. The 185 ml measurement often appears in European recipes, while American versions use cups. Knowing that 185 ml of buttermilk equals roughly 0.78 cups bridges this gap effortlessly.</p>

<h3>Measuring 185 ml of Buttermilk Without a Scale</h3>
<p>No measuring jug? No problem. To measure 185 ml of buttermilk in cups, use a standard 240 ml (1 cup) measure and fill it just below the ¾ cup line. Alternatively, 12 tablespoons plus 2 teaspoons will get you close. For sticky batters like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, slight variations won’t hurt—but precision matters for delicate bakes.</p>
