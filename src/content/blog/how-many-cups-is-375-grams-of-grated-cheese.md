<h2>Why Accurate Cheese Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of grated cheese is key to achieving the perfect texture and flavour in your air fryer dishes. Too much cheese can make your meal overly greasy, while too little might leave it dry. Whether you’re making crispy air fryer nachos or a melty cheese toastie, 375 grams of grated cheese (about 3.75 cups) ensures consistency. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>Different types of cheese can also affect the volume. Hard cheeses like cheddar or parmesan will yield slightly different cup measurements compared to softer varieties like mozzarella. For best results, always measure by weight when possible, especially for baking or cheesy casseroles in your air fryer.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Grated Cheese Properly</h3>  
<p>To measure 375 grams of grated cheese accurately, use a kitchen scale for precision. If you don’t have one, lightly spoon the grated cheese into a measuring cup without packing it down. Overpacking can lead to using more cheese than needed, throwing off your recipe. For air fryer-friendly cheesy dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, this method ensures even distribution.</p>  

<h2>Converting Grams to Cups for Different Cheeses</h2>  
<p>While 375 grams of grated cheese generally equals 3.75 cups, the type of cheese matters. Finely grated hard cheeses like parmesan will yield more cups per gram, while coarsely grated softer cheeses like mozzarella take up more space. If you’re adapting a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, always check whether the cheese is specified by weight or volume.</p>  

<p>For example, 375 grams of finely grated cheddar might fill closer to 4 cups, while the same weight of chunkier mozzarella could be just 3.5 cups. When in doubt, weigh your cheese for the most accurate results, especially for air fryer dishes where even cooking is crucial.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing and Using Grated Cheese</h2>  
<p>If you’ve pre-measured 375 grams of grated cheese but aren’t using it all, store it properly to maintain freshness. Keep it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days, or freeze for longer storage. When ready to use, let it thaw slightly before adding to your air fryer dishes for even melting.</p>
