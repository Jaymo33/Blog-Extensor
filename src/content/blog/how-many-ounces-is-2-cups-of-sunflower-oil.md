<h2>How to measure sunflower oil for air frying</h2>
<p>Measuring 2 cups of sunflower oil to ounces is straightforward with the right tools. A liquid measuring cup is ideal for cups, while a kitchen scale is best for ounces. If your recipe calls for 16 ounces of sunflower oil, you can confidently use 2 cups. For smaller batches, halve the measurement to 1 cup (8 ounces) or quarter it to ½ cup (4 ounces). This flexibility makes it easy to adapt recipes to your needs.</p>

<h3>Using a kitchen scale</h3>
<p>A kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure sunflower oil for air frying. Simply place your container on the scale, tare it to zero, and pour the oil until you reach 16 ounces. This method eliminates the guesswork and ensures consistency. <h2>Common mistakes when measuring oil</h2>
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is using the wrong type of measuring cup. Liquid measuring cups have a spout for pouring, while dry measuring cups are designed for solids. Using a dry cup for oil can lead to inaccurate measurements. Another mistake is eyeballing the amount, which often results in too much or too little oil. For perfect air fryer results, always measure carefully.</p>

<p>Storing sunflower oil properly also affects its quality and measurement accuracy. Keep it in a cool, dark place to prevent rancidity. If your oil smells off, it’s best to replace it.
