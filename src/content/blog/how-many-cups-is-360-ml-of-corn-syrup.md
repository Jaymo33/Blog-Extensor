<h2>Understanding the Conversion from ml to Cups</h2>
<p>Converting millilitres to cups can be tricky, especially with thick liquids like corn syrup. One standard metric cup holds 240 ml, so 360 ml of corn syrup equals 1.5 cups. This measurement is crucial for air fryer baking, where precision affects texture and consistency. For more conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>. It’s a handy tool for all your cooking needs.</p>

<p>Corn syrup’s viscosity means it doesn’t pour as easily as water, so measuring correctly is key. Using a liquid measuring cup with clear markings ensures accuracy. If you’re working with smaller quantities, a kitchen scale can also help. Remember, 360 ml of corn syrup in cups is the same whether you’re in the UK or the US. This consistency makes it easier to follow international recipes.</p>

<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but only if the ingredients are measured correctly. Too much or too little corn syrup can alter the texture of your dish. For example, a glaze with too much syrup might become overly sticky, while too little could lack flavour. <h3>Tips for Measuring Corn Syrup</h3>
<p>To measure 360 ml of corn syrup accurately, lightly grease your measuring cup first. This helps the syrup slide out easily, ensuring you get the full amount. Alternatively, use a spatula to scrape out every last drop. For sticky ingredients like corn syrup, precision is everything. If you’re doubling a recipe, remember that 720 ml equals 3 cups.</p>
