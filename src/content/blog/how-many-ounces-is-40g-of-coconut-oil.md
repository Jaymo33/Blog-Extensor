<p>40g of coconut oil equals approximately 1.41 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements can make or break your dish. Whether you're making crispy roast potatoes or baking a cake, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Why accurate coconut oil measurements matter</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of coconut oil in your air fryer recipes affects texture, browning and moisture levels. Too little oil and your food might dry out or stick to the basket. Too much can make dishes greasy or cause smoking in your appliance. That's why conversions like 40g coconut oil to ounces are so valuable for home cooks.</p>

But if you only have measuring spoons or cups, knowing that 40g equals 1.41 ounces helps bridge the gap. This conversion works for both refined and unrefined coconut oil varieties.</p>

<h2>How to measure 40g of coconut oil without scales</h2>
<p>If you don't have kitchen scales handy, there are simple ways to estimate 40g of coconut oil (1.41 ounces). In its solid state, coconut oil fills about 2.5 UK tablespoons. When melted, it's roughly 2.5 tablespoons or 37ml by volume. These approximations work well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>.</p>

<h3>Coconut oil in popular air fryer recipes</h3>
<p>Many favourite air fryer dishes use around 40g of coconut oil. This includes crispy chicken wings (about 6-8 pieces), roasted vegetables for 2-3 people, or a small batch of cookies. The oil helps create that perfect golden crust while keeping food moist inside.</p>

<h2>Storing and handling coconut oil for air frying</h2>
<p>Proper storage maintains coconut oil's quality between uses. Keep it in an airtight container away from heat and sunlight. In cooler UK kitchens, it will solidify - simply scoop out 40g (1.41oz) as needed. For liquid measurements, gently warm the required amount.</p>
<p>After cooking, remember to <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>clean your air fryer</a> properly to prevent oil buildup. Coconut oil can solidify again as it cools, making regular maintenance important. <h3>Converting other coconut oil amounts</h3>
<p>The same conversion ratio applies whether you're working with 40g or other amounts. Simply multiply grams by 0.035 to get ounces. So 20g would be 0.7oz, 100g would be 3.5oz, and so on. Bookmark this page for quick reference during your next cooking session.</p>
