<p>50 grams of broccoli is roughly equivalent to 0.5 cups when chopped. This measurement can vary slightly depending on how finely the broccoli is cut, but it’s a reliable starting point for air fryer recipes. Whether you’re making roasted broccoli or adding it to a stir-fry, knowing this conversion ensures consistent results every time.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Precision is key when using an air fryer, as overcrowding or uneven portions can affect cooking times and texture. If you’re working with 50 grams of broccoli, understanding its cup equivalent helps you portion correctly. This is especially useful for recipes like air-fried broccoli bites or mixed vegetable trays, where even cooking is essential.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 50 Grams of Broccoli Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can estimate 50 grams of broccoli using a measuring cup. Lightly packed chopped broccoli fills about half a standard US cup (120ml). For UK cooks, this is roughly 0.42 imperial cups. Remember, florets take up more space than finely chopped pieces, so adjust accordingly.</p>  

<h2>Broccoli Conversions for Popular Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes, like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable-based dishes</a>, call for broccoli in grams or cups. If a recipe lists 50 grams of broccoli, you now know it’s about 0.5 cups. This is handy for dishes like crispy broccoli bites or cheesy broccoli melts, where portion size affects cooking time.</p>  

<p>For larger batches, simply scale up—100 grams of broccoli equals roughly 1 cup. This makes it easy to adapt recipes for family meals or meal prep. <h3>Does Fresh vs. Frozen Broccoli Affect the Measurement?</h3>  
<p>Fresh and frozen broccoli have similar cup measurements for 50 grams, but frozen broccoli may release more moisture during cooking. If using frozen, pat it dry before air frying to ensure crispiness. For best results, stick to fresh broccoli when precision is crucial, like in <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep recipes</a>.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Cooking 50 Grams of Broccoli in Your Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>When air frying 50 grams of broccoli (or 0.5 cups), toss it lightly in oil and seasonings for even coating. Cook at 180°C for 8–10 minutes, shaking the basket halfway. This ensures crispy, golden florets without overcooking.
