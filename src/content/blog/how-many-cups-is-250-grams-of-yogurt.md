Thicker yogurts may require slight adjustments.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>While 250 grams of yogurt generally equals 1 cup in both regions, slight differences exist due to cup sizing. A UK cup holds 250ml, whereas a US cup is 240ml. For most air fryer recipes, this minor discrepancy won’t affect outcomes, but for precision baking, weigh ingredients. If your recipe originates from the US, stick to their measurements unless specified otherwise.</p>  

<h2>Converting Yogurt for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer dishes, from marinades to dips, use yogurt as a base. When a recipe calls for 250 grams of yogurt, you now know it’s about 1 cup. For <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, yogurt helps tenderise meat while adding flavour. <p>For thicker yogurts like Greek or Icelandic, 250 grams may fill slightly less than a cup due to density. Stirring before measuring ensures consistency. If substituting, adjust liquids in your recipe accordingly. Always level off your cup measurement with a knife for accuracy. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Yogurt</h3>  
<p>Avoid scooping yogurt directly from the tub, as this compacts it and skews measurements. Instead, spoon it lightly into the cup. For 250 grams of yogurt, fluff it first, then measure. If using grams, tare your scale with the container to exclude its weight. These small steps make a big difference in air fryer cooking, where precision impacts texture and cook time.</p>
