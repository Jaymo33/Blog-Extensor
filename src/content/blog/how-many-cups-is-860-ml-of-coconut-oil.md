<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Frying</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve that perfect crispiness without overcooking. Coconut oil, a popular choice for its high smoke point and rich flavour, needs to be measured accurately. Too much oil can make your food greasy, while too little can leave it dry. Converting 860 ml of coconut oil to cups (3.63 cups) ensures you get it just right. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those for baked goods or fried dishes, call for coconut oil. Whether you’re making coconut chicken tenders or a vegan curry, knowing how many cups 860 ml of coconut oil equals saves time and hassle. It’s also useful for scaling recipes up or down, so you can cook for one or a crowd with confidence.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 860 ml of Coconut Oil in Cups</h2>
<p>Measuring liquids like coconut oil can be tricky, but it’s straightforward once you know the conversion. One cup equals 236.59 ml, so 860 ml divided by 236.59 gives you roughly 3.63 cups. For practical purposes, you can round this to 3 and 2/3 cups. If you don’t have a measuring cup, use a kitchen scale: 860 ml of coconut oil weighs about 790 grams, which translates to 3.63 cups.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Coconut Oil in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>
<p>When using coconut oil in your air fryer, always melt it first for accurate measuring. Solid coconut oil can be packed unevenly, leading to inconsistencies. For recipes like coconut cookies or crispy tofu, melted oil ensures even coating and cooking. <h2>Common Uses for 860 ml of Coconut Oil in Air Frying</h2>
<p>860 ml of coconut oil (3.63 cups) is enough for multiple batches of air-fried favourites. Try it in coconut-crusted shrimp, where the oil helps the coating crisp up beautifully. Or use it for roasting vegetables, giving them a subtle tropical flavour. Coconut oil also works wonders in baking—think moist coconut cupcakes or golden-brown banana bread.</p>

<p>For savoury dishes, 860 ml of coconut oil can fry up a batch of crispy chicken wings or tofu nuggets. Its high smoke point makes it ideal for air frying, as it won’t burn easily. <h3>Storing Leftover Coconut Oil</h3>
<p>If you have leftover coconut oil after measuring out 860 ml (3.63 cups), store it in an airtight container in a cool, dark place. Coconut oil solidifies below 24°C, so you may need to re-melt it before your next use. Avoid contamination by using clean utensils, and it’ll last for months. For tips on keeping your air fryer clean, check out our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer maintenance</a>.</p>
