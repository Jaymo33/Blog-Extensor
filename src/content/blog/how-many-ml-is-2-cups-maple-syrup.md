<h2>Why Accurate Maple Syrup Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of maple syrup ensures your dishes aren’t too sweet or too bland. In air fryer cooking, balance is key—especially when glazing meats or baking desserts. Too much syrup can make food overly sticky, while too little might leave it lacking flavour. For consistent results, 2.0 cups of maple syrup (473 ml) is a standard measurement in many recipes. <p>Maple syrup’s thickness also affects how it distributes in recipes. Unlike water, it’s viscous, so measuring by volume (cups or ml) rather than weight ensures accuracy. This is particularly important in air fryer baking, where even distribution prevents uneven cooking. Whether you’re making sticky ribs or maple-glazed carrots, 473 ml of syrup will give you the perfect consistency.</p>
