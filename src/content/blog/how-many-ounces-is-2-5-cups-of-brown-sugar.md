<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of brown sugar can make or break your air fryer recipe. Too much sugar can cause burning, while too little may leave your dish lacking flavour. Air fryers cook food quickly, so precision is key to avoid mishaps. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer dessert recipes</a> rely on exact measurements for perfect texture. Always measure brown sugar correctly to achieve the best results.</p>  

<p>Brown sugar is denser than granulated sugar, so volume-to-weight conversions are crucial. Packed brown sugar weighs more than loosely filled cups, affecting your recipe’s outcome. A kitchen scale is the most accurate tool, but cup measurements work if done properly. <h3>How to measure brown sugar correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 2.5 cups of brown sugar accurately, pack it firmly into the measuring cup. Use the back of a spoon to press it down, eliminating air gaps. Level off the top with a straight edge for consistency. This method ensures you get the correct weight—around 20 ounces for 2.5 cups. For sticky ingredients like brown sugar, a scale is even more reliable.</p>
