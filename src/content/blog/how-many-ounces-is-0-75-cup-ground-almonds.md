<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect ingredient quantities can lead to uneven cooking. Too many ground almonds in a coating, for example, might create a dry or crumbly texture. Conversely, too little could leave your dish undercoated. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> helps eliminate guesswork, especially for almond-based recipes like frangipane tarts or gluten-free breading.</p>  

<p>Ground almonds are a staple in many air fryer recipes due to their versatility. They work beautifully in desserts like macarons or as a low-carb alternative to breadcrumbs. When converting 0.75 cup to ounces, remember that almond density can vary slightly depending on grind size and packing method. For absolute precision in recipes from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>, weighing ingredients is always best.</p>  

<h3>How to measure ground almonds without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don't have digital scales, use the spoon-and-level method for cups. Gently spoon ground almonds into your measuring cup without packing them down, then level off the top with a straight edge. This prevents over-measuring, which is common when scooping directly from the bag. For 0.75 cup, you'd fill a cup three-quarters full using this technique.</p>

<h3>Storing ground almonds for freshness</h3>  
<p>Because ground almonds contain natural oils, they can turn rancid if stored improperly. Keep your 0.75 cup portions fresh by storing them in airtight containers in a cool, dark place. For longer storage, freeze measured amounts in labelled bags. This maintains quality for when you next need precisely 3 ounces (0.75 cup) for an air fryer recipe.</p>
