<p>810 ml of heavy cream is approximately 3.42 cups. <h2>How to Measure 810 ml of Heavy Cream</h2>  
<p>Measuring liquids like heavy cream can be tricky if you don’t have a metric measuring jug. To measure 810 ml of heavy cream in cups, use a standard US cup measurement, which holds about 240 ml. This means 810 ml is roughly 3.42 cups. For UK bakers, note that a UK cup is slightly larger at 284 ml, so adjust accordingly if your recipe specifies UK measurements.</p>  

<h3>Using a Kitchen Scale</h3>  
<p>For even greater accuracy, consider using a kitchen scale. Heavy cream weighs approximately 240 grams per cup, so 810 ml would weigh around 820 grams. This method eliminates any guesswork and ensures your measurements are spot-on, whether you're preparing a creamy sauce or a fluffy dessert in your air fryer.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Smaller Batches</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for less than 810 ml of heavy cream, simply divide the measurement accordingly. For example, half of 810 ml is 405 ml, which equals about 1.71 cups. This flexibility allows you to scale recipes up or down depending on your needs, making it easier to experiment with new dishes in your air fryer.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing Heavy Cream</h2>  
<p>Once opened, heavy cream should be stored in the refrigerator and used within a week.
