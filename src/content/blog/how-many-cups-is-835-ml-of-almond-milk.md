<h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h2>
<p>It's worth noting that UK and US cup sizes differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. This means 835 ml of almond milk translates to roughly 3.5 UK cups or 3.52 US cups. For most recipes, this small difference won't be noticeable, but for precision baking, it's good to know.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 835 ml of Almond Milk Without a Scale</h3>
<p>If you don't have a measuring jug or scale, you can still measure 835 ml of almond milk using standard cups. Fill a UK cup (284 ml) three times, then add roughly half a cup to reach 835 ml. For US cups, use three full cups (720 ml) plus another 115 ml, which is just under half a cup. This method works well for most recipes, though a jug with ml markings is ideal for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Storing Leftover Almond Milk</h3>
<p>If your recipe doesn't use the full 835 ml of almond milk, store the remainder in an airtight container in the fridge. Homemade almond milk lasts 3-4 days, while shop-bought varieties can keep for 7-10 days after opening. Always give it a shake before using, as separation is normal. Never freeze almond milk as it changes texture when thawed.</p>
