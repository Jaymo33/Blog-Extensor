<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so using the right amount of minced beef ensures your dishes cook uniformly. Too much or too little can lead to uneven browning or dryness. For example, if you’re making air-fryer meatballs, 283 grams of minced beef will yield about 12 medium-sized portions. This precision is especially helpful for meal preppers or those following specific recipes. <p>Measuring by weight (grams) is more reliable than volume (cups) because minced beef can vary in density. Packed tightly or loosely, the same cup measurement can weigh differently. <h3>How to Measure Minced Beef Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method for cups. Gently spoon minced beef into a measuring cup without packing it down, then level off the top. Remember, 1.25 cups should roughly equal 283 grams, but slight variations are normal. For best results, invest in an affordable kitchen scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking.
