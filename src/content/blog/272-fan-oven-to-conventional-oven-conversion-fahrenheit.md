<p>272°F in a fan oven converts to approximately 250°F in a conventional oven. This adjustment accounts for the efficient heat distribution of fan-assisted ovens. If you're using an air fryer, which operates similarly to a fan oven, you might not need to adjust the temperature at all. For more precise conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>

<h2>Understanding Fan Oven vs Conventional Oven</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. This means you often need to reduce the temperature by about 20°F when converting from a fan oven to a conventional oven. For example, 272°F in a fan oven becomes 250°F in a conventional oven. This principle applies whether you're baking cakes or roasting vegetables.</p>
<p>If you're adapting recipes for an air fryer, which works like a mini fan oven, you might not need to adjust the temperature. However, cooking times can vary, so it's always best to keep an eye on your food. For more recipe ideas, visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>.</p>

<h2>Why Temperature Conversion Matters</h2>
<p>Using the wrong temperature can lead to overcooked or undercooked food. For instance, baking at 272°F in a fan oven without converting could result in a dry cake if you're using a conventional oven. Conversely, air fryers, which mimic fan ovens, might require slight adjustments based on the model.</p>
<p>This is especially important for delicate dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes</a> or <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cookies>cookies</a>, where precise temperatures are key. Always refer to your appliance's manual for specific guidelines, and consider using an oven thermometer for accuracy.</p>

<h3>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h3>
<p>When converting 272 fan oven to conventional oven, you might also need to adjust the cooking time. Conventional ovens generally take longer to cook food than fan ovens, so add a few extra minutes and check for doneness. Air fryers, on the other hand, often cook faster, so reduce the time by 10-20%.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h2>
<p>One common mistake is forgetting to adjust the temperature when switching between oven types. Another is assuming all air fryers behave the same way—some models might require slight tweaks. Always test new recipes with a shorter cooking time and adjust as needed.</p>
