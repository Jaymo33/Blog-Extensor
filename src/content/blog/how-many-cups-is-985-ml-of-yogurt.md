<h2>Understanding Yogurt Measurements</h2>
<p>Yogurt is a versatile ingredient used in everything from baking to marinades, and knowing how to measure it accurately ensures your recipes turn out perfectly. While some recipes list yogurt in millilitres (ml), others use cups, which can vary between the UK and US. The good news is that converting 985 ml of yogurt to cups is straightforward once you know the standard conversions.</p>
<p>In the US, 1 cup equals 240 ml, meaning 985 ml of yogurt works out to roughly 4.16 cups. In the UK, where 1 cup is 284 ml, 985 ml converts to about 3.46 cups. This difference matters when following international recipes, especially for air fryer dishes where precision affects texture and cooking times. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement swaps.</p>

<h3>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient ratios can make or break a dish. Too much or too little yogurt in a batter, for example, can lead to uneven cooking or dryness. Whether you're making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a> or marinating chicken, measuring yogurt correctly ensures consistency.</p>

<h2>Converting 985 ml of Yogurt for Different Recipes</h2>
<p>Depending on what you're making, you might need to adjust your measurements slightly. For thicker yogurts like Greek yogurt, the volume may differ slightly due to density, so it's always best to measure by weight if precision is crucial. However, for most standard yogurt types, the 985 ml to cups conversion holds true.</p>
<p>If you're scaling a recipe up or down, remember that 985 ml is just under a litre (1000 ml), making it easy to halve or double as needed. For more recipe ideas using yogurt, browse our collection of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, where yogurt often adds moisture and flavour.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Yogurt Without a Scale</h3>
<p>If you don’t have measuring cups or a scale, you can use a standard tablespoon (15 ml) to estimate. 985 ml would be roughly 65 tablespoons, though this method is less precise. For best results, invest in a set of measuring cups or a kitchen scale.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for 985 ml of Yogurt in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>
<p>Yogurt isn’t just for breakfast—it’s a secret weapon in air fryer cooking. From tenderising meats to creating light, fluffy batters, 985 ml (about 4 US cups) is enough for multiple dishes. Try it in marinades for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken</a> or as a base for dips served with crispy veggies.</p>
<p>Yogurt also works wonders in baking—think moist cakes or even savoury breads. Since air fryers cook faster than conventional ovens, the right moisture balance is key. Too much yogurt can make batters runny, while too little can dry them out. Stick to the 985 ml to cups conversion for reliable results every time.</p>
