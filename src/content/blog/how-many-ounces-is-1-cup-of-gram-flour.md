<p>1 cup of gram flour equals approximately 4.2 ounces. This measurement is essential for precise cooking, especially when using an air fryer, where accurate ingredient quantities can make or break a recipe. Gram flour, also known as besan, is a staple in many cuisines and air fryer recipes, so knowing its weight in ounces is incredibly useful.</p>

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking and precise ingredient ratios to deliver perfect results every time. Using the correct amount of gram flour ensures your batters, coatings, and doughs have the right consistency. Too much or too little can affect texture and cooking times, leading to uneven results. <h3>How to measure gram flour correctly</h3>
<p>To get the most accurate measurement, spoon the gram flour into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to over-measuring. For best results, consider using a kitchen scale to weigh your gram flour in ounces for absolute precision.</p>

<h2>Common uses for gram flour in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Gram flour is incredibly versatile in the air fryer. It's often used as a coating for fried foods, a binder in veggie burgers, or even as a base for gluten-free baking. Its nutty flavour and high protein content make it a favourite among health-conscious cooks. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a> for creative ways to use gram flour.</p>
<p>When converting between cups and ounces, remember that humidity and how settled the flour is can affect the weight slightly. For consistent results in your air fryer cooking, it's always best to weigh your ingredients when possible. <p>Gram flour can absorb more liquid than wheat flour, so you may need to adjust recipes accordingly.
