<p>50g of honey equals approximately 1.76 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements can make all the difference. Whether you’re glazing chicken or sweetening a dessert, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 50g of Honey to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes, especially those for air fryers, use metric measurements like grams. However, some kitchen scales or older recipes might list honey in ounces. Converting 50g of honey to ounces (1.76 oz) helps you follow recipes accurately. This is particularly useful for sticky ingredients like honey, where slight variations can affect texture and taste.</p>  
<p>For example, if you’re making honey-glazed salmon in your air fryer, using the right amount ensures the perfect balance of sweetness. <h2>How to Measure 50g of Honey Accurately</h2>  
<p>Measuring honey can be tricky due to its sticky consistency. To measure 50g of honey (1.76 oz), use a digital kitchen scale for the most accurate results. If you don’t have a scale, two tablespoons of honey roughly equal 50g, but this can vary slightly depending on the honey’s density.</p>  
<p>For air fryer recipes, like honey-glazed carrots or sticky wings, accuracy ensures even cooking and flavour distribution. Visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient conversions. Remember, honey’s thickness can affect volume measurements, so weighing is always best.</p>

<h3>Common Uses for 50g of Honey in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>50g of honey is a versatile amount for many air fryer dishes. It’s perfect for glazing proteins like chicken or salmon, sweetening roasted vegetables, or even drizzling over air-fried desserts. For a quick snack, try brushing 50g of honey over air-fried apple slices for a caramelised treat.</p>

<h3>Tips for Working with Honey in the Air Fryer</h3>  
<p>Honey can burn easily in the air fryer due to its high sugar content. To prevent this, mix it with a little oil or water before brushing it onto food. Also, apply honey towards the end of cooking for a glossy finish without overcaramelisation.</p>
