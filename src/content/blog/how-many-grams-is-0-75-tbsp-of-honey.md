<h3>How to measure 0.75 tbsp of honey accurately</h3>  
<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like honey can be tricky. For 0.75 tbsp, use a standard tablespoon and fill it three-quarters full. Alternatively, use digital kitchen scales for greater accuracy – simply tare the scale with your container, then pour honey until it reaches 15.75 grams. This method eliminates guesswork and ensures perfect proportions every time.</p>

<h2>Tips for working with honey in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Honey can be challenging to work with due to its sticky nature. To make measuring 0.75 tbsp (15.75g) easier, lightly coat your spoon with oil first – this helps the honey slide off cleanly. When adding honey to marinades or glazes, warm it slightly to make it more fluid and easier to mix with other ingredients.</p>  

<p>Remember that honey browns quickly in the air fryer's concentrated heat. For best results, apply honey glazes during the last few minutes of cooking or reduce the temperature slightly to prevent burning. This is especially important when using precise amounts like 0.75 tbsp, where every gram counts towards the final flavour profile.</p>  

<h3>Storing honey for accurate future measurements</h3>  
<p>To ensure your 0.75 tbsp (15.75g) measurements remain consistent, store honey properly in a cool, dry place. Crystallised honey can throw off measurements, so gently warm the jar in warm water if needed before measuring. Always use the same type of honey for consistent results – different varieties (like manuka or wildflower) have slightly different densities.</p>
