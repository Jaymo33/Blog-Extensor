Whether you’re meal prepping or following a specific recipe, knowing the exact weight helps avoid under or over-seasoning. Chickpeas are a staple in many air fryer dishes, from crispy snacks to hearty mains, so accuracy matters. <h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on hot air circulation to cook food evenly, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Too many chickpeas crammed into the basket won’t crisp up properly, while too few might burn. Measuring by weight (ounces) is more reliable than volume (cups) because chickpeas can settle differently in a cup. This is especially important for recipes like air-fried falafel or roasted chickpeas, where texture is key. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe hub</a> for inspiration on using chickpeas in your air fryer.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.66 cup of chickpeas</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a 0.66 cup measure, you can use a standard 1/3 cup twice to get close. Alternatively, weigh out 5.28 ounces on a kitchen scale for precision. For UK cooks, note that 1 US cup is roughly 240ml, but chickpeas by weight remain consistent. Pre-soaked or canned chickpeas may vary slightly in density, so adjust as needed. <h2>Converting chickpeas for different air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Recipes like spicy roasted chickpeas or air-fried hummus require exact measurements for balanced flavours. If a recipe calls for 0.66 cup of chickpeas (5.28 oz), you can scale up or down using this ratio. For batch cooking, multiply the ounces accordingly—e.g., 1.32 cups would be 10.56 oz. <h3>Common mistakes when measuring chickpeas</h3>  
<p>Packing chickpeas tightly into a cup can add extra ounces, leading to drier results. For crispy outcomes, lightly fill the measuring cup and level it off. Canned chickpeas should be drained and patted dry before measuring to avoid excess moisture. Remember, 0.66 cup of chickpeas is roughly half a standard 15oz can.
