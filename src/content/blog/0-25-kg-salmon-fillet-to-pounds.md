<h2>Common Mistakes When Converting and Cooking Salmon</h2>
<p>One common mistake is forgetting to convert the 0.25 kg salmon fillet to pounds correctly, leading to inaccurate cooking times. Another error is overcrowding the air fryer basket, which prevents even air circulation. This can result in unevenly cooked salmon, with some parts overcooked and others undercooked. Using old or inaccurate scales can also throw off your measurements. Regularly calibrating your scales ensures they provide reliable readings for all your cooking needs.</p>
<p>Not accounting for the thickness of the 0.25 kg salmon fillet is another frequent oversight. Thicker fillets may require slightly longer cooking times, even if the weight is the same. Always adjust cooking times based on the fillet's dimensions rather than weight alone. Keeping a cooking journal can help you note what works best for future reference. <h3>How to Avoid Overcooking</h3>
<p>To avoid overcooking your 0.25 kg salmon fillet, start checking for doneness a minute or two before the recommended time. The salmon should flake easily with a fork but still be moist in the centre. Remember that residual heat will continue to cook the salmon after it's removed from the air fryer. Using a timer and setting it for the minimum suggested time can prevent dryness. With practice, you'll learn to judge the perfect cooking time for your specific air fryer model.</p>
