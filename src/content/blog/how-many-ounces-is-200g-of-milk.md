<p>200g of milk equals approximately 7.05 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements can make all the difference. Whether you're whipping up a creamy sauce or baking a cake, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise cooking times and temperatures, and ingredient measurements play a crucial role. Using the correct amount of milk, like 200g (or 7.05 ounces), ensures your recipes turn out perfectly. Too much or too little liquid can affect texture and cooking time, especially in baked goods or batters.</p>  

<p>For example, if you're making air fryer pancakes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>, an extra ounce of milk could make the batter too runny. <h3>How to measure 200g of milk without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 200g of milk using volume. Roughly, 200g of milk is about 200ml, as milk’s density is close to water. For ounces, this translates to just over 7 fluid ounces. A liquid measuring cup with both metric and imperial markings is handy for this.</p>
