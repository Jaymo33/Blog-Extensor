<p>0.33 cup of chilli sauce equals approximately 2.64 ounces. This conversion is handy when preparing air fryer recipes that require precise measurements. Whether you’re making spicy wings or a saucy stir-fry, knowing the exact weight ensures perfect results every time. <h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so precise ingredient measurements are crucial. Too much or too little sauce can affect texture and cooking time. For example, 0.33 cup of chilli sauce (2.64 oz) adds just the right kick without overpowering the dish. If you’re experimenting with new recipes, visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<p>Measuring by weight (ounces) is often more accurate than volume (cups), especially for thick sauces like chilli sauce. A kitchen scale can help ensure consistency, whether you’re cooking for one or meal prepping. For more tips on air fryer meal prep, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep category</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.33 cup of chilli sauce</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for the most accurate results. Fill it to the 1/3 cup mark, then add a teaspoon to reach 0.33 cup. Alternatively, weigh 2.64 ounces on a kitchen scale. This method is ideal for sticky sauces like chilli sauce, which can cling to measuring cups.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 0.33 cup of chilli sauce</h2>  
<p>This amount is perfect for marinating chicken wings or adding zest to air-fried tofu. It’s also great for glazing salmon or mixing into meatballs.
