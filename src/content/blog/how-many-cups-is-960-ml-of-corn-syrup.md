<p>960 ml of corn syrup equals approximately 4 cups. This conversion is essential for baking and air fryer recipes where precision ensures the best results. Whether you're making sticky buns or glazing meats in your air fryer, knowing how many cups is 960 ml of corn syrup saves time and avoids guesswork.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Corn syrup is a common ingredient in many recipes, from desserts to glazes. Using the correct amount ensures the right texture and sweetness. Too much or too little can throw off your dish, especially in air fryer cooking where consistency is key. For more tips on perfecting your measurements, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Measuring 960 ml of corn syrup in cups is straightforward, but it's worth noting that UK and US cup sizes differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup is 284 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. For this conversion, we're using the US standard, which is more common in recipes. If you're using a UK recipe, adjust accordingly.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 960 ml of Corn Syrup</h2>  
<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like corn syrup can be tricky. To get an accurate 960 ml of corn syrup in cups, use a liquid measuring cup with clear markings. Lightly grease the cup with oil or cooking spray to help the syrup slide out easily. This method ensures you don’t waste any syrup and keeps your measurements precise.</p>  

<h3>Using Corn Syrup in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>Corn syrup is a versatile ingredient for air fryer cooking. It adds shine to glazes, helps crisp up coatings, and enhances the caramelization of meats and vegetables. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer sticky chicken wings</a> rely on a perfect balance of corn syrup and spices for that irresistible glaze.</p>  

<h2>Common Recipes Using 960 ml of Corn Syrup</h2>  
<p>Many desserts and sauces call for 960 ml of corn syrup. Pecan pie, caramel sauces, and marshmallow treats often require this quantity. If you're scaling a recipe up or down, knowing how to convert 960 ml of corn syrup to cups ensures consistency. For more inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a> for air fryer-friendly treats.</p>  

<p>When working with large quantities like 960 ml of corn syrup, it's helpful to divide the measurement into smaller, more manageable portions. For instance, 4 cups can be split into two 2-cup batches if your measuring cup isn’t large enough. This approach minimizes spills and makes pouring easier.</p>  

<h3>Substitutes for Corn Syrup</h3>  
<p>If you're out of corn syrup, honey or golden syrup can work as substitutes, though they may alter the flavor slightly. Keep in mind that substitutes might affect the texture of your dish, especially in air fryer recipes where moisture balance is crucial. Always test a small batch first to ensure the results meet your expectations.</p>  

<h2>Cleaning Up After Using Corn Syrup</h2>  
<p>Corn syrup is sticky and can be a challenge to clean. To avoid residue in your air fryer or on utensils, rinse them with warm water immediately after use. For stubborn spots, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner guide</a> offers practical solutions. Keeping your equipment clean ensures better performance and longevity.</p>  

<p>Storing leftover corn syrup is simple. Transfer any unused syrup to an airtight container and keep it in a cool, dry place. Proper storage prevents crystallization and maintains the syrup's quality for future use. Always check the label for specific storage recommendations.</p>
