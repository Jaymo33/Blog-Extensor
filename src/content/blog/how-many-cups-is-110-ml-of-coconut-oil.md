<h2>Understanding Coconut Oil Measurements</h2>  
<p>Coconut oil can be tricky to measure because it changes consistency with temperature. At room temperature, it’s solid, but when heated, it turns liquid. This means 110 ml of coconut oil could look different depending on how you measure it. For accuracy, always measure coconut oil in its liquid form if the recipe calls for volume. <h2>Converting 110 ml of Coconut Oil to Cups</h2>  
<p>To convert 110 ml of coconut oil to cups, you’ll need to know which cup measurement your recipe uses. In the US, 1 cup = 240 ml, so 110 ml is roughly 0.46 cups. In the UK, where 1 cup = 250 ml, 110 ml equals about 0.44 cups. This small difference can affect recipes, so always double-check. For air fryer-friendly recipes, explore <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>airfryerrecipe.co.uk’s chicken recipes</a>.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup, you can use a kitchen scale. Coconut oil weighs approximately 0.92 grams per ml, so 110 ml weighs about 101 grams. This method ensures accuracy, especially when working with solid coconut oil. Whether you’re baking or air frying, precise measurements lead to better results.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Measuring Coconut Oil</h3>  
<p>For best results, melt coconut oil before measuring if your recipe calls for liquid oil. If the recipe requires solid oil, pack it firmly into the measuring spoon or cup. Always level off the top for accuracy. These small steps make a big difference in recipes, especially when using an air fryer.</p>
