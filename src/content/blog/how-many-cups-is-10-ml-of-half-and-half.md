<h2>Understanding Half And Half Measurements</h2>  
<p>Half and half is a dairy product made by combining equal parts whole milk and cream. It’s commonly used in coffee, soups, and desserts for its rich, smooth texture. When a recipe calls for 10 ml of half and half, it’s usually a small but crucial amount to balance flavours or add creaminess. Measuring accurately is key, especially in air fryer recipes where ingredient ratios affect cooking times and results.</p>  

<p>If you’re working with an air fryer, precise measurements help avoid overly soggy or dry dishes. <h2>Converting 10 ml of Half And Half to Cups</h2>  
<p>To convert 10 ml of half and half to cups, you’ll need to know that 1 cup equals 240 ml. This means 10 ml is roughly 0.042 cups. While this might seem like a tiny amount, it’s often just enough to achieve the desired texture or taste in delicate recipes. For larger quantities, you can scale up accordingly—for example, 100 ml would be about 0.42 cups.</p>  

<p>If you’re unsure about measurements, using a kitchen scale or a precise measuring spoon can help. <h3>Tips for Measuring Half And Half Accurately</h3>  
<p>When measuring small amounts like 10 ml, use a graduated measuring spoon or syringe for precision. Pour slowly to avoid spills, and level off the liquid for an exact measurement. If you don’t have a 10 ml measure, remember that 2 teaspoons equal roughly 10 ml, making it a handy substitute in a pinch.</p>
