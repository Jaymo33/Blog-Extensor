<p>285 ml of apple cider vinegar equals approximately 1.2 cups. This conversion is essential for precise cooking, especially when using an air fryer where exact measurements can impact texture and flavour. Whether you’re marinating chicken, baking, or making dressings, knowing how to convert millilitres to cups ensures consistent results. <h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of apple cider vinegar can make or break a recipe. Too much can overpower flavours, while too little might not deliver the desired tang. Air fryers, known for their efficiency, rely on balanced ingredients for even cooking. A precise 285 ml to cups conversion ensures your dishes, like crispy <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air-fried chicken</a>, turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially marinades and glazes, call for apple cider vinegar. If you’re working with 285 ml, knowing it’s roughly 1.2 cups helps avoid guesswork. This accuracy is crucial for dishes like pulled pork or tangy coleslaw, where vinegar plays a starring role. For more recipe ideas, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 285 ml of Apple Cider Vinegar Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, use a standard cup. Since 1 cup equals 240 ml, 285 ml is just over 1 cup (1 cup + 1.5 tablespoons). For smaller quantities, a tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so 19 tablespoons would equal 285 ml. Keep in mind that viscosity can affect measurements, so pour slowly for accuracy.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 285 ml of Apple Cider Vinegar</h2>  
<p>Apple cider vinegar is a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. From marinades to pickling, 285 ml (or 1.2 cups) is a common quantity for recipes like honey-glazed salmon or tangy barbecue sauces. Its acidity also helps tenderise meats, making it ideal for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/beef>beef dishes</a>.</p>  

<p>Beyond cooking, apple cider vinegar is used in cleaning solutions. A mix of 285 ml vinegar and water can help <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>clean your air fryer basket</a>, removing grease and odours. Always rinse thoroughly to avoid lingering flavours in your next meal.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for UK vs. US Measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, recipes often use millilitres, while US recipes favour cups. If a recipe calls for 285 ml of apple cider vinegar, US cooks can use 1.2 cups. For smaller adjustments, remember that 1 tablespoon is about 15 ml. This ensures your air fryer dishes, like crispy tofu or roasted vegetables, turn out as intended.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing Apple Cider Vinegar</h2>  
<p>Once opened, store apple cider vinegar in a cool, dark place to preserve its quality. A 285 ml bottle is convenient for frequent use, but ensure the lid is tightly sealed to prevent oxidation. For longer storage, transfer to a glass container if the original packaging isn’t airtight.</p>  

<p>Apple cider vinegar’s shelf life is long, but its flavour can diminish over time. If using 285 ml for a large batch of marinade or sauce, label the container with the date. This helps track freshness, especially for meal preppers planning <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>air fryer meal prep</a>.</p>
