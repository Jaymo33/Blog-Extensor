<h2>Converting 250g wholemeal flour to ounces</h2>
<p>The standard conversion rate is 1 gram equals 0.035274 ounces. For 250g wholemeal flour, you multiply 250 by 0.035274 to get 8.8185 ounces. In practical terms, this rounds to 8.82 ounces. Some bakers might simplify this to 8¾ ounces for easier measuring with standard kitchen scales.</p>

<p>If you don't have digital scales, 250g of wholemeal flour is roughly 2¼ US cups or 2 UK cups. However, the scoop-and-sweep method can vary by up to 20% in weight. <h3>Storing wholemeal flour</h3>
<p>Because of its higher oil content, wholemeal flour goes rancid faster than white flour. Store your 250g (8.82oz) quantities in airtight containers in a cool, dark place. For longer storage, keep it in the freezer where it will last up to six months without losing quality.</p>

<h2>Common conversion mistakes</h2>
<p>One frequent error is confusing fluid ounces with weight ounces when converting 250g wholemeal flour to oz. Fluid ounces measure volume, while the ounces we use for flour measure weight. Another pitfall is using US cup measurements (240ml) instead of UK cups (284ml), which can throw off your 250g wholemeal flour conversion.</p>
