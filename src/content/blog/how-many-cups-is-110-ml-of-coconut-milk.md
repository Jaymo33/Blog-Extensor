<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient ratios must be spot-on. Too much or too little coconut milk can alter texture and cooking times. For example, adding extra liquid might make batters too runny, while too little can dry out dishes. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> call for coconut milk, especially in Asian-inspired or dairy-free dishes. Measuring 110 ml of coconut milk correctly ensures your meals turn out perfectly every time. It’s a small detail that makes a big difference.</p>

<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>The slight difference between US and UK cups matters when converting 110 ml of coconut milk. A US cup is 240 ml, while a UK cup is 250 ml. This means 110 ml equals 0.46 US cups or 0.44 UK cups. For most recipes, the variance won’t ruin your dish, but it’s good to know. If you’re following a British recipe, adjust accordingly. Air fryer cooking is forgiving, but accuracy never hurts.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 110 ml of Coconut Milk Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>No measuring jug? Use a tablespoon—15 ml equals one tablespoon, so 110 ml is about 7.3 tablespoons. For rough estimates, a US half-cup is 120 ml, so 110 ml is just shy of that. Stir the coconut milk first, as the cream and liquid can separate.
