<h3>How to measure 0.75 tbsp of garlic paste</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a tablespoon measure, you can use a teaspoon (3 tsp = 1 tbsp). For 0.75 tbsp, measure 2¼ tsp of garlic paste. Alternatively, a digital kitchen scale provides the most accurate reading. Simply tare your scale, add the paste until it reaches 11.25 grams, and you’re ready to cook. <h3>Adjusting recipes with 0.75 tbsp garlic paste</h3>  
<p>If a recipe calls for a different amount of garlic paste, you can scale the 0.75 tbsp measurement up or down. For instance, 1.5 tbsp would be 22.5 grams, while 0.375 tbsp (half of 0.75) would be 5.625 grams. Keeping these conversions handy simplifies adjustments without compromising taste.</p>  

<h2>Storing garlic paste for future use</h2>  
<p>To avoid waste, store leftover garlic paste in an airtight container in the fridge. It typically lasts up to two weeks.
