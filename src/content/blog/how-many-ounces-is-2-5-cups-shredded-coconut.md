<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect ingredient quantities can lead to uneven results. Too much shredded coconut, for example, might burn before the dish is fully cooked. Conversely, too little could leave your recipe lacking flavour or crunch. That's why converting 2.5 cups of shredded coconut to ounces (7.5 oz) is so important. For more air fryer measurement tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>

By measuring 2.5 cups accurately (7.5 oz), you ensure your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time. <h3>How to measure shredded coconut correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate conversion of 2.5 cups to ounces, lightly pack the shredded coconut into your measuring cup. Avoid pressing too firmly, as this can compact the coconut and skew the measurement. If you're using desiccated coconut, note that it's denser, so 2.5 cups may weigh slightly more. Always level off the top of the measuring cup for consistency.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for shredded coconut in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Shredded coconut works beautifully in air fryer recipes, adding crunch and sweetness. Try it in coconut shrimp, where 2.5 cups (7.5 oz) coats about 20 large prawns perfectly. It's also great for adding texture to air-fried desserts like coconut macaroons or tropical fruit crisps. <p>Another popular use is in coconut-crusted chicken or fish. The 2.5 cups (7.5 oz) measurement ensures enough coating for a family-sized portion. The air fryer crisps the coconut beautifully without burning, giving a golden finish. If you're meal prepping, shredded coconut stores well, so measure out 2.5 cups in advance for quick weeknight dinners. <h3>Storing shredded coconut for freshness</h3>  
<p>After measuring your 2.5 cups (7.5 oz), store any leftover shredded coconut in an airtight container. It keeps for months in the pantry or longer in the fridge. For air fryer recipes, slightly moist coconut can help coatings adhere better. If your coconut has dried out, a quick spritz of water before measuring 2.5 cups can revive it.</p>
