<h2>Why Accurate Rice Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of rice is crucial, especially when cooking in an air fryer. Too much rice can lead to uneven cooking, while too little might leave you with a dry or underwhelming dish. For air fryer recipes like crispy rice cakes or stuffed peppers, 1.5 tbsp of rice (about 18 grams) is often the perfect portion. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes</a> section for inspiration on how to use this measurement in delicious meals.</p>  

<p>Different types of rice, such as basmati, jasmine, or brown rice, may have slight variations in weight per tablespoon. However, 18 grams is a reliable average for most white rice varieties. If you're meal prepping or scaling up a recipe, this conversion ensures you stay on track without needing a scale every time.</p>  

<h3>Types of Rice and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>While 1.5 tbsp of standard white rice weighs around 18 grams, brown rice tends to be slightly denser, coming in at about 20 grams for the same volume. Short-grain rice, like sushi rice, may also differ. For precise air fryer cooking, consider weighing your rice the first time to confirm the conversion for your preferred variety.</p>
