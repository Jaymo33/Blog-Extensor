<h2>Why Accurate Buttermilk Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of buttermilk can make or break your air fryer dish. Too little, and your batter might be dry; too much, and it could become soggy. For 0.75 cups of buttermilk, 177 ml is the magic number for perfect texture. This conversion is especially handy for recipes like buttermilk-brined chicken or air-fried doughnuts.</p>  

<p>If you're working with a UK or US measuring cup, rest assured that 0.75 cups of buttermilk in ml remains consistent. For more conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> to simplify your cooking process.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Buttermilk Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.75 cups of buttermilk accurately, use a liquid measuring jug with ml markings. Pour the buttermilk up to the 177 ml line for precision. If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use a standard cup and fill it three-quarters full. For best results, always shake the buttermilk well before measuring.</p>  

<h2>Converting 0.75 Cups of Buttermilk for Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking requires exact measurements, and 0.75 cups of buttermilk in ml (177 ml) ensures your cakes and breads rise perfectly. Whether you're making buttermilk biscuits or a moist sponge cake, this conversion guarantees consistency. Visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> for more air fryer-friendly baking ideas.</p>  

<p>If you're scaling a recipe up or down, remember that 1 cup of buttermilk equals 237 ml. So, 0.75 cups buttermilk to ml is simply three-quarters of that amount. This conversion works for both UK and US recipes, so you’ll never have to second-guess your measurements again.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Buttermilk</h2>  
<p>If you have leftover buttermilk after measuring 0.75 cups (177 ml), store it in an airtight container in the fridge. It typically lasts up to two weeks, so you can use it for multiple air fryer dishes. For longer storage, freeze buttermilk in ice cube trays—each cube is roughly 30 ml, making future measurements a breeze.</p>  

<p>To keep your air fryer in top condition after cooking with buttermilk, check out our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning your air fryer</a>. Buttermilk can leave residue, so a quick wipe-down ensures your appliance stays fresh.</p>  

<h3>Substitutes for Buttermilk</h3>  
<p>If you're out of buttermilk, you can make a substitute by mixing 177 ml (0.75 cups) of milk with 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar. Let it sit for 5 minutes before using. This works well in most air fryer recipes, though the flavour may vary slightly.</p>
