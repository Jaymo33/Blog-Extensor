<p>2.5 tablespoons (tbsp) of plain flour equals approximately 20 grams. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements ensure perfect results. Whether you're baking cakes or coating chicken, knowing how many grams are in 2.5 tbsp of plain flour helps avoid kitchen mishaps.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but inaccurate measurements can lead to dry or dense results. Using 2.5 tbsp of plain flour without converting to grams might throw off your recipe. For example, a slight excess can make coatings clumpy, while too little won’t crisp properly. That’s why <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion charts</a> are a lifesaver for air fryer enthusiasts.</p>

If you only have measuring spoons, converting 2.5 tbsp plain flour to grams ensures your dish turns out as intended. This is particularly important for baking, where precision is key.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 2.5 tbsp of plain flour correctly</h3>  
<p>To get an accurate 20 grams from 2.5 tbsp, spoon the flour into the tablespoon and level it off with a knife. Scooping directly from the bag can compact the flour, leading to more than 20 grams. For best results, use digital scales—especially when preparing air fryer recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes</a> or batters.</p>
