<h2>Understanding Fan Oven vs Conventional Oven Temperatures</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. This means you typically need to reduce the temperature by about 20°C (36°F) when converting from fan to conventional. The 248 fan oven to conventional oven conversion follows this standard rule. Many modern recipes, especially those from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>, provide temperatures for both types of ovens.</p>
<p>When dealing with 248 f fan oven to normal oven conversions, it's helpful to understand why the adjustment is necessary. The circulating air in fan ovens transfers heat more efficiently, so foods cook faster at lower temperatures. This principle applies whether you're baking cakes, roasting meats, or preparing <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken dishes</a> in your air fryer.</p>

<h2>How to Convert 248 Fan Oven to Fahrenheit</h2>
<p>The straightforward conversion for 248 fan oven to fahrenheit is 428°F. This calculation comes from first converting the fan oven temperature to conventional celsius (248°F - 20°C = 228°C), then converting celsius to fahrenheit (228°C × 9/5 + 32 = 428°F). Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> makes this process even simpler for all your cooking needs.</p>

Remember that oven thermostats can vary, so it's always wise to check your food a few minutes before the recommended cooking time ends.</p>
