<h2>Understanding Half And Half Measurements</h2>  
<p>Half and half is a dairy product made by combining equal parts whole milk and cream. It’s commonly used in recipes to add richness without being as heavy as pure cream. When converting 160 ml of half and half to cups, it’s important to note that US and UK cup measurements differ slightly. In the US, 1 cup equals 240 ml, while in the UK, 1 cup is 284 ml. This means 160 ml of half and half is roughly 0.68 US cups or 0.56 UK cups.</p>  
<p>Precision matters, especially when using an air fryer, as too much or too little liquid can affect cooking times and results. For example, adding too much half and half to a batter might make it too runny, while too little could dry out your dish. If you’re working with air fryer recipes, always measure liquids carefully. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> for dishes that use half and half perfectly.</p>

<h2>Converting 160 ml of Half And Half for Baking</h2>

For US bakers, 0.68 cups is the equivalent, while UK bakers should use 0.56 cups. This small difference can impact the final product, so always check which measurement system your recipe uses.</p>

<p>Half and half isn’t just for baking—it’s also great in savoury dishes like creamy soups or mashed potatoes. <h2>Tips for Measuring Half And Half</h2>  
<p>To measure 160 ml of half and half accurately, use a liquid measuring cup with clear markings. Pour the half and half slowly to avoid spills and check the measurement at eye level. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale can help—160 ml of half and half weighs roughly 163 grams. This method is especially useful for air fryer recipes where precision is key.</p>  
<p>Keeping your air fryer clean is just as important as accurate measurements. This ensures your next dish tastes as good as the first.</p>

<h3>Substitutes for Half And Half</h3>  
<p>If you’re out of half and half, you can make a substitute by mixing equal parts milk and cream. For 160 ml of half and half, combine 80 ml of milk with 80 ml of cream. This DIY version works well in most recipes, though the fat content may vary slightly. Always measure substitutes carefully to maintain recipe accuracy.</p>
