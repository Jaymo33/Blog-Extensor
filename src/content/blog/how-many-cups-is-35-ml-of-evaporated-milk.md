<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>Using the correct amount of evaporated milk can make or break your recipe. Too little, and your dish might lack creaminess; too much, and it could become overly rich or soggy. <h2>How to Measure 35 ml of Evaporated Milk</h2>
<p>If you don't have a measuring jug with millilitre markings, you can use a standard cup measure. Fill it to just under a fifth of a cup to get roughly 35 ml. For even more precision, consider using a kitchen scale set to grams, as 35 ml of evaporated milk weighs about 36 grams. This method is particularly useful for air fryer recipes, where consistency is key.</p>

<h3>Tips for Working with Evaporated Milk</h3>
<p>Evaporated milk is thicker than regular milk but thinner than cream. When measuring 35 ml, make sure to stir it well first, as it can separate slightly in the can. For best results in your air fryer recipes, always use fresh evaporated milk and store any leftovers properly in the fridge.</p>
