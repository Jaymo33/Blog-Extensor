<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of oil is crucial in air frying to avoid soggy or overly dry results. Too much oil can lead to greasy food, while too little may cause sticking or uneven cooking. Rapeseed oil, with its high smoke point, is a popular choice for air fryers. Converting 0.5 tbsp rapeseed oil to grams (6.8g) ensures you get the perfect crispiness. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurements.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes call for small amounts of oil, making precision vital. A digital kitchen scale is the best tool for measuring grams, but spoon measurements work too with the right conversions. Whether you’re making crispy potatoes or golden chicken wings, 0.5 tbsp rapeseed oil in grams (6.8g) helps keep things light and healthy. <h3>How to measure 0.5 tbsp rapeseed oil without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use a standard UK tablespoon to measure 0.5 tbsp rapeseed oil. Fill the spoon halfway for an approximate 6.8-gram portion. Rapeseed oil has a density of about 0.92 g/ml, so volume-to-weight conversions are reliable. For best results, level off the spoon to avoid excess oil. This method works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> and roasted vegetables.</p>

<p>This small but mighty amount of oil is perfect for coating vegetables, marinating meats, or greasing the air fryer basket. <h3>Comparing rapeseed oil to other cooking oils</h3>  
<p>While 0.5 tbsp rapeseed oil converts to 6.8 grams, other oils have slightly different weights. Olive oil, for instance, weighs about 6.7g per 0.5 tbsp due to its density. These small differences matter when precision is key in air frying. Rapeseed oil’s high smoke point (around 230°C) makes it particularly suitable for air fryer cooking at higher temperatures.</p>
