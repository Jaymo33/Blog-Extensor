<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to deliver crispy, evenly cooked meals. Using too much or too little maple syrup can affect texture and flavour. For example, 3 cups of maple syrup to ounces (24 oz) is a common measurement for glazes and marinades. If you’re preparing a batch of air-fried chicken wings, this conversion ensures the right balance of sweetness and stickiness. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>Maple syrup is denser than water, so volume-to-weight conversions differ. While 1 cup of water is 8 ounces, 1 cup of maple syrup weighs closer to 8.3 ounces due to its viscosity. This slight difference adds up when scaling recipes. For air fryer desserts like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes</a>, accuracy ensures your batter isn’t too runny or thick. Always measure syrup at room temperature for the most consistent results.</p>  

<h3>How to measure maple syrup without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use liquid measuring cups for 3 cups of maple syrup in ounces. Fill the cup to the brim and level it off with a straight edge. For smaller amounts, tablespoons work well—16 tablespoons make 1 cup. Remember, 3 cups of maple syrup equals 24 ounces, so adjust your recipe accordingly. For sticky ingredients like syrup, lightly greasing your measuring cup helps it slide out easily.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for maple syrup in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Maple syrup isn’t just for pancakes—it’s a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. Use it to glaze air-fried salmon, sweeten roasted vegetables, or add depth to marinades. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes</a> section features creative ways to incorporate maple syrup into meals. For example, a 3-cup maple syrup to oz conversion might be needed for a large batch of granola or energy bars. The natural sweetness pairs perfectly with nuts and oats for a crunchy air-fried snack.</p>  

<p>When using maple syrup in savoury dishes, balance it with acidic ingredients like vinegar or citrus. This prevents the dish from becoming overly sweet. For air-fried tofu or tempeh, a maple-soy glaze caramelises beautifully under high heat. Visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/tofu>tofu recipes</a> for inspiration. Always preheat your air fryer to ensure even cooking and optimal caramelisation of syrupy glazes.</p>  

<h3>Storing maple syrup for long-term use</h3>  
<p>Proper storage keeps maple syrup fresh for months. After opening, refrigerate it to prevent mould growth. For larger quantities, freezing is an option—maple syrup retains its flavour and texture when thawed.
