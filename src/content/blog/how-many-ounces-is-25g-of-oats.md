<h2>How to Measure 25g of Oats Accurately</h2>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure 25g of oats. If you don’t have one, a standard tablespoon holds about 5-6g of oats, so 25g would be roughly 4-5 tablespoons. However, for best results in air fryer cooking, we recommend weighing ingredients. This eliminates variability and ensures your dishes are perfect every time.</p>

<p>25g of oats is a common measurement for single servings or small batches. You might use it for coating chicken or fish, making energy balls, or adding texture to air-fried desserts. <h2>Converting Other Weights of Oats to Ounces</h2>  
<p>If you need to convert other amounts, remember that 1 gram equals approximately 0.035 ounces. So, 50g of oats would be 1.76 ounces, and 100g would be 3.53 ounces. Keeping these conversions in mind simplifies scaling recipes up or down. For more tips on air fryer cooking, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Storing Oats for Air Fryer Use</h3>  
<p>Store oats in an airtight container to keep them fresh. If you’re using them frequently for air fryer recipes, consider pre-measuring 25g portions for convenience. This saves time and ensures consistency in your cooking.</p>
