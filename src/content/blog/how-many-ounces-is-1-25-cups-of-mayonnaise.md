<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve that perfect crispiness. Too much mayonnaise can make your dish greasy, while too little might leave it dry. <h3>How to Measure Mayonnaise for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>
<p>Measuring mayonnaise can be tricky due to its thick consistency. For 1.25 cups (10 ounces), use a liquid measuring cup and level it off with a spatula. If you’re using a kitchen scale, simply weigh out 10 ounces for precision. For more recipe ideas, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipes section</a>.</p>

<p>Many air fryer recipes call for 1.25 cups of mayonnaise, such as creamy coleslaw or crispy chicken tenders. This amount (10 ounces) is perfect for coating proteins or mixing into dips. If you’re meal prepping, this measurement ensures consistency across batches.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Mayonnaise Measurements</h3>

<h2>Tips for Storing Mayonnaise in Your Kitchen</h2>
<p>Once opened, mayonnaise should be stored in the fridge and used within two months. If you’ve measured out 1.25 cups (10 ounces) and have leftovers, transfer them to an airtight container. This keeps it fresh for your next air fryer adventure.</p>
