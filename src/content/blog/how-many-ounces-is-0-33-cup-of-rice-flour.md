<p>0.33 cup of rice flour equals approximately 1.5 ounces (oz). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements ensure the best texture and consistency. Whether you’re baking gluten-free treats or coating ingredients for frying, knowing the exact weight of rice flour makes all the difference.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of rice flour ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time. Too much flour can make coatings clumpy, while too little might not provide enough crispiness. For example, if you’re making air-fried chicken or crispy tofu, 0.33 cup of rice flour (1.5 oz) is often the ideal amount for a light, even coating.</p>  

<p>Rice flour is a popular choice for gluten-free cooking, and its weight can vary slightly depending on how it’s packed. Scooping versus spooning the flour into the measuring cup can affect the final weight, so weighing it in ounces removes any guesswork. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.33 Cup of Rice Flour Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate measurement, use a kitchen scale to weigh 1.5 oz of rice flour. If you don’t have one, lightly spoon the flour into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing the flour down, as this can lead to using more than needed. This method ensures consistency, especially for delicate recipes like air fryer cookies or cakes.</p>
