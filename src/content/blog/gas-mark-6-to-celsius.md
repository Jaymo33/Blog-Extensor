<p>Gas Mark 6 converts to 200°C, the standard equivalent for most ovens and air fryers. This medium-high temperature works perfectly for everything from roasting vegetables to baking cakes in your air fryer. <h2>Understanding Gas Mark 6 Temperature</h2>
<p>Gas Mark 6 represents 200°C or 392°F in conventional ovens and air fryers. This temperature sits right in the middle of the gas mark scale, making it versatile for various cooking methods. When converting gas mark 6 to celsius for your air fryer, you'll find it's ideal for most recipes requiring moderate heat. Many traditional British recipes specify Gas Mark 6, so knowing this conversion helps adapt them for air fryer use. The consistent heat of an air fryer often means you can reduce cooking times slightly compared to conventional ovens.</p>

<p>For perfect results at Gas Mark 6 in celsius, always preheat your air fryer for 3-5 minutes. This ensures even cooking from the start, especially important for baked goods. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> shows how Gas Mark 6 compares to other common temperatures. Interestingly, 200°C works particularly well for roasting potatoes or cooking <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> in the air fryer.</p>

<h3>Why 200°C Works So Well in Air Fryers</h3>
<p>The 200°C equivalent of Gas Mark 6 creates the perfect balance between browning and thorough cooking. At this temperature, the air fryer's rapid circulation creates crispy exteriors while keeping interiors moist. This makes Gas Mark 6 in celsius ideal for everything from cookies to roasted vegetables. Many air fryer manufacturers recommend 200°C as a default setting for general cooking.</p>

<h2>Converting Recipes from Gas Mark 6 to Air Fryer</h2>
<p>When adapting oven recipes specifying Gas Mark 6 to celsius for your air fryer, remember cooking times may differ. The concentrated heat circulation means most dishes cook 20% faster in an air fryer at 200°C. For example, cookies that take 12 minutes in a conventional oven at Gas Mark 6 might need just 9-10 minutes in the air fryer. Always check food a few minutes before the suggested time.</p>

<p>Our collection of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> includes many tested at the Gas Mark 6 equivalent temperature. For baking, reduce quantities by about a third as air fryer baskets are smaller than oven trays. The 200°C setting works particularly well for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> adapted from traditional Gas Mark 6 instructions.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations for Baking at Gas Mark 6</h3>
<p>When baking at the Gas Mark 6 to celsius equivalent, use oven-safe containers that fit your air fryer basket. Silicone or metal pans work best at 200°C, while some plastics may warp. For even baking at this temperature, consider shaking or turning items halfway through cooking. This ensures all sides get equal exposure to the circulating hot air.</p>

<h2>Common Foods Cooked at Gas Mark 6</h2>
<p>Many everyday foods cook perfectly at the Gas Mark 6 to celsius conversion of 200°C. Roast potatoes achieve perfect crispiness at this temperature, while chicken thighs get golden skin without drying out. The 200°C setting also works wonders for reheating leftovers, giving them new life with refreshed texture. Even frozen foods like chips or nuggets benefit from this moderate heat setting.</p>

When cooking fatty foods at this temperature, consider lining the basket to catch drips and splatters.</p>
