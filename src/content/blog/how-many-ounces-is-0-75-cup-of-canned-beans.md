<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Using 0.75 cup of canned beans (6 ounces) ensures your dish isn’t too dry or too soggy. Many recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipes page</a> call for precise bean quantities to achieve the perfect crispiness. <p>If you’re using an air fryer liner, the right amount of beans prevents overcrowding. Overloading can block airflow, leading to uneven cooking. <h3>How to measure 0.75 cup of canned beans</h3>  
<p>Use a dry measuring cup for the most accurate results. Drain and rinse the beans first, then lightly spoon them into the cup without packing. Level off the top with a straight edge for consistency. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale set to ounces works perfectly. Six ounces of canned beans will give you the right amount for most air fryer dishes.</p>
