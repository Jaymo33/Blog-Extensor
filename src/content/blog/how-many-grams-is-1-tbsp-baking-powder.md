<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking powder is a leavening agent, and using the wrong amount can ruin your air fryer creations. Too little, and your cakes won’t rise; too much, and they’ll taste bitter. Converting 1 tbsp of baking powder to grams (15g) ensures reliability. This is especially crucial for air fryer recipes, where heat distribution differs from conventional ovens. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe hub</a> for perfectly measured treats.</p>  

<p>Many UK bakers rely on volume measurements, but weight (grams) is far more precise. A slight variance in how you scoop baking powder can alter results. Digital scales eliminate guesswork, making 1 tbsp baking powder to grams conversions foolproof. For consistent air fryer bakes, always weigh ingredients.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 1 Tbsp Baking Powder Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t own scales, lightly spoon baking powder into a tablespoon and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can add extra grams. While not as precise as weighing, this method works in a pinch. For best results, invest in a cheap digital scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking.</p>
