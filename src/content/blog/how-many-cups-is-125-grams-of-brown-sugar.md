<h2>Understanding Brown Sugar Measurements</h2>  
<p>Brown sugar can be tricky to measure because its texture varies between loose and packed. Lightly packed brown sugar means gently pressing it into the measuring cup without compacting it too much. For 125 grams of brown sugar, this typically translates to just over half a cup. If you're using an air fryer for baking, precision matters—too much sugar can affect texture, while too little may leave your dessert lacking sweetness.</p>  

<p>Different brands or types of brown sugar (light vs. dark) may have slight variations in density. For consistent results, consider using a kitchen scale for accuracy. <h3>Why Measuring Brown Sugar Correctly Matters</h3>  
<p>In baking, especially with air fryers, precise measurements ensure even cooking and the right texture. Too much brown sugar can make your dessert overly moist or sticky, while too little may result in a dry or bland outcome. Whether you're making cookies, cakes, or even glazes, 125 grams of brown sugar should be measured carefully for the best results.</p>  

<h2>Converting 125 Grams of Brown Sugar to Cups</h2>  
<p>For those without a scale, here’s a quick reference: 125 grams of brown sugar equals approximately 0.63 cups (or 5/8 cup). If you’re using a standard 250ml measuring cup, fill it just over halfway. Remember, the way you pack the sugar affects the volume—lightly packed is ideal for most recipes. For air fryer baking, consistency is key, so sticking to this measurement ensures your treats come out perfectly every time.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Brown Sugar Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use the spoon-and-level method: spoon the brown sugar into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down unless the recipe specifies "firmly packed." For air fryer recipes, this technique helps maintain the right balance of moisture and sweetness. <p>To keep brown sugar soft and ready for baking, store it in an airtight container with a slice of bread or a terra cotta brown sugar saver. This prevents hardening, ensuring your 125 grams measure accurately every time. Proper storage also makes it easier to scoop and level when preparing air fryer recipes.</p>
