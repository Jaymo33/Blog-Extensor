<h3>How to measure 0.33 cup of caster sugar without a scale</h3>
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method to measure 0.33 cup of caster sugar. Gently spoon the sugar into a measuring cup until it’s slightly overflowing, then level it off with a straight edge. This method minimizes compaction, giving you the closest approximation to 2.64 ounces. For sticky ingredients, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>non-stick liner</a> can make cleanup easier.</p>

<p>This amount of sugar is perfect for small-batch recipes, like air fryer mug cakes or single-serving desserts. <h3>Converting other sugar amounts for air fryer recipes</h3>
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of caster sugar, use this ratio: 1 cup equals 8 ounces, so 0.33 cup is roughly one-third of that. For larger batches, multiply accordingly—0.66 cup would be 5.28 ounces. Always measure carefully, as air fryers are less forgiving than ovens when it comes to ingredient imbalances.</p>
