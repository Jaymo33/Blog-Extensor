<p>0.75 tablespoons (tbsp) of icing sugar equals approximately 5.6 grams. This precise measurement ensures your air fryer desserts and baked goods turn out perfectly every time. Whether you're dusting doughnuts or sweetening a cake, knowing this conversion is essential for UK bakers.</p>

<h2>Why accurate icing sugar measurements matter</h2>
<p>Getting the right amount of icing sugar can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too much can make desserts cloyingly sweet, while too little might leave them lacking flavour. The 0.75 tbsp to grams conversion is particularly useful for small batches or when adjusting recipes. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we know precision matters when creating perfect air-fried treats.</p>

<p>Icing sugar, also known as powdered sugar or confectioners' sugar, has a different density to granulated sugar. This means volume measurements like tablespoons don't always translate directly to weight. That's why converting 0.75 tbsp of icing sugar to grams (5.6g) gives you more reliable results in your air fryer baking.</p>

<h2>How to measure 0.75 tbsp icing sugar accurately</h2>
<p>For the most precise 0.75 tbsp measurement, use proper measuring spoons rather than eating utensils. Fill the tablespoon slightly less than full to achieve 0.75 tbsp. Alternatively, you can measure 5.6 grams directly on digital kitchen scales for perfect accuracy every time.</p>

<p>If you don't have scales, the spoon-and-level method works well. Use a knife to level off the icing sugar at the top of the spoon. Remember that packed icing sugar will weigh more than loosely spooned, so consistency in technique matters. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement conversions.</p>
