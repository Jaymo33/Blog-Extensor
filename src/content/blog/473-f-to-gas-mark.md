<p>473 F converts to Gas Mark 9 in UK ovens and air fryers. This high temperature is ideal for achieving crispy textures when roasting or baking in your air fryer. Whether you're cooking frozen foods or preparing fresh vegetables, knowing this conversion ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Understanding 473 F to Gas Mark Conversion</h2>
<p>The Gas Mark scale is commonly used in British recipes and oven settings. When your air fryer recipe calls for 473 F, setting your oven to Gas Mark 9 will give you the equivalent temperature. This high heat is perfect for achieving that desirable golden-brown finish on roasted potatoes or crispy chicken wings. Many air fryer users find this conversion helpful when adapting traditional oven recipes for their appliance.</p>

<h2>When to Use 473 F (Gas Mark 9) in Your Air Fryer</h2>
<p>This high temperature setting is ideal for several air fryer cooking techniques. Use 473 F to Gas Mark 9 when you want to achieve maximum crispiness on the outside while keeping food moist inside. It's perfect for roasting vegetables, cooking frozen foods, or giving meats that restaurant-quality sear.</p>
<p>Many of our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> recommend starting at this temperature before reducing heat for even cooking. The intense initial blast at 473 F (Gas Mark 9) helps create that desirable texture contrast in foods like chicken wings or roasted Brussels sprouts. Just remember to shake or turn your food halfway through cooking for even results.</p>
