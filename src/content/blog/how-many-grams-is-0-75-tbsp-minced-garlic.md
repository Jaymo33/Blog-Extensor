<h2>Why accurate garlic measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Garlic is a powerhouse ingredient that can dominate a dish if overused. In air fryer cooking, where flavours intensify due to rapid air circulation, getting the right amount is crucial. Too much minced garlic can overpower delicate proteins like cod or shrimp, while too little might leave your dish lacking depth. <h3>How to measure minced garlic accurately</h3>  
<p>When converting 0.75 tbsp minced garlic to grams, consistency in preparation matters. Minced garlic can vary in density depending on how finely it’s chopped and whether it’s freshly prepared or from a jar. For the most accurate results, lightly pack the minced garlic into your measuring spoon without compressing it too tightly. This method ensures your 0.75 tablespoon measurement truly reflects 11.25 grams of garlic.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 0.75 tbsp minced garlic</h2>  
<p>This quantity of minced garlic (11.25 grams) is perfect for seasoning proteins like chicken breasts or salmon fillets in the air fryer. It’s also an ideal amount for garlic butter sauces or rubs, where balanced flavour is key. Many of our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> call for this measurement, especially in Mediterranean and Asian-inspired dishes where garlic plays a supporting rather than dominant role.</p>  

<p>If you’re preparing multiple servings, simply scale up the conversion proportionally. For instance, 1.5 tbsp would be 22.5 grams, and 3 tbsp would convert to 45 grams. This linear scaling makes it easy to adjust recipes while maintaining perfect flavour balance. <h3>Converting other garlic measurements</h3>  
<p>While we’ve focused on 0.75 tbsp minced garlic to grams, you might also need to convert other quantities. One whole clove of garlic typically yields about 1 teaspoon minced (about 5 grams), so 0.75 tbsp would be roughly equivalent to 2-3 cloves. Remember that fresh garlic tends to be more potent than pre-minced jarred varieties, so adjust quantities to taste when substituting.</p>  

<h2>Storing minced garlic for air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Once you’ve measured out your 11.25 grams (0.75 tbsp) of minced garlic, proper storage ensures freshness for future use. Transfer any leftover minced garlic to an airtight container, covering it with a thin layer of olive oil to prevent oxidation. Stored this way in the refrigerator, it will keep for about 2 weeks – ready for your next air fryer creation. For more kitchen organisation tips, check our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>keeping your air fryer clean</a> between garlic-heavy recipes.</p>  

<p>If you find yourself frequently converting 0.75 tbsp minced garlic to grams, consider pre-mincing a batch and freezing it in 11.25 gram portions. This time-saving trick means you’ll always have the right amount on hand for spontaneous air fryer meals. Frozen minced garlic works particularly well in cooked dishes, though the texture may change slightly for raw applications.</p>
