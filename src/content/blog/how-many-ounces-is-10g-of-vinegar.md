<h2>Why accurate vinegar measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Vinegar plays a crucial role in balancing flavours, tenderising meats, and even cleaning your air fryer. Too much can overpower a dish, while too little might leave it bland. For air fryer recipes, where ingredients cook quickly, precision is key. A slight miscalculation could alter the texture or taste of your meal.</p>  

<p>Many UK cooks prefer using grams, but some recipes, especially older or American ones, list vinegar in ounces. That’s where conversions come in handy. Whether you’re making crispy chicken wings or a tangy glaze, knowing that 10g of vinegar is roughly 0.35 ounces ensures consistency. <h3>How to measure 10g of vinegar without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have digital scales, you can still measure 10g of vinegar accurately. One teaspoon holds about 5ml of liquid, and since vinegar’s density is close to water, 10g is roughly two teaspoons. For ounces, this translates to just over a third of an ounce. Keep in mind that viscosity varies slightly between vinegar types, but this method works for most cooking purposes.</p>

<p>From marinades to glazes, 10g of vinegar (or 0.35 ounces) is a versatile quantity. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> call for this amount to tenderise the meat without making it soggy.</p>
