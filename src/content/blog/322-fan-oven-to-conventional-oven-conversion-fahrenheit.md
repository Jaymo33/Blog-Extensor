<h2>Why Convert 322 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air, which means they cook food more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. This efficiency means you often need to lower the temperature when converting from a fan oven to a conventional one. For example, 322°F in a fan oven is roughly equivalent to 300°F in a conventional oven. This adjustment ensures your dishes don’t overcook or burn. If you're using an air fryer, which also relies on circulating hot air, you might find similar results to a fan oven.</p>

<h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>Converting 322 fan oven to conventional oven isn’t just about temperature—it’s also about timing. Since fan ovens cook faster, you may need to extend the cooking time slightly when using a conventional oven. For example, if a recipe calls for 20 minutes in a fan oven at 322°F, you might need 25-30 minutes in a conventional oven at 300°F. Always keep an eye on your food to avoid overcooking.</p>
<p>Air fryers, like fan ovens, cook food quickly due to their efficient air circulation. If you're adapting a fan oven recipe for an air fryer, you might not need to adjust the time as much. However, it’s always best to check your food a few minutes early to ensure it doesn’t dry out.
