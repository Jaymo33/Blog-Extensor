<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient quantities must be spot-on. Too much or too little evaporated milk can alter texture and taste. For example, a custard might turn out runny or overly thick if measurements are off. That’s why converting 0.33 cup of evaporated milk to ounces (2.64 oz) is so useful. It’s a small detail that makes a big difference in your cooking.</p>  

<p>Precision is especially important for baking in an air fryer. Unlike stovetop cooking, where you can adjust as you go, air fryer recipes often require exact amounts. <h3>How to measure 0.33 cup of evaporated milk</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a 1/3 cup measure, you can use tablespoons. 0.33 cup equals roughly 5 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon of evaporated milk. Alternatively, weigh it out for accuracy—2.64 ounces on a kitchen scale. This method eliminates guesswork and guarantees perfect results in your air fryer dishes.</p>
