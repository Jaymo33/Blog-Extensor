<h2>Why Convert 396 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food, reducing cooking time and often requiring lower temperatures. A conventional oven, on the other hand, relies on radiant heat, which can take longer to cook food evenly. Converting 396 fan oven to conventional oven ensures your recipes turn out perfectly, whether you're baking, roasting, or air frying. This is especially useful if you're following a recipe designed for a fan oven but only have a conventional oven or air fryer at home.</p>
<p>Many modern recipes, especially those from the UK and Europe, specify fan oven temperatures. If you're using an air fryer, which mimics a fan oven's cooking style, you might not need to adjust the temperature. However, for traditional ovens, reducing the temperature by 20°F (or 10°C) is a good rule of thumb. <h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>When converting 396 fan oven to conventional oven, it's not just the temperature that changes—cooking times may need adjusting too. Fan ovens cook food faster due to the circulating air, so if you're using a conventional oven, you might need to increase the cooking time by 10-15%. For air fryers, which also use rapid air technology, cooking times are often shorter than conventional ovens.</p>
<p>For example, if a recipe calls for 20 minutes in a 396°F fan oven, you might need 22-25 minutes in a conventional oven set to 356°F. <h3>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h3>
<p>One of the biggest mistakes when converting 396 fan oven to conventional oven is forgetting to adjust the temperature or cooking time. This can lead to undercooked or overcooked food. Another common error is assuming all ovens heat the same way—fan ovens and air fryers are more efficient, so adjustments are necessary. Always use an oven thermometer to verify your oven's temperature accuracy.</p>
