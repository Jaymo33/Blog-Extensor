<h2>Why converting 30g rapeseed oil to ounces matters</h2>
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial in cooking, especially when using an air fryer. Too much or too little oil can affect texture, crispiness, and even cooking times. Rapeseed oil is a popular choice for air frying due to its high smoke point and neutral flavour. Converting 30g of rapeseed oil to ounces (about 1.06 oz) helps you follow recipes precisely, whether they're from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a> or elsewhere.</p>

<p>Many UK recipes use grams, while some air fryer manuals might reference ounces. This conversion becomes particularly important when scaling recipes up or down. For example, if you're adapting a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipe</a> that calls for 30g of rapeseed oil, knowing it's roughly 1.06 ounces helps maintain the right balance of moisture and crispiness.</p>

<h2>How to measure 30g rapeseed oil in ounces</h2>
<p>The simplest way to measure 30g of rapeseed oil in ounces is to use digital kitchen scales that switch between metric and imperial units.
