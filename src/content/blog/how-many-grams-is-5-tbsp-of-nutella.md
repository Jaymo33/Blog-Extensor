<h2>Why converting 5 tbsp of Nutella to grams matters</h2>
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial in baking and air frying, where precision affects texture and taste. Nutella, being a dense spread, can vary in weight per tablespoon depending on how it's scooped. Converting 5 tbsp of Nutella to grams (around 85g) eliminates guesswork, ensuring your recipes are consistent. This is especially important for air fryer recipes, where even small deviations can alter cooking times. Visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>

<p>Using grams instead of tablespoons also simplifies scaling recipes up or down. For example, if a recipe calls for 10 tbsp of Nutella, you'll know it's roughly 170 grams. This method is more reliable than volume measurements, which can be inconsistent. Whether you're making a batch of Nutella brownies or a quick air fryer dessert, grams provide the accuracy you need. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> for inspiration on how to use Nutella in your air fryer.</p>

<h3>How to measure Nutella accurately</h3>
<p>To measure 5 tbsp of Nutella in grams, use a kitchen scale for the most precise results. Scoop the Nutella into a bowl or onto parchment paper, then weigh it until you reach 85 grams. If you don't have a scale, lightly spoon the Nutella into a tablespoon and level it off for a close approximation. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to over-measuring. For more tips on accurate measuring, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>.</p>

<h2>Common uses for 5 tbsp of Nutella in air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Nutella is a versatile ingredient that shines in air fryer desserts. With 5 tbsp (85g), you can make Nutella-stuffed pastries, lava cakes, or even drizzle it over air-fried bananas. The rich, hazelnut flavor pairs perfectly with crispy textures from the air fryer. For a quick treat, try spreading 5 tbsp of Nutella on toast and air frying it for a minute to melt it slightly. Find more creative ideas in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes category</a>.</p>

<p>Another popular option is Nutella-filled doughnuts or croissants, where 5 tbsp of Nutella provides enough filling for 4-6 servings. The air fryer gives these treats a golden exterior while keeping the Nutella center gooey. Remember, precise measurements ensure balanced flavors and textures. If you're experimenting with new recipes, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> will help keep your air fryer in top condition.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with Nutella in the air fryer</h3>
<p>When using Nutella in the air fryer, avoid overheating it, as it can burn quickly. Stick to lower temperatures (around 160°C) and shorter cooking times. If you're coating or stuffing foods with Nutella, seal edges well to prevent leaks. For best results, chill Nutella-filled items for 10-15 minutes before air frying to help them hold their shape. These small adjustments make a big difference in achieving perfect results every time.</p>
