<h2>Understanding the Conversion: 110 ml to Cups</h2>  
<p>Converting millilitres to cups can be tricky, but it's simpler than you think. One standard cup equals 240 ml, so 110 ml is just under half a cup. This measurement is particularly useful for UK bakers who often work with metric units but follow recipes using cups. For air fryer recipes, precision is key to avoid overly dense or runny results.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Heavy Cream Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>Don’t have a measuring jug? Use a tablespoon—15 ml equals one tablespoon, so 110 ml is about 7.3 tablespoons. Alternatively, fill a liquid measuring cup to just below the halfway mark for an approximate 0.46 cups. For best results, always level off your measurements to avoid discrepancies.</p>  

<h2>Converting 110 ml of Heavy Cream in Different Cup Sizes</h2>  
<p>Not all cups are created equal. A US cup is 240 ml, while a UK cup is 284 ml. For 110 ml of heavy cream, the US measurement is 0.46 cups, whereas in the UK, it’s roughly 0.39 cups. Always check which cup measurement your recipe uses to avoid mistakes.</p>
