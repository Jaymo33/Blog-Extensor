<p>985 ml of milk is approximately 4.16 cups when using the standard UK metric cup (240 ml per cup). This conversion is essential for precise baking and cooking, especially when using an air fryer where measurements can impact texture and cooking times.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Milk Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of milk in air fryer recipes ensures consistency and optimal results. Too much liquid can make batters soggy, while too little may dry out your dish. Whether you're making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer pancakes</a> or a creamy sauce, knowing how to convert 985 ml of milk to cups is key. The air fryer's rapid air circulation means moisture levels must be spot-on for perfect cooking.</p>  

<p>For those who love baking in their air fryer, such as cakes or muffins, 985 ml of milk in cups translates to roughly 4 and 1/6 cups. This precision helps maintain the right batter consistency. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common measurement conversions.</p>  

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for Milk</h3>  
<p>In the UK, a standard metric cup holds 240 ml, while the US customary cup is slightly smaller at 236.59 ml. For 985 ml of milk, this means 4.16 UK cups or 4.23 US cups. The difference is minor but worth noting for exact recipes, especially in air fryer cooking where precision matters.</p>  

<h2>Converting 985 ml of Milk for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>Whether you're preparing a savoury dish or a sweet treat, 985 ml of milk in cups can be adjusted based on your needs. Always measure carefully to avoid altering the dish's texture.</p>  

<p>If you're scaling a recipe up or down, remember that 985 ml of milk equals about 4 and 1/6 cups. This conversion is handy for batch cooking or meal prepping in your air fryer. <h3>Tips for Measuring Milk Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a measuring jug, use a standard metric cup to portion 985 ml of milk. Fill it four times (960 ml) and add another tablespoon (about 25 ml) to reach the full amount. This method ensures accuracy even without specialised tools, making air fryer cooking more accessible.</p>
