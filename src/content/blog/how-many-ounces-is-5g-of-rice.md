<h2>How to measure 5g of rice without scales</h2>  
<p>Not everyone has kitchen scales handy, but there are easy ways to estimate 5g of rice. A level teaspoon holds about 5g of uncooked rice, which translates to 0.176 ounces. This method is handy for quick measurements when you’re mid-recipe. Remember, though, that this is an approximation—for baking or precise air fryer recipes, scales are best.</p>  
<p>Another trick is to use a standard UK penny as a reference. A stack of five 1p coins weighs roughly 5g, so you can balance your rice against them. This is a handy hack if you’re cooking on the go or don’t have measuring spoons. <h3>Tips for cooking rice in an air fryer</h3>  
<p>Rice cooks differently in an air fryer than on the stove. For best results, pre-soak your rice (even just 5g) for 10 minutes to ensure even cooking. A light spray of oil prevents sticking and adds crispiness where desired. Always check your air fryer’s manual for specific rice-cooking settings.</p>
