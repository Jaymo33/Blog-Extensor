<h2>Why Accurate Potato Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of potatoes ensures even cooking and perfect texture in your air fryer. Too many potatoes can lead to overcrowding, resulting in unevenly cooked or soggy results. Too few might leave you with undercooked or overly crispy edges. Whether you're making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>air fryer potato recipes</a> or meal prepping, 385 grams (2.75 cups) is a reliable starting point. This measurement works well for dishes like roast potatoes, wedges, or even homemade crisps.</p>

<p>Different potato types can slightly alter the weight, but 385 grams is a safe average. For example, waxy potatoes like Charlotte might weigh a tad less, while floury ones like King Edward could be slightly heavier. If precision is crucial, like in baking, using a kitchen scale eliminates guesswork. <h3>How to Measure 2.75 Cups of Potatoes</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a measuring cup designed for dry ingredients. Fill it loosely with diced or sliced potatoes until slightly heaped, then level off the top. If you don’t have a measuring cup, 385 grams of potatoes equates to roughly 2 medium-large potatoes, peeled and cubed. This method ensures you’re close to the 2.75-cup mark without fuss.</p>

<h2>Converting Cups to Grams for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Air fryers thrive on precise measurements to achieve that perfect crispiness. While cups are convenient, grams offer unbeatable accuracy. For 2.75 cups of potatoes, 385 grams is your go-to figure. This conversion is especially useful for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> where balance is key. Too much or too little can affect cooking time and texture.</p>

<p>If you’re scaling a recipe up or down, remember that 1 cup of diced potatoes is roughly 140 grams. So, 2.75 cups × 140 grams = 385 grams total. Keep this formula handy for quick adjustments. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Potatoes</h3>  
<p>Avoid packing potatoes tightly into the cup, as this can skew the measurement. Instead, let them settle naturally. Also, peeling or not peeling can change the volume slightly—385 grams assumes peeled potatoes. For unpeeled, add about 10-15 grams to account for the skin. Always adjust air fryer times slightly if your potato quantity varies.</p>

<h2>Best Potato Types for Air Frying</h2>  
<p>Not all potatoes are created equal when it comes to air frying. Floury varieties like Maris Piper or King Edward crisp up beautifully, while waxy ones like Charlotte hold their shape better.
