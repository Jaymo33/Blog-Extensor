<h2>Why Accurate Honey Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of honey ensures your air fryer dishes turn out perfectly sweetened without being overly sticky or dry. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement swaps.</p>  

<p>Honey’s thickness also affects how it pours and spreads in the air fryer basket. Unlike runny liquids, honey clings to measuring tools, so using weight (grams) is often more reliable than volume (cups). If you’re adapting a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, always double-check whether it uses weight or volume measurements.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 50 Grams of Honey Without Scales</h3>  
<p>No kitchen scales? No problem. A standard metric tablespoon holds about 21 grams of honey, so 2.5 tablespoons will get you close to 50 grams. For cup measurements, lightly grease your measuring cup with oil first—this helps the honey slide out cleanly. Remember, 50 grams of honey in cups is roughly 1/6 cup, but slight variations can occur depending on honey density.</p>
