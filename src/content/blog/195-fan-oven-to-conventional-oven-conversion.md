<p>195 fan oven converts to approximately 175°C in a conventional oven. This 20°C reduction accounts for the efficient heat distribution of fan-assisted ovens. Whether you're adapting recipes for cakes, roasts, or air fryer meals, understanding this conversion ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air constantly, cooking food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens. The forced air movement means you can typically reduce temperatures by 20°C compared to traditional oven settings. This principle applies whether you're using a built-in oven or countertop appliances like air fryers, which essentially function as mini convection ovens.</p>

<h2>Practical Conversion Examples</h2>
<p>When converting 195 fan oven to conventional oven settings, remember the golden rule: subtract 20°C. So 195°C fan becomes 175°C conventional. This adjustment works for most recipes, though some delicate bakes might need further fine-tuning.</p>
<p>Here are some common cooking scenarios where this conversion matters:</p>
<ul>
<li>Roast chicken: 195 fan (175 conventional) for golden, juicy results</li>
<li>Victoria sponge: 195 fan (175 conventional) prevents over-browning</li>
<li>Roast vegetables: 195 fan (175 conventional) ensures even caramelisation</li>
</ul>

<h3>Air Fryer Equivalent Temperatures</h3>
<p>Since air fryers use similar convection technology to fan ovens, you can often use the same temperature. However, cooking times will be shorter due to the compact size. For 195 fan oven recipes, start checking air fryer food 5-10 minutes earlier than the original recipe suggests.</p>

<h2>Common Conversion Mistakes</h2>
<p>Many home cooks forget to adjust temperatures when switching between oven types, leading to over or undercooked food. Another frequent error is assuming all ovens heat accurately - it's worth investing in an oven thermometer to verify your appliance's true temperature.</p>
<p>For perfect <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> every time, always use converted temperatures and check internal doneness with a meat thermometer. The same principle applies to baking - a toothpick test never lies, regardless of your oven type.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations for Baking</h3>
<p>When converting 195 fan oven to conventional oven settings for cakes or pastries, position matters. Conventional ovens often have hot spots, so rotate trays halfway through cooking. Fan ovens eliminate this need, but the temperature conversion remains crucial for texture and rise.</p>
