<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Using 0.375 cup of chopped walnuts might seem simple, but weight (ounces) is more reliable than volume (cups). This is because chopped walnuts can vary in density depending on how finely they’re chopped. For example, loosely packed walnuts will weigh less than tightly packed ones. That’s why converting 0.375 cup of chopped walnuts to ounces (1.5 oz) gives you a more precise measurement. <h3>How to measure 0.375 cup of chopped walnuts correctly</h3>
<p>To measure 0.375 cup of chopped walnuts, use a dry measuring cup and fill it lightly without packing the walnuts down. Level off the top with a straight edge for accuracy. If you don’t have a 0.375 cup measure, you can use 3/8 cup or 6 tablespoons as an equivalent. For even better precision, a kitchen scale set to ounces will confirm you have the right amount (1.5 oz). This method eliminates guesswork and ensures your air fryer dishes turn out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Tips for storing chopped walnuts for air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Once you’ve measured your 0.375 cup (1.5 oz) of chopped walnuts, proper storage keeps them fresh. Store unused walnuts in an airtight container in a cool, dark place for up to a month. For longer storage, keep them in the fridge for up to 6 months or freeze for up to a year. This ensures your walnuts stay fresh for future air fryer recipes. Proper storage also maintains the quality of the walnuts’ natural oils, which contribute to their rich flavour in dishes.</p>
