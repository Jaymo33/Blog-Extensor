<h2>Why accurate garlic measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Crushed garlic can vary in density, making volume measurements like tablespoons less reliable than weight. Using grams ensures consistency, especially in air fryer cooking where even heat distribution is key. Too much garlic can overpower a dish, while too little might leave it bland. Recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> often specify weights for this reason. Keeping a kitchen scale handy simplifies the process.</p>  

<p>Garlic’s potency also depends on how finely it’s crushed. A coarse chop will weigh slightly less than a finely minced paste for the same volume. If you’re adapting a recipe from tablespoons to grams, consider the texture of your crushed garlic. For air fryer dishes like roasted vegetables or garlic bread, precise measurements ensure balanced flavours.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.25 tbsp of crushed garlic</h3>  
<p>Measuring small amounts like 0.25 tbsp can be tricky without the right tools. Use a 1/4 tablespoon measure and fill it halfway, or opt for a digital scale set to grams. If you’re preparing a larger batch, multiply the conversion accordingly. For example, 1 tbsp of crushed garlic equals roughly 15 grams. This method works perfectly for air fryer marinades or rubs.</p>
