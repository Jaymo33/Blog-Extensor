<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of coconut oil can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too little, and your food might stick or dry out; too much, and it could become greasy. That’s why converting 1.5 cups of coconut oil to ounces (12 oz) is so helpful. Many air fryer recipes, like those found in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>, rely on precise measurements for the best results. Whether you're making roasted vegetables or a batch of cookies, accuracy is key.</p>  

<p>Coconut oil is versatile, but its solid-to-liquid state can confuse measurements. When melted, 1.5 cups of coconut oil will still weigh 12 ounces, but the volume may appear different. For consistent results, weigh it in its solid form if your recipe calls for it. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> can help with other common conversions, too.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 1.5 Cups of Coconut Oil Correctly</h2>  
<p>Measuring coconut oil isn’t as straightforward as water or flour. If your recipe calls for 1.5 cups (12 ounces), here’s how to get it right. First, pack the solid oil into a measuring cup, ensuring no air gaps. Level it off with a knife for an exact measure. If you’re using liquid coconut oil, pour it slowly into a liquid measuring cup until it reaches the 1.5-cup mark.</p>  

<h3>Using a Kitchen Scale for Precision</h3>  
<p>A kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure 1.5 cups of coconut oil, especially since weight doesn’t change with temperature. Set your scale to ounces, place your container on it, and tare the weight. Add coconut oil until it reads 12 ounces. This method eliminates guesswork, which is crucial for air fryer recipes like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a>.</p>
