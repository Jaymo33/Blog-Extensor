<h2>How to measure 250g of cream without a scale</h2>  
<p>Not everyone has a kitchen scale, but you can still measure 250g of cream accurately. One UK tablespoon of cream weighs roughly 15g, so 250g would be about 16.5 tablespoons. Alternatively, 250ml of cream is close to 250g, as cream has a similar density to water. However, this method isn’t foolproof, as thicker creams may vary slightly. For the most accurate results, we recommend using a digital scale or referring to our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe section</a> for measured ingredients.</p>  

<h3>Using measuring cups for 250g of cream</h3>  
<p>If you’re using a measuring cup, 250g of cream is roughly 1 cup plus 1 tablespoon. This works for most standard double creams, but lighter creams may differ slightly. Always check the packaging for specific weight-to-volume conversions, as brands can vary. For air fryer recipes, where consistency is key, weighing ingredients is the best way to ensure perfect results every time.</p>
