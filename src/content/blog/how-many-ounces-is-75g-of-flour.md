<h3>The science behind flour measurements</h3>
<p>Flour's weight varies based on humidity, packing density, and even how you scoop it. That's why 75g of sifted flour takes up more volume than 75g of compacted flour, though both weigh the same. In ounces, this remains 2.65 oz regardless of volume. For consistent air fryer results, always weigh rather than use cup measurements.</p>

<h2>How to measure 75g of flour without scales</h2>
<p>While we recommend scales for accuracy, you can approximate 75g of flour using household items. One slightly heaped UK metric cup (250ml) of unsifted flour equals roughly 150g, so half would be close to 75g (2.65 oz). For better accuracy, fluff the flour with a fork before gently spooning it into the cup without packing it down.</p>
