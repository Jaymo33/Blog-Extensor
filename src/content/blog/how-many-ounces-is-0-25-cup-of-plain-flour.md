<p>0.25 cup of plain flour equals approximately 1.06 ounces (30 grams). This conversion is essential for accurate baking and air frying, ensuring your recipes turn out perfectly every time. Whether you're whipping up a cake or coating chicken for the air fryer, knowing the exact weight of your flour makes all the difference.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small measurement errors can lead to dry cakes, dense bread, or unevenly cooked coatings in your air fryer. Using weight (ounces or grams) instead of volume (cups) eliminates inconsistencies caused by how densely the flour is packed. For air fryer recipes, especially coatings or batters, precise flour measurements ensure crispy, golden results without sogginess.</p>

<h3>How to measure 0.25 cup of plain flour correctly</h3>  
<p>If you don't have scales, use the spoon-and-level method: lightly spoon flour into your measuring cup without packing it down, then level off the excess with a knife. This prevents over-measuring, which is common when scooping directly from the bag. Remember, 0.25 cup is half of a 1/2 cup measure or four tablespoons.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 0.25 cup plain flour in air fryers</h2>  
<p>This small quantity of flour is perfect for light coatings on chicken wings, fish fillets, or vegetable fritters. It's also ideal for thickening sauces or creating crispy layers in <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken recipes</a>. When combined with spices, 0.25 cup flour makes enough coating for 2-3 servings.</p>  

<p>For sweet treats, this amount works well in small-batch cookies or as a dusting for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>air fryer desserts</a> like churros. The air fryer's rapid circulation ensures even cooking, but only if your flour proportions are spot-on. Too much flour leads to dryness; too little won't provide proper structure.</p>  

<h3>Converting other flour amounts to ounces</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for different quantities, here's a quick reference: 1/8 cup = 0.53 oz, 1/2 cup = 2.13 oz, and 1 cup = 4.25 oz. <h2>Tips for working with plain flour in air fryers</h2>  
<p>Always preheat your air fryer when using flour coatings - this prevents sticking and promotes even crisping. For extra crunch, mix flour with breadcrumbs or cornstarch. After cooking, promptly <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>clean your air fryer</a> to prevent flour residue from hardening.</p>
