<p>1.75 cups of golden syrup equals approximately 21 ounces (oz). This conversion is essential for precise measurements in air fryer recipes, ensuring your dishes turn out perfectly every time. Golden syrup, a staple in British baking, has a thick consistency, so measuring it accurately is crucial for achieving the right texture and flavour.</p>

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>When using an air fryer, precise measurements can make or break your recipe. Too much golden syrup can lead to overly sticky or caramelised results, while too little might leave your dish lacking in sweetness. Whether you're making sticky toffee pudding or glazing roasted vegetables, knowing how many ounces are in 1.75 cups of golden syrup ensures consistency. For more air fryer tips, check out <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>our conversion chart</a>.</p>

<p>Golden syrup is denser than water, so volume-to-weight conversions aren’t straightforward. A standard cup holds 8 fluid ounces, but golden syrup weighs more due to its viscosity. This is why converting 1.75 cups of golden syrup to ounces requires a bit of calculation. If you're working on a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, this conversion will come in handy.</p>

<h2>How to measure golden syrup for air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Measuring golden syrup can be tricky because of its sticky nature. To get an accurate 1.75 cups, lightly grease your measuring cup with oil or warm it slightly to help the syrup slide out easily. This method ensures you’re not leaving half the syrup stuck to the cup, which could throw off your measurements. <h3>Using a kitchen scale for precision</h3>
<p>If you want to avoid the mess altogether, a kitchen scale is your best friend. Simply tare your scale with the empty container, then pour golden syrup until it reaches 21 ounces. This method eliminates guesswork and guarantees accuracy, especially for air fryer recipes where precision matters.
