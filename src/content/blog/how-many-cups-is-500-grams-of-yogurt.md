<h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h2>  
<p>It's important to note that UK and US cup sizes differ slightly. A UK cup holds 284 ml, while a US cup is 240 ml. This means 500 grams of yogurt equals about 1.75 UK cups or 2.1 US cups. Always check which measurement system your recipe uses to avoid mistakes.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe page</a>, provide measurements in both grams and cups for convenience. If you're adapting a traditional oven recipe for the air fryer, converting grams to cups ensures the right consistency.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Yogurt Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate measurement, use a kitchen scale to weigh 500 grams of yogurt. If you don't have one, spoon the yogurt into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to over-measuring. Strained yogurt (like Greek yogurt) may require slight adjustments due to its thicker consistency.</p>

<h3>Storing Leftover Yogurt</h3>  
<p>If you don't use all 500 grams of yogurt at once, store the remainder in an airtight container in the fridge. Yogurt stays fresh for up to two weeks, but always check for signs of spoilage before using. For longer storage, consider freezing it in portioned cups for future recipes.</p>
