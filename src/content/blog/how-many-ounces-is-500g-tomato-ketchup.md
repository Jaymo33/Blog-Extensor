<h2>Why converting 500g of tomato ketchup to ounces matters</h2>
<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those from the UK, use grams for measurements. However, some cooks prefer ounces for precision. Converting 500g of tomato ketchup to ounces (17.64 oz) ensures you use the right amount. This is crucial for sauces, marinades, or glazes in dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken</a>. A slight miscalculation can alter the flavour or texture of your meal.</p>
<p>For example, if a recipe calls for 500g of tomato ketchup and you’re using a scale set to ounces, you’ll need to measure 17.64 oz. This avoids over- or under-seasoning your dish. <h2>How to measure 500g of tomato ketchup in ounces</h2>
<p>To measure 500g of tomato ketchup in ounces, you can use a kitchen scale or measuring cups. A digital scale set to ounces will give you the most accurate result (17.64 oz). If you don’t have a scale, 500g of tomato ketchup is roughly 1.1 cups, but this can vary slightly due to density. For best results, stick to weight measurements.</p>
<p>When preparing sauces for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>air fryer vegetables</a>, precision ensures even coating. Too much ketchup can make the dish soggy, while too little may leave it dry. Always double-check your conversions to avoid mistakes.</p>

<h3>Common uses for 500g of tomato ketchup in air fryer recipes</h3>
<p>500g of tomato ketchup is often used in marinades, glazes, or dipping sauces for air fryer dishes. For instance, it’s a key ingredient in BBQ-style <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/beef>air fryer beef</a> or homemade burgers. The 17.64-ounce measurement ensures consistency across batches. You can also use it for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> like sweet and sour chicken or meatloaf.</p>

<h2>Tips for storing tomato ketchup after measuring</h2>
<p>After measuring 500g (17.64 oz) of tomato ketchup, store any leftovers properly. Transfer unused ketchup to an airtight container and refrigerate. This prevents spoilage and maintains freshness for future use. Always check the expiry date on the bottle, especially if you’re using it for meal prep.</p>
