The .87 represents a fraction of a pound. Since there are 16 ounces in a pound, you multiply 0.87 by 16 to find the ounces. 0.87 x 16 = 13.92 ounces. <h2>Tips for handling large quantities in your air fryer</h2>
<p>Working with a significant amount of food, like 26.25 kg (or 57.87 pounds), requires a bit of strategy for air frying. The golden rule is to cook in batches. Even the largest family-sized air fryers cannot handle this much weight at once. Cooking in single layers ensures hot air can circulate freely around each piece of food, giving you that signature crispy texture. While the first batch is cooking, you can keep the remaining food chilled to maintain food safety and prevent it from getting warm and soggy before it even hits the basket.</p>
<p>Another great tip is to keep your air fryer clean between batches. A build-up of grease or crumbs from the first batch can smoke or burn during the second, affecting the flavour of your food. Giving the basket a quick wipe with a <a href="https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner">suitable air fryer cleaner</a> between loads is a small step that makes a big difference. Preheating your air fryer is also recommended when cooking multiple batches, as it helps maintain a consistent temperature and reduces overall cooking time. Planning your cooking process is just as important as the conversion itself.</p>
