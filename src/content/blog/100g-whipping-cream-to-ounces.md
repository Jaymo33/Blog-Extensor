<p>100g of whipping cream is approximately 3.53 ounces. <h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of whipping cream can make or break a recipe. In an air fryer, ingredients cook quickly, so even small measurement errors can lead to dry or overly dense results. For example, adding too much whipping cream to a cake batter might make it soggy, while too little could leave it dry. That's why knowing that 100g of whipping cream equals roughly 3.53 ounces is so helpful.</p>

Whether you're making a creamy pasta sauce or a light mousse, accuracy is key.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Whipping Cream Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 100g of whipping cream using volume. One tablespoon of whipping cream weighs about 15g, so roughly 6.5 tablespoons will give you 100g (or 3.53 ounces). However, this method is less precise, so for best results, invest in a digital scale. Many affordable options are available, and they're perfect for air fryer cooking where precision matters.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Whipping Cream</h2>  
<p>Once opened, whipping cream should be used within a few days. Store it in the coldest part of your fridge, and always check for spoilage before use. If you're prepping ingredients ahead for an air fryer meal, measure out your 100g (3.53 ounces) in advance and keep it sealed to maintain freshness.</p>  

<p>For longer storage, whipping cream can be frozen, though it may separate when thawed. If you're planning to use it in cooked dishes, this isn't usually a problem. Just be sure to give it a good stir before adding it to your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> creations.</p>
