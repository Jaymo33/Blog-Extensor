<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Too much harissa paste might overpower the dish, while too little could leave it bland. Using 16 ounces (2 cups) of harissa paste ensures balanced flavours, especially in recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>spicy air-fried chicken</a>. Precision also helps when scaling recipes up or down. For example, halving a recipe would mean using 8 ounces (1 cup) instead. Always measure ingredients like harissa paste by weight for the best results.</p>  

<p>Harissa paste is dense, so volume measurements (like cups) can be tricky. A kitchen scale is the most reliable tool for converting 2 cups of harissa paste to ounces. If you don’t have one, spoon the paste into a measuring cup and level it off. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to over-measuring. For more tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<h2>How to use harissa paste in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Harissa paste adds a smoky, spicy flavour to dishes, making it a favourite for air fryer cooking. Mix 16 ounces (2 cups) with olive oil for a marinade, or toss vegetables in it before air frying. It’s also great for coating proteins like chicken or tofu. For inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>harissa chicken recipes</a>. Remember, a little goes a long way—start with a small amount and adjust to taste.</p>  

<h3>Converting harissa paste for UK measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, recipes often use grams or ounces, so converting 2 cups of harissa paste to ounces is handy. As a rule, 1 cup of harissa paste weighs about 8 ounces, so 2 cups equal 16 ounces. This conversion works for most thick pastes and sauces. If you’re using a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, check whether it lists weights or volumes to avoid confusion.</p>  

<h2>Common mistakes when measuring harissa paste</h2>  
<p>One mistake is using liquid measuring cups for thick pastes like harissa, which can lead to inaccuracies. Instead, use dry measuring cups or a scale. Another error is forgetting to account for the paste’s density—16 ounces of harissa paste won’t fill 2 cups the same way water would. Always level off the top for consistency. <h3>Storing leftover harissa paste</h3>  
<p>If you have leftover harissa paste after measuring out 16 ounces (2 cups), store it in an airtight container in the fridge. It typically lasts up to a month.
