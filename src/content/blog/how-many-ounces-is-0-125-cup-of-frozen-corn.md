<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on hot air circulation, so ingredient quantities directly impact cooking times and textures. Too much frozen corn can lead to uneven crisping, while too little may dry out. Measuring 0.125 cup of frozen corn in ounces ensures consistency, especially for recipes like corn fritters or vegetable mixes. <p>Frozen corn retains moisture differently than fresh, so weight measurements (like ounces) are more reliable than volume. A kitchen scale is ideal, but if you’re using cups, 0.125 cup translates to roughly 1 ounce. This precision is key for dishes like air-fried cornbread or cheesy corn bites. <h3>Converting 0.125 cup frozen corn to ounces</h3>  
<p>While 0.125 cup of frozen corn equals about 1 ounce, slight variations exist based on kernel size and packing density. For UK measurements, 1 ounce is 28.35 grams, but most recipes round down to 28 grams for simplicity. If your air fryer recipe calls for 0.125 cup of frozen corn, you can confidently use 1 ounce instead.</p>

<h3>Tips for cooking frozen corn in an air fryer</h3>  
<p>For best results, toss 0.125 cup (1 ounce) of frozen corn with a teaspoon of oil before air frying. Spread it evenly in the basket to prevent clumping. Cook at 190°C for 5–7 minutes, shaking halfway. This method yields lightly charred, sweet kernels—ideal for salads or as a side.</p>
