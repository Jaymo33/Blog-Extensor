<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of beef stock can make or break a recipe, particularly in an air fryer where evaporation happens quickly. Too little liquid, and your dish may dry out; too much, and it could become soggy. Converting 0.25 cups of beef stock to ml (59 ml) ensures consistency, whether you're following a British or American recipe. For more handy conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Beef stock adds depth to dishes like air-fried stews, casseroles, or even marinades. If you're working with 0.25 cups of beef stock, measuring it precisely in ml helps maintain the intended flavour balance. Many air fryer recipes, especially those from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, rely on accurate liquid measurements for perfect results.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.25 Cups of Beef Stock Without a Measuring Cup</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup handy, you can use a standard metric measuring jug to measure 59 ml of beef stock. Alternatively, a tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so four tablespoons will give you roughly 0.25 cups (59 ml). For sticky or thick beef stock, lightly grease the spoon or cup for easier pouring and cleaning—a tip we often share in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guides</a>.</p>  

<h2>Converting 0.25 Cups of Beef Stock for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>Whether you're making a sauce, soup, or marinade, 0.25 cups of beef stock (59 ml) is a common measurement in many recipes. If scaling up, remember that 1 cup equals 237 ml, so doubling 0.25 cups would give you 0.5 cups (118 ml). For more recipe ideas using beef stock, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/beef>beef recipe category</a>.</p>  

<p>Some recipes may call for reduced beef stock, where 0.25 cups (59 ml) simmers down to a more concentrated flavour. In an air fryer, reducing liquids isn’t always practical, so pre-measuring ensures you get the right intensity. Always taste and adjust seasonings after adding beef stock, as brands vary in saltiness.</p>  

<h3>Does the Type of Beef Stock Affect the Measurement?</h3>  
<p>Homemade, store-bought, or powdered beef stock all measure the same—0.25 cups equals 59 ml. However, thicker or gelatinous stocks may require stirring before measuring for accuracy. If using powdered stock, dissolve it fully in water first, then measure out 0.25 cups (59 ml) for consistent results in your air fryer dishes.</p>
