<p>635 ml of condensed milk is approximately 2.68 cups in the US measurement system and roughly 2.23 cups in the UK metric cup system. Whether you're baking a cake or whipping up a dessert in your air fryer, knowing this conversion ensures your recipes turn out perfectly every time. <h2>Understanding Condensed Milk Measurements</h2>
<p>Condensed milk is a staple in many desserts, from fudgy brownies to creamy custards. Its thick, sweet consistency means accurate measurements are crucial. If you're using an air fryer for baking, even small measurement errors can affect texture and cooking times. A 635 ml tin of condensed milk is a common size in the UK, but recipes often call for cup measurements, which can vary between countries.</p>
<p>For US bakers, 1 cup equals 240 ml, while UK metric cups are slightly larger at 250 ml. This means 635 ml converts to about 2.68 US cups or 2.23 UK cups. If you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, always check whether it uses US or UK measurements to avoid mishaps.</p>

<h3>Why Accuracy Matters in Air Fryer Baking</h3>
<p>Air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, which means ingredients cook faster and more evenly than in conventional ovens. This makes precise measurements even more critical. Too much condensed milk can make desserts overly dense, while too little might leave them dry. For perfect results every time, use a kitchen scale or measuring jug alongside cup conversions.</p>

<h2>Converting 635 ml of Condensed Milk for Recipes</h2>
<p>Many classic recipes, like banoffee pie or millionaire's shortbread, call for a full tin of condensed milk. Knowing that 635 ml equals roughly 2.68 US cups helps when scaling recipes up or down. If you're experimenting with air fryer desserts, this conversion ensures consistency across batches.</p>
<p>For example, if a recipe on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>our dessert page</a> requires 1.5 cups of condensed milk, you'll know that's about half a 635 ml tin. This precision is especially handy when making smaller portions in your air fryer, where space is limited.</p>

<h3>Handy Tips for Measuring Condensed Milk</h3>
<p>Condensed milk is sticky and dense, making it tricky to measure. To get an accurate 635 ml conversion, lightly grease your measuring cup or spoon with oil first. This helps the milk slide out cleanly, leaving no residue behind. For air fryer recipes, consider using a silicone spatula to scrape every last bit into your mix.</p>
