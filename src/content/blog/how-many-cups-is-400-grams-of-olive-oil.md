<h2>Why Accurate Olive Oil Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of olive oil in your air fryer ensures even cooking and prevents dryness or sogginess. Too little oil, and your food may stick or lack crispiness; too much, and it can become greasy. For recipes like roasted vegetables or crispy chicken, 400 grams of olive oil (about 1.8 cups) is often ideal. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient swaps.</p>  

<p>Olive oil is a staple in Mediterranean and healthy cooking, and its density affects volume-to-weight conversions. Unlike water, olive oil has a different density, meaning 400 grams won’t equal the same volume in cups. This is why weighing ingredients, especially oils, is more precise than relying solely on cup measurements.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 400 Grams of Olive Oil Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 400 grams of olive oil using standard cups. One cup of olive oil weighs approximately 224 grams, so 400 grams is just under 2 cups. For greater accuracy, use a liquid measuring cup with clear markings. This method works well for air fryer recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>roasted vegetables</a> or homemade fries.</p>
