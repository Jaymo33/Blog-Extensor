<h3>How to Measure 100g of Butter Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don't have scales, you can still measure 100g of butter using tablespoons. One tablespoon of butter weighs about 14g, so 100g is roughly 7 tablespoons. This method isn't as precise as weighing, but it works in a pinch. For best results, we recommend using digital scales, especially for air fryer recipes where consistency matters.</p>

<h2>Tips for Working with Butter in the Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Butter can sometimes cause smoke in the air fryer if it drips onto the heating element. To prevent this, use liners or parchment paper. <p>For baking, softened butter blends better with other ingredients, ensuring even distribution in batters and doughs. If you're making a savoury dish, melted butter works well for coating vegetables or meats before air frying. Remember, 100g of butter equals 3.53 ounces, so adjust recipes accordingly.</p>  

<h3>Butter Alternatives for Air Fryer Cooking</h3>
