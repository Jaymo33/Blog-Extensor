<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>

<h3>How to Measure Minced Garlic Correctly</h3>  
<p>When measuring minced garlic, it’s best to use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy. Pack the garlic lightly to avoid air gaps, but don’t compress it too tightly. If you’re converting from cups to ounces, a kitchen scale is your best friend—especially for air fryer cooking, where precision matters.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Garlic Measurements</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for cloves instead of cups, remember that one medium garlic clove yields about 0.5 teaspoon of minced garlic. For larger batches, 0.33 cup (2.64 oz) is equivalent to roughly 16 cloves. This conversion is handy when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing Minced Garlic</h2>  
<p>If you’ve minced extra garlic, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze it in ice cube trays—each cube holds about 0.33 cup (2.64 oz). This way, you’ll always have pre-measured garlic ready for your next air fryer adventure.</p>
