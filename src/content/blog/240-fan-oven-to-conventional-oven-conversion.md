<h2>Why Convert 240 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air, cooking food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens. This means recipes designed for conventional ovens need adjustment when using a fan oven. The general rule is to reduce the temperature by 20°C when converting from fan to conventional. For example, 240 fan oven to conventional oven becomes 220°C. This adjustment prevents overcooking and ensures your dishes turn out perfectly every time.</p>
<p>Many modern recipes specify fan oven temperatures, but older cookbooks or international recipes might use conventional oven settings. Knowing how to convert 240 fan oven to celsius in conventional terms is especially useful for air fryer users. Air fryers work similarly to fan ovens, so these conversions apply directly. <h2>How to Apply 240 Fan Oven Conversion</h2>
<p>When you see a recipe calling for 240°C in a fan oven, simply set your conventional oven to 220°C. This conversion works for most baked goods, from cakes to roasts. However, some delicate items like soufflés or meringues might need further adjustment. Always monitor your food during cooking, as oven performance can vary. The 240 fan oven to conventional oven conversion is particularly useful for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> where precise temperatures matter.</p>
