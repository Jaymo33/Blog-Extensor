<h3>How to Measure 75g of Gelatin Powder</h3>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure 75g of gelatin powder. If your scale displays ounces, you’ll see it’s roughly 2.65 oz. For those without a scale, a standard tablespoon holds about 7g of gelatin powder, meaning 75g is roughly 10.5 tablespoons. However, weighing is always more precise.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Gelatin Amounts</h3>  
<p>If you need to convert different amounts of gelatin powder, the same rule applies: 1g equals roughly 0.035 ounces. So, 50g would be 1.76 oz, and 100g would be 3.53 oz. Keeping a conversion chart handy can save time and prevent mistakes in the kitchen.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Working with Gelatin Powder</h2>  
<p>Always bloom gelatin powder in cold water before using it in recipes. This means sprinkling the powder over water and letting it absorb for 5–10 minutes. Once bloomed, gently heat it to dissolve completely. This step ensures smooth, lump-free results in your dishes.</p>  
<p>For cleaning up after working with gelatin, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner guide</a> has helpful tips. Gelatin can be sticky, so quick cleanup prevents residue buildup in your air fryer or kitchen tools.</p>  

<h3>Storing Gelatin Powder Properly</h3>  
<p>To keep gelatin powder fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Exposure to moisture can cause clumping, making it harder to measure accurately. Proper storage ensures your 75g (2.65 oz) portions remain consistent for future recipes.</p>
