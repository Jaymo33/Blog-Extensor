<p>Air fryers rely on hot air circulation to cook food, so using the right amount of oil is key. Too much oil can make food greasy, while too little may leave it dry. Measuring 0.25 tbsp of vegetable oil in grams (3.7g) ensures you hit that sweet spot. Many air fryer recipes, like those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipes page</a>, specify oil in grams for accuracy.</p>

<h3>How to measure 0.25 tbsp vegetable oil without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have digital scales, you can still measure 0.25 tbsp of vegetable oil accurately. Use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and fill it to one-quarter capacity. Alternatively, use a 1/4 tsp measure and fill it completely. This roughly equals 3.7 grams of vegetable oil, perfect for coating air fryer baskets or tossing with vegetables.</p>
