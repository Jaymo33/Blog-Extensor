<h3>How to Measure Heavy Cream Correctly</h3>  
<p>When measuring heavy cream, always use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy. Fill it to the 3.0-cup mark, or pour 710 ml if using a metric scale. Avoid packing or compressing the cream, as this can lead to incorrect measurements. For best results, measure at eye level to ensure precision.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Cup Measurements to ml</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of heavy cream, here’s a quick reference: 1 cup equals 237 ml, 2 cups equal 473 ml, and 4 cups equal 946 ml. Keeping these conversions handy ensures you’re never caught off guard in the kitchen.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing and Using Heavy Cream</h2>  
<p>Heavy cream should be stored in the fridge and used within a week of opening. If you’ve measured out 3.0 cups (710 ml) but don’t need it all, freeze the excess in an airtight container for up to three months. Thaw it in the fridge before using it in your next air fryer creation.</p>

<p>If you’re out of heavy cream, you can use alternatives like coconut cream or evaporated milk. However, these substitutes may alter the texture and flavour of your dish. For best results, stick to the original measurement of 3.0 cups (710 ml) of heavy cream when possible.</p>
