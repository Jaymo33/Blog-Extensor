<h3>How to Measure 0.375 Cup of Flaxseed Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 0.375 cup of flaxseed accurately. Use a standard measuring cup and fill it halfway, then remove a tablespoon to reach the 0.375 cup mark. This method ensures you’re close to the 1.5-ounce weight needed for recipes. <h2>Common Uses for Flaxseed in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Flaxseed adds a nutty flavour and crunchy texture to air-fried dishes. From coating chicken tenders to mixing into homemade granola, 0.375 cup (1.5 oz) of flaxseed can elevate your cooking. It’s also a great egg substitute in vegan recipes—just mix with water to create a gel-like consistency. For inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> featuring flaxseed.</p>  

<p>Another popular use is in energy bites or protein bars, where 0.375 cup of flaxseed provides fibre and omega-3s. The air fryer can toast the seeds lightly, enhancing their natural flavour. Just spread them evenly in the basket and cook at 160°C for 3–4 minutes. Always let them cool before adding to recipes to avoid clumping.</p>  

<h3>Storing Flaxseed for Freshness</h3>  
<p>To keep your flaxseed fresh, store it in an airtight container in the fridge or freezer. Ground flaxseed spoils faster, so measure 0.375 cup (1.5 oz) as needed rather than in bulk. Proper storage ensures your air fryer dishes taste their best every time.
