<h2>Why accurate hoisin sauce measurements matter</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of hoisin sauce ensures your dishes aren’t too salty or overly sweet. In an air fryer, precise measurements help achieve even cooking and perfect texture. Too much sauce can make food soggy, while too little might leave it bland. Hoisin sauce is thick and sticky, so measuring by weight (ounces) is often more reliable than volume (cups). If you’re experimenting with new recipes, start with 10 ounces for 1.25 cups and adjust to taste. For recipe ideas, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipe collection</a>.</p>

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring hoisin sauce</h3>
<p>One mistake is scooping hoisin sauce directly from the jar with a measuring cup, which can lead to uneven amounts. Instead, use a spoon to transfer the sauce into the cup for accuracy. Another error is not levelling off the cup, resulting in excess sauce. For sticky ingredients like hoisin, lightly greasing the measuring cup helps with clean pouring. <h2>How to use hoisin sauce in your air fryer</h2>
<p>Hoisin sauce adds depth to air-fried dishes like chicken wings, pork ribs, or tofu. For a glaze, mix 10 ounces (1.25 cups) of hoisin with garlic, ginger, and a splash of rice vinegar. Brush it onto food during the last few minutes of cooking for a caramelised finish. Marinades benefit from accurate measurements too—too much hoisin can overpower other flavours.
