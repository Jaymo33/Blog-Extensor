<h2>How to Measure 75 Grams of Chicken Breast in Cups</h2>  
<p>The exact cup equivalent depends on how the chicken is prepared. Diced chicken breast fills about 0.4 cups, while shredded chicken may measure closer to 0.3 cups due to air pockets. Always lightly pack shredded chicken for consistent measurements.</p>  

<h3>Using a Kitchen Scale for Precision</h3>  
<p>For absolute accuracy, weigh your chicken breast before dicing or shredding. Place a bowl on your kitchen scale, tare it to zero, then add chicken until it reads 75 grams. This method eliminates guesswork and ensures perfect portions for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>.</p>  

<h2>Converting 75g Chicken Breast for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>When adapting recipes from grams to cups, remember that 75 grams of chicken breast equals about one small chicken breast half. This portion works well for single servings in the air fryer, whether you're making crispy tenders or adding protein to salads.</p>  
<p>For meal prep enthusiasts, knowing that 75 grams equals roughly 0.3-0.4 cups helps when dividing bulk-cooked chicken into individual portions. Store measured amounts in containers for easy grab-and-go lunches all week.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times for 75 Grams of Chicken</h3>  
<p>In an air fryer, 75 grams of chicken breast typically cooks in 10-12 minutes at 180°C. Smaller pieces cook faster than whole breasts, so adjust times accordingly. Always check internal temperature reaches 74°C for food safety.</p>
