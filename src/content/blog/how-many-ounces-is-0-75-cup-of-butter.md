<h2>Why accurate butter measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of butter is crucial, especially in air fryer recipes where space and heat distribution are limited. <h2>How to measure 0.75 cup of butter</h2>  
<p>Measuring 0.75 cup of butter can be tricky if you don’t have a measuring cup handy. One easy way is to use the markings on the butter wrapper. A standard UK block of butter is 250g, which is roughly 1 cup or 8 ounces. So, 0.75 cup would be three-quarters of that block, equalling 6 ounces.</p>  
<p>If you’re using a scale, simply weigh out 6 ounces of butter for 0.75 cup. This method is especially useful for air fryer recipes where precision is key. For more tips on measuring ingredients, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> where butter measurements are often critical.</p>  

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring butter</h3>  
<p>One common mistake is confusing weight and volume. Ounces can refer to both, but in this case, we’re talking about weight (6 ounces). Another pitfall is not accounting for the butter’s temperature—softened butter packs differently than cold butter. Always measure 0.75 cup of butter as it’s intended in the recipe.</p>  

<h2>Converting 0.75 cup of butter to other units</h2>  
<p>If you need to convert 0.75 cup of butter to grams, it’s approximately 170g. This is useful for UK bakers who often use metric measurements. For tablespoons, 0.75 cup of butter equals 12 tablespoons, which can be handy for smaller air fryer recipes. <h3>Butter substitutes in air fryer recipes</h3>  
<p>If you’re out of butter, you can use margarine or coconut oil as a substitute for 0.75 cup of butter. Just keep in mind that the texture and flavour might vary slightly. For best results, stick to butter unless the recipe specifies otherwise.</p>
