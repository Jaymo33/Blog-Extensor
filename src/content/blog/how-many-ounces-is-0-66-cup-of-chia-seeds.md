<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can throw off texture and taste. Chia seeds, for instance, absorb liquid and expand, so using the right amount is crucial. <h3>How to measure 0.66 cup of chia seeds accurately</h3>  
<p>Use a dry measuring cup for chia seeds, levelling off the top with a straight edge for accuracy. If you don’t have a 0.66 cup measure, fill a 1/3 cup twice to reach the correct amount. For those who prefer weighing, 3.17 ounces is your target. A kitchen scale eliminates guesswork, especially for air fryer recipes where precision matters.</p>

<p>Chia seeds are versatile and work well in both sweet and savoury air fryer dishes. Try them in granola clusters, as a binding agent for veggie burgers, or sprinkled over air-fried fruit crisps. <h3>Converting chia seeds between cups and ounces</h3>  
<p>While 0.66 cup equals 3.17 ounces, it’s helpful to know other common conversions. One full cup of chia seeds weighs roughly 4.8 ounces, so halving that gives you 2.4 ounces for 0.5 cup. These conversions are handy when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer.</p>  

<h2>Tips for storing chia seeds</h2>  
<p>Keep chia seeds in an airtight container in a cool, dark place to maintain freshness. They can last up to two years if stored properly. For bulk purchases, divide them into smaller portions to avoid spoilage. Proper storage ensures your 0.66 cup measurements remain consistent in weight and quality.</p>
