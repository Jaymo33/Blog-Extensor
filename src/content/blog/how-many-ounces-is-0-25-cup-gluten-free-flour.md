<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Gluten-free flour behaves differently than regular flour, making precise measurements crucial. Too much or too little can affect the texture of your air-fried goods. For example, 0.25 cup of gluten-free flour to ounces ensures your batter isn’t too thick or runny. Many air fryer recipes, like those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe page</a>, rely on exact conversions for consistent results. Always measure carefully to avoid dense cakes or crumbly coatings.</p>  

<p>Using a kitchen scale is the most reliable way to measure 0.25 cup of gluten-free flour in ounces. Scooping flour directly from the bag can lead to compacted measurements, throwing off your recipe. Instead, spoon the flour into your measuring cup and level it off for accuracy. If you’re converting 0.25 cups of gluten-free flour to oz UK, remember that UK and US cup sizes are identical, so no adjustment is needed.</p>
