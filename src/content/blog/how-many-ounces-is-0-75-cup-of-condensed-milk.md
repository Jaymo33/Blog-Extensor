<p>0.75 cup of condensed milk equals approximately 6 ounces (or 6 oz) in UK measurements. Whether you're prepping a dessert or adjusting a recipe for your air fryer, this conversion ensures accuracy. Condensed milk is a staple in many treats, from fudgy brownies to creamy cheesecakes, and getting the measurement right is key to success.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Precision is crucial when working with air fryers because they cook food quickly and evenly. Too much or too little condensed milk can throw off the texture of your dish. For example, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>popular air fryer cheesecake</a> relies on the perfect balance of ingredients to set properly. Measuring 0.75 cup of condensed milk correctly ensures your desserts turn out just right.</p>  

<p>If you're new to air fryer baking, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>handy conversion chart</a> can save time. Many UK recipes use metric measurements, but some still reference cups, making conversions essential. Knowing that 0.75 cup of condensed milk is 6 ounces helps streamline your prep work.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Condensed Milk Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy. Pour the condensed milk slowly to avoid air bubbles, and level it off with a spatula. For sticky ingredients like condensed milk, lightly greasing the measuring cup can help it slide out easily. This method ensures you get exactly 0.75 cup (or 6 oz) every time.</p>  

<h2>Common Recipes Using 0.75 Cup Condensed Milk</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer desserts call for condensed milk, including fudge, flans, and no-bake pies. A <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>decadent chocolate fudge</a> might require 0.75 cup (6 oz) of condensed milk for the perfect creamy texture. Similarly, air fryer caramel slices often use this measurement for the filling.</p>  

<p>Condensed milk also works wonders in savoury dishes, like glazes for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air-fried chicken wings</a>. The sweetness balances spicy or smoky flavours, creating a crowd-pleasing dish. Always measure carefully to avoid overpowering other ingredients.</p>  

<h3>Converting Other Condensed Milk Measurements</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount, here’s a quick reference: 1 cup of condensed milk equals 8 oz, while 0.5 cup is 4 oz. Bookmarking a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion guide</a> can simplify future baking sessions. Remember, precise measurements lead to consistent results in air fryer cooking.</p>  

<h2>Storing Leftover Condensed Milk</h2>  
<p>After measuring 0.75 cup (6 oz) for your recipe, store leftovers in an airtight container. Refrigerated condensed milk lasts up to two weeks. For longer storage, freeze it in portions—thaw in the fridge before using. Proper storage ensures you always have condensed milk ready for your next <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>air fryer recipe</a>.</p>  

<p>To prevent waste, label containers with the date and amount. For example, note “4 oz remaining” if you’ve used half a cup. This makes it easier to adjust recipes later.
