<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so using the right amount of ingredients is crucial. Too much balsamic vinegar can make your dish overly acidic or soggy, while too little might leave it bland. Measuring 1.5 cups of balsamic vinegar correctly (12 oz) ensures balanced flavours. If you're new to air frying, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> for foolproof ideas.</p>  

<p>Balsamic vinegar is a staple in many air fryer recipes, from glazes to dressings. Its thick consistency means it coats ingredients evenly, enhancing flavour without excess oil. Whether you're cooking salmon or roasting vegetables, 1.5 cups (12 oz) of balsamic vinegar can transform your dish. <h3>How to measure 1.5 cups of balsamic vinegar</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy, filling it to the 1.5-cup mark at eye level. Balsamic vinegar's viscosity means it pours slowly, so take your time. If you don’t have a measuring cup, 12 fluid ounces is roughly 24 tablespoons. Need a quick clean-up tip? Try our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner guide</a> after cooking sticky glazes.</p>

<p>This quantity is perfect for batch cooking or meal prep. Reduce 1.5 cups (12 oz) of balsamic vinegar into a thick glaze for drizzling over air-fried vegetables or proteins. It’s also ideal for marinating tofu or beef before air frying. <h3>Converting 1.5 cups of balsamic vinegar for UK measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, fluid ounces are slightly different (1 UK fl oz = 28.41 ml), but 1.5 US cups still equate to ~12 UK fluid ounces. This minor difference won’t affect most air fryer recipes. Whether you're cooking for one or meal prepping, 1.5 cups (12 oz) of balsamic vinegar is a handy measurement to master.</p>
