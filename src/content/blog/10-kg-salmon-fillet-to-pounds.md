<h2>How to prepare and cook a 10 kg salmon fillet in your air fryer</h2>
<p>Handling a 10 kg salmon fillet to pounds conversion is just the first step; next comes preparation and cooking. Since most home air fryers can't accommodate a whole 22-pound fillet, you'll need to portion it into smaller pieces. Aim for steaks or sections that are about 1-2 inches thick to ensure even cooking. Pat the salmon dry with paper towels to help achieve a crispy skin, and season generously with herbs, lemon, or your favourite spices. Preheating your air fryer is recommended to get that initial sear and lock in moisture. Remember to not overcrowd the basket to allow proper air circulation for that perfect cook.</p>
<p>Cook times will vary based on the thickness of your portions, but a good starting point is 12-15 minutes at 180°C for 1-inch thick pieces. Always check for doneness by ensuring the salmon flakes easily with a fork and has an internal temperature of 63°C. For larger batches, you might need to cook in multiple rounds, keeping finished portions warm in a low oven. Leftover cooked salmon can be refrigerated and used in salads, pastas, or sandwiches throughout the week.
