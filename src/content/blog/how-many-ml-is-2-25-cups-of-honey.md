<p>Using the correct amount of honey in your air fryer recipes ensures balanced flavours and textures. <p>Honey’s viscosity means it doesn’t pour like water, so measuring by volume (cups) can be tricky. Converting to millilitres (ml) provides a more reliable measurement, especially when using scales or liquid measuring tools. This is particularly useful for recipes like honey-mustard chicken or sticky honey ribs, where consistency is key.</p>  

<h2>How to Convert 2.25 Cups of Honey to ml</h2>  
<p>The standard conversion for honey is 1 cup = 236.588 ml. Multiplying this by 2.25 gives you 532.32 ml. However, it’s worth noting that honey’s density can vary slightly depending on temperature and type (e.g., raw, processed). For most cooking purposes, 532 ml is a safe and accurate measurement.</p>

<h3>Tools for Measuring Honey Accurately</h3>  
<p>A liquid measuring cup or a digital kitchen scale is ideal for measuring honey. If using a cup, lightly grease it with oil to help the honey slide out easily. For ml measurements, a graduated measuring jug is your best bet. These tools are especially handy when preparing sticky ingredients like honey for air fryer recipes.</p>

<h2>Tips for Working with Honey in the Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Honey can burn easily in the air fryer due to its high sugar content. To prevent this, mix it with other liquids like soy sauce or vinegar when making glazes. Also, avoid applying honey too early in the cooking process—brush it on during the last few minutes for a perfect finish.
