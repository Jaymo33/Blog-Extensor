Wholemeal flour has a slightly different density compared to plain flour, so measuring by weight ensures consistency in your recipes. Whether you're making air fryer cookies or a healthy loaf, precise measurements matter. <h2>Why measuring wholemeal flour by weight matters</h2>  
<p>Wholemeal flour can vary in density depending on how it's packed, making volume measurements less reliable. Using ounces (or grams) ensures your bakes turn out perfectly every time. This is especially important for air fryer recipes, where even small measurement errors can affect texture. For example, too much flour in air fryer cakes can make them dense. If you're adapting a recipe from cups to weight, always double-check conversions. <h3>How to measure 0.33 cup of wholemeal flour without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don't have kitchen scales, spoon the flour lightly into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to using more flour than intended. For 0.33 cup, you'd fill your cup measure one-third full, ensuring an even distribution. Remember that this method is less precise than weighing, so results may vary slightly. For perfectly measured ingredients every time, consider investing in digital scales – they're a game-changer for air fryer baking.</p>
