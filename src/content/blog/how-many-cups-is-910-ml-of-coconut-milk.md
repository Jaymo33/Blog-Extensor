<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of coconut milk can make or break your dish. Too little, and your recipe might turn out dry; too much, and it could become soggy. This is especially important in air fryer cooking, where precise measurements ensure even cooking. Coconut milk is a staple in many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>, so getting it right is key. Always measure liquids at eye level for accuracy.</p>  

<p>Different brands of coconut milk may vary in thickness, so measuring by volume rather than weight is often more reliable. If you're unsure, shake the can well before measuring. <h2>Converting 910 ml of Coconut Milk to Cups</h2>  
<p>To convert 910 ml of coconut milk to cups, divide the millilitres by 236.59 (the number of ml in a standard US cup). This gives you roughly 3.85 cups. If you're using a UK cup, which holds 284 ml, the conversion would be about 3.2 cups. Always check which cup measurement your recipe uses to avoid mistakes.</p>  

<p>For air fryer recipes, consistency is key. Whether you're making a creamy sauce or a fluffy cake, the right amount of coconut milk ensures the texture is spot on. If you're scaling a recipe up or down, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> has plenty of inspiration.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Measuring Coconut Milk</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for the most accurate results. Pour the coconut milk slowly to avoid spills and check the measurement at eye level. If your recipe calls for a partial cup, like 3.85 cups, you can eyeball it or use tablespoons for precision. One tablespoon is roughly 15 ml, so 910 ml would be about 61 tablespoons.</p>
