<h2>Why accurate powdered milk measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Powdered milk is a staple in many recipes, from baked goods to creamy sauces. Using the correct weight ensures your dish turns out as intended. Too much or too little can alter texture and flavour. For air fryer recipes, precision is key—especially when making batters or coatings. <p>Many UK recipes list powdered milk in grams rather than tablespoons. This avoids discrepancies caused by how densely the powder is packed. A loosely filled tablespoon will weigh less than a tightly packed one. By converting 2.5 tbsp of powdered milk to grams, you eliminate guesswork. This is particularly useful for air fryer cooking, where even small variations can affect results. <h2>How to measure powdered milk correctly</h2>  
<p>To get 18.75 grams of powdered milk, use a digital kitchen scale for the most accurate results. If you don’t have one, lightly spoon the powder into the tablespoon and level it off with a knife. Avoid tapping or packing it down, as this increases the weight. For air fryer recipes like crispy coatings or doughs, consistency is crucial. <h3>Common uses for powdered milk in air fryer cooking</h3>  
<p>Powdered milk is often used in air fryer recipes for breading, batters, and baked goods. It adds richness without the moisture of liquid milk, ensuring crispier results. For example, a light dusting of powdered milk can enhance the crunch of fried chicken or fish. It’s also great for making homemade seasoning blends.
