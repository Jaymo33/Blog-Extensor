<h2>Understanding Gas Mark and Fahrenheit</h2>
<p>Gas Mark is a temperature scale used in the UK, while Fahrenheit is more common in the US. Converting between them is essential for following recipes accurately. 356°F sits between Gas Mark 4 and 5, making it 4.5 for precise measurements. This knowledge is particularly useful when adapting oven recipes for your air fryer, as seen in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>
<p>Many air fryer models don't use Gas Mark, so understanding the Fahrenheit equivalent helps you set the right temperature. For example, if a recipe calls for Gas Mark 4.5, you'll know to set your air fryer to 356°F. This ensures your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes</a> and other baked goods cook evenly without burning or undercooking.</p>

<h2>Why 356°F Matters in Air Fryers</h2>
<p>356°F (Gas Mark 4.5) is a versatile temperature for many air fryer recipes. It's ideal for foods that need gentle cooking without excessive browning. At this temperature, you can perfectly cook items like scones, muffins, or even <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken</a> without drying them out.</p>
<p>The circulating hot air in an air fryer cooks food faster than conventional ovens, so timing adjustments are necessary. When converting from Gas Mark to Fahrenheit for your air fryer, you might need to reduce cooking times by 20-30%.
