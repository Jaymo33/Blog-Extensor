<h2>Why convert 15g of granulated sugar to ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, while some kitchen scales or older cookbooks might list ounces. Converting 15g of granulated sugar to ounces (0.53 oz) helps you follow recipes accurately. This is especially useful for air fryer desserts like cookies or muffins, where sugar ratios affect texture and browning. If you’re exploring our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, precise measurements make all the difference.</p>  

<p>Granulated sugar’s weight can vary slightly by brand, but 15g is consistently close to 0.53 ounces. For air fryer cooking, even small deviations can impact caramelisation or crispiness. <h3>Tools to measure 15g of granulated sugar</h3>  
<p>A digital kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure 15g of granulated sugar (0.53 oz). If you don’t have one, use measuring spoons: roughly 1 tablespoon of granulated sugar equals 12.5g, so you’d need just over 1 tbsp for 15g. For air fryer recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes</a>, precision ensures even cooking and perfect sweetness.</p>

<h3>Converting other sugar amounts</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for 30g, double the 15g-to-ounces conversion to get 1.06 oz.
