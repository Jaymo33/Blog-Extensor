<p>200g of BBQ sauce equals approximately 7.05 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements can make or break your dish. Whether you’re marinating chicken or glazing ribs, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures consistency. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we understand the importance of accurate measurements for perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 200g of BBQ Sauce to Ounces?</h2>
<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those from the UK, use metric measurements like grams. However, some kitchen scales or recipes might require ounces. Converting 200g of BBQ sauce to ounces helps you follow recipes accurately, whether you’re using a digital scale or measuring cups. This is particularly useful for sauces, where consistency affects cooking times and results.</p>
<p>For example, if you’re making sticky BBQ chicken in your air fryer, the right amount of sauce ensures even coating and caramelisation. Too little, and your dish might lack flavour; too much, and it could become soggy. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement swaps.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 200g of BBQ Sauce</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can still measure 200g of BBQ sauce using volume. Roughly, 200g is about 12 tablespoons or ¾ cup. However, this can vary slightly depending on the sauce’s thickness. For the most accurate results, we recommend using a kitchen scale set to grams or ounces.</p>
<p>When preparing air fryer meals like BBQ pulled pork or glazed salmon, precise measurements ensure even cooking. Visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> for inspiration on how to use your converted 200g of BBQ sauce in delicious dishes.</p>

<h3>Common Uses for 200g of BBQ Sauce</h3>
<p>200g of BBQ sauce is perfect for marinating 4-6 chicken thighs or coating a rack of ribs. It’s also ideal for making BBQ-flavoured meatballs or glazing vegetables like cauliflower or sweet potatoes in the air fryer. The versatility of BBQ sauce makes it a staple in many kitchens, and knowing the ounce equivalent helps streamline your cooking process.</p>

<h2>Tips for Working with BBQ Sauce in the Air Fryer</h2>
<p>BBQ sauce can caramelise quickly in the air fryer, so it’s best to apply it during the last 5-10 minutes of cooking. This prevents burning and ensures a glossy, sticky finish. For dishes like wings or drumsticks, toss them in 200g of BBQ sauce after they’re nearly done, then return them to the air fryer for a final crisp.</p>
