<h2>How to Measure 660 ml of Balsamic Vinegar</h2>
<p>Measuring liquids like balsamic vinegar can be tricky, especially if you don’t have a measuring jug with millilitre markings. Here’s a simple method: use a standard US cup, which holds 240 ml. To measure 660 ml, you’d need just under 2 and 3/4 cups. For UK cooks, a metric cup holds 250 ml, so 660 ml would be roughly 2.64 cups.</p>
<p>If you’re working with smaller quantities, you might need to break it down further. For example, half of 660 ml is 330 ml, which is about 1.4 cups. This level of precision is especially useful for recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken dishes</a>, where balance is key.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Balsamic Vinegar</h3>
<p>Once you’ve measured out your 660 ml of balsamic vinegar, proper storage is key. Keep it in a cool, dark place to preserve its flavour.
