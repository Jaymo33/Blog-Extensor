<p>250 grams of icing sugar is roughly equivalent to 2 cups when lightly spooned into a measuring cup. This conversion is essential for precise baking, especially when using an air fryer where measurements can make or break a recipe. Whether you're making cakes, cookies, or frosting, knowing how many cups is 250 grams of icing sugar ensures consistency in your bakes.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Baking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers require precise ingredient ratios to achieve the perfect texture and rise. Too much or too little icing sugar can affect the moisture and structure of your baked goods. For example, if you're making air fryer cupcakes, 250 grams of icing sugar in cups must be measured correctly to avoid overly dense or dry results.</p>  
<p>Using a kitchen scale is the most accurate method, but if you don’t have one, spooning the sugar lightly into a cup works well. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to using more sugar than needed. <h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>It’s worth noting that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 250 grams of icing sugar equals about 2 cups, while in the US, it’s closer to 2.1 cups due to slight variations in cup sizes. Always double-check your recipe’s origin to ensure accuracy.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 250g of Icing Sugar Without Scales</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, use the spoon-and-level method for the most accurate 250 grams of icing sugar in cups. Gently spoon the sugar into a measuring cup until it’s slightly heaped, then level it off with a straight edge. This prevents compaction and gives you the closest approximation to 250 grams.</p>
