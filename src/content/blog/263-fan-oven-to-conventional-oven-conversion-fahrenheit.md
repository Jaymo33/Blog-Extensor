<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. This means you typically need to reduce the temperature by about 20°F when converting from conventional to fan settings. However, when going from fan to conventional, as with our 263 fan oven to conventional oven conversion, you'll need to increase the temperature. This ensures your food cooks through properly without drying out or burning. Many modern air fryers operate similarly to fan ovens, making this conversion particularly useful for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>.</p>

<p>The science behind this difference lies in heat transfer efficiency. In a fan oven, the moving air transfers heat more effectively to the food's surface. This allows for lower temperature settings while maintaining the same cooking results. When adapting recipes between these oven types, it's crucial to make the correct adjustment to avoid under or overcooking. Our 263 f fan oven to normal oven conversion guide takes the guesswork out of this process.</p>

<h3>How This Conversion Affects Air Fryer Cooking</h3>
<p>Air fryers use rapid air technology similar to fan ovens, but with even more intense air circulation. When converting 263 fan oven to air fryer equivalent temperatures, you might need to reduce the temperature slightly further. Most air fryer recipes recommend reducing conventional oven temperatures by about 25°F, but this can vary by model. Check your air fryer's manual or our comprehensive <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for specific guidance.</p>
