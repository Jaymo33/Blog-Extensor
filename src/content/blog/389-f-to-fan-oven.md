<h2>Understanding Fan Oven Temperatures</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air for faster, more even cooking compared to conventional ovens. When converting from Fahrenheit to Celsius for a fan oven, you typically reduce the temperature by about 20 C from the standard Celsius equivalent. This means 389 F (198 C in a conventional oven) becomes 175 C in a fan oven.</p>
<p>The fan oven's efficiency makes it similar to an air fryer's cooking method. Both use rapid air circulation to cook food quickly and evenly. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we often recommend using fan oven temperatures as a guide for air fryer cooking, especially when adapting traditional recipes.</p>

<h2>Why 389 F Matters for Air Frying</h2>
<p>389 F (175 C fan) is a versatile temperature for many air fryer recipes. It's hot enough to crisp foods like chicken wings or roast vegetables, yet not so high that it burns delicate items. This temperature works particularly well for frozen foods that need thorough cooking without becoming dry.</p>
<p>Many of our favourite <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> use this temperature range. The circulating air at 175 C fan creates perfect crispiness while keeping the meat juicy inside. It's also excellent for reheating leftovers, giving them new life without drying them out.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Fahrenheit Temperatures</h3>
<p>If you frequently work with American recipes, knowing how to convert various Fahrenheit temperatures to fan oven settings is invaluable. The general rule is to subtract 32 from the Fahrenheit number, multiply by 5/9, then subtract another 20 for the fan oven setting. For 389 F, this gives you (389-32)Ã—5/9=198 C, then 198-20=178 C (we round to 175 C for practical use).</p>

When using 389 F (175 C fan) in an air fryer, you might need to reduce cooking times by about 20% compared to oven instructions.</p>

<h3>Tips for Perfect Results at 389 F</h3>
<p>To get the best results when cooking at 389 F in your fan oven or air fryer, always preheat the appliance. Shake or turn food halfway through cooking for even browning. Use a light spray of oil for extra crispiness, especially with vegetables or frozen foods. And don't overcrowd the basket - leaving space for air circulation is crucial.</p>
