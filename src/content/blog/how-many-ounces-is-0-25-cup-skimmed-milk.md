<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on precise measurements to deliver consistent results. Too much or too little liquid, like skimmed milk, can affect texture and cooking times. For example, adding 2 ounces (0.25 cup) of skimmed milk to a batter ensures it’s neither too thick nor too runny. This is especially important for recipes like air fryer pancakes or muffins, where balance is key.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 0.25 Cup of Skimmed Milk</h2>
<p>Measuring 0.25 cup of skimmed milk is simple with the right tools. Use a liquid measuring cup with clear markings, or a digital scale for ounces. Pour the milk slowly to the 0.25 cup line, or weigh it to 2 ounces. This method works for all dairy and non-dairy milks in air fryer recipes.</p>
<p>If you’re scaling up, remember that 1 cup of skimmed milk equals 8 ounces. This makes it easy to adjust recipes like our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer French toast</a> or creamy sauces. For more tips, check out our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning your air fryer</a> after cooking with dairy.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Milk Quantities to Ounces</h3>
<p>Need to convert different amounts of skimmed milk? Here’s a quick reference: 0.5 cup is 4 ounces, and 0.75 cup is 6 ounces. These conversions are useful for larger batches or meal prep. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast recipes</a> often call for precise milk measurements to achieve the perfect consistency.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for 0.25 Cup of Skimmed Milk</h2>
<p>0.25 cup (2 ounces) of skimmed milk is a versatile quantity in air fryer cooking. It’s perfect for lightening batters, adding moisture to baked goods, or creating creamy dips. Try it in air fryer cornbread or as a base for a cheese sauce. The possibilities are endless!</p>
<p>For more inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>, where skimmed milk often plays a key role. From puddings to cakes, knowing how many ounces are in 0.25 cup ensures your treats turn out just right.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Skimmed Milk</h3>
<p>To keep skimmed milk fresh, store it in the coldest part of your fridge and use it within a week of opening. If your recipe calls for 0.25 cup (2 ounces), measure it straight from the fridge for best results. For longer storage, freeze skimmed milk in 2-ounce portions for future air fryer recipes.</p>
