<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of coconut oil ensures your food cooks evenly and achieves the desired crispiness. Too little oil, and your dish might turn out dry; too much, and it could become greasy. Air fryers rely on hot air circulation, so balancing ingredients like coconut oil is crucial. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> often call for precise oil measurements to guarantee consistency.</p>  

<p>Converting 235 ml of coconut oil to cups is straightforward, but it’s worth noting that slight variations can occur based on oil density. For most cooking purposes, 1 cup is a reliable equivalent. If you’re working with a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>, this conversion ensures your cakes or cookies turn out perfectly moist.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Coconut Oil for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>When measuring 235 ml of coconut oil, use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy. If your coconut oil is solid, gently melt it before pouring. This method prevents air gaps and ensures you get the full 1 cup measurement. For more tips, check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a>, which covers common ingredient measurements.</p>
