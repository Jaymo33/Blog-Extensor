<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking powder is a leavening agent, and even small measurement errors can affect texture and rise. Too much can make your baked goods bitter, while too little may leave them dense. For air fryer recipes, where cooking times are shorter, precision is even more critical. <h3>How to Measure 150g of Baking Powder Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use measuring spoons. One teaspoon of baking powder weighs roughly 4g, so 150g would be about 37.5 teaspoons. However, for accuracy, we recommend investing in a digital scale. Air fryer cooking thrives on precision, and eyeballing measurements can lead to uneven results.</p>
