<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but only if ingredients are measured correctly. Too much or too little almond milk can affect texture, moisture, and cooking times. For example, adding extra almond milk to a cake batter might make it too dense, while too little could dry it out. That’s why converting 2.5 tbsp of almond milk to grams (37.5g) ensures consistency.</p>

<h2>How to measure almond milk for air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the easiest way to measure 37.5 grams of almond milk. If you don’t have one, a standard UK tablespoon holds 15ml, so 2.5 tbsp equals 37.5ml. Since almond milk has a similar density to water (1g/ml), this translates neatly to 37.5 grams.</p>  

<h3>Tips for measuring without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you’re using measuring spoons, fill them level for accuracy. Avoid heaping tablespoons, as this can add extra grams unintentionally. For sticky or thicker almond milk varieties (like barista-style), gently stir before measuring to ensure an even consistency.</p>

<h3>Storing almond milk for air fryer recipes</h3>  
<p>Once opened, almond milk lasts about 7–10 days in the fridge. Shake well before each use, as separation is normal. For longer storage, freeze it in ice cube trays—each cube is roughly 15g, so 2.5 cubes replace 2.5 tbsp neatly.</p>
