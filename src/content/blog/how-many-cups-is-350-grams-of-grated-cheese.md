<h2>Why Accurate Cheese Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of grated cheese can make or break a dish. Too little, and your meal lacks flavour; too much, and it can become greasy or unevenly cooked. Whether you're making crispy cheese bites or a cheesy pasta bake, 350 grams of grated cheese translates to about 3.5 cups, but always check your specific cheese type.</p>

<h3>Types of Cheese and Their Cup Equivalents</h3>  
<p>Cheddar, mozzarella, and Parmesan all measure differently due to texture and moisture content. For 350 grams of cheddar, expect around 3.5 cups when grated, while mozzarella may fill closer to 4 cups due to its lightness. Hard cheeses like Parmesan pack more densely, so 350 grams might only fill 3 cups. Always fluff the cheese lightly with a fork before measuring for accuracy.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Grated Cheese Correctly</h2>  
<p>For consistent results, use the spoon-and-level method: lightly spoon grated cheese into a measuring cup without packing it down, then level off the top. Avoid tapping the cup, as this compresses the cheese and skews the measurement. If precision is critical, weigh the cheese directly on a kitchen scale—350 grams is always 350 grams, regardless of volume.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Cheese</h3>  
<p>Overpacking the cup is the most frequent error, leading to more cheese than a recipe calls for. Another pitfall is using pre-shredded cheese, which often contains anti-caking agents and may measure differently. For best results, grate your own cheese and measure it freshly. If you're converting 350 grams of grated cheese to cups, fluff it first to avoid dense clumps.</p>
