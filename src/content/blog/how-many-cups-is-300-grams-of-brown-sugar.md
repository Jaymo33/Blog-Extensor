<p>300 grams of brown sugar equals approximately 1.5 cups when lightly packed. This conversion is essential for baking, especially when using an air fryer, where precise measurements can make or break your recipe. Whether you’re whipping up cookies, cakes, or even savoury dishes, knowing how to convert grams to cups ensures consistent results every time. <h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Baking</h2>
<p>Air fryers cook food faster and more evenly than traditional ovens, so precise measurements are crucial. Too much or too little brown sugar can affect texture, browning, and even cooking times. For example, adding extra sugar might cause your baked goods to brown too quickly or burn. On the other hand, too little sugar can result in dry, crumbly treats. That’s why converting 300 grams of brown sugar to cups correctly is so important. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>

<p>Brown sugar is denser than white sugar, so it packs differently in a measuring cup. Lightly packed brown sugar means filling the cup and gently pressing it down without compacting it too tightly. This method ensures you get the right amount without overloading your recipe. If you’re baking something like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cookies>cookies</a>, this precision can be the difference between chewy perfection and a baking disaster.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 300 Grams of Brown Sugar Without Scales</h3>
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem! You can still measure 300 grams of brown sugar using standard cups. One cup of lightly packed brown sugar weighs around 200 grams, so 1.5 cups will give you roughly 300 grams. Remember, the key is to lightly pack the sugar—don’t press it down too hard, or you’ll end up with more than you need. This trick works perfectly for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cakes</a> and other air fryer desserts.</p>

<h2>Converting 300 Grams of Brown Sugar for Different Recipes</h2>
<p>Whether you’re following a UK or US recipe, the conversion remains the same: 300 grams of brown sugar equals 1.5 cups. However, some recipes might call for firmly packed brown sugar, which can slightly alter the measurement. If you’re unsure, it’s always best to weigh your ingredients for accuracy. For more air fryer-friendly recipes, explore our collection at <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a>.</p>

<p>Brown sugar adds moisture and depth to dishes, making it a staple in many air fryer recipes. From glazes for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken</a> to sweetening desserts, getting the measurement right ensures balanced flavours. If you’re working with frozen ingredients, like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/frozen-foods>frozen foods</a>, brown sugar can also help enhance caramelisation during cooking.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Brown Sugar</h3>
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is scooping brown sugar directly from the bag with a measuring cup. This can lead to uneven packing and inaccurate measurements. Instead, spoon the sugar into the cup and level it off for consistency. Another error is using white sugar measurements interchangeably—brown sugar’s moisture content means it behaves differently in recipes. Always follow the specific instructions for the best results.</p>
