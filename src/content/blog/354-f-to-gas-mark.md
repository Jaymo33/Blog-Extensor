<p>354°F converts to Gas Mark 4 in UK ovens and air fryers. This moderate heat is ideal for baking cakes, roasting vegetables, or cooking casseroles in your air fryer. Understanding temperature conversions helps you adapt recipes from ovens to air fryers successfully.</p>

<h2>Understanding 354 F to Gas Mark Conversion</h2>
<p>The Gas Mark scale is a British temperature measurement system used in ovens. When converting 354 Fahrenheit to Gas Mark, you get Gas Mark 4. This temperature works well for many air fryer recipes that originally specify oven cooking. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we recommend checking conversion charts when trying new recipes.</p>
<p>Gas Mark 4 equals 180°C or 354°F, making it a versatile medium heat setting. Many frozen foods and ready meals suggest cooking at this temperature. If your air fryer uses Gas Mark settings, you'll find this perfect for items like fish fingers or chicken nuggets. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> helps with these adjustments.</p>

<h2>When to Use 354 F (Gas Mark 4) in Your Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Gas Mark 4 (354°F) is ideal for baking and roasting in air fryers. This temperature gently cooks food without burning the outside. It's particularly good for items that need even heating throughout, like cakes or meatloaf. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a> specify this temperature for perfect results.</p>
<p>For frozen foods, 354 F to Gas Mark conversion ensures proper cooking without drying out. The moderate heat allows thorough cooking while maintaining moisture. This setting also works well for reheating leftovers in your air fryer, giving them a crispy finish without overheating.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times at Gas Mark 4</h3>
<p>When using 354 F in your air fryer, cooking times may differ from oven recipes. Air fryers circulate heat more efficiently, often reducing cooking time by 20%. Start checking food a few minutes early when converting oven recipes to Gas Mark 4 in your air fryer.</p>

<h2>Common Foods Cooked at 354 F (Gas Mark 4)</h2>
<p>Many everyday foods cook perfectly at 354 F to Gas Mark 4 in air fryers. This includes roasted vegetables, baked potatoes, and chicken pieces. The temperature works well for items that need gentle, even cooking without excessive browning.</p>
<p>For <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>potato recipes</a>, Gas Mark 4 creates crispy outsides with fluffy interiors. It's also excellent for fish fillets and delicate baked goods. When cooking multiple items, remember air fryers work best with single layers at this temperature.</p>

<h3>Temperature Accuracy in Air Fryers</h3>
<p>Not all air fryers display Gas Mark settings, so you may need to set 354 F directly. Some models have presets that approximate Gas Mark 4. Check your manual for specific temperature controls and consider an oven thermometer for accuracy.</p>

<p>Beyond 354 F to Gas Mark, you might need other conversions for air fryer cooking. Gas Mark 5 equals 190°C or 374°F, while Gas Mark 3 is 170°C or 338°F. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> includes various temperature options for different dishes.</p>
