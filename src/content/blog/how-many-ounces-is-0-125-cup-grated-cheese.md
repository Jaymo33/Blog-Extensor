<p>Measuring grated cheese by weight (ounces) is often more accurate than using volume (cups), as the density can vary based on how finely the cheese is grated. A tightly packed 0.125 cup of grated cheese might weigh slightly more than a loosely filled one, so using a kitchen scale removes any guesswork. This is especially helpful for recipes like air fryer stuffed peppers or cheesy potato skins, where precision matters.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 0.125 Cup of Grated Cheese</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 0.125 cup of grated cheese accurately. Use a standard measuring cup and lightly fill it without packing the cheese down. For reference, 0.125 cup is equivalent to 2 tablespoons or 30 millilitres. This method works well for recipes like air fryer grilled cheese or cheesy cauliflower bites.</p>
