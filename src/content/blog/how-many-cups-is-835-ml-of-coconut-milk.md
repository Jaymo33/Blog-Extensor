<p>Using the right amount of coconut milk can make or break your air fryer dish. Too much liquid can lead to soggy results, while too little might leave your food dry. <h3>How to Measure 835 ml of Coconut Milk Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use standard cups to measure 835 ml of coconut milk. A UK metric cup holds 250 ml, so 3.5 cups will give you the right amount. Stir the coconut milk well before measuring, as it can separate in the tin.</p>  

<h2>Using Coconut Milk in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Coconut milk adds richness and depth to air fryer dishes, from curries to desserts. When converting 835 ml to cups (3.5 cups), you’ll find it’s perfect for batch cooking or meal prep. Try it in a fragrant <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken curry</a> or a creamy vegan dessert.</p>  

<p>For best results, use full-fat coconut milk, as it provides the creamiest texture. Light coconut milk can be substituted, but you may need to adjust other liquids in the recipe. Always check your air fryer’s capacity to avoid overfilling when using liquid ingredients like coconut milk.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Coconut Milk</h3>  
<p>One mistake people make is not shaking the tin before opening it, leading to uneven measurements. Another is using imperial and metric cups interchangeably—always stick to one system. For more measurement tips, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h2>Converting Other Coconut Milk Measurements</h2>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of coconut milk, here’s a quick guide: 400 ml is roughly 1.7 cups, and 1 litre equals about 4.2 cups. These conversions are handy for scaling recipes up or down. For more air fryer-friendly conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>detailed chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Remember, coconut milk can vary in thickness depending on the brand. If you’re using homemade coconut milk, strain it well to ensure accurate measurements. This is especially important for air fryer baking, where consistency matters.</p>  

<h3>Storing Leftover Coconut Milk</h3>  
<p>If you don’t use all 835 ml (3.5 cups) of coconut milk, transfer the leftovers to an airtight container. It keeps in the fridge for up to 4 days or can be frozen for later use. Just give it a good stir after thawing to restore its creamy texture.</p>
