<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Frying</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve the perfect texture and flavour. Too much or too little coconut oil can affect crispiness, moisture, and even cooking times. For example, 210 ml of coconut oil (0.89 cups) is a common measurement in recipes like air-fried banana bread or crispy coconut shrimp. Using the right amount ensures even heat distribution and prevents soggy results. <p>Coconut oil’s unique properties also play a role. Unlike butter, it’s liquid at warmer temperatures but solidifies when cool. This means 210 ml of melted coconut oil will fill a cup differently than its solid form. For accuracy, always measure coconut oil in its intended state (melted or solid) as specified in your recipe. <h3>How to Measure 210 ml of Coconut Oil Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup for melted coconut oil. Pour slowly until the oil reaches the 210 ml mark, which is just shy of a full cup. For solid coconut oil, pack it into a dry measuring cup and level it off. Remember, 210 ml equals roughly 0.89 cups, so aim for just under the 1-cup line. This method works well for recipes like air-fryer granola or roasted vegetables, where precision matters. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Coconut Oil</h3>  
<p>One mistake is using a dry cup for liquid oil or vice versa, leading to inaccurate measurements. Another is eyeballing—210 ml of coconut oil (0.89 cups) is easy to overpour. Always use the right tools and double-check. For sticky solid oil, lightly grease your measuring cup first for easy release. These small steps ensure your air-fryer dishes, from cookies to fish, turn out flawless.</p>
