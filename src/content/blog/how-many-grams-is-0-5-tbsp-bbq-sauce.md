<h2>Why Measuring BBQ Sauce Matters in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient measurements to achieve crispy, evenly cooked results. Too much BBQ sauce can make food soggy, while too little might leave it bland. Converting 0.5 tbsp of BBQ sauce to grams (7.5g) ensures consistency, especially in marinades or glazes. If you're preparing sticky ribs or saucy wings, this accuracy is key. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>BBQ air fryer recipes</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<p>Different brands of BBQ sauce vary slightly in density, but 7.5 grams is a reliable average. Thicker sauces might weigh a gram more, while lighter ones could be closer to 7 grams. For best results, use a digital kitchen scale. Need more tips? Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner guide</a> helps tackle sticky sauce messes.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.5 Tbsp of BBQ Sauce Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, fill a tablespoon halfway for 0.5 tbsp (about 7.5g). Use a levelled measuring spoon for accuracy. For sticky sauces like BBQ, lightly grease the spoon first to help it slide off cleanly. This trick works for all <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> requiring precise sauce amounts.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 0.5 Tbsp of BBQ Sauce</h2>  
<p>Half a tablespoon (7.5g) of BBQ sauce is perfect for glazing small portions like chicken tenders or tofu bites in the air fryer. It’s also ideal for mixing into marinades or brushing onto <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>roasted vegetables</a>. Since air fryers circulate heat intensely, a little sauce goes a long way. Overdoing it can lead to smoking or burning.</p>  

<p>For larger batches, multiply the 0.5 tbsp (7.5g) measurement accordingly. Six servings? That’s 3 tbsp or 45 grams. Always adjust cooking times if using extra sauce, as moisture affects crispiness. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe hub</a> for saucy air fryer ideas.</p>  

<h3>Does BBQ Sauce Brand Affect the Weight?</h3>  
<p>Yes, thicker, sugar-heavy BBQ sauces weigh more per 0.5 tbsp (up to 8g), while lighter ones hover near 7g. Always check the label for viscosity clues. For consistent results, stick to one brand per recipe. Homemade sauces vary widely, so scales are best.</p>
