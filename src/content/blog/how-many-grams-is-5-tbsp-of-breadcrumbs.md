<h2>Why Accurate Breadcrumb Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of breadcrumbs is crucial for air fryer cooking. Too little, and your food won’t crisp up properly; too much, and the coating may burn or dry out. For example, 5 tbsp of breadcrumbs to grams ensures even distribution for dishes like air-fried chicken tenders or crispy tofu. <p>Breadcrumbs also absorb moisture differently depending on their type—fresh, dried, or panko. Fresh breadcrumbs weigh slightly more, while panko (Japanese-style) is lighter. <h3>Types of Breadcrumbs and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Not all breadcrumbs are created equal. Dried breadcrumbs, the most common type, weigh about 7 grams per tablespoon. Panko breadcrumbs are flakier and lighter, closer to 5 grams per tbsp. Fresh breadcrumbs, made from blended stale bread, can weigh up to 10 grams per tbsp due to higher moisture content. Always check your recipe for the recommended type.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 5 Tbsp of Breadcrumbs Perfectly</h2>  
<p>For accuracy, use a flat-edged knife to level off your tablespoons. Scoop the breadcrumbs loosely into the spoon, then sweep the excess away. Avoid packing them down, as this can add extra grams unintentionally. If you’re converting 5 tbsp of breadcrumbs into grams regularly, consider investing in a kitchen scale for precision.</p>
