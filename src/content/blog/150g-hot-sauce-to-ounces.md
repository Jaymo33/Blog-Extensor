<h3>How to Measure 150g of Hot Sauce Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can estimate 150g of hot sauce using tablespoons. Roughly 10 tablespoons equal 150g, but this depends on the sauce’s thickness. For thicker sauces, like sriracha, it might be slightly less. Always check the label for weight-to-volume conversions if available. For more precision, invest in a digital scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking.</p>

<h3>Does Hot Sauce Weight Change When Cooked?</h3>  
<p>Hot sauce doesn’t lose significant weight when cooked in an air fryer, as it’s mostly liquid. However, reducing it into a glaze might concentrate the flavours. Always measure before cooking for accuracy. If you’re unsure, start with less—you can always add more later.</p>
