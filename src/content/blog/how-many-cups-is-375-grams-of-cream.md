<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for Cream</h3>  
<p>In the UK, 1 cup of cream is approximately 240ml, while in the US, it's around 237ml. This small difference means 375 grams of cream equals about 1.6 UK cups or 1.5 US cups. For air fryer recipes, especially desserts like custards or mousses, this precision ensures your dish sets correctly. Always use a kitchen scale for the most accurate results.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 375 Grams of Cream Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 375 grams of cream using cups. Fill a UK measuring cup to just over 1.5 cups or a US cup to the 1.5-cup mark. For recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>airfryerrecipe.co.uk's dessert section</a>, this method works well in a pinch. Remember, though, that weighing ingredients is always more precise.</p>  
<p>Another tip is to use tablespoons as a guide—16 UK tablespoons (15ml each) or 16 US tablespoons (14.8ml each) roughly equal 375 grams of cream. This method is handy for smaller air fryer batches or when adjusting recipes on the fly.</p>
