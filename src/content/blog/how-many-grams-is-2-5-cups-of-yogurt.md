<h2>Why Convert Cups to Grams for Yogurt?</h2>  
<p>Measuring ingredients by weight (grams) is far more accurate than using volume (cups). Yogurt can vary in density depending on its thickness, brand, or whether it's Greek or regular. For air fryer recipes, precision ensures even cooking and perfect texture. A slight variation in yogurt quantity can affect moisture levels in cakes, marinades, or sauces. That’s why <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>our conversion chart</a> is a must-have for any home cook.</p>  

<p>Using grams also simplifies scaling recipes up or down. If you’re meal prepping or adjusting portion sizes, weight measurements remove the guesswork. Whether you’re making a creamy dip or a fluffy cake, 600 grams of yogurt gives you the exact amount needed. <h3>Types of Yogurt and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Not all yogurts weigh the same per cup. Greek yogurt, for example, is denser and may weigh slightly more than regular yogurt. If you’re using a thicker variety, 2.5 cups could be closer to 625 grams. Always check the label for nutritional info, as brands differ. For best results, weigh your yogurt if precision is crucial.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Yogurt Accurately</h2>  
<p>To measure 2.5 cups of yogurt correctly, use a liquid measuring cup for consistency. Spoon the yogurt into the cup without packing it down, then level it off. For even better accuracy, a kitchen scale is ideal—simply tare the scale with your bowl and add yogurt until it hits 600 grams. This method eliminates inconsistencies and ensures perfect results in dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>air fryer desserts</a>.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a scale, remember that 1 cup of yogurt is roughly 240 grams, so 2.5 cups equal 600 grams. Keep in mind that slight variations won’t ruin most recipes, but precision is key for baking.
