<h2>How to measure 1000g of apple cider vinegar</h2>
<p>When working with 1000g of apple cider vinegar (35.27 ounces), using a digital kitchen scale is the most accurate method. Simply place your measuring container on the scale, tare it to zero, then pour the vinegar until you reach 1000g. For those without scales, 35.27 ounces roughly equals 4.4 US cups or 4.2 UK cups, though weighing is always more precise.</p>

<h3>The science behind liquid measurements</h3>
<p>Apple cider vinegar has a density of about 1.05g/ml, which means 1000g occupies approximately 952ml. This slight difference from water's density (1g/ml) is why volume measurements can sometimes lead to small inaccuracies. For air fryer cooking, where evaporation rates affect outcomes, weight measurements like 1000g to ounces conversions provide superior consistency.</p>
