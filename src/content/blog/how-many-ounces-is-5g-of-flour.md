<h2>Why Accurate Flour Measurements Matter in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect flour measurements can lead to dry or dense results. For example, 5g of flour to ounces (0.18 oz) might seem small, but it’s crucial for recipes like air fryer cookies or breaded dishes. Too much flour can make coatings clumpy, while too little won’t crisp properly. Always use a digital scale for accuracy, especially with small quantities like 5 grams of flour to oz UK conversions.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 5g of Flour Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, 5g of flour is roughly 1 teaspoon (leveled, not heaped). However, this method isn’t as accurate as weighing, especially for air fryer recipes where consistency matters. For best results, invest in a kitchen scale—it’s a game-changer for converting 5g flour to ounces and beyond.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 5g of Flour in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Small amounts of flour, like 5g (0.18 oz), are often used in air fryer recipes for dusting or light coatings. For instance, a light sprinkle of flour helps breadcrumbs stick to chicken or fish.
