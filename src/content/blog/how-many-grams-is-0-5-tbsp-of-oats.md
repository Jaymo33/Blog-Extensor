<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of ingredients ensures even cooking and perfect texture in your air fryer. Too many oats can make coatings clumpy, while too few might not provide enough crunch. For recipes like oat-crusted chicken or homemade granola, 0.5 tbsp of oats (4 grams) is often just the right amount. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>Air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, so ingredient ratios are crucial. If you're adapting a traditional recipe, converting tablespoons to grams avoids guesswork. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>breakfast recipes</a> often call for oats, and precise measurements guarantee fluffy, crispy results.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.5 Tbsp of Oats Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a measuring spoon and level it off with a knife for accuracy. A heaped or scant tablespoon can vary by several grams, affecting your dish. For consistent results, consider investing in a digital scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking!</p>
