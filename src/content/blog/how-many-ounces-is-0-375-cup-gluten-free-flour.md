<p>0.375 cup of gluten-free flour equals approximately 1.5 ounces (oz). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, where precise measurements ensure perfect texture and consistency. Whether you're baking gluten-free cookies or coating chicken for a crispy finish, knowing this conversion helps avoid kitchen mishaps.</p>

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect flour quantities can lead to uneven cooking or overly dense results. For gluten-free baking, which often requires precise ratios, measuring 0.375 cup correctly as 1.5 oz ensures your recipes turn out light and fluffy. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>

<p>Gluten-free flours vary in density, so weighing ingredients removes guesswork. A digital kitchen scale is ideal, but if you're using cups, spoon the flour lightly into the measuring cup and level it off. This method prevents packing too much flour into 0.375 cup, which could throw off your recipe's balance.</p>

<h3>Common uses for 0.375 cup of gluten-free flour</h3>
<p>This measurement frequently appears in small-batch air fryer recipes, like coating for 2-3 chicken tenders or thickening sauces for single servings. It's also perfect for making personal-sized gluten-free muffins or adjusting breading mixes. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>gluten-free recipe collection</a> for creative ways to use this amount.</p>

<h2>Converting between cups and ounces for gluten-free flour</h2>
<p>While 0.375 cup equals 1.5 oz, it's helpful to know nearby conversions too. 0.25 cup is 1 oz, and 0.5 cup is 2 oz of gluten-free flour. These measurements come in handy when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer. Remember that different gluten-free flour blends may vary slightly in weight.</p>

<p>For UK bakers, note that 1.5 oz is approximately 42.5 grams. If your recipe uses metric measurements, this conversion ensures accuracy. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>gluten-free dessert recipes</a> include both imperial and metric measurements for convenience.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with gluten-free flour in air fryers</h3>
<p>Gluten-free flour can behave differently than wheat flour in air fryers. For best results when using 0.375 cup (1.5 oz), combine it with binding agents like xanthan gum or eggs. This prevents crumbling and helps achieve that desirable crispy texture air fryers are known for.</p>

<p>When coating foods with 0.375 cup of gluten-free flour, shake off excess before air frying to prevent gummy spots. For even cooking, arrange items in a single layer with space between them.
