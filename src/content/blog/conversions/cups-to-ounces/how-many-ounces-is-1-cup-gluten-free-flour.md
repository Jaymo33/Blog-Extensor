<h2>Why Measuring Gluten-Free Flour Correctly Matters</h2>  
<p>Gluten-free flour behaves differently than wheat flour due to its unique composition. Many blends contain rice flour, almond flour, or tapioca starch, which vary in weight. Using volume measurements (like cups) can lead to inaccurate results, affecting your air fryer recipes. A kitchen scale ensures you add the right amount, preventing dry or dense bakes. <h3>How to Measure 1 Cup of Gluten-Free Flour Accurately</h3>  
<p>To measure 1 cup of gluten-free flour correctly, fluff the flour in its bag first, then spoon it into a measuring cup. Level it off with a knife—never pack it down. For best results, use a digital scale set to ounces or grams. This method eliminates guesswork, especially for air fryer desserts like cookies or muffins. Need a reliable scale? We recommend one with a tare function for zeroing out containers.</p>
