<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient quantities must be precise for consistent results. For recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>walnut-crusted chicken</a>, 0.33 cup (1.4 oz) is often the ideal amount to coat without overwhelming the dish. If you're converting from volume to weight, always account for how finely the walnuts are chopped, as this affects density.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 0.33 Cup of Chopped Walnuts</h3>  
<p>Use a dry measuring cup for chopped walnuts, filling it lightly without pressing down. Level off the top with a knife for an exact 0.33 cup. If you don’t have a 1/3 cup measure, fill a standard cup to roughly one-third of its capacity. For larger batches, multiply the conversion—0.66 cup would be 2.8 oz, and so on. Storing walnuts in an airtight container preserves freshness, ensuring your 0.33 cup portions stay flavourful.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing and Using Walnuts</h3>  
<p>Store chopped walnuts in the freezer to extend their shelf life and prevent rancidity. When ready to use, measure out 0.33 cup (1.4 oz) straight from the freezer—no need to thaw. Toasting enhances their flavour, so consider air-frying small batches before adding to recipes. For sweet applications, toss toasted walnuts with a bit of honey or cinnamon before cooling.</p>
