<h3>Converting between cups and ounces</h3>
<p>For those who prefer working in ounces rather than cups, our handy <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> makes switching between units simple. When measuring lime juice, use a clear liquid measuring cup for the most accurate results, checking at eye level.</p>

<h2>Tips for measuring lime juice perfectly</h2>
<p>To ensure you're getting exactly 8.45 ounces of lime juice per cup, follow these professional techniques. First, always use fresh limes when possible - you'll need about 8-10 medium limes to yield 1 cup (8.45 oz) of juice. Roll the limes firmly on your countertop before juicing to maximize yield. For consistent results in recipes like our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/seafood>seafood dishes</a>, consider investing in a citrus juicer that separates pulp automatically. After juicing, strain through a fine mesh sieve if your recipe calls for clear lime juice.</p>

<p>Storage is another key factor when working with lime juice measurements. Freshly squeezed lime juice loses its vibrancy quickly, so measure what you need and use it promptly. If you must store it, keep it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. For longer storage, freeze measured portions (like 8.45 oz batches) in ice cube trays - perfect for grabbing just what you need for future air fryer recipes. Don't forget to label your containers with both volume (cups) and weight (ounces) for easy reference.</p>
