<h3>How to measure gelatin powder correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a kitchen scale to weigh your gelatin powder. Scooping directly from the bag can lead to inconsistencies due to settling or fluffiness. If you don't have a scale, fluff the powder lightly with a fork before spooning it into your measuring cup. Level it off with a straight edge for precision.</p>

<h3>Converting other gelatin measurements</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of gelatin powder, the general rule is that 1 cup equals about 8 ounces. This makes scaling recipes up or down straightforward. For example, 0.5 cup would be 4 ounces, while 1.5 cups would be 12 ounces. Always double-check your conversions to avoid mishaps.</p>  

<p>Gelatin sheets are another option, but their weight varies by brand. If substituting sheets for powder, refer to the packaging for exact equivalents. <h2>Storing gelatin powder for longevity</h2>  
<p>To keep your gelatin powder fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Moisture can cause clumping, which affects measurement accuracy. <p>Gelatin powder has a long shelf life if stored correctly—usually up to two years.
