<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small discrepancies in flour weight can lead to dense cakes or dry bread. Using a kitchen scale to measure 1 cup of wheat flour in ounces eliminates guesswork. For air fryer recipes, where space and heat distribution are limited, accuracy is even more critical. A slight variation could mean the difference between perfectly golden and undercooked results.</p>  
<p>Many home bakers rely on volume measurements, but weight (in ounces or grams) is far more reliable. Flour can settle or become compacted in a cup, leading to inconsistent amounts. If you're adapting traditional recipes for your air fryer, converting 1 cup of wheat flour to ounces ensures you stay true to the intended texture and rise. <h2>Converting cups to ounces for UK bakers</h2>  
<p>In the UK, recipes often use grams, but ounces are still widely understood. One cup of wheat flour translates to roughly 4.5 ounces or 128 grams. This conversion is particularly useful for American recipes, which frequently list ingredients in cups. If you're preparing <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a>, knowing how many ounces are in 1 cup of wheat flour helps avoid mishaps.</p>  

<h3>How to measure flour correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate 1 cup wheat flour to ounces conversion, fluff the flour with a spoon before lightly scooping it into a measuring cup. Level off the excess with a straight edge—don’t pack it down. Better yet, use a digital scale for precision. This method is especially helpful for air fryer baking, where even distribution matters.</p>

<h3>Adjusting for different flour types</h3>  
<p>While 1 cup of wheat flour weighs about 4.5 ounces, other flours vary. Whole wheat flour is denser, while cake flour is lighter. Always check specific conversions if substituting. For air fryer baking, sticking to the recommended type ensures optimal texture and browning.</p>  

<h2>Tips for measuring flour without a scale</h2>  
<p>No scale? No problem. The spoon-and-level method is your best bet for approximating 1 cup of wheat flour in ounces.
