<h2>Why accurate olive oil measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of olive oil in your air fryer recipes is crucial for texture and flavour. Too little oil, and your food might dry out; too much, and it could become greasy. For example, crispy air-fried potatoes need just enough oil to coat them evenly without soaking. If you’re working with 1.25 cups of olive oil, knowing it’s 10 fluid ounces helps you portion it correctly. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<p>Olive oil is denser than water, so its weight and volume don’t align perfectly. While 1 cup of water is 8 fluid ounces, 1 cup of olive oil weighs about 7.7 ounces. That’s why 1.25 cups of olive oil translates to roughly 10.6 ounces by weight. For most air fryer recipes, fluid ounces (volume) are more practical, but weight can be useful for baking. If you’re prepping a marinade or dressing, fluid ounces will give you the consistency you need.</p>  

<h3>How to measure olive oil for air frying</h3>  
<p>The easiest way to measure 1.25 cups of olive oil is with a liquid measuring cup. Fill it to the 1-cup mark, then add another quarter cup. If you don’t have a measuring cup, you can use a kitchen scale set to ounces. Pour the oil until it reaches 10 fluid ounces (or 10.6 ounces by weight). For smaller batches, tablespoons and teaspoons work too—1.25 cups equals 20 tablespoons or 60 teaspoons.
