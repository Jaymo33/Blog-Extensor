<p>1.5 cups of condensed milk equals approximately 12 ounces. This conversion is essential for anyone using condensed milk in recipes, especially when preparing air fryer desserts or baked goods. Accurate measurements ensure your dishes turn out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Understanding condensed milk measurements</h2>
<p>Condensed milk is a thick, sweetened dairy product often used in desserts. When converting cups to ounces, it's important to note that condensed milk is denser than regular milk. This means 1.5 cups of condensed milk weighs more than 1.5 cups of liquid milk. For precise baking, especially in air fryer recipes, getting this measurement right is crucial.</p>
<p>Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer dessert recipes</a> call for condensed milk as a key ingredient. <h3>Why accurate measurements matter</h3>
<p>Using the correct amount of condensed milk affects both texture and sweetness in your recipes. Too much can make desserts overly sweet or dense, while too little might result in dry or crumbly textures. This is particularly important in air fryer cooking, where precise measurements lead to better results.</p>

<h2>Converting cups to ounces for condensed milk</h2>
<p>The standard conversion for condensed milk is 1 cup = 8 ounces. Therefore, 1.5 cups equals 12 ounces. This measurement applies to both US and UK recipes, though always check your specific brand as densities can vary slightly. When in doubt, weighing your ingredients is the most accurate method.</p>
<p>If you're working with a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>air fryer dessert recipes</a>, you might need to adjust other ingredients based on your condensed milk measurement. Keeping a kitchen scale handy ensures you get perfect results every time.</p>

<h3>Tips for measuring condensed milk</h3>
<p>For sticky ingredients like condensed milk, lightly greasing your measuring cup can help it slide out more easily. Alternatively, use a spatula to scrape out every last bit. Remember to level off the top of your measuring cup for the most accurate measurement.</p>

<h2>Using condensed milk in air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>Condensed milk works wonderfully in many air fryer recipes, from quick desserts to sweet glazes. Its thick consistency and sweetness make it ideal for creating caramelized textures in the air fryer. When substituting other sweeteners with condensed milk, remember to account for its liquid content.</p>
<p>Popular <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>air fryer recipes</a> using condensed milk include banoffee pie, coconut macaroons, and tres leches cake. The concentrated sweetness means you often need less sugar elsewhere in the recipe. Always follow your specific recipe's measurements for best results.</p>

<h3>Storing leftover condensed milk</h3>
<p>After opening a can of condensed milk, transfer any unused portion to an airtight container. It will keep in the refrigerator for up to two weeks. You can also freeze condensed milk for longer storage - just be sure to thaw it completely before using in your next air fryer creation.</p>

<h3>Alternative measurement methods</h3>
<p>If you don't have measuring cups, you can use tablespoons - there are 16 tablespoons in 1 cup, so 1.5 cups would be 24 tablespoons. For metric measurements, 1.5 cups of condensed milk equals approximately 354 ml. These conversions can be helpful when following international recipes.</p>
