<p>Getting the right amount of mustard can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too little, and your dish might lack flavour; too much, and it could overpower other ingredients. <h3>How to Measure Mustard Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 2 cups of mustard accurately, use a liquid measuring cup for runny mustards or a dry measuring cup for thicker varieties. Level it off with a knife for precision. If you’re scaling a recipe up or down, remember that 1 cup of mustard equals 8 ounces. This makes it easy to adjust quantities without guesswork.</p>

<h3>Converting Mustard Measurements for UK Cooks</h3>  
<p>In the UK, mustard measurements follow the same standard: 2 cups equal roughly 16 ounces. However, if you’re using a kitchen scale, note that UK ounces (fluid ounces) differ slightly from US ounces. For most home cooking, the difference is negligible, but for precision, use a digital scale set to grams (454 grams per pound).</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing Leftover Mustard</h2>  
<p>If you’ve measured out 2 cups of mustard but don’t use it all, proper storage keeps it fresh. Transfer leftovers to an airtight container and refrigerate. Mustard can last for months if stored correctly, though homemade varieties may have a shorter shelf life. For more kitchen organisation tips, visit our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning and maintaining your air fryer</a>.</p>  

<p>Mustard’s acidity acts as a natural preservative, but always check for off smells or mould before reuse. If you’re unsure, it’s better to err on the side of caution. For recipes that call for small amounts, consider freezing mustard in ice cube trays for portioned convenience.</p>  

<h3>Mustard Substitutes in a Pinch</h3>  
<p>If you’re short on mustard, try substitutes like horseradish, wasabi, or a mix of vinegar and turmeric. These won’t replicate mustard exactly but can provide a similar tang. For recipes where mustard is a key flavour, though, sticking to the 2-cup (16-ounce) measurement is best.</p>
