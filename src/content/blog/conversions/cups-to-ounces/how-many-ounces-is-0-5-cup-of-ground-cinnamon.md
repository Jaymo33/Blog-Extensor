<h2>Why measuring cinnamon correctly matters</h2>  
<p>Ground cinnamon is a powerful spice, and even a small variation can alter the flavour of your dish. Too much can overpower, while too little might leave your recipe bland. In air fryer cooking, where heat distribution is rapid, precise measurements prevent uneven seasoning. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>cinnamon apple chips recipe</a> relies on the perfect balance of sweetness and spice. Measuring by weight (ounces) rather than volume (cups) eliminates guesswork.</p>  

<p>Many home cooks assume a cup measurement is universal, but density varies between spices. Ground cinnamon is lighter than sugar but heavier than flour, so converting cups to ounces is crucial. A digital kitchen scale is the best tool for accuracy, especially when scaling recipes. If you’re new to air frying, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient conversions.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.5 cup of ground cinnamon</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.5 cup of ground cinnamon accurately, fluff the spice in its container first. Scoop it lightly into a measuring cup without packing it down, then level it off with a knife. For best results, weigh it on a scale to confirm it’s 2.5 ounces. If you don’t have a scale, spoon the cinnamon into the cup instead of scooping to avoid compaction.</p>

<p>Storing ground cinnamon properly ensures it retains its flavour. Keep it in an airtight container away from heat and sunlight. <h3>Converting other cinnamon measurements</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for tablespoons or grams instead of cups, here’s a quick guide: 1 tablespoon of ground cinnamon is roughly 0.25 ounces (7 grams). For larger batches, 1 cup equals about 5 ounces (140 grams). These conversions are handy when adapting recipes for your air fryer, ensuring flavours stay balanced.</p>
