<h2>Understanding Fan Oven vs Conventional Oven</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air using a built-in fan, cooking food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens. This means you typically need to reduce the temperature by 20°C when converting from fan to conventional. <p>Many modern recipes specify fan oven temperatures, leaving home cooks to figure out conventional oven equivalents. <h3>Why 185°C Fan is a Popular Baking Temperature</h3>
<p>185°C fan (160°C conventional) hits the sweet spot for many baked goods. It's hot enough to create good browning without burning, while allowing even cooking throughout. The circulating air in fan ovens and air fryers means you can often reduce cooking times slightly compared to conventional ovens.</p>
