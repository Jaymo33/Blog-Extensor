<h3>How to Measure 910 ml of Oat Milk</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for the most accurate results. Fill it to the 910 ml mark, or if using cups, measure just under 4 cups (since 1 cup is about 237 ml). For air fryer recipes like custards or batters, slight variations matter, so a kitchen scale can help verify the amount.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Cup Sizes</h3>  
<p>Note that US and UK cup sizes differ slightly. A US cup is 240 ml, while a UK metric cup is 250 ml. For 910 ml of oat milk, this means roughly 3.79 US cups or 3.64 UK cups. Most modern recipes standardise to the US measurement, but always check the source to avoid discrepancies.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Working with Oat Milk</h2>  
<p>Oat milk thickens when heated, so reduce cooking times slightly in the air fryer to prevent over-drying. It also browns faster than dairy milk, so lower temperatures may be needed.
