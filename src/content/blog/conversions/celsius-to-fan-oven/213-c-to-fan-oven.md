<p>213°C in a fan oven is equivalent to approximately 190°C when using the convection setting. This adjustment accounts for the efficient heat circulation in fan-assisted ovens and air fryers, ensuring even cooking without overheating. Whether you're baking, roasting, or air frying, this conversion is essential for perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 213°C to Fan Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens and air fryers circulate hot air, which cooks food faster and more evenly than traditional ovens. This means you often need to reduce the temperature by 20-25°C to avoid burning or drying out your dishes. For example, 213°C in a conventional oven becomes 190°C in a fan oven or air fryer. This adjustment is crucial for recipes that require precise temperatures, like cakes or roasted meats.</p>

<h2>How to Use 213°C in an Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Air fryers operate similarly to fan ovens, so the same temperature conversion applies. If a recipe calls for 213°C, set your air fryer to 190°C instead. This ensures your food cooks evenly without becoming overly crispy or dry. Popular recipes like crispy chicken or roasted vegetables benefit greatly from this adjustment.</p>
