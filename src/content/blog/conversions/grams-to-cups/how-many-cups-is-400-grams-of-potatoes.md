<h2>How to Measure 400 Grams of Potatoes Without a Scale</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 400 grams of potatoes using standard cup measurements. A medium-sized potato typically weighs around 150-200 grams, so two medium potatoes will get you close to 400 grams. When chopped, this usually fills about 2.5 to 3 cups, depending on the cut. For recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>air fryer roasted potatoes</a>, this measurement ensures even cooking and perfect crispiness.</p>

<h3>Does the Type of Potato Affect the Measurement?</h3>
<p>Yes, the type of potato can slightly alter the cup measurement for 400 grams. Waxy potatoes like Charlotte or Anya are denser, so they may yield slightly fewer cups than floury varieties like Maris Piper. However, the difference is minimal for most home cooking. Whether you’re using 400 grams of potatoes for mash, fries, or a bake, the 2.5 to 3 cup guideline works well across the board.</p>
