<h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens operate differently from conventional ovens due to their built-in fan that circulates hot air. This circulation means fan ovens cook food about 20°C faster than conventional ovens. When a recipe calls for 210 fan oven, it's specifically designed for this efficient cooking method. The same dish in a conventional oven would need slightly lower temperature to prevent burning. <h3>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h3>
<p>When converting 210 fan oven to conventional oven settings, timing adjustments are just as crucial as temperature changes. As a general rule, increase conventional oven cooking times by 10-15% compared to fan oven instructions. For example, if a recipe suggests 30 minutes at 210 fan, try 35 minutes at 190 conventional. Always check food a few minutes early to prevent overcooking. This technique works well for our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> where precise cooking is essential.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations for Baking</h3>
<p>Baking at 210 fan oven versus standard oven requires extra attention. The circulating air in fan ovens can cause delicate items like soufflés or meringues to cook unevenly. When converting 210 c fan oven equivalent in conventional settings, consider reducing the temperature further for sensitive baked goods. Many bakers find that 180°C conventional works better for items that would use 210 fan. This adjustment helps prevent over-browning while ensuring thorough cooking.</p>
