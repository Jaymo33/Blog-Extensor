<p>75 grams of honey is roughly equivalent to 0.24 cups in the US or 0.21 cups in the UK. This conversion is essential for baking, cooking, or preparing air fryer recipes where precise measurements matter. Honey’s thickness and stickiness can make measuring tricky, but with the right techniques, you’ll get accurate results every time.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Honey Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>When baking or cooking in an air fryer, precise measurements ensure consistent results. Too much honey can make dishes overly sweet or sticky, while too little might leave them dry. For recipes like honey-glazed chicken or air-fried desserts, getting the ratio right is key. If you’re following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, knowing how to convert grams to cups will help you nail the dish.</p>  

<p>Honey’s density varies slightly depending on temperature and type, but 75 grams generally translates to just under a quarter cup in US measurements. In the UK, where cup sizes differ slightly, it’s closer to 0.21 cups. Keep this in mind when adapting recipes from different regions.</p>  

<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements for Honey</h3>  
<p>In the US, a standard cup holds 240ml, while a UK cup is 250ml. This small difference affects conversions, especially for dense ingredients like honey. For 75 grams of honey, US cooks will use about 0.24 cups, whereas UK cooks will measure 0.21 cups. Always check which system your recipe uses to avoid mistakes.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Measuring Honey Accurately</h2>  
<p>Measuring honey can be messy due to its sticky texture. To make it easier, lightly coat your measuring cup or spoon with oil or non-stick spray. This helps the honey slide out cleanly. Alternatively, use a kitchen scale for the most precise measurement—75 grams is 75 grams, no matter the cup size!</p>  

<p>If you’re working with an air fryer, consider using a silicone liner to catch drips from honey-based marinades or glazes. <h3>Storing Leftover Honey</h3>  
<p>If you have leftover honey after measuring 75 grams, store it in an airtight container at room temperature. Honey doesn’t spoil easily, but it can crystallise over time. To liquefy crystallised honey, gently warm the jar in a bowl of warm water—never microwave it, as this can degrade its quality.</p>
