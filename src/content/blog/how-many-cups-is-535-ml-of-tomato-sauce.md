<h3>How to Measure 535 ml of Tomato Sauce Without a Measuring Cup</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup, a standard UK mug holds about 284 ml, so 535 ml is just under two mugs. Alternatively, use a kitchen scale—1 ml of tomato sauce weighs roughly 1 gram, so 535 grams equals 535 ml. This method is handy for air fryer recipes requiring precision.</p>

<h3>Does Tomato Sauce Thickness Affect the Measurement?</h3>  
<p>Thicker sauces, like passata, may settle differently in a measuring cup, but 535 ml remains consistent by volume. Stirring before measuring ensures accuracy. For sticky sauces, lightly grease the measuring cup for easy pouring.</p>  

<h2>Converting 535 ml of Tomato Sauce in US vs UK Cups</h2>  
<p>In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, so 535 ml is approximately 1.88 UK cups. In the US, 1 cup is 240 ml, making 535 ml roughly 2.26 US cups. Always check which standard your recipe uses to avoid confusion.</p>
