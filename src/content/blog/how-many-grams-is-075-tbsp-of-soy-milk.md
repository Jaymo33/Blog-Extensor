<h3>How to measure 0.75 tbsp of soy milk without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have digital scales, you can still measure 0.75 tbsp of soy milk accurately. Use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and fill it three-quarters full. For smaller quantities, a teaspoon (5ml) can help—just use 2.25 tsp to match 0.75 tbsp. Remember, 0.75 tbsp of soy milk in grams is roughly 11.25g, so slight variations won’t ruin your recipe. This method works well for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>air fryer desserts</a> where precision matters.</p>

<h3>Does soy milk brand affect the weight?</h3>  
<p>Different brands of soy milk can have slight variations in density due to additives or thickness. However, the difference is minimal—most unsweetened soy milks weigh about 15 grams per tbsp, making 0.75 tbsp roughly 11.25 grams. Sweetened or flavoured versions might be slightly heavier, but the variance is usually less than a gram. For most air fryer recipes, this small difference won’t impact the outcome significantly.</p>
