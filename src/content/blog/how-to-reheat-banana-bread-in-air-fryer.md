<h3>Best Temperature for Reheating Banana Bread</h3>  
<p>160°C (320°F) is the sweet spot for reheating banana bread in an air fryer. This temperature warms it thoroughly without burning the edges or drying it out. If your air fryer runs hot, reduce the temp slightly to 150°C (300°F) and check frequently. For frozen banana bread, add an extra minute or two at the same temperature. Always preheat the air fryer for the best results.</p>  

<h2>Step-by-Step Guide to Reheating Banana Bread</h2>  
<p>Start by slicing your banana bread into even portions—this ensures uniform reheating. Place the slices in the air fryer basket in a single layer, leaving space between them for airflow. Avoid stacking, as this can lead to uneven heating. Set the air fryer to 160°C (320°F) and cook for 3-5 minutes, checking at the 3-minute mark. For a softer texture, wrap the slices in foil before reheating.</p>  

<p>If you’re reheating a whole loaf, wrap it in foil and air fry at the same temperature for 5-7 minutes. <h3>How to Prevent Banana Bread from Drying Out</h3>  
<p>To keep banana bread moist, avoid overcooking—stick to the recommended 3-5 minute window. If reheating multiple slices, work in batches to maintain airflow. A light spritz of water or a damp paper towel placed underneath the bread can help retain moisture. For stored banana bread, ensure it’s wrapped tightly in cling film or foil before refrigerating.
