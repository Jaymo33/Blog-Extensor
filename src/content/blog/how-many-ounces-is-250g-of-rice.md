<h2>How to measure 250g of rice without scales</h2>
<p>Don’t have kitchen scales? You can still measure 250g of rice in ounces using household items. A standard UK mug holds roughly 200g of uncooked rice, so 250g is just over a mugful. Alternatively, 8.82 ounces of rice fills about 1.25 US cups. While these methods aren’t as precise as scales, they work in a pinch for air fryer recipes. Remember, rice expands during cooking, so 250g uncooked yields about 500g cooked – perfect for meal prep.</p>

<h3>Common rice types and their weights</h3>
<p>Different rice varieties have slightly varying weights. For example, 250g of basmati rice occupies less volume than sticky rice due to grain shape. However, the 250g rice to ounces conversion (8.82 oz) remains consistent by weight. When adapting recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable stir-fries</a> to rice puddings, always weigh your rice for accuracy. This becomes especially important when cooking rice in air fryer baskets or liners.</p>

<h2>Air fryer rice cooking tips</h2>
<p>When cooking 250 grams of rice in your air fryer, preheating is key. Spread the rice evenly in an oven-safe dish or air fryer liner for consistent results. The 8.82 ounces of rice should be combined with double its volume in liquid (about 17.6 oz water). Most air fryers cook rice perfectly at 180°C for 15-20 minutes. For more techniques, visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> to maintain your appliance’s performance.</p>

<p>Leftover rice? The 250g rice how many ounces question becomes handy for storage too. Portion cooked rice into 8.82 oz servings before refrigerating or freezing. Reheating these precise portions in your air fryer takes just 3-5 minutes at 160°C. This method prevents the rubbery texture microwaves often create. Whether you’re making fried rice or rice salads, proper measurement ensures perfect texture every time.</p>

<h3>Scaling recipes up or down</h3>
<p>Need to adjust a recipe? The 250g rice oz conversion makes scaling simple. For half portions, use 125g (4.41 oz), or double to 500g (17.64 oz) for crowds. This flexibility is why we love air fryers for everything from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep</a> to dinner parties. Just remember to adjust cooking times slightly when changing quantities – more rice may need extra minutes, while less cooks faster.</p>
