<p>When it comes to baking or cooking, precision is key. Using the right amount of cream ensures your dishes turn out perfectly every time. <h2>How to Measure Cream Correctly</h2>
<p>Measuring cream accurately is simple if you follow a few basic steps. First, use a liquid measuring cup for the most precise results. Pour the cream into the cup until it reaches the 2.0-cup mark, which is 473 ml. Avoid using dry measuring cups, as they can lead to inaccuracies.</p>
<p>If you don't have a measuring cup, you can use a kitchen scale. One cup of cream weighs approximately 240 grams, so 2.0 cups would be 480 grams. This method is just as accurate and can be especially useful for recipes that require exact measurements.</p>

<h3>US vs UK Measurements</h3>
<p>It's worth noting that US and UK cup measurements can differ slightly. In the UK, a cup is typically 250 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. For 2.0 cups of cream, this means 473 ml in the US and 500 ml in the UK. Always check which standard your recipe uses to avoid confusion.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Leftover Cream</h3>
<p>If you have leftover cream, store it in an airtight container in the fridge. It will stay fresh for up to a week, so you can use it in another recipe later. Just make sure to give it a quick stir before using it again, as it can separate slightly over time.</p>
