<h3>How to measure 50g of coconut oil in ounces</h3>  
<p>Measuring 50g of coconut oil in ounces is simple with a kitchen scale. If your scale displays ounces, you’ll see it reads roughly 1.76 oz for 50g. No scale? Use a measuring spoon—1.76 oz is just under 3.5 tablespoons of coconut oil. Remember, coconut oil’s consistency changes with temperature, so measure it in its solid state for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with coconut oil in your air fryer</h3>  
<p>Coconut oil has a high smoke point, making it great for air frying. However, it solidifies below 24°C, so warm it slightly for easy measuring. If your recipe calls for 50g of coconut oil (1.76 oz), melt it gently first, then measure. Avoid overheating, as this can affect its nutritional benefits.</p>
