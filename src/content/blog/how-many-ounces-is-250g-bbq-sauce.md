<p>250g of BBQ sauce equals approximately 8.82 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements can make or break your dish. Whether you're marinating chicken wings or glazing ribs, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures consistency. For more handy conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of BBQ sauce ensures your food isn’t overly saucy or dry. Air fryers circulate hot air, so too much sauce can create a mess, while too little might leave your dish bland. Converting 250g of BBQ sauce to ounces (8.82 oz) helps you stick to recipes perfectly. If you're prepping a batch of <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken recipes</a>, this precision is key.</p>  

<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but some air fryer manuals list measurements in ounces. Knowing that 250g of BBQ sauce is roughly 8.82 ounces bridges this gap. This is especially useful when following American recipes or using older kitchen scales. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 250g of BBQ sauce without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can use volume measurements. 250g of BBQ sauce is roughly 1 metric cup or 240ml. However, this can vary slightly depending on the sauce’s thickness. For sticky sauces, a kitchen scale is best. Need to clean sticky residue? Try our recommended <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 250g of BBQ sauce in air fryers</h2>  
<p>This amount is perfect for coating 500g of chicken wings or a rack of ribs. Spread evenly, 250g of BBQ sauce (8.82 oz) ensures full coverage without dripping. For <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a>, you might use less, but it’s great for glazing.</p>  

<p>Some air fryer models have sauce-friendly settings, like the "glaze" or "marinate" function. Always check your manual to avoid overheating thick sauces. Converting 250g of BBQ sauce to ounces helps adjust pre-set programs accurately.</p>  

<h3>Storing leftover BBQ sauce after measuring</h3>  
<p>If you have extra, store it in an airtight container for up to a week. Reheat gently before reusing in your air fryer. Avoid cross-contamination by using clean utensils.</p>
