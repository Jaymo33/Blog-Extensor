<h2>Why Measuring Chicken Breast Matters</h2>  
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial for consistent results in air frying. Too much chicken can overcrowd the basket, leading to uneven cooking, while too little may dry out. Converting 300 grams of chicken breast to cups helps standardise recipes, whether you’re baking, grilling, or air frying. This is especially useful for meal prep, where portion control is key. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>  

<p>Different cuts and preparation methods can slightly alter the volume. For example, finely diced chicken packs more densely into a cup than shredded chicken. If you’re working with 300 grams of chicken breast, consider how you’ll prepare it before measuring. This ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 300 Grams of Chicken Breast</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a kitchen scale to weigh 300 grams of chicken breast. If you don’t have one, visual estimates can help: 300 grams is roughly the size of two medium chicken breasts. Once cooked, the volume may shrink slightly, so adjust accordingly. For recipe ideas, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipe category</a>.</p>  

<h2>Converting Grams to Cups for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>While weight (grams) is more precise, volume (cups) is often used in recipes. For 300 grams of chicken breast, expect about 1.5 to 2 cups when raw. Cooked chicken reduces in volume, so adjust if your recipe calls for pre-cooked meat. This conversion is handy for dishes like stir-fries, salads, or air-fried chicken bites.</p>  

<p>If you’re adapting a recipe from grams to cups, remember that density matters. Ground or minced chicken will yield a different cup measurement than whole pieces. Always err on the side of slightly less to avoid overfilling your air fryer basket. For more guidance, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Chicken</h3>  
<p>One mistake is packing chicken too tightly into a cup, which skews the measurement. Instead, lightly fill the cup and level it off. Another error is using liquid measuring cups for solids, which can lead to inaccuracies. For 300 grams of chicken breast, a dry measuring cup works best. Keep your air fryer in top shape with <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>our cleaning tips</a>.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Cooking 300 Grams of Chicken Breast in an Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>For juicy results, season your 300 grams of chicken breast before air frying. Preheat the air fryer to 180°C and cook for 12-15 minutes, flipping halfway. Smaller pieces cook faster, so adjust timing if diced. Always check the internal temperature reaches 75°C for safety.</p>  

<p>Marinating the chicken beforehand adds flavour and moisture. A simple mix of olive oil, garlic, and herbs works wonders. For crispier skin, pat the chicken dry before cooking. Experiment with spices and sauces to find your favourite combo.</p>
