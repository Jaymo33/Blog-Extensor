<h2>Understanding Millilitres to Cups Conversion</h2>  
<p>Converting millilitres to cups depends on the cup measurement system you're using. In the UK, 1 cup is 284 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. For 910 ml of water, this means roughly 3.85 UK cups or 3.84 US cups. This slight difference might not matter for some recipes, but for baking or delicate air fryer dishes, precision is key. Always check whether your recipe uses UK or US measurements to avoid mishaps.</p>  
<p>If you're using an air fryer, liquid measurements can impact how your food cooks. Too much water might make dishes soggy, while too little could dry them out. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies these calculations, so you never have to guess. Whether you're making crispy potatoes or a moist cake, getting the liquid right is crucial.</p>  

<h3>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>Air fryers circulate hot air to cook food, so the right amount of liquid ensures even cooking. For example, adding 910 ml of water to a batter instead of 3.85 cups could throw off the consistency. This is especially important for recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a>, where precision affects rise and texture. Measuring correctly helps avoid undercooked or overly dense results.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Liquids in an Air Fryer</h3>  
<p>Use a clear measuring jug for liquids to avoid spills and ensure accuracy. When converting 910 ml of water to cups, fill the cup to the brim and level it off. For sticky batters or oils, lightly grease the measuring cup first. These small steps make a big difference in air fryer cooking, where every millilitre counts.</p>
