<h2>Why Measuring Minced Onion Accurately Matters</h2>  
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial in cooking, especially when using an air fryer. Too much or too little minced onion can alter the flavour and texture of your dish. For example, adding extra minced onion might make a recipe overly pungent, while too little could leave it bland. Air fryers cook food quickly, so balanced ingredients are key. <h3>How to Measure Minced Onion Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure minced onion accurately, lightly pack the tablespoon without pressing down too hard. Overpacking can result in more than 10 grams, while underpacking may leave you short. If your recipe calls for grams, using a kitchen scale is the most precise method. <h2>Common Uses for Minced Onion in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Minced onion adds depth to air fryer dishes like meatballs, stuffed peppers, and marinades. Its fine texture ensures even distribution, enhancing flavour without overwhelming other ingredients. Since air fryers circulate hot air, minced onion cooks quickly, releasing its aroma and taste efficiently. <h3>Converting Other Onion Measurements</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for diced or sliced onion instead of minced, the weight per tablespoon will vary. Diced onion is slightly heavier, at around 12 grams per tbsp, due to larger pieces. Always adjust measurements based on the onion's form to ensure accuracy in your air fryer recipes.</p>
