<h2>Why Accurate Vinegar Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Vinegar is a staple in many recipes, from tangy dressings to tenderising meats. Using too much or too little can throw off flavours or even affect food safety, especially in pickling. If you’re air frying, vinegar-based marinades or glazes need precision to avoid overpowering your dish. <p>In baking, vinegar reacts with baking soda for lift, so exact measurements are crucial. Even when cleaning your air fryer, a diluted vinegar solution needs the right ratio to be effective without residue. That’s why knowing 885 ml of vinegar in cups saves time and hassle.</p>  

<h2>US vs. UK Cup Conversions</h2>  
<p>In the US, 1 cup equals 240 ml, so 885 ml of vinegar is about 3.7 cups. In the UK, where 1 cup is 250 ml, it’s closer to 3.5 cups. This small difference matters in recipes requiring precision. For air fryer cooking, sticking to one system avoids inconsistencies.</p>  

<h3>Converting for Baking and Cooking</h3>  
<p>If a recipe calls for 885 ml of vinegar, use a measuring jug for accuracy. <h2>Common Uses for 885 ml of Vinegar</h2>  
<p>This quantity is often used in bulk cooking, like pickling or large-batch dressings. It’s also handy for deep-cleaning your air fryer—mix with water for a natural degreaser. <h3>Tips for Measuring Without a Jug</h3>  
<p>No measuring jug? Use a standard cup and multiply: 4 US cups (240 ml each) hold 960 ml, so 885 ml is just under. For UK cups, 3.5 cups (250 ml each) equal 875 ml—close enough for most recipes. Always level off measurements for accuracy.</p>
