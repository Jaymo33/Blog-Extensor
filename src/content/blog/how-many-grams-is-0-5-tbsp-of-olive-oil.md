<h2>Why accurate olive oil measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of olive oil in your air fryer ensures your food cooks evenly without becoming greasy or dry. Too much oil can lead to soggy results, while too little might leave your dish undercooked or lacking flavour. For recipes like crispy potatoes or golden chicken, 0.5 tbsp (6.5 grams) of olive oil is often the perfect amount. If you’re exploring new dishes, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<p>Olive oil is a staple in many kitchens, and its weight can vary slightly depending on factors like temperature and brand. However, 6.5 grams per 0.5 tbsp is a reliable standard for most cooking needs. Keeping a kitchen scale handy can help you measure precisely, especially for air fryer recipes where every gram counts.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.5 tbsp of olive oil without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 0.5 tbsp of olive oil accurately. A standard tablespoon holds 15 ml, so half of that is 7.5 ml, which translates to roughly 6.5 grams. Use a measuring spoon for the best results, and avoid eyeballing it to prevent inconsistencies. <h3>Converting other olive oil measurements to grams</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of olive oil, here’s a quick reference: 1 tbsp equals about 13 grams, while 1 teaspoon is roughly 4.5 grams. These conversions are handy for scaling recipes up or down. For example, if you need 1.5 tbsp, that’s around 19.5 grams. Always measure carefully to avoid altering the dish’s texture or taste.</p>
