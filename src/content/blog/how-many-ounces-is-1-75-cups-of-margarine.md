<p>1.75 cups of margarine equals 14 ounces (or 396 grams) in the UK. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements ensure perfect texture and flavour. Whether you’re whipping up cakes, cookies, or savoury dishes, knowing how to convert cups to ounces helps avoid kitchen mishaps. <h2>Why accurate margarine measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Margarine plays a crucial role in baking and cooking, especially in air fryer recipes where even distribution affects crispiness and moisture. Too much margarine can make dishes greasy, while too little may leave them dry. Measuring 1.75 cups of margarine correctly (14 ounces) ensures consistency. If you’re preparing <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a>, precision is key for the perfect rise.</p>  

<p>In the UK, margarine is often sold in blocks measured in grams, so converting cups to ounces simplifies recipes. A kitchen scale is handy, but if you don’t have one, our guide helps you measure confidently. For more recipe ideas, explore <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 1.75 cups of margarine without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the displacement method. Fill a measuring jug with 1.75 cups of water, then add margarine until the water level rises to 3.5 cups. This works because 1 cup of margarine equals 8 ounces. For sticky margarine, lightly grease your measuring cup first. Always level off the top with a knife for accuracy.</p>  

<h2>Common air fryer recipes using 1.75 cups of margarine</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes call for 1.75 cups of margarine, such as shortbread biscuits or pastry dough. Margarine also works well in savoury dishes like garlic bread or crispy potatoes.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US margarine measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, margarine is typically sold in 250g or 500g blocks, while US brands may use cups or sticks. 1.75 UK cups of margarine (14 ounces) is equivalent to 1.5 US cups plus 2 tablespoons. Always check packaging for weight conversions if unsure. For international recipes, a digital scale is your best friend.</p>
