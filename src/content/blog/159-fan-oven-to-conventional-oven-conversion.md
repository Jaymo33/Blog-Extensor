<p>Converting 159 fan oven to conventional oven is straightforward: it equals approximately 175°C in a traditional oven. This conversion ensures your recipes cook evenly when switching between oven types, especially useful for air fryer users who often need precise temperature adjustments.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 159 Fan Oven to Conventional?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air for faster cooking, while conventional ovens rely on radiant heat. When a recipe calls for 159°C in a fan oven, you'll need to increase the temperature by about 10% for a conventional oven. This difference matters most for baked goods and meats where precise heat control affects texture and doneness. <h3>How Temperature Affects Cooking Results</h3>
<p>The 10% increase from 159 fan to conventional oven compensates for the reduced heat circulation. In an air fryer, which works similarly to a fan oven, you might actually decrease the temperature slightly from conventional oven settings. This prevents overcooking while maintaining that desirable crispy texture air fryers are known for.</p>
