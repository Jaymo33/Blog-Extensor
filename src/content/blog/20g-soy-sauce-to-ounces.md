<h2>Why Convert 20g of Soy Sauce to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes, especially those for air fryers, use metric measurements like grams. However, some kitchen scales or older recipes might still list ingredients in ounces. Converting 20g of soy sauce to ounces (0.705 oz) helps avoid guesswork and ensures accuracy. This is particularly important for balancing flavours in marinades or sauces, where even a small discrepancy can alter the taste.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 20g of Soy Sauce Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>Don't have a kitchen scale? No problem! You can still measure 20g of soy sauce (0.705 ounces) using common household tools. One tablespoon of soy sauce weighs roughly 15g, so 20g is just over 1.3 tablespoons. Alternatively, a standard UK teaspoon holds about 5g, meaning four teaspoons will give you 20g.</p>  
<p>For air fryer cooking, accuracy matters, especially when preparing marinades or glazes. If you're working with thicker sauces like teriyaki, the weight might vary slightly, so adjust accordingly. <h3>Common Uses for 20g of Soy Sauce in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>20g of soy sauce (0.705 ounces) is a versatile amount for many air fryer dishes. It's perfect for marinating chicken wings, glazing salmon, or seasoning tofu. The salty, umami flavour enhances meats and vegetables alike, making it a staple in Asian-inspired recipes.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Cooking with Soy Sauce in Your Air Fryer</h2>  
<p>Soy sauce can burn easily in the air fryer due to its high sugar content. To prevent this, mix 20g of soy sauce with a bit of oil or water before brushing it onto food. This ensures even cooking and prevents sticky residue. For easy cleanup, try our recommended <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a>.</p>  
<p>Experiment with 20g of soy sauce in dishes like air-fried dumplings or crispy tofu. Pair it with ginger, garlic, or honey for extra depth of flavour. <h3>Does Soy Sauce Weight Vary by Brand?</h3>  
<p>Most soy sauces have a similar density, so 20g will always be close to 0.705 ounces. However, low-sodium or thick varieties might weigh slightly more. Always check the label if precision is crucial for your recipe.</p>
