<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air frying</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Sunflower seeds, for example, can burn quickly if too many are added. Using 0.375 cup (1.5 oz) ensures they toast evenly without overcrowding. This precision is especially useful for recipes like air-fried granola or seed-crusted proteins. <h2>How to measure 0.375 cup of sunflower seeds</h2>
<p>For dry ingredients like sunflower seeds, use a standard measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Scooping directly from the bag can compress the seeds, leading to inaccuracies. If your recipe requires 0.375 cup (1.5 oz), fill the cup halfway, then add half again for precision.</p>
<p>Alternatively, weigh 1.5 ounces on a digital scale for exactness. This method eliminates guesswork and ensures consistency across batches. For more tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/snacks>snack recipes</a> featuring sunflower seeds.</p>

<h2>Converting other cup measurements to ounces</h2>
<p>While 0.375 cup equals 1.5 oz, other conversions are equally useful. For instance, 0.5 cup is 2 oz, and 0.25 cup is 1 oz. Keeping these ratios in mind simplifies scaling recipes up or down. Always adjust air fryer times slightly when changing quantities.</p>

<h3>Storing sunflower seeds for air fryer use</h3>
<p>Keep sunflower seeds in an airtight container to maintain freshness. Measure out 0.375 cup portions in advance for quick access during cooking. Proper storage prevents rancidity, ensuring your 1.5 oz servings taste great every time.</p>
