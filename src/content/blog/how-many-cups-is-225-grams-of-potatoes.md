<h3>How Potato Type Affects Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>Floury potatoes, such as King Edward or Russet, tend to be less dense, so 225 grams may fill slightly more than 1.5 cups when diced. Waxy potatoes, like Jersey Royals, are denser and may measure closer to 1.25 cups for the same weight. If precision matters—especially for baking—weighing is best. For air frying, a small variance won’t drastically affect results, but consistency ensures even cooking.</p>
