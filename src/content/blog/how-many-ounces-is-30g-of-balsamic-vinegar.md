<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of balsamic vinegar can elevate your air fryer dishes from good to great. Too little, and you might miss out on that rich, tangy flavour. Too much, and your food could end up overly acidic or soggy. For recipes like glazed salmon or sticky chicken wings, 30g of balsamic vinegar (or 1.06 ounces) is often the sweet spot. <h3>How to measure 30g of balsamic vinegar without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 30g of balsamic vinegar (1.06 ounces) using tablespoons. One tablespoon of balsamic vinegar weighs roughly 15g, so two tablespoons will give you 30g. This method isn’t as precise as using scales, but it works in a pinch. For best results, invest in a digital scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 30g of balsamic vinegar in air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Balsamic vinegar adds depth and complexity to air fryer dishes. A 30g portion (1.06 ounces) is perfect for glazing meats, dressing salads, or enhancing roasted vegetables. Try it in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> for a delicious twist. The acidity also helps tenderise proteins, making it a great marinade base.</p>  

<p>Another popular use for 30g of balsamic vinegar is in reduction sauces. Simmering it down concentrates the flavours, creating a thick, syrupy glaze ideal for drizzling over air-fried dishes. Pair it with honey or garlic for extra depth. <h3>Storage tips for balsamic vinegar</h3>  
<p>To keep your balsamic vinegar fresh, store it in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. A tightly sealed bottle prevents oxidation, preserving its rich flavour. If you’re using 30g (1.06 ounces) frequently, consider decanting it into a smaller, easy-to-pour bottle for convenience.</p>
