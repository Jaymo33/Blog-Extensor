<h3>Types of Rice and Their Weights</h3>
<p>Not all rice weighs the same, so it’s important to adjust your measurements accordingly. Long-grain rice like jasmine or basmati tends to be lighter, averaging 200 grams per cup. Short-grain or sushi rice is denser, often weighing 210 grams or more per cup. For 1.75 cups, this means a range of 350–370 grams. <h2>How to Measure 1.75 Cups of Rice Correctly</h2>
<p>Measuring rice accurately starts with using the right tools. A standard US cup holds 240 ml, while a UK cup is 250 ml—this slight difference can affect your grams. For 1.75 cups, we recommend using a kitchen scale for the most reliable results. Scoop the rice lightly into the cup and level it off for consistency. <p>If you don’t have a scale, the spoon-and-level method is your next best option. Avoid packing the rice into the cup, as this can add up to 20% extra weight. For 1.75 cups, gently spoon the rice in and level it with a knife. This method works well for most rice varieties, from white to brown.
