<h3>How Air Fryers Compare to Fan Ovens</h3>  
<p>Air fryers and fan ovens both use convection heating, but air fryers are more compact, leading to faster cooking times. When converting 447 F to an air fryer, you might reduce the time by 10-15%. For example, a dish that takes 30 minutes in a fan oven could be ready in 25 minutes in an air fryer. Always monitor your food to avoid overcooking.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 447 F in Fan Ovens</h2>  
<p>This high temperature is perfect for roasting meats like beef or lamb, creating a delicious crust while keeping the inside juicy. It's also great for baking bread or pastries that need a quick rise and crisp finish. If you're reheating frozen foods, 447 F (230 C) can help restore crispiness without drying them out.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times</h3>  
<p>Since fan ovens cook faster than conventional ones, you may need to reduce cooking times by about 10%. For example, if a recipe calls for 40 minutes at 447 F in a standard oven, try 35-37 minutes in a fan oven. Always use a meat thermometer for accuracy, especially with poultry or large cuts of meat.</p>
