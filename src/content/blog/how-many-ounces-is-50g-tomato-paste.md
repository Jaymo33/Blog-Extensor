<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise cooking times and ingredient quantities to deliver crispy, evenly cooked results. Using too much or too little tomato paste can throw off the balance of your recipe. For example, a sauce might become too thick or too watery if the tomato paste measurement isn’t spot-on. That’s why converting 50g of tomato paste to ounces (1.76 oz) is so helpful—it ensures consistency. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>  

<p>Tomato paste is a concentrated ingredient, so even small variations in quantity can significantly impact flavour and texture. Whether you’re making a rich bolognese or a tangy BBQ glaze, getting the 50g tomato paste to ounces conversion right is key. Plus, many air fryer recipes, like stuffed peppers or meatballs, call for precise amounts to avoid soggy or dry results. Trust us, your taste buds will thank you for the accuracy!</p>  

<h2>How to measure 50g of tomato paste without scales</h2>  
<p>Not everyone has kitchen scales handy, but don’t worry—there are easy ways to measure 50g of tomato paste in ounces (1.76 oz) without them. A standard tablespoon holds about 15g of tomato paste, so 3 heaped tablespoons will give you roughly 50g. Alternatively, a small 50g tin of tomato paste is a common supermarket size, making it a convenient option. For more air fryer-friendly recipes, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>.</p>  

<h3>Using visual cues for accuracy</h3>  
<p>If you’re eyeballing it, 50g of tomato paste is roughly the size of a golf ball. This visual trick helps when you’re in a pinch and need a quick estimate. Remember, though, that for baking or sauces where precision matters, scales are best. For dishes like air fryer pizza or stuffed mushrooms, a little extra or less tomato paste might not ruin the dish, but sticking to 50g (1.76 oz) ensures consistency.</p>

<h3>Storing leftover tomato paste</h3>  
<p>If your recipe only uses part of a 50g tin, transfer the leftover paste to an airtight container and refrigerate. It’ll keep for up to a week, ready for your next air fryer adventure. You can also freeze it in ice cube trays for portioned convenience—just pop out a cube whenever you need roughly 15g (about 0.5 oz) of tomato paste.</p>
