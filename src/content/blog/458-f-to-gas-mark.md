<h2>How to Use 458 F in Your Air Fryer</h2>
<p>When your air fryer recipe calls for 458 F (Gas Mark 8), preheating is crucial for even cooking. Most modern air fryers reach this temperature within 3-5 minutes. For best results, avoid overcrowding the basket—this allows hot air to circulate freely around your food, whether it’s crispy roast potatoes or juicy turkey breasts.</p>
<p>At this high temperature, timing adjustments are often needed compared to oven cooking. Check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> for tailored cooking times. For example, chicken wings might need 20 minutes at 458 F instead of 25 minutes in a conventional oven. Always use a meat thermometer for proteins to ensure safe internal temperatures.</p>

<h3>Foods That Shine at 458 F (Gas Mark 8)</h3>
<p>This high heat works wonders for achieving restaurant-quality textures. Think crispy-skinned salmon, perfectly puffed Yorkshire puddings, or caramelised roasted vegetables. Even delicate items like shrimp or tofu benefit from a quick blast at 458 F to develop flavour without drying out.</p>

<h3>Safety Tips for High-Temperature Air Frying</h3>
<p>Always handle your air fryer basket with oven gloves at 458 F—the metal gets extremely hot. Ensure proper ventilation in your kitchen when cooking at Gas Mark 8 equivalents.</p>

<p>When converting oven recipes specifying Gas Mark 8 to air fryer cooking, reduce cooking times by 15-20%. The concentrated heat circulation cooks food faster.
