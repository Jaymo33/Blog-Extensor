Whether you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> or adapting a traditional dish, knowing exact measurements makes all the difference.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Butter Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of butter is crucial for texture and flavour in both baking and air frying. Too much butter can make dishes greasy, while too little may leave them dry. Many recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a> rely on precise measurements to achieve the best results. Converting cups to grams eliminates guesswork, especially when working with air fryers, where even small deviations can affect cooking times.</p>  

<p>Butter density can vary slightly depending on temperature and brand, but 1.25 cups typically translates to 284 grams. This standardisation helps when scaling recipes up or down. For more conversion tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air-fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Butter Correctly</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 1.25 cups of butter accurately. One standard stick of butter in the UK is about 113 grams, so 2.5 sticks equal roughly 1.25 cups. Pack the butter firmly into a measuring cup to avoid air gaps, ensuring consistency. For air fryer recipes, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake category</a>, precise butter measurements help avoid soggy or unevenly cooked results.</p>  

<h2>Converting Butter Measurements for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Air fryers cook food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens, so ingredient accuracy is key. Whether you’re making crispy cookies or fluffy cakes, knowing that 1.25 cups of butter is 284 grams ensures your dish turns out perfectly. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> rely on this conversion for consistent results.</p>  

<p>If you’re adapting a traditional recipe for the air fryer, double-check your butter measurements. A slight miscalculation can lead to overcooking or undercooking. For example, too much butter in an air fryer cake might cause it to brown too quickly on the outside while remaining raw inside.</p>  

<h3>Common Butter Conversion Mistakes</h3>  
<p>One frequent error is confusing US and UK cup measurements. While 1.25 US cups of butter equal 284 grams, UK cups may differ slightly due to varying standards. Always verify which measurement system your recipe uses. Another mistake is using melted butter instead of solid butter for cup measurements, which can throw off the weight.</p>
