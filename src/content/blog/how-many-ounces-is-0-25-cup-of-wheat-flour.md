<h2>Why accurate flour measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small discrepancies in flour measurements can affect the texture and rise of your goods. Too much flour can make your cakes dense, while too little can leave them crumbly. This is especially important when using an air fryer, as its compact cooking environment requires precise ingredient ratios. If you're working with recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a>, converting 0.25 cup of wheat flour to ounces ensures consistency. A digital kitchen scale is the most reliable tool for this task.</p>  

<p>Many bakers prefer weighing ingredients rather than using volume measurements like cups. This eliminates variables like how tightly packed the flour is in the cup. For air fryer baking, where space and heat distribution are key, accuracy is even more critical. Whether you're making cookies or a quick bread, knowing that 0.25 cup of wheat flour equals 1.06 ounces takes the guesswork out of the process.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.25 cup of wheat flour without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a scale, you can still measure 0.25 cup of wheat flour accurately. First, fluff the flour in its container with a spoon to aerate it. Then, gently spoon the flour into your measuring cup without packing it down. Finally, level off the top with a straight edge, like a knife. This method helps approximate the weight of 1.06 ounces (30 grams) for 0.25 cup of wheat flour. For air fryer recipes, consistency is key, so consider investing in a scale for best results.</p>
