<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect ingredient quantities can lead to uneven cooking. Agave syrup, being a liquid sweetener, affects texture and caramelisation. Using 40g (1.41 oz) precisely ensures your dish isn’t overly sweet or under-flavoured. For more conversion tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>. <h3>How to Convert 40g of Agave Syrup to Ounces</h3>  
<p>The conversion formula is simple: 1 gram equals 0.035 ounces. Multiply 40g by 0.035 to get 1.41 ounces. This applies to all types of agave syrup, whether light or dark. For larger batches, scaling up proportionally ensures accuracy. If you’re working with UK measurements, rest assured this conversion is consistent across regions.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Agave Syrup Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you lack a scale, use the spoon method mentioned earlier or a graduated liquid measuring cup. Agave syrup’s density means 40g occupies about 30ml. Stirring it before measuring ensures uniformity. For more precision, invest in a digital scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking.</p>
