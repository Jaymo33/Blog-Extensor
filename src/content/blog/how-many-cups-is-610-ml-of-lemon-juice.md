<h2>Understanding Lemon Juice Measurements</h2>  
<p>Lemon juice is a common ingredient in both sweet and savoury dishes, and accurate measurements are crucial. Converting 610 ml of lemon juice to cups helps streamline your cooking process. One standard metric cup holds 236.59 ml, so dividing 610 ml by this value gives you roughly 2.58 cups. This measurement is particularly useful for air fryer recipes, where precision can affect texture and flavour.</p>  

<p>If you're working with UK or US measurements, it's worth noting that the UK cup is slightly larger at 284 ml. However, most modern recipes standardise around the metric cup for consistency. <h3>Why Accuracy Matters in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve crispy, evenly cooked results. Too much or too little lemon juice can alter the moisture balance in batters or marinades. For example, a lemon-infused chicken dish or a citrusy dessert requires exact measurements to avoid sogginess or dryness. Always measure liquids like lemon juice carefully for the best air fryer outcomes.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Lemon Juice Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 610 ml of lemon juice accurately, use a liquid measuring cup with clear metric markings. Avoid using dry measuring cups, as they won't account for the liquid's meniscus. Freshly squeezed lemon juice may contain pulp, so strain it if a smooth consistency is needed. For consistent results, consider using bottled lemon juice, which offers uniform acidity and flavour.</p>
