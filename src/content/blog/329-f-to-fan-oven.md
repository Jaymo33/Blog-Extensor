<h2>Understanding 329°F in Fan Ovens</h2>
<p>Fan ovens, also known as convection ovens, circulate hot air to cook food more evenly and efficiently. When converting 329°F to a fan oven setting, you're looking at 165°C – about 25°C lower than a conventional oven setting. This difference accounts for the enhanced airflow in fan ovens, which speeds up cooking times.</p>
<p>For air fryer users, this conversion is particularly useful. Many air fryer recipes list temperatures in Fahrenheit, but UK fan ovens typically use Celsius. <h3>Converting Other Temperatures</h3>
<p>While we're focusing on 329°F to fan oven conversion, it's helpful to understand the general rule. For most conversions, subtract 20°C from the conventional oven temperature to get the fan oven equivalent. This rule applies whether you're working with air fryers or traditional ovens.</p>

<h2>Practical Applications of 329°F in Fan Ovens</h2>
<p>At 165°C fan (329°F), you can achieve excellent results with various dishes. This temperature works well for slow-roasting vegetables, baking custards, or proofing dough. It's also ideal for cooking frozen foods that require gentle reheating.</p>
<p>For air fryer users, this temperature helps prevent burning while ensuring crispy textures.
