<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect measurements can throw off cooking times and textures. For example, using too much almond milk in a batter might make it too runny, leading to soggy results. Conversely, too little could dry out your dish. That’s why converting 5 tbsp of almond milk to grams (75g) ensures consistency. <h3>How to measure 5 tbsp of almond milk without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use standard measuring spoons for 5 tbsp of almond milk. Fill the spoon to the brim without letting it overflow, then level it off with a knife. While this method works in a pinch, weighing ingredients (75g for 5 tbsp) is far more reliable. <h3>Does almond milk brand affect the weight?</h3>  
<p>While most almond milks weigh roughly 15g per tablespoon, some brands may be slightly thicker or thinner. Homemade almond milk, for instance, can vary based on how much water is used. For consistency, stick to commercial brands when possible, and always measure by weight (75g for 5 tbsp) for baking. This small step makes a big difference in air fryer recipes where precision is key.</p>
