<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Too much hot sauce might make your dish overly spicy, while too little could leave it bland. Converting 0.5 cup of hot sauce to ounces (4 oz) helps maintain balance. For more air fryer measurement tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Hot sauce density can vary slightly, but 0.5 cup typically weighs 4 ounces. This measurement is especially useful for UK cooks who prefer using weight over volume. If you're preparing a batch of spicy <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a>, this conversion ensures every piece is perfectly coated.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.5 Cup of Hot Sauce Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup for accuracy. Fill it to the 0.5 cup mark, which equals 4 fluid ounces. For thicker hot sauces, level off the top with a spatula. This method works well for air fryer marinades and sauces featured in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>.</p>
