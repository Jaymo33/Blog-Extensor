<p>Converting 363 fan oven to conventional oven in Fahrenheit is straightforward: it equals 350°F. This adjustment ensures your baked goods or meals cook evenly, whether you're using a fan-assisted oven or a traditional one. Understanding this conversion is especially useful if you're transitioning between appliances or following a recipe designed for a different oven type.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 363 Fan Oven to Fahrenheit?</h2>  
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air, cooking food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens. Recipes often specify temperatures for fan ovens, but if yours doesn't have this setting, you'll need to adjust. Converting 363 fan oven to Fahrenheit (350°F) ensures your dish cooks properly without burning or undercooking. This is particularly important for baking, where precise temperatures matter.</p>  
<p>For air fryer users, this conversion is equally handy. Many air fryer recipes are adapted from fan oven instructions, so knowing the equivalent conventional oven temperature helps. <h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>  
<p>While 363 fan oven converts to 350°F in a conventional oven, cooking times may vary. Fan ovens cook faster, so you might need to add 5-10 minutes when using a conventional oven. Always check your food a few minutes before the recommended time to avoid overcooking.</p>

<h3>Common Dishes at 363 Fan Oven</h3>  
<p>Many baked goods, like cakes and cookies, are cooked at 363 fan oven (350°F conventional). This temperature allows even baking without drying out the centre. Roasts and casseroles also work well at this heat, developing a golden crust while staying juicy inside.</p>
