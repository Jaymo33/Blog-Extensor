<h2>Why Convert 15g of Protein Powder to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Converting 15g of protein powder to ounces (0.53 oz) is crucial for accuracy in recipes. Many UK kitchen scales display both grams and ounces, but some older models or recipes may only use one unit. If you’re following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, this conversion ensures you add the right amount. Too much or too little protein powder can affect texture and taste, especially in air-fried baked goods. Consistency is key, whether you’re meal prepping or experimenting with new dishes.</p>  

<p>Protein powder is often used in air fryer recipes like protein muffins or energy bites. <h3>How to Measure 15g of Protein Powder Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can use measuring spoons to approximate 15g of protein powder. A level tablespoon typically holds around 10–12g, so 1.5 tablespoons would be close to 15g. However, this method isn’t as precise as weighing, especially for recipes where accuracy matters. For best results, invest in a digital kitchen scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer cooking. You’ll find recommendations for reliable scales in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep section</a>.</p>

<h3>Does Protein Powder Type Affect the Weight?</h3>  
<p>Different types of protein powder (whey, plant-based, casein) can vary slightly in density, but 15g will always equal roughly 0.53 ounces. Whey protein tends to be lighter and fluffier, while plant-based powders may be denser. For precise air fryer recipes, stick to weighing your protein powder rather than relying on volume measurements. This ensures consistency, especially when baking.
