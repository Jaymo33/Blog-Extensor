<h2>Why Accurate Flour Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct weight for 2 cups of flour ensures consistent results in your air fryer recipes. Too much flour can make baked goods dry, while too little may cause them to collapse. The UK standard for 2 cups of flour is 8.8 ounces, but slight variations exist based on how you scoop the flour. For best results, spoon flour into the measuring cup and level it off with a knife. <h3>How to Measure 2 Cups of Flour Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 2 cups of flour accurately, fluff the flour in its bag before scooping. Use a spoon to fill the measuring cup, then level it off without packing it down. This method ensures you get the true 8.8-ounce weight. For even more precision, weigh the flour directly in ounces or grams using a digital scale. <h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Flour</h2>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is scooping flour directly with the measuring cup, which compacts it and adds extra weight. This can turn 2 cups of flour into 10 ounces or more, throwing off your recipe. Another error is tapping the cup to settle the flour, which also increases density. Always spoon and level for the correct 8.8-ounce measurement. For more baking tips, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<p>Humidity can also affect flour weight, making it clumpier and heavier. If your flour feels damp, fluff it thoroughly before measuring. Storing flour in an airtight container helps maintain consistency. For air fryer recipes like cookies or bread, precise ounces ensure even cooking and perfect texture. <h3>Converting 2 Cups of Flour to Other Units</h3>  
<p>If you need to convert 2 cups of flour to grams, it’s roughly 250 grams. For tablespoons, 2 cups equal about 32 tablespoons. These conversions are handy when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer. Remember, 8.8 ounces is the standard UK weight for 2 cups of all-purpose flour. Wholemeal or self-raising flour may vary slightly, so always check the packaging.</p>
