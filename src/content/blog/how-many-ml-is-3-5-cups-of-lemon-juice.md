<h2>How to Measure 3.5 Cups of Lemon Juice Accurately</h2>
<p>Measuring 3.5 cups of lemon juice (828 ml) requires the right tools. A liquid measuring cup with clear millilitre markings is ideal for accuracy. If you're using fresh lemons, note that one medium lemon yields about 50 ml of juice, so you'd need roughly 16-17 lemons for 3.5 cups. For convenience, bottled lemon juice is a great alternative, but always check the label for exact millilitre measurements.</p>

<h3>Converting Cups to ml for Different Ingredients</h3>
<p>While 3.5 cups of lemon juice equals 828 ml, other liquids may have slight variations due to density. For example, 3.5 cups of water is also 828 ml, but thicker liquids like syrup or oil may differ slightly. Always double-check conversions for specific ingredients to avoid discrepancies in your air fryer recipes.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Leftover Lemon Juice</h3>
<p>If you have leftover lemon juice after measuring 3.5 cups (828 ml), store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. Alternatively, freeze it in ice cube trays for portioned use in future recipes. This ensures no waste and ready-to-use lemon juice for your next air fryer creation.</p>
