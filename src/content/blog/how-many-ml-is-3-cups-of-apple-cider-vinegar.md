<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Precision is key when using an air fryer, as ingredients cook faster and more evenly. Apple cider vinegar, often used in marinades or dressings, needs the right balance to avoid overpowering flavours. Too much can make dishes overly acidic, while too little might not deliver the desired tang. Converting 3.0 cups of apple cider vinegar to ml (710 ml) ensures consistency in every recipe.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Apple Cider Vinegar Correctly</h3>  
<p>When measuring liquids like apple cider vinegar, always use a clear measuring jug with millilitre markings for accuracy. Pour slowly to avoid spills, and check at eye level for the correct amount. If you’re using cups, remember that 1 cup equals approximately 237 ml, so 3.0 cups will be 710 ml.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for Apple Cider Vinegar in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Apple cider vinegar is a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. It’s great for tenderising meats, adding zest to dressings, or even deglazing pans. For example, a 710 ml (3.0 cups) batch can be used to marinate a whole chicken, infusing it with flavour before air frying.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Cup Measurements to ml</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of apple cider vinegar, here’s a quick reference: 1 cup = 237 ml, 2 cups = 473 ml, and 4 cups = 946 ml. This makes scaling recipes up or down a breeze, whether you’re cooking for one or a crowd.</p>  

<h2>Cleaning Your Air Fryer After Using Vinegar</h2>  
<p>Apple cider vinegar can leave behind strong odours if not cleaned properly. After cooking, wipe down your air fryer basket with warm, soapy water or use a dedicated <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a>. This prevents lingering smells and keeps your appliance in top condition for future recipes.</p>
