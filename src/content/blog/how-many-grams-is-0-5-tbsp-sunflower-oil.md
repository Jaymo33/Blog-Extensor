<h2>Why accurate oil measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of oil in your air fryer can make or break a dish. Too little oil, and your food may turn out dry; too much, and it can become greasy. Sunflower oil is a popular choice due to its high smoke point and neutral flavour. Measuring 0.5 tbsp of sunflower oil in grams ensures you get the perfect balance every time. For more recipe ideas, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>.</p>  

<p>Air fryers rely on hot air circulation, so even a small excess of oil can affect texture. By converting 0.5 tbsp of sunflower oil to grams, you avoid guesswork and achieve crisp, golden results. This is especially useful for dishes like roasted vegetables or crispy chicken. Consistency is key, and precise measurements help replicate your favourite meals perfectly.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.5 tbsp of sunflower oil accurately</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a tablespoon measure handy, a kitchen scale is your best friend. Simply tare your scale, pour the oil until it reads 6.8 grams, and you’re good to go. For those without scales, half a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) is roughly 0.5 tbsp. Remember, sunflower oil’s density means volume and weight aren’t always a 1:1 match.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 0.5 tbsp of sunflower oil in air fryers</h2>  
<p>This small amount of oil is perfect for lightly coating vegetables, chicken wings, or even homemade chips. It’s enough to enhance crispiness without overpowering the dish. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> call for a minimal oil coating to achieve that irresistible crunch. Measuring 0.5 tbsp of sunflower oil in grams ensures you don’t overdo it.</p>  

<p>Another great use is for greasing air fryer baskets or trays. A light 6.8-gram coating prevents sticking without making food soggy. For cleaning tips after oil use, visit our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a>. Whether you’re cooking frozen foods or fresh ingredients, precise oil measurements elevate your air frying game.</p>  

<h3>Sunflower oil vs other oils in air fryers</h3>  
<p>Sunflower oil isn’t your only option—olive, coconut, or avocado oil work too. However, their weights per 0.5 tbsp differ slightly due to varying densities. Sunflower oil’s 6.8 grams per 0.5 tbsp makes it a reliable middle-ground choice. Experiment with different oils in your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable dishes</a> to find your favourite.</p>
