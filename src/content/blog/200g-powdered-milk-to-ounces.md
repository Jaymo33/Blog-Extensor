<h2>Why Convert 200g of Powdered Milk to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those from the UK, use metric measurements like grams. However, some kitchen scales or recipe books might list ingredients in ounces. Converting 200g of powdered milk to ounces (7.05 oz) ensures you’re using the right amount for your dish. This is particularly useful for baking, where precision is key to achieving the perfect texture and flavour.</p>  

<p>If you’re following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, you’ll often find measurements in grams. Powdered milk is a common ingredient in air fryer desserts, breads, and even savoury dishes, so getting it right matters.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 200g of Powdered Milk Accurately</h3>  
<p>To measure 200g of powdered milk, use a digital kitchen scale for the most accurate results. If your scale only shows ounces, set it to metric mode if possible, or manually convert 200g to 7.05 oz. Scoop the powdered milk gently into your measuring cup or spoon, levelling it off for consistency. Avoid packing it down, as this can lead to over-measuring.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Powdered Milk</h3>  
<p>Once you’ve measured out 200g (7.05 oz) of powdered milk, store the rest in an airtight container to keep it fresh. Moisture is the enemy of powdered milk, so keep it in a cool, dry place. If you’re using it frequently for air fryer recipes, consider portioning it into smaller amounts for convenience.</p>
