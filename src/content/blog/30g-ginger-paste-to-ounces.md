<h3>How to Measure 30g of Ginger Paste Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, you can estimate 30g of ginger paste using tablespoons. One tablespoon of ginger paste weighs roughly 15g, so 30g would be two level tablespoons. However, for accuracy, we recommend using digital scales. Air fryer recipes often rely on precise ratios, especially for marinades or sauces where ginger paste plays a starring role. <h2>Common Uses for 30g of Ginger Paste in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Ginger paste is a versatile ingredient that shines in air fryer recipes. From marinades for air-fried salmon to glazes for tofu, 30g (1.06 oz) of ginger paste adds warmth and complexity. It’s a staple in Asian-inspired dishes, such as air-fried dumplings or honey-ginger chicken wings. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we love how ginger paste elevates simple ingredients with minimal effort.</p>  

<p>For example, a 30g portion of ginger paste can be mixed with soy sauce, garlic, and honey for a quick marinade. This works beautifully with air-fried shrimp or vegetables. Ginger paste also pairs well with citrus, making it ideal for glazes or dipping sauces. <h3>Storing Leftover Ginger Paste</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for 30g of ginger paste but you’ve opened a larger tub, proper storage is key. Transfer unused paste to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to two weeks. You can also freeze ginger paste in ice cube trays for portioned use. This way, you’ll always have the right amount (like 1.06 oz) ready for your next air fryer adventure.</p>
