<h2>Why accurate ginger paste measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Ginger paste adds a punchy, aromatic kick to dishes, but too much can overpower your meal. In an air fryer, where flavours concentrate, precision is key. For example, 5g of ginger paste to ounces (0.18 oz) might seem minor, but it ensures consistency. Recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer chicken tikka</a> rely on balanced spices. Measuring correctly avoids wasted ingredients and guarantees repeatable results every time.</p>  

<p>Sticky ingredients like ginger paste can be tricky to measure. Using a digital scale for 5g ginger paste to oz conversions is the most reliable method. If you don’t have one, a teaspoon holds roughly 5g (0.18 oz) when lightly packed. <h3>How to measure ginger paste without scales</h3>  
<p>If you’re converting 5g of ginger paste in ounces without scales, use a measuring spoon. A level teaspoon holds about 5g (0.18 oz) of ginger paste. For thicker pastes, scrape off excess with a knife for accuracy. This method works well for recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>air-fried vegetable stir-fry</a>, where ginger paste is a star ingredient.</p>
