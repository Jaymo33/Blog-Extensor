<h2>How Many Ounces Are in 1.66 Cups of Lemon Juice?</h2>  
<p>1.66 cups of lemon juice equals approximately 13.28 ounces. This conversion is essential for precise cooking, especially when using an air fryer where ingredient ratios can affect texture and flavour. Lemon juice is a common ingredient in marinades, dressings, and even desserts, so getting the measurement right matters. If you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, knowing this conversion ensures your dish turns out perfectly. Whether you're making crispy lemon chicken or a zesty dessert, accuracy is key.</p>  

<p>Understanding liquid measurements can be tricky, but it’s simpler once you know the basics. One cup of liquid, including lemon juice, is equivalent to 8 fluid ounces. Therefore, 1.66 cups of lemon juice translates to 13.28 ounces. This measurement is particularly useful for UK cooks who might be using recipes with imperial or metric units. If you're exploring <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, having a reliable conversion chart handy can save time and avoid mistakes. No one wants their dish to be too tart or too bland because of a measurement error.</p>

<h2>How to Measure Lemon Juice for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Measuring lemon juice correctly starts with using the right tools. A liquid measuring cup is ideal for accuracy, as it accounts for the liquid’s meniscus. If your recipe calls for 1.66 cups of lemon juice, fill the measuring cup to the 1 and 2/3 mark. For smaller quantities, a kitchen scale can help—13.28 ounces is roughly 376 grams. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> on our site use lemon juice, so mastering this measurement opens up a world of flavour possibilities.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup, you can use tablespoons as a substitute. There are 16 tablespoons in a cup, so 1.66 cups of lemon juice would be about 26.56 tablespoons. While this method works in a pinch, it’s less precise than using a liquid measure. For consistent results in your air fryer, stick to standardised tools.
