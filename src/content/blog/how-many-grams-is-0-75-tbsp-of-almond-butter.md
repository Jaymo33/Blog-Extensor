<h2>Why accurate almond butter measurements matter</h2>
<p>Getting the right amount of almond butter is crucial for both texture and flavour in your air fryer creations. Too much can make dishes overly dense, while too little might leave them dry. <h3>How to measure 0.75 tbsp almond butter accurately</h3>
<p>The easiest way is to use digital kitchen scales set to grams. Simply scoop your almond butter until the scale reads 11.25g. If you don't have scales, fill a tablespoon three-quarters full - though this method is less precise. For sticky ingredients like almond butter, lightly greasing your measuring spoon can help it slide out cleanly.</p>

<h3>Storing almond butter for best results</h3>
<p>To keep your almond butter fresh between uses, store it upside down in the cupboard - this helps redistribute the natural oils. Stir well before measuring out your 0.75 tbsp (11.25g) portions. If separation occurs, don't pour off the oil - it's part of what makes almond butter so good for air fryer cooking.</p>
