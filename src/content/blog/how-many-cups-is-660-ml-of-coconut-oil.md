<h2>Understanding Coconut Oil Measurements</h2>  
<p>Coconut oil is a staple in many kitchens, especially for those who love air frying or baking. Measuring it accurately ensures your dishes have the right texture and flavour. While millilitres (ml) are commonly used in the UK, cups are a popular measurement in the US and other regions. If you're using an air fryer, precise measurements matter even more, as the cooking process is faster and more intense. <p>When converting 660 ml of coconut oil to cups, it's important to note that UK and US cup sizes differ slightly. A UK cup holds 250 ml, while a US cup is 240 ml. This small difference can affect your results, especially in delicate recipes like cakes or pastries. Always double-check which cup measurement your recipe uses to avoid confusion.</p>

Whether you're making crispy tofu or golden roast potatoes, measuring 660 ml of coconut oil correctly ensures consistent results. <h2>Converting 660 ml of Coconut Oil to Cups</h2>  
<p>To convert 660 ml of coconut oil to UK cups, divide 660 by 250 (the ml per UK cup). This gives you roughly 2.64 cups. For US cups, divide 660 by 240, resulting in approximately 2.75 cups. However, most measuring cups aren't this precise, so rounding to 2.8 cups is practical. If you're unsure, a kitchen scale can help verify the amount.</p>  

<p>For those who prefer visual measurements, 660 ml of coconut oil fills a standard UK measuring cup just over two and a half times. If you're using an air fryer, consider using a spray bottle for even distribution. This method helps reduce excess oil while keeping food crispy.
