<h2>How to measure 2.5 tbsp of honey accurately</h2>
<p>Measuring honey can be tricky due to its sticky consistency. For the most accurate 2.5 tbsp measurement, lightly grease your measuring spoon with oil first. This allows the honey to slide off easily, ensuring you get exactly 53 grams without waste. Alternatively, you could weigh it directly on kitchen scales for precision.</p>
<p>If you’re adapting a recipe from cups to tablespoons, remember that 16 tablespoons make up one cup. So 2.5 tbsp is just under 1/6 of a cup. This conversion is particularly useful when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer.</p>

<h3>Converting other honey measurements to grams</h3>
<p>While we’ve focused on 2.5 tbsp honey to grams, you might need other conversions too. One tablespoon of honey typically weighs about 21 grams, while a teaspoon weighs roughly 7 grams. These measurements are consistent across most UK honey brands, though very thick or crystallised honey might vary slightly.</p>

<h3>Storing and handling honey for air fryer cooking</h3>
<p>To keep your honey at the perfect consistency for measuring, store it at room temperature in a tightly sealed container. If your honey crystallises, gently warm the jar in warm water to return it to liquid form before measuring out your 2.5 tbsp (53 grams). This ensures accurate measurements and easy pouring for your air fryer recipes.</p>
