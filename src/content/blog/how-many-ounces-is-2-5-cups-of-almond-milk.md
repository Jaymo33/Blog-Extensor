<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>When it comes to cooking or baking, precision can make or break a dish. Almond milk, like other liquids, needs to be measured correctly to avoid inconsistencies. Too much or too little can affect texture and taste, particularly in air fryer recipes where balance is key. For example, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>pancake batter</a> with too much almond milk might turn out too runny, while too little could make it dense.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those for cakes or custards, rely on exact liquid measurements. If you’re converting from cups to ounces, it’s helpful to know that 1 cup of almond milk is roughly 8 fluid ounces. This means 2.5 cups of almond milk to ounces is straightforward: just multiply 2.5 by 8, giving you 20 ounces. Simple, right?</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Almond Milk Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate measurement, use a liquid measuring cup rather than dry cups. Fill it to the 2.5-cup mark at eye level to avoid over or under-pouring. If you’re using a scale, 20 fluid ounces of almond milk will weigh slightly more than 20 ounces in weight due to density, but for most recipes, the fluid ounce measurement is sufficient.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Almond Milk</h2>  
<p>Once opened, almond milk should be refrigerated and used within 7–10 days. If your recipe calls for 2.5 cups but you only need half, store the rest in an airtight container. Shake well before reusing, as separation is natural. For longer storage, freeze almond milk in measured portions—just thaw and stir before adding to your next <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>air fryer breakfast</a>.</p>
