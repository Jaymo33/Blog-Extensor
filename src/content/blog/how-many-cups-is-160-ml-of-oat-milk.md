<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>When cooking or baking, especially with an air fryer, precise measurements are crucial. Using too much or too little oat milk can affect texture, moisture, and even cooking time. For example, adding 160 ml of oat milk instead of the correct ⅔ cup could make your batter too runny or your sauce too thick. This is why understanding conversions like 160 ml of oat milk to cups is so important.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 160 ml of Oat Milk</h2>
<p>Measuring 160 ml of oat milk is straightforward if you have the right tools. A liquid measuring cup with millilitre markings is ideal, but if you only have standard cups, remember that 160 ml equals roughly ⅔ cup. For even more precision, a kitchen scale can help, as 160 ml of oat milk weighs about 160 grams.</p>
<p>If you’re using an air fryer for recipes like muffins or cakes, precise measurements ensure your batter has the perfect consistency. Too much liquid can lead to soggy results, while too little might make your dish dry. For more air fryer baking tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>cake recipes</a>.</p>

<h3>Converting 160 ml of Oat Milk in Different Cup Sizes</h3>
<p>It’s worth noting that cup sizes can vary by region. In the UK, a standard cup is 250 ml, so 160 ml is just under ⅔ cup. In the US, a cup is 240 ml, making 160 ml roughly ⅔ cup as well. For international recipes, always double-check which cup measurement is being used to avoid confusion.</p>
