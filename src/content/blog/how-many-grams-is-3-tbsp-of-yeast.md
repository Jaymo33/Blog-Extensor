<h2>Common yeast types and their weights</h2>  
<p>Active dry yeast and instant yeast weigh slightly differently, but for most recipes, the 28-gram rule applies. Active dry yeast may require activation in warm water, while instant yeast can be mixed directly into dry ingredients. Both work well in air fryer recipes, but always check your specific yeast packaging for exact weights.</p>  

<p>Fresh yeast, often sold in blocks, has a different conversion. If substituting fresh yeast for dry, use about twice the amount by weight. For example, 3 tbsp of fresh yeast would be roughly 56 grams. <h3>Adjusting yeast for high-altitude baking</h3>  
<p>At higher altitudes, you may need slightly less yeast due to faster rising times. Reduce the amount by about 10-15% to prevent overproofing.
