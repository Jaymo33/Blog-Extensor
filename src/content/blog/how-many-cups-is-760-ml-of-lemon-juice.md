<h2>Converting 760 ml of Lemon Juice to Cups</h2>
<p>The standard conversion for liquid measurements is 1 cup = 237 ml. Therefore, 760 ml divided by 237 ml per cup gives roughly 3.2 cups. This applies whether you're in the UK or the US, though slight variations exist due to cup sizes. For air fryer recipes, consistency is key, so always measure carefully.</p>
<p>If you don't have a measuring jug, you can use a standard 250 ml cup (common in the UK) to approximate. Three full cups would be 750 ml, leaving just 10 ml extra—about two teaspoons. <h3>Using Lemon Juice in Air Fryer Recipes</h3>
<p>Lemon juice is a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. From marinades for chicken or fish to glazes for desserts, 760 ml (3.2 cups) can cover multiple recipes. <h3>Adjusting Recipes for Different Quantities</h3>

<h3>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h3>
<p>One mistake is confusing millilitres with grams—lemon juice’s volume (ml) differs from its weight. Another is using dry measuring cups for liquids, which can lead to inaccuracies. Always opt for liquid measures when working with 760 ml of lemon juice.</p>
