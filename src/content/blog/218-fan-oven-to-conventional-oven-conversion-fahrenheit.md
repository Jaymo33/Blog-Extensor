<p>218 fan oven converts to approximately 200°F in a conventional oven. This conversion is essential when adapting recipes between fan-assisted ovens and traditional ovens or air fryers. Understanding this temperature difference ensures your dishes cook evenly and come out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 218 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air for faster, more even cooking, while conventional ovens rely on radiant heat. When a recipe calls for 218 fan oven, it's designed for this efficient heat distribution. Converting to 200°F in a conventional oven compensates for the slower heat transfer. This is particularly important when using an air fryer, which operates similarly to a fan oven. <h3>How to Apply This Conversion to Air Fryers</h3>
<p>Air fryers essentially function as compact fan ovens, making the 218 fan oven to conventional oven conversion directly applicable. When your air fryer recipe specifies 218°F fan, you can use the same temperature setting. For conventional oven recipes converted to air fryer use, reduce by 10-20°F as with fan ovens.</p>

<h2>Temperature Conversion Science Explained</h2>
<p>The 218 fan oven to conventional oven conversion works because moving air transfers heat more efficiently than still air. Fan ovens' forced convection means lower actual temperatures achieve equivalent cooking results. This explains why 218 fan equals about 200°F conventional in heat transfer terms.</p>
