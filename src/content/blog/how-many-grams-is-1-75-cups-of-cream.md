<h2>Why Convert Cups to Grams for Cream?</h2>  
<p>Measuring cream by weight (grams) is far more accurate than using cups, especially for air fryer recipes. Cups can vary based on how the cream is poured or scooped, leading to inconsistencies. Grams provide a standardised measurement, ensuring your dishes turn out perfectly every time. If you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipes section</a>, converting to grams will help avoid mishaps.</p>  

<p>For example, 1.75 cups of cream in grams is roughly 420g, but this can slightly vary depending on the cream's fat content. Heavy cream (double cream in the UK) is denser than light cream, so always check your recipe's requirements. Using a kitchen scale eliminates guesswork and guarantees precision, whether you're baking or making sauces.</p>  

<h3>Types of Cream and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Not all creams weigh the same per cup. Heavy cream (double cream) weighs about 240g per cup, while light cream (single cream) is closer to 225g. For 1.75 cups, this means heavy cream would be around 420g, and light cream about 394g. Always refer to your recipe or <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>our conversion chart</a> for clarity.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 1.75 Cups of Cream Accurately</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method for measuring cream in cups. Gently spoon the cream into a measuring cup without packing it down, then level it off with a knife. This avoids over-pouring, which can throw off your recipe. For air fryer cooking, precision is key—especially when making custards or batters where texture matters.</p>  

<p>For best results, invest in a digital kitchen scale. Weighing 1.75 cups of cream (420g) ensures consistency, whether you're making a creamy pasta sauce or a decadent dessert.
