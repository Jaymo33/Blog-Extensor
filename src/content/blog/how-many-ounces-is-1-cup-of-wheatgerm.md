<p>Using the right amount of wheatgerm can make or break your air fryer creations. Too little, and you'll miss out on its nutty flavour and nutritional benefits. <h2>Converting wheatgerm measurements for air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>When adapting traditional recipes for your air fryer, converting 1 cup of wheatgerm to ounces (3.5 oz) helps maintain perfect texture. This conversion works whether you're making crunchy toppings or incorporating wheatgerm into batters.</p>

<h3>Tips for measuring wheatgerm without scales</h3>
<p>If you don't have kitchen scales, use the spoon-and-level method for measuring 1 cup of wheatgerm (3.5 oz). Gently spoon wheatgerm into your measuring cup until heaping, then level off with a straight edge. Avoid tapping or packing the cup, as this compacts the wheatgerm and leads to inaccurate measurements that could affect your air fryer results.</p>

<h3>Storing wheatgerm for freshness</h3>
<p>Because wheatgerm contains natural oils, proper storage maintains its quality between uses. After measuring out your 3.5 ounces (1 cup), transfer any remaining wheatgerm to an airtight container. Store it in the refrigerator to prevent rancidity, especially if you use it infrequently in your air fryer cooking.</p>
