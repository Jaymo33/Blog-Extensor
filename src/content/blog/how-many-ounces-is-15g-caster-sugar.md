<p>15g of caster sugar equals approximately 0.53 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precise measurements can make all the difference. Whether you're baking a cake or whipping up a dessert in your air fryer, knowing how to convert grams to ounces ensures perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Why accurate sugar measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of caster sugar is crucial for air fryer recipes. Too much sugar can cause burning, while too little might leave your dish lacking in sweetness. The air fryer's intense heat means ingredients behave differently compared to conventional ovens. That's why precise measurements, like knowing 15g of caster sugar is 0.53 ounces, are so important.</p>

<h2>How to measure 15g of caster sugar without scales</h2>
<p>If you don't have digital scales, you can still measure 15g (0.53 ounces) of caster sugar using simple household items. One level tablespoon holds about 15g of caster sugar, though we recommend investing in scales for complete accuracy. For air fryer cooking, where precision matters, proper measuring tools are worth having.</p>

<h3>The difference between caster and granulated sugar</h3>
<p>While 15g of any sugar equals 0.53 ounces, caster sugar's finer grains mean it measures differently by volume. This is particularly important in air fryer baking where texture matters. Caster sugar dissolves more quickly, making it ideal for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a> and delicate desserts.</p>

<h3>Tips for working with sugar in your air fryer</h3>
<p>When using sugar in air fryer recipes, remember that it can caramelise quickly under the intense heat. For best results with 15g (0.53 ounces) portions, spread sugary toppings evenly and check your food regularly. This prevents burning and ensures even cooking throughout.</p>
