<p>2 cups of maple syrup equals approximately 16 ounces (or 1 pound). This conversion is essential for precise baking and cooking, especially when using an air fryer where ingredient ratios can affect texture and flavour. Whether you're drizzling it over air-fried pancakes or incorporating it into a glaze, knowing the exact weight ensures consistent results.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven cooking or overly sweet dishes. Maple syrup, being a dense liquid, needs careful measuring to avoid overpowering other ingredients. For example, if you're making sticky air-fryer chicken wings, too much syrup can cause excessive caramelisation, while too little may leave the dish bland.</p>

<h2>How to measure maple syrup correctly</h2>  
<p>Using a liquid measuring cup is the easiest way to measure maple syrup for air fryer recipes. Pour the syrup slowly to avoid bubbles, and level it off with a spatula for accuracy. Alternatively, a kitchen scale provides the most precise measurement, especially for sticky ingredients like syrup.</p>  
<p>If you're adapting a recipe from cups to ounces, remember that 1 cup of maple syrup weighs roughly 8 ounces. This makes scaling recipes up or down straightforward, whether you're preparing a single serving or a family-sized batch of air-fryer desserts.</p>  

<h3>Common uses for maple syrup in air fryer recipes</h3>  
<p>Maple syrup isn’t just for pancakes—it’s a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. Use it to glaze roasted vegetables, sweeten homemade granola, or add depth to marinades. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> features creative ways to incorporate maple syrup, from breakfast to dinner.</p>  

<h3>UK vs. US measurements for maple syrup</h3>  
<p>While 2 cups of maple syrup equal 16 ounces in both the UK and US, cup sizes can vary slightly in other contexts. Always double-check if using a recipe from a different region. For more tips on adapting recipes, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert category</a>, where precise measurements are crucial.</p>  

<h2>Storing maple syrup for air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>To keep maple syrup fresh, store it in an airtight container in the fridge after opening. This prevents crystallisation and maintains its flavour for future air fryer creations. If your syrup thickens, gently warm it before measuring for accurate results.</p>
