<p>1 tablespoon (tbsp) of yeast weighs approximately 8 grams in the UK. This measurement is crucial for baking, especially when using an air fryer for recipes like bread or dough-based dishes. Precise yeast quantities ensure your dough rises perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate yeast measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of yeast is essential for successful baking. Too little yeast, and your dough won’t rise properly. Too much, and you might end up with an overly yeasty flavour or a collapsed bake. For air fryer recipes, where cooking times are shorter, precision is even more critical. Whether you’re making air fryer pizza dough or fluffy rolls, knowing that 1 tbsp of yeast equals 8 grams helps avoid mishaps.</p>  

<p>Many UK bakers rely on tablespoons for convenience, but grams offer greater accuracy. Digital scales eliminate guesswork, ensuring consistent results. If you’re exploring <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, this conversion will come in handy for everything from savoury bakes to sweet treats.</p>  

<h3>How to measure yeast without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and level it off for accuracy. Remember, yeast granules can settle, so fluff them up before measuring. For best results, consider investing in a digital scale—especially if you frequently bake with your air fryer. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<h2>Common yeast types and their weights</h2>  
<p>Not all yeast is the same. Active dry yeast, instant yeast, and fresh yeast have different weights per tablespoon. Active dry yeast weighs about 7 grams per tbsp, while instant yeast is closer to 8 grams. Fresh yeast, though less common in home baking, is heavier. Always check your recipe for the specific type required.</p>  

<p>For air fryer baking, instant yeast is often preferred because it doesn’t require proofing. This makes it ideal for quick recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/bread>air fryer bread</a>. If substituting one type for another, adjust the weight accordingly to avoid texture issues.</p>  

<h3>Converting yeast for different recipes</h3>  
<p>When scaling recipes up or down, use grams for precision. For example, if a recipe calls for 2 tbsp of yeast, you’ll need 16 grams. This is particularly useful for smaller batches in the air fryer, where slight measurement errors can have a bigger impact. <h2>Tips for storing yeast</h2>  
<p>Yeast loses potency over time, so proper storage is key. Keep it in an airtight container in the fridge or freezer to extend its shelf life. Before use, check for freshness by dissolving a pinch in warm water—if it bubbles, it’s active.
