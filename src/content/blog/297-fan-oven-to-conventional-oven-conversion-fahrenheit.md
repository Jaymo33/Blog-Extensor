<h2>Why Convert 297 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens use a convection system to distribute heat evenly, which means they cook faster than conventional ovens. A 297°F fan oven setting is roughly equivalent to 325°F in a standard oven. This difference accounts for the increased efficiency of fan-assisted cooking. If you're adapting a recipe from a fan oven to a conventional one, this conversion is essential. <h3>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h3>
<p>When converting 297 fan oven to Fahrenheit, remember that cooking times may also need tweaking. Conventional ovens generally take 10-15% longer to cook at the same temperature. For example, if a recipe calls for 20 minutes in a fan oven, aim for 22-23 minutes in a conventional one. Always check for doneness a few minutes early to avoid overbaking. This rule applies whether you're using an oven or an air fryer.</p>
