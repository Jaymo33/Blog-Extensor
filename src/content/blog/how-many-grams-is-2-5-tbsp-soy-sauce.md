<h2>Why measuring soy sauce in grams matters</h2>  
<p>Using grams instead of tablespoons guarantees consistency, especially in air fryer cooking where sauces reduce quickly. Soy sauce’s density varies slightly by brand, but 1 tbsp typically weighs 15 grams. For 2.5 tbsp, you’re looking at roughly 37.5 grams. This precision is crucial for dishes like teriyaki-glazed tofu or sticky ribs, where too much soy sauce can overpower the dish. Check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other common measurements.</p>

Digital kitchen scales are a worthwhile investment for air fryer enthusiasts who love Asian-inspired dishes.</p>  

<h3>How to measure soy sauce without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and level it off for accuracy. For 2.5 tbsp, fill the spoon halfway for the final portion. Remember, thicker soy sauces like dark or sweet varieties may weigh slightly more – up to 16 grams per tbsp. For best results, stick to light soy sauce unless the recipe specifies otherwise.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 2.5 tbsp soy sauce in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>This quantity is perfect for marinating two chicken breasts or a batch of air-fried mushrooms. Combine it with garlic, ginger, and a splash of honey for a quick glaze. <h3>Soy sauce substitutions by weight</h3>  
<p>If you’re out of soy sauce, 37.5 grams of tamari or coconut aminos works well. Liquid aminos are slightly lighter at about 14g per tbsp, so you’d need nearly 2.7 tbsp to match 2.5 tbsp of regular soy sauce. For gluten-free options, check labels carefully – some brands have different densities that could affect your recipe.</p>  

<h2>Tips for cleaning soy sauce residue from your air fryer</h2>  
<p>Soy sauce can leave sticky marks if spilled in your air fryer basket. After cooking, wipe with a damp cloth while the unit is slightly warm. For stubborn stains, use our recommended <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a> or a baking soda paste. Always dry thoroughly to prevent corrosion, especially with salty marinades.</p>  

<p>When using soy sauce in air fryer recipes, line the basket with parchment to catch drips. This prevents smoking and makes cleanup easier. For crispy results with glazed items like salmon or tofu, brush on the soy sauce mixture during the last few minutes of cooking to avoid burning.</p>
