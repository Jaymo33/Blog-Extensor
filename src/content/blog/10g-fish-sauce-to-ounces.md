<h2>Why Convert 10g of Fish Sauce to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those from the UK or US, list ingredients in ounces rather than grams. Converting 10g of fish sauce to ounces (0.35 oz) helps avoid guesswork and ensures your dish turns out perfectly. Fish sauce is a potent ingredient, and even small variations can affect flavour. For example, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> often call for precise amounts to balance salty, umami notes. Using a kitchen scale or conversion chart simplifies the process.</p>  

<p>If you don’t have a scale, visualising 10g of fish sauce can help. It’s roughly two teaspoons, but weighing is always more accurate. This is particularly useful when adapting recipes from different regions or scaling portions up or down. Consistency is key, especially when working with strong flavours like fish sauce.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 10g of Fish Sauce Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you’re without a scale, use a standard measuring spoon. Two level teaspoons of fish sauce will be close to 10g, though densities can vary slightly between brands. For best results, opt for a liquid measuring spoon or a syringe for precision. This method works well for marinades in <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> or dressings where balance is crucial. Remember, fish sauce is salty, so it’s better to slightly under-measure than overdo it.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 10g of Fish Sauce in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Fish sauce adds depth to air fryer dishes like glazes, dips, or stir-fry sauces. For instance, 10g (0.35 oz) can elevate a simple <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/shrimp>shrimp recipe</a> with minimal effort. It’s also a key component in many Asian-inspired marinades, pairing well with garlic, lime, and chilli. Because air fryers intensify flavours, precise measurements prevent overpowering your dish. A little goes a long way, so 10g is often enough for 2–3 servings.</p>  

<p>Another great use is in homemade air fryer sauces. Combine 10g of fish sauce with honey, soy sauce, and ginger for a quick glaze. <h3>Does Fish Sauce Brand Affect the Weight?</h3>  
<p>Most fish sauces have a similar density, so 10g will consistently equal ~0.35 ounces across brands. However, thicker or artisanal varieties might vary slightly.
