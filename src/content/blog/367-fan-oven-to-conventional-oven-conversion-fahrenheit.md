<h2>Why Convert 367 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air, reducing cooking time and often requiring lower temperatures. A conventional oven lacks this feature, so you'll need to increase the temperature slightly for similar results. For example, 367°F in a fan oven translates to about 392°F in a conventional oven. This ensures your baked goods or roasted dishes turn out just right. If you're adapting recipes for an air fryer, this conversion is equally useful.</p>
