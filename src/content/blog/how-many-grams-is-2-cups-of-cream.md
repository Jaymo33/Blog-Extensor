<h2>UK vs. US Measurements</h2>
<p>It’s worth noting that cup measurements can vary slightly between the UK and the US. In the UK, a standard cup holds 250ml, while in the US, it’s 240ml. For cream, this slight difference usually doesn’t drastically change the gram equivalent, but it’s good to be aware of. In both cases, 2.0 cups of cream will roughly translate to 480 grams.</p>

<h3>Types of Cream and Their Weights</h3>
<p>Not all creams weigh the same. Double cream, whipping cream, and single cream have slightly different densities, which can affect the gram measurement. For most standard recipes, however, the 480-gram rule for 2.0 cups holds true. If you’re working with a specific type, it’s always best to weigh it for precision.</p>

<h2>How to Measure Cream Without Scales</h2>
<p>If you don’t have kitchen scales, you can still measure 2.0 cups of cream accurately. Use a liquid measuring cup for the best results, ensuring the cream reaches the 2-cup mark without air bubbles. For thicker creams like double cream, gently spoon it into the cup to avoid compressing it, which could lead to over-measuring.</p>
