<p>1 tablespoon (tbsp) of gelatin powder weighs approximately 9 grams in the UK. This measurement is essential for precise cooking, especially when using an air fryer where accuracy matters. <h2>Why accurate gelatin measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Gelatin powder is a versatile ingredient used in everything from air fryer cheesecakes to homemade gummies. Too much or too little can affect texture and setting. For example, adding extra gelatin might make your dessert rubbery, while too little could leave it runny. That’s why converting 1 tbsp gelatin powder to grams is so important.</p>

<h2>Common mistakes when measuring gelatin</h2>  
<p>One frequent error is confusing gelatin powder with sheet gelatin, which has different measurements. Another is assuming all tablespoons are equal—UK and US tablespoons differ slightly. Always confirm you’re using UK measurements (1 tbsp = 15ml) for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Gelatin substitutes and conversions</h3>  
<p>If you’re out of gelatin powder, agar-agar is a popular substitute, but it requires different measurements. Typically, 1 tbsp gelatin powder (9g) equals about 1 tsp agar-agar. Always adjust your recipe accordingly and test small batches first.</p>
