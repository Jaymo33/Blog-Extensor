<h2>Why accurate garlic measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Garlic is a powerhouse ingredient that can make or break a dish. Too little, and your meal lacks depth; too much, and it overpowers other flavours. In an air fryer, where ingredients cook quickly, balanced seasoning is crucial. Many recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a> rely on precise garlic measurements for consistency. Whether you're cooking chicken, vegetables, or seafood, getting the garlic right elevates the dish.</p>  

<p>Minced garlic is denser than whole cloves, so volume-to-weight conversions are key. A standard US cup holds about 8 ounces of minced garlic, but UK measurements may vary slightly. Always check your recipe's origin to ensure accuracy. For a handy reference, bookmark <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to measure minced garlic correctly</h3>  
<p>Use a dry measuring cup for minced garlic, packing it lightly without crushing. Scoop the garlic into the cup and level it off with a straight edge. Avoid pressing down, as this can compact the garlic and skew the measurement. If your recipe calls for ounces, a kitchen scale is your best friend for precision.</p>

<h3>Storing minced garlic for freshness</h3>  
<p>Keep minced garlic in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze it in ice cube trays with a bit of oil. This way, you'll always have pre-measured garlic ready for your air fryer creations.</p>  

<h2>Converting other garlic measurements</h2>  
<p>If your recipe uses cloves instead of cups, here's a quick guide: one medium clove yields about half a teaspoon of minced garlic. That means a cup of minced garlic equals roughly 48 cloves.
