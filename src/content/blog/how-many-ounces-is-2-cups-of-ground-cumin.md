<h3>How to measure ground cumin without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method for cups. Fluff the cumin with a fork, spoon it into a measuring cup, and level off the excess. Avoid packing the spice down, as this leads to uneven measurements. For 2 cups of ground cumin to oz, this method ensures you're close to the 5.3-ounce mark. Storing cumin in airtight containers also preserves its potency—key for vibrant flavours in air fryer cooking.</p>

<h3>Buying and storing ground cumin</h3>  
<p>For the freshest flavour, buy whole cumin seeds and grind them as needed. Pre-ground cumin loses potency faster, so store it in a cool, dark place. Two cups of ground cumin (5.3 oz) will last 6–12 months if sealed properly. Label containers with the purchase date to track freshness—essential for maintaining bold flavours in air fryer dishes.</p>
