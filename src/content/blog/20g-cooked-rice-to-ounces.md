<p>20g of cooked rice equals approximately 0.71 ounces. This conversion is essential for air fryer users who need precise measurements for perfect results every time. Whether you're preparing a quick rice dish or measuring portions for meal prep, understanding this conversion will help you achieve consistent cooking outcomes in your air fryer.</p>

<h2>Why accurate rice measurements matter in air frying</h2>
<p>Getting your rice quantities right makes all the difference when cooking in an air fryer. Too much rice can lead to uneven cooking, while too little might dry out. The 20g cooked rice to ounces conversion (0.71 oz) helps you follow recipes precisely, especially when adapting traditional oven recipes for your air fryer. Many of our favourite <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> call for specific rice amounts to ensure perfect texture.</p>
<p>For those who love meal prepping, knowing that 20 grams of cooked rice equals 0.71 ounces helps with portion control. This measurement is particularly useful when making rice bowls or fried rice in your air fryer. You can find more handy conversions on our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> to simplify all your cooking measurements.</p>

<h2>How to measure 20g of cooked rice accurately</h2>
<p>While kitchen scales give the most precise measurement, not everyone has one handy. For 20g of cooked rice (0.71 ounces), you can use these visual estimates: about 2 level tablespoons or roughly a small ice cream scoop's worth. Remember that cooked rice expands, so these measures apply only after cooking.</p>

<h3>Using kitchen scales for perfect portions</h3>
<p>Digital scales are the gold standard for measuring 20 grams of cooked rice. Simply place your bowl on the scale, tare it to zero, then add rice until you hit 20g. This method eliminates guesswork and ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time. It's especially helpful when preparing multiple portions of our popular <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep recipes</a>.</p>

<h2>Converting other rice quantities</h2>
<p>Once you've mastered the 20g cooked rice to ounces conversion, you can easily scale up or down. For example, 40g would be 1.41 ounces, while 10g would be 0.35 ounces. These conversions come in handy when adjusting serving sizes for different air fryer models or family needs.</p>

<ul>
<li>50g cooked rice = 1.76 ounces</li>
<li>100g cooked rice = 3.53 ounces</li>
<li>150g cooked rice = 5.29 ounces</li>
</ul>
These conversions help when adapting recipes from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable rice dishes</a> collection for your air fryer.</p>

<h2>Tips for cooking rice in your air fryer</h2>
<p>Now that you know 20g of cooked rice equals 0.71 ounces, here are some pro tips for air frying success. Always spread rice evenly in the basket for consistent cooking, and consider adding a teaspoon of water to prevent drying out.
