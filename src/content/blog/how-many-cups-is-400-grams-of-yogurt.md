<h2>How to Measure 400 Grams of Yogurt Without a Scale</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the spoon-and-level method to measure yogurt in cups. Stir the yogurt first to ensure even consistency, then spoon it into a measuring cup. Level off the top with a straight edge for accuracy. Remember, 400 grams of yogurt equals roughly 1.6 to 1.7 cups, depending on your location. For thicker yogurts like Greek or Icelandic, you might need slightly less by volume. <h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements for Yogurt</h3>
<p>In the US, a standard cup holds 240 ml, while a UK cup is 250 ml. This means 400 grams of yogurt is about 1.7 US cups or 1.6 UK cups. These small differences matter in baking, where precision is key. If you’re using a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, check whether it follows UK or US measurements. When in doubt, opt for weight measurements (grams) for the most reliable results. This is especially helpful for air fryer recipes, where even small deviations can affect cooking times.</p>
