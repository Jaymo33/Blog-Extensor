<h3>How to measure margarine without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 500g of margarine using volume equivalents. One tablespoon of margarine weighs roughly 14g, so 500g would be about 35 tablespoons. However, weighing is more accurate, especially for air fryer recipes where consistency matters. For best results, invest in a digital scale or refer to our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> for pre-converted measurements.</p>

<h3>Does margarine type affect the weight?</h3>  
<p>Most margarines have a similar density, so 500g will generally equal 17.64 ounces regardless of brand. However, whipped or light margarines may vary slightly due to added air.
