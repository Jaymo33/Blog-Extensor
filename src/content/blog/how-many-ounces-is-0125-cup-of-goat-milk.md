<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to deliver crispy, evenly cooked results. Using the correct amount of goat milk—like 0.125 cup (1 oz)—can make or break a recipe. Too little, and your dish might dry out; too much, and it could become soggy. For more air fryer tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Goat milk is a popular alternative to cow's milk, especially in recipes like custards, pancakes, or even <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a>. Its slightly tangy flavour adds depth to dishes, but measuring it correctly is key. If you're using 0.125 cup of goat milk in an air fryer recipe, knowing it's 1 oz helps maintain consistency.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 0.125 Cup of Goat Milk</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.125 cup of goat milk, you can use a standard measuring cup. Fill it to the 1/8 cup mark, or use 2 tablespoons (since 1 cup = 16 tablespoons). For even more precision, a kitchen scale set to ounces will confirm you have exactly 1 oz of goat milk. This method is especially useful for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> where accuracy matters.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 0.125 Cup of Goat Milk</h2>  
<p>This small but mighty measurement pops up in many recipes. For example, 0.125 cup (1 oz) of goat milk might be used to thin a batter for air-fried <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/pancakes>pancakes</a> or to add creaminess to a marinade. It's also a great way to moisten breadcrumbs for coating chicken or fish before air frying.</p>
