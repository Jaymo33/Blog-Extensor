<p>0.33 cup of sesame oil equals approximately 2.64 ounces (oz). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precision ensures perfect results every time. Whether you’re whipping up a stir-fry or marinating chicken, knowing the exact weight of sesame oil can elevate your cooking.</p>

<h2>Why accurate sesame oil measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of sesame oil in your air fryer recipes ensures balanced flavours and prevents greasiness. Too little oil, and your dish might lack depth; too much, and it could become overly oily. For example, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>sesame-glazed salmon</a> requires just the right touch of oil to crisp the skin without overpowering the fish.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, like those found on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, rely on precise measurements for consistency. Whether you’re converting 0.33 cup of sesame oil to ounces or scaling up a recipe, accuracy is key. This is especially true for marinades, dressings, and sauces where oil ratios affect texture and taste.</p>  

<h3>How to measure sesame oil without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a liquid measuring cup for 0.33 cup of sesame oil. Fill it to the 1/3 cup mark, then subtract a teaspoon to get close to 0.33 cup. Remember, 0.33 cup sesame oil to ounces is roughly 2.64 oz, so you can also use a tablespoon measure (0.5 oz per tablespoon) for smaller adjustments.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 0.33 cup of sesame oil in air frying</h2>  
<p>Sesame oil adds a nutty aroma to dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air-fried chicken wings</a> or tofu. A 0.33 cup (2.64 oz) portion is ideal for coating 4-6 servings evenly. Try mixing it with soy sauce and honey for a glossy glaze, or drizzle it over vegetables before air frying for extra crispiness.</p>  

<p>For larger batches, check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> to scale up measurements. Whether you’re cooking for one or meal prepping, knowing how many ounces are in 0.33 cup of sesame oil saves time and reduces waste. It’s also handy for adapting recipes from cups to weight-based measurements.</p>  

<h3>Storing sesame oil for air fryer recipes</h3>  
<p>To keep sesame oil fresh, store it in a cool, dark place. Once opened, use it within 6 months for optimal flavour. Since 0.33 cup equals 2.64 oz, you’ll likely use it up quickly in recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>roasted Asian greens</a>. Always check for rancidity before use—fresh sesame oil should smell nutty, not bitter.</p>  

<h2>Converting other sesame oil amounts</h2>  
<p>Need to convert different amounts? Here’s a quick reference: 1 cup sesame oil = 8 oz, so 0.33 cup is one-third of that. For smaller quantities, 1 tablespoon sesame oil = 0.5 oz.
