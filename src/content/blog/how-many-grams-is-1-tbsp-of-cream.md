<h3>Types of cream and their weights</h3>  
<p>Single cream usually weighs about 15 grams per tbsp, while double cream can be closer to 18 grams due to its higher fat content. Whipped cream, being lighter, may weigh slightly less. Knowing these differences helps when substituting one type for another in your air fryer dishes.</p>  

<h2>How to measure cream without scales</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) for liquid cream, filling it just level for accuracy. For thicker creams, gently spoon it into the tablespoon to avoid air pockets. This method ensures you get as close to 15 grams as possible without weighing.</p>

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring cream</h3>  
<p>Overpacking the tablespoon or using a heaped scoop can lead to using more cream than needed, altering your recipe’s outcome. Always level off the tablespoon for the most accurate 1 tbsp cream to grams conversion.</p>
