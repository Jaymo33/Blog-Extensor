<h2>Why accurate paprika measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Paprika is a key ingredient in many air fryer recipes, from crispy chicken wings to flavourful roasted potatoes. Using too much or too little can throw off the balance of your dish. For example, 1.75 cups of paprika translates to roughly 14 ounces, but slight variations can occur depending on how tightly packed the spice is. That’s why weighing your ingredients is often more reliable than measuring by volume.</p>

<h2>Converting cups to ounces for paprika</h2>  
<p>When converting 1.75 cups of paprika to ounces, the general rule is that 1 cup of paprika weighs about 8 ounces. Therefore, 1.75 cups would be 14 ounces. However, this can vary slightly based on factors like the grind of the paprika and how it’s packed into the measuring cup. For best results, fluff the paprika with a spoon before measuring to avoid compacting it too tightly.</p>  
<p>If you’re working with a recipe that uses metric measurements, 14 ounces is roughly 397 grams. You can find more conversion tips on our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>, which covers everything from spices to meats and vegetables.</p>  

<h3>How to measure paprika for air fryer recipes</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate measurement, use a kitchen scale to weigh out your paprika. If you don’t have one, spoon the paprika into your measuring cup and level it off with a knife—don’t scoop directly from the bag, as this can pack the spice too densely. This method ensures you’re getting the right amount for recipes like smoky air-fried chicken or paprika-dusted fries.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 1.75 cups of paprika</h2>  
<p>1.75 cups (or 14 ounces) of paprika might seem like a lot, but it’s perfect for batch cooking or making spice blends. For example, you could use it to season a large batch of air-fried chicken thighs or mix it into a dry rub for ribs. Paprika also adds depth to vegetarian dishes, like roasted cauliflower or stuffed peppers.</p>

<h3>Storing paprika for freshness</h3>  
<p>Paprika loses its potency over time, so proper storage is key. Keep it in an airtight container in a cool, dark place—not near the stove or in direct sunlight. If you’ve measured out 1.75 cups (14 ounces) but only need a portion, transfer the rest to a sealed jar to maintain its vibrant colour and flavour.</p>  

<h2>Adjusting paprika in recipes</h2>  
<p>If a recipe calls for 1.75 cups of paprika but you prefer a milder or stronger flavour, you can adjust the amount. Just remember that 1.75 cups equals about 14 ounces, so scale up or down accordingly. For example, half the amount would be 7 ounces, while doubling it would be 28 ounces. Always taste as you go, especially when working with bold spices like paprika.</p>
