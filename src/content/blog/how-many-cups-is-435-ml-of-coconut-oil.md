<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>When it comes to cooking, especially with an air fryer, precision is key. Too much or too little coconut oil can affect texture, flavour, and cooking time. <h2>How to Measure Coconut Oil Correctly</h2>
<p>Coconut oil can be tricky to measure because it changes consistency with temperature. At room temperature, it's solid, but it melts when heated. Here's how to measure 435 ml of coconut oil accurately, whether it's solid or liquid.</p>

<h3>Measuring Solid Coconut Oil</h3>
<p>If your coconut oil is solid, pack it firmly into a measuring cup. Use a spoon or spatula to press it down, eliminating air gaps. Level off the top with a straight edge for an exact measurement. This method ensures you get the full 1.84 cups from 435 ml.</p>

<h3>Measuring Liquid Coconut Oil</h3>
<p>For liquid coconut oil, simply pour it into a liquid measuring cup until it reaches the 435 ml mark. If your recipe calls for cups, remember that 435 ml converts to roughly 1.84 cups. This method is quicker but works best for recipes where precision isn't as critical.</p>
