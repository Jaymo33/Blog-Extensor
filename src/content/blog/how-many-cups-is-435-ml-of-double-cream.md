<h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h2>  
<p>It's important to note that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. A UK cup holds 250 ml, while a US cup is 240 ml. This means 435 ml of double cream equals roughly 1.84 UK cups or 1.83 US cups. For most recipes, this small difference won’t drastically alter the outcome, but it’s good to be aware of it, especially when following international recipes.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Double Cream Without a Jug</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use a standard cup to measure 435 ml of double cream. Fill the cup to just below the brim for an approximate measurement. For more accuracy, consider using a kitchen scale—1 ml of double cream weighs roughly 1 gram, so 435 ml would be about 435 grams. This method is especially handy for air fryer recipes where precision matters.</p>

<h3>Storing Leftover Double Cream</h3>  
<p>If you don’t use all 435 ml of double cream at once, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to five days. You can also freeze it for longer storage, though the texture may change slightly when thawed. For best results, whisk it gently before using it in recipes. Always check for freshness—if it smells sour or develops lumps, it’s best to discard it.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Working with Double Cream</h2>  
<p>When heating double cream, do so gently to avoid curdling. In an air fryer, this means using lower temperatures and shorter cooking times. For whipped cream, chill the cream and your mixing bowl beforehand to achieve stiff peaks. If you’re adding it to a hot dish, temper it first by mixing a small amount of the hot liquid into the cream before combining everything.</p>
