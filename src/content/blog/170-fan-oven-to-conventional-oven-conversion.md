<h2>Understanding Fan Oven vs Conventional Oven Temperatures</h2>
<p>The main difference between fan ovens and conventional ovens lies in heat distribution. A fan oven circulates hot air evenly around the food, cooking it more efficiently at lower temperatures. When converting 170 fan oven to conventional oven settings, you typically need to increase the temperature by 20°C. This means 170°C in a fan oven becomes 150°C in a conventional oven.</p>
<p>This conversion becomes particularly important when using air fryers, which operate similarly to fan ovens. Understanding this 170 fan oven to conventional oven conversion helps you adapt recipes across different cooking methods while maintaining perfect results.</p>

<h2>Why 170 Fan Oven Conversion Matters for Air Fryers</h2>
<p>Air fryers essentially work like compact fan ovens, circulating hot air rapidly around food. When a recipe calls for 170 fan oven, your air fryer will typically use this exact temperature. However, if you're adapting a conventional oven recipe to your air fryer, you'll need to reverse the conversion - reducing the temperature by 20°C.</p>
<p>For example, if a traditional recipe suggests 170°C in a conventional oven, you'd set your air fryer to 150°C fan. This ensures your food cooks evenly without burning.
