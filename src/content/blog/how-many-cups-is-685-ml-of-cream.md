<p>685 ml of cream is approximately 2.89 cups in the UK or 2.9 cups in the US. This conversion is essential for recipes requiring precise measurements, especially when using an air fryer for baking or cooking creamy dishes. Whether you're whipping up a dessert or a savoury sauce, knowing how to convert millilitres to cups ensures consistency and perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Understanding 685 ml of Cream in Cups</h2>
<p>When converting 685 ml of cream to cups, it's important to note that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, while in the US, 1 cup is 240 ml. This means 685 ml of cream translates to roughly 2.89 UK cups or 2.9 US cups. For air fryer recipes, precision matters, so using a measuring jug or scales can help avoid guesswork.</p>
<p>Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> call for cream, from creamy pasta bakes to indulgent desserts. If you're adapting a recipe from millilitres to cups, this conversion ensures you won't end up with a dish that's too runny or too thick. Always level off your measuring cups for accuracy, especially when working with rich ingredients like cream.</p>

<h3>Why Accurate Cream Measurements Matter</h3>
<p>Using the correct amount of cream affects texture, consistency, and taste. Too much cream in an air fryer recipe can make dishes soggy, while too little might leave them dry. For perfect <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a> or sauces, stick to the 685 ml to cups conversion and adjust slightly if needed based on your air fryer's performance.</p>

For <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversions</a>, remember that ingredients behave differently under rapid air circulation, so precise measurements help maintain balance.</p>

Always check whether your recipe uses UK or US cup measurements to avoid discrepancies.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Cream Without Scales</h3>
<p>If you don't have measuring cups or scales, you can estimate 685 ml of cream using common household items. A standard UK mug holds about 300 ml, so 685 ml would be just over two mugs. For more accuracy, use a liquid measuring jug and check the millilitre markings at eye level to avoid errors.</p>
