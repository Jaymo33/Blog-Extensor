<h2>Why Convert Grams to Ounces for Double Cream?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but some kitchen scales or international recipes might list ounces. Converting 50g of double cream to ounces (1.76 oz) ensures you’re using the right amount for your air fryer creations. Double cream is thicker and richer than single cream, so even small measurement errors can affect texture and taste. For more conversion tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>If you’re making a dessert like a creamy custard or a savoury sauce, precision matters. Air fryers cook quickly, so balancing ingredients correctly is key. A slight overpour of double cream could make your dish too heavy, while too little might leave it lacking richness. Stick to 1.76 ounces for 50g to nail the consistency every time.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 50g of Double Cream Accurately</h3>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the easiest way to measure 50g of double cream. If your scale displays ounces, it should read 1.76 oz. No scale? A UK tablespoon holds about 15g of double cream, so roughly 3.5 tablespoons will get you close to 50g. For more air fryer measurement hacks, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>.</p>
