<p>218°C in a fan oven equals approximately 200°C in a conventional oven. This 18°C difference is crucial for perfect baking and roasting results. <h2>Why Fan Oven Temperatures Differ</h2>
<p>Fan ovens use a built-in fan to distribute heat evenly, reducing cooking times by about 20%. This means you'll typically set a fan oven 20°C lower than a conventional oven. The 218 fan oven to conventional oven conversion follows this general rule, landing at 200°C. This principle applies whether you're baking cookies or roasting chicken - the circulating air ensures even cooking without hot spots.</p>
