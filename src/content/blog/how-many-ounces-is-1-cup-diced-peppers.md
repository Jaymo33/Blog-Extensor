<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise cooking times and temperatures, so ingredient measurements need to be spot-on. Using the correct weight ensures your peppers cook evenly and retain their vibrant colour and crunch. <h3>How to measure diced peppers correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a kitchen scale to weigh your diced peppers. If you don’t have one, lightly pack the peppers into a measuring cup without crushing them. Remember that smaller dice will fit more tightly into a cup, potentially increasing the weight slightly. For best results, stick to the 5.3-ounce benchmark.</p>  

<h2>Common recipes using 1 cup of diced peppers</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes call for 1 cup of diced peppers, such as stuffed peppers, stir-fries, and fajitas. <h3>Adjusting recipes for different pepper types</h3>  
<p>Bell peppers, jalapeños, and other varieties have slightly different densities, so weights may vary. As a rule, 1 cup of any diced pepper will hover around 5–6 ounces. For spicy dishes, adjust quantities based on heat preference. Always taste as you go!</p>
