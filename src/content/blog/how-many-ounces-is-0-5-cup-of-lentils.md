Whether you’re making a hearty lentil stew or crispy air-fried lentil snacks, knowing the exact weight helps with consistency. <h2>Why accurate lentil measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of lentils ensures your air fryer recipes have the right texture and flavour. Too many lentils can make dishes dry, while too few might leave them undercooked. For example, if you’re making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air-fried lentil falafel</a>, 0.5 cup (3.5 oz) is the perfect portion for four servings. Lentils are also packed with protein and fibre, so measuring them correctly helps you track nutritional intake. Whether you’re cooking for one or meal prepping, accuracy saves time and reduces waste. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy tips.</p>  

<p>Different types of lentils, like red, green, or brown, have slightly varying densities, but 0.5 cup generally weighs around 3.5 ounces. If you’re using pre-cooked lentils, remember they absorb water, so adjust your measurements accordingly. For <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable-based air fryer recipes</a>, lentils add a hearty touch without overpowering other ingredients. Always level off your measuring cup for the most accurate results. A kitchen scale can also help if you’re doubling or halving recipes.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.5 cup of lentils without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use a dry measuring cup and fill it halfway for 0.5 cup. Avoid packing the lentils down, as this can lead to over-measuring. For smaller quantities, a tablespoon holds roughly 0.3 ounces of lentils, so 11 tablespoons equal 0.5 cup. This method is handy for quick adjustments mid-recipe. Keep in mind that <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning your air fryer</a> after cooking lentils prevents residue buildup. Proper measurement and maintenance ensure your appliance lasts longer.</p>  

<h2>Common air fryer recipes using 0.5 cup lentils</h2>  
<p>From crispy snacks to protein-packed mains, 0.5 cup of lentils (3.5 oz) is a versatile ingredient. Try air-fried lentil patties by mixing them with breadcrumbs and spices for a quick vegetarian burger. For a crunchy snack, toss cooked lentils in olive oil and air fry until crisp. Lentils also work well in <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep recipes</a>, like stuffed peppers or savoury muffins. Experiment with spices to match your taste—cumin and paprika add a smoky depth. Always preheat your air fryer for even cooking.</p>  

<p>Another great option is lentil-based dough for flatbreads or crackers. Combine 0.5 cup of blended lentils with flour and water, then air fry for a gluten-free alternative. For a hearty breakfast, mix lentils with eggs and cheese before air frying. The key is to balance moisture, so add a splash of water if the mixture feels dry. Visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> for more creative ways to use lentils in your air fryer. With precise measurements, the possibilities are endless.</p>  

<h3>Storing and prepping lentils for air frying</h3>  
<p>Cooked lentils can be stored in the fridge for up to five days, making them ideal for batch cooking. Measure out 0.5 cup portions (3.5 oz) and freeze them for later use. When ready to cook, thaw and pat dry to avoid excess moisture in the air fryer. Dry lentils can also be air-fried directly for a crunchy texture—just soak them first to reduce cooking time. Proper storage ensures you always have the right amount on hand for quick meals.</p>
