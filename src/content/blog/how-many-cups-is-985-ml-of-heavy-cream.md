<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>It's worth noting that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. This means 985 ml of heavy cream converts to roughly 4.16 cups in the UK and 4.17 cups in the US. For air fryer recipes, always check whether the recipe uses UK or US measurements to ensure accuracy.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 985 ml of Heavy Cream</h2>  
<p>If you don't have a measuring jug with millilitre markings, you can use standard cups to measure 985 ml of heavy cream. Start by measuring out 4 full cups (either UK or US, depending on your recipe), then add an additional 2-3 tablespoons to reach the exact amount. This method works well for most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> that call for heavy cream.</p>  

<p>For even greater precision, consider using a kitchen scale. Weighing heavy cream can be more accurate than volume measurements, especially for delicate desserts like mousses or custards. One millilitre of heavy cream weighs approximately 1.04 grams, so 985 ml would be around 1024 grams. This approach is particularly useful for professional bakers or those experimenting with new recipes.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Heavy Cream</h2>  
<p>Once opened, heavy cream should be stored in the refrigerator and used within a few days. To extend its shelf life, freeze it in an airtight container or ice cube trays for smaller portions. When thawed, give it a good stir or whisk to restore its texture before using it in your air fryer recipes. Proper storage ensures your heavy cream stays fresh and ready for your next culinary creation.</p>  

<p>If you're unsure whether your heavy cream is still good, give it a quick sniff test. Spoiled cream will have a sour smell and may appear lumpy.
