<h2>How to measure 125g minced garlic without scales</h2>  
<p>Not everyone has kitchen scales handy, but there are easy ways to estimate 125g of minced garlic. One tablespoon of minced garlic weighs roughly 10g, so 12.5 tablespoons would equal 125g. However, this method isn’t as precise as using scales, so it’s best for rough estimates. For air fryer recipes where accuracy is crucial, investing in a digital scale is worthwhile.</p>

<h2>Common mistakes when measuring minced garlic</h2>  
<p>One mistake is confusing minced garlic with garlic paste or puree, which have different densities. Minced garlic is coarser, so 125g will take up more volume than paste. Another error is packing the garlic too tightly when measuring, which can lead to inaccuracies. Always use a light hand for consistent results. <p>Storing minced garlic correctly also affects its weight and freshness. If your 125g minced garlic seems dry or clumpy, it might have lost moisture, altering its weight slightly. Freshly minced garlic is ideal for air fryer recipes, as it delivers the best flavour. Keep it in an airtight container in the fridge to maintain its quality.</p>
