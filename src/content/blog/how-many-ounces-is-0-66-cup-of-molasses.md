<h2>Why accurate molasses measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Molasses is a thick, sticky sweetener that can easily throw off a recipe if not measured correctly. Too much can make your dish overly sweet or dense, while too little might leave it lacking flavour. For air fryer recipes, precise measurements ensure even cooking and perfect texture. Whether you’re making sticky ribs or molasses cookies, 0.66 cup (7.5 oz) is a common quantity in many British recipes. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for other handy measurement swaps.</p>  

<p>Using a kitchen scale for molasses is the most accurate method, but cup measurements work well too if you level them properly. Molasses clings to measuring cups, so lightly greasing the cup can help it slide out cleanly. For more tips on measuring sticky ingredients, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>, which includes detailed guides for air fryer success.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 0.66 cup of molasses without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, use the displacement method: fill a liquid measuring cup with water to 1 cup, then add molasses until the water reaches 1.66 cups. The molasses will displace the water, giving you 0.66 cup. This trick works for any thick liquid and is especially handy for air fryer marinades. For more clever kitchen hacks, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a>, where molasses often stars.</p>  

<h2>Common uses for 0.66 cup (7.5 oz) of molasses</h2>  
<p>This quantity of molasses is perfect for glazing ham, baking ginger cakes, or making barbecue sauces in your air fryer. Its rich, caramelised flavour enhances both sweet and savoury dishes. Many traditional British recipes, like parkin or treacle tart, rely on this measurement for balanced sweetness. <h3>Storing molasses for long-term use</h3>  
<p>Once opened, molasses lasts up to a year if stored in a cool, dark place. Transfer it to an airtight container to prevent crystallisation. For frequent air fryer users, keeping molasses on hand means you’re always ready to whip up a delicious glaze or marinade. Remember, 0.66 cup is roughly half a standard UK jar, making it easy to portion.</p>
