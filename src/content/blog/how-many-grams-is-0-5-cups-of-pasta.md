<h2>Why Accurate Pasta Measurements Matter in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>Air fryers cook food faster than conventional ovens, so ingredient measurements need extra precision. Using 56 grams (0.5 cups) of pasta ensures even cooking without overcrowding the basket. Overfilling leads to clumpy, unevenly cooked noodles, while too little may dry out. <p>Different pasta shapes also affect volume-to-weight ratios. Penne and fusilli take up more space in a measuring cup than spaghetti, but weigh less gram-for-gram. <h3>Measuring 0.5 Cups of Pasta Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don't have kitchen scales, use the "finger test" for short pasta like macaroni. Fill a 0.5 cup measure loosely without packing, then level off the top. For long noodles like spaghetti, a 0.5 cup bundle is roughly the diameter of a £1 coin. Remember that 0.5 cups of cooked pasta expands to about 1 cup, so adjust liquid ingredients accordingly in <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer pasta recipes</a>.</p>

<h3>Common Pasta Types and Their 0.5 Cup Weights</h3>  
<p>While 56g is the average, here's how 0.5 cups converts for specific shapes: elbow macaroni (50g), penne (60g), fusilli (55g), and spaghetti (58g).
