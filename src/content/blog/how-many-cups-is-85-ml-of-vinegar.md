<p>85 ml of vinegar is approximately 0.36 cups in the UK or 0.35 cups in the US. This small but crucial measurement is often needed for marinades, dressings, or pickling in air fryer recipes. Whether you're whipping up a tangy glaze for chicken or balancing flavours in a salad dressing, knowing this conversion ensures precision in your cooking.</p>  

<h2>Understanding Vinegar Measurements for Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Vinegar is a versatile ingredient used in everything from air fryer marinades to pickling solutions. When a recipe calls for 85 ml of vinegar, it’s essential to measure accurately to avoid overpowering or underwhelming flavours. In the UK, 1 cup equals 250 ml, so 85 ml converts to roughly 0.36 cups. In the US, where 1 cup is 240 ml, 85 ml is about 0.35 cups.</p>  

<p>For air fryer enthusiasts, precise measurements like this can make or break a dish. Too much vinegar can lead to overly acidic flavours, while too little might leave your meal tasting bland. If you're working with an <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>, always double-check whether the recipe uses UK or US measurements to avoid confusion.</p>
