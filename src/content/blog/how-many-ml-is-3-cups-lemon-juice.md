<h2>Why Accurate Lemon Juice Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of lemon juice can make or break a dish. Too little, and your recipe might lack brightness; too much, and it could become overpoweringly sour. For air fryer cooking, where flavours concentrate due to rapid air circulation, precision is even more critical. <h3>US vs UK Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>It’s worth noting that cup sizes differ slightly between the US and UK. A US cup holds 240 ml, while a UK cup is 284 ml. However, most modern recipes—including those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>—use the US standard. So, 3.0 US cups of lemon juice equal 710 ml, whereas 3.0 UK cups would be 852 ml. Always check which measurement system your recipe follows to avoid errors.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Lemon Juice for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a liquid measuring cup or a kitchen scale. If you’re converting 3.0 cups of lemon juice to ml, a measuring jug with millilitre markings is ideal. Freshly squeezed lemon juice is best for flavour, but bottled works in a pinch—just ensure it’s pure juice without added sugars or preservatives.</p>
