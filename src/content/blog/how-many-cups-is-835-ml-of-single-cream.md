<h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h2>
<p>It's worth noting that cup measurements differ slightly between the UK and the US. In the UK, 1 cup is equivalent to 250 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. This means 835 ml of single cream converts to 3.5 cups in the UK and 3.52 cups in the US. While the difference may seem minor, it can add up in recipes requiring large quantities.</p>
<p>For air fryer cooking, where ingredients are often measured precisely, these small variations can impact the final dish. If you're using a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>our conversion chart</a>, always check whether it follows UK or US measurements to avoid discrepancies.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Single Cream Without a Measuring Cup</h3>
<p>If you don't have a measuring cup handy, you can still measure 835 ml of single cream accurately. A standard UK metric measuring jug will have millilitre markings, making it easy to pour the exact amount. Alternatively, you can use a kitchen scale—single cream weighs roughly 1 gram per millilitre, so 835 ml would be 835 grams.</p>

<h3>Storing Leftover Single Cream</h3>
<p>If your recipe doesn't use the full 835 ml of single cream, you can store the leftovers in the fridge for up to three days. Make sure it's in an airtight container to maintain freshness. Alternatively, freeze it in ice cube trays for smaller portions—just thaw and stir well before using.</p>

<h2>Tips for Cooking with Single Cream in an Air Fryer</h2>
<p>When using single cream in air fryer recipes, it's important to avoid overheating, as it can curdle. For best results, add the cream towards the end of cooking or use it in recipes with shorter cooking times. Dishes like creamy sauces or custards benefit from gentle heating to maintain a smooth texture.</p>
