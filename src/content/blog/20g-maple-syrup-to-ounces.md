<h2>Why accurate maple syrup measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Maple syrup is a sticky, dense liquid where small weight differences can significantly impact your recipe’s sweetness and texture. In air fryer cooking, where heat circulates rapidly, overly sweet glazes can burn while too little syrup might leave dishes tasting bland. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> helps eliminate guesswork for consistent outcomes.</p>  
<p>British recipes often use grams, while some older cookbooks reference ounces. When adapting <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> across measurement systems, this 20g maple syrup to ounces conversion (0.71 oz) becomes invaluable. For context, 1 UK fluid ounce of maple syrup weighs about 28g – but weight ounces (used here) differ slightly from fluid ounces.</p>  

<h3>Tools for measuring maple syrup</h3>  
<p>Digital kitchen scales are ideal for weighing 20g of maple syrup directly. If using measuring spoons, note that 1 tablespoon equals roughly 20g (0.71 oz), but viscosity varies with temperature. For sticky ingredients like maple syrup, lightly greasing your measuring spoon or cup prevents waste and ensures accuracy.</p>
