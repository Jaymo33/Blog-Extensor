<h3>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly and evenly, but only if the ingredients are measured correctly. Too much or too little coconut milk can alter the moisture content of your dish, leading to undercooked or overly dry results. For example, if you're making a coconut milk-based marinade for chicken, the right balance ensures crispiness without sogginess.</p>

<h2>UK vs. US Cup Conversions for 810 ml of Coconut Milk</h2>
<p>In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, meaning 810 ml of coconut milk is roughly 3.42 cups. In the US, where 1 cup is 240 ml, 810 ml converts to about 3.38 cups. While the difference seems minor, it’s worth noting if you're following a precise recipe. Many air fryer recipes on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> specify which measurement system they use, so always check before starting.</p>
<p>If you're unsure, err on the side of the UK measurement, as most British recipes will use this standard. For dishes like coconut shrimp or tofu curry, the exact amount of coconut milk can impact the sauce's thickness and flavour. A handy tip is to keep a set of measuring cups specifically for air fryer cooking to avoid confusion.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 810 ml of Coconut Milk Without a Measuring Cup</h3>
<p>If you don’t have a measuring cup, you can use a standard mug or glass, though this method is less precise. A typical UK mug holds about 300 ml, so 810 ml would be just under 3 mugs full. For US measurements, a standard cup is 240 ml, so you’d need roughly 3 and a third cups. For best results, invest in a good set of measuring tools, especially if you frequently use your air fryer.</p>

<h3>Storing Leftover Coconut Milk</h3>
