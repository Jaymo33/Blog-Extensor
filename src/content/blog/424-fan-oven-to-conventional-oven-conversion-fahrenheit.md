<h2>Why Convert Fan Oven to Conventional Oven Temperatures?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens use a built-in fan to circulate hot air, reducing cooking times and ensuring even heat distribution. This means recipes designed for conventional ovens often need temperature adjustments. A 424°F fan oven setting roughly translates to 400°F in a conventional oven, but this can vary slightly depending on your appliance. Always refer to your oven's manual for precise guidelines, especially when baking delicate items like cakes or cookies.</p>

<h3>How Does This Affect Baking and Roasting?</h3>
<p>Baking at 424°F in a fan oven versus 400°F in a conventional oven can impact texture and cooking time. For example, cakes may rise more evenly in a fan oven, while roasted meats could develop a crispier exterior. If you're transitioning from a fan oven to a conventional one, keep an eye on your food during the last few minutes of cooking to prevent burning.</p>
