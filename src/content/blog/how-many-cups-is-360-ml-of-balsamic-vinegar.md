<h2>Understanding Balsamic Vinegar Measurements</h2>  
<p>Balsamic vinegar is a staple in many kitchens, especially for air fryer recipes like glazes, dressings, or marinades. Measuring it correctly ensures balanced flavours. A standard US cup holds 240 ml, while a UK cup holds 284 ml. This means 360 ml of balsamic vinegar translates to roughly 1.52 US cups or 1.27 UK cups. For air fryer cooking, precision matters—whether you're making sticky chicken wings or roasted vegetables.</p>

<h3>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h3>  
<p>Too much balsamic vinegar can overpower a dish, while too little might leave it lacking depth. For air fryer recipes, where ingredients cook quickly and intensely, balance is key. A precise 360 ml (or 1.52 US cups) ensures your marinade or glaze has the right tangy-sweet ratio. If you're scaling recipes up or down, always double-check conversions to keep flavours consistent.</p>  

<h2>Converting 360 ml of Balsamic Vinegar for Recipes</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes call for balsamic vinegar—whether for drizzling over roasted veggies or glazing meats. If a recipe lists 360 ml, you'll need about 1.5 US cups or just over 1.25 UK cups. For smaller batches, halving or quartering the amount ensures you don’t waste ingredients. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> has plenty of ideas to put your balsamic vinegar to good use.</p>  

<p>If you're baking or making sauces, remember that balsamic vinegar reduces when heated. Starting with 360 ml (1.52 cups) gives you room to simmer it down to a thicker consistency. This is especially useful for air fryer dishes where a sticky glaze enhances texture and flavour.</p>
