<p>1.5 tablespoons (tbsp) of tomato paste equals approximately 23 grams. This conversion is essential for precise cooking, especially when using an air fryer where measurements matter. Whether you're making a rich pasta sauce or a flavourful marinade, knowing the exact weight ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve the perfect texture and flavour. Too much tomato paste can make dishes overly thick or intense, while too little might leave them lacking depth. By converting 1.5 tbsp of tomato paste to grams, you ensure your recipes turn out just right. <p>Tomato paste is a concentrated ingredient, so small measurement differences can have a big impact. Using grams instead of tablespoons removes guesswork, especially when scaling recipes up or down. This is particularly useful for air fryer cooking, where even distribution of ingredients is key.</p>

<h3>How to measure 1.5 tbsp of tomato paste</h3>
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can still measure 1.5 tbsp of tomato paste accurately. Use a standard tablespoon and level it off with a knife for precision. <h2>Common uses for 1.5 tbsp of tomato paste in air fryer recipes</h2>
<p>This small but mighty amount of tomato paste works wonders in air fryer dishes. It’s perfect for adding depth to marinades, glazes, or even as a base for homemade ketchup. <h3>Storing leftover tomato paste</h3>
<p>After measuring out your 1.5 tbsp of tomato paste, you’ll likely have some left over. Transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze tablespoon-sized portions in ice cube trays – perfect for future air fryer creations.</p>
