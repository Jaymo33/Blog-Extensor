<h2>Why accurate treacle measurements matter in air frying</h2>  
<p>Treacle’s thick, sticky consistency means even small variations can affect your dish. In an air fryer, where heat circulates rapidly, too much treacle can cause burning, while too little may leave your food lacking flavour. Measuring 0.5 tbsp of treacle in grams (10.5g) ensures consistency across batches. This is especially useful for recipes like gingerbread or marinades found in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>.</p>  

<p>Air fryers excel at caramelising sugars quickly, so precise treacle measurements prevent over-browning. If you’re adapting oven recipes for your air fryer, converting tablespoons to grams eliminates guesswork. For sticky glazes or desserts, 10.5 grams of treacle per 0.5 tbsp delivers balanced sweetness without overpowering other ingredients.</p>  

<h3>How to measure treacle without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have digital scales, use the spoon-and-level method for 0.5 tbsp of treacle. Fill your tablespoon halfway, then scrape off excess with a straight edge for an accurate 10.5-gram portion. Treacle’s viscosity makes it tricky, so warming the spoon slightly helps it slide off cleanly. For more measurement hacks, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning and usage guides</a>.</p>

<h3>Converting other treacle measurements</h3>  
<p>Once you know 0.5 tbsp equals 10.5 grams, scaling recipes becomes simple. Double it for 1 tbsp (21g), or halve for 0.25 tbsp (5.25g). <h2>Storing treacle for air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Proper storage keeps treacle at the right consistency for measuring 0.5 tbsp (10.5g) accurately. Keep it in a cool cupboard, and wipe the rim after each use to prevent hardening. If your treacle crystallises, gently warm the jar in hot water before measuring. This ensures every 0.5 tbsp portion weighs exactly 10.5 grams for consistent air fryer results.</p>
