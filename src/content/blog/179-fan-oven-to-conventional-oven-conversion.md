<h2>Understanding Fan Oven vs Conventional Oven Temperatures</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air using a fan, cooking food faster and more evenly than conventional ovens. When a recipe calls for 179 fan oven, it's specifically designed for this efficient heat distribution system. The general rule for conversion is to reduce the temperature by 20°C when moving from fan to conventional ovens.</p>

<h3>Why 179°C Fan Oven is Common</h3>
<p>179°C fan oven (160°C conventional) is a popular temperature for baking and roasting. It's hot enough to crisp surfaces while gently cooking interiors - perfect for everything from Victoria sponges to roasted vegetables. This temperature also prevents burning while ensuring thorough cooking.</p>
