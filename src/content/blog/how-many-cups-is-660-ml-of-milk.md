<p>660 ml of milk is approximately 2.79 cups. This conversion is essential for anyone using recipes that require precise measurements, especially when cooking or baking with an air fryer. <h2>Understanding the Conversion</h2>
<p>Converting millilitres to cups can seem tricky, but it's straightforward once you know the basics. One standard cup holds about 236.59 ml of liquid, which means 660 ml of milk is roughly 2.79 cups. This measurement is crucial for recipes where accuracy matters, such as baking or making sauces for your air fryer dishes. For more conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>

<p>Different countries use varying cup sizes, so it's important to note whether your recipe follows UK or US measurements. In the UK, a cup is typically 250 ml, while in the US, it's 236.59 ml. This slight difference can affect your recipe, so always double-check the origin of your measurements. If you're unsure, stick to the standard 236.59 ml per cup for consistency.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>Precision is key in cooking and baking, especially when using an air fryer. Too much or too little milk can alter the texture and taste of your dish. For example, adding too much milk to a cake batter can make it soggy, while too little can result in a dry, crumbly texture. Accurate measurements ensure your recipes turn out as intended, whether you're making a creamy pasta sauce or a fluffy sponge cake.</p>

<p>If you're working with a recipe that lists ingredients in millilitres but prefer using cups, this conversion will save you time and hassle. It also helps when scaling recipes up or down to suit your needs.</p>
