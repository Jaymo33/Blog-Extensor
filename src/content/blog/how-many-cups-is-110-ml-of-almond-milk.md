<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of almond milk can affect the texture and taste of your air fryer recipes. Too much liquid can make batters runny, while too little may result in dry, crumbly outcomes. For example, if a recipe calls for 110 ml of almond milk, measuring it as 0.46 cups ensures your dish turns out perfectly. Many of our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> rely on precise measurements to deliver consistent results every time.</p>  

<p>Almond milk is a popular dairy-free alternative, but its slightly thinner consistency means measurements need extra attention. If you're converting 110 ml of almond milk to cups, remember that US and UK cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup is 284 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. For most air fryer recipes, the US measurement (0.46 cups) is the standard reference.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 110 ml of Almond Milk Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use a standard tablespoon to measure 110 ml of almond milk. One tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so 110 ml is roughly 7 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon. This method is handy for small batches or when you're improvising with ingredients. <h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Almond Milk</h2>  
<p>One frequent error is confusing fluid ounces with millilitres, leading to incorrect measurements. Another is not shaking the almond milk carton before pouring, which can result in uneven consistency. Always stir or shake well before measuring 110 ml of almond milk to ensure accuracy.
