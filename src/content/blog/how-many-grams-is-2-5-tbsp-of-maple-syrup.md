<p>2.5 tablespoons (tbsp) of maple syrup equals approximately 50 grams (g). This conversion is essential for precise measurements in air fryer recipes, ensuring your dishes turn out perfectly every time. Whether you’re drizzling it over roasted vegetables or using it as a glaze for meats, knowing the exact weight helps maintain consistency.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of maple syrup can make or break your air fryer recipes. Too much syrup can lead to overly sticky or burnt results, while too little might leave your dish lacking flavour. For example, when making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer glazed salmon</a>, 2.5 tbsp of maple syrup (50g) provides the ideal balance of sweetness without overpowering the fish. Accurate measurements also ensure even cooking, which is crucial for air fryer success.</p>

<h2>How to measure maple syrup correctly</h2>  
<p>Measuring sticky ingredients like maple syrup can be tricky, but a few simple tips can help. First, lightly grease your measuring spoon with oil to prevent the syrup from sticking. Alternatively, warm the spoon slightly under hot water before scooping. This ensures the syrup slides off easily, giving you an accurate 2.5 tbsp measurement.</p>  

<h3>Using a kitchen scale for precision</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, weigh your maple syrup directly in grams. Place your mixing bowl on the scale, tare it to zero, then pour the syrup until it reaches 50g. This method eliminates guesswork and is especially useful for baking recipes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a>, where precision is key.</p>
