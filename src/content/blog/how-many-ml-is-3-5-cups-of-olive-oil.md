<h2>Understanding the Conversion</h2>
<p>Converting cups to millilitres can be tricky, especially when dealing with liquids like olive oil. One standard UK cup holds around 236.6 ml, so multiplying this by 3.5 gives you the total millilitres. This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes, where even small measurement errors can affect texture and cooking times. If you're unsure, our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> can help simplify the process.</p>

<p>Different countries use varying cup sizes, which can lead to confusion. For example, the US cup is slightly larger at 240 ml, but the difference is minimal for most recipes. Always check whether your recipe uses UK or US measurements to avoid discrepancies. Olive oil’s density also means it won’t compress, so measuring by volume is reliable for consistent results.</p>
