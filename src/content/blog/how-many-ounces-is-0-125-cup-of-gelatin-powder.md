<h2>Why Accurate Gelatin Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of gelatin powder is crucial for achieving the desired texture in your air fryer desserts. This is particularly important for recipes like air fryer cheesecakes or mousses, where gelatin plays a key role.</p>

<h3>How to Measure Gelatin Powder Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, measuring 0.125 cup of gelatin powder can be done using standard measuring spoons. One ounce of gelatin powder is roughly equivalent to 2 tablespoons. However, for the most precise results, especially in air fryer cooking, a digital scale is recommended. Lightly spoon the powder into the measuring cup and level it off for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Does Gelatin Type Affect the Weight?</h3>  
<p>Different types of gelatin, such as powdered or sheet gelatin, have slight variations in density. However, 0.125 cup of standard powdered gelatin will still weigh approximately 1 ounce. If you’re using sheet gelatin, refer to the packaging for specific conversions, as it may differ slightly from powdered forms.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Working with Gelatin in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>When using gelatin in air fryer recipes, always bloom it first by soaking it in cold water before dissolving it in warm liquid. This ensures smooth incorporation and prevents clumping. Since air fryers cook quickly, gelatin-based desserts may need refrigeration to set properly after cooking.</p>
