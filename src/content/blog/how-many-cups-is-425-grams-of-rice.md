<h2>Why Accurate Rice Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of rice is crucial, especially in an air fryer where space and heat distribution affect cooking. Too much rice can lead to uneven cooking, while too little may dry out. 425 grams of rice is a common quantity for family-sized portions, making this conversion handy for meal prep. If you're exploring new recipes, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipe collection</a> for inspiration.</p>  

<p>Different rice types, like basmati or jasmine, have slight variations in volume per gram. However, the standard conversion for 425 grams works well for most white and brown rice varieties. For best results, fluff the rice before measuring to avoid compacting it into the cup.</p>  

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>In the UK, 1 cup of rice is roughly 200 grams, so 425 grams equals about 2.1 cups. In the US, a cup is closer to 170 grams, making 425 grams approximately 2.5 cups. Always check your recipe's origin to avoid measurement mishaps. For more tips, visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep category</a>.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Rice for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>For air fryer cooking, lightly spoon the rice into a measuring cup without pressing down. This prevents overpacking and ensures accurate volume. If your air fryer has a rice setting, 425 grams typically cooks evenly in batches. Need a cleaner for your appliance? Try our recommended <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a>.</p>  

<p>Pre-rinsing rice removes excess starch, which can affect volume. After rinsing, let the rice drain well before measuring 425 grams. This step is especially useful for sticky rice dishes or fried rice made in the air fryer.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Converting Grams to Cups</h3>  
<p>Using a liquid measuring cup for rice is a frequent error, as it can lead to incorrect readings. Always use dry measuring cups for grains. Another pitfall is ignoring rice type—short-grain rice may need slightly fewer cups than long-grain for 425 grams.</p>  

<h2>Air Fryer Rice Cooking Tips</h2>  
<p>Cooking 425 grams of rice in an air fryer works best with a little extra water to account for faster evaporation. Covering the rice with foil helps retain moisture.
