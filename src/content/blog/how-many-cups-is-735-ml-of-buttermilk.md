<h2>Understanding Buttermilk Measurements</h2>  
<p>Buttermilk is a staple in baking, adding moisture and tang to recipes like pancakes, cakes, and fried chicken. Measuring it correctly ensures consistency. In the UK, 1 cup equals 284 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. For 735 ml of buttermilk, this means approximately 2.59 UK cups or 3.06 US cups. Small discrepancies can arise due to buttermilk's thickness, so using a measuring jug is best.</p>  

<p>If you're adapting a recipe for your air fryer, precise measurements matter even more. Air fryers cook quickly, and imbalances in wet ingredients can affect texture. <h3>Why Accuracy Matters in Baking</h3>  
<p>Baking is a science, and buttermilk plays a key role in activating baking soda. Too much or too little can throw off the rise and texture of cakes or bread. For air fryer baking, where heat distribution is intense, exact measurements prevent dryness or sogginess. Always level off your cups or use a digital scale for best results.</p>
