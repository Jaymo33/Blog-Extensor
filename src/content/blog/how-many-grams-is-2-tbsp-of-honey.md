<p>2 tablespoons (tbsp) of honey weigh approximately 42 grams (g). This conversion is essential for air fryer recipes where precision ensures perfect results. Whether you're glazing chicken or sweetening desserts, knowing the exact weight helps avoid over or under-measuring.</p>

<h2>Why accurate honey measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of honey ensures your dishes caramelise properly without burning. Too much honey can make food overly sticky, while too little may leave it bland. For recipes like honey-glazed salmon or sticky chicken wings, 42 grams of honey per 2 tbsp is the sweet spot.</p>

<h2>How to measure 2 tbsp of honey accurately</h2>  
<p>For best results, use a digital kitchen scale to weigh honey directly in grams. If using measuring spoons, lightly coat them with oil first so the honey slides off easily. This method avoids waste and ensures you get exactly 42 grams per 2 tbsp portion.</p>  
<p>Honey’s thickness varies by type (e.g., manuka vs. clover), but 2 tbsp generally equals 42 grams across most varieties. For recipe inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>honey-glazed chicken recipes</a> tailored for air fryers.</p>  

<h3>Common mistakes when measuring honey</h3>  
<p>Many cooks scoop honey straight from the jar, which packs extra into the spoon. Instead, pour it gently to achieve an accurate 2 tbsp (42g) measure. Sticky residue can also throw off measurements, so wipe utensils clean between uses.</p>
