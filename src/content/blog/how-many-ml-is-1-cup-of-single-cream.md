<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>It’s worth noting that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup is typically 250 ml, while in the US, it’s 240 ml. For single cream, we use the US standard (240 ml) unless specified otherwise. Always double-check the recipe origin to avoid discrepancies.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for Single Cream in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>Single cream is versatile in air fryer cooking. It’s perfect for creamy sauces, like a garlic mushroom sauce for chicken or a rich topping for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>desserts</a>. You can also use it in batters for cakes or as a base for custards. The 240 ml measurement ensures your dish has the right consistency.</p>  

<p>Another popular use is in scrambled eggs or quiches, where single cream adds richness. For best results, measure carefully and avoid overheating, as single cream can separate. <h3>Tips for Measuring Single Cream</h3>  
<p>For accuracy, use a liquid measuring cup rather than dry cups. Fill it to the 240 ml mark at eye level to avoid over- or under-pouring. If your recipe calls for a different amount, adjust proportionally—e.g., 0.5 cups would be 120 ml.</p>
