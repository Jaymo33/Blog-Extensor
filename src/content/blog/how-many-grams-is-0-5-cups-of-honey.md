<h2>Converting 0.5 Cups of Honey to Grams</h2>
<p>The conversion from cups to grams is straightforward once you know honey’s density. One cup of honey typically weighs around 340 grams, so halving that gives you 170 grams for 0.5 cups. This measurement applies to most honey varieties, including clover, wildflower, and manuka. If you’re working with a particularly thick or runny honey, a kitchen scale is your best friend for precision.</p>

<h3>How Temperature Affects Honey Measurements</h3>
<p>Honey’s viscosity changes with temperature, which can slightly alter its volume-to-weight ratio. Cold honey is thicker and may pack more densely into a cup, while warm honey pours more easily and takes up less space. For the most accurate 0.5 cups of honey in grams, measure at room temperature. If your honey has crystallised, gently warm it before measuring to ensure consistency.</p>
