<h2>How to Convert 3.75 Cups of Cream to ml</h2>  
<p>The standard conversion for cups to millilitres is 1 cup = 236.59 ml. Multiplying this by 3.75 gives you 887.21 ml. This measurement is crucial for recipes like creamy soups, custards, or even air-fried cheesecakes. If you’re working with a UK recipe, note that metric measurements are often preferred for precision.</p>  

<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements</h3>  
<p>While the US and UK use slightly different cup sizes, the difference is minimal for liquids like cream. A US cup is 236.59 ml, while a UK cup is 284.13 ml. However, most modern recipes standardise to the US measurement. For air fryer cooking, sticking to 887.21 ml (3.75 US cups) ensures consistency.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Recipes for Smaller Batches</h3>  
<p>If you need less cream, simply halve or quarter the measurement. For example, 1.875 cups of cream equals 443.6 ml. This flexibility is handy when adapting family-sized recipes for smaller portions in your air fryer.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Measuring Cream Accurately</h2>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for the most precise results. Pour the cream to the desired line without estimating. For sticky creams like double cream, lightly grease the measuring cup for easy pouring. Always level off the measurement for accuracy.</p>
