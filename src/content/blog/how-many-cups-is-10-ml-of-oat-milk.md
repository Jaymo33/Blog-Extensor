<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient ratios can make or break a recipe. Too much or too little liquid, like oat milk, can affect texture and cooking time. For example, adding 10 ml of oat milk to a cake batter instead of the intended 0.042 cups might seem minor, but it could lead to a denser result. Always measure carefully for consistent outcomes.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 10 ml of Oat Milk Without a Measuring Cup</h3>  
<p>Don’t have a measuring cup handy? Use a teaspoon—10 ml is roughly 2 teaspoons. For larger batches, a kitchen scale set to grams (10 ml of oat milk weighs about 10.3 grams) can be more accurate. <h3>Converting 10 ml of Oat Milk for UK vs. US Cups</h3>  
<p>Note that UK cups (284 ml) differ slightly from US cups (240 ml). For 10 ml, the difference is negligible (0.042 UK cups vs. 0.042 US cups), but it’s worth double-checking for larger quantities. Always refer to your air fryer’s manual or our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion guide</a> for clarity.</p>
