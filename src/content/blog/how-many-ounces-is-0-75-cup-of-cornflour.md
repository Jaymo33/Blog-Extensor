<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of cornflour is crucial for achieving the right texture in air-fried foods. <h3>How to Measure 0.75 Cup of Cornflour Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.75 cup of cornflour, use a dry measuring cup and level it off with a straight edge. Avoid packing the flour down, as this can lead to inaccurate measurements. For best results, weigh it on a kitchen scale to confirm it’s 3.17 ounces. This method is especially useful for air fryer recipes where precision is key.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for Cornflour in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Cornflour is a versatile ingredient that adds crispiness to air-fried foods. It’s often used in coatings for chicken, fish, or vegetables, giving them a golden, crunchy exterior. If you love crispy textures, explore <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>our chicken recipes</a> for delicious ideas.</p>  

<p>Another popular use for cornflour is in gluten-free baking. It’s a great alternative to wheat flour, making it ideal for those with dietary restrictions. Whether you’re making onion rings or tempura prawns, 0.75 cup of cornflour (3.17 oz) ensures your batter is light and crispy.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Cornflour for Air Fryer Use</h2>  
<p>To keep your cornflour fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Moisture can cause clumping, which affects measurement accuracy. If you’re using it frequently for air fryer recipes, consider buying in bulk and storing it properly to maintain quality.</p>
