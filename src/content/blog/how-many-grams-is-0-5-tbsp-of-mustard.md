<h3>Types of Mustard and Their Weights</h3>
<p>Not all mustards weigh the same due to differences in texture and ingredients. Smooth mustards like Dijon are denser, while wholegrain varieties are slightly lighter. However, the 7.5-gram benchmark for 0.5 tbsp works well for most common types. If you’re using a specialty mustard, it’s worth double-checking with a kitchen scale for absolute precision. This is especially helpful when preparing delicate dishes like air-fried tofu or vegetables.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 0.5 Tbsp of Mustard Without Scales</h2>
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 0.5 tbsp of mustard accurately. Use a standard tablespoon and fill it halfway, levelling off the top with a knife for precision. Remember, 1 tbsp equals 15 grams, so half of that is 7.5 grams. This method works well for sticky ingredients like mustard, ensuring you don’t add too much to your air fryer dishes.
