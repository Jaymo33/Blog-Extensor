<p>685 ml of buttermilk equals approximately 2.89 cups in the US measurement system and roughly 2.42 cups in the UK metric cup system. This conversion is essential for baking and cooking, especially when using precise recipes in your air fryer. Whether you're whipping up fluffy pancakes or tender fried chicken, getting the buttermilk measurement right makes all the difference.</p>

<h2>Understanding Buttermilk Measurements</h2>  
<p>Buttermilk is a staple in many recipes, from fluffy American-style pancakes to crispy air-fried chicken. Its slightly tangy flavour and thick consistency make it perfect for tenderising meats and adding moisture to baked goods. When a recipe calls for 685 ml of buttermilk, you'll want to measure it accurately to ensure perfect results every time.</p>  
<p>In the UK, 1 metric cup equals 250 ml, while in the US, 1 customary cup is about 237 ml. This slight difference matters when converting larger quantities like 685 ml. For UK bakers, 685 ml converts to about 2.74 metric cups (685 ÷ 250), while US measurements give roughly 2.89 cups (685 ÷ 237). Always check which cup measurement your recipe uses for best results.</p>  

<h3>Why Buttermilk Measurements Matter in Air Frying</h3>  
<p>When using your air fryer, precise liquid measurements like 685 ml of buttermilk ensure even cooking and perfect texture. Too much liquid can make coatings soggy, while too little might dry out your dish. Many <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a> rely on buttermilk for its tenderising properties, especially for fried chicken or onion rings.</p>
