<h2>Understanding Coconut Oil Measurements</h2>
<p>Coconut oil is a staple in many kitchens, especially for those who love air frying or baking. Unlike water, coconut oil has a different density, which means ml to cup conversions aren’t always straightforward. For 560 ml of coconut oil, you’ll need roughly 2.37 cups. This measurement is based on the standard UK cup size of 240 ml.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Coconut Oil</h2>
<p>One mistake people make is assuming all liquids measure the same. Coconut oil is denser than water, so 560 ml doesn’t equal 560 grams. Always use volume measurements (like cups) for accuracy. Another error is not accounting for temperature—coconut oil solidifies when cold, so melt it before measuring.</p>
