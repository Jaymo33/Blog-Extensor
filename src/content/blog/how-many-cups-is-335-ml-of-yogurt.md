<h2>Understanding the Conversion</h2>  
<p>Converting 335 ml of yogurt to cups depends on which cup measurement you're usingâ€”US or UK. The US cup holds 240 ml, while the UK cup is slightly larger at 284 ml. For 335 ml, this means roughly 1.42 US cups or 1.18 UK cups. This distinction is crucial for recipes, particularly when baking or making air fryer desserts like cakes or yogurt-based dips.</p>  

<p>If you're working with an air fryer, precise measurements ensure even cooking and perfect texture. For example, a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>yogurt-based dessert</a> requires the right consistency, so measuring correctly avoids soggy or dry results. Always check whether your recipe uses UK or US measurements to avoid mistakes.</p>  

<h3>Why Accuracy Matters in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>Air fryers rely on circulating hot air, so ingredient ratios impact cooking times and outcomes. Too much yogurt in a batter can make it dense, while too little may dry it out. Measuring 335 ml of yogurt correctly ensures your dishes turn out as intended. <h3>Converting Other Yogurt Amounts</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for more or less yogurt, the same principles apply. For example, 500 ml is about 2.11 US cups, while 250 ml is roughly 1.06 US cups. Keeping a conversion chart handy simplifies adjustments, especially when scaling recipes for your air fryer.</p>  

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Yogurt</h2>  
<p>One error is using liquid measuring cups for thick yogurt, which can lead to inaccurate readings. Instead, spoon yogurt into the cup and level it off. Another mistake is ignoring the US/UK cup difference, which affects recipes. Always confirm which standard your recipe follows.</p>
