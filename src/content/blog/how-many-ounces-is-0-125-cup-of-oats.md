<h2>How to measure 0.125 cup of oats accurately</h2>
<p>Measuring small quantities like 0.125 cup of oats can be tricky without the right tools. Here’s how to do it properly:</p>
<ul>
<li>Use a 1/8 cup measuring cup if you have one</li>
<li>Alternatively, measure 2 tablespoons (which equals 0.125 cup)</li>
<li>For ultimate precision, use digital kitchen scales set to ounces</li>
</ul>
<p>Remember that the weight of oats can vary slightly depending on how packed they are in the cup. For absolute accuracy in your air fryer recipes, weighing your 0.125 cup of oats (1 ounce) is always best.</p>

<h3>Converting 0.125 cup of oats to other measurements</h3>
<p>If you need to convert 0.125 cup of oats to other units, here are some useful equivalents:</p>
<ul>
<li>0.125 cup = 1 ounce (0.99 oz)</li>
<li>0.125 cup = 28 grams</li>
<li>0.125 cup = 2 tablespoons</li>
</ul>

<h2>Common uses for 0.125 cup of oats in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>This small but mighty measurement of oats appears in many air fryer recipes. Here are some popular ways to use 0.125 cup (1 ounce) of oats:</p>
<ul>
<li>Coating for air fryer chicken or fish</li>
<li>Base for single-serve granola</li>
<li>Addition to air fryer cookies or muffins</li>
<li>Topping for air fryer fruit crumbles</li>

<h3>Tips for working with oats in your air fryer</h3>
<p>Oats can behave differently in an air fryer compared to conventional oven cooking. Here are some expert tips:</p>
<ul>
<li>Mix oats with a little oil to prevent drying out</li>
<li>Stir halfway through cooking for even results</li>
<li>Use rolled oats rather than instant for better texture</li>
<li>Combine with other dry ingredients when using as coating</li>
</ul>
