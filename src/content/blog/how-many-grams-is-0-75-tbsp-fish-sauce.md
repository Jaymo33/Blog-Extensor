<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to uneven results. Too much fish sauce might overpower your dish, while too little could leave it bland. Grams provide a more precise measurement than tablespoons, especially for dense liquids like fish sauce. This is particularly important for recipes like air-fried salmon or crispy tofu. For a full <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a>, check our resources.</p>

<p>Many air fryer enthusiasts swear by weighing ingredients rather than using volume measurements. This method eliminates variables like how firmly you pack the tablespoon. When working with potent ingredients like fish sauce, precision becomes even more crucial. Our measurement guide takes the guesswork out of your cooking process. You'll find this especially helpful when preparing Asian-inspired air fryer dishes.</p>

<h3>How to measure fish sauce accurately</h3>
<p>For the most precise 0.75 tbsp fish sauce to grams conversion, use digital kitchen scales. Simply set your scales to grams, place a small bowl on top, and tare to zero before adding the fish sauce. This method ensures you get exactly 11.25 grams every time. If you don't have scales, use a standard UK tablespoon (15ml) and fill it precisely three-quarters full. Remember that different brands of fish sauce may vary slightly in density.</p>

<h3>Converting other fish sauce measurements</h3>
<p>If your recipe calls for different amounts, here's a quick reference: 1 tbsp equals 15 grams, while 1 tsp equals 5 grams. These conversions are vital when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer. Remember that fish sauce is quite potent, so small measurement differences can significantly impact flavour. Always measure carefully, especially when trying new recipes in your air fryer.</p>

<h2>Tips for working with fish sauce in air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Fish sauce can splatter in high heat, so mix it well with other marinade ingredients before adding to your air fryer basket. The 0.75 tbsp (11.25g) measurement works well when combined with 1-2 tbsp of oil for even coating. After cooking, clean your air fryer promptly to prevent lingering odours - our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning guide</a> has all the tips you need.</p>

For vegetarian alternatives, you might use mushroom sauce in the same 11.25 gram quantity. These substitutions work well in most air fryer recipes while maintaining the intended flavour balance. Experiment with different combinations to find your perfect match.</p>
