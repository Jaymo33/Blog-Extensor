<p>100 grams of plain flour is roughly 0.8 cups in the UK and 0.85 cups in the US. Getting this conversion right is essential for baking and air frying, where precise measurements can make or break a recipe. Whether you're whipping up cakes, bread, or batters for air-fried treats, knowing how to convert grams to cups ensures consistency. <h2>Why Accurate Flour Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of flour is crucial for texture and structure in baked goods. Too much flour can make your creations dense, while too little may cause them to collapse. Air fryer recipes, like those found on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipes page</a>, often rely on precise ratios to ensure even cooking. Whether you're making air-fried doughnuts or crispy coatings for chicken, 100 grams of plain flour should be measured carefully.</p>  

<p>Different countries use varying cup sizes, which can lead to confusion. The UK standard cup holds 250ml, while the US cup is 240ml. This slight difference means 100 grams of plain flour converts to 0.8 cups in the UK and 0.85 cups in the US. For air fryer recipes, sticking to one measurement system avoids inconsistencies.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure 100 Grams of Plain Flour Without Scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, spooning flour into a measuring cup and levelling it off works well. Avoid scooping directly from the bag, as this compacts the flour and leads to excess. For 100 grams of plain flour, lightly fill your cup and sweep off the top with a knife. This method ensures you’re close to the correct weight, though scales are always more accurate.</p>  

<h2>Converting 100 Grams of Plain Flour for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Air fryer recipes often require adjustments from traditional baking. When converting 100 grams of plain flour to cups, consider the recipe’s origin. UK-based recipes, like those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>our conversion chart</a>, will use the 0.8-cup measurement, while US recipes may use 0.85 cups. This small difference can affect moisture levels in batters and doughs.</p>  

<p>For best results, weigh your flour if possible. If using cups, fluff the flour first to aerate it, then spoon it in. This technique helps achieve consistency, especially when making air-fried favourites like onion rings or tempura. Check out <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>our cake recipes</a> for ideas on using 100 grams of plain flour in your air fryer.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Flour</h3>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is packing flour into a cup, which can add up to 20% extra. Another is using liquid measuring cups for dry ingredients, leading to inaccuracies. Always use dry measuring cups for 100 grams of plain flour and level them off properly. These small steps ensure your air fryer creations turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>UK vs. US Cup Measurements for 100 Grams of Plain Flour</h2>  
<p>As mentioned, the UK and US have slightly different cup sizes. For 100 grams of plain flour, this means 0.8 UK cups or 0.85 US cups. If you’re following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, it’s best to stick to UK measurements unless specified otherwise. This avoids any confusion and ensures your air fryer dishes have the right texture.</p>  

<p>When adapting recipes, consider the flour’s density. Sifted flour will weigh less per cup, so 100 grams may take up more volume. For air frying, where coatings and batters are common, consistency is key. Whether you’re breading chicken or making muffins, knowing how many cups 100 grams of plain flour equals helps you nail the recipe.</p>  

<h3>Tips for Storing Plain Flour</h3>  
<p>To keep your flour fresh, store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. This prevents moisture absorption, which can affect weight and volume measurements. If your flour clumps, sift it before measuring out 100 grams for your air fryer recipes. Fresh flour ensures accurate conversions and better results in your cooking.</p>
