<p>178 C in a fan oven is equivalent to 160 C in a conventional oven with the fan setting turned on. This 20-degree reduction is standard for fan-assisted cooking, whether you're using an air fryer or traditional oven. The circulating hot air in fan ovens cooks food more efficiently, allowing for lower temperature settings.</p>

<h2>Understanding Fan Oven Conversions</h2>
<p>When converting 178 C to fan oven settings, remember that fan-assisted cooking typically requires temperatures 20 degrees lower than conventional recipes suggest. This means if a recipe calls for 178 C in a regular oven, you'd set your fan oven to 158 C. The same principle applies when using an air fryer, which operates similarly to a fan oven.</p>

<h3>Why the Temperature Difference Matters</h3>
<p>The 20-degree reduction when converting 178 C to fan oven settings isn't arbitrary. Fan ovens circulate hot air constantly, creating more even heat distribution and faster cooking times. Without this adjustment, food could overcook or dry out. This is particularly important for delicate items like cakes or pastries where precise temperature control is crucial.</p>

<h2>Applying 178 C Conversions in Your Air Fryer</h2>
<p>Air fryers essentially work like compact fan ovens, making the 178 C to fan oven conversion directly applicable. When preparing recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>our cake collection</a>, you'll often need to make this adjustment. For example, a Victoria sponge that normally bakes at 178 C would cook perfectly at 160 C in an air fryer.</p>
<p>The smaller cooking chamber of an air fryer means heat transfers even more efficiently than in a full-sized fan oven. This is why monitoring cooking times is equally important as temperature adjustments. Many air fryer users find they need to reduce cooking times by 10-20% compared to conventional oven recipes.</p>
