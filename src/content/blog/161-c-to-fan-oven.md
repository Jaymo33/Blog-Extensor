<p>161 C in a fan oven is equivalent to approximately 140 C in a conventional oven. This conversion is crucial for baking, roasting, and air frying, ensuring your dishes cook evenly without burning. Whether you're using a fan-assisted oven or an air fryer, understanding this temperature adjustment will help you achieve perfect results every time.</p>

<h2>Understanding Fan Oven Temperatures</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and quickly than conventional ovens. When a recipe calls for 161 C in a fan oven, it's typically referring to the temperature needed for delicate baked goods or slow-roasted meats. This setting is particularly useful for air fryers, which operate on similar convection principles. You'll find this temperature perfect for items like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cookies>cookies</a> or <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken thighs</a>.</p>
<p>Many modern recipes, especially those on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, specify fan oven temperatures for precision. The 161 C mark falls into what chefs consider a moderate oven temperature - ideal for cakes that need gentle rising or proteins that benefit from slow cooking. Remember that air fryers, being compact versions of fan ovens, might cook slightly faster at this temperature due to their efficient air circulation.</p>

<h3>Why 161 C Matters in Baking</h3>
<p>This specific temperature creates the perfect environment for certain chemical reactions in baking. At 161 C (140 C conventional), proteins coagulate at just the right rate, sugars caramelise beautifully, and starches gelatinise to create tender textures. It's particularly good for recipes that need a gentle touch, like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>sponge cakes</a> or meringues.</p>
