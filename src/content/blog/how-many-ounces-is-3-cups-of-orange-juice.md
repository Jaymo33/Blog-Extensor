<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>
<p>Air fryers rely on precise cooking times and ingredient ratios to deliver crispy, evenly cooked meals. Using the correct amount of orange juice in your recipes ensures balanced flavours and proper caramelisation. <h2>How to Measure Orange Juice for Air Fryer Recipes</h2>
<p>When measuring orange juice for your air fryer dishes, always use liquid measuring cups for accuracy. The standard UK conversion is 8 fluid ounces per cup, making 3 cups equal to 24 fluid ounces. This measurement applies whether you're using freshly squeezed or store-bought orange juice in your recipes.</p>

<h3>Converting Other Orange Juice Measurements</h3>
<p>If your recipe calls for different amounts, here are some common conversions: 1 cup = 8 oz, 2 cups = 16 oz, and 4 cups = 32 oz. These conversions work for all types of citrus juices in air fryer cooking. <p>Orange juice adds wonderful flavour and helps tenderise meats in the air fryer. The natural sugars in orange juice can also help create beautiful caramelisation on chicken, pork, or even vegetables.</p>

<p>For dessert applications like orange-infused cakes or glazes, the 3 cups to 24 ounces conversion ensures your batter or topping has the perfect consistency.
