<h3>Factors That Affect the Weight of Minced Beef</h3>  
<p>The exact weight of 2.5 cups of minced beef can vary based on its fat ratio (e.g., 5%, 10%, or 20% fat) and how densely it's packed into the cup. Leaner beef will weigh slightly less, while fattier mince may compact more. For precision, we recommend using a kitchen scale—especially when adapting recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a> to suit your air fryer.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Minced Beef Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, lightly spoon the minced beef into a measuring cup without packing it down. Level it off with a knife for the most accurate result. However, for air fryer cooking, we always recommend weighing ingredients when possible—especially for recipes like meatloaf or stuffed mushrooms, where precision affects cooking time.</p>

<h3>Storing Leftover Minced Beef</h3>
