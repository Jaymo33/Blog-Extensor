<h2>Why accurate measurements matter in air fryer cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to achieve the perfect texture and flavour. Too much or too little olive oil can affect crispiness and cooking times. For example, 1.66 cups of olive oil (13.28 ounces) is often used in larger batches of roasted vegetables or marinades. If you're experimenting with <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>, getting the measurements right ensures delicious results every time.</p>  

<p>Olive oil is a staple in many air fryer dishes, from crispy potatoes to succulent chicken. Measuring 1.66 cups (13.28 ounces) accurately helps maintain the balance between moisture and crispiness. Over-pouring can lead to greasy results, while under-measuring might leave food dry. Always use a kitchen scale for the most precise conversions, especially when working with larger quantities like 1.66 cups.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 1.66 cups of olive oil without a scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 1.66 cups of olive oil using standard measuring cups. One cup of olive oil equals 8 ounces, so 1.66 cups would be roughly 13.28 ounces. For smaller adjustments, tablespoons and teaspoons can help fine-tune the measurement. Remember, accuracy is key for air fryer success, so double-check your conversions.</p>
