<p>Air fryers rely on precise ingredient ratios to deliver even cooking and perfect textures. Too much or too little condensed milk can throw off your recipe. <h3>How to measure condensed milk correctly</h3>  
<p>Use a liquid measuring cup for the most accurate results when measuring 2 cups of condensed milk. Scoop it gently to avoid air pockets, and level it off with a spatula. If you’re using a kitchen scale, set it to ounces for the 20-ounce equivalent. This method is especially helpful for sticky ingredients like condensed milk.</p>

<h3>Converting other measurements for condensed milk</h3>  
<p>If a recipe calls for metric measurements, 20 ounces of condensed milk is roughly 567 grams. For smaller batches, half a cup equals 5 ounces. Always double-check conversions to avoid errors, especially when scaling recipes up or down.</p>  

<h2>Tips for working with condensed milk in air fryers</h2>  
<p>Condensed milk can caramelize quickly in an air fryer due to its high sugar content. To prevent burning, lower the temperature slightly and check frequently. For even heating, stir or rotate dishes halfway through cooking. This is especially important for recipes like air fryer banoffee pie or sticky toffee pudding.</p>
