<p>4 tablespoons (tbsp) of gelatin powder equals approximately 28 grams. This conversion is essential for precise baking and cooking, especially when using an air fryer for recipes like jellies, mousses, or even savoury dishes. Whether you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> or experimenting with your own creations, accurate measurements ensure perfect results every time.</p>  

<h2>Why Accurate Gelatin Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Gelatin powder is a versatile ingredient used in everything from desserts to savoury dishes. Too much or too little can drastically alter the texture of your dish. For air fryer recipes, precision is key—especially when making delicate items like panna cotta or jelly-based treats. A slight miscalculation could lead to a runny or overly firm result, so knowing how to convert 4 tbsp gelatin powder to grams is crucial.</p>  
<p>If you're working with UK measurements, 4 tbsp of gelatin powder is roughly 28 grams. This conversion is based on standard UK tablespoon sizes, which differ slightly from US measurements. For more air fryer-friendly conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> to simplify your cooking process.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Gelatin Powder Correctly</h2>  
<p>Measuring gelatin powder by volume (tablespoons) can be tricky due to its light, fluffy texture. For the most accurate results, use a digital kitchen scale. Simply scoop 4 tbsp of gelatin powder into a bowl and weigh it—you should see around 28 grams. If you don’t have a scale, lightly spoon the powder into the tablespoon and level it off with a knife for consistency.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Gelatin</h3>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is packing the gelatin powder into the tablespoon, which can lead to an incorrect weight. Another error is using a heaped tablespoon instead of a level one. Both can throw off your recipe, especially in air fryer cooking where precise temperatures and measurements are vital. Always aim for a level 4 tbsp measurement to ensure 28 grams.</p>  

<h2>Using Gelatin Powder in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Gelatin isn’t just for desserts—it’s also great for binding ingredients in air fryer dishes like meatloaf or terrines. When converting 4 tbsp gelatin powder into grams for these recipes, accuracy ensures the right texture. <p>If you're out of gelatin powder, agar-agar is a popular plant-based substitute. However, the conversion differs—4 tbsp of agar-agar equals about 24 grams. Always adjust measurements based on the substitute’s properties, especially when using an air fryer, as cooking times may vary.
