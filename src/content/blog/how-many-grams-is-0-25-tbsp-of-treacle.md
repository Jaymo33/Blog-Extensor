<h2>How to measure 0.25 tbsp of treacle without a scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 0.25 tbsp of treacle accurately. A standard tablespoon holds 15 ml, so 0.25 tbsp is roughly 3.75 ml. To measure this, fill your tablespoon halfway and then divide that portion in half again. Treacle’s sticky consistency can make this tricky, so lightly greasing the spoon with oil can help it slide off easily. This method ensures you get close to the 4.5-gram mark without needing specialised equipment.</p>

<h2>Converting other treacle measurements to grams</h2>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different amount of treacle, here’s a quick reference guide: 1 tbsp of treacle weighs about 18 grams, while 1 teaspoon (0.33 tbsp) is roughly 6 grams. These conversions are useful when scaling recipes up or down for your air fryer. Remember, treacle’s density means it’s heavier than lighter syrups like golden syrup, so always measure by weight for accuracy. <h3>Tips for working with treacle in the air fryer</h3>  
<p>Treacle can be sticky and messy, but a few simple tricks make it easier to handle. Warming the treacle slightly (either in the microwave or by placing the jar in warm water) will make it easier to pour and measure. Always wipe the rim of the treacle jar after use to prevent it from sticking shut. And if you’re using 0.25 tbsp of treacle in a marinade or glaze, mix it well with other liquids to ensure even distribution.
