This adjustment accounts for the fan oven's efficient heat distribution, which cooks food faster and more evenly. If you're using an air fryer, which operates similarly to a fan oven, you might not need to adjust the temperature at all. <h2>Why Convert 380 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens and conventional ovens heat food differently, which is why conversions are necessary. A fan oven circulates hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and quickly. A conventional oven relies on radiant heat, which can lead to uneven cooking if not adjusted properly. For example, 380 fan oven to Fahrenheit in a conventional oven requires a slight reduction to 356°F. This ensures your baked goods or meats cook perfectly without burning.</p>

<h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>When converting 380 fan oven to conventional oven in Fahrenheit, you'll also need to adjust cooking times. Since fan ovens cook faster, reduce the time by about 10-15% when using a conventional oven. For instance, if a recipe calls for 20 minutes in a fan oven, try 17-18 minutes in a conventional oven. Always check for doneness with a skewer or thermometer.</p>
