<h3>How to measure 125g of wholemeal flour accurately</h3>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the most precise way to measure 125g of wholemeal flour. If you don’t have one, spoon the flour into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. Avoid packing the flour down, as this can lead to over-measuring.</p>

<h3>Does wholemeal flour behave differently in an air fryer?</h3>  
<p>Wholemeal flour can make baked goods denser than plain flour, so adjustments might be needed. When converting 125g to ounces (4.41 oz), keep in mind that wholemeal flour absorbs more liquid. You may need to add a touch more moisture to your air fryer recipes.</p>  

<h2>Storing wholemeal flour for air fryer baking</h2>  
<p>Because wholemeal flour contains more natural oils, it can go rancid faster than white flour. Store your 125g (4.41 oz) portions in airtight containers in a cool, dark place. For longer storage, keep it in the freezer – just bring it to room temperature before using in air fryer recipes.</p>
