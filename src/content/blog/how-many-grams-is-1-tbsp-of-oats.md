<h2>Why accurate oat measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of oats ensures your air fryer recipes turn out just right. Too many oats can make dishes dry, while too few might leave them soggy. For breakfast favourites like overnight oats or crispy granola clusters, 5 grams per tablespoon is the golden rule. <p>Oats are a staple in many kitchens, offering fibre and a nutty flavour. Whether you prefer rolled, jumbo, or instant oats, the weight per tablespoon remains consistent. This makes scaling recipes up or down a breeze. For meal preppers, knowing this conversion saves time and reduces waste. It’s also handy for tracking macros if you’re health-conscious.</p>  

<h3>Types of oats and their weights</h3>  
<p>While 1 tbsp of oats generally weighs 5 grams, the type can slightly affect density. Rolled oats are flatter and may pack differently compared to steel-cut oats. However, the difference is minimal for most home cooking. If precision is critical, like in baking, weighing your oats is always best. A digital kitchen scale takes the guesswork out of measurements.</p>  

<h2>Converting tablespoons to grams for UK cooks</h2>  
<p>In the UK, tablespoon measurements follow the metric system, making conversions straightforward. For oats, 1 tbsp equals 5 grams, but this can vary slightly with packing density. If you’re doubling a recipe, 2 tbsp of oats would be 10 grams, and so on. <h3>Common mistakes when measuring oats</h3>  
<p>Scooping oats directly from the bag with a tablespoon can lead to uneven amounts. For accuracy, spoon oats lightly into the measure and level off the top. Packed oats will weigh more, skewing your recipe. If you don’t have scales, fluffing the oats first helps standardise the tablespoon measure. This tip is especially useful for air fryer recipes where balance is key.</p>
