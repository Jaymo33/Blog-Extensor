<h2>Why Accurate Lentil Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct gram measurement for 2.0 cups of lentils ensures your air fryer recipes turn out just right. Too many lentils can overcrowd the basket, leading to uneven cooking, while too few might leave your dish lacking texture. Lentils expand when cooked, so starting with the right dry measurement is crucial. For meal preppers, this conversion saves time and reduces food waste. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurements.</p>

If you're using pre-cooked lentils, remember they’ll weigh more due to absorbed water. Always level your measuring cups for consistency, especially when following air fryer recipes. A kitchen scale is the most accurate tool, but cup measurements work well with this conversion.</p>

<p>In the UK, a standard metric cup holds 250ml, while the US cup is slightly smaller at 240ml. However, for lentils, the difference is minimal—around 5 grams per 2.0 cups. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Lentils</h3>  
<p>Avoid packing lentils into the cup, as this leads to over-measuring. Instead, gently spoon them in and level the top. If using a scoop, fluff the lentils first to prevent compaction. For split lentils, the gram weight may differ slightly, so check packaging details. Storing lentils in airtight containers keeps them fresh for future <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep</a>.</p>
