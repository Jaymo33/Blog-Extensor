<h2>How to Measure 175 Grams of Cream</h2>
<p>If you don't have a kitchen scale, measuring 175 grams of cream can be tricky. Cream's density means it doesn't always fill a cup the same way as liquids like water. For the most accurate measurement, use a liquid measuring cup and fill it to just under the 3/4 cup mark. This method works well for both single and double cream.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>
<p>It's worth noting that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. A UK cup is 284 ml, while a US cup is 240 ml. For 175 grams of cream, the difference is minimal, but it's something to keep in mind for precision baking. Most modern recipes specify which measurement system they use, so always double-check.</p>

<h3>Tips for Working with Cream</h3>
<p>When measuring 175 grams of cream, ensure it's at room temperature for the most accurate results. Cold cream can be denser, leading to slight variations in volume. Also, avoid over-packing the cream into the cup, as this can throw off your measurements.</p>
