<h2>Understanding the Conversion: Cups to Ounces</h2>  
<p>Converting cups to ounces can be tricky, especially with thick ingredients like marmalade. Generally, 1 cup of marmalade weighs around 8 ounces, so 1.25 cups would be 10 ounces. This measurement is essential for air fryer recipes, where consistency affects cooking times. For more conversions, check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Different brands of marmalade may vary slightly in density, so weighing is always best. If you don’t have scales, use a measuring jug with clear ounce markings. Precision ensures your air fryer dishes turn out just right, whether you're making sticky chicken wings or marmalade-glazed desserts.</p>  

<h3>Why Weight Matters in Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even heat distribution, so ingredient weights impact cooking times. Too much marmalade can make dishes overly sticky, while too little might leave them dry. For perfect results, stick to the 10-ounce measurement when using 1.25 cups of marmalade.</p>  

<h2>Using Marmalade in Air Fryer Recipes</h2>  
<p>Marmalade adds a sweet, tangy kick to many air fryer dishes. From glazes to marinades, it’s a versatile ingredient. The 10-ounce measurement ensures balanced flavours every time.</p>  

<p>For sticky ribs or wings, mix marmalade with soy sauce and garlic for a delicious glaze. The 1.25 cups (10 ounces) ratio keeps the sauce thick enough to coat without dripping. <h3>Tips for Measuring Marmalade Accurately</h3>  
<p>To measure 1.25 cups of marmalade easily, lightly grease your measuring cup to prevent sticking. For sticky ingredients, a kitchen scale is your best friend—simply tare the scale and pour until you hit 10 ounces.</p>  

<h2>Common Mistakes When Measuring Marmalade</h2>  
<p>One mistake is packing marmalade into the cup, which can lead to excess weight. Instead, spoon it in gently and level it off. Another error is ignoring temperature—cold marmalade is denser, so let it soften slightly for accurate measuring.</p>
