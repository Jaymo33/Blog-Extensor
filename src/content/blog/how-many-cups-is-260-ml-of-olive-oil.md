<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>
<p>Using the right amount of olive oil in your air fryer is crucial for achieving crispy, golden results without excess grease. Too little oil, and your food may dry out; too much, and it can become soggy. For example, 260 ml of olive oil in cups translates to roughly 1.1 cups, which is handy for recipes like roasted potatoes or chicken wings. <h2>How to Measure 260 ml of Olive Oil Without a Measuring Cup</h2>
<p>Don’t have a measuring cup? No problem! You can use a kitchen scale to weigh 260 ml of olive oil, which is roughly 240 grams. Alternatively, a standard tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so 260 ml would be around 17 tablespoons. This method is handy for quick adjustments in your air fryer cooking.</p>
<p>Another trick is to use a liquid measuring jug with clear markings. Pour the olive oil up to the 260 ml line, and you’ll have your 1.1 cups ready to go. For more tips on keeping your air fryer in top shape, visit our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a>.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Cup Measurements</h3>
<p>It’s worth noting that UK and US cup measurements differ slightly. In the UK, 1 cup is 284 ml, while in the US, it’s 240 ml. This means 260 ml of olive oil is about 0.92 UK cups or 1.1 US cups. Always double-check which standard your recipe uses to avoid confusion.</p>
