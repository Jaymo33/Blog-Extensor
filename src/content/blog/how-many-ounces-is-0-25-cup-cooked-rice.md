<p>0.25 cup of cooked rice weighs approximately 2 ounces (56.7 grams). This measurement is handy when portioning rice for air fryer recipes or meal prep, ensuring consistent results every time. Whether you're cooking for one or scaling up a dish, knowing this conversion helps maintain perfect texture and flavour.</p>

<h2>Why measuring cooked rice matters for air fryer cooking</h2>
<p>Accurate measurements are crucial when using an air fryer, as overcrowding or underfilling can affect cooking times and results. A 0.25 cup portion of cooked rice equals about 2 ounces, which is ideal for single servings or as a base for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer recipes</a>. This small quantity works perfectly for fried rice dishes or as a side to proteins cooked in your air fryer.</p>
<p>When converting 0.25 cup of cooked rice to ounces, remember that cooked rice weighs more than uncooked due to absorbed water. This 2-ounce portion provides about 75-100 calories, making it easy to control portions while enjoying your favourite <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken dishes</a> or vegetables from the air fryer.</p>

<h3>How to measure 0.25 cup of cooked rice accurately</h3>
<p>For the most precise 0.25 cup measurement, use a dry measuring cup and lightly spoon the cooked rice in without packing it down. Level off the top with a straight edge for exactly 2 ounces of cooked rice. This method ensures consistency across all your <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> creations, from stir-fries to stuffed peppers.</p>
