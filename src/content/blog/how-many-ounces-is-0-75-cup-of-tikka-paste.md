<h3>How to Measure 0.75 Cup of Tikka Paste</h3>  
<p>For best results, use a liquid measuring cup for tikka paste, as its thick consistency can be tricky. Scoop the paste gently, levelling it off with a knife for accuracy. If you don’t have a measuring cup, 6 ounces is roughly 12 tablespoons. This method works perfectly for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken tikka recipes</a> in your air fryer.</p>  

<h2>Converting 0.75 Cup of Tikka Paste for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>Whether you're making a marinade or a sauce, 0.75 cup (6 ounces) of tikka paste is versatile. For a milder flavour, dilute it with yogurt or coconut milk. If you prefer extra heat, pair it with fresh chillies. Explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable tikka options</a> for plant-based twists.</p>  

<p>Storing leftover tikka paste? Transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze measured portions in ice cube trays. This way, you’ll always have 6-ounce equivalents ready for quick air fryer meals. Don’t forget to clean your air fryer with our recommended <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning tips</a> after cooking.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Tikka Paste</h3>  
<p>Avoid packing the paste into the cup, as this leads to excess. Instead, spoon it in loosely for an accurate 6-ounce measure. Also, ensure your measuring tools are dry, as moisture can alter the paste’s consistency. These small steps guarantee perfect results every time.</p>
