<h3>How to Measure 50g of Apple Cider Vinegar Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>No scale? No problem! One tablespoon of apple cider vinegar weighs roughly 15 grams, so 3 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon will get you close to 50g (about 1.76 oz). For even more accuracy, use a liquid measuring cup marked in millilitres—50g of apple cider vinegar is approximately 48ml due to its density.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for 50g of Apple Cider Vinegar in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>Apple cider vinegar is a versatile ingredient in air fryer cooking. A 50g (1.76 oz) portion works perfectly for marinating <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken</a>, tenderising meats, or adding zest to roasted vegetables. Try mixing it with olive oil and herbs for a quick dressing or using it to deglaze your air fryer basket after cooking.</p>  

<p>For crispy <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/potatoes>potatoes</a>, toss 50g of apple cider vinegar with parboiled spuds before air frying—the acidity helps break down starches for extra crunch. It’s also a great addition to homemade <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>air fryer cleaner</a> solutions, cutting through grease naturally.</p>  

<h3>Does the Brand of Apple Cider Vinegar Affect the Weight?</h3>  
<p>Most apple cider vinegar brands have a similar density, so 50g will always equal roughly 1.76 ounces. However, unfiltered varieties with "the mother" might be slightly denser. If precision is crucial, stick to measuring by weight rather than volume for consistent results.</p>
