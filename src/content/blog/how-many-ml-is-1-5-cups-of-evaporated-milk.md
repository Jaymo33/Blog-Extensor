<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>
<p>Using the correct amount of evaporated milk can make or break your dish. Too little, and your recipe might lack creaminess; too much, and it could become overly dense. Air fryers cook food quickly, so precise measurements ensure even cooking and perfect texture. For more conversion tips, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>

<p>Evaporated milk is a staple in many recipes, from custards to casseroles. Its concentrated nature means small measurement errors can have a big impact. By converting cups to millilitres, you eliminate guesswork and achieve consistent results. This is especially useful for air fryer baking, where precision is key.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 1.5 Cups of Evaporated Milk Correctly</h2>
<p>To measure 355 ml of evaporated milk, use a liquid measuring jug with clear millilitre markings. Pour the milk slowly to avoid spills and check at eye level for accuracy. If you don’t have a measuring jug, a kitchen scale can also work—1.5 cups weighs roughly 340 grams.</p>

<h3>Using Metric vs. Imperial Measurements</h3>
<p>In the UK, millilitres are the standard for liquid measurements, while cups are more common in the US. Converting 1.5 cups of evaporated milk to ml (355 ml) bridges this gap, making recipes accessible no matter where you are. This is particularly helpful when following international air fryer recipes.</p>

<h3>Substitutes for Evaporated Milk</h3>
<p>If you’re out of evaporated milk, you can make a substitute by simmering regular milk until it reduces by half. Alternatively, use a mix of milk and cream for a similar richness. Just remember to adjust the measurements accordingly—1.5 cups of substitute should still equal 355 ml.</p>
