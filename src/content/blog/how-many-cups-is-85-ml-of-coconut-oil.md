<h3>How to Measure 85 ml of Coconut Oil Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, use a standard tablespoon to measure 85 ml of coconut oil. One tablespoon holds about 15 ml, so you’ll need roughly 5.5 tablespoons to reach 85 ml (or 0.36 cups). For sticky coconut oil, lightly grease the spoon for easy release. This method works well for recipes like air-fried cookies or cakes.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Coconut Oil</h3>  
<p>Avoid packing coconut oil into the measuring cup, as this leads to excess. Instead, spoon it lightly for accuracy. Also, melted and solid coconut oil have different volumes—85 ml of melted oil may settle differently than solid oil. For consistent results, stick to the recipe’s specified state (melted or solid).</p>
