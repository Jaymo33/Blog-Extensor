<h2>How to measure 200g of cottage cheese without a scale</h2>  
<p>Don’t have a kitchen scale? No problem. You can estimate 200g of cottage cheese using measuring cups. One cup of cottage cheese typically weighs around 225g, so 200g is just under a cup. For smaller amounts, a tablespoon holds about 15g, so 13 tablespoons would give you roughly 200g.</p>  
<p>Keep in mind that cottage cheese can vary in density, so these are rough estimates. For best results, especially in air fryer recipes, invest in a digital scale. <h2>Common mistakes when converting grams to ounces</h2>  
<p>One of the biggest mistakes is confusing fluid ounces with weight ounces. Fluid ounces measure volume, while weight ounces measure mass. When converting 200g of cottage cheese to ounces, you’re dealing with weight, not volume.</p>  
<p>Another pitfall is rounding numbers too much. While 200g of cottage cheese is roughly 7 ounces, precise measurements (7.05 ounces) matter in baking or delicate recipes. Always double-check your conversions to avoid mishaps. For more tips, visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/breakfast>breakfast recipe category</a>.</p>  

<h3>Storing cottage cheese for air fryer use</h3>  
<p>To keep your cottage cheese fresh, store it in an airtight container in the fridge. If you’ve measured out 200g (7.05 ounces) for a recipe but don’t use it all, it’ll stay good for a few days. Avoid freezing cottage cheese, as it can become grainy when thawed.</p>
