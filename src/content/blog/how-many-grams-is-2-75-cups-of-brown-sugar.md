<p>2.75 cups of brown sugar equals approximately 550 grams in UK measurements or 586 grams in US cups. This conversion is essential for air fryer baking, where precision ensures perfect texture and consistency. Whether you're making cookies, cakes, or glazes, knowing the exact gram measurement helps avoid kitchen disasters.</p>

<h2>Why Accurate Brown Sugar Measurements Matter</h2>
<p>Brown sugar's moisture content means it packs differently than white sugar, making volume measurements unreliable. For air fryer recipes, especially those from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, grams provide consistency. A slight variation can affect caramelisation, spread, and moisture in baked goods. That's why professional bakers always weigh ingredients.</p>
<p>Your air fryer's compact cooking space amplifies measurement importance. Unlike conventional ovens, air fryers circulate hot air intensely, so balanced sugar quantities prevent burning or undercooking. <h2>UK vs US Cup Measurements for Brown Sugar</h2>
<p>In the UK, 1 cup of packed brown sugar weighs 200g, making 2.75 cups equal to 550g. US cups are slightly larger at 213g per cup, totaling 586g for 2.75 cups. This 36g difference could impact recipes, especially in small-batch air fryer cooking where ratios are crucial.</p>
<p>Always check your recipe's origin. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cookies>cookie recipes</a> use UK measurements unless specified. For American recipes, add an extra 13g per cup or use our gram conversions for precision. A kitchen scale eliminates this guesswork entirely.</p>

<h3>How to Properly Measure Brown Sugar</h3>
<p>For accurate cup-to-gram conversions, pack brown sugar firmly into the measuring cup. Unlike flour, brown sugar shouldn't be levelled - the dome indicates proper packing. Alternatively, spoon sugar into the cup and press down to remove air pockets. These techniques ensure consistent density for reliable conversions.</p>
