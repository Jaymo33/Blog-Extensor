<h2>Understanding Cup Measurements for Orange Juice</h2>  
<p>Cup sizes vary between the US and UK, which can affect your cooking results. In the US, 1 cup equals 240 ml, while in the UK, it's 284 ml. This means 935 ml of orange juice converts to roughly 3.95 US cups or 3.29 UK cups. If you're using an air fryer for recipes like orange-glazed chicken or citrus-infused desserts, this distinction is crucial for consistency.</p>  

<p>For air fryer cooking, liquid measurements impact texture and flavour. Too much orange juice in a marinade could make food soggy, while too little might leave it dry. <p>Air fryers circulate hot air rapidly, so ingredient ratios must be precise. If a recipe calls for 935 ml of orange juice, using the correct cup measurement ensures even cooking. This is especially important for dishes like orange cake or glazed salmon, where excess liquid can alter results.</p>  

<h2>Converting 935 ml of Orange Juice for Baking</h2>  
<p>Baking in an air fryer requires exact measurements for the best rise and texture. If a cake recipe lists 935 ml of orange juice, converting it correctly prevents a dense or overly moist outcome. For UK bakers, 3.29 cups (using 284 ml per cup) is the right amount, while US bakers should use 3.95 cups (240 ml per cup).</p>
