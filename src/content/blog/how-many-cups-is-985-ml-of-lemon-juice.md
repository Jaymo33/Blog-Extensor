<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Using the correct amount of lemon juice can make or break a recipe. Too little, and your dish may lack flavour; too much, and it could become overly acidic. For air fryer recipes, precision is key to achieving the right texture and taste. Whether you're following a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe</a> for lemon drizzle cake or a citrus-glazed chicken, 985 ml of lemon juice converts to roughly 4.16 cups. This measurement is based on the standard UK cup size of 240 ml.</p>  

<p>If you're working with a US recipe, note that their cup measurement is slightly different (236.59 ml). However, the difference is minimal, and 985 ml still rounds to about 4.16 cups. For air fryer cooking, this small variance won’t drastically affect your dish. Always double-check your conversions using a reliable <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> to avoid mistakes.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Lemon Juice Correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a liquid measuring cup rather than dry measuring cups. Fill the cup to the 985 ml mark, or measure out 4 cups plus 2 tablespoons (since 0.16 cups equals roughly 2 tbsp). If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale can help—985 ml of lemon juice weighs about 985 grams. This method is especially handy for <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/dessert>dessert recipes</a> where precision is crucial.</p>

<h3>Tips for Storing Lemon Juice</h3>  
<p>To keep your lemon juice fresh, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze it in ice cube trays—each cube holds about 30 ml, making it easy to thaw the exact amount you need. If you’re using bottled lemon juice, ensure it’s free from additives for the best results in your air fryer recipes.</p>
