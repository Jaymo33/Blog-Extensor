Whether you’re whipping up garlic-infused chicken or a flavourful vegetable stir-fry, knowing the exact weight ensures consistency. <h2>Why accurate garlic measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Garlic is a powerhouse ingredient that can elevate any dish, but too much or too little can throw off the balance. In air fryer cooking, where flavours intensify due to rapid air circulation, precision is key. For example, 150g of chopped garlic (or 5.29 ounces) is often used in recipes like garlic butter shrimp or roasted potatoes. Overdoing it can lead to overpowering flavours, while under-measuring might leave your dish lacking depth.</p>  
<p>If you’re scaling up a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, converting grams to ounces ensures you stay on track. A kitchen scale is the best tool for this, but if you don’t have one, visual cues can help—150g of chopped garlic roughly fills a 1-cup measure loosely packed. For more conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<h2>How to measure 150g of chopped garlic without a scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, don’t worry—there are easy ways to estimate 150g (5.29 ounces) of chopped garlic. One medium-sized garlic bulb typically yields about 10-12 cloves, which translates to roughly 50g when chopped. So, for 150g, you’d need around 3 bulbs or 30-36 cloves. Keep in mind that garlic cloves vary in size, so this method isn’t as precise as weighing.</p>  
<p>Another handy trick is using tablespoons. One tablespoon of chopped garlic weighs about 9g, so 150g would be approximately 16-17 tablespoons. This method works well for recipes like garlic bread or marinades from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken category</a>. Just remember, air fryer cooking benefits from exact measurements, so investing in a scale is worthwhile.</p>  

<h3>Common uses for 150g of chopped garlic</h3>  
<p>150g of chopped garlic (5.29 ounces) is a versatile quantity for many air fryer dishes. It’s perfect for batch-cooking garlic butter mushrooms or a large tray of garlic-roasted vegetables. You might also use this amount in marinades for meats like lamb or beef, ensuring every bite is packed with flavour. For inspiration, browse our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a>.</p>  

<h3>Storing leftover chopped garlic</h3>  
<p>If you’ve prepped 150g of chopped garlic but only need half, proper storage keeps it fresh. Transfer the leftovers to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze portions in ice cube trays—each cube holds about 10g, making it easy to grab what you need. Just thaw before adding to your air fryer dishes for consistent results.</p>
