<h2>How to measure 150g of chopped garlic without a scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, don’t worry—there are easy ways to estimate 150g (5.29 ounces) of chopped garlic. One medium-sized garlic bulb typically yields about 10-12 cloves, which translates to roughly 50g when chopped. So, for 150g, you’d need around 3 bulbs or 30-36 cloves. Keep in mind that garlic cloves vary in size, so this method isn’t as precise as weighing.</p>  
<p>Another handy trick is using tablespoons. One tablespoon of chopped garlic weighs about 9g, so 150g would be approximately 16-17 tablespoons. This method works well for recipes like garlic bread or marinades from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken category</a>. Just remember, air fryer cooking benefits from exact measurements, so investing in a scale is worthwhile.</p>

<h3>Storing leftover chopped garlic</h3>  
<p>If you’ve prepped 150g of chopped garlic but only need half, proper storage keeps it fresh. Transfer the leftovers to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to a week. For longer storage, freeze portions in ice cube trays—each cube holds about 10g, making it easy to grab what you need. Just thaw before adding to your air fryer dishes for consistent results.</p>
