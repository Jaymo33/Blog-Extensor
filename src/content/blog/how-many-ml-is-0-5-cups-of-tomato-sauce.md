Accurate measurements also ensure your dish matches the expected taste and presentation.</p>

Whether you're making sauces, marinades, or dips, measuring 0.5 cups of tomato sauce correctly (118 ml) is a small but crucial step.</p>

<h2>How to Measure 0.5 Cups of Tomato Sauce Without a Measuring Cup</h2>
<p>If you don't have a measuring cup handy, you can still measure 0.5 cups of tomato sauce accurately. A standard UK tablespoon holds 15 ml, so 8 tablespoons equal 120 ml, which is close enough to 118 ml. Alternatively, use a kitchen scale: 0.5 cups of tomato sauce weighs roughly 120 grams, which converts to 118 ml.</p>
<p>For those who prefer visual cues, 0.5 cups of tomato sauce fills about half a standard UK mug. This method is handy when you're mid-recipe and realise your measuring cups are in the dishwasher. Just remember, slight variations won't ruin your dish, but precision helps, especially in air fryer cooking.</p>

<h3>UK vs. US Measurements for Tomato Sauce</h3>
<p>While 0.5 cups of tomato sauce is 118 ml in the UK, it's worth noting that US cups differ slightly. A US cup is 240 ml, so 0.5 US cups equal 120 ml. This small difference usually doesn't affect recipes, but for precision, stick to the measurement system your recipe uses. Most <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a> recipes follow UK measurements.</p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Leftover Tomato Sauce</h2>
<p>If your recipe only uses part of a 0.5-cup (118 ml) measurement, store the rest properly. Transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 5 days. You can also freeze tomato sauce in ice cube trays for portioned future use. This is handy for quick <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a> or last-minute meals.</p>
<p>Always label your stored tomato sauce with the date and quantity. For example, note "60 ml" if you've stored half of your 0.5-cup measurement. This makes it easier to grab the right amount next time you're cooking. Proper storage ensures your tomato sauce stays fresh and ready for your next air fryer adventure.</p>
