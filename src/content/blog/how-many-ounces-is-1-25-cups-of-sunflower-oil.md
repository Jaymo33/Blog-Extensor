<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of oil in your air fryer ensures even cooking and prevents excess grease. Too little oil can lead to dry, unevenly cooked food, while too much can make dishes soggy. Sunflower oil, a popular choice for air frying due to its high smoke point, works best when measured correctly. For more tips on air fryer conversions, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, especially those for crispy vegetables or chicken, call for precise oil measurements. If you're preparing a dish that requires 1.25 cups of sunflower oil, knowing it’s roughly 10 ounces helps streamline your cooking process. This accuracy is particularly useful for meal prep or batch cooking, where consistency is key.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Sunflower Oil for Air Fryer Recipes</h3>  
<p>Measuring sunflower oil by weight (ounces) is often more accurate than using volume (cups). A kitchen scale ensures you get the exact amount needed, especially for recipes where oil plays a critical role. If you don’t have a scale, remember that 1 cup of sunflower oil weighs about 8 ounces, so 1.25 cups equals 10 ounces.</p>  

<h2>Common Uses for Sunflower Oil in Air Fryers</h2>  
<p>Sunflower oil is versatile and works well for frying, roasting, and even baking in an air fryer. Its neutral flavour and high smoke point (around 230°C) make it ideal for crispy potatoes, chicken wings, or even <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>air-fried vegetables</a>. When using 1.25 cups (10 ounces), you can coat ingredients evenly without over-saturating them.</p>  

<p>For those who love experimenting with air fryer desserts, sunflower oil is also great for cakes and cookies. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a> for inspiration. Whether you're making savoury or sweet dishes, precise oil measurements ensure consistent results every time.</p>  

<h3>Converting Other Oil Measurements for Air Fryer Cooking</h3>  
<p>If your recipe calls for a different oil amount, the same principle applies. For example, 1 cup of sunflower oil is 8 ounces, while 0.5 cups equal 4 ounces. Keeping these conversions handy simplifies cooking and reduces guesswork. For more measurement tips, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion guide</a>.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing and Using Sunflower Oil</h2>  
<p>To maintain freshness, store sunflower oil in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. Always check the expiry date, as rancid oil can ruin your dish. After cooking, clean your air fryer with our recommended <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning products</a> to prevent residue buildup.</p>  

<p>When substituting oils, remember that weights may vary slightly. For instance, olive oil is denser than sunflower oil, so 1.25 cups would weigh closer to 10.5 ounces. Always adjust measurements based on the oil type for best results.</p>
