Season the fish generously with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice to enhance its natural flavour. Preheat your air fryer to 200°C to ensure it cooks through without drying out, which usually takes around 12-15 minutes depending on thickness. Always check that the internal temperature reaches 63°C for food safety, using a digital thermometer for accuracy.</p>

<h3>Tips for Buying and Storing Cod Fillet</h3>
<p>When buying a 2.25 kg cod fillet, look for firm, glossy flesh with a fresh, sea-breeze smell and no discoloration or drying around the edges. If you are converting 2.25 kg cod fillet to pounds to match a recipe, ask your fishmonger to cut it to the exact weight you need to avoid waste. Once home, store the cod in the coldest part of your refrigerator and use it within one to two days for the best quality. Proper storage ensures your cod stays fresh and delicious, ready for air frying whenever you are.</p>
