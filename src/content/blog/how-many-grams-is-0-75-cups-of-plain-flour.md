<h2>Why Convert Cups to Grams?</h2>  
<p>Using grams instead of cups ensures consistency in your baking. Cups can vary based on how you scoop or pack the flour, leading to uneven results. Grams provide a precise measurement, which is particularly useful for air fryer recipes where even cooking is key. For example, a cake made with 94 grams of flour will rise evenly, while an imprecise cup measurement might leave it dense or dry. Many professional bakers and chefs rely on weight measurements for this very reason.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 0.75 Cups of Plain Flour Correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.75 cups of plain flour accurately, fluff the flour with a spoon before lightly scooping it into a measuring cup. Avoid packing it down, as this can add extra grams unintentionally. Level off the excess with a straight edge for a precise 0.75 cups. For best results, use a kitchen scale to confirm the weight—94 grams is the ideal target for most plain flour types.</p>

For example, a cake that bakes for 30 minutes in an oven might need just 20 minutes in an air fryer.</p>
