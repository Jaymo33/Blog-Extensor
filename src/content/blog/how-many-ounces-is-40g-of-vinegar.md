<h2>Why convert 40g of vinegar to ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but some kitchen scales or older cookbooks might list ounces. Converting 40g of vinegar to ounces (1.41 oz) ensures you don’t over or under-measure, which can affect flavour and texture. This is especially important in air fryer cooking, where precise ingredient ratios can make or break a dish. For example, too much vinegar in a glaze could overpower your chicken or salmon. <h3>How to measure 40g of vinegar without scales</h3>  
<p>No scales? No problem. One tablespoon of vinegar weighs about 15g, so 40g is roughly 2.5 tablespoons. For ounces, that’s just under 1.5 fluid ounces (since 1 fluid ounce ≈ 29.57ml, and vinegar’s density is close to water). A shot glass or small measuring jug can help eyeball it. Just avoid using teaspoons—they vary too much for precise conversions.</p>

<h3>Does vinegar type affect the 40g-to-ounces conversion?</h3>  
<p>No—whether it’s apple cider, white, or balsamic, 40g of any vinegar equals ~1.41 ounces by weight. However, thicker varieties (like balsamic) may measure slightly different in volume due to density. For recipes where consistency matters, stick to weight measurements for accuracy. This is key for air fryer sauces that need the right balance of liquid and thickness.</p>
