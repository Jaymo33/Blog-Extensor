<p>338°F converts to Gas Mark 3 in UK ovens and air fryers. This medium heat setting is ideal for baking cakes, roasting vegetables, or cooking casseroles with even results. Whether you're using a traditional oven or modern air fryer, understanding temperature conversions ensures your recipes turn out perfectly every time.</p>

<h2>Why Convert 338 F to Gas Mark?</h2>
<p>Many British recipes still use Gas Mark temperatures, while air fryers typically display Fahrenheit or Celsius. Converting 338 Fahrenheit to Gas Mark 3 helps maintain accuracy when switching between appliances. This particular temperature works beautifully for delicate bakes that need gentle, consistent heat without over-browning.</p>
<p>Gas Mark 3 (338°F/170°C) creates the perfect environment for Victoria sponges, custards, and slow-roasted meats. In air fryers, you might need to reduce cooking times by 15-20% compared to conventional ovens. <h3>How Gas Mark Compares to Air Fryer Settings</h3>
<p>While 338 F translates to Gas Mark 3, air fryers circulate heat more efficiently than conventional ovens. You may find foods cook faster at this temperature in an air fryer. For example, a 25-minute cake in a Gas Mark 3 oven might be done in 20 minutes using an air fryer at 338°F. Always check doneness 5 minutes early when converting recipes.</p>

<h2>Common Uses for 338 F (Gas Mark 3)</h2>
<p>This moderate temperature suits various cooking techniques. It's the sweet spot for baking madeleines, roasting root vegetables, or reheating leftovers without drying them out. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/cakes</a>, you'll find dozens of recipes specifically tested at 338 F to Gas Mark 3 for foolproof results.</p>
<p>When cooking frozen foods like fish fingers or chicken nuggets at 338 F, expect crispier results than higher temperatures provide. The lower heat allows thorough cooking without burning the exterior. For best results, preheat your air fryer for 3 minutes at this setting before adding food.</p>

<h3>Adjusting Cooking Times</h3>
<p>At 338 F to Gas Mark 3, meats generally require 25-30% longer cooking than at higher heats but retain more moisture. For vegetables, toss them in oil and cook for 15-20 minutes, shaking the basket halfway. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetable recipes</a> include precise timing guides for this temperature.</p>

<h2>Temperature Conversion Tips</h2>
<p>Remember that 338 Fahrenheit equals Gas Mark 3 and 170°C. When using an air fryer, the rapid air circulation means you might need to reduce standard oven temperatures by 10-15°F for equivalent results. Keep an eye on food during the last few minutes of cooking, as air fryers can vary between models.</p>
<p>For cleaning your appliance after cooking at 338 F, visit <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>our maintenance guide</a>. Sticky baked goods or oily foods might require extra care at this moderate temperature setting. Always let the air fryer cool completely before cleaning.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations</h3>
<p>When converting 338 degrees Fahrenheit to Gas Mark for recipes containing sugar (like caramel or meringues), the air fryer's concentrated heat may cause faster browning. Consider reducing the temperature by 5-10°F or covering delicate items with foil during the last few minutes.</p>
