<h2>Why Accurate Buttermilk Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Buttermilk plays a crucial role in baking, adding moisture and a slight tang to recipes. Using the wrong amount can affect texture and taste. For example, 310 ml of buttermilk in cups differs slightly between the UK and US due to varying cup sizes. In the UK, a standard cup holds 250 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. This means 310 ml converts to 1.31 UK cups or 1.25 US cups. If you're following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes</a>, always check which measurement system it uses.</p>

<h3>How to Measure 310 ml of Buttermilk Without a Measuring Cup</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a measuring cup, you can still measure 310 ml of buttermilk accurately. Use a kitchen scale: 310 ml of buttermilk weighs approximately 315 grams. Alternatively, a standard UK tablespoon holds 15 ml, so 310 ml is about 20.5 tablespoons. For US tablespoons (14.8 ml), it's roughly 21 tablespoons. This method is handy when adapting recipes for your air fryer, ensuring you get the right consistency every time.</p>

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Buttermilk</h3>  
<p>One mistake people make is confusing fluid ounces with millilitres. While 310 ml of buttermilk is roughly 10.5 fluid ounces, this doesn't directly translate to cups. Another error is using a dry measuring cup for liquids, which can lead to inaccuracies. Always use a liquid measuring cup for 310 ml of buttermilk to ensure the correct amount.
