<h2>Why Accurate Flour Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and slight variations in flour quantities can drastically alter your results. Too much flour can make baked goods dense, while too little may leave them crumbly. For air fryer recipes, where heat distribution is rapid, precise measurements like converting 3.0 cups of plain flour to grams ensure consistency. <p>Using a kitchen scale is the most reliable method, as cup measurements can vary based on how the flour is scooped. Fluffing the flour before spooning it into a cup and leveling it off helps, but weighing eliminates inconsistencies. For 3.0 cups of plain flour in grams, 375g is the standard, but always check your recipe’s specific requirements.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Flour Perfectly</h3>  
<p>Avoid packing flour into the cup, as this compacts it and leads to excess. Instead, spoon flour lightly into the measuring cup and level it with a knife. For absolute precision, use a digital scale set to grams. This method is especially useful for air fryer baking, where even small deviations can affect cooking times and textures.</p>
