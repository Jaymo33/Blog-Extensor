<p>1.75 cups of soy sauce equals approximately 14 fluid ounces (or 14.8 oz by weight). Whether you're whipping up a marinade for your air fryer or measuring ingredients for a stir-fry, knowing this conversion ensures your dish turns out just right. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we understand the importance of precise measurements, especially when working with bold flavours like soy sauce.</p>  

<h2>Why accurate soy sauce measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Soy sauce is a powerful ingredient that can easily overpower a dish if used incorrectly. Whether you're marinating chicken for the air fryer or adding depth to a stir-fry, getting the measurement right is crucial. Too much can make your food overly salty, while too little might leave it lacking in flavour. That's why converting 1.75 cups of soy sauce to ounces (or grams) helps maintain balance in your cooking.</p>  

<p>Many air fryer recipes, like those found in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>, rely on precise liquid measurements for marinades and sauces. Since soy sauce is denser than water, its weight conversion differs slightly from volume measurements. This is particularly important when scaling recipes up or down for different serving sizes.</p>  

<h3>Fluid ounces vs weight ounces</h3>  
<p>When converting 1.75 cups of soy sauce to ounces, it's essential to know whether you need fluid ounces (volume) or weight ounces. For most cooking purposes, fluid ounces are sufficient, as they measure the space the liquid occupies. However, some recipes, especially baking or precise marinades, may require weight measurements for consistency.</p>  

<h2>How to measure soy sauce for air fryer recipes</h2>  
<p>Measuring soy sauce accurately is key to successful air fryer cooking. For 1.75 cups, you'll need a liquid measuring cup with clear markings. Fill to the 1 3/4 cup line at eye level for the most accurate reading. If your recipe calls for ounces instead, remember that 1.75 cups equals 14 fluid ounces - a measurement that works perfectly for many air fryer marinades.</p>

This is particularly handy when adapting traditional oven recipes for your air fryer, where precise ratios make all the difference in cooking times and results.</p>  

<h3>Adjusting soy sauce in recipes</h3>  
<p>If you're working with a recipe that calls for 1.75 cups of soy sauce but need to adjust the quantity, knowing the ounce equivalent (14 fl oz) makes scaling simpler. For example, halving the recipe would require 7 fluid ounces, while doubling would need 28 fluid ounces. This flexibility is especially useful when meal prepping multiple air fryer dishes at once.</p>  

<h2>Soy sauce alternatives and substitutions</h2>  
<p>If you're out of soy sauce or need a lower-sodium option, there are several alternatives that can work in your air fryer recipes. Coconut aminos, tamari, or even a homemade blend can substitute for 1.75 cups (14 oz) of soy sauce. Just remember that each alternative may slightly alter the flavour profile of your dish, so adjustments might be needed.</p>  

<p>For those watching their salt intake, reducing the soy sauce quantity while maintaining other flavours is key. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipe category</a> includes several dishes where soy sauce measurements can be adjusted without sacrificing taste. The air fryer's intense heat helps concentrate flavours, meaning you can often use slightly less soy sauce than traditional recipes suggest.</p>  

<h3>Cleaning after soy sauce use</h3>  
<p>Soy sauce can leave stubborn stains and odours in your air fryer basket if not cleaned properly.
