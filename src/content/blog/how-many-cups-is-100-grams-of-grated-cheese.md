<h2>How to Convert 100 Grams of Grated Cheese to Cups</h2>
<p>Converting 100 grams of grated cheese to cups depends on the cheese type and grating style. Finely grated cheese packs more densely, so 100 grams may fill just under 1 cup. Coarsely grated cheese is fluffier, meaning 100 grams could be slightly more than 1 cup.</p>
<p>For popular cheeses like cheddar or mozzarella, 100 grams usually equals about 1 cup when lightly packed. If you're working with harder cheeses like parmesan, 100 grams may be closer to ¾ cup. Always double-check with a kitchen scale for accuracy, especially when baking.</p>

<h3>Common Cheese Types and Their Cup Equivalents</h3>
<p>Cheddar, mozzarella, and similar semi-soft cheeses typically measure 100 grams to 1 cup when grated. Harder cheeses like parmesan or pecorino are denser, so 100 grams may only fill ¾ cup. Soft cheeses like feta or goat cheese crumble differently, making volume measurements less precise.</p>
