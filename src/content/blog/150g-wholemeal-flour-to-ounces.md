<h2>Why converting 150g wholemeal flour to ounces matters</h2>  
<p>Baking is a science, and even small measurement discrepancies can affect your air fryer recipes. Wholemeal flour, with its denser texture, requires precise weighing for the best results. Converting 150g to ounces (5.29 oz) ensures your dough or batter isn’t too dry or sticky. Many UK bakers prefer using grams, but some recipes, especially older or American ones, list ingredients in ounces. <p>Using the correct weight also prevents overpacking the flour, which can lead to dense, heavy bakes. A kitchen scale is the most accurate tool, but if you’re without one, knowing that 150g wholemeal flour equals roughly 5.29 ounces helps. <h3>How to measure 150g wholemeal flour without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have scales, use the spoon-and-level method for 150g (5.29 oz) of wholemeal flour. Gently spoon the flour into a measuring cup, then level it off with a knife. Avoid scooping directly from the bag, as this compacts the flour and adds extra weight. One UK metric cup of wholemeal flour holds about 120g, so 150g is roughly 1¼ cups. For best results, invest in a digital scale—it’s a game-changer for air fryer baking.</p>
