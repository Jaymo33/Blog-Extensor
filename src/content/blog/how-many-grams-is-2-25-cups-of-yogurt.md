<h3>Types of Yogurt and Their Weights</h3>  
<p>Not all yogurts weigh the same per cup due to differences in thickness and fat content. Greek yogurt, for instance, is denser than regular yogurt, so 2.25 cups may weigh closer to 600 grams. Always check the label or use a kitchen scale for the most accurate measurement, especially when following air fryer recipes where precision matters.</p>

<h3>Tips for Measuring Yogurt Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>No kitchen scale? No problem! Use the spoon-and-level method: spoon yogurt into a measuring cup and level it off with a knife. For 2.25 cups, fill two cups completely and add a quarter cup. While this method isn’t as precise as weighing, it’s a handy alternative for quick conversions.</p>
