Whether you’re coating chicken, fish, or vegetables, knowing the exact weight helps avoid soggy or overly dry dishes. <h2>Why accurate breadcrumb measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Using the right amount of breadcrumbs ensures your air-fried dishes turn out perfectly crispy. Too little, and your coating won’t stick; too much, and it can become dry or burn. For example, 20g of breadcrumbs (0.71 oz) is ideal for coating two chicken breasts or a small batch of air-fried vegetables. If you’re following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our collection</a>, precise measurements guarantee consistency. Plus, converting grams to ounces helps if your kitchen scale only displays one unit. No more second-guessing—just perfect results every time.</p>  

<p>Breadcrumbs also absorb moisture differently depending on their type. Fresh breadcrumbs weigh more than dried ones, so 20g of fresh crumbs will look like less in volume. For air fryer cooking, dried breadcrumbs work best as they crisp up evenly. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> for other common ingredient weights. Whether you’re cooking fish, chicken, or veggies, accurate measurements make all the difference.</p>  

<h2>How to measure 20g of breadcrumbs without a scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can still measure 20g of breadcrumbs using tablespoons. One tablespoon of dried breadcrumbs weighs roughly 7g, so 20g is about 3 tablespoons. However, this can vary slightly based on how finely the crumbs are ground. For best results, fluff the breadcrumbs lightly before measuring to avoid packing them down. This method works well for recipes like air-fried chicken tenders or crispy tofu from our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/tofu>tofu category</a>. Remember, slight variations won’t ruin your dish, but precision helps.</p>  

<h3>Types of breadcrumbs and their weights</h3>  
<p>Not all breadcrumbs weigh the same. Panko breadcrumbs, for example, are lighter and flakier than traditional dried crumbs. 20g of panko will fill more volume than regular breadcrumbs, so adjust accordingly. For air fryer recipes, panko creates an extra-crispy texture, perfect for dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air-fried chicken katsu</a>. Homemade breadcrumbs, on the other hand, can vary depending on the bread used. If you’re unsure, weigh them once to get a baseline for future recipes.</p>  

<h2>Common air fryer recipes using 20g of breadcrumbs</h2>  
<p>Many air fryer recipes call for 20g of breadcrumbs, making this conversion handy to know. For example, crispy air-fried mushrooms or zucchini fries often use this amount for a light, even coating. If you’re meal prepping, check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/meal-prep>meal prep section</a> for ideas that use 20g portions efficiently. Breadcrumbs also help bind ingredients in veggie burgers or meatballs, ensuring they hold their shape in the air fryer. With the right measurement, you’ll avoid waste and achieve consistent results.</p>  

<h3>Storing leftover breadcrumbs</h3>  
<p>If you have leftover breadcrumbs after measuring 20g, store them in an airtight container to keep them fresh. Dried breadcrumbs last up to 6 months in the pantry, while panko stays fresh for about 3 months. For longer storage, freeze them in a sealed bag. When you’re ready to use them again, let them come to room temperature to avoid clumping. This way, you’ll always have the right amount on hand for your next air fryer creation.</p>
