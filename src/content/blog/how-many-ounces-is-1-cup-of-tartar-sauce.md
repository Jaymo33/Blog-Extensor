<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h2>  
<p>Getting the right amount of tartar sauce ensures your dishes aren't too dry or overly saucy. For air fryer cooking, precise measurements help achieve even cooking and perfect textures. Too much sauce can lead to soggy results, while too little might leave your dish lacking flavour. Whether you're making crispy fish or chicken tenders, knowing how many ounces are in 1 cup of tartar sauce keeps your recipes balanced. Check out our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> for more handy measurement tips.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure Tartar Sauce Correctly</h2>  
<p>Using a liquid measuring cup is the best way to measure tartar sauce accurately. Scoop the sauce into the cup and level it off with a spatula for an exact 8-ounce measurement. If you're working with a thicker consistency, gently press down to remove air pockets. For smaller quantities, a kitchen scale can also help verify the weight. Visit our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a> for ideas on how to use tartar sauce in your air fryer creations.</p>  

<h3>Common Uses for Tartar Sauce in Air Fryers</h3>  
<p>Tartar sauce is a versatile condiment that pairs perfectly with air-fried seafood. Try it with crispy cod, shrimp, or even as a dip for homemade fish cakes. You can also mix it into batters or use it as a marinade for extra flavour. <h3>Storing Tartar Sauce Properly</h3>  
<p>Once opened, tartar sauce should be refrigerated and used within a few weeks. If you've made a large batch, consider freezing smaller portions for later use. Proper storage ensures your sauce stays fresh and ready for your next air fryer meal. Always check for spoilage before using leftover sauce in recipes.</p>
