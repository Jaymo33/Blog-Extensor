<p>560 ml of milk is approximately 2.37 cups in the US measurement system and roughly 2.46 cups in the UK system. Knowing this conversion is essential for precision in recipes, especially when using an air fryer where measurements can impact cooking times and textures. Whether you're whipping up a cake or preparing a creamy sauce, getting the milk quantity right ensures consistent results every time.</p>

<p>When cooking or baking, especially with an air fryer, precise measurements can make or break a dish. Too much milk can lead to soggy textures, while too little might dry out your food. For example, if you're making <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>air fryer pancakes</a>, 560 ml of milk (or roughly 2.37 US cups) ensures the batter has the right consistency for fluffy results. <p>In the UK, where metric measurements dominate, 560 ml is a common quantity in recipes. However, many older cookbooks or international recipes still use cups, making conversions necessary. A slight variation between US and UK cup sizes (240 ml vs. 250 ml) means paying attention to which system your recipe follows. For air fryer cooking, where timing is critical, even small measurement discrepancies can affect outcomes.</p>  

<h3>US vs. UK Cup Measurements for 560 ml of Milk</h3>  
<p>In the US, 1 cup equals 240 ml, so 560 ml converts to about 2.37 cups. In the UK, 1 cup is 250 ml, making 560 ml roughly 2.46 cups. This difference might seem minor, but for recipes requiring precision, like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cakes</a>, it’s worth noting. Always check whether your recipe uses US or UK measurements to avoid mishaps.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 560 ml of Milk Without a Scale</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use standard cups to measure 560 ml of milk. In the US, 2 cups (480 ml) plus 2 tablespoons (30 ml) will get you close to 560 ml. In the UK, 2 cups (500 ml) plus 2 tablespoons (30 ml) approximates 560 ml. For air fryer recipes, like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversions for batters</a>, this method works well in a pinch.</p>

This is especially useful for recipes where precision matters, such as custards or sauces. If you’re adapting a stovetop recipe for the air fryer, accurate milk measurements ensure even cooking and perfect texture.</p>
