<p>Buttermilk is a staple in many recipes, from cakes to marinades, and getting the measurement right is crucial. When a recipe calls for 660 ml of buttermilk, it's helpful to know the cup equivalent to avoid guesswork. In the UK, 1 cup equals 250 ml, while in the US, it's 240 ml. This slight difference means 660 ml of buttermilk converts to 2.8 cups in the UK and 2.79 cups in the US.</p>

<h3>Measuring Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don't have a measuring jug, you can use standard cups to measure 660 ml of buttermilk. Fill a UK cup (250 ml) twice and add another 3/4 cup to reach roughly 660 ml. For US cups (240 ml), fill two cups and add another 3/4 cup plus 1 tablespoon.</p>
