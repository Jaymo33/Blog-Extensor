<h2>Why Convert 200g of Rice to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, but some kitchen scales or older cookbooks might list ingredients in ounces. <h3>How to Measure 200g of Rice Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can use measuring cups to estimate 200g of rice. One UK metric cup holds about 200g of uncooked rice, but this varies slightly by rice type. For long-grain rice, 200g fills roughly 1 cup, while short-grain varieties might be slightly denser. Always fluff the rice before measuring for the most accurate results.</p>  

<h2>Types of Rice and Their Weight Differences</h2>  
<p>Not all rice weighs the same per cup. For instance, 200g of basmati rice occupies more volume than 200g of sticky rice due to grain density. This means converting 200g of rice to ounces (7.05 oz) remains consistent, but the physical amount differs. When adapting recipes for your air fryer, consider the rice type to avoid overpacking the basket. <p>Brown rice, wild rice, and jasmine rice also have unique weights and cooking times. Brown rice, for example, is denser and may require slight adjustments in liquid ratios when air-frying. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Rice</h3>  
<p>One frequent error is compacting rice into a measuring cup, which leads to excess weight. For 200g of rice (7.05 oz), gently spoon the grains into the cup and level it off. Another mistake is ignoring rice expansion during cooking—200g of uncooked rice nearly triples in volume once prepared. Avoid overfilling your air fryer basket by accounting for this expansion.</p>
