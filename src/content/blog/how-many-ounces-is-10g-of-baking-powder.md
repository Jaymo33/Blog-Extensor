<h2>Why Convert 10g of Baking Powder to Ounces?</h2>  
<p>Many UK recipes use grams, while others rely on ounces, making conversions a common kitchen task. Baking powder, a leavening agent, requires precision—too much or too little can affect texture and rise. For air fryer recipes, where space and heat distribution differ from conventional ovens, exact measurements are even more critical. A slight miscalculation could lead to dense cakes or overly puffed batters. Our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>air fryer conversion chart</a> simplifies these adjustments, ensuring your bakes turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<p>If you’re adapting a US recipe, which often uses ounces, converting 10g of baking powder to 0.35 ounces ensures you stay on track. This small but vital step guarantees your air fryer creations, like fluffy scones or crispy fritters, have the right lift. For more recipe ideas, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipe collection</a>, tailored for air fryer enthusiasts.</p>  

<h2>How to Measure 10g of Baking Powder Accurately</h2>  
<p>Using a digital kitchen scale is the most reliable way to measure 10g of baking powder. Scales eliminate guesswork and provide precise readings, crucial for baking success. If you don’t have a scale, a measuring spoon can work—but note that baking powder’s density means 10g roughly equals two level teaspoons. However, for air fryer recipes, where even distribution matters, weighing is always best.</p>  

<h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Baking Powder</h3>  
<p>One frequent error is packing baking powder into a spoon, which leads to excess. Always spoon it lightly and level it off for accuracy. Another pitfall is using old or clumped baking powder, which won’t measure correctly. Store it in a cool, dry place to maintain its potency. For tips on keeping your air fryer in top condition, check our guide on <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-cleaner>cleaning your air fryer</a>.</p>

<p>While 10g of baking powder equals 0.35 ounces, other ingredients have different conversions. For example, 10g of flour is roughly 0.37 ounces, and 10g of sugar is about 0.38 ounces.
