<h3>How to measure crushed garlic correctly</h3>  
<p>For the most accurate results, use a digital kitchen scale to weigh your crushed garlic. If you don’t have one, lightly pack the garlic into a tablespoon without pressing too hard, as this can affect the weight. Freshly crushed garlic tends to be denser than pre-packaged varieties, so adjust accordingly. Always level off the spoon for consistency, especially when following recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h2>Common conversions for crushed garlic</h2>  
<p>Beyond 1 tbsp, here are some handy conversions for crushed garlic: 1 teaspoon (tsp) equals roughly 3 grams, while 2 tbsp weigh around 18 grams. These measurements are useful when scaling recipes up or down. If you’re working with whole cloves, one medium clove yields about 1 tsp of crushed garlic.
