<h2>Understanding 364 F to Gas Mark Conversion</h2>
<p>The Gas Mark scale is uniquely British, with each increment representing a specific temperature range. Gas Mark 4.5 sits between Gas Mark 4 (350°F/180°C) and Gas Mark 5 (375°F/190°C), providing precise control for delicate bakes. When converting 364 fahrenheit to gas mark, it's helpful to know that each full Gas Mark equals about 25-30°F. This 364 degrees f to gas mark conversion is particularly useful for American recipes that need adapting for UK ovens or air fryers.</p>
<p>Many modern ovens now display both Fahrenheit and Gas Mark, but if yours doesn't, memorising key conversions like 364 f in gas mark can save time. For air fryer users, this temperature is ideal for items like scones, muffins or delicate pastries that benefit from moderate, even heat. <h3>Adjusting for Fan Ovens</h3>
<p>If you're using a fan-assisted oven with the 364 f fan oven to gas mark conversion, you'll typically reduce the temperature by 20°C. However, since Gas Marks don't have decimal points in most oven controls, you'd round Gas Mark 4.5 down to Gas Mark 4 for fan ovens. Always check your food 5-10 minutes early when making these adjustments.</p>
