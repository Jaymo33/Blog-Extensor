<h2>How to Measure 410 ml of Lemon Juice</h2>  
<p>If you don’t have a measuring jug, you can use standard cups to measure 410 ml of lemon juice. One metric cup holds 250 ml, so 410 ml is roughly 1 and 2/3 cups. For US cups (240 ml), it’s about 1.73 cups. Always use fresh lemon juice for the best results in your air fryer dishes.</p>

<h3>Adjusting for Different Cup Sizes</h3>  
<p>Remember that cup sizes vary by region. UK and Australian cups are 250 ml, while US cups are 240 ml. For 410 ml of lemon juice, the difference is minimal but worth noting for precision baking. Always confirm which standard your recipe follows.</p>  

<h2>Tips for Storing Leftover Lemon Juice</h2>  
<p>If you have leftover lemon juice, store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. You can also freeze it in ice cube trays for future use. This way, you’ll always have lemon juice ready for your next air fryer adventure.</p>
