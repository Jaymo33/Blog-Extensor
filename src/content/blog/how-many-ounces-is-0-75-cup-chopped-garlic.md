<p>Garlic is a powerhouse of flavour, and using the right amount can elevate your dish. Too little, and your meal might lack depth; too much, and it can overpower other ingredients. <h3>How to measure chopped garlic correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.75 cup of chopped garlic accurately, use a dry measuring cup and lightly pack the garlic without crushing it. If you’re converting to ounces, a kitchen scale is your best friend. Simply tare the scale with your bowl, add the garlic until it reads 3.6 ounces, and you’re good to go. This method works perfectly for air fryer recipes like garlic bread or crispy garlic potatoes.</p>

<h3>Tips for storing chopped garlic</h3>  
<p>If you’ve measured out 0.75 cup of chopped garlic but don’t need it all, proper storage is key. Transfer leftovers to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to a week. <h2>Converting other garlic measurements</h2>  
<p>While 0.75 cup of chopped garlic equals 3.6 ounces, other conversions are handy too. One clove of garlic is roughly 0.1 ounces, so 0.75 cup is equivalent to about 36 cloves. If a recipe calls for minced instead of chopped garlic, the weight remains the same, but the volume may differ slightly due to texture. For more conversion help, explore our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<p>Garlic powder and granulated garlic have different weights, so always check the packaging. As a rule, one teaspoon of garlic powder equals one fresh clove. Keeping these conversions in mind ensures your air fryer dishes, from garlic-roasted potatoes to crispy tofu, turn out perfectly seasoned every time.</p>
