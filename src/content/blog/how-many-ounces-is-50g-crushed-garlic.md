<h2>Why accurate garlic measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Garlic is a powerhouse ingredient that can transform any dish, but too much or too little can throw off flavours. In air fryer cooking, even small measurement discrepancies can affect texture and taste. For example, 50g of crushed garlic (1.76 oz) is roughly equivalent to 10-12 cloves, depending on size. This precision ensures your garlic-infused dishes, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>recipes section</a>, turn out just right.</p>  

<p>Using a kitchen scale is the most reliable way to measure 50g of crushed garlic, but if you don’t have one, visual estimates can help. A heaped tablespoon of crushed garlic is roughly 15g, so 50g would be just over three tablespoons. For more tips on air fryer measurements, check our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to measure 50g of crushed garlic without scales</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a scale, you can estimate 50g of crushed garlic using common kitchen tools. A standard garlic clove weighs about 4-5g when crushed, so 50g would be around 10-12 cloves. Alternatively, a packed UK tablespoon holds roughly 15g, meaning three slightly heaped tablespoons will get you close to 50g. For recipes requiring exact amounts, like those in our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/vegetables>vegetables category</a>, scales are best.</p>

For example, a garlic butter sauce for air-fryer potatoes might call for 50g (1.76 oz) of crushed garlic—now you’ll know exactly how much to use.</p>
