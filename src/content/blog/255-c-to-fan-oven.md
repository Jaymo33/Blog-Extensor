<p>255°C in a conventional oven converts to approximately 230°C in a fan oven. This 25°C reduction is standard for most fan-assisted cooking, ensuring even heat distribution without overcooking. Whether you're baking, roasting, or using your air fryer, understanding this conversion is key to perfect results.</p>

<h2>Understanding Fan Oven Temperature Conversions</h2>
<p>Fan ovens circulate hot air around the food, cooking it more evenly and efficiently than conventional ovens. This means you typically need to reduce the temperature by 20-25°C when converting from conventional to fan settings. At <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>, we recommend starting with a 20°C reduction and adjusting based on your appliance's performance.</p>
<p>The 255°C to fan oven conversion is particularly useful for high-temperature cooking like roasting meats or baking artisan bread. Many air fryer users find our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> helpful when adapting traditional oven recipes. Remember that cooking times may also need adjustment when using fan settings.</p>

<h2>How 255°C Converts in Different Appliances</h2>
<p>While 255°C becomes 230°C in a fan oven, the conversion differs slightly for air fryers. Most air fryers operate similarly to fan ovens but with more intense air circulation. For 255°C conventional oven recipes, we suggest trying 220°C in your air fryer and checking for doneness a few minutes early.</p>
<p>This temperature works beautifully for crispy <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>chicken recipes</a> or roasted vegetables. The concentrated heat of an air fryer often produces better results than conventional ovens at these high temperatures. Just be sure not to overcrowd the basket for proper air circulation.</p>

<h3>Special Considerations for Baking at 255°C</h3>
<p>When baking at high temperatures like 255°C (230°C fan), watch your goods carefully. The intense heat can cause items to brown too quickly on the outside while remaining undercooked inside. Rotating trays halfway through cooking helps ensure even results in fan ovens and air fryers alike.</p>

<h2>Why Temperature Accuracy Matters</h2>
<p>Precise temperature control makes all the difference in cooking. A true 255°C conventional oven temperature delivers different results than its 230°C fan equivalent. This is why professional recipes often specify which oven type they're designed for.</p>
