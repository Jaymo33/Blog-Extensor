<p>10 ml of milk equals approximately 0.042 cups. This small measurement is often used in air fryer recipes, especially for sauces, batters, or glazes where precision matters. Whether you're baking or cooking, knowing how to convert millilitres to cups ensures your dishes turn out perfectly every time.</p>  

<h2>Understanding 10 ml of Milk in Cups</h2>  
<p>When working with small quantities like 10 ml of milk, accuracy is key. In the UK, metric measurements are standard, but many recipes—especially those from the US—use cups. For air fryer cooking, where ingredient ratios can make or break a dish, getting the conversion right is essential. A quick tip: 1 cup equals 240 ml, so 10 ml is just a fraction of that.</p>  
<p>If you're using an air fryer for baking, such as making muffins or cakes, even small amounts like 10 ml of milk can affect texture. Too much liquid can make batters runny, while too little can dry them out. That’s why <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion charts</a> are a lifesaver for home cooks.</p>

<h2>Converting 10 ml of Milk for Different Recipes</h2>  
<p>Different recipes may require slight adjustments when converting 10 ml of milk to cups. For baking, sticking to exact measurements ensures the right rise and texture.
