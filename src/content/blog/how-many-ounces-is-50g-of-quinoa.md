<h3>How to Measure 50g of Quinoa Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, you can use measuring cups to estimate 50g of quinoa. One cup of uncooked quinoa weighs around 170g, so 50g is roughly a quarter cup. However, for precision, we recommend using a scale. This ensures your air fryer recipes turn out perfectly every time. <h2>Best Air Fryer Recipes Using 50g of Quinoa</h2>  
<p>Quinoa is a fantastic ingredient for air fryer meals, offering a protein-packed base for many dishes. Try using 50g (1.76 oz) of quinoa in recipes like crispy quinoa fritters or as a stuffing for roasted vegetables. The air fryer’s even heat distribution ensures perfectly cooked quinoa every time. <p>Another great option is toasting 50g of quinoa in the air fryer for a crunchy salad topping. Simply spread the quinoa evenly in the basket and cook at 180°C for 5-7 minutes, shaking occasionally. This method enhances the nutty flavour of quinoa, making it a delicious addition to any dish. <h3>Common Mistakes When Measuring Quinoa</h3>  
<p>One common mistake is confusing cooked and uncooked quinoa measurements. 50g of uncooked quinoa yields about 150g when cooked, so always check your recipe’s requirements. Also, ensure your scale is set to grams for accurate conversions. These small details can make a big difference in your air fryer cooking results.</p>
