<h2>Why Accurate Measurements Matter in Air Fryer Cooking</h2>  
<p>Air fryers rely on even cooking, and incorrect measurements can lead to undercooked or dry results. When converting 400 grams of minced beef to cups, the density of the meat plays a role. Loosely packed minced beef will measure closer to 2 cups, while tightly packed may be around 1.7 cups. This variation is why weighing ingredients is often more reliable than volume measurements. For perfectly cooked minced beef dishes, always aim for consistency.</p>  

<p>If you're preparing a <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/beef>beef-based recipe</a>, knowing the exact cup measurement helps balance flavours and textures. Too much meat can overcrowd the air fryer basket, while too little may dry out quickly. For best results, use a kitchen scale for precision, especially when following recipes from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk>airfryerrecipe.co.uk</a>.</p>  

<h3>How to Measure Minced Beef Without a Scale</h3>  
<p>If you don’t have a kitchen scale, use the spoon-and-level method for minced beef. Gently spoon the meat into a measuring cup without packing it down, then level it off with a knife. This method gives a rough estimate of 400 grams of minced beef in cups, though weighing remains the gold standard. For air fryer recipes, slight variations can affect cooking time, so adjust accordingly.</p>
