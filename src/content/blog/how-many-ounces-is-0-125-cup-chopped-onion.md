<h2>Why accurate onion measurements matter</h2>  
<p>Chopped onion is a staple in many dishes, and getting the amount right can make or break a recipe. In air fryer cooking, where ingredients cook quickly and evenly, precise measurements ensure consistent results. If you're working with 0.125 cup of chopped onion, knowing it’s roughly 0.5 oz helps streamline your prep.</p>

<h3>How to measure chopped onion correctly</h3>  
<p>To measure 0.125 cup of chopped onion, use a standard measuring cup and pack the onion lightly. Avoid pressing too firmly, as this can compress the onion and skew the weight. If you don’t have a measuring cup, a kitchen scale is the most accurate way to measure 0.5 oz of chopped onion. This method is especially useful for air fryer recipes, where consistency is crucial.</p>

<h2>Tips for storing chopped onion</h2>  
<p>Chopped onion can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. For longer storage, freeze it in portioned amounts (like 0.125 cup or 0.5 oz) for easy use later. This is a great time-saver for busy cooks who want to prep ingredients in advance. Just thaw and pat dry before adding to your air fryer recipes.</p>
