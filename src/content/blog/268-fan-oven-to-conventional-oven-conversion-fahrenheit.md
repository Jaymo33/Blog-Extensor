<h2>Why Convert 268 Fan Oven to Conventional Oven?</h2>
<p>Fan ovens cook faster and more evenly than conventional ovens due to their built-in fan. This means recipes designed for conventional ovens need adjustments when using a fan oven or air fryer. A 268°F fan oven setting is roughly equivalent to 248°F in a conventional oven. This 20°F difference can make or break delicate dishes like cakes or pastries. Air fryers cook food quickly with intense circulating heat, so starting with the right temperature is key. For example, if a recipe calls for 268°F in a fan oven, you’d typically set your air fryer to 248°F. This ensures your food cooks evenly without burning. <h2>How to Adjust Cooking Times</h2>
<p>Beyond temperature, cooking times also need adjustment when converting between oven types. A 268°F fan oven recipe might take 20% less time than a conventional oven at 248°F. This is because the circulating heat cooks food more efficiently. For air fryers, which are even more intense, you might need to reduce cooking times by 25-30%. Always check food a few minutes early to avoid overcooking.</p>
