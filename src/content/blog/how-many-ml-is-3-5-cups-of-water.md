<h2>Understanding Cup to ml Conversions</h2>  
<p>Converting cups to millilitres is essential for precision in cooking, especially with air fryer recipes. A standard UK cup holds 237 ml, while a US cup is slightly larger at 240 ml. For 3.5 cups of water, this means 828 ml in the UK or 840 ml in the US. If you’re following a recipe from <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipes>our recipe collection</a>, always check which measurement system it uses. Small differences can impact texture and cooking times, so accuracy matters.</p>  

<p>Air fryers require precise liquid measurements to avoid soggy or dry results. Too much water can steam food instead of crisping it, while too little may cause burning. For perfect dishes like <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/chicken>air fryer chicken</a>, stick to the recommended ml conversions. Keep a measuring jug or digital scale handy for consistency, and refer to our <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/air-fryer-conversion-chart>conversion chart</a> for quick checks.</p>  

<h3>Why Accurate Measurements Matter</h3>  
<p>Air fryers cook food quickly, so even small measurement errors can lead to uneven results. Using 828 ml (3.5 cups) instead of guessing ensures your recipes turn out perfectly every time. This is especially important for baking, where ratios are critical. For example, a moist <a href=https://www.airfryerrecipe.co.uk/recipe-category/cakes>air fryer cake</a> relies on the right balance of liquids and dry ingredients.</p>

<p>Whether you’re making soups, sauces, or marinades, 3.5 cups of water (828 ml) is a common measurement. For broths or stocks, this amount ensures enough liquid without diluting flavours. When measuring 3.5 cups of water (828 ml), place the jug on a flat surface and check at eye level for accuracy.
