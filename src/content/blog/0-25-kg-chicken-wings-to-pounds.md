<h2>Practical tips for cooking 0.25 kg chicken wings in your air fryer</h2>
<p>Once you’ve converted 0.25 kg chicken wings to pounds, preheating your air fryer is the next key step. For most models, 200°C works beautifully for achieving crispy skin without drying out the meat. Arrange the wings in a single layer with a bit of space between them to allow hot air to circulate. Cooking time is usually around 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through for even browning. Always check the internal temperature reaches 74°C for food safety before serving.</p>

<p>Seasoning your 0.25 kg chicken wings before air frying enhances flavour without adding extra oil. A simple mix of salt, pepper, and paprika works wonders, or you can try marinades for more depth. Lightly spraying with oil can boost crispiness, but it’s not essential—the air fryer does most of the work. For saucy wings, add glaze in the last few minutes of cooking to avoid burning. <h3>Common mistakes to avoid</h3>
<p>Overcrowding the basket is the biggest mistake when cooking 0.25 kg chicken wings in pounds or any amount. Even if they fit, overlapping wings steam rather than crisp up, leading to soggy results. Not patting the wings dry before seasoning can also prevent that desirable crunch. Skipping the preheat step might extend cooking time unevenly, so always give your air fryer a few minutes to warm up. Finally, forgetting to shake or flip halfway through can leave one side undercooked while the other overcooks.</p>
